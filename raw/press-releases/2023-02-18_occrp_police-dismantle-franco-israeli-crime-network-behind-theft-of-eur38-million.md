## Summary

OCCRP reported that the Franco-Israeli network used CEO-fraud and man-in-the-email techniques to steer more than EUR 38 million into accounts it controlled. The report described the French metallurgy and Paris real-estate victim cases, the discovery of the link between them in January 2022, and the international follow-on involving Croatia, Portugal, and Hungary. OCCRP also highlighted that more than EUR 10 million was frozen during the laundering phase, although the final value of seizures recorded by the operation was EUR 5.5 million.

## Key Data Points

- **Case framing:** CEO fraud / man-in-the-email scam network
- **Victim losses:** over EUR 38 million
- **International freezing efforts:** over EUR 10 million traced or frozen during laundering
- **Final seizures reported:** EUR 5.5 million
- **Operational climax:** eight raids and suspect arrests in France and Israel
