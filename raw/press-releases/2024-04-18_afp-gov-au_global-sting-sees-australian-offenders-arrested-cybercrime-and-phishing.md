## Key Facts (verbatim from AFP release)

- **AFP operation codename (Australia arm):** "Operation Nebulae".
- **Lead AFP unit:** Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3).
- **Joint release partners:** AFP, Victoria Police, Queensland Police Service, NSW Police Force, WA Police Force.
- **Australian arrests:** 5 individuals — 1 Melbourne man + 1 Adelaide man charged with cybercrime offences as alleged LabHost users; 3 additional Melbourne men charged with drug-related offences during the warrants.
- **Australian search warrants:** 22 executed on 2024-04-17 — 14 Victoria, 2 Queensland, 3 NSW, 1 South Australia, 2 Western Australia. >200 AFP/state/territory officers involved.
- **Servers taken down by JPC3 (Australian operation):** 207 criminal servers hosting LabHost phishing sites.
- **Australian suspect identification:** more than 100 suspects identified in Australia using LabHost to target Australian victims.
- **Australian victim count:** more than 94,000 Australians targeted via LabHost-generated phishing.
- **Australian harm estimate (quote):** "LabHost alone had the potential to cause $28 million in harm to the Australians through the sale of stolen Australian credentials" — Acting Assistant Commissioner Cyber Command Chris Goldsmid.
- **National context (Scamwatch):** "Scamwatch last year receiving more than 108,000 reports of phishing attacks, totaling nearly $26 million in losses" — Goldsmid.
- **Global figures cited by AFP:** Europol-coordinated, 70 simultaneous search warrants in multiple countries; 37 arrests globally including 4 UK-based individuals linked to the running of the site, including the original developer of the platform.
- **LabHost scale at takedown:** more than 40,000 phishing domains; more than 10,000 active global cybercriminal users.
- **LabHost subscription model:** as little as $270 per month for phishing kits + infrastructure to host phishing websites + email/text content generation + campaign overview services.
- **LabHost origin:** "originated in Canada in 2021, targeting North America, and expanded to the United Kingdom (UK) and Ireland, before going global. Australian criminals are believed to be among its top three user countries."
- **Phishing technique described:** more than 170 fraudulent websites of reputable banks, government entities and other major organisations replicated as a "one-stop-shop" for phishing.

## Named Quotes (verbatim)

- AFP Acting Assistant Commissioner Cyber Command **Chris Goldsmid**: "Phishing had become a serious threat, with Scamwatch last year receiving more than 108,000 reports of phishing attacks, totaling nearly $26 million in losses."
- Goldsmid: "LabHost alone had the potential to cause $28 million in harm to the Australians through the sale of stolen Australian credentials."
- Goldsmid: "In addition to financial losses, victims experience ongoing security risks including identity takeovers, extortion and blackmail. LabHost exemplifies cybercrime's borderless nature. Australians using LabHost should not expect anonymity—we've gathered extensive evidence."
- Victoria Police Detective Superintendent **Tim McKinney**: "Cybercrimes like phishing may be virtual, but their impact on victims is real and devastating. … If you commit cybercrime targeting everyday Australians, know we'll pursue you globally."
- Queensland Police Service Detective Superintendent **Craig McGrath**: "As cybercrimes rise, community impact is undeniable. We must work together."
- NSW Police Force State Crime Command's Cybercrime Squad Commander, Acting Detective Superintendent **Gillian Lister**: "Cybercrime is borderless. We target offenders across jurisdictions to destroy networks and prevent victimization."
- WA Police Force Detective Superintendent **Peter Foley**: "Western Australia isn't safe for cybercriminals. We continue pursuing offenders with law enforcement partners."

This release is tier-1 (national LE own-domain afp.gov.au, jointly issued with four state police forces) and provides the Australian-side enumeration for the international LabHost takedown coordinated by Europol J-CAT.
