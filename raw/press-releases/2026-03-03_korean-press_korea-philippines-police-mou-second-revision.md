# 한국-필리핀 경찰 MOU 2차 개정 (2026-03-03)

**Source: 경찰청 announcement on 2026-03-04 (Korea.kr 정책브리핑 newsId=156747062). Korea.kr is connection-reset on TLS-spoof and WebFetch; verbatim body captured from 시사경찰일보 (police112.net) reproduction of the same press conference content.**

## Signing Event

- **Signing date**: 2026-03-03 (local time, Manila, Philippines)
- **Korean signer**: 유재성 경찰청장 직무대행 (Yoo Jae-sung, Acting KNPA Commissioner)
- **Philippine signer**: Jose Melencio Nartatez Jr., Philippine National Police Chief
- **Agreement**: Second revision of the Korea–Philippines police "경찰협력 양해각서" since the original 2007 signing.

## Cooperation Scope (verbatim from announcement)

The revised MOU strengthens joint response to "마약·온라인 스캠 등 지능화된 초국가범죄" (drugs, online scams, and sophisticated transnational crimes), expanding cooperation beyond traditional investigative cooperation. The bilateral agreement specifically commits to:

- Strengthening investigative information sharing.
- Expanding cooperation for rapid apprehension and repatriation of overseas Korean fugitives.
- Joint response to drugs, online scam, and other transnational crime patterns.

## Korean Desk (코리안 데스크) Expansion

- **Original Korean Desk**: established at the Philippine National Capital Region Police Office in 2012, targeting prevention of violent crimes against Korean nationals.
- **2026 expansion**: KNPA will deploy one additional liaison officer (경찰협력관) to the Philippine National Capital Region Police Office during the first half of 2026.

## Related Multilateral Mechanisms (named in the announcement)

- **IICA** — International Cooperation Consultative Body led by Korean police with Philippine participation.
- **ANCRA** — Asia Narcotic Crime Response Cooperation.
- **ICON** — International Drug Investigation Conference, scheduled for Seoul in 2026.

## Additional Bilateral Discussions

The KNPA Acting Commissioner also held discussions with:

- **Joel Antonio Viado**, Philippine Immigration Commissioner, on extradition procedures for Korean fugitives.
- **Benjamin Acorda Jr.**, Philippine organized crime response committee chair.
