## Summary

Between 27 and 29 May 2024, **Operation Endgame** — the largest-ever international action against botnets — targeted dropper malware families including IcedID, SystemBC, Pikabot, Smokeloader, Bumblebee, and Trickbot. Coordinated from Europol's headquarters with a command post of 20+ officers from Denmark, France, Germany, and the US, the operation resulted in 4 arrests, over 100 servers taken down across 10 countries, and over 2,000 domains seized. These malware droppers serve as initial-access vectors linked to at least 15 ransomware groups, including BlackBasta, REvil, and Conti. One key suspect accumulated at least EUR 69 million in cryptocurrency from renting criminal infrastructure for ransomware deployment.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation Endgame
- **Dates:** 27-29 May 2024
- **Coordination:** Europol headquarters (command post with 20+ officers)
- **Participating countries:** Denmark, France, Germany, Netherlands, United Kingdom, United States
- **Supporting countries:** Armenia, Bulgaria, Lithuania, Portugal, Romania, Switzerland, Ukraine
- **Arrests:** 4 (1 in Armenia, 3 in Ukraine)
- **Location searches:** 16
- **Servers taken down:** 100+ (across 10 countries)
- **Domains seized:** 2,000+
- **Cryptocurrency (key suspect):** At least EUR 69 million accumulated
- **Malware families targeted:** IcedID, SystemBC, Pikabot, Smokeloader, Bumblebee, Trickbot
- **Linked ransomware groups:** 15+ (including BlackBasta, REvil, Conti)
- **Crime type:** Botnet/dropper malware infrastructure enabling ransomware
- **Enforcement actions:** Arrests, server takedowns, domain seizures, cryptocurrency monitoring
