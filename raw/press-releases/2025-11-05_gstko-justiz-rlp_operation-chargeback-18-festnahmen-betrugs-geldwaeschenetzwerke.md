# Operation Chargeback — 18 Festnahmen bei international koordiniertem Vorgehen gegen mutmaßliche Betrugs- und Geldwäschenetzwerke; Verdacht des systematischen Missbrauchs von Zahlungsdienstleistern

**Publisher**: Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Koblenz — Landeszentralstelle Cybercrime (LZC) / Rheinland-Pfalz Ministry of Justice
**Action day**: 4 November 2025
**Publish date**: 5 November 2025
**Source URL**: https://gstko.justiz.rlp.de/presse-aktuelles/detail-1/operation-chargeback-18-festnahmen-bei-international-koordiniertem-vorgehen-gegen-mutmassliche-betrugs-und-geldwaeschenetzwerke-verdacht-des-systematischen-missbrauchs-von-zahlungsdienstleistern

## Key facts (extracted)

- **Codename**: Operation Chargeback
- **Lead authorities**: Landeszentralstelle Cybercrime (LZC) of the Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Koblenz and the Bundeskriminalamt (BKA)
- **Supporting German authorities**: Financial Intelligence Unit Deutschland (FIU, at Zoll/Generalzolldirektion), Bundesanstalt für Finanzdienstleistungsaufsicht (BaFin), various Landeskriminalämter and prosecutors in Baden-Württemberg, Bavaria, Berlin, Hesse, Rhineland-Palatinate, Saxony, Hamburg and Schleswig-Holstein
- **Cooperating foreign jurisdictions (verbatim from Koblenz release)**: "die enge Zusammenarbeit mit den Behörden in Großbritannien, Italien, Kanada, Luxemburg, den Niederlanden, Singapur, Spanien, den Vereinigten Staaten und Zypern" — i.e. United Kingdom, Italy, Canada, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Singapore, Spain, United States and Cyprus
- **Multilateral coordinators**: Eurojust and Europol provided coordination support
- **Arrests / warrants**: 18 arrest warrants executed on the action day, 5 of them in Germany; 44 suspects total under investigation (36 men, 8 women, aged 32–70)
- **Searches**: more than 60 search locations worldwide; 29 in Germany (across 8 federal states)
- **Assets seized**: approximately EUR 35 million in Germany alone (frozen accounts, real estate, vehicles, luxury goods)
- **Charges (Germany)**: gang-/commercially-organised computer fraud (gewerbs- und bandenmäßiger Computerbetrug), money laundering, misuse of payment service providers
- **Modus operandi**: the three criminal networks allegedly compromised German payment-service-provider systems with malicious software and abused stolen credit-card data from approximately 4.3 million cardholders across 193 countries (period 2016–2021). They allegedly created more than 19 million fake subscriptions through professionally-built fake websites (streaming, dating, entertainment) used solely to charge small recurring fees to victims' cards; total estimated damage exceeds EUR 300 million.

## Cooperation structure (per Koblenz / BKA / Europol releases)

The investigation has been led since 2020 by the LZC at Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Koblenz with BKA. Eurojust hosted multiple coordination meetings between the German, Italian, Canadian, Luxembourgish, Dutch, Singaporean, Spanish, US and Cypriot judicial authorities; Europol coordinated the law-enforcement operational picture via EC3. Foreign LE agencies in the named nine partner countries executed parallel searches and arrests in their jurisdictions on the joint action day (4 November 2025). FIU Deutschland and BaFin contributed financial-intelligence and supervisory data on the misuse of regulated payment service providers.
