# U.S.-ROK-Japan Joint Statement on DPRK Information Technology Workers

**Issuer**: U.S. Department of State, Office of the Spokesperson — joint statement co-issued with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Korea.

**Press release date**: August 27, 2025.

## Verbatim joint statement text (key passages, as reproduced by State Department, MOFA Japan, METI Japan and mirror at globalsecurity.org)

> The United States, Japan, and the Republic of Korea (ROK) remain united in our efforts to counter the threat posed by North Korean information technology (IT) workers. North Korea continues to dispatch its IT workers around the world to generate revenue, which funds its unlawful weapons of mass destruction (WMD) and ballistic missile programs, in violation of UN Security Council resolutions (UNSCRs).

> The United States, Japan, and the ROK express serious concerns over the evolving malicious activities of North Korean IT workers. North Korean IT workers use a variety of techniques to disguise themselves as non-North Korean IT workers with false identities and locations, including by leveraging AI tools as well as cooperating with foreign facilitators.

> North Korean IT workers themselves are also highly likely to be involved in malicious cyber activities, particularly in the blockchain industry. Hiring, supporting, or outsourcing work to North Korean IT workers increasingly poses serious risks, ranging from theft of intellectual property, data, and funds to reputational harm and legal consequences.

> On August 26, the three countries hosted an event in Tokyo in partnership with Mandiant to improve public-private partnership and support international industry collaboration in the fight against North Korean exploitation.

> The United States, Japan, and the Republic of Korea reaffirm their commitment to enhancing their coordination among the three countries, and deepening collaboration between the public and private sector to counter malicious cyber activities and illicit revenue generation by North Korea.

## Concurrent enforcement action

The same August 27, 2025 trilateral framework was accompanied by U.S. Treasury OFAC sanctions designations against four entities and individuals in **Russia, Laos, and China** for serving as DPRK IT-worker facilitators (see Treasury press release sb0230 of the same week).

## Mandate framing

The statement positions DPRK IT-worker dispatch as a violation of UN Security Council Resolutions implementing the DPRK sanctions regime (1718, 2087, 2094, 2270, 2321, 2356, 2371, 2375, 2397). The three governments acknowledge that the 2024 disbandment of the UN 1718 Sanctions Committee Panel of Experts (following Russia's March 2024 veto) created a monitoring gap that the trilateral framework, in conjunction with the Multilateral Sanctions Monitoring Team (MSMT) established October 2024, partially fills.

## Crime types implicated

- Sanctions evasion via fraudulent foreign IT employment (money-laundering-ic)
- Identity fraud / impersonation by DPRK workers using stolen or AI-generated identities (online-fraud-ic)
- Subsequent malicious cyber activity by DPRK IT workers, particularly cryptocurrency theft in blockchain/Web3 industry (hacking-ic)
