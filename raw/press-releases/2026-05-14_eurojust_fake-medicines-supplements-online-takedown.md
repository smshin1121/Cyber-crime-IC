### Key Findings

- Action day on 2026-05-12 saw 113 locations searched across six countries, with main suspects detained in Poland, Romania and Moldova.
- The criminal network, active since 2019, operated hundreds of fraudulent websites and social-media pages selling fake medicines and supplements to seriously ill victims, generating an estimated EUR 240 million in illicit transactions.
- Romania blocked 196 websites; Poland froze EUR 1.8 million in assets; large quantities of fake supplements were seized in Hungary, Romania and Bulgaria.
- Eurojust hosted ten coordination meetings and a JIT; Europol deployed a Virtual Command Post and on-the-spot experts.
- Five lead jurisdictions explicitly cooperated through named LE and prosecutor agencies: Romania, Bulgaria, Hungary, Poland, and Moldova; ten further EU jurisdictions assisted via European Investigation Orders.
