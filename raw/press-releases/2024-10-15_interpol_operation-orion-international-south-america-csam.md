# Operation Orion International — South America CSAM Crackdown

## Summary

INTERPOL coordinated "Operation Orion International," a region-wide initiative across 12 South American countries targeting the transnational distribution of child sexual abuse material (CSAM) on instant messaging applications. The operation ran from May to September 2024 and was launched following information from Chile's cybercrime unit and the Buenos Aires prosecutor's office.

## Key Facts

- **Operation name:** Orion International
- **Period:** May – September 2024
- **Coordinating body:** INTERPOL
- **Participating countries (12):** Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Ecuador, Guyana, Paraguay, Peru, Suriname, Uruguay, Venezuela
- **Supporting organizations:** United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC)

## Enforcement Results

- **Total arrests:** 144
- **Children rescued/safeguarded:** 20
- **Direct abusers arrested:** 18
- **Charged with possession/distribution:** 111
- **Red Notice subjects arrested:** 7

### Country Highlights
- **Argentina:** Identified and safeguarded a 9-year-old victim after at least 6 years of abuse; hardware seizures opened new investigation avenues
- **Chile:** Arrested offender producing abuse material; safeguarded a 14-year-old girl
- **Ecuador:** Apprehended website manager through Korea-Ecuador collaboration

## Arrest Demographics

Offenders aged 14-86, predominantly male. Professions included: professor, graphic designer, security guard, mechanic, musician, journalist, psychologist, and taxi driver.

## Seizures

Desktop computers, laptops, mobile phones, tablets, and digital storage devices recovered during searches across all 12 countries.
