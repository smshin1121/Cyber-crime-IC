# Operation Stream — Kidflix CSAM Platform Takedown

## Summary

An international law enforcement operation named "Operation Stream" dismantled Kidflix, one of the largest known child sexual abuse material (CSAM) streaming platforms on the dark web. The operation was led by the Bavarian State Criminal Police (Bayerisches Landeskriminalamt) and the Bavarian Central Office for the Prosecution of Cybercrime (ZCB), with coordination by Europol across 35 countries.

## Key Facts

- **Platform:** Kidflix — CSAM streaming platform operational since 2021
- **Investigation period:** 2022 to March 11, 2025
- **Server seizure date:** March 11, 2025 (servers in Germany and Netherlands)
- **Takedown announced:** April 4, 2025
- **Participating countries:** 35+
- **Lead agencies:** Bavarian State Criminal Police (BKA Bavaria), ZCB, Europol
- **Registered users:** 1.8 million (April 2022 – March 2025)
- **Unique CSAM videos hosted:** 91,000
- **Videos on server at seizure:** ~72,000
- **Total content duration:** 6,288 hours
- **Upload rate:** 3.5 new videos per hour

## Enforcement Results

- **Suspects identified:** 1,393 worldwide
- **Arrests:** 79
- **Electronic devices seized:** 3,000+
- **Children identified and protected:** 39

## Financial Methods

The platform accepted cryptocurrency through multiple channels: international exchanges, decentralized finance (DeFi) platforms, Bitcoin ATMs, peer-to-peer exchanges, and US-based centralized exchanges. Administrators routed funds through international DeFi platforms.

## Platform Features

Kidflix operated differently from typical CSAM sites by enabling streaming rather than downloads. It used a cryptocurrency-based payment and token-earning system for uploaders who categorized content.
