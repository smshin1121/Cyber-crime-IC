## Source 1 — Rigspolitiet / NSK (Denmark, primary investigative authority)

**Date:** 28 February 2025, 10:49 CET
**Headline:** "Danskledet international politiindsats mod AI-genereret overgrebsmateriale"
**Issuing unit:** National enhed for Særlig Kriminalitet (NSK) — National Special Crime Unit / National Cyber Crime Center (NC3)
**URL:** https://politi.dk/national-enhed-for-saerlig-kriminalitet/nyhedsliste/danskledet-international-politiindsats/2025/02/28

### Operational summary (translated, original Danish preserved in legal-term quotes)

In an NSK-led operation, international police authorities have struck against
buyers of AI-generated sexual abuse material. Twenty-five persons have been
arrested worldwide.

The international operation is an offshoot of a Danish case run by Østjyllands
Politi (East Jutland Police). In that case, a 29-year-old man was convicted in
January 2025 by Retten i Randers (Randers District Court) for having produced
and sold an extensive number of AI-generated sexual images of children to buyers
worldwide.

The international investigation was initiated and led by NSK and is directed at
the buyers of the 29-year-old Danish man's images. The operation was coordinated
in Europol's **Joint Cybercrime Action Task Force (J-CAT)**.

### Key figures (NSK release)

- **273** buyers of the illegal images identified.
- **25** persons arrested worldwide as part of the operation.
- **2** Danish customers among those charged — two men aged 30 and 32 — charged
  with having purchased the 29-year-old's images. Cases ongoing, investigated
  by Nordsjællands Politi (North Zealand Police).
- The 29-year-old Danish producer was convicted by Retten i Randers; sentence
  one year three months' imprisonment, three months unconditional. The defence
  has appealed for acquittal; the prosecution at Østjyllands Politi has
  cross-appealed seeking a stiffer sentence. Pending date for hearing at the
  Court of Appeal (Landsretten).

### Quoted statement — NSK police commissioner Sasha Nellemann Nielsen

> "Operationen markerer en fælles international indsats mod AI-genereret
> overgrebsmateriale, som vi desværre ser mere og mere af. Ikke alene har vi
> bremset administratoren, der har stillet billederne til rådighed. Vi har også
> markeret over for købere i hele verden, at det ikke tolereres, at man besidder
> og distribuerer krænkende seksuelt materiale af børn — også selvom der ikke
> er tale om rigtige personer på billederne."

> Working translation: "The operation marks a joint international effort against
> AI-generated abuse material, which we unfortunately see more and more of. Not
> only have we stopped the administrator who made the images available. We have
> also signalled to buyers around the world that possessing and distributing
> abusive sexual material of children is not tolerated — even when the persons
> in the images are not real."

## Source 2 — Europol (coordinating supranational body)

**Date:** 28 February 2025
**Headline:** "25 arrested in global hit against AI-generated child sexual abuse material"
**URL:** https://www.europol.europa.eu/media-press/newsroom/news/25-arrested-in-global-hit-against-ai-generated-child-sexual-abuse-material

### Operation name and lead

The Europol release confirms the operation codename **Operation Cumberland**,
**led by Danish law enforcement**, and supported by authorities from
**19 countries** in a "large-scale hit against child sexual exploitation that
has led to 25 arrests worldwide."

> "Most of the arrests were carried out simultaneously on **26 February 2025**
> during a global operation (Operation Cumberland) led by Danish law enforcement.
> More arrests are expected in the coming weeks, as the operation is still
> ongoing."

### Headline figures (Europol release, "results so far")

- **273** suspects identified
- **25** suspects arrested
- **33** house searches
- **173** electronic devices seized

### Coordination role

> "The Joint Cybercrime Action Task Force (J-CAT), hosted by Europol, provided
> operational coordination to the law enforcement agencies involved in the case.
> Experts from Europol's European Cybercrime Centre also facilitated information
> exchanges and provided intelligence and operational analysis to national
> investigators."

### Significance flagged by Europol

> "Operation Cumberland has been one of the first cases involving AI-generated
> child sexual abuse material (CSAM), making it exceptionally challenging for
> investigators, especially due to the lack of national legislation addressing
> these crimes."

### Background — main suspect

> "The main suspect, a Danish national who was arrested in November 2024, ran
> an online platform where he distributed the AI-generated material he produced.
> Following a symbolic online payment, users from around the world were able to
> obtain a password to access the platform and watch children being abused."
