## Summary-Only Archive

DOJ announced the coordinated international disruption of Genesis Market, a marketplace selling stolen account credentials, cookies, and device fingerprints from compromised computers. The release identifies the action as Operation Cookie Monster and states that authorities seized 11 domains supporting Genesis Market infrastructure under an Eastern District of Wisconsin warrant.

The release reports that Genesis Market offered data from more than 1.5 million compromised computers and more than 80 million account credentials. It lists international partners including the UK National Crime Agency, Dutch National Police, Europol, Eurojust, and many national law enforcement agencies. DOJ also records mutual legal assistance from Bulgaria and Latvia.
