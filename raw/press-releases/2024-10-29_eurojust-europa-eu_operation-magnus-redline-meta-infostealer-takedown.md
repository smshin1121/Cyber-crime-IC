## Press Release — Verbatim Extract (Eurojust, 29 October 2024)

**Title**: Malware targeting millions of people taken down by international coalition
**Date**: 29 October 2024
**Publisher**: Eurojust

### Summary

A global law enforcement operation, supported by Eurojust, has led to the takedown of the RedLine and META infostealers. The takedown took place on 28 October 2024 during a worldwide operation. The infostealers RedLine and META were able to steal personal data from infected devices, including saved usernames and passwords, and automatically saved form data such as addresses, email addresses, phone numbers, cryptocurrency wallets, and cookies. Millions of people worldwide were victims of the malware.

### Operation name

The operation is publicly branded **Operation Magnus**, with a victim-check resource at https://www.operation-magnus.com/.

### Investigation history

Investigations into RedLine and META started after victims came forward and a security company notified authorities about possible servers in the Netherlands linked to the software. Authorities discovered that over 1,200 servers in dozens of countries were running the malware.

### Cooperation and coordination

To take down the transnational malware, Eurojust coordinated cooperation between authorities from:

- the Netherlands
- the United States
- Belgium
- Portugal
- the United Kingdom
- Australia

### Operational results (28 October 2024)

- Three (3) servers shut down in the Netherlands.
- Two (2) domains seized.
- Two (2) people taken into custody in Belgium.
- Charges unsealed in the United States (against Maxim Rudometov, identified as one of the developers and administrators of RedLine Infostealer; charges include access device fraud, conspiracy to commit computer intrusion, and money laundering — per companion DOJ statement).
- A client database from RedLine and META platforms was retrieved by investigators.

### Companion sources

The operation is mirrored on the police-and-Eurojust public site `operation-magnus.com`, which lists the same six participating jurisdictions plus the agency seals (Netherlands National Police, FBI, US DOJ, NCIS, IRS, DCIS, UK NCA, Australian Federal Police, Polícia Judiciária, Belgian Federal Police, Eurojust). ESET provided a free RedLine/META scanner for victims (referenced via operation-magnus.com).

### Stolen-data scope

Stolen by RedLine and META included: saved usernames and passwords; automatically saved form data (addresses, email addresses, phone numbers); cryptocurrency wallets; cookies. Eurojust frames the scale as "millions" of victims worldwide.
