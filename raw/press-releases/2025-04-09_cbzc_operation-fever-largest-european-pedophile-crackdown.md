# Operation FEVER — Largest European CSAM Crackdown Organised by Polish CBZC

## Original title (Polish)

> Największa operacja w Europie uderzająca w przestępczość o charakterze pedofilskim. Polska Policja włączyła do działań 11 państw!
> ("Largest operation in Europe targeting paedophile crime. The Polish Police involved 11 countries in the action!")

**Publisher:** Centralne Biuro Zwalczania Cyberprzestępczości (CBZC) — Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime, Polish Police
**Date of publication:** 2025-04-09
**Language:** Polish (pl)

## Summary (English translation)

While Poland was holding the Presidency of the Council of the European Union, the **Centralne Biuro Zwalczania Cyberprzestępczości (CBZC)**, in cooperation with the **Komenda Główna Policji (KGP — National Police Headquarters)**, organised the international operation **"FEVER"** against producers, distributors and possessors of child sexual abuse material (CSAM). The press release states that 166 suspects were detained in total, **including 98 in Poland**.

This is described as the **largest operation that the CBZC has organised together with KGP to date**.

## Key facts

- **Operation name:** FEVER (operacja "FEVER")
- **Lead organiser:** Centralne Biuro Zwalczania Cyberprzestępczości (CBZC), Polish Police
- **Co-organiser:** Komenda Główna Policji (KGP — National Police Headquarters), with the Department against Trafficking in Human Beings of the Criminal Bureau of KGP
- **International support:** Europol; J-CAT (Joint Cybercrime Action Taskforce, hosted at Europol)
- **Polish prosecutorial participation:** Departament do Spraw Cyberprzestępczości i Informatyzacji Prokuratury Krajowej (Department for Cybercrime and Informatisation of the National Public Prosecutor's Office) and prosecutors from across Poland
- **Period framing:** Action conducted while Poland held the EU Council Presidency (1 January – 30 June 2025); press release published 9 April 2025
- **Total suspects detained:** **166** (across all participating jurisdictions)
- **Detained in Poland:** **98**, aged 22 to 78
- **Suspects arrested for sharing/distributing CSAM (per release):** 111
- **Pre-trial detentions ordered by Polish courts:** 48
- **Searches in Poland:** 159
- **Polish officers deployed:** nearly 600 (CBZC + Central Police Bureau against Trafficking in Human Beings + Capital Police Headquarters KSP + voivodeship headquarters in Gdańsk, Katowice, Kraków, Lublin, Olsztyn, Poznań, Radom, Rzeszów, Wrocław, and Gorzów Wielkopolski)
- **Devices/data carriers seized in Poland:** more than **1,700** (computers, hard drives, phones, SIM cards)
- **CSAM files seized in Poland:** more than **520,000 video files and photographs**
- **Other seizures:** narcotics; **3 firearms** held without permit
- **Maximum statutory penalty cited (Poland):** up to 15 years' imprisonment for public presentation of pornographic content

## Sub-operations highlighted in the release

### "Kidflix" component (CBZC + German police)

The CBZC release explicitly identifies **"Kidflix"** as one element of FEVER, executed jointly with German police. Per the release:

- The platform was one of the world's largest paedophile streaming platforms.
- From April 2022 through March (2025), approximately **1.8 million users worldwide** logged onto the platform.
- On **11 March 2025**, German and Dutch authorities seized the server, which at the time held approximately **72,000 videos**.
- CBZC reached **12 individuals** in Poland who actively used the service; some were charged with possession and distribution of CSAM.
- The release notes that Europol published its own communication on the matter.

### FBI-cooperation component

The release also describes a parallel CBZC–FBI track that targeted members of "one of the largest international forums in hidden Internet resources" exchanging CSAM. Several individuals were charged with possession and distribution of illegal files.

## Earlier Polish CBZC operations referenced

The release lists five earlier domestic operations conducted by CBZC in 2022–2024 ("ANASTAZJA", "BARBOSSA", "CARLOS", "DAKOTA", "ENOLA GAY"). Across those five operations:

- **More than 280 persons** charged, aged 16 to 78
- **More than 2 million CSAM files** secured

## Cooperation mechanisms identified in the release

- **Europol coordination platform** (umbrella support to FEVER as a whole)
- **J-CAT (Joint Cybercrime Action Taskforce)** at Europol — explicitly named
- **Bilateral CBZC–German police cooperation** for the "Kidflix" sub-operation
- **Bilateral CBZC–FBI cooperation** for the dark web forum sub-operation
- **NCMEC information sharing** on US side
