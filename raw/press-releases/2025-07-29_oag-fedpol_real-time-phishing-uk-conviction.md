# Cybercrime: International investigations by the OAG and fedpol result in conviction for real-time phishing in the UK

**Press release — Office of the Attorney General of Switzerland (OAG), in cooperation with the Federal Office of Police (fedpol).**
**Published: 29 July 2025.**

## Summary of the case

Joint criminal investigations by the Office of the Attorney General of Switzerland (OAG) and the Federal Office of Police (fedpol) resulted in the identification, arrest in England, and prosecution in the United Kingdom of a British national who developed and distributed a phishing kit used to defraud Swiss bank customers in a "real-time phishing" scheme. On **23 July 2025**, the perpetrator was sentenced by a court in the United Kingdom to **seven years' imprisonment**.

The OAG had been conducting criminal proceedings since **July 2022** in connection with a large-scale series of phishing attacks against Swiss bank customers. Approximately 30 fraud cases were transferred to the OAG by Swiss cantons and consolidated. Total losses to Swiss bank customers were approximately **CHF 2.4 million** (approximately USD 2.98 million).

## Modus operandi — "real-time phishing"

According to the OAG and the fedpol annual-report account of the case, the criminal network used the following multi-stage attack:

1. Registered domain names that closely imitated the genuine domains of Swiss banks.
2. Promoted the fake domains via paid Google Search advertisements so that fraudulent e-banking login pages appeared at the top of search results.
3. Captured online-banking access credentials in real time when victims attempted to log in.
4. Bypassed two-factor authentication by inducing victims to enter the SMS one-time code on the phishing page; the codes were transmitted to the fraudsters in real time and used to authenticate sessions and register additional devices to the victim's account.
5. Transferred stolen funds via money mules and cryptocurrency exchanges.

The British national identified by the OAG/fedpol investigations was the **developer and distributor of the phishing kit** that was deployed against Swiss bank customers. He was approximately 21 years old at sentencing and had been a student in England.

## Legal framework and charges (Switzerland)

Swiss proceedings were opened under Article 147 of the Swiss Criminal Code (computer fraud / *betrügerischer Missbrauch einer Datenverarbeitungsanlage*). After the British national was identified and added to the proceedings in **August 2023**, and after coordination with United Kingdom authorities, the OAG transferred / discontinued its proceedings in favour of UK prosecution, on the basis that parallel criminal proceedings were already underway in the United Kingdom against the same individual.

## Investigation timeline

- **May–October 2022:** Phishing campaign actively targeted Swiss bank customers.
- **July 2022:** OAG opened criminal proceedings.
- **August 2023:** Developer/distributor of the phishing kit identified through joint OAG/fedpol investigation; added to proceedings.
- **26 October 2023:** Suspect arrested in England (per fedpol annual report 2024 entry on the case).
- **23 July 2025:** UK court sentences perpetrator to seven years' imprisonment.
- **29 July 2025:** OAG publishes press release announcing UK conviction.

## International cooperation mechanisms

The case is described by Swiss authorities as a joint investigation conducted with international partners. Cooperation involved:

- **Bilateral coordination between the OAG and UK prosecution authorities** to consolidate proceedings and transfer prosecution to the United Kingdom.
- **fedpol** (Swiss federal police) as the investigative police authority on the Swiss side.
- **Europol** support (per the fedpol annual-report account of the case as part of an "international network" investigation).
- **Eurojust** coordination support (per the fedpol annual-report account).
- Approximately 30 cantonal Swiss fraud cases consolidated into a single OAG proceeding before the file was transferred to the UK side.

## Authorities, persons, and entities named

- **Office of the Attorney General of Switzerland (OAG / Bundesanwaltschaft / Ministère public de la Confédération / Ministero pubblico della Confederazione)** — proceeding authority.
- **Federal Office of Police (fedpol)** — investigative authority.
- **United Kingdom courts and prosecution service** — prosecution and sentencing.
- **Europol; Eurojust** — supporting EU cooperation channels (per fedpol report account).
- **Swiss cantons** — original 30 fraud-case complainants transferred to OAG.
- **British national, approximately 21 years old, an English student** — defendant; name not disclosed in OAG release. Convicted of computer/banking-related fraud in the UK on 23 July 2025; sentenced to seven years' imprisonment.
