## New 2026 ‘IOCTA’ highlights sophisticated tactics and emerging challenges in the digital landscape

Europol today published the new edition of the Internet Organised Crime Threat Assessment (IOCTA) 2026, providing an in-depth analysis of the latest trends and developments in cybercrime affecting the European Union. The report, titled "How encryption, proxies, and AI are expanding cybercrime," underscores the accelerating pace and sophistication of cyber threats, emphasising the need for enhanced law enforcement capabilities and international cooperation.
