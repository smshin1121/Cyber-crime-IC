## Source Archive Record

This record preserves the source metadata and source-page digest for the BKA/ZIT joint press release announcing **Operation Final Exchange**, the 19 September 2024 takedown of 47 Germany-hosted no-KYC cryptocurrency exchange services catering to a Russian-language clientele. The full BKA primary text was reachable on 2026-05-09 at the canonical BKA press-release URL above (note: the live URL contains the `_PM_` segment which earlier internal references omitted). Wayback Machine had no archived snapshot of the BKA URL at the time of ingest, but the BKA primary text was directly readable; corroboration was also obtained from the official Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Frankfurt am Main / BKA presseportal mirror, the BKA SharedDocs Kurzmeldungen mirror, Heise Online (en), Chainalysis, AMLP Forum, Infosecurity Magazine, Duane Morris ESE blog, and The Record (Recorded Future).

## Key Statements (paraphrased / partial-quote, copyright-respecting)

- The Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Frankfurt am Main – ZIT and the BKA jointly shut down 47 Germany-hosted exchange services that converted conventional currencies and cryptocurrencies and were used for criminal purposes.
- The operators are accused of obscuring the origin of criminally obtained funds through deficient implementation of statutory anti-money-laundering requirements (Know-Your-Customer principle) on a large scale, thereby committing money laundering and operating criminal trading platforms on the internet pursuant to **§§ 127, 261 Abs. 1 Satz 1 Nr. 2 und Abs. 4 StGB** (German Criminal Code).
- The release describes the platforms' offering as designed to allow exchange transactions "schnell, einfach und anonym" — quickly, simply, and anonymously — without registration process and without verification of identity documents.
- Users of the platforms are stated to have included **ransomware groups, darknet vendors, and botnet operators** who used the services to convert criminal proceeds into ordinary currency cycles.
- During the action, the BKA secured extensive user and transaction data from the shut-down exchanges, described as valuable investigative leads in combating cybercrime.
- The release situates the operation in a chronology of BKA infrastructure-takedown successes against the cybercrime underground economy alongside ChipMixer (≈ EUR 90 million, 2023), Qakbot, Emotet, and Operation Endgame.

## Statutory Citation Verbatim

> §§ 127, 261 Abs. 1 Satz 1 Nr. 2 und Abs. 4 StGB

- § 127 StGB — Operating criminal trading platforms on the internet (Betreiben krimineller Handelsplattformen im Internet)
- § 261 Abs. 1 Satz 1 Nr. 2 und Abs. 4 StGB — Money laundering (Geldwäsche), aggravated form

## Splash Banner Text on Seized Sites

The seized exchange domains were redirected to a takedown notice with the message (translated): "We have found their servers and seized them … We have their data — and therefore we have your data. Transactions, registration data, IP addresses." The splash page was hosted at finalexchange.de and reachable also via www.bka.de/finalexchange.

## Notable Statistics (from corroborating tier-2 sources, not the BKA verbatim text)

- Xchange.cash: ~410,000 user accounts, ~1.28–1.3 million transactions processed (per The Record / Chainalysis)
- Top three by transaction volume: Xchange.cash, 60cek.org, Bankcomat.com (per Chainalysis)
- 17 of the 47 exchanges had months in which >50% of direct on-chain inflows came from illicit sources (Chainalysis on-chain analysis)
- 12 of the 47 had months with >30% of inflows from darknet marketplaces (Chainalysis)
- 6 of the 47 had months in which stolen funds exceeded 30% of inflows (Chainalysis)

## Cooperation Pattern

- Joint German action: BKA (Bundeskriminalamt) + Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Frankfurt am Main / ZIT (Zentralstelle zur Bekämpfung der Internetkriminalität).
- The release does not announce a Europol/Eurojust co-coordination role for this action.
- Targets were Germany-hosted infrastructure operated for a primarily Russian-speaking clientele.
- No arrests were announced in the BKA release dated 2024-09-19 (subsequent BKA press releases — e.g., 31 October 2024 — announce later arrests in Hesse and Rhineland-Palatinate; the 2024-09-19 release covers infrastructure seizure only).
