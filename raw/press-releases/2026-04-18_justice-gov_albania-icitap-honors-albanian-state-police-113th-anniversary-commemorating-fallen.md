Albania: ICITAP Honors Albanian State Police 113th Anniversary, Commemorating Fallen Officers, Security Academy Achievements, and Strengthened U.S.-Albania Law Enforcement Partnership

Blog Post

Tuesday, January 13, 2026

On January 13, ICITAP Albania joined the U.S. Embassy Tirana and international law enforcement partners in recognizing the 113th Anniversary of the Albanian State Police (ASP), commemorating over a century of dedicated service to public safety and order in Albania. ICITAP participated in ceremonial events honoring the sacrifices of fallen officers, including the annual wreath-laying ceremony at the Security Academy’s memorial for the Martyrs of the State Police, where ASP leadership emphasized the enduring commitment to protecting life, property, and national symbols. Following the ceremony, a celebratory event attended by the Deputy Minister of Interior, police leadership, and international law enforcement attaches highlighted ASP achievements in 2025, including ICITAP-supported advancements such as the Security Academy achieving IADLEST accreditation and receiving the IADLEST “Excellence” Award, reflecting elevated training standards, professional development, and international recognition. In his remarks, the Rector of the Security Academy emphasized that 2025 marked a year of institutional growth, with the Academy graduating 800 police officers and crime specialists, modernizing application processes for greater transparency, and expanding programs to strengthen investigative, leadership, and ethical capacities across the ASP. He highlighted ICITAP’s critical role in helping the Academy integrate U.S. best practices, develop curricula on ethics and crime investigation, and establish partnerships with regional and international police institutions, noting that “challenge is our opportunity, change is our mission, and excellence – as proven in 2025 – is our standard.” Since 1997, ICITAP has partnered with the ASP to provide foundational and advanced training, mentoring, and operational support, strengthening ethical conduct, investigative capacity, and interagency cooperation across Albania’s justice institutions. By enhancing ASP capabilities, ICITAP directly contributes to countering organized crime, narcotics trafficking, and transnational criminal networks that threaten U.S. national security and the safety of American citizens and interests abroad. ICITAP’s collaboration with the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL) and the U.S. Embassy ensures sustained, strategic capacity building, reinforcing regional security and enabling Albania’s law enforcement to operate in alignment with international standards.
