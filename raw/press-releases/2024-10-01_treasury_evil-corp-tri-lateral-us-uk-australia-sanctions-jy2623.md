# Treasury Sanctions Members of the Russia-Based Cybercriminal Group Evil Corp in Tri-Lateral Action with the United Kingdom and Australia

**Press Release Number:** JY-2623
**Date:** October 1, 2024
**Issuer:** U.S. Department of the Treasury — Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)
**Coordinated with:** United Kingdom Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO); Australia Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT)

## Subtitle

The United States takes additional action against the Russia-based cybercriminal group Evil Corp, identifying and sanctioning additional members and affiliates.

## Body (verbatim, summary-only excerpts)

WASHINGTON — Today, the Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) is designating seven individuals and two entities associated with the Russia-based cybercriminal group Evil Corp, in a tri-lateral action with the United Kingdom's Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office (FCDO) and Australia's Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade (DFAT).

On December 5, 2019, OFAC designated Evil Corp, its leader and founder Maksim Viktorovich Yakubets and over a dozen Evil Corp members, facilitators, and affiliated companies pursuant to Executive Order (E.O.) 13694, as amended by E.O. 13757 ("E.O. 13694, as amended"). The United Kingdom and Australia are concurrently designating select Evil Corp-affiliated individuals designated by OFAC today or in 2019.

Additionally, the U.S. Department of Justice has unsealed an indictment charging one Evil Corp member in connection with his use of BitPaymer ransomware targeting victims in the United States.

Today's designation also coincides with the second day of the U.S.-hosted Counter Ransomware Initiative summit which involves over 50 countries working together to counter the threat of ransomware.

Quote — Acting Under Secretary of the Treasury for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence Bradley T. Smith: "Today's trilateral action underscores our collective commitment to safeguard against cybercriminals like ransomware actors, who seek to undermine our critical infrastructure and threaten our citizens. The United States, in close coordination with our allies and partners, including through the Counter Ransomware Initiative, will continue to expose and disrupt the criminal networks that seek personal profit from the pain and suffering of their victims."

### Background

Evil Corp is a Russia-based cybercriminal organization responsible for the development and distribution of the Dridex malware. Dridex was used to harvest login credentials from hundreds of banks and other financial institutions in over 40 countries, resulting in more than $100 million in theft losses to U.S. and international financial institutions and their customers.

In a concurrent action with OFAC's December 2019 sanctions, the U.S. Department of Justice indicted Maksim Yakubets and Evil Corp administrator Igor Turashev on computer-hacking and bank-fraud charges, and the U.S. Department of State's Transnational Organized Crime Rewards Program issued a reward of up to $5 million for information leading to Maksim's capture or conviction. Maksim used his employment at the Russian National Engineering Corporation (NIK) — established by Igor Yuryevich Chayka and Aleksei Valeryavich Troshin — as cover for ongoing criminal activity. In October 2022, OFAC designated Chayka, Troshin, and NIK pursuant to E.O. 14024.

### Persons designated by OFAC on October 1, 2024

**Eduard Benderskiy** — former Spetsnaz officer of the Russian Federal Security Service (FSB); current Russian businessman; father-in-law of Evil Corp leader Maksim Viktorovich Yakubets. Identified as a key enabler of Evil Corp's relationship with the Russian state, leveraging his status and contacts to facilitate Evil Corp's developing relationships with officials of Russian intelligence services. After the December 2019 sanctions and indictments, Benderskiy used his extensive influence to protect the group, including by providing senior members with security and ensuring they were not pursued by Russian internal authorities. Designated under E.O. 14024 (acting on behalf of the Government of the Russian Federation) and E.O. 13694, as amended (materially assisting Maksim Yakubets).

**Vympel-Assistance LLC** and **Solar-Invest LLC** — Russia-based business and management consulting companies; Benderskiy is general director, founder, and 100% owner of both. Designated under E.O. 14024 and E.O. 13694, as amended.

**Viktor Grigoryevich Yakubets** — Maksim's father; a member of Evil Corp who in 2020 likely procured technical equipment in furtherance of Evil Corp's operations. Designated under E.O. 13694, as amended.

**Aleksandr Viktorovich Ryzhenkov** ("Guester") — long-term associate and second-in-command of Maksim Yakubets. Worked with Maksim from ~2013 (Business Club era), helped develop a number of Evil Corp's most prolific ransomware strains. In 2016 sought internet bots in operations targeting Switzerland-based victims; in mid-2017 targeted a New York-based bank. After the December 2019 sanctions and indictment, Ryzhenkov and Maksim returned to operations against U.S. victims; in 2020 worked with Maksim to develop "Dridex 2.0." Designated under E.O. 13694, as amended.

**Sergey Viktorovich Ryzhenkov** — brother of Aleksandr Ryzhenkov; helped move Evil Corp operations to a new office in 2019; in 2020 helped develop "Dridex 2.0." Designated under E.O. 13694, as amended.

**Aleksey Yevgenevich Shchetinin** — in 2017–2018 worked with Evil Corp members Denis Igorevich Gusev, Dmitriy Konstantinovich Smirnov, and Aleksei Bashlikov to purchase and exchange millions of dollars' worth of virtual and fiat currencies. Designated under E.O. 13694, as amended.

**Beyat Enverovich Ramazanov** — provided general support to Evil Corp activities and operations. Designated under E.O. 13694, as amended.

**Vadim Gennadievich Pogodin** — in early 2020 played a crucial role in an Evil Corp ransomware attack; in mid-2020 contributed to an Evil Corp ransomware attack on a U.S. company. Designated under E.O. 13694, as amended.

### Concurrent DOJ-EDTX action

The U.S. Department of Justice unsealed an indictment charging **Aleksandr Ryzhenkov** with using the **BitPaymer ransomware** variant to target numerous victims throughout the United States. The FBI published a wanted poster for Ryzhenkov for his alleged involvement in ransomware attacks and money laundering. (Treasury press release notes the unsealed indictment but does not name the U.S. district; the indictment is reported as filed in the Eastern District of Texas, EDTX.)

### Concurrent UK and Australia designations

- **United Kingdom** — FCDO designated 15 Evil Corp members and affiliates on the same day.
- **Australia** — DFAT designated 3 Evil Corp members and affiliates on the same day.

(The Treasury release does not enumerate the UK-15 or AU-3 by name; those are listed by the respective sanctions authorities.)

### Sanctions implications

All property and interests in property of the designated persons in the United States or in possession or control of U.S. persons are blocked and must be reported to OFAC. Entities owned 50% or more by blocked persons are also blocked. OFAC's regulations prohibit transactions by U.S. persons or transiting the United States with the designated persons. Foreign financial institutions and others that engage in certain transactions or activities with the sanctioned persons may expose themselves to sanctions or enforcement action.

The release also references OFAC's updated **Advisory on Potential Sanctions Risk for Facilitating Ransomware Payments** and OFAC's **Sanctions Compliance Guidance for the Virtual Currency Industry**.
