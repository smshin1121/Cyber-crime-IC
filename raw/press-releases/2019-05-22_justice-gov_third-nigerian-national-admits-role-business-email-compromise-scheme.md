Stanley Hugochukwu Nwoke ("Stanley Banks," "Banks," "Hugo Banks," "Banky," "Jose Calderon"), a 28-year-old Nigerian citizen, pleaded guilty in May 2019 before U.S. District Judge Janet C. Hall in New Haven to one count of conspiracy to commit wire fraud stemming from his role in the Connecticut BEC scheme. The indictment charged him in a seven-count action involving an attempted loss of approximately $2.6 million, including at least $440,000 in actual losses to one Connecticut victim. Nwoke was arrested in Mauritius on May 8, 2018, and extradited to the U.S. on May 25, 2018 — the first extradition from Mauritius in over 15 years. The plea also covered a co-conspirator agreement that the government attributed over $1.8 million in losses to Nwoke.
