## Summary

Portugal's Policia Judiciaria announced on 22 September 2025 that, through its National Unit for Combating Cybercrime and Technological Crime (UNC3T) and its International Cooperation Unit (UCI), it had executed a European Arrest Warrant issued by the DIAP da Amadora (Departamento de Investigacao e Accao Penal da Amadora, Public Ministry) against a 30-year-old Portuguese-Angolan dual national suspected of computer fraud (burla informatica), unauthorized computer access (acesso ilegitimo), and money laundering (branqueamento de capitais).

Within an investigation opened in 2024 and directed by the DIAP da Amadora, UNC3T compiled evidence of a structure dedicated to laundering proceeds from cyber-enabled fraud through online gaming platforms. The suspect had been residing in the United Kingdom since April 2025 and was located and arrested by Spanish authorities in September 2025 on the basis of the European Arrest Warrant. Handover to Portuguese authorities took place at the Caya/Elvas land border crossing. The suspect was subsequently presented to a judge at the Criminal Court of Amadora, which ordered preventive detention.

## IC Scope (countries named in source)

- Portugal (issuing state, lead investigation, custody after handover)
- Spain (executing state of the European Arrest Warrant; arrest carried out by Spanish authorities)
- United Kingdom (suspect's residence from April 2025 until relocation to Spain)
- Angola (suspect's secondary nationality; dual citizenship cited in source)

## Cooperation channels named

- European Arrest Warrant (mandado de detencao europeu)
- PJ International Cooperation Unit (UCI) liaison with Spanish counterparts
- Land-border surrender at Caya/Elvas (Portugal-Spain frontier)

No mention of Eurojust or Europol involvement appears in this PJ press release.
