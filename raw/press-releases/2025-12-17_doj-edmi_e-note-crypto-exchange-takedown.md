# E-Note Crypto Exchange Takedown — December 2025

**Source: USAO Eastern District of Michigan announcement at a 2025-12-17 press conference, as reported in same-day tier-2 coverage (CyberScoop, BleepingComputer, Detroit News, ClickOnDetroit, The Register). ## Operation Overview

The U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of Michigan (EDMI), the FBI Detroit Division, and the Michigan State Police announced on 2025-12-17 the takedown of the E-Note cryptocurrency exchange and payment processing service, alleged to have been used to launder illicit funds for cybercriminals since at least 2017. The operation included server seizures, mobile-application takedowns, and seizure of three associated domains: e-note.com, e-note.ws, and jabb.mn.

## Indictment

A federal grand jury in the Eastern District of Michigan indicted Mykhalio Petrovich Chudnovets, 39, a Russian national alleged to have controlled and operated E-Note from approximately 2011 through 2025, on one count of money laundering conspiracy. The conspiracy charge carries a maximum penalty of 20 years in prison.

## Court Documents — Allegations Summarised

Court documents allege Chudnovets has laundered money for cybercriminals since 2010, beginning with personal money-mule networks and evolving over time into a more scalable online platform offering payment processing and value-transfer to cybercriminal clients.

## Scale of Activity

The FBI identified more than USD 70 million in illicit proceeds transferred through the E-Note payment service and its associated money mule network since 2017. The proceeds included funds stolen or extorted from US victims, including organizations in healthcare and critical infrastructure sectors.

- Servers and associated mobile applications hosting the E-Note crypto exchange and payment-processing service.
