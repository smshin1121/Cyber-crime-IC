Jordan: ICITAP Strengthens Partnership With Jordan’s Anti-Narcotics Department Through Workshop To Boost Regional Security Through Establishment of Analytical Unit

Blog Post

Wednesday, November 5, 2025

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

From November 4–5, ICITAP delivered a two‑day workshop for the Public Security Directorate’s Anti‑Narcotics Department (AND) in Amman, Jordan, focusing on analytics and report management systems to enhance data‑driven law enforcement. The workshop strengthened participants’ ability to transform raw data into actionable intelligence and improve analytical reporting processes critical to combating drug trafficking and related crimes in a country that has seen significant narcotics enforcement activity, with the Jordanian Anti‑Narcotics Department handling tens of thousands of drug cases and seizures of millions of narcotic pills and large quantities of hashish, methamphetamine, and other drugs in recent years. Participants explored a range of topics including analytical methodologies, data collection and interpretation, structuring intelligence reports, and integrating analytical insights into operational decision‑making. The training combined theoretical instruction with practical exercises, enabling officers to apply new concepts to real‑world scenarios they face in narcotics enforcement, including identifying patterns in smuggling routes and trafficking networks. AND leadership expressed appreciation for ICITAP’s ongoing partnership and commitment to empowering Jordan’s law enforcement community with modern analytical tools and strategic approaches. The workshop represents a significant step toward building a more agile, data‑informed system capable of responding to evolving drug threats and enhancing information sharing and cooperation with U.S. investigations involving Jordan. This initiative supports Executive Order 14161, “Protecting the United States from Foreign Terrorists and Other National Security and Public Safety Threats,” and is funded by the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL) in partnership with the U.S. Embassy in Amman.
