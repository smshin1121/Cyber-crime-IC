## Summary

INTERPOL's Operation Synergia II ran from 1 April to 31 August 2024 and specifically targeted phishing, ransomware, and information stealer infrastructure. The operation was a joint effort involving law enforcement agencies from 95 INTERPOL member countries alongside private sector partners Group-IB, Trend Micro, Kaspersky, and Team Cymru. Of approximately 30,000 suspicious IP addresses identified, 76% were taken down. Additionally, 59 servers and 43 electronic devices (laptops, mobile phones, hard disks) were seized, and 41 individuals were arrested with 65 others still under investigation.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation Synergia II
- **Date:** 1 April - 31 August 2024
- **Lead agency:** INTERPOL
- **Participating countries:** 95 member countries
- **Private sector partners:** Group-IB, Trend Micro, Kaspersky, Team Cymru
- **Suspicious IP addresses identified:** ~30,000
- **IP addresses taken down:** 76% (~22,800)

- **Arrests:** 41 (65 others under investigation)
- **Crime types targeted:** Phishing, ransomware, information stealers
- **Notable country actions:**

  - Macau (China): took down 291 servers
  - Mongolia: searched 21 houses, seized server, identified 93 linked individuals
  - Madagascar: identified 11 people, seized 11 devices
  - Estonia: seized 80GB+ of server data
- **Enforcement actions:** Server takedowns, IP address disruption, arrests, device seizures
