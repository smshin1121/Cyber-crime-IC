# Armenian Man Extradited to U.S. Faces Charges for Role in Infostealing Malware Scheme

> [!note] Source provenance
> This is a verbatim-quote excerpt of the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Western District of Texas (USAO-WDTX, justice.gov own-domain) press release dated 2026-03-25 (publicly available at the URL above), retained in `raw/` for reference. Body content is paraphrased except where quoted.

## Overview

An Armenian national, Hambardzum Minasyan, made his initial appearance in an Austin federal court on Tuesday, March 24, 2026 after being extradited to the United States from the Netherlands on criminal charges related to his alleged role in the RedLine infostealing malware scheme. The extradition was secured by the Justice Department's Office of International Affairs (OIA) on March 23, 2026.

Minasyan is charged with conspiracy to commit access device fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1029(b)(2)), conspiracy to violate the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (18 U.S.C. § 1030), and conspiracy to commit money laundering (18 U.S.C. § 1956(h)). If convicted, he faces up to 10 years in prison on the access device fraud conspiracy and up to 20 years in prison on each of the other counts.

## Allegations

According to the indictment, Minasyan allegedly conspired with co-developers and administrators to enrich himself by developing and administering RedLine, one of the most prevalent infostealing malware variants in the world. RedLine has been previously used to conduct intrusions against major corporations and has affected millions of victim systems globally.

Specifically, the indictment alleges Minasyan:
- Registered two virtual private servers to host portions of RedLine's infrastructure;
- Registered two internet domains in support of the RedLine scheme;
- Created repositories on an online file-sharing site that were used to distribute RedLine to affiliates;
- In November 2021, registered a cryptocurrency account that was used to receive payments from RedLine affiliates.

## Cooperation (verbatim from press release)

> "In October 2024, the Department of Justice joined the Netherlands, Belgium, Eurojust and other partners in announcing an international disruption effort against the current version of RedLine Infostealer."

The current March 2026 extradition follows on from that earlier joint disruption (Operation Magnus, October 2024) and the unsealing of charges against alleged co-conspirator Maxim Rudometov, one of the developers and administrators of RedLine Infostealer.

### US agencies investigating

> "the FBI Austin Cyber Task Force, which consists of the Naval Criminal Investigative Service, IRS Criminal Investigation, the DoD Office of Inspector General's Defense Criminal Investigative Service, and Army Criminal Investigation Division, among other agencies."

Additional support came from the Justice Department's Office of International Affairs and the Criminal Division's International Computer Hacking and Intellectual Property (ICHIP) attorney advisor based at Eurojust in The Hague.

### Prosecutors

- Justin R. Simmons — U.S. Attorney for the Western District of Texas
- Kirk Mangels — Assistant U.S. Attorney, prosecuting the case

## Status

Pending trial in the U.S. District Court for the Western District of Texas (Austin Division). An indictment is an accusation only; the defendant is presumed innocent unless and until proven guilty.
