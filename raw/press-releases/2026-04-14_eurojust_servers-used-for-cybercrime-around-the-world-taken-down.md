## Extracted Fact Summary

Official Eurojust source for the March 2026 action against a paid IP proxy service used by cybercriminals. The original local ingest captured only the opening paragraph; this structured extraction records the full public metric set verified from Eurojust on 2026-04-27.

Key extracted facts:

- Authorities from eight countries targeted a paid IP proxy service used by cybercriminals.
- The service used malware to gain access to IP addresses through infected modems, routers, and other devices.
- The compromised-device footprint was approximately 369,000 routers and other devices across 163 countries.
- The customer base was approximately 124,000 users.
- Customer payments to the associated payment platform exceeded EUR 5 million.
- Servers used to spread malware were located in France, Germany, Hungary, the Netherlands, Romania, and the United States.
- Judicial cooperation was coordinated by Eurojust; Europol supplied operational and analytical support, including crypto tracing, malware analysis, network analysis, database cross-checks, and an action-day Virtual Command Post.
- During the coordinated operation on 2026-03-11, authorities took down 24 servers in seven countries, seized 34 domains, disconnected infected modems from the service, and froze approximately EUR 3.5 million in cryptocurrency through US authorities.
- Publicly identified participating countries were Austria, Bulgaria, France, Germany, Hungary, the Netherlands, Romania, and the United States.
