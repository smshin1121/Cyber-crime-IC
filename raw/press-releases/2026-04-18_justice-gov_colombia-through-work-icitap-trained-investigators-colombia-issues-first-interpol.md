Colombia: Through Work By ICITAP-Trained Investigators, Colombia Issues First INTERPOL Silver Notice In Latin America

Blog Post

Friday, May 9, 2025

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

On May 9, the ICITAP‑Colombia mission reported a significant development in international criminal finance cooperation when Colombia became the first country in Latin America to issue an INTERPOL Silver Notice, an innovative international alert designed to help identify and track assets linked to transnational criminal activity rather than seek arrests. The Silver Notice, introduced by INTERPOL as part of the 2025 pilot project, enables member countries to request information about potentially illicit assets—from properties and vehicles to financial accounts and businesses—associated with individuals suspected of serious crimes such as corruption, drug trafficking, and money laundering, enhancing international cooperation to trace and disrupt criminal financial networks. In Colombia’s case, the first Silver Notice targeted allegedly illicit assets located in Spain tied to a Colombian national involved in organized crime, illustrating how the tool supports asset tracing and proactive investigatory measures across borders. Investigators from INTERPOL Colombia, the Colombian National Police, and the Financial Crimes Division of DIJIN coordinated the action, building on specialized training in asset tracing and financial disruption provided through the ICITAP‑Colombia mission with support from the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL). This milestone aligns with U.S. Executive Orders 14159 and 14165, which prioritize dismantling transnational criminal organizations and their financial infrastructure, and represents a shift in strategy toward targeting the economic foundations of criminal networks while strengthening global law enforcement cooperation.
