Albania: ICITAP Helps Enhance Police Investigations Security Academy Launch of 11th Crime Investigation Course

Blog Post

Wednesday, March 5, 2025

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

On March 5, the ICITAP‑supported Albanian Security Academy, School of Crime Investigations, launched the 11th session of the three‑week Crime Investigation Course, Level 1, at the Security Academy in Tirana, bringing together seventeen crime specialists from various State Police directorates to enhance core investigative skills. This course is part of a broader, long‑standing partnership between ICITAP and the Security Academy to professionalize investigations in Albania by integrating national standards, modern investigative methodologies, and internationally recognized best practices. The curriculum emphasizes a victim‑centric approach and incorporates behavioral science‑based interviewing techniques, mirroring components of the Police Criminal Investigations Training Manual and Curriculum designed by ICITAP and endorsed by Albanian prosecutorial and police leadership, which focus on ethical, legally grounded procedures for investigating frequently reported offenses. Training blends classroom instruction with practical demonstrations to reinforce both theoretical understanding and field application, preparing officers to handle complex aspects of crime scene management, evidence collection, witness interaction, and suspect interview strategies. Strengthening investigative capability at this foundational level supports broader reforms aimed at increasing transparency, accountability, and public trust in law enforcement. Funded by the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL), the program aligns with U.S. priorities for combating transnational criminal organizations and improving justice sector effectiveness, while contributing to Albania’s progress toward meeting EU‑aligned policing standards.
