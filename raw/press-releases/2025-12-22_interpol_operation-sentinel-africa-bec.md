## Summary

INTERPOL coordinated Operation Sentinel from 27 October to 27 November 2025, focusing primarily on business email compromise (BEC), digital extortion, and ransomware across Africa. Law enforcement in 19 countries arrested 574 suspects and recovered approximately USD 3 million. Over 6,000 malicious links were taken down and six distinct ransomware variants were decrypted. The investigated cases were linked to estimated financial losses exceeding USD 21 million. The operation was held under the umbrella of the African Joint Operation against Cybercrime (AFJOC), funded by the UK's Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office, and through the Global Action on Cybercrime Enhanced (GLACY-e) project, a joint initiative of the European Union and the Council of Europe.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation Sentinel
- **Date:** 27 October - 27 November 2025
- **Lead agency:** INTERPOL, under AFJOC (African Joint Operation against Cybercrime) umbrella
- **Participating countries (19):** Benin, Botswana, Burkina Faso, Cameroon, Chad, Congo, Djibouti, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Gabon, Ghana, Kenya, Malawi, Nigeria, Senegal, South Africa, South Sudan, Uganda, Zambia, Zimbabwe
- **Arrests:** 574
- **Assets recovered:** USD 3 million
- **Estimated financial losses (cases investigated):** USD 21 million+
- **Malicious links taken down:** 6,000+
- **Ransomware variants decrypted:** 6
- **Crime types:** Business email compromise (BEC), digital extortion, ransomware
- **Funding:** UK Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office; EU-Council of Europe GLACY-e project
- **Notable BEC cases:**
  - Senegal: Major petroleum company detected sophisticated BEC scheme; fraudsters infiltrated internal email systems and impersonated executives to authorize fraudulent wire transfer of USD 7.9 million (intercepted by authorities)
  - Ghana: Dismantled major cyber-fraud network mimicking fast-food brands for online payment collection; 10 suspects arrested, 100+ digital devices seized
  - Benin: 43 malicious domains taken down, 4,318 social media accounts linked to extortion/scams shut down, 106 arrests
- **Enforcement actions:** Arrests, malicious infrastructure takedown, ransomware decryption, asset recovery

## Relevance to BEC/International Cooperation

Operation Sentinel is notable as an INTERPOL-coordinated operation with BEC as one of its primary focus areas, conducted across 19 African countries. The Senegal BEC case -- where fraudsters infiltrated a petroleum company's email systems to impersonate executives and authorize a USD 7.9 million wire transfer -- exemplifies the sophistication of modern BEC attacks targeting high-value corporate targets. The operation's funding structure (UK FCDO and EU-Council of Europe GLACY-e) demonstrates the international donor support model for building African cybercrime enforcement capacity. The 574 arrests across 19 countries represent one of the largest BEC-related enforcement actions on the African continent, where many BEC syndicates originate or operate from.
