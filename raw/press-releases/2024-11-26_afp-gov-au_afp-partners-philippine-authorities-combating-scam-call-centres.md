# AFP partners with Philippine authorities in combating scam call centres

Source: Australian Federal Police news centre (https://www.afp.gov.au/news-centre/media-release/afp-partners-philippine-authorities-combating-scam-call-centres)
Date: 26 November 2024, 8:27am AEDT
Captured (WebFetch): 2026-05-10

---

The Australian Federal Police (AFP) and the Philippine Presidential Anti-Organized Crime Commission (PAOCC) and National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) have shut down a major scam call centre in Manila, Philippines.

Australian authorities are working to identify Australian victims of romance, investment and cryptocurrency scams perpetrated from the boiler room operation, with more than 250 suspected cyber criminals arrested by Philippine authorities.

Of the 250 suspects, 190 were foreign nationals — including from the People's Republic of China, Vietnam, Indonesia and other countries — and 68 were Filipino nationals. More than 300 computer towers, more than 1,000 mobile phones and thousands of SIM cards were seized in the raid.

The activity was conducted under Operation Firestorm, which was set up to disrupt offshore organised crime syndicates that deceive Australians through romance, investment or cryptocurrency scams. The AFP, with the support of the PAOCC and NBI, will provide technical and cryptocurrency-related assistance to identify Australian victims and methodologies used to extract cash.

AFP Assistant Commissioner David McLean said the operation marked the first significant joint takedown of a Philippine scam compound under Operation Firestorm.

"We commend the PAOCC and NBI for their work in identifying this boiler room and taking swift action to shut it down. I thank them for the trust they have shown in the AFP by allowing us to work with them," Assistant Commissioner McLean said.

"This is the first significant joint takedown of a Philippine scam compound under Operation Firestorm, but it will not be the last. The AFP, working with our regional partners, will continue to identify and disrupt these scam centres which target Australians and cause significant financial and emotional damage to victims."

The PAOCC and NBI led the on-site enforcement action and are conducting domestic prosecutions against the suspects under Philippine law. The AFP's role is to provide intelligence, technical expertise and cryptocurrency-tracing capability through its JPC3 (Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre)-led Operation Firestorm framework, and to help identify victims in Australia and elsewhere.

Operation Firestorm was launched in August 2024 by the AFP-led JPC3 to target offshore scam centres operating in Southeast Asia that target Australian residents. The Manila takedown is the first of three Manila scam centres disrupted under Operation Firestorm in the period August 2024 – April 2025 (per AFP follow-up press release of 1 April 2025).

The AFP's International Network, which includes liaison officers posted in Manila, was the bilateral channel through which intelligence was shared with PAOCC and NBI ahead of the action day.
