# United States and Taiwan Collaborate to Dismantle International Financial Fraud Network

> Source: American Institute in Taiwan, Press Release PR-2510, issued 2025-05-14.
> Joint press conference held at Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutors Office, 2025-05-14 (~100 attendees).

## Press Release Body (verbatim)

The Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutors Office, in collaboration with U.S. and Taiwan law enforcement authorities earlier this year, successfully apprehended key Taiwanese suspects involved in a major Taiwan-U.S. cross-border financial fraud case that targeted elderly victims and involved over $US100 million dollars of laundered money.

The investigation, which began in May 2023, was initiated when law enforcement identified suspicious financial documents carried by two Taiwanese travelers. This led to the uncovering of a highly sophisticated international criminal organization responsible for defrauding hundreds of elderly victims across the United States and laundering money through a network of shell companies and international accounts.

This case marks a historic milestone in international cooperation. For the first time, investigators from the Criminal Investigation Bureau and prosecutors from the Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutors Office (TTDPO) traveled to the United States to conduct a proffer interview with a Taiwanese defendant in custody pursuant to a Mutual Legal Assistance Agreement (MLAA) request. This led to the arrest of a suspect surnamed LIAO who is believed to be a high-level ranking leader within the criminal organization responsible for orchestrating vast financial fraud schemes worldwide.

Key partners in this investigation include Taiwan's National Police Agency (NPA) Criminal Investigation Bureau (CIB), Taiwan's Ministry of Justice (MOJ), the Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutors Office (TTDPO), and the following Homeland Security Investigations Offices: HSI Taipei, HSI Los Angeles, and HSI Nashville.

"When international partnerships are forged in trust and driven by a shared mission, there is no criminal enterprise beyond our collective reach," said HSI Supervisory Special Agent Brian Sherota.

The success of the investigation is attributed to the tireless work and commitment of all involved agencies. As the investigation continues, further arrests are anticipated, and efforts to track illicit financial flows and bring perpetrators to justice remain steadfast.

The joint press conference was held at the Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutors Office on May 14, 2025. Approximately 100 participants attended, including representatives from the American Institute in Taiwan (AIT), Homeland Security Investigations (HSI), the Executive Yuan Minister without Portfolio Lin Ming-hsin, the Ministry of Justice, the Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutors Office, the Administrative Enforcement Agency, the National Police Agency/Criminal Investigation Bureau, and the Taichung City Police Department.

## Cross-source supplementary facts (Taipei Times 2025-05-15, Focus Taiwan 2025-05-14)

Note: AIT's PR-2510 references "over US$100 million laundered" and "hundreds of elderly victims". The Taichung District Prosecutors' Office indictment narrative published the same day (reported in Taipei Times and Focus Taiwan) names a specific subset of three U.S. victims defrauded of US$5.32 million; this subset is part of, not the entirety of, the broader scheme described by AIT. The two figures are not contradictory — they describe different scopes (overall criminal organisation vs. specific indictment).

Defendants named in the Taichung District Prosecutors' Office indictment:

- Liao (廖), age 36 — alleged ring leader; arrested at Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport on January 24, 2025; prosecutors recommended a 10-year sentence.
- Huang (黃), age 39 — alleged collections handler; recruited in 2023; prosecutors recommended a 5-year sentence.
- Hsiao (肖) and Sun (孫) — U.S.-based co-conspirators (U.S. citizens); witness Sun was questioned in Nashville under MLAA.

Charges filed under Taiwan law: aggravated fraud and money laundering under the Organized Crime Prevention Act (組織犯罪防制條例), Money Laundering Control Act (洗錢防制法), Passport Act (護照條例), and Criminal Code.

Modus operandi: U.S. bank accounts opened with fake passports; victims targeted by romance-scam pretext, then solicited for fraudulent investments; proceeds laundered through Liao's mother's accounts and luxury purchases (Swiss watch, diamond ring, Bitcoin, Tether, Lexus vehicle).

Cooperation modality: U.S.-Taiwan MLAA (signed March 2002, in force 2002). HSI team visited the Taichung District Prosecutors' Office in March 2025 to share intelligence; Taiwanese prosecutors and CIB investigators traveled to Los Angeles and Nashville in April 2025 — the first time a Taiwanese prosecutor (Chang Fu-chun, 張福鈞 of TTDPO) traveled to the U.S. to directly question a witness on-site under the MLAA since its 2002 signing.
