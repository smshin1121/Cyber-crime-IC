## Summary-Only Archive

Institutional press release from the Fiscalia General del Estado de Ecuador (FGE) dated 19 September 2024, announcing Ecuador's leg of Operacion KAERB — the multinational takedown of the iServer phishing-as-a-service platform.

The release reports:

- Six-country coordinated action: Spain, Argentina, Colombia, Chile, Ecuador, and Peru — each contributing both fiscalias (prosecutors) and police forces.
- Supporting organizations: Europol, PAcCTO 2.0 (Programa de Asistencia Contra el Crimen Transnacional Organizado, EU-funded), Ameripol (Comunidad de Policias de America), and the private-sector firm Group-IB.
- Aggregate enforcement: 17 detainees across all participating jurisdictions, with the by-country distribution given as: Argentina 2 detained + 1 fugitive, Bulgaria 1, Colombia 4, Spain 2, Ecuador 2, Peru 3, Venezuela 2, Uruguay 1.
- Ecuador-side enforcement: on 10 September 2024, the Fiscalia General del Estado de Ecuador, in coordination with the Policia Nacional de Ecuador, executed an operation in Santo Domingo de los Tsachilas, resulting in two arrests of suspected participants in this international criminal network.
- Investigation duration: approximately 2 years prior to the September 10-17, 2024 action days.
- Target platform: iServer — an international cybercriminal platform that generated phishing messages designed to obtain credentials from victims of stolen high-end mobile devices.
- iServer history: operated for approximately 5 years under multiple domains and anonymous payment methods; 2,000+ registered subscriber accounts.
- Victim estimates: 483,000 victims globally; Ecuador specifically reports approximately 42,000 victims.

Verbatim quotes (Spanish, for L24 cooperation attestation):

> "El exito de esta operacion es el resultado de una intensa cooperacion internacional entre los cuerpos policiales y fiscalias de Espana, Argentina, Colombia, Chile, Ecuador y Peru."

> "En el marco de la operacion KAERB, la Fiscalia General del Estado (FGE), en coordinacion con la Policia Nacional, ejecuto un operativo el 10 de septiembre de 2024, en Santo Domingo de los Tsachilas, que dejo como resultado la detencion con fines investigativos de dos presuntos involucrados en esta trama criminal de alcance internacional."

This is a tier-1 own-domain release from the Ecuadorian prosecutor's office (Fiscalia General del Estado de Ecuador). It corroborates the existing Argentine-side tier-1 source (argentina.gob.ar Ministerio de Seguridad release of 2024-09-24) and adds:

1. Ecuador-side enforcement specifics: 2 arrests on 10 September 2024 in Santo Domingo de los Tsachilas, with both the Fiscalia and Policia Nacional de Ecuador named as the executing agencies.
2. Per-country detainee distribution that includes jurisdictions not detailed in the Argentine release (Bulgaria, Venezuela, Uruguay involvement as detention locations rather than participating-LE jurisdictions).
3. Explicit recognition that the operation involved both fiscalias (prosecutor offices) and policies (police forces) of each of the six countries — making the cross-jurisdictional prosecutorial-coordination angle more visible than the Argentine release, which emphasized the police-cooperation architecture.
4. Investigation duration estimate (approximately 2 years), which is not stated in the Argentine release.

L24 strict tier-1 own-domain status: confirmed. fiscalia.gob.ec is the institutional domain of the Ecuador prosecutor's office, a national prosecutorial authority. The release explicitly names Spain, Argentina, Colombia, Chile, Ecuador, and Peru's prosecution-and-police cooperation. ≥2-LE: confirmed (Ecuador FGE + Policia Nacional de Ecuador + foreign-LE partners explicitly named in the operation).
