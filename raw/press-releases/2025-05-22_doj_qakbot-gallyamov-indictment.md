## Summary

The US Department of Justice unsealed an indictment charging Rustam Rafailevich Gallyamov, 48, of Moscow, Russia, with leading a group of cybercriminals who developed and deployed the Qakbot malware since 2008. From 2019 onward, Gallyamov allegedly used Qakbot to infect thousands of victim computers worldwide, establishing a botnet and providing access to co-conspirators who deployed ransomware including ProLock, DopplePaymer, Egregor, REvil, Conti, Name Locker, Black Basta, and Cactus. Even after the FBI's August 2023 takedown of the Qakbot botnet, Gallyamov allegedly continued criminal activity, orchestrating spam bomb attacks as recently as January 2025 and deploying Black Basta and Cactus ransomware. The DOJ filed a civil forfeiture complaint against over USD 24 million in cryptocurrency seized from Gallyamov. This action was part of a multinational effort involving the US, France, Germany, the Netherlands, Denmark, the United Kingdom, and Canada, with Europol also providing support.
