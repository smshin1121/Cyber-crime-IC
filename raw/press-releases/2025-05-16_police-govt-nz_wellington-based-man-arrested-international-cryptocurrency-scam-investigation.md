# Wellington-based man arrested in international cryptocurrency scam investigation

**Issuing body**: New Zealand Police — Financial Crime Group
**Published**: Friday, 16 May 2025
**Source URL**: https://www.police.govt.nz/news/release/wellington-based-man-arrested-international-cryptocurrency-scam-investigation

---

## Press release (verbatim summary, en-NZ)

A Wellington-based individual was arrested by New Zealand Police's Financial Crime Group in Auckland as part of an FBI investigation into a criminal organisation accused of stealing approximately US$265 million (approximately NZD$450 million) in cryptocurrency from seven victims.

The alleged theft occurred between March and August 2024. Members of the organised criminal group are accused of fraudulently obtaining cryptocurrency from the victims and laundering it across multiple platforms.

Search warrants were executed in a coordinated operation across **Auckland**, **Wellington**, and **California** over a three-day period. The combined search-warrant activity resulted in the arrest of **13 individuals in total** across the New Zealand and US legs of the operation.

The New Zealand suspect has been indicted by the **United States Department of Justice** under U.S. federal law on charges of:

- **Racketeering (RICO)** — racketeer-influenced and corrupt organisations conspiracy
- **Conspiracy to commit wire fraud**
- **Conspiracy to commit money laundering**

According to the U.S. statement cited in the New Zealand Police release: *"the defendants scammed seven victims, spending the stolen virtual currency to purchase, among other things, $9 million of exotic cars, watches, jewellery, designer handbags and clothing, designer luxury goods, and rented out luxury homes in Los Angeles, the Hamptons and Miami, regularly engaged in nightclub spending of hundreds of thousands of dollars on a nightly basis, and travelled by private jet."*

The accused appeared in **Auckland District Court** and was granted **interim name suppression**. He is scheduled to reappear on **3 July 2025**.

**Detective Inspector Christiaan Barnard** (Manager of New Zealand Police's Financial Crime Group) noted that cross-border criminal activity of this nature requires strong international law-enforcement partnerships, and acknowledged the cooperation of the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation in the joint investigation.

## Editor's notes (post-fetch)

- The New Zealand Police press release does not name the defendant (interim name suppression). External U.S. court records and reporting tie the broader 13-person ring to a U.S. District of Columbia RICO superseding indictment of mid-May 2025 that charged 12 additional defendants (in addition to **Malone Lam**, originally charged in the District of Columbia on 19 September 2024). The Wellington-based suspect is the additional New Zealand-arrested co-defendant aligned with that superseding indictment timing. Defendant identity in this raw file is preserved only as the New Zealand release stated it (i.e. with interim name suppression).
- The cited US$265 million stolen-cryptocurrency figure is the New Zealand Police framing. U.S. court documents have variously described the headline aggregate theft as approximately US$263 million, with the largest single victim loss being approximately 4,100 BTC (~US$230 million) on 18 August 2024. The figures reflect different valuation cuts of the same underlying conspiracy.
- The press release names only two countries explicitly as IC partners: **New Zealand** and the **United States**. Additional jurisdictions referenced in the broader US indictment (e.g. Dubai for at-large defendants) are NOT named in this New Zealand source and must not be added to the operation page from this source alone.
