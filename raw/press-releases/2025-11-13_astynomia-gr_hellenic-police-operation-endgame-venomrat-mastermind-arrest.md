## English Summary

- Publisher: Hellenic Police (Ελληνική Αστυνομία) — official press release, Cyber Crime Directorate (Διεύθυνση Δίωξης Ηλεκτρονικού Εγκλήματος) lead.
- Date of release: 2025-11-13 (Athens).
- Action day in Greece: 2025-11-03 (arrest in Attica). Wider European action window: 2025-11-10 through 2025-11-14 (multi-country search/seizure phase).
- Operation: Operation Endgame (international, multi-year, coordinated by Europol and Eurojust). The November 2025 phase targeted three malware ecosystems: the Rhadamanthys infostealer, the VenomRAT remote-access trojan, and the Elysium botnet.
- Greek arrest: a 38-year-old foreign national (multiple Greek-media corroborations identify the suspect as an Albanian national resident in Attica) arrested in Attica on 2025-11-03 pursuant to a European Arrest Warrant issued by French judicial authorities. The suspect is assessed by investigators to be the principal developer/seller of the VenomRAT remote-access trojan since 2020.
- Modus operandi: subscription-based malware-as-a-service platform. VenomRAT provided buyers with the ability to remotely take control of infected devices, log keystrokes, capture video/audio via the device camera, exfiltrate text data, and access cryptocurrency wallets. Pricing tier reported in Greek media: €150/month to €1,550/year subscription.
- Seizures from the Attica residence: 7 hard drives, 3 USB storage devices, 11 debit cards, mobile phones, technical equipment used for malware development and distribution, and a digital wallet containing cryptocurrency valued at approximately US$140,424 at time of seizure.
- International law-enforcement cooperation explicitly named in the release:
  - Hellenic Police Cyber Crime Directorate (Διεύθυνση Δίωξης Ηλεκτρονικού Εγκλήματος) — arresting and on-site search authority in Greece.
  - Europol — operational coordination and analytical support.
  - Eurojust — judicial coordination across participating jurisdictions.
  - U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) — representatives participated on-site at the search of the suspect's residence in Attica.
  - French judicial authorities — issued the underlying European Arrest Warrant.
- Wider November 2025 phase outcomes (across all participating jurisdictions, attributed to the Europol-coordinated international operation): 1,025 servers dismantled, 20 domains seized, 11 physical search locations executed across Germany (1), Greece (1), and the Netherlands (9). Participating jurisdictions in this phase per the umbrella Europol release: Australia, Belgium, Canada, Denmark, France, Germany, Greece, Lithuania, the Netherlands, the United Kingdom, and the United States.
- Crime types: cyber-dependent — malware development and distribution; provision of cybercrime-as-a-service infrastructure; aiding and abetting subsequent computer intrusions, credential theft, and cryptocurrency theft worldwide.
