## Headline facts (Europol, 2026-04-29)

- **Operation name**: Operational Taskforce GRIMM (OTF GRIMM)
- **Anniversary**: One year since launch (founded April 2025)
- **Coordinating body**: Europol — European Serious and Organised Crime Centre (ESOCC)
- **Lead country**: Sweden (Swedish Police Authority initially led; Asa Granstrom of the Swedish Police Authority is publicly named as the OTF GRIMM author for Europol-aligned analytic outputs)
- **Total arrests in 12-month window (Apr 2025 – Apr 2026)**: 280
- **Total individuals identified as linked to violence-as-a-service networks**: more than 1,400
- **Recruitment accounts identified by the taskforce**: ~14,000 digital accounts linked to recruitment and coordination of violent crimes
- **Six-month interim figure** (December 2025 update): 193 arrests
- **Three high-value targets newly published on EU Most Wanted**: two Swedish nationals, one German national, charges including murder, drug trafficking, and money laundering linked to violence-as-a-service networks

## Participating countries (11) — verbatim Europol roster

Belgium, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Iceland, the Netherlands, Norway, Spain, Sweden, the United Kingdom.

## Crime model

- "Violence-as-a-service" (VaaS): senior organised crime figures outsource physical violence — intimidation, torture, contract murder — by recruiting young, often inexperienced perpetrators online.
- Four functional roles in OTF GRIMM's recruitment chain (Europol classification): **instigator → recruiter → enabler → perpetrator**.
- Recruitment surfaces: encrypted messaging apps, gaming platforms, mainstream social media. Europol has held coordination meetings with Google, Meta, TikTok, and Snapchat regarding recruitment prevention.
- Recruitment lure: promises of money, status, or belonging; coded language, memes, and gamified tasks to lower the threshold of violent participation.
- Some arrested perpetrators are minors; reporting cites Swedish gangs recruiting children under age 15 (below criminal responsibility threshold) and a "handful" of children aged 10-12.

## Notable cases attributed to OTF GRIMM

- **2025-03-28**: Three suspects (Sweden / Germany) arrested in connection with the killing of three people in Oosterhout, Netherlands.
- **2025-05-12**: Murder attempt in Tamm, Germany.
- **2025-06**: Seven minors / young adults arrested in Denmark for recruitment via encrypted messaging.
- **2025-07-01**: Six arrested in Spain over a planned murder; firearms seized.
- **2025-07 to 2025-08**: A Dutch national arrested as the alleged getaway driver for two minors involved in explosions in Germany.
- **2026-01**: A minor arrested in Sweden in connection with a shooting outside the Alphen aan den Rijn prison facility (Netherlands). Europol's OTF GRIMM was central in establishing the link between the Swedish suspect and the Dutch incident.
- **(undated 2025/26)**: A Swedish organiser of serious violence linked to the Foxtrot network arrested in Iraq; extradition proceedings underway.

## Sweden-specific footprint (NordiskPost, 2026-04-29)

- Approximately 120-130 of the 280 detentions are connected to Swedish criminal environments (Foxtrot network and adjacent groups).
- "Mohamed 'Moewgli' Mohdhi" named publicly as second-in-command in the Foxtrot network and listed as a high-priority OTF GRIMM target.
- Swedish police superintendent **Theodor Smedius** quoted: "It is a ruthless structure where serious criminals exploit children."

## Europol framing (verbatim, abbreviated)

> "Violence is no longer confined to isolated acts or local dynamics. It is increasingly offered as a service: accessible, scalable and driven by online ecosystems."

> "These individuals are groomed or coerced into committing a range of violent crimes, from acts of intimidation and torture to murder."

## EU Most Wanted listings (2026-04-29 publication)

Three OTF GRIMM-linked high-value targets newly published on the EU Most Wanted website on the one-year-anniversary date:

1. Swedish national #1 — alleged role in violence-as-a-service network (murder / drug trafficking / money laundering).
2. Swedish national #2 — alleged role in violence-as-a-service network.
3. German national — alleged role in violence-as-a-service network.

The publication is intended to intensify location-and-arrest pressure on the most dangerous fugitives associated with the VaaS model.

## International cooperation legal posture

- Europol coordination through the European Serious and Organised Crime Centre (ESOCC).
- Operation crosses 11 jurisdictions (10 EU/EEA + UK); cross-border arrests proceed under domestic legal authorities, European Arrest Warrants (where applicable), and direct police-to-police channels coordinated by Europol.
- Tier-1 sources do not designate OTF GRIMM as a Joint Investigation Team (JIT) and do not name Eurojust as a coordinating body for the taskforce overall.
- Public-private channel: Europol meetings with Google, Meta, TikTok, Snapchat constitute provider-side cooperation distinct from formal MLA / EIO instruments.
