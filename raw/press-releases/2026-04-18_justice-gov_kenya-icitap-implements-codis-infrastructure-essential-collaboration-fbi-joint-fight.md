Kenya: ICITAP Implements CODIS Infrastructure Essential To Collaboration With FBI In Joint Fight Against Terrorism

Blog Post

Friday, October 10, 2025

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

From October 6–10, an ICITAP Senior Forensic Advisor led the delivery and installation of critical infrastructure to deploy the FBI’s Combined DNA Index System (CODIS) software at the National Forensic Laboratory DNA and Biology Unit in Kenya, marking a major advancement in the country’s forensic DNA capabilities. CODIS, managed by the FBI, allows law enforcement agencies to create, store, and search DNA profiles in a secure, structured database, enabling investigators to link evidence to individuals, connect previously unrelated investigations, and generate actionable leads across jurisdictions. With the system and associated hardware now operational, Kenyan forensic scientists can input DNA profiles from criminal casework and other eligible samples, producing forensically sound matches that support coordinated investigative responses and strengthen the integrity of criminal investigations. This deployment represents a significant milestone in U.S.–Kenya cooperation on forensics and counterterrorism, enhancing the nation’s ability to investigate terrorism and transnational crime, deny violent actors anonymity or safe havens, and facilitate timely information sharing with U.S. and regional partners. The initiative directly supports U.S. national security priorities, including Executive Order 14161, which protects the United States from foreign terrorists and other national security threats, and Executive Order 14150, which seeks to make America safer and stronger by confronting threats at their source. Funded by the U.S. Department of State Bureau of Counterterrorism (DOS/CT) and implemented in coordination with the U.S. Embassy in Nairobi, this project underscores ICITAP’s commitment to advancing forensic science, strengthening justice systems abroad, and reinforcing global security through cutting-edge DNA technology.
