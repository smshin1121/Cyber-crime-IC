# United States and Indonesia Dismantle Global Phishing Network

**By:** U.S. Embassy Jakarta — Press Release
**Date:** April 23, 2026
**Source:** https://id.usembassy.gov/united-states-and-indonesia-dismantle-global-phishing-network/
**Photo credit (as published):** U.S. Embassy Jakarta / Erik Kurniawan

---

## Body (verbatim)

JAKARTA – The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) Atlanta Field Office and the Indonesian National Police (INP) have successfully concluded a multi-year joint investigation resulting in the dismantlement operation of a sophisticated global phishing network. This first-of-its-kind joint cyber investigation between the United States and Indonesia specifically targeted the developer of the notorious "W3LL" phishing kit. The announcement of the operation was revealed during a press conference at the INP Criminal Investigation Division (Bareskrim Polri) in Jakarta, Wednesday, April 22.

FBI Atlanta Special Agent in Charge Marlo Graham stated, "This wasn't just phishing—it was a full-service cybercrime platform. And we will continue to work with our domestic and foreign law enforcement partners, using all available tools to protect the public." The United States is deeply grateful to the Indonesian National Police for their expertise and commitment to this joint effort.

The operation led to the successful apprehension of the alleged developer, identified as G.L., by the INP, alongside the execution of search warrants that resulted in the seizure of assets and hardware containing evidence of thousands of global targets.

The phishing infrastructure was supported by an online marketplace known as W3LLSTORE. Between 2019 and 2023, this central hub facilitated the sale of more than 25,000 compromised accounts and unauthorized system accesses. From 2023 to 2024 alone, the phishing kit was used to target more than 17,000 victims across nearly every continent. In total, the developer of the W3LL phishing kit facilitated the theft of thousands of account credentials and attempted more than $20 million (approximately 350 billion Rupiahs) in fraudulent transactions worldwide.

"The success of the takedown relied on the coordinated execution between cyber investigators from FBI Atlanta, INP Bareskrim, and East Nusa Tanggera (Kupang Polri)," said FBI Representative Robert Lafferty during the press conference at the INP on Wednesday. "While FBI monitored digital footprints and traced financial flows within the United States, the INP conducted critical on-the-ground operation to locate the operation's mastermind and collect critical digital evidence."
