## Summary

BleepingComputer reported that Romanian authorities arrested five suspects linked to CTB-Locker and Cerber spam campaigns after receiving intelligence from Europol, the FBI, and Dutch National Police. The report emphasized that the suspects were not ransomware developers but affiliates using ransomware-as-a-service, keeping roughly 70 percent of the proceeds while portal operators retained the rest. It also noted that the initial CTB-Locker arrests led to the identification of two additional Cerber-linked suspects.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation Bakovia
- **Arrests:** 5
- **Investigative support:** Europol, FBI, Dutch National Police, McAfee
- **Modus operandi:** ransomware-as-a-service affiliate distribution through spammed invoice archives
- **Jurisdictional emphasis:** Romania with U.S. linkage for Cerber infections

## Relevance to IC

This source clarifies that the defendants were distributors rather than core malware authors, which materially improves the operation page's description of the criminal role and business model.
