# Justice Department Disrupts Covert Russian Government-Sponsored Foreign Malign Influence Operation Targeting Audiences in the United States and Elsewhere

**Date:** September 4, 2024
**Source:** U.S. Department of Justice, Office of Public Affairs

## Headline Facts (verified)

- **32 internet domains seized** by the U.S. Department of Justice — used by the Russian government to conduct covert foreign malign influence campaigns ("Doppelganger") targeting U.S. and foreign audiences, including with respect to the 2024 U.S. Presidential Election.
- **Domain seizure venue:** Eastern District of Pennsylvania (USAO EDPA) and FBI Philadelphia Field Office.
- **Companion criminal case:** Two-count indictment unsealed in the **Southern District of New York** charging two employees of RT (Russia Today) — **Konstantin Kalashnikov** and **Elena Mikhaylovna Afanasyeva** — with (1) conspiracy to violate the Foreign Agents Registration Act (FARA) and (2) conspiracy to commit money laundering. The indictment alleges a nearly $10 million scheme to covertly fund and direct a Tennessee-based online content company (publicly identified as **Tenet Media**) to publish English-language content amplifying Russian government messaging.
- **Parallel Treasury action (same day):** OFAC designated **10 individuals and 2 entities** in connection with Russian malign influence efforts targeting the 2024 election.

## Russian Entities Identified (Doppelganger network)

| Entity | Role |
|---|---|
| Social Design Agency (SDA) | Lead Russian PR/influence vendor running cybersquatted domains |
| Structura National Technology (Structura) | Russian technology contractor supporting SDA |
| ANO Dialog | Russian autonomous non-profit organization producing influence content |

Operating allegedly under the direction of **Sergei Vladilenovich Kiriyenko**, First Deputy Chief of Staff to President Vladimir Putin, and other members of the Presidential Executive Office.

## Methods (Doppelganger)

- Cybersquatted domains mimicking legitimate U.S. and Western news outlets (e.g., **washingtonpost.pm** spoofing washingtonpost.com).
- Unique faux media brands (e.g., "Recent Reliable News").
- Worldwide network of paid "influencers" amplifying content.
- Paid social-media advertisements (some AI-generated).
- Fake U.S./Western persona accounts posting comments on social platforms with links to the cybersquatted domains.

## Charged Conduct (RT / Tenet Media indictment)

- Defendants: **Konstantin Kalashnikov** (RT digital media projects manager) and **Elena Mikhaylovna Afanasyeva** (RT Digital Media Projects Department).
- Funded a Tennessee-based online content creation company (Tenet Media) with approximately **$9.7 million** routed through shell entities.
- Directed the company to publish English-language video content amplifying Russian government talking points (e.g., on Ukraine, U.S. domestic divisions).
- Charges: 18 U.S.C. § 371 conspiracy to violate FARA; 18 U.S.C. § 1956(h) conspiracy to commit money laundering.

## Notes on IC Relevance

This release sits at the **borderline of IC-scope inclusion** for this wiki. It is a US-led domestic enforcement action with no named foreign LE cooperation. It is preserved in the wiki because:

1. It is a tier-1 primary source on a foreign state-sponsored cyber-enabled operation.
2. The defendants are foreign nationals (Russian RT employees) currently fugitive in Russia — extradition is functionally blocked, which itself illustrates IC limits.
3. It pairs with parallel Treasury OFAC sanctions, which represents the U.S.'s preferred unilateral substitute for cooperation when MLAT pathways are unavailable with the requested state.
4. The cybersquatted domains spoofed European and other foreign-language outlets, implying downstream registrar interactions across multiple ccTLDs, even if not explicitly credited.

The IC analysis pages should reflect that **this is an instance of "no-cooperation cyber enforcement"** — a comparison case for ops where bilateral cooperation succeeded.
