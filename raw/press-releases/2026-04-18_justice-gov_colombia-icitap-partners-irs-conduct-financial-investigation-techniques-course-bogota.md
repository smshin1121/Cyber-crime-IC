Colombia: ICITAP Partners With The IRS To Conduct Financial Investigation Techniques Course In Bogota, Colombia

Blog Post

Friday, January 30, 2026

Share

X

LinkedIn

Email

From January 26 to 30, ICITAP-Colombia, in partnership with the Internal Revenue Service Criminal Investigation Division, conducted a Financial Investigation Techniques course for 30 investigators from the Colombian National Police and the Technical Investigation Corps of the Attorney General’s Office. Designed for officers assigned to financial crime and complex investigations, the training addressed a key challenge in modern law enforcement: the use of sophisticated financial schemes by criminal organizations to move, conceal, and legitimize illicit proceeds. Using a practical, case-based methodology, instructors guided participants through the steps of a financial investigation, including asset reconstruction, money flow analysis, identification of unreported income, and application of indirect methods of proof, equipping investigators with skills directly applicable to active cases to strengthen evidentiary quality and support effective prosecutions. By targeting the financial foundations of criminal networks, the course enhanced participants’ ability to dismantle transnational criminal organizations and disrupt their operations at a structural level. The joint participation of investigators from both agencies reinforced interinstitutional coordination, promoting a unified approach to financial crime investigations and fostering long-term institutional capacity. This effort supports U.S. strategic objectives by strengthening partner-nation law enforcement capabilities, protecting the integrity of financial systems, and preventing illicit proceeds from fueling broader criminal activity that can affect the United States. Through targeted training, sustained mentorship, and international cooperation, ICITAP advances shared security interests, builds more resilient institutions, and enhances the ability of partner nations to confront complex criminal threats proactively. The initiative was funded and supported by the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL), reflecting a continued investment in professionalizing law enforcement and reinforcing mechanisms to combat financial crime at its source while safeguarding regional and U.S. national security interests.
