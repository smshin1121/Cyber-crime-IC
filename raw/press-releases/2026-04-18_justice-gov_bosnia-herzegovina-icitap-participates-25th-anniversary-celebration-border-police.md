Bosnia-Herzegovina: ICITAP Participates In 25th Anniversary Celebration For Border Police

Blog Post

Wednesday, June 11, 2025

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

On June 11, at the invitation of the Bosnia & Herzegovina (BiH) Border Police, ICITAP’s Balkans Regional Counterterrorism Cell accompanied the U.S. Embassy‑Sarajevo Chargé d’Affaires (CDA) to participate in the 25th anniversary celebration of the BiH Border Police, which was established in 2000 and has since evolved into a modern, professional state‑level agency responsible for safeguarding the country’s extensive international borders and contributing to regional stability. The ceremony highlighted the long‑standing partnership between the United States and BiH in strengthening border security, law enforcement capacity, and professional development; in recognition of this support, the CDA was presented with the prestigious BiH Golden Seal Award for the U.S. government’s outstanding training, logistical assistance, and sustained cooperation with the Border Police over many years. The event drew participation from diplomatic missions including Croatia, Montenegro, Italy, the European Union, and the Office of Security Cooperation Europe, reflecting broad international engagement in enhancing BiH’s border management. The Border Police play a key role in addressing irregular migration, human trafficking, organized crime, and related security challenges, working alongside international partners to implement best practices and integrated border management strategies. This engagement directly supports Executive Order 14161’s focus on protecting the United States from foreign terrorist and security threats by building partner capacity to counter transnational threats at the source, and aligns with broader U.S. directives to strengthen global counterterrorism cooperation and security partnerships.
