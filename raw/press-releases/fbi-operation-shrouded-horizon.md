### Key Findings
- On 15 July 2015, the FBI's Pittsburgh Field Office (Western District of Pennsylvania) unsealed indictments and coordinated a global takedown of the Darkode cybercrime forum — codenamed Operation Shrouded Horizon — after an 18-month undercover investigation
- International scope: 20 countries participated in the action day, described by the FBI as 'the largest-ever coordinated law enforcement effort directed at an online cyber criminal forum'. Law enforcement actions resulted in searches, arrests or equipment seizures against ~70 individuals globally
- US charges: 12 individuals indicted on charges including computer fraud conspiracy, wire fraud conspiracy, money laundering conspiracy, spamming, identity theft, racketeering, bank fraud and access device fraud
- Darkode's profile: An invitation-only forum with 250-300 vetted members functioning as a premier English-language marketplace for malware, botnets, stolen PII, credit card data, hacked server credentials and custom exploit tools
- Cooperation architecture: FBI lead, Europol EC3 coordination, NCFTA (National Cyber-Forensics & Training Alliance) private-sector support. The 18-month undercover infiltration is the rare publicly disclosed example of an FBI operation that gained access to an invite-only forum through 'undisclosed means'
- The operation is frequently cited as a benchmark for multi-country cybercrime forum takedowns and provided a template later used in Alphabay, Hansa, RaidForums and BreachForums operations
