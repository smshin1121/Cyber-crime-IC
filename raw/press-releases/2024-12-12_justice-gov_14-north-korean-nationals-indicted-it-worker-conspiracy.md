A federal arrest warrant for each defendant was issued in the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Missouri, Eastern Division (St. Louis), in December 2024. All 14 defendants remain fugitives believed to be located in or operating on behalf of the Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK). The FBI has published Wanted notices for the 14 defendants under the "DPRK IT WORKERS" listing on fbi.gov/wanted/cyber.

## Charges

All 14 defendants are charged with:
- Conspiracy to violate the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA)
- Conspiracy to commit wire fraud
- Conspiracy to commit money laundering
- Conspiracy to commit identity theft

Eight of the 14 defendants are additionally charged with aggravated identity theft.

## Period of Conduct

Approximately April 2017 through approximately March 2023.

## Scheme Summary

The 14 defendants are alleged to have conspired to generate revenue for the DPRK regime — in violation of U.S. and international sanctions — by deploying overseas as remote IT workers under two DPRK-controlled front companies:

- **Yanbian Silverstar Network Technology Co., Ltd.** — based in Yanji, Jilin Province, People's Republic of China.
- **Volasys Silver Star** — based in Vladivostok, Russian Federation.

The IT workers used the stolen, borrowed, and purchased identities of hundreds of U.S. persons (and other persons), as well as AI-generated credentials and front companies, to conceal their North Korean identities and obtain remote IT employment with U.S. and other businesses worldwide. The State Department's Rewards for Justice listing reports that the 14 defendants worked alongside approximately 130 DPRK IT workers in total. The scheme generated at least USD 88 million in illicit revenue for the DPRK regime over approximately six years.

In some cases, after being terminated, the workers extorted their U.S. employer victims by threatening to release stolen sensitive data unless paid additional sums.

## Seizures and Concurrent Actions

- **USD 2.26 million** seized from bank accounts associated with the scheme (multiple court-authorized seizure orders).
- **29 internet domains** used in the scheme seized in two waves: 17 domains seized in October 2023, plus 12 additional domains seized in May 2024.
- **CyberScoop reports** an additional USD 764,800 seized via two court-authorized seizure orders unsealed concurrently with the indictment unsealing on December 12, 2024.

## State Department / Rewards for Justice (concurrent action)

The U.S. Department of State's **Rewards for Justice** program — administered by the **Diplomatic Security Service (DSS)** — announced concurrently with the DOJ unsealing a reward of **up to USD 5 million** for information leading to the disruption of the financial mechanisms supporting the DPRK regime, including (a) information on Yanbian Silverstar Network Technology Co., Ltd., (b) information on Volasys Silver Star, and (c) information on the 14 named DPRK nationals. The reward listing also references the IT-worker network's connection to DPRK weapons of mass destruction (WMD) program funding.
