North Macedonia: With ICITAP Support, Border Police Leadership Strengthens Regional Fight Against Human Trafficking and Migrant Smuggling At Western Balkans Task Force Meeting

Blog Post

Tuesday, April 8, 2025

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

On April 7 and 8, the Ministry of Interior State Secretary of North Macedonia, serving as National Coordinator for Combating Human Trafficking, led the North Macedonian delegation—including representatives from the ICITAP‑supported Border Police and Trafficking in Persons (TIP) Unit—at the annual Western Balkans Task Force meeting in Budva, Montenegro. Organized by the International Organization for Migration (IOM) under the regional project “Joint Actions of the Western Balkans Countries against Migrant Smuggling and Trafficking (WBJAST),” and funded by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Denmark, the event brought together regional partners to strengthen cooperation in suppressing human trafficking and migrant smuggling. Delegates from North Macedonia actively engaged in discussions on evolving challenges and trends along Western Balkan migrant smuggling routes at international, regional, and European Union levels, and contributed substantive proposals for coordinated, effective solutions to address these serious and organized crimes. Participants emphasized the importance of information sharing, joint operational frameworks, and capacity‑building to disrupt criminal networks that exploit migration routes and traffic victims across borders. The meeting reinforced regional commitments to integrated border management, victim protection, and adherence to international legal standards. This activity supports Executive Order 14165, “Securing Our Borders,” and Executive Order 14161, “Protecting the United States from Foreign Terrorists and Other National Security and Public Safety Threats,” by enhancing partner capacity to counter transnational threats at their source. In North Macedonia, ICITAP implements these efforts in coordination with the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL) and the U.S. Embassy in Skopje, strengthening institutional response to trafficking and smuggling through sustained training, mentoring, and strategic cooperation.
