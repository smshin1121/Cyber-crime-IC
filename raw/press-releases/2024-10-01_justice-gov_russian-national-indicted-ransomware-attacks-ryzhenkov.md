# Russian National Indicted for Series of Ransomware Attacks

**Date:** Tuesday, October 1, 2024
**Issuer:** U.S. Department of Justice — Office of Public Affairs (OPA)
**Permalink:** https://www.justice.gov/archives/opa/pr/russian-national-indicted-series-ransomware-attacks
**Alternate USAO permalink:** https://www.justice.gov/usao-ndtx/pr/russian-national-indicted-series-ransomware-attacks

## Subtitle

Defendant Was Second-in-Command of Russia-Based Cybercriminal Organization "Evil Corp", Which Is Also Believed to Be Responsible for Distributing the Dridex Malware

## Body Summary

The U.S. Department of Justice on October 1, 2024 unsealed an indictment charging Russian national **Aleksandr Viktorovich Ryzhenkov** with using the **BitPaymer** ransomware variant to attack numerous victims in Texas and throughout the United States and hold their sensitive data for ransom.

According to the indictment, beginning in at least June 2017, Ryzhenkov allegedly gained unauthorized access to information stored on victims' computer networks. Ryzhenkov and his conspirators then allegedly deployed the strain of ransomware known as BitPaymer to encrypt the files of victim companies, rendering them inaccessible. An electronic note left on victims' systems contained a ransom demand and instructions on how to contact the attackers to begin ransom negotiations.

The indictment further alleges that Ryzhenkov and others used a variety of methods to intrude into computer systems, including phishing campaigns, malware, and exploiting vulnerabilities in computer hardware and software.

## Charges

- Multiple violations of the **Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)**, 18 U.S.C. § 1030
- **Conspiring to commit money laundering**, 18 U.S.C. § 1956(h)

## Defendant

- **Aleksandr Viktorovich Ryzhenkov** — Russian national, alleged to be the second-in-command at the Russia-based cybercriminal organization **Evil Corp**, which is also believed to be responsible for distributing the **Dridex** malware. Believed to remain in Russia.

## Venue

- U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Texas — indictment unsealed October 1, 2024.

## Coordination with OFAC and Five-Eyes Partners

The release explicitly notes that in coordination with the unsealing of the indictment, the **U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)** announced that Ryzhenkov, along with six other Evil Corp related persons, was added to the Specially Designated Nationals and Blocked Persons (SDN) List.

The DOJ release further references that the **United Kingdom's National Crime Agency (NCA)** reported Ryzhenkov is also a suspected affiliate of **LockBit**, a separate ransomware group disrupted by the FBI-led Operation Cronos in February 2024.

The action was timed with day 2 of the **U.S.-hosted Counter Ransomware Initiative (CRI) summit**, attended by over 50 countries; concurrent same-day designations were made by the **UK FCDO** (15 additional persons) and **Australia DFAT** (3 additional persons), constituting the tri-lateral US-UK-AU cyber-sanctions action documented in OFAC press release JY-2623.

## Investigating and Prosecuting Authorities
