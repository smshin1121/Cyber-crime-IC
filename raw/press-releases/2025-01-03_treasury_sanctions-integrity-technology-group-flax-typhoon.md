## Body (reconstructed from secondary mirrors)

The Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanctioned **Integrity Technology Group, Incorporated** (Integrity Tech), a Beijing-based cybersecurity company, for its involvement in computer intrusion incidents targeting U.S. victims. These activities have been attributed to **Flax Typhoon**, a Chinese state-sponsored malicious cyber group operational since at least 2021, with particular focus on U.S. critical infrastructure sectors.

Flax Typhoon has compromised computer networks in **North America, Europe, Africa, and across Asia, with a particular focus on Taiwan**. Flax Typhoon exploits publicly known vulnerabilities to gain initial access to victims' computers and then leverages legitimate remote access software to maintain persistent control over their network.

Between **summer 2022 and fall 2023**, Flax Typhoon hackers used infrastructure tied to Integrity Tech during their computer network exploitation activities against multiple victims, regularly transmitting and receiving information through Integrity Tech's systems. Reporting by independent telemetry sources (Lumen / Black Lotus Labs) describes the underlying botnet (publicly named **"Raptor Train"**) as comprising more than **260,000 internet-connected devices** at peak — including IP cameras, network-attached storage devices, and SOHO routers.

On **September 18, 2024**, the **Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)**, in coordination with the **Cyber National Mission Force (CNMF)**, the **National Security Agency (NSA)**, and **Five Eye partners**, published a joint cybersecurity advisory highlighting Flax Typhoon's tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) as well as **Integrity Tech's role in supporting** Flax Typhoon's malicious cyber activities. The same-day DOJ announcement of the court-authorized Raptor Train botnet disruption identified the FBI's assessment that Integrity Technology Group was the entity behind Flax Typhoon.

### Quote — Bradley T. Smith, Acting Under Secretary of the Treasury for Terrorism and Financial Intelligence

> "The Treasury Department will not hesitate to hold malicious cyber actors and their enablers accountable for their actions. The United States will use all available tools…"

### Sanctions Authority and Legal Basis

The designation was issued under:

- **Executive Order 13694** ("Blocking the Property of Certain Persons Engaging in Significant Malicious Cyber-Enabled Activities", April 1, 2015)
- **as amended by Executive Order 13757** (December 28, 2016)

These authorities permit OFAC to designate persons (including entities) determined to be responsible for or complicit in cyber-enabled activities that pose a significant threat to the national security, foreign policy, or economic health or financial stability of the United States.

### Sanctions Effects

- All property and interests in property of Integrity Tech that are in the United States or in the possession or control of U.S. persons are **blocked** and must be reported to OFAC.
- Any entities owned, directly or indirectly, 50% or more by Integrity Tech are also blocked.
- U.S. persons are generally prohibited from engaging in any transactions involving Integrity Tech unless authorized by an OFAC general or specific license, or otherwise exempt.
- Foreign financial institutions that knowingly facilitate significant transactions with Integrity Tech may be subject to U.S. correspondent or payable-through account sanctions.

### Coordinated Partner Sanctions

> [!warning] No parallel partner-state sanctions documented in this release
> The Treasury press release does **not** document any coordinated UK / Australia / Canada / New Zealand parallel sanctions designations issued the same day or against the same entity. The "Five Eye partners" reference applies only to the **September 18, 2024 joint cybersecurity advisory** (companion to the DOJ Raptor Train takedown), not to the January 3, 2025 OFAC designation. The OFAC sanction is a **U.S. unilateral targeted designation**.
