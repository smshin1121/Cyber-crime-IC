# NSW teen charged for multiple hoax calls to major retail, education institutions in United States

**13 January 2026, 7:11am AEDT — Australian Federal Police (joint media release with United States Federal Bureau of Investigation)**

A juvenile from regional New South Wales has been charged by the Australian Federal Police (AFP) with making multiple hoax "swatting" reports to emergency services falsely claiming mass shootings were taking place at major retail and educational institutions in the United States.

The AFP launched its investigation in November 2025 after the United States Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) provided information to the AFP suggesting an offender located in Australia was responsible for making a series of hoax calls to emergency services in the United States.

AFP investigators identified the alleged offender as a juvenile from regional New South Wales, who allegedly made 12 false reports of imminent mass shootings between October 2025 and January 2026 against major retail and educational institutions across the United States.

Each call allegedly resulted in armed police deployment, building lockdowns and emergency evacuations, causing widespread alarm and turmoil to thousands of people, businesses and services in the United States.

The teenager was arrested on 9 January 2026 at a property in regional NSW by AFP officers with assistance from NSW Police. During a search warrant executed at the property, AFP investigators allegedly located and seized one prohibited firearm, ammunition, multiple electronic devices, and digital communications relevant to the swatting offences.

The juvenile has been charged with:
- 12 counts of using a telecommunications network with intention to cause harm or menace contrary to section 474.14(1) of the Commonwealth Criminal Code (maximum penalty 5 years' imprisonment)
- 1 count of unauthorised possession of a prohibited firearm contrary to section 7(1) of the Firearms Act 1996 (NSW) (maximum penalty 14 years' imprisonment)

The juvenile appeared before a NSW Children's Court on 10 January 2026 and was remanded in custody.

## Quotes

**AFP Commander Cybercrime Operations Graeme Marshall:**
"Swatting is not a prank — it is a malicious and dangerous crime that puts lives at risk. The targets of these calls — innocent staff, children at school, customers shopping — were terrified, and emergency responders were diverted from genuine calls. The AFP will continue to work closely with international law enforcement partners, particularly Five Eyes partners, to target members of the decentralised online crime network."

**FBI Assistant Director Jason A. Kaplan, Criminal Investigative Division:**
"The FBI views swatting as a dangerous and disruptive crime that endangers lives and drains critical emergency resources. This case demonstrates that anonymity online is an illusion, and we are committed to working with the AFP, our international partners, and private sector partners to identify and hold accountable those who exploit technology to cause harm to communities."

## Investigation context

The AFP investigation forms part of Taskforce Pompilid, the AFP's specialist taskforce established in October 2025 to identify, disrupt and dismantle decentralised online crime networks responsible for sadistic exploitation of youth, swatting, cyberattacks on communication companies, and violent extremist material production. The taskforce works closely with the FBI and other Five Eyes Law Enforcement Group partners.
