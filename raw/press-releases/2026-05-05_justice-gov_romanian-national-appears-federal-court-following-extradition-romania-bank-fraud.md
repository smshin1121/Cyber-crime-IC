## Key facts (from tier-1 DOJ press release as quoted by tier-2 outlets)

- **Defendant**: Gavril Sandu, 53, Romanian national.
- **Charges**: One count of conspiracy to commit bank fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1349) and one count of bank fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1344). Indictment returned by federal grand jury in Charlotte, North Carolina on **2017-11-14**.
- **Underlying conduct**: Between **May 2009 and October 2010**, Sandu and co-conspirators hacked small-business VoIP (Voice-over-IP) systems in the United States and used the compromised systems to conduct mass automated "vishing" (voice-phishing) campaigns against U.S. bank customers. Victims were tricked into surrendering debit card numbers and PINs. Sandu's specific role: cloned victims' card data onto fake physical cards with magnetic stripes, withdrew cash from victims' accounts at ATMs as a money mule, retained a share, and remitted the rest to the hacker ring.
- **Arrest**: Sandu was arrested in **Romania on 2026-01-09** by Romanian authorities pursuant to a U.S. provisional arrest request.
- **Extradition**: Sandu was extradited from Romania to the United States on **2026-04-30**.
- **First federal court appearance**: U.S. District Court for the Western District of North Carolina, Charlotte, the Monday following extradition arrival (≈ 2026-05-05). Sandu was remanded into custody.
- **Maximum penalty**: 30 years in federal prison (combined statutory max for conspiracy + substantive bank fraud counts).
