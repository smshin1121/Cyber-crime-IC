# 326 Persons Arrested And 119 Investigated In Cross-Border Operation Targeting Online Child Sexual Exploitation Activities

Publisher: Singapore Police Force (SPF)
Publication date: 28 April 2026
URL: https://www.police.gov.sg/media-hub/news/2026/04/20260428_326_persons_arrested_and_119_investigated_in_cross_border_operation

## Summary as captured

The Singapore Police Force (SPF), collaborating with law enforcement agencies from Hong Kong, Japan, South Korea, Malaysia, Thailand, and Brunei, arrested 326 individuals between 23 March and 17 April 2026 in a coordinated cross-border operation against online child sexual exploitation (OCSE). An additional 119 persons are assisting with investigations, bringing the total number of subjects to 445 (430 men, 15 women, ages 12-72).

## Participating jurisdictions and agencies (seven)

- Singapore Police Force (SPF) - lead, with operational lead on the Singapore side held by the Specialised Crime Branch, Criminal Investigation Department (CID)
- Hong Kong Police Force (HKPF)
- National Police Agency of Japan (JNPA / 警察庁)
- Korean National Police Agency (KNPA)
- Royal Malaysia Police (RMP)
- Royal Thai Police (RTP)
- Royal Brunei Police Force (RBPF)

## Operation scale

- Operation period: 23 March 2026 - 17 April 2026 (approximately four weeks)
- Locations raided: 382 locations across the seven participating countries
- Total subjects: 445 (326 arrested + 119 assisting with investigations)
- Demographics: 430 men, 15 women, ages 12-72
- Singapore figures: 11 arrested (men aged 22-44), 16 under investigation

## Items seized (across all jurisdictions)

- 116 computers
- 340 handphones
- 25 tablets
- 140 storage devices
- 16 routers

## Offences targeted

- Production and distribution of child sexual abuse materials (CSAM)
- Possession of CSAM
- Sexual communication with minors
- Commercial sexual exploitation of children
- Threatening to distribute intimate recordings (intimate image abuse)
- Obscene material transmission / distribution

## Notable Singapore-side cases mentioned

- Two individuals aged 26 and 28 allegedly making cross-border payments for obscene materials via Telegram.
- A foreign offender allegedly sexually exploiting two victims through an online platform; materials suggesting further potential victims were discovered.
- Eight Singapore individuals reportedly accessed CSAM via messaging platforms and peer-to-peer services; cross-border financial channels facilitated offences.

## Senior-officer statement

Deputy Commissioner Zhang Weihan (SPF) is quoted in the release: "By bringing together law enforcement efforts and private sector capabilities, we can intervene swiftly to identify and arrest offenders."

## IC scope

Multi-country (>= 7 jurisdictions). Singapore-led, with parallel domestic enforcement under coordinated operational windows in Hong Kong, Japan, South Korea, Malaysia, Thailand, and Brunei. Cooperation pattern is consistent with the prior 2025 SPF-led six-jurisdiction OCSE crackdown ([[operation-cyber-guardian]] in this wiki); the 2026 edition adds Brunei as a seventh participant.

## Notes for ingest

- This press release does not give the operation an umbrella name in the released text, but the cooperation structure (shared operational window, multi-jurisdiction simultaneous enforcement, SPF coordination, OCSE focus) closely mirrors the prior year's operation.
- Publicly disclosed arrest figures by partner jurisdiction are not broken out in this SPF release; the SPF release reports the aggregate (326 + 119) and the Singapore subset (11 + 16).
- No formal MLAT, treaty article, or INTERPOL Notice is named in the release text. Cooperation appears to be police-to-police coordinated enforcement, supplemented by private-sector technical cooperation referenced in the senior-officer statement.
