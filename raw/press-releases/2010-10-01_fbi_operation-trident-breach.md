## Summary-Only Archive

The FBI announced that Operation Trident Breach disrupted a large international organized cybercrime operation using the Zeus botnet to steal online-banking credentials and move stolen funds through money mules.

The release states that the investigation began in May 2009 after FBI agents in Omaha identified ACH batch payments to 46 separate U.S. bank accounts. It records cooperation with partners in the Netherlands, Ukraine, and the United Kingdom, attempted theft of USD 220 million, actual losses of USD 70 million, and the participation of the FBI, New York Money Mule Working Group, Newark Cyber Crime Task Force, Omaha Cyber Crime Task Force, Netherlands Police Agency, Security Service of Ukraine, and Metropolitan Police Service.

The release reports that on 30 September 2010 the SBU detained five key subjects and that about 50 SBU officers executed eight search warrants.
