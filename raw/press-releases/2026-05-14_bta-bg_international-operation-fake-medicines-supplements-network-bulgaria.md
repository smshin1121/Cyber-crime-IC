# International Operation Targeting Network Selling Fake Medicines and Supplements Ongoing in Bulgaria

**URL:** https://www.bta.bg/en/news/bulgaria/1126139-international-operation-targeting-network-selling-fake-medicines-and-supplements

**Byline:** BTA (Bulgarian News Agency — Bulgaria's official state news agency)

**Date:** May 14, 2026, 18:08 (reporting); operation action day May 12, 2026

---

## Article Body (verbatim summary)

An international law enforcement operation targeting a criminal group distributing dietary supplements falsely marketed as medicines was conducted across 15 countries, with Bulgaria serving as a central focus of the action.

The Sofia City Prosecution Office (SCPO) led the Bulgarian component of the effort, which involved prosecutors and law enforcement from Bulgaria, Romania, Hungary, Poland, Moldova, Cyprus, Czechia, Estonia, Greece, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Slovakia, Slovenia, and Spain. Eurojust and Europol provided assistance.

A joint investigation team (JIT) was established under the agreement of the supervising prosecutor between Romania, Poland, Hungary, Moldova, and Bulgaria.

## Bulgarian Action Day

On May 12, 2026, Bulgarian authorities conducted over 30 searches across homes, offices, and vehicles. Participating Bulgarian investigators came from:

- Sofia City Prosecution Office (SCPO) — investigation department
- Cybercrime Directorate at the General Directorate for Combating Organized Crime (GDCOC)
- Sofia Directorate of Internal Affairs

Numerous witnesses were interviewed during the Bulgarian phase of the action.

## Criminal Activity

The network reportedly generated "EUR 240 million through criminal activities." Products were advertised using false claims about treating serious illnesses, including conditions such as cancer, diabetes, and other incurable or chronic diseases. Victims included citizens of EU member states.

## Key Discovery and Cross-Border Findings

Romanian authorities identified a central warehouse in Bulgaria during the joint investigation. In Romania, "250,000 boxes of tablets and bottles containing various substances" were seized, with products priced at approximately "EUR 35 per item." A total of 196 websites promoting the fraudulent products were blocked in Romania.

The investigation began after a former Bulgarian health minister reported that his name and image were being misused in advertisements for products of unclear origin.

## EU Coordination

- Eurojust: hosted the JIT and coordination meetings; coordinated the action day from its headquarters
- Europol: deployed a Virtual Command Post (VCP) on the action day and dispatched experts to assist intervening authorities on the spot
