Adeyemi Odufuye ("Micky," "Yemi," "Bawz," "Jefe"), a 31-year-old Nigerian citizen, pleaded guilty before Chief U.S. District Judge Janet C. Hall in New Haven to one count of conspiracy to commit wire fraud and one count of aggravated identity theft, stemming from his role in a multimillion-dollar business e-mail compromise (BEC) scheme that targeted CEOs, CFOs, and controllers at U.S. businesses. Odufuye was arrested in the United Kingdom on December 19, 2016, where he was a student at Sheffield Hallam University, and was extradited to the U.S. to face charges. The FBI identified 36 wire confirmations totaling more than $1.6 million that Odufuye and conspirators received between September 2015 and May 2016, including more than $500,000 in transfers from a Connecticut victim company in Torrington.
