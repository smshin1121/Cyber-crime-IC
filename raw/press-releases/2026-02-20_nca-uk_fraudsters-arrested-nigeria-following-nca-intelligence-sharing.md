# Fraudsters arrested in Nigeria following NCA intelligence sharing

**Source: UK National Crime Agency, 2026-02-20.**

## Operation Overview

Seven men were arrested in Agbor, Nigeria, on 2026-01-13 following intelligence sharing from the UK National Crime Agency (NCA) to the Nigerian Police, in coordination with Meta Platforms. Police seized 26 phones, 42 SIM cards, and one laptop in the operation. The cohort allegedly operated online investment scams, cryptocurrency fraud, and phishing attacks targeting UK residents and US persons resident in the UK.

## Cooperation Pattern

The NCA describes the operation as a partnership between the NCA, Meta, and the Nigerian Police. The Nigerian Police's National Cybercrime Center is named as the operational counterpart in the parallel Meta announcement. Meta's role was to provide platform-level account-removal and intelligence-sharing support.

## Direct Quote

> "These arrests were made possible thanks to our close partnership working with Meta" — Nick Sharp, Deputy Director of Fraud at the National Crime Agency, on the collective action against "fraudsters operating overseas."

## Notes on Victim Scope

The cited NCA release explicitly notes that "It is not known at this stage how many UK victims there were." The Meta announcement co-published on the same date frames the targets as British residents and American persons resident in the UK, consistent with NCA's UK-victim focus.

## Linkage with Meta Announcement (same date)

The Meta blog post on 2026-02-20 — "Meta Works With the UK National Crime Agency and the Nigerian Police Force to Disrupt Alleged Online Scams Centre in Nigeria" — corroborates the same arrests, seizures, and operation date. Meta's Global Head of Counter Fraud Nathaniel Gleicher is named as Meta's spokesperson; the Nigerian Police National Cybercrime Centre is named as the operational counterpart on the Nigerian side. The methodology described by Meta is the use of fake social-media accounts impersonating cryptocurrency traders, plus fraudulent Facebook groups featuring fabricated testimonials, to target individuals engaging with legitimate investment platforms.
