## Summary-Only Archive

The Argentine Ministry of Security (Ministerio de Seguridad de la Nación, via the Policía Federal Argentina sub-section of argentina.gob.ar) announced on 24 September 2024 the dismantlement of an international cybercriminal network operating the "iServer" phishing-as-a-service platform, in a coordinated action named **Operación KAERB** ("Break" reversed).

The release reports:

- 17 arrests across 6 countries: Spain, Argentina, Colombia, Chile, Ecuador, and Peru.
- 28 home searches conducted starting 10 September 2024; 921 electronic devices seized.
- The platform had operated for at least 5 years, had over 2,000 registered users (operator-side cybercriminals known as "desbloqueadores"), and an estimated 483,000 victims globally — broken down as: Chile 77,000; Colombia 70,000; Ecuador 42,000; Peru 41,500; Spain 30,000; Argentina 29,000; ~193,500 in other countries.
- iServer generated phishing messages that tricked theft victims into surrendering credentials needed to unlock stolen high-end mobile phones, enabling onward fraud, extortion, unauthorized access, and data theft.
- The administrator (named in the release as a citizen residing in Santa Fe Province, Argentina) sat at the apex of the criminal organization and supplied technical support to subscriber "desbloqueadores".
- Coordination was provided by **EUROPOL** (Agencia de la Unión Europea para la Cooperación Policial), **AMERIPOL** (Comunidad de Policías de América), and **PAcCTO 2.0** (Programa de Asistencia Contra el Crimen Transnacional Organizado, an EU-funded LATAM technical-assistance programme), with private-sector support from **Group-IB**.
- In Argentina, 5 individuals were arrested; 12 raids were authorized, distributed across the Autonomous City of Buenos Aires (5), Santa Fe (3), Córdoba (3), and Jujuy (1).
- Press conference participants named in the release: Secretary of Security Alejandra Monteoliva; Spanish Counsellor Francisco Arenas Morales; Comisario Gustavo Cabada (División Ciberpatrullaje, Policía Federal Argentina); and Comandante General Claudio Miguel Brilloni (Gendarmería Nacional Argentina).
- Argentine victims are encouraged to report via line 134, the official complaints website, or by email, providing the IMEI of the stolen device.
