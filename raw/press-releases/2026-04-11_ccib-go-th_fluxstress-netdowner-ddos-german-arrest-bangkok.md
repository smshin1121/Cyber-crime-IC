## Source content (translated summary from CCIB own-domain release, cross-checked against Thairath, Bangkok Post, Pattaya Mail, ChiangRai Times, ThaiExaminer, MGR Online coverage)

On 11 April 2026 (B.E. 2569), officers of the Royal Thai Police Cyber Crime Investigation Bureau (CCIB / กองบัญชาการตำรวจสืบสวนสอบสวนอาชญากรรมทางเทคโนโลยี — บช.สอท.) together with the Immigration Bureau Investigation Division arrested NOAH CHRISTOPHER, a 27-year-old (some reports: 26-year-old) German national, at a luxury condominium on Thonglor Soi 25, Watthana District, Bangkok.

The arrest was executed pursuant to an INTERPOL Red Notice and was made on the basis of 74 outstanding arrest warrants issued by German authorities and the European Union. The CCIB statement identifies the cooperation partners as follows:

> "ตำรวจไซเบอร์ ร่วมกับสำนักงานตรวจคนเข้าเมือง โดยการประสานข้อมูลของหน่วยงานบังคับใช้กฎหมายของประเทศเยอรมนีและสำนักเลขาธิการตำรวจสากล (INTERPOL) เข้าจับกุมตัวนาย Noah Christopher สัญชาติเยอรมัน ผู้ต้องหาตามหมายแดงอินเตอร์โพลและหมายจับ 74 หมายของทางการเยอรมัน"
> (Cyber Police, together with the Immigration Bureau, by coordinating intelligence with the law-enforcement agencies of the Federal Republic of Germany and the INTERPOL General Secretariat, arrested Mr Noah Christopher, German national, the suspect under an INTERPOL Red Notice and 74 German arrest warrants.)

Named participants in the operation (per official release + Bangkok Post / Thairath / Pattaya Mail coverage of CCIB press conference):

**Thai side:**
- Royal Thai Police Cyber Crime Investigation Bureau (CCIB / บช.สอท.) — lead Thai agency
- Immigration Bureau (สำนักงานตรวจคนเข้าเมือง, สตม.) — Investigation Division
  - Commander Phanop Warathanachakun
  - Deputy Commanders Ratchot Chotikun and Chitdecha Songhong
  - Superintendent Pisit Sri-on
- International Criminal Investigation Bureau (Division 2)
- Royal Thai Police Division 3 (Legal Proceedings)

**Foreign LE counterparts explicitly named in the release:**
- German Federal Police / law-enforcement authorities of the Federal Republic of Germany (per CCIB: "หน่วยงานบังคับใช้กฎหมายของประเทศเยอรมนี"; per Bangkok Post / Thai Examiner: "German Federal Security Service / German federal authorities") — issued the 74 arrest warrants and provided intelligence
- INTERPOL General Secretariat — issued the Red Notice and acted as international coordination channel
- European Union authorities — also issued warrants (per Thai Examiner)

## Alleged criminal conduct

- The suspect is identified as the developer and one of the principal operators of two DDoS-for-hire (DDoS-as-a-Service / "stresser/booter") platforms — **Fluxstress** and **Netdowner** (also rendered "Neldowner" in some translations)
- Platforms were active from approximately 2021 through 2025
- Platforms accepted cryptocurrency payments and provided customers worldwide with the ability to launch Distributed Denial-of-Service attacks against targets of their choosing — explicitly marketed as "rented attack services on organisational servers at a global level"
- The CCIB further attributes to the suspect the development of ransomware and a broader cybercrime-as-a-service offering
- German charges include 74 warrants spanning hacking, extortion, ransomware, cryptocurrency-payment cybercrime
- The suspect is reported to have moved between the United Arab Emirates, China and Thailand before being located and arrested
- Immigration revoked his Thai visa on 9 April 2026, two days prior to the arrest

## Seizures during the raid (per CCIB statement)

- 1 computer (desktop/laptop)
- 3 iPads
- 1 tablet device
- 3 mobile phones
- 1 hardware cryptocurrency wallet
- Various other electronic devices and documentation

## Next steps (per CCIB)

The suspect is being held by Thai authorities pending finalisation of extradition paperwork to return him to Germany to face the 74 outstanding warrants. Investigation continues; digital forensic analysis of the seized electronic devices ongoing.
