This record preserves source metadata and a digest for the Eurojust public release on the Austria-Latvia-Estonia online scams JIT enforcement action of October 2025.

## Source Digest

- Title: Decisive action against various online scams in Austria and Latvia: seven arrests
- Publisher: Eurojust (European Union Agency for Criminal Justice Cooperation)
- Source type: press-release
- Publication date: 2025-10-17
- URL: https://www.eurojust.europa.eu/news/decisive-action-against-various-online-scams-austria-and-latvia-seven-arrests

### Cooperation acknowledgement (verbatim)

"To tackle the criminal network, Eurojust supported and financed a joint investigation team (JIT) between the Austrian, Estonian and Latvian authorities. Further assistance was provided by the Finnish authorities."

### Key facts

- Action day produced 7 arrests across Austria, Latvia, and Estonia.
- 1,700+ victims in Austria, 1,500+ victims in Latvia, EUR 3 million+ defrauded.
- EUR 700,000 in funds frozen.
- Seizures: 1,200 SIM box devices, 40,000 SIM cards, 5 servers, 4 luxury cars; 26 search locations executed.
- Authorities named: Public Prosecutor's Office Vienna, Federal Criminal Intelligence Service (Austria), Criminal Investigation Department Salzburg; Riga Judicial Region Prosecution Office, Riga Northern Prosecution Office, State Police of Latvia; Estonian Police, Northern District Prosecutor's Office (Estonia); National Cyber-Enabled Crimes Units and National Bureau of Investigations (Finland).
- Eurojust set up and financed the JIT and coordinated the joint action day; Europol deployed a specialist team to Riga, provided analytical support, and facilitated Shadowserver Foundation cooperation.
