# Armenian man extradited to U.S. faces charges for role in infostealing malware scheme

> [!note] Source provenance
> This is a paraphrase + verbatim-quote excerpt of the Internal Revenue Service — Criminal Investigation (IRS-CI, irs.gov own-domain) press release dated 2026-03-25 (publicly available at the URL above), retained in `raw/` for reference. Body content is paraphrased except where quoted.

## Dateline / byline (verbatim)

> "Austin, TX – An Armenian man made his initial appearance in an Austin federal court Tuesday after being extradited to the U.S. on criminal charges related to his alleged role in an infostealer scheme, announced U.S. Attorney for the Western District of Texas, Justin R. Simmons."

## Overview

An Armenian national, **Hambardzum Minasyan**, made his initial appearance in an Austin federal court on Tuesday after being extradited to the United States to face federal criminal charges related to his alleged role as a co-developer/administrator of the RedLine infostealing malware scheme. The Justice Department's Office of International Affairs (OIA) secured the arrest and extradition on March 23, 2026.

Minasyan is charged with three federal conspiracy counts:
- Conspiracy to commit access device fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1029(b)(2)) — up to 10 years
- Conspiracy to violate the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (18 U.S.C. § 1030) — up to 20 years
- Conspiracy to commit money laundering (18 U.S.C. § 1956(h)) — up to 20 years

## Allegations

The indictment alleges Minasyan and co-developers/administrators of RedLine:
- Registered two virtual private servers to host portions of RedLine's infrastructure;
- Registered two internet domains in support of the RedLine scheme;
- Created repositories on an online file-sharing site that were used to distribute RedLine to affiliates;
- In November 2021, registered a cryptocurrency account used to receive payments from RedLine affiliates.

RedLine is described as one of the most prevalent infostealing malware variants in the world, used in intrusions against major corporations and affecting millions of victim systems globally.

## International cooperation (verbatim quote — L24 evidence)

> "In Oct. 2024, the Department of Justice joined the Netherlands, Belgium, Eurojust and other partners in announcing an international disruption effort against the current version of RedLine Infostealer."

Additionally:

> "The Criminal Division's International Computer Hacking and Intellectual Property (ICHIP) attorney advisor, who is based at Eurojust in The Hague, also provided assistance."

And on the technical assistance framework:

> "The Justice Department provides cybercrime technical assistance to foreign law enforcement, prosecutorial, and judicial partners in other countries through the ICHIP program."

## U.S. investigative coalition (named)

The IRS-CI release names the FBI Austin Cyber Task Force as the investigative lead, with the task force composition including:

- **Federal Bureau of Investigation** (FBI) — lead
- **IRS Criminal Investigation** (IRS-CI) — task force member; this is IRS-CI's own-domain announcement
- **Naval Criminal Investigative Service** (NCIS)
- **DoD Office of Inspector General's Defense Criminal Investigative Service** (DCIS)
- **Army Criminal Investigation Division** (Army CID)

Prosecution: Assistant U.S. Attorney Kirk Mangels (USAO-WDTX, Austin Division).

## Notes

- An indictment is an accusation; defendants are presumed innocent unless proven guilty.
- This is the **second** RedLine defendant publicly charged in the United States, following the October 2024 unsealing of charges against alleged co-conspirator **Maxim Rudometov** in connection with Operation Magnus.
- The IRS-CI release confirms IRS-CI's own role on the FBI-led Austin Cyber Task Force — directly evidencing the U.S. multi-agency civilian/military investigative coalition for infostealer-as-a-service prosecutions.
