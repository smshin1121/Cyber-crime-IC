# Victims also from Slovenia. Europol's international operation Pandora shuts down 12 phone fraud call centres

**Source:** Slovenska policija (Slovenian Police), official news archive, English edition
**Date:** 3 May 2024 (Slovenian release; references Europol press release of 2 May 2024)
**Language:** English

## Body (verbatim from Slovenian Police press release)

> Yesterday, 2 May 2024, Europol published a press release on the successful completion of an international investigation into a large number of crimes linked to fake call centres, mostly located in Eastern European countries and Lebanon. Online fraudsters carried out their crimes under the guise of technical assistance, banking services and other activities.

### Slovenian police prevented high levels of damage to its citizens in several cases

> The Slovenian police took an active part in the operation Pandora as well, as there were also victims from the Slovenian territory. After months of intensive cooperation, in particular with the German law enforcement authorities, the police managed to prevent a large number of Slovenian citizens from being cheated to the tune of hundreds of thousands of euros.

### Fraudsters mainly target legal persons

> Since July 2023, the Slovenian police have been detecting cases of telephone calls made under the guise of technical assistance by bank employees, with the fraudsters targeting mainly legal persons. More than 40 cases have been investigated, with a total damage of around 3.8 million euros and stolen funds flowing into bank accounts in Germany.

### It is important to report any damage immediately

> International cooperation and the prompt reaction of law enforcement authorities are crucial in this type of cybercrime with an international element, as they can work with banks to prevent funds being transferred from victims' accounts to the accounts of money mules abroad.

> The international element makes it extremely difficult, if not impossible, for victims to recover the funds afterwards. It is therefore extremely important that you report any damage to the police and your bank immediately.

### Police advice (paraphrased)

- Banks will never send emails with links to online banks; such emails are fraud attempts.
- Phishing links lead to spoofed online-bank pages that capture credentials.
- Fraudsters then call posing as bank technical support to drain the account.
- Victims should report immediately to police and bank, preserving evidence (emails, transactions, mobile numbers, IP addresses).

### Europol-stated participating countries (verbatim)

> Germany, Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Kosovo*, Lebanon
> *This designation is without prejudice to positions on status and is in line with UNSCR 1244/1999 and the ICJ Opinion on the Kosovo declaration of independence.

### Key facts from Europol body (verbatim excerpts)

- Trigger: a bank teller in Freiburg, Germany, in December 2023 reported a EUR 100,000 cash-withdrawal request from a victim of a fake-police-officer scam to the Freiburg Police (Polizeipräsidium Freiburg).
- Investigation handed to Baden-Württemberg State Criminal Police Office (Landeskriminalamt Baden-Württemberg), supported by the Federal Criminal Police Office (Bundeskriminalamt).
- Prosecutorial supervision: Freiburg Public Prosecutor's Office (Staatsanwaltschaft Freiburg) and Karlsruhe Public Prosecutor's Office (Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Karlsruhe).
- 100+ German officers monitored conversations from the call centres in real time and secured content of over 1.3 million calls.
- 7,500 calls passed the criminal-proceedings threshold within four months; financial losses prevented in over 80% of indicted crimes.
- Total potential damages would have amounted to over EUR 10 million.
- Action day: 18 April 2024, early hours; raids on 12 call centres simultaneously.
- 21 persons taken into custody on action day; 39 suspects identified by the operation in total.
- Country specialisation: Bosnia-Herzegovina — debt-collection fraud; Kosovo — online banking fraud; Albania — investment fraud; Lebanon — prepaid-card fraud.
- Seizures: data carriers, documents, cash and assets totalling EUR 1 million; large amount of electronic evidence.
- Europol role: hosted operational meetings before the action day, provided operational coordination and financial support, hosted the operational coordination centre at HQ on the action day, deployed officers with mobile offices abroad to facilitate forensic evidence collection.
