# Take down of Austrian-based fraud scheme offering new type of crypto coin

**Source: Eurojust press release, 08 May 2024.** URL: https://www.eurojust.europa.eu/news/take-down-austrian-based-fraud-scheme-offering-new-type-crypto-coin

The Austrian Federal Criminal Police Office (Bundeskriminalamt) referred to the action day under the operational name **"Operation LoopX"**, after the cryptocurrency token brand used by the scheme. Europol's parallel newsroom release, also dated 08 May 2024 ("Austrian scammers escape investors, but not law enforcement"), reports the same facts with additional details on the asset seizures (EUR 500,000 in cryptocurrencies and EUR 250,000 in fiat currency frozen, dozens of bank accounts frozen, two vehicles seized, plus the EUR 1.4 million luxury property).

## Lead Paragraph (verbatim, Eurojust)

National authorities in Austria, Cyprus and the Czech Republic have taken decisive action against an online scam pretending to sell rights or tokens to an alleged new crypto currency. In a coordinated operation, supported by Eurojust and Europol, the six main suspects were arrested and six places were searched. In total, EUR 750.000 in assets, a property worth EUR 1.4 million and two cars were frozen/seized.

## The Scheme (verbatim)

Between December 2017 and February 2018, the scammers pretended to have set up a genuine online trading company that had launched a new cryptocurrency. They offered 10 million tokens or respective rights to the new currency for sale. Investors paid them in regular crypto values such as bitcoin or ethereum. The Austrian-based fraudsters also claimed to have developed their own software and algorithm for the sale of the tokens, in order to gain credibility with investors.

In February 2018, the perpetrators closed all their social media accounts and took the fake company's homepage offline. This so-called exit scam made it clear to investors that they had been conned. They lost around EUR 6 million. Not all victims of the fraud have been identified at this stage.

## Cooperation Mechanisms (verbatim)

Eurojust supported the action day against the online scam with a coordination centre, enabling real-time communication between all the authorities involved. This also enabled the rapid execution of **European Arrest Warrants and search warrants**. Europol assisted in the operation with a **mobile office in Cyprus**. Austrian police officers were present on the ground during a search in Cyprus.

Europol's specialists organised **five operational meetings** and worked closely with the **Austrian desk at Eurojust**, providing a holistic analysis picture of the investigation. In addition to supporting the **Virtual Command Post** set up at Eurojust, Europol deployed a specialist with mobile office to Cyprus in order to support the operational activities and facilitate the exchange of information.

## Participating Authorities (verbatim, Eurojust)

The following authorities coordinated and supported the operations on the ground:

- **Austria**: Central Public Prosecutor's Office for Combatting Economic Crimes and Corruption (Wirtschafts- und Korruptionsstaatsanwaltschaft, WKStA); Criminal Intelligence Service Austria – Cybercrime Competence Center (C4)
- **Cyprus**: Cyprus Police (Europol parallel release specifies **Cyprus Police in Larnaca**)
- **Czech Republic**: National Organised Crime Agency (Europol parallel release specifies **Národní centrála proti organizovanému zločinu – NCOZ**, Police of the Czech Republic)

Partners: **Eurojust, Europol**.

Participating countries: **Austria, Cyprus, Czech Republic**.

Related tags (Eurojust): Cybercrime, Fraud.

## Europol Parallel Release (additional facts)

The Europol newsroom release of the same date adds:

- **Six Austrians** are the named suspects (nationality identified verbatim as Austrian).
- The Austrian fraudsters claimed to have developed **their own software and a unique algorithm** for the sale of the tokens.
- Asset seizure breakdown: **over EUR 500,000 in cryptocurrencies and EUR 250,000 in fiat currency** seized; **dozens of bank accounts** frozen; **two cars and a luxury property worth EUR 1.4 million** seized; **six house searches**.
- Europol's role: organised five operational meetings; deployed a specialist with mobile office to Cyprus; supported the Eurojust coordination centre with the Virtual Command Post; Austrian police officers were present on the ground in Cyprus, providing advice on cryptocurrency seizure and supporting the interrogation of the main suspect.
- Europol crime-area tag: **Cyber-attacks**.
- Europol keyword: **EU Policy Cycle (EMPACT)**.

## Notes

- The fraud scheme operated in **2017–2018**; the **action day and arrests took place in 2024**, with the press releases announcing the takedown published on **08 May 2024**.
- Eurojust attributes the action to "the Austrian-based fraudsters"; Europol clarifies that the **six suspects are Austrian nationals**, and that the arrest of the main suspect occurred **in Cyprus** (Larnaca area), with searches also in Czechia.
- The investigation is primarily driven by the Austrian Cybercrime Competence Center (C4) within the Federal Criminal Police Office (Bundeskriminalamt) and prosecuted by the WKStA.
- This is a **post-event MLA + EAW + Eurojust coordination** model, distinct from the more recent JIT-led action day patterns (e.g., Operation SIMCartel 2025 — Austria/Estonia/Latvia/Finland/Europol/Eurojust JIT).
