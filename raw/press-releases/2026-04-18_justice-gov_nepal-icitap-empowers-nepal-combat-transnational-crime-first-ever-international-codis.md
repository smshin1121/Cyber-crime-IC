Nepal: ICITAP Empowers Nepal To Combat Transnational Crime With First-Ever International CODIS Launch

Blog Post

Friday, August 8, 2025

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

From August 4–8, ICITAP Nepal achieved a major milestone as the Nepal Police Forensic Laboratory successfully installed and began using the Combined DNA Index System (CODIS), marking the culmination of five years of sustained training and mentorship. This development introduces advanced American DNA-matching capabilities to Nepal, significantly enhancing efforts to resolve crimes and locate missing persons. The achievement was made possible through a coordinated effort involving the U.S. Embassy in Kathmandu, the FBI’s Legal Attaché offices in Dhaka and New Delhi, ICITAP’s Forensic Operations Unit, and the FBI CODIS Unit. During the training week, the laboratory officially began inputting DNA samples into CODIS, representing a historic first for an international deployment of the system and demonstrating how long-term, expert mentorship can translate into practical forensic outcomes. CODIS, managed by the FBI, allows local, state, and national DNA index sharing across multiple categories, including crime scene samples and missing persons, and its deployment in Nepal enables investigators to efficiently match DNA evidence in cases involving human trafficking, child sexual exploitation, and other transnational crimes, supporting U.S.-based prosecutions. Fully funded by the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL), the program aligns with U.S. national security priorities, including combating illegal migration and global crime networks, and complements Executive Orders 14150, 14161, 14165, 14157, DOJ/DAG’s Operation Take Back America, and DOJ/AG guidance on eliminating cartels and transnational criminal organizations. This initiative underscores ICITAP‑Nepal’s role in fostering modern forensic capabilities, strengthening justice systems abroad, and safeguarding global security, while continuing coordination with INL, OPDAT, and the U.S. Embassy to address human trafficking and transnational crime through cutting-edge forensic science.
