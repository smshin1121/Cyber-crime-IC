# Schockanrufe: Polizei zerschlägt internationale Betrugsorganisationen

**Publisher**: Bundesministerium für Inneres (BMI), Republik Österreich
**Publish date**: 2026-02-16 (press conference)
**Operation codename**: Operation Casus
**Press conference**: Interior Minister Gerhard Karner + Bundeskriminalamt Director Andreas Holzer

## Key Facts

- **Operation Casus** established at LKA Niederösterreich (Lower Austria State Criminal Office) on 2024-12-21 as the federally responsible (bundeszuständige) police unit to combat Schockanruf-Betrug (shock-call fraud).
- Investigation conducted by LKA Niederösterreich (Lower Austria State Criminal Office) in cooperation with the Zentrale Staatsanwaltschaft zur Verfolgung von Wirtschaftsstrafsachen und Korruption (Central Public Prosecutor's Office for the Prosecution of Economic Crimes and Corruption / WKStA) and foreign police authorities (ausländische Polizeibehörden).
- **23 suspects** identified from Poland, Czech Republic, Germany, Slovakia, Serbia and Spain since the beginning of 2025.
- **17 of 23 suspects arrested**.
- **Total damage: EUR 4.8 million** caused via shock-call fraud against (primarily elderly) victims in Austria.
- "Keiler-Zelle" (caller cell) in Vienna dismantled.
- "Keiler-Zelle" in Brno (Brünn, Czech Republic) uncovered, led by a 27-year-old Czech citizen; the cell was responsible for telephone scams targeting victims in Austria, Poland and Slovakia. In Austria alone, the cell leader is charged with four completed offences causing EUR 483,000 in damage. He was arrested in Slovakia via a European arrest warrant and was sentenced to six years in Poland.
- Additional arrests via European arrest warrant: 25-year-old Slovak citizen apprehended in Vienna-Donaustadt on 2025-10-07 with information originating from Slovak investigative authorities; 20-year-old Czech woman arrested in her home country via WKStA-issued EAW and extradited to Austria; multiple suspects arrested in Salzburg (Bischofshofen, Salzburg city), Innsbruck, Villach.
- Across the broader counter-Schockanruf effort 2022–2026, Bundeskriminalamt Director Holzer reported **189 arrests with a total damage sum of approximately EUR 53 million**, with reported cases and damages recently reduced by approximately 50 percent.

## International Cooperation Quote (BMI / Bundeskriminalamt, 2026-02-16)

Per the BMI release (verbatim summary): "Betrugsermittler des Landeskriminalamts Niederösterreich klärten in Zusammenarbeit mit der WKStA und ausländischen Polizeibehörden zahlreiche 'Schockanrufe' auf. 23 Verdächtige aus Polen, Tschechien, Deutschland, der Slowakei, Serbien und Spanien wurden seit Anfang des Vorjahres ausgeforscht; 17 von ihnen wurden festgenommen. Der angerichtete Schaden: 4,8 Millionen Euro."

Interior Minister Karner described the Schockanrufe as "particularly insidious" ("besonders heimtückisch"), exploiting victims' trust in institutions such as police, courts, doctors and banks.

## Crime Type

Shock-call fraud (Schockanrufe / "bail trick") with international call-centre infrastructure ("Keiler-Zellen") in Vienna and Brno. Cyber-enabled elements include VoIP-based phone calls into Austrian numbers from foreign caller cells (typical Schockanruf modus operandi), coordination of money-mule "pickup" logistics, and laundering of proceeds across multiple EU jurisdictions.

## Cooperating Agencies (Named)

**Austria (lead)**:
- Landeskriminalamt Niederösterreich (LKA NÖ) — Lower Austria State Criminal Office
- Bundeskriminalamt (BKA Austria) — Austrian Federal Criminal Police Office
- WKStA — Central Public Prosecutor's Office for the Prosecution of Economic Crimes and Corruption

**Foreign LE explicitly mentioned**:
- Slovak investigative authorities (information for 2025-10-07 Vienna-Donaustadt arrest)
- Czech authorities (Brno cell raid; 2026 Czech extradition)
- European Arrest Warrant cooperation with Slovakia (Brno cell leader's arrest) and Czech Republic (Czech-citizen extradition)

## Relevance to International Cooperation

Tier-1 primary source from the Austrian Ministry of Interior documenting a multi-jurisdictional cooperation pattern (Austria + Slovakia + Czechia, with Polish, German, Serbian, Spanish suspects) coordinated via European Arrest Warrants and bilateral information exchange. Operation Casus is a notable example of a dedicated federally-responsible policing unit (LKA NÖ) coordinating cross-border counter-fraud investigation with explicit attribution of intelligence sharing to Slovak counterpart agencies.
