Bangladesh: Strengthening Global Security: ICITAP’s Role In Advancing U.S. Foreign Policy Goals Through Police Reform

Blog Post

Tuesday, February 4, 2025

Share

LinkedIn

Email

On February 4, ICITAP provided an update on the impact of its assistance to the Bangladesh Police, highlighting a critical partnership formed after the collapse of the Bangladesh government in August 2024 to support comprehensive police reform led by the interim Bangladesh Police Reform Commission (BPRC). In coordination with the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL), ICITAP engaged in multiple consultations with senior police leadership and BPRC members to help develop 108 key recommendations aimed at modernizing the force, strengthening human rights protections, use‑of‑force policy, accountability, and operational capacity in line with international standards. These reform proposals, drawn from the Police Reform Commission’s January 2025 report, advocated for a structured five‑tier use‑of‑force framework based on United Nations guidelines to minimize harm and reduce fatalities during crowd control and other encounters, a recommendation underscored by local media coverage and public debate on policing practices in Bangladesh. ICITAP’s training on human rights and appropriate use of force contributed to the adoption of this approach, and the mission also endorsed advanced technologies—such as body‑worn cameras and GPS tracking—to improve transparency and accountability, concepts echoed in the commission’s broader modernization agenda. Additional ICITAP‑supported recommendations included creating a citizen complaint hotline and promoting non‑partisan oversight mechanisms to reduce political influence and enhance merit‑based recruitment, consistent with the drive for more transparent and accountable policing. These efforts support U.S. objectives to combat transnational crime, bolster law enforcement effectiveness, and foster regional security, reinforcing a framework for a professional, rights‑respecting police service as reforms progress amid ongoing institutional and public scrutiny.
