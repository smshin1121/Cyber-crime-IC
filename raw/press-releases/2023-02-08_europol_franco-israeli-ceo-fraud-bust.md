## Summary

Europol supported the dismantling of a Franco-Israeli criminal network that employed business email compromise (BEC) and CEO fraud attacks to divert corporate payments to accounts under the threat actors' control. In one case, the fraudsters stole approximately EUR 38 million from a single company within days. The investigation, led by France, involved five action days between January 2022 and January 2023 across France and Israel. Eight suspects were arrested (six in France, two in Israel), including the group leader based in Israel. The criminal proceeds were laundered through a pre-existing money laundering scheme involving multiple bank accounts in the EU, China, and Israel. Authorities seized approximately EUR 5.5 million in total across multiple countries.

## Relevance to BEC/International Cooperation

This case is a textbook example of high-value CEO fraud/BEC with international cooperation: criminals based in Israel impersonated company executives, convincing financial officers to authorize urgent confidential transfers. The EUR 38 million single-victim theft is one of the largest reported BEC losses in Europe. The investigation required coordination across 7 countries, spanning the EU and Israel, with complex cross-border money laundering involving accounts in China. Europol's role in providing analytical support and coordination was essential for connecting the dots across multiple jurisdictions. The case demonstrates how BEC proceeds are rapidly moved through international banking systems, requiring swift multilateral cooperation for asset recovery.
