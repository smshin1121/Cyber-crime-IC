# First Cross-Border Collaboration Among Taiwan, Singapore, and Malaysia to Dismantle Mobile Trojan Fraud Syndicate

**Criminal Investigation Bureau, National Police Agency (Taiwan) — News Releases (English) | 17 July 2024**

## Investigating Departments

**Taiwan:** Taiwan Ciaotou District Prosecutors Office; Criminal Investigation Bureau (CIB-NPA); Taipei Representative Office in Singapore; Criminal Investigation Brigade of Kaohsiung City Police Department; Nangang Precinct of Taipei City Police Department; Criminal Investigation Brigade of Pingtung County Police Department; and Criminal Investigation Brigade of Penghu County Police Department.

**Singapore:** Criminal Investigation Department (CID) and Commercial Affairs Department (CAD) of the Singapore Police Force.

**Malaysia:** Commercial Crime Investigation Department of the Royal Malaysian Police (CCID-RMP).

## Summary

The Criminal Investigation Bureau, National Police Agency of Taiwan (CIB-NPA) announced the conclusion of a trilateral joint investigation with the Singapore Police Force and the Royal Malaysian Police against a transnational mobile-trojan fraud syndicate. The CIB-NPA characterises the operation as "the first instance of trilateral police cooperation among Taiwan, Singapore, and Malaysia in fighting crime."

The syndicate operated a remote-access-trojan (SpyMax-type) malware family distributed as malicious Android Package Kit (APK) software. Fraud operators induced victims — overwhelmingly Singaporean — to download the APK from spoofed websites posing as legitimate banking, e-commerce or government applications. Once installed, the remote-access trojan exfiltrated banking credentials and authentication codes, enabling the syndicate to perform unauthorised fund transfers from victim bank accounts that were then converted to cryptocurrency for distribution among the syndicate's principals and money launderers.

## Operational Results

**Taiwan arrests (15 May 2024)** — Four Taiwanese nationals arrested in a coordinated raid in Kaohsiung City. The arrests were executed by the Criminal Investigation Brigades of Kaohsiung City, Pingtung County and Penghu County police departments, with the Nangang Precinct of Taipei City Police Department and the CIB-NPA First Investigation Corps.

Arrestees (per CIB-NPA release):

**Malaysia arrests (12 June 2024)** — Two Malaysian nationals arrested in Penang by CCID-RMP with SPF and CIB-NPA support:

## Victim Impact (Singapore)

Per the CIB-NPA citation of Singapore-side analysis: 1,899 victims were identified in Singapore during 2023 alone, with total reported financial losses of approximately **NTD 784.3 million** (S$34.1 million).

## Cooperation Statement

The CIB-NPA characterises the trilateral cooperation directly:

> "This marks the first instance of trilateral police cooperation among Taiwan, Singapore, and Malaysia in fighting crime. It demonstrates a strong determination of police cross-border collaboration in law enforcement."

The CIB-NPA release also acknowledges parallel investigative actions by the SPF (lead jurisdiction for victim reports and downstream principal extraditions) and CCID-RMP (lead jurisdiction for the principal-operator arrests in Penang), and confirms that Taiwan acted both as a (i) take-down site of a fraudulent customer-service-centre node operating from Kaohsiung City and (ii) trilateral joint-investigation partner sharing operational intelligence with the SPF and CCID-RMP during the November 2023 – June 2024 operational period.

## Modus Operandi

The syndicate's mobile-trojan campaign followed a four-stage attack chain:

1. Spoofed website distribution of malicious APK files masquerading as legitimate Singapore-banking, e-commerce or government apps.
2. Social engineering induced Singaporean victims to side-load the malicious APK on their Android devices.
3. The SpyMax-type remote-access trojan elevated permissions, exfiltrated banking credentials and one-time-password codes, and granted the syndicate live remote control of the infected device.
4. The syndicate executed unauthorised fund transfers from victim bank accounts, with proceeds laundered through Taiwan-based money mules and converted to USDT/DOGE/TRX cryptocurrencies for distribution.

The Kaohsiung node operated as a fraudulent customer-service centre that received victim calls, impersonated bank fraud-prevention agents, and directed victims into authorising further transfers — a typology consistent with FRONTIER+-tracked Asia-Pacific scam-centre patterns.

## Prosecutorial Forwarding

The Taiwan-side investigation was forwarded to the **Taiwan Ciaotou District Prosecutors Office** for prosecution of the four Taiwanese-national arrestees. The prosecution of the two Malaysian-national principals continues under Singapore State Court charges following their extradition from Malaysia on 14 June 2024.
