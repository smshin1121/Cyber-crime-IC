# Police Bust Organized Crime Group Running Fraudulent Online Investment Platforms

**URL:** https://www.bta.bg/en/news/bulgaria/1026797-police-bust-organized-crime-group-running-fraudulent-online-investment-platforms

**Byline:** BTA (Bulgarian News Agency)

**Date:** December 12, 2025, 12:54 (reporting); operation date December 11, 2025

---

## Article Body (verbatim summary)

A large-scale operation targeting an organized crime group operating multiple call centres in Sofia took place on December 11, 2025, according to the Sofia City Prosecutor's Office.

The Sofia City Prosecutor's Office (SCPO) led the investigation with support from the investigative department of the SCPO, the National Investigation Service, the State Agency for National Security (SANS), the General Directorate for Combatting Organized Crime (GDCOC), the General Directorate National Police (GDNP), and Spain's Guardia Civil.

The criminal network — composed of Bulgarian and foreign nationals — ran fraudulent investment schemes through call centres. The group operated multiple websites used to attract clients and deceive them into investing money in fictitious trading with regulated financial instruments, promising high returns. Victims included EU member state citizens.

**Timeline:** The investigation determined the scheme had operated since mid-2022.

**Seizures:**

- More than 20 mobile phones
- Numerous SIM cards
- More than 30 USB flash drives
- Personal computers, hard drives, notebooks, handwritten notes, lists of employees
- Narcotics
- 17 investment gold bars (each valued at approximately BGN 7,000)
- More than BGN 45,000 in cash
- 5 hardware cryptocurrency wallets — one containing cryptoassets worth over USD 230,000

The National Investigation Service's Cybercrime Department assisted with the seizures.

International cooperation: The operation was conducted in close international cooperation with Spain's Guardia Civil, with joint teams working under the direction of Bulgarian authorities and within the framework of EU police cooperation mechanisms.
