## Summary

INTERPOL's Operation HAECHI VI, the sixth iteration of the Korea-initiated HAECHI series, ran from April to August 2025 and involved law enforcement authorities from 40 countries and territories. The operation recovered USD 439 million in total: USD 342 million in government-backed currencies and USD 97 million in physical and virtual assets. Investigators blocked over 68,000 bank accounts, froze close to 400 cryptocurrency wallets, and recovered approximately USD 16 million in suspected illicit profits from crypto wallets. The operation continued to target the same seven types of cyber-enabled financial crime: voice phishing, romance scams, online sextortion, investment fraud, money laundering associated with illegal online gambling, business email compromise, and e-commerce fraud.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation HAECHI VI
- **Date:** April - August 2025
- **Lead agency:** INTERPOL (Korea-initiated series)
- **Participating countries:** 40
- **Assets recovered:** USD 439 million total (USD 342 million fiat, USD 97 million physical/virtual assets)
- **Crypto recovered:** ~USD 16 million from cryptocurrency wallets
- **Bank accounts blocked:** 68,000+
- **Crypto wallets frozen:** ~400
- **Crime types:** Voice phishing, romance scams, online sextortion, investment fraud, money laundering (illegal online gambling), BEC, e-commerce fraud
- **Notable sub-operations:**
  - **Korea-UAE (BEC recovery):** Korean National Police Agency worked with Emirati authorities to recover KRW 6.6 billion (USD 3.91 million) sent to an illegitimate bank account in Dubai after a Korean steel company discovered forged shipping documents. Rapid communication via I-GRIP enabled full recovery.
  - **Portugal (social security fraud):** 45 suspects arrested for illegally accessing social security accounts, resulting in EUR 228,000 (USD 270,000) stolen from 531 victims.
  - **Thailand (BEC):** Royal Thai Police seized USD 6.6 million in stolen assets, the largest single-case recovery in Thailand, involving a transnational organized crime group of Thai and West African nationals that deceived a major Japanese corporation.
- **Series context:** Sixth iteration of HAECHI; continues Korea's leadership in INTERPOL financial crime operations

## Relevance to Voice Phishing / Phone Scams

Voice phishing remained a primary target category in HAECHI VI. The Korea-UAE sub-operation demonstrated the continued effectiveness of rapid international fund interception (I-GRIP) for recovering stolen funds, a mechanism critical for voice phishing cases where victims are typically pressured into immediate wire transfers. Korea's role as the initiator and consistent leader of the HAECHI series underscores the country's centrality in combating voice phishing at the international level.
