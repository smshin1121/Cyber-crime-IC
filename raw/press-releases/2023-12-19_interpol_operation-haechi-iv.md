## Summary

INTERPOL's Operation HAECHI IV, the fourth iteration of the Korea-initiated HAECHI series, ran from July to December 2023 and involved law enforcement agencies from 34 countries. The operation resulted in the arrest of approximately 3,500 suspects and the seizure of USD 300 million in assets, including USD 199 million in hard currency and USD 101 million in virtual assets. Authorities blocked 82,112 suspicious bank accounts. The operation targeted seven types of cyber-enabled fraud: voice phishing, romance scams, online sextortion, investment fraud, money laundering associated with illegal online gambling, business email compromise (BEC), and e-commerce fraud. Investment fraud, BEC, and e-commerce fraud accounted for 75% of cases investigated. The operation saw a 260% increase in arrests compared to HAECHI III (June-November 2022).

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation HAECHI IV
- **Date:** July - December 2023
- **Lead agency:** INTERPOL (Korea-initiated series)
- **Participating countries:** 34 (including the United States, United Kingdom, Japan, Hong Kong (China), and India)
- **Arrests:** ~3,500
- **Assets seized:** USD 300 million total (USD 199 million in hard currency, USD 101 million in virtual assets)
- **Bank accounts blocked:** 82,112
- **Crime types:** Voice phishing, romance scams, online sextortion, investment fraud, money laundering (illegal online gambling), BEC, e-commerce fraud
- **Notable sub-operation:** Filipino-Korean cooperation led to arrest in Manila of high-profile online gambling criminal after two-year manhunt by Korea's National Police Agency
- **Emerging threats identified:** Two INTERPOL Purple Notices issued warning of fraudulent NFT sales and deepfake technology use in scams; UK police reported AI-generated voice cloning in impersonation scams
- **Series context:** Fourth iteration of INTERPOL HAECHI operations; 260% arrest increase over HAECHI III

## Relevance to Voice Phishing / Phone Scams

Voice phishing was one of the seven primary crime types targeted in HAECHI IV. The operation also identified emerging trends in AI-enabled voice cloning being used for impersonation scams and online sexual blackmail, signaling the growing sophistication of voice phishing operations. The Korea-Philippines cooperation component demonstrated bilateral enforcement against telecom-enabled fraud in the Asia-Pacific region.
