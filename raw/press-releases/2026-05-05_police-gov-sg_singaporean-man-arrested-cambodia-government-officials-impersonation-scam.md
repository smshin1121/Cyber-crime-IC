# Singaporean Man Arrested In Connection With Organised Criminal Group Based In Phnom Penh, Cambodia And Additional Interpol Red Notices Issued Against Two Other Fugitives

**Date:** 5 May 2026, 7:10 PM (released)

**Source:** Singapore Police Force, Public Affairs Department — https://www.police.gov.sg/Media-Hub/News/2026/05/20260505_singaporean_man_arrested_in_connection_with_organised_criminal_group_based

---

A 30-year-old Singaporean man, **Ngiam Siow Jui**, was apprehended overseas for his alleged involvement with an organised criminal group ("OCG") operating from **Phnom Penh, Cambodia** that authorities believe conducted **Government Officials Impersonation Scams** targeting victims in Singapore.

**The Cambodian National Police (CNP) arrested Ngiam in Phnom Penh, Cambodia.** On 4 May 2026, Ngiam was repatriated to Singapore and arrested on his arrival. He will face court charges on 6 May 2026 for one count of Facilitating the Commission of a Serious Offence under Section 12(2)(b) of the Organised Crime Act 2015. The offence carries up to five years' imprisonment and/or a fine of up to S$100,000.

The OCG is allegedly responsible for at least **535 reported scam cases** with approximately **S$53 million in total losses**. The OCG operated from a scam compound in Phnom Penh, **which was disrupted during a joint operation by the SPF and CNP on 9 September 2025.**

The arrest follows close collaboration and intelligence sharing between the SPF and the CNP.

Ngiam was among 34 initially identified suspects in the Singapore-side investigation.

On **25 March 2026**, the SPF published additional **INTERPOL Red Notices** against two other male Singaporeans believed to be involved as members of the syndicate:

- **Jonathan Boneta**, age 27
- **Lee Ding Hao**, age 33

Both remain at large.

The SPF continues international cooperation efforts and has implemented asset seizures and prohibition of disposal orders connected to the OCG.

Anyone with information is asked to call the police hotline at 1800-255-0000 or to submit information online at www.police.gov.sg/i-witness.
