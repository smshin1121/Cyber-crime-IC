# AFP intelligence leads to eight arrests over phishing kits

**Publisher:** Australian Federal Police (AFP) — Media Release
**Publication date:** 27 November 2023, 2:02 pm AEDT
**URL:** https://www.afp.gov.au/news-centre/media-release/afp-intelligence-leads-eight-arrests-over-phishing-kits

## Summary of the AFP media release (paraphrased; original English preserved in quoted phrases)

On 6 November 2023, the Royal Malaysian Police (RMP) arrested eight people in Malaysia following intelligence developed and shared by the AFP's Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3). The intelligence identified a 35-year-old Malaysian national alleged to have been advertising and selling phishing kits that impersonated Australian Government services — including the myGov citizen-services portal — and government services in Malaysia and the United States. The kits replicated legitimate government website templates and were sold to other cybercriminals to harvest victim credentials.

Coordinated raids on the suspect's residence in Borneo and at a Malaysian technology park led to the seizure of four servers, networking equipment (power cables, monitors, modem) and other devices. Investigators recovered more than 60 terabytes of data, including usernames, passwords and cryptocurrency wallet seed phrases. One primary suspect (35) and seven alleged money mules were arrested and charged under Malaysian law.

The Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) separately linked the hosting infrastructure used by the syndicate to organised crime, supporting coordinated tri-national action between Australia, Malaysia and the United States.

## Quoted attributions in the AFP release

- AFP Acting Detective Superintendent Darryl Parrish on consumer harm: noted that "Australians lost over $24.6 million to phishing attacks last year" (calendar 2022 figure cited in the release) and emphasised the importance of international law enforcement collaboration in disrupting the global phishing-kit economy.

## International cooperation elements

- **Lead enforcement:** Royal Malaysian Police (RMP) — arrests, searches, and Malaysian-law charges.
- **Intelligence development and lead agency on the Australian side:** AFP, through JPC3 (the AFP-led Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre based in Sydney).
- **United States contribution:** FBI provided infrastructure attribution linking the hosting estate to organised crime, supporting the trilateral picture.
- **Cooperation modality (as described in the release):** informal police-to-police intelligence sharing through cybercrime liaison channels; arrests and searches executed under domestic Malaysian authority; no formal MLAT cited in the public release.

## Reported results

- 8 arrests in Malaysia (1 alleged developer/seller; 7 alleged money mules).
- 4 servers + power cables + monitors + modem seized.
- 60+ terabytes of data recovered.
- Credentials and cryptocurrency wallet seed phrases recovered.
- Phishing kits targeted Australian Government services (myGov), Malaysian Government services, and US Government services.

## Public victim guidance in the release

- Australian victims directed to ReportCyber, their banking institution, and the Australian Cyber Security Centre hotline 1300 CYBER1 (1300 292 371).
