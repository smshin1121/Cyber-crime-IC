## Summary

On 5 March 2025, the US Department of Justice announced coordinated efforts to disrupt the malicious cyber activities of 12 Chinese nationals, including two officers of the People's Republic of China's Ministry of Public Security (MPS), eight employees of Anxun Information Technology Co. Ltd. (i-Soon), and members of Advanced Persistent Threat 27 (APT27). These actors conducted computer intrusions as freelancers or i-Soon employees at the direction of China's MPS and Ministry of State Security (MSS), and also on their own initiative. i-Soon generated tens of millions of dollars in revenue, charging USD 10,000 to USD 75,000 per successfully exploited email inbox, making it a key player in China's hacker-for-hire ecosystem. Victims included US-based critics and dissidents of the PRC, a large religious organization in the US, and foreign ministries of governments in Asia (Taiwan, India, South Korea, Indonesia). The coordinated action involved the DOJ, FBI, Naval Criminal Investigative Service (NCIS), and the Departments of State and Treasury. The State Department's Rewards for Justice program announced a reward of up to USD 10 million for information leading to identification of persons engaged in malicious cyber activities against US critical infrastructure under foreign government direction.

## Key Data Points

- **Date:** 5 March 2025
- **Lead agencies:** US DOJ, FBI, Naval Criminal Investigative Service (NCIS), Department of State, Department of the Treasury
- **Defendants:** 12 Chinese nationals (2 MPS officers, 8 i-Soon employees, APT27 members)
- **Affiliated entities:** Anxun Information Technology Co. Ltd. (i-Soon), Advanced Persistent Threat 27 (APT27), PRC Ministry of Public Security (MPS), PRC Ministry of State Security (MSS)
- **Revenue generated by i-Soon:** Tens of millions of dollars (USD 10,000-75,000 per exploited inbox)
- **Victim categories:** US-based PRC critics/dissidents, US religious organization, foreign ministries (Taiwan, India, South Korea, Indonesia)
- **Rewards for Justice offer:** Up to USD 10 million
- **Crime types:** State-sponsored cyber espionage, contract hacking, computer intrusion campaigns
- **Enforcement actions:** Indictments, coordinated multi-agency disruption, Rewards for Justice designation, Treasury sanctions
