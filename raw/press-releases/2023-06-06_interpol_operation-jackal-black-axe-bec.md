## Summary

INTERPOL coordinated Operation Jackal from 15 to 29 May 2023, mobilizing police forces, financial crime units, and cybercrime agencies across 21 countries to deliver a targeted strike against Black Axe and similar West African organized crime groups. The operation resulted in 103 arrests, the identification of 1,110 suspects, the blocking of 208 bank accounts, and the seizure or freezing of EUR 2.15 million. Black Axe is a violent mafia-style gang renowned for cyber-enabled financial fraud, in particular business email compromise (BEC) schemes, romance scams, inheritance scams, credit card fraud, tax fraud, advance payment scams, and money laundering.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation Jackal
- **Date:** 15-29 May 2023
- **Lead agency:** INTERPOL
- **Participating countries (21):** Argentina, Australia, Belgium, Brazil, Canada, Cote d'Ivoire, France, Germany, Indonesia, Ireland, Italy, Malaysia, Netherlands, Nigeria, Portugal, South Africa, Spain, Switzerland, United Arab Emirates, United Kingdom, United States
- **Arrests:** 103
- **Suspects identified:** 1,110
- **Bank accounts blocked:** 208
- **Assets seized/frozen:** EUR 2.15 million
- **Crime types:** Business email compromise (BEC), romance scams, inheritance scams, credit card fraud, tax fraud, advance payment scams, money laundering
- **Target organization:** Black Axe and similar West African organized crime groups
- **Notable regional results:**
  - Ireland: 34 arrests (12 investigative, 22 for money laundering and gangland-style offences)
  - Portugal: 4 investigations yielded seizure/recovery of nearly EUR 1.4 million
- **Enforcement actions:** Arrests, bank account blocking, asset seizure/freezing
- **Series context:** First dedicated Operation Jackal targeting Black Axe; followed by Jackal III in 2024

## Relevance to BEC/International Cooperation

Operation Jackal is significant as one of INTERPOL's first dedicated operations specifically targeting the Black Axe criminal network, which is identified as responsible for the majority of the world's cyber-enabled financial fraud, with BEC as a primary crime type. The 21-country scope spanning five continents demonstrates the truly global nature of BEC enforcement and the necessity for multinational law enforcement cooperation. Black Axe's estimated 30,000 members in dozens of countries and yearly proceeds exceeding USD 5 billion underscore the scale of the BEC threat addressed by this operation.
