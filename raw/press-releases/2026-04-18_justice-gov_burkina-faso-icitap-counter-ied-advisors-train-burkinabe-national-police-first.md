Burkina Faso: ICITAP Counter-IED Advisors Train Burkinabe National Police In First Demolition Course

Blog Post

Monday, January 19, 2026

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

On January 19, ICITAP Counter-IED Advisors delivered Burkina Faso’s Groupe Spéciale d’Intervention de la Police Nationale (GSIPN) its first-ever explosives safety and demolition range instruction, marking a historic milestone in ICITAP’s engagement with the Burkinabe National Police. This session was part of the ICITAP-sponsored “Improvised Explosives Device Awareness & Operations” Course, held at Camp Badnogo from January 12 to 23, 2026, and one of 23 modules designed to build comprehensive Counter-IED capabilities. Students received classroom instruction on the safe handling and administrative requirements for operating a demolition range, coupled with hands-on practical exercises where they safely executed demolition procedures under ICITAP guidance. This approach not only ensures immediate operational competence but also fosters sustainable, in-country capacity for the GSIPN to manage explosives independently. ICITAP’s mentorship underscores global Counter-IED best practices, strengthens unit cohesion, and enhances Burkina Faso’s ability to counter terrorist groups across the country and the Sahel. The program is implemented with the support of the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of Counterterrorism, reflecting ICITAP’s leadership in advancing partner nation security and operational professionalism. Photos in right column show GSIPN students practicing range operations using inert training materials at Camp Badnogo. Photos in left column show GSIPN students conducting range operations and demolition procedures at Camp Badnogo. In Burkina Faso, ICITAP works with the support of and in collaboration with the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of Counterterrorism (CT).
