## Summary

On 27 September 2024, the U.S. Department of Justice's Office of Public Affairs, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Columbia, the DOJ National Security Division, and the FBI announced the unsealing of a federal indictment in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia charging three Iranian nationals — all employees of Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) — with a multi-year hack-and-leak campaign targeting U.S. government officials, media organizations, NGOs, and individuals associated with U.S. presidential campaigns. The same day, the U.S. Department of State's Rewards for Justice program announced a reward of up to USD 10 million for information on the three subjects, IRGC interference in U.S. elections, or associated individuals and entities; the U.S. Treasury Department's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) designated defendant Masoud Jalili pursuant to Executive Order 13694 (as amended) and Executive Order 13848.

## Extracted Text (reconstructed from authoritative public summaries)

Defendants:
- Masoud Jalili (مسعود جلیلی), age 36, Iranian national, IRGC employee.
- Seyyed Ali Aghamiri (سید علی آقامیری), age 34, Iranian national, IRGC employee.
- Yaser Balaghi (یاسر بلاغی), age 37, Iranian national, IRGC employee.

Indicting court: U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia (D.D.C.).

Conduct charged:
- Beginning in or around January 2020 and continuing into 2024, the three defendants, working on behalf of the IRGC, conducted a wide-ranging spearphishing and social-engineering campaign against U.S. government officials (current and former), members of the media, nongovernmental organizations, and individuals associated with U.S. political campaigns.
- Tradecraft included: virtual private networks and virtual private servers to obscure location; fraudulent email accounts in the names of prominent U.S. persons and international institutions; spoofed login pages to harvest credentials; spearphishing from compromised victim accounts; and social engineering to obtain login credentials and multi-factor authentication / recovery codes.
- In or around May–June 2024, the conspirators successfully compromised accounts of individuals associated with U.S. Presidential Campaign 1 and exfiltrated non-public campaign material. Beginning in June 2024, they conducted a "hack-and-leak" operation, sharing stolen material with members of the media and with individuals associated with U.S. Presidential Campaign 2.

Charges (per DOJ statement):
- Conspiracy (statutes commonly cited as 18 U.S.C. § 371 in this fact pattern).
- Aggravated identity theft (per DOJ statement, eight counts; commonly 18 U.S.C. § 1028A; mandatory consecutive 2-year minimum per count).
- Identity theft and access device fraud.
- Unauthorized access to computers (per DOJ statement; commonly 18 U.S.C. § 1030 — Computer Fraud and Abuse Act).
- Wire fraud — eight counts (per DOJ statement; commonly 18 U.S.C. § 1343; statutory maximum cited at 27 years per count in DOJ release context).
- Wire fraud while falsely registering domain names (statutory maximum 12 years per DOJ release).
- Conspiracy to provide material support to a designated foreign terrorist organization — IRGC (statutory maximum 20 years; commonly 18 U.S.C. § 2339B).

Quoted officials (per DOJ release):
- Attorney General Merrick B. Garland.
- FBI Director Christopher Wray.
- Assistant Attorney General Matthew G. Olsen, DOJ National Security Division: "These hack-and-leak efforts by Iran are a direct assault on the integrity of our democratic processes."
- U.S. Attorney Matthew M. Graves for the District of Columbia.

Investigating offices (per DOJ release):
- FBI Washington Field Office (lead).
- FBI Cyber Division.
- FBI Springfield Field Office.
- FBI Minneapolis Field Office.
- Assistant U.S. Attorneys, U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Columbia, prosecuting in coordination with the National Security Division's Counterintelligence and Export Control Section / National Security Cyber Section.

Parallel U.S. government actions announced 2024-09-27:
- Department of State, Rewards for Justice: reward up to USD 10 million for information on Jalili, Aghamiri, Balaghi, IRGC interference in U.S. elections, or associated individuals and entities.
- Department of the Treasury, OFAC: designation of Masoud Jalili pursuant to E.O. 13694 (as amended) and E.O. 13848 (election-interference sanctions).

Strategic context (per DOJ release):
- The activity is described as part of Iran's continuing effort to "stoke discord, erode confidence in the U.S. electoral process, and unlawfully acquire information relating to current and former U.S. officials that could be used to advance the malign activities of the IRGC, including ongoing efforts to avenge the death of Qasem Soleimani," former commander of IRGC-Quds Force.
