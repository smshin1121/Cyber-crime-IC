## Background

While technological innovation has the power to drive positive change, it can just as easily be exploited for malicious purposes. In the wrong hands, these same advancements become powerful tools for cybercrime. One example is the use of botnets by cybercriminals.

Botnets are networks of devices that are infected with malware, allowing attackers to take remote control without the owners’ knowledge. These malware infections usually begin with the use of droppers, a type of malicious software designed to install additional malware onto a targeted system.

Droppers are typically deployed in the early stages of an attack, helping criminals bypass security measures and enabling the installation of more dangerous software, such as viruses, ransomware, or spyware.

## Objectives

Operation Endgame is part of a series of operations dedicated to disrupting botnets, making it one of the largest operations of its kind.

Throughout its lifetime, Europol and the Joint Cybercrime Action Taskforce (J-CAT), continue to support investigations by facilitating information exchange between the authorities involved and providing analytical and forensic support to the investigators.

The objectives of Operation Endgame include:

Disrupting and dismantling infrastructure supporting ransomware attacks.

Targeting and neutralising malware used for initial access into victim systems.

Seizing criminal assets, including cryptocurrency.

Following up on leads, helping them to link online personas/ usernames to real-life individuals.
