## Summary

Portugal's Polícia Judiciária announced that its cybercrime unit participated in an international operation that dismantled a money-laundering structure tied to organized cybercrime and CEO/BEC fraud. The PJ said the group created four companies in Portugal, opened 17 bank accounts at different credit institutions, and used three false identities. Portuguese investigators reported seizing EUR 3 million, along with computer and telecommunications equipment, in the national leg of the operation.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operação "Sefricime 2.2"
- **Connection to case:** Portuguese strand of the Franco-Israeli CEO fraud investigation
- **Publisher role:** participating national judicial police authority
- **Portugal-specific investigative findings:** 4 companies, 17 bank accounts, 3 false identities
- **Assets seized in Portugal:** EUR 3 million

## Relevance to IC

This official Portuguese source materially improves the money-laundering dimension of the case by documenting the shell-company and bank-account infrastructure used in Portugal.
