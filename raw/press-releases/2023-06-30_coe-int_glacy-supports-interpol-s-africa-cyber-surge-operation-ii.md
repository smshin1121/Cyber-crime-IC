## Summary

**Africa Cyber Surge II** was a major [[interpol|INTERPOL]]-[[afripol|AFRIPOL]] operation spanning 25 African countries. INTERPOL reported 14 arrests, the identification of 20,674 suspicious cyber networks, and linked losses exceeding USD 40 million across schemes involving phishing, business email compromise, digital extortion, and online fraud.

On 26 - 30 June 2023, in DAR ES SALAM, TANZANIA, the Cybercrime Directorate of [INTERPOL](https://www.interpol.int/Crimes/Cybercrime), under the auspices of the INTERPOL Africa Cybercrime Operations desk, with the participation of [INTERPOL’s Support Program for the African Union, in relation to AFRIPOL (ISPA)](https://www.interpol.int/en/Our-partners/Our-partnerships-with-regional-policing-bodies/Support-for-AFRIPOL), coordinated the Africa Cyber Surge II operation. This initiative, which was launched in April 2023, focused on identifying cybercriminals and compromised infrastructure.

The Africa Cyber Surge II operation led to strengthening cybercrime departments in member countries and reinforced partnerships with essential stakeholders. A one-week tabletop exercise on cybercrime and cryptocurrency investigations preceded the operation, aiming at preparing officials from some 20 African countries with the knowledge and skills necessary for the successful execution of the operation.

The [GLACY+ project](https://coe.int/en/web/cybercrime/glacyplus) supported the participation of law enforcement agencies from its priority countries in Africa.
