# Melbourne man charged for helping launder tens of millions from cybercrime victims

**Publisher:** Australian Federal Police (AFP) — Media Release
**Publication date:** 26 October 2023, 7:28 am AEDT
**URL:** https://www.afp.gov.au/news-centre/media-release/melbourne-man-charged-helping-launder-tens-millions-cybercrime-victims

## Summary of the AFP media release (paraphrased; original English preserved in quoted phrases)

The Australian Federal Police (AFP) announced charges against a 37-year-old Chinese national, a Melbourne resident, alleged to have laundered approximately AUD 100 million in proceeds derived from a global investment-fraud scheme that primarily targeted victims in the United States. The man becomes the seventh alleged offender charged under **Operation Wickham**, a joint AFP–United States Secret Service (USSS) investigation launched on **22 August 2022**.

Operation Wickham commenced when the USSS advised the AFP-led Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3) that funds generated through investment scams in the United States were being transferred into an Australian business bank account. Investigators allege the funds — totalling more than AUD 160 million worldwide — flowed through accounts associated with the **Changjiang Currency Exchange** and a network of fake Australian businesses controlled by the wider Long River money-laundering organisation, before being moved on through cryptocurrency and other channels.

Search warrants were executed on 25 October 2023 at residences in Sydney and Melbourne. The accused was arrested locally; no extradition was involved at the date of the release. He has been charged with two offences: recklessly engaging in conduct that resulted in dealing with proceeds of general crime (value exceeding AUD 10 million), carrying a maximum penalty of 15 years' imprisonment; and providing unregistered remittance services, carrying a maximum penalty of 2 years' imprisonment. The AFP states that more than AUD 21 million has been restrained in Australian bank accounts believed to be proceeds of the laundered funds.

## Quoted attributions in the AFP release

- **AFP Commander Kate Ferry** on the modus operandi: "The AFP alleges victims were led to believe the vast majority of trades were successful in yielding significant financial returns, when in reality, the data was manipulated to show positive returns."
- AFP–USSS cooperation framing: the operation is described as a "joint" AFP–USSS investigation that originated in a USSS referral to AFP JPC3 about scam-derived funds being transferred from the United States to an Australian business bank account.

## International cooperation elements

- **Origin of cooperation (US → Australia information sharing):** USSS proactively notified the AFP-led JPC3 that millions of US-investment-fraud-derived dollars were being transferred to an Australian business bank account; AFP took the Australian-side investigation forward as Operation Wickham.
- **Joint investigation pairing:** AFP and USSS describe Wickham as a joint investigation; the Australian leg targets money-laundering and remittance offences in Australia, while the US side concerns the underlying investment-fraud victimisation in the United States.
- **Investigative-platform context:** investigation focuses on scam victims being routed onto manipulated trading platforms (MetaTrader and certain cryptocurrency platforms), with proceeds laundered through remittance and shell-company structures connected to the Changjiang Currency Exchange / Long River network.
- **Cooperation modality (as described in the release):** police-to-police joint investigation between AFP and USSS, with no specific MLAT or treaty instrument cited in the public release. Charges and restraining-order proceedings are executed under domestic Australian law.

## Reported results (release-attributed)

- 7 alleged offenders charged under Operation Wickham at the date of the release (the 26 October 2023 release covers the seventh).
- AUD 100 million+ allegedly laundered by the seventh-charged accused; AUD 160 million+ in total estimated stolen worldwide through the underlying scams.
- AUD 21 million+ restrained in Australian bank accounts believed to be scam proceeds.
- Search warrants executed at Sydney and Melbourne residences on 25 October 2023.
- Two charges laid (proceeds-of-crime: max 15 years; unregistered remittance: max 2 years).

## Public victim guidance in the release

- Victims of online investment fraud are directed by AFP to ReportCyber and the Australian Cyber Security Centre.
