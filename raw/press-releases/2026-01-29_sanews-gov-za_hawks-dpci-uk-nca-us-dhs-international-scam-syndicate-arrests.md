# Alleged International Scam Syndicate Members Arrested

**Publisher:** South African Government News Agency (SAnews) — official state news wire of the Government Communication and Information System (GCIS), Republic of South Africa.

**URL:** https://www.sanews.gov.za/south-africa/alleged-international-scam-syndicate-members-arrested

**Publish date:** 2026-01-29

## Summary (verbatim extract of cooperating-agency paragraph)

> "The arrests were the culmination of prosecutorial, internationally coordinated investigations conducted by the DPCI, the South African Police Service Crime Intelligence, the Financial Intelligence Centre, the United Kingdom National Crime Agency, the United States Department of Homeland Security Service, Australian IFW and CyberTrace investigation companies, working closely with South Africa's private investigation company, IRS."

## Key facts extracted

- 23 suspects arrested in Gauteng, South Africa, the week of 2026-01-26 to 2026-01-29.
- Charges: contravention of the Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act (FAIS Act) 37 of 2002 — call centres allegedly providing financial-advisory services without registration.
- Domestic cooperating agencies: Directorate for Priority Crime Investigation (DPCI/Hawks), South African Police Service (SAPS) Crime Intelligence, Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC), National Prosecuting Authority (NPA).
- Foreign cooperating LE agencies (named in primary source): United Kingdom National Crime Agency (NCA), United States Department of Homeland Security (DHS).
- Private-sector cooperation partners (named in primary source, NOT counted as LE under L24): Australian IFW, CyberTrace, South African IRS.
- Victim countries (named in primary source): United States, Australia, New Zealand, United Kingdom.
- Reported victim loss: more than R1 billion across 40+ identified victims.
- Investigation origin: complaints received by Gauteng Hawks Serious Commercial Crime Investigation team beginning 2022 (43 initial complaints from Australian victims).
- Court: Johannesburg Commercial Crimes Court; case postponed for bail hearing.
- NPA Gauteng spokesperson: Phindi Mjonondwane.
