## Summary

INTERPOL's Operation First Light 2024 was a coordinated global crackdown on online scams conducted from March to May 2024 across 61 countries. The operation resulted in 3,950 arrests and the identification of 14,643 additional possible suspects. Authorities froze 6,745 bank accounts and seized assets worth USD 257 million, comprising USD 135 million in fiat currency, USD 2 million in cryptocurrency, and approximately USD 120 million in physical assets including real estate, luxury cars, and jewelry. The operation targeted phishing, investment fraud, fake online shopping sites, romance scams, and impersonation scams. It was bolstered by regional policing bodies including AFRIPOL, ASEANAPOL, GCCPOL, and Europol, and utilized INTERPOL's Global Rapid Intervention of Payments (I-GRIP) mechanism for cross-border fund recovery.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation First Light 2024
- **Date:** March - May 2024
- **Lead agency:** INTERPOL
- **Participating countries:** 61
- **Regional partners:** AFRIPOL, ASEANAPOL, GCCPOL, Europol
- **Arrests:** 3,950
- **Additional suspects identified:** 14,643
- **Assets seized:** USD 257 million total (USD 135 million fiat, USD 2 million crypto, ~USD 120 million in real estate/luxury goods)
- **Bank accounts frozen:** 6,745
- **Crime types:** Phishing, investment fraud, fake online shopping, romance scams, impersonation scams
- **Notable cases:**
  - Namibia: rescue of 88 youths forced into conducting scams; seizure of 163 computers and 350 mobile phones
  - Spain: USD 331,000 intercepted in BEC fraud via I-GRIP mechanism
  - Australia: AUD 5.5 million recovered for impersonation scam victim
- **Series context:** Part of the longstanding First Light operation series targeting telecom and online fraud

## Relevance to Voice Phishing / Phone Scams

Operation First Light has historically focused on phone scams and telecom fraud since its inception. The 2024 iteration expanded to cover all forms of online scams including phishing and impersonation, which are closely related to voice phishing tactics. The involvement of ASEANAPOL highlights the Asia-Pacific dimension, and the I-GRIP mechanism enabled rapid cross-border fund interception critical for phone scam cases where speed of fund movement is a key challenge.
