# Children removed from harm in Mexico and two men charged with child abuse after international investigations

**Joint media release** — Queensland Police Service (QPS), Australian Federal Police (AFP), Mexico's Office of the Attorney General (FGR) through the Specialized Prosecutor's Office for Organized Crime (FEMDO), and Mexico's Secretariat of Security and Citizen Protection (SSPC).

**Published**: 08 May 2026, 4:01 pm AEST (Australian Federal Police — News Centre)

## Operation overview

Two Mexican men have been detained and ordered to stand trial, and multiple Mexican child victims are in the process of being identified and brought to safety, following international child-protection investigations driven by Australian intelligence.

Queensland Police Service (QPS) **Task Force Argos** members provided detailed intelligence to AFP Liaison Officers based in Mexico about two separate cases of Mexican-based men allegedly selling child abuse material online.

The AFP in Mexico City worked closely with the FGR through FEMDO, and with the SSPC, to enable Mexican authorities to obtain judicial warrants for the two suspects.

## Case 1 — Yucatán, 7 April 2026

On 7 April 2026, investigators from the **SSPC, FGR-FEMDO, and the Secretariat of the Navy (Secretaría de Marina)** detained a 38-year-old man in the state of Yucatán, Mexico, after executing an arrest warrant and a search warrant at his residence.

Mexican authorities allege the man offered football training to groom and control children, whom he then sexually abused and recorded during the commission of the offences. He is alleged to have subsequently sold the images and videos online and to have exploited children for approximately a decade.

He has been ordered to stand trial for **aggravated human trafficking** — namely inducing persons under 18 years of age to engage in sexual acts or acts of sexualised exhibitionism (whether real or simulated) for the purpose of producing recorded material and deriving economic benefit from that exploitation.

Multiple child victims are in the process of being identified. Analysis of electronic devices seized during execution of the warrants is ongoing, with the objective of identifying as many child victims as possible.

## Case 2 — Nayarit, March 2026

A few weeks before the Yucatán operation, the SSPC and FGR-FEMDO detained a 31-year-old man in the state of Nayarit, in western Mexico, in March 2026. The man is alleged to have sexually abused children and subsequently sold images and videos of the offences through **instant messaging platforms and the dark web**.

Items seized from the suspect's premises include multiple mobile phones, computers and other computer equipment, hard drives and USB storage devices, as well as items of clothing and bedding.

One child has been brought to safety, and investigations are continuing into other potential child victims. The man has been ordered to stand trial for alleged sexual offences committed against children, with the laying of additional charges not ruled out.

## Cooperation channel

The investigation originated in QPS Task Force Argos (Brisbane-based victim-identification unit). Intelligence was passed through AFP Liaison Officers stationed in Mexico City to Mexican authorities, who then obtained domestic judicial warrants. The AFP-FGR/SSPC channel is described in the release as a **long-standing cooperative relationship**; no specific MLAT instrument is cited.

## Notes

- Spanish-language coverage by Mexican outlets (El Imparcial, Por Esto, Infobae, Informador, La Voz de Michoacán, SDP Noticias) attributed to the joint FGR/SSPC press conference identifies the suspects by name as **Carlos Fernando Aguilar González**, 31, detained in Tuxpan, Nayarit; and **Josué Dorantes Escamilla**, 38, captured in Kanasín, Yucatán. The AFP press release withholds names consistent with Australian and Mexican pre-trial reporting practice.
