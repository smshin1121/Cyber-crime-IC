# Leading Dark Web Marketplace Creator and Operator Extradited from Colombia to the United States

**Date:** April 30, 2026
**Publisher (mirror):** Internal Revenue Service — Criminal Investigation (irs.gov)
**Original issuing office:** U.S. Attorney's Office, District of New Jersey (U.S. Attorney Robert Frazer)

## Defendant

Patrick Schmitz, 37, a German national resident in Taganga, Colombia. Co-founder and operator of "The Versus Project" (a.k.a. "Versus") darknet marketplace.

## The Versus Project

A Tor-hidden marketplace that operated from approximately November 2019 to May 2022, enabling users to buy and sell illegal goods including heroin and other illicit drugs, stolen and fraudulent identification documents and access devices, counterfeit currency, malware, and hacking tools. At its peak Versus reportedly had 380,000+ registered users, 32,000+ product listings, and facilitated 300,000+ completed orders worth millions of dollars in transactions before going dark in 2022.

## Schmitz's role

Schmitz was a co-founder of Versus and handled day-to-day management — responding to user support tickets, reviewing vendor applications, and resolving disputes between vendors and customers.

## Timeline

- **November 2019 – May 2022**: Versus Project operates as a Tor-hidden marketplace.
- **June 2024**: Schmitz arrested in Colombia (provisional arrest at the request of the United States).
- **April 29, 2026**: Schmitz extradited from Colombia to the United States; initial appearance before U.S. Magistrate Judge Cari Fais; detained pending trial.
- **April 30, 2026**: Eight-count indictment unsealed by the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of New Jersey.

## Charges (eight counts)

1. Engaging in a continuing criminal enterprise — mandatory minimum 20 years, max life
2. Narcotics conspiracy — mandatory minimum 10 years, max life
3. Conspiracy to import controlled substances — max 20 years
4. Distribution of controlled substances by means of the internet — max 20 years
5. Use of a communications facility — max 4 years
6. Conspiracy to commit access device fraud — max 10 years
7. Conspiracy to unlawfully transfer an identification document — max 15 years
8. Money laundering conspiracy — max 20 years

## International cooperation summary

- **Cooperating jurisdictions (explicit, named):** United States (HSI Newark, IRS-CI Newark, FBI Kansas City, USMS, DOJ OIA / Judicial Attache Bogota, USAO-NJ) and **Colombia (Policía Nacional de Colombia)**.
- **Legal mechanism:** Extradition (provisional arrest in June 2024 → formal extradition April 29, 2026), supported by the bilateral US–Colombia extradition framework and DOJ Office of International Affairs through the Judicial Attache.

## Key findings

- First extradition from Colombia of a darknet-marketplace operator to face U.S. federal charges in 2026.
- Demonstrates routine but high-value use of the US–Colombia extradition channel for non-traditional crimes (darknet/cyber-enabled crime), with a 22-month delay from provisional arrest to surrender.
- DOJ explicitly credits the Policía Nacional de Colombia as a cooperation partner — meets ≥2-country IC threshold.
