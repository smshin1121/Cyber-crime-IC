Kosovo: With ICITAP Support, 280 Kosovo Police Recruits Graduate As Leadership Vows Accountability and Reform Amid Tragedy

Blog Post

Friday, July 11, 2025

Share

Facebook

X

LinkedIn

Email

On July 11, a total of 280 police recruits graduated from the Kosovo Police Basic Academy as the 62nd class at the Youth and Sports Center in Pristina, in a ceremony attended by Kosovo’s Deputy Prime Minister, the Minister of Internal Affairs, the General Director of Police, the class president, families of the graduates, international community members, ICITAP staff, U.S. Embassy representatives—including the ICITAP Attaché representing Embassy leadership—senior Kosovo officials, the Mayor of Pristina, and the Minister of Justice. The graduation occurred just four days after the death of a 27-year-old man in police custody, an event addressed publicly by the General Director of Police, who expressed condolences to the family, condemned any misuse of police authority, and emphasized that the Police Inspectorate, Internal Affairs, and other institutions would fully investigate the circumstances of the death. He affirmed that Kosovo Police uphold the law without prejudice and that abuses of authority would not be tolerated, while acknowledging the dedication of officers and investigators working to ensure accountability. The ceremony highlighted the critical role of professional, well-trained officers in building public trust, promoting justice, and supporting both national and international security. ICITAP has supported Kosovo Police in these efforts by providing sustained training, mentoring, and guidance, in coordination with the U.S. Department of State’s Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs (INL) and the U.S. Embassy in Pristina. Through these partnerships, ICITAP strengthens Kosovo’s law enforcement institutions, enhances accountability, and equips officers to serve the public with integrity and professionalism, ensuring that the principles of justice, transparency, and respect for human rights remain central to policing in Kosovo. This graduation represents both a milestone in capacity building and a reaffirmation of public service values.
