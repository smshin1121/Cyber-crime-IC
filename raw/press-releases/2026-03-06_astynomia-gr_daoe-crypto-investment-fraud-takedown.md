## Source Archive Record

This record preserves the official Hellenic Police (Ελληνική Αστυνομία) press release dated 2026-03-06 announcing the dismantling by the Directorate for the Confrontation of Organised Crime (Διεύθυνση Αντιμετώπισης Οργανωμένου Εγκλήματος, Δ.Α.Ο.Ε.) of a transnational multi-member criminal organisation that operated a cryptocurrency-investment Ponzi network from at least 2019, defrauding 73 identified victims of at least €760,900 with a total illicit gain exceeding €14.5 million.

## English Summary

- Publisher: Hellenic Police Headquarters (Αρχηγείο Ελληνικής Αστυνομίας) — official press release ("ΔΕΛΤΙΟ ΤΥΠΟΥ").
- Date of release: 2026-03-06 (Athens). Action day: 2026-03-05 (early morning).
- Lead unit: Department for the Confrontation of Organised Crime of Crete (Τμήμα Αντιμετώπισης Οργανωμένου Εγκλήματος Κρήτης, Τ.Α.Ο.Ε.Κ. Κρήτης), part of the Directorate for the Confrontation of Organised Crime (Δ.Α.Ο.Ε.).
- Action geography (Greece): Crete (Κρήτη), Attica (Αττική), and Igoumenitsa (Ηγουμενίτσα, in Thesprotia regional unit).
- Arrests: 12 (members of the criminal organisation). Six additional persons named in the case file, of whom two are members of the organisation.
- Charges: criminal organisation, aggravated fraud, money laundering, provision of crypto-asset services without certification, concealment of assets to obtain bankruptcy declaration, and weapons-law offences.
- Operating since: at least 2019.
- Modus operandi: Ponzi structure (πυραμιδικό σύστημα / σχήμα Ponzi); legitimate-looking corporate vehicles and online platforms; in-house "academy" 90-day training programme; 21 internal ranks (e.g., "ambassador", "leader", "founder's council"); referral/bonus rewards; private confidentiality agreements; rebranding when platforms collapsed.
- Hierarchy: 4-person leadership nucleus, 3 senior recruiters, 7 operational members, plus support members.
- Total illicit gain: more than €14.5 million.
- Identified victims at preliminary inquiry: 73 (financial damage at least €760,900).
- On-site forensic finding: Hellenic Police Forensic Sciences Division (Διεύθυνση Εγκληματολογικών Ερευνών) — Sub-Directorate for Digital Forensic Investigation and Analysis — identified circulation of digital currencies exceeding the equivalent of USD 2,000,000 at current value across seized digital evidence.
- Cyber Crime Directorate (Διεύθυνση Δίωξης Κυβερνοεγκλήματος) contributed digital technical investigation and digital-currency flow analysis.
- Hellenic Capital Market Commission (Επιτροπή Κεφαλαιαγοράς) — supervisor of crypto-asset business — provided a unit during the operation.

### International cooperation explicitly named in the source

- **European Investigation Orders (Ευρωπαϊκές Εντολές Έρευνας / EIO)** issued to: France (Γαλλία), Germany (Γερμανία), Malta (Μάλτα).
- **Mutual Legal Assistance (Αμοιβαία Δικαστική Συνδρομή / MLA)** requests issued to: United Kingdom (Ηνωμένο Βασίλειο), Ireland (Ιρλανδία), Denmark (Δανία).
- **Bulgarian offshore corporate vehicles**: two members of the criminal organisation are linked to four offshore companies in Bulgaria (Βουλγαρία) — established in cooperation with the Greek National Anti-Money-Laundering Authority (Εθνική Αρχή Καταπολέμησης της Νομιμοποίησης Εσόδων από Εγκληματικές Δραστηριότητες).

### Seizures (from house and business searches)

- €37,210 cash and ALL 4,000 (Albanian Lek)
- 4 luxury vehicles
- 1 military assault rifle with magazine and 67 cartridges; 2 pistols; 2 hunting weapons; 57 additional cartridges
- Crypto mining equipment ("μηχανήματα εξόρυξης")
- Numerous digital exhibits (USB drives, hard disks, telephony items)
- Documents confirming the organisation's activity, transaction records with crypto-related platforms, hand-written notes/agendas of victim payments, platform credential cards, and offshore-company-related documents.
- Notification to the National Anti-Money-Laundering Authority for asset-freezing action covering bank accounts in Greece and abroad, digital wallets, securities, financial products, safe-deposit boxes, and offshore companies. Seized funds will go to the Loans and Consignments Fund (Ταμείο Παρακαταθηκών και Δανείων); seized digital exhibits and weapons to the Forensic Sciences Division.
- Arrestees brought before the competent prosecutorial authority.
