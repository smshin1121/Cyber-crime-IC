## Arrest posture

- Ostapenko and Oleynik were arrested on 1 December 2024 in a coordinated international action by:
  - Netherlands Fiscal Information and Investigative Service (FIOD / Fiscale Inlichtingen- en Opsporingsdienst)
  - Finnish National Bureau of Investigation (NBI / Keskusrikospoliisi, KRP)
  - U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), Atlanta Field Office
- Tarasov remains at large.

## Conduct alleged

- Operated **Blender.io** as a cryptocurrency mixer from 2018 through approximately April 2022 (ceased one month before OFAC sanctions of 6 May 2022).
- After Blender.io ceased, the same operators allegedly relaunched the service as **Sinbad.io** in approximately October 2022, marketed as a successor mixer with a "no logs" policy and active deletion of transaction traces.
- Sinbad.io infrastructure was seized and the entity was sanctioned by OFAC on 29 November 2023.

## Customers / illicit proceeds laundered

DOJ release identifies the mixers as having processed proceeds from:

- **Lazarus Group / DPRK state-sponsored hacking**, including:
  - Axie Infinity Ronin Bridge heist of approximately USD 620 million (March 2022) — proceeds laundered through Blender.io
  - Subsequent DPRK heist proceeds laundered through Sinbad.io (Treasury 2023 designation cited Horizon Bridge USD 100M (June 2022) and Atomic Wallet USD 100M (June 2023) flows)
- **Ransomware groups**: TrickBot, Conti (formerly Ryuk), Sodinokibi (REvil), GandCrab
- General virtual-currency theft, wire fraud, and dark-market proceeds

## Officials quoted

- Principal Deputy Assistant Attorney General **Brent S. Wible**, DOJ Criminal Division: "the defendants made it easier for state-sponsored hacking groups and other cybercriminals to profit from offenses."
- U.S. Attorney **Ryan K. Buchanan** for the Northern District of Georgia: the services "were allegedly used by criminals across the world to launder funds stolen from victims of ransomware."
- Statements also issued by FBI Atlanta Special Agent in Charge.
