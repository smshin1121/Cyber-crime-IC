# Sexual exploitation of children: 12 children identified during Europol's Victim Identification Taskforce — CBZC participation

**Publisher**: Centralne Biuro Zwalczania Cyberprzestępczości (CBZC) — Polish Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime
**Publish date**: 24 April 2026
**Operation timeframe**: 13–24 April 2026
**Venue**: Europol headquarters, The Hague, Netherlands

## Key facts (extracted from primary CBZC release)

- 34 victim-identification specialists from Europol and INTERPOL together with experts from 31 countries worldwide gathered at Europol's headquarters in The Hague between 13 and 24 April 2026 for a coordinated Victim Identification Taskforce (VIDTF) action.
- The taskforce tentatively identified 12 children depicted in child sexual abuse material (CSAM) circulating online and generated 204 leads, which will be forwarded to national authorities for further investigation.
- 317 datasets were analysed during the action period; cumulative VIDTF figures since 2014 reported: 330 perpetrators arrested, 1,190 victims secured, over 118 million files processed since 2016.
- Officers of Poland's CBZC participated as the Polish national experts within the VIDTF cohort.

## Cybercrime-IC relevance

This is a recurring Europol-hosted Victim Identification Taskforce (VIDTF) — a standing international cooperation mechanism through which national victim-identification specialists pool images, files, and intelligence to attribute CSAM victims to real-world children for safeguarding and to attribute perpetrators for downstream investigation. The April 2026 iteration is a discrete VIDTF action within Europol's EC3 child-protection workflow.
