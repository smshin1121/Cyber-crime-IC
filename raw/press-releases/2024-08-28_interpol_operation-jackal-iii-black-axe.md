## Summary

INTERPOL coordinated Operation Jackal III from 10 April to 3 July 2024 across 21 countries on five continents, targeting online financial fraud and the West African organized crime syndicates behind it, particularly Black Axe. The operation resulted in approximately 300 arrests, the identification of over 400 additional suspects, the blocking of more than 720 bank accounts, and the seizure of assets worth USD 3 million. Multiple criminal networks were dismantled worldwide. Black Axe and similar groups are considered responsible for the majority of the world's cyber-enabled financial fraud, with business email compromise (BEC) as a primary crime type.

## Key Data Points

- **Operation name:** Operation Jackal III
- **Date:** 10 April - 3 July 2024
- **Lead agency:** INTERPOL
- **Participating countries:** 21 (across five continents)
- **Arrests:** ~300
- **Additional suspects identified:** 400+
- **Bank accounts blocked:** 720+
- **Assets seized:** USD 3 million
- **Crime types:** Business email compromise (BEC), online financial fraud, money laundering, counterfeiting
- **Target organization:** Black Axe and similar West African organized crime groups
- **Notable regional results:**
  - Argentina: Dismantled Nigerian-led transnational criminal network after 5-year investigation; Federal Police seized USD 1.2 million in "supernotes" (high-quality counterfeit banknotes), arrested 72 suspects, froze ~100 bank accounts
  - Ireland: 63 arrested by gardai, 17 charged (including 6 juveniles), 17 bank accounts under fake names blocked
  - Switzerland: Police seized cocaine, approximately EUR 45,000 in cash, arrested multiple suspects involved with West African organized crime syndicates
- **Money laundering methods:** Large transfers to Nigerian bank accounts, cryptocurrency transactions, sophisticated money laundering operations
- **Enforcement actions:** Arrests, bank account blocking, asset seizure, network dismantling
- **Series context:** Third iteration of Operation Jackal; follows Jackal (2023, 103 arrests) and Jackal I (2022, 75 arrests)

## Relevance to BEC/International Cooperation

Operation Jackal III represents a significant escalation in the international response to Black Axe-linked BEC and financial fraud, nearly tripling the arrest count from the 2023 Operation Jackal (103 arrests to ~300). The five-continent scope across 21 countries demonstrates the global reach of West African BEC syndicates and the corresponding need for multinational enforcement coordination. The operation's success in blocking 720+ bank accounts highlights the financial infrastructure disruption approach that is critical for combating BEC, where stolen funds are rapidly laundered through international banking networks. The Argentina case, involving a 5-year investigation culminating in 72 arrests and the seizure of counterfeit currency, illustrates the intersection of BEC fraud with broader financial crime networks.
