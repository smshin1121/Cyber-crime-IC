Three former DHS employees were imprisoned for stealing proprietary government software and databases containing personal information on 200,000 federal workers. Charles K. Edwards (former Acting Inspector General) received 18 months, Murali Y. Venkata received 4 months, and Sonal Patel received probation. The defendants shared stolen materials with India-based software developers to create a commercial product, with obstruction of investigation involving deleted communications with the foreign contractors.
