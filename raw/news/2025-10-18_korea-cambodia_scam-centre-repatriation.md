## Summary

In October 2025, South Korea repatriated 64 South Korean nationals from Cambodia who were held for suspected links to online scam operations including voice phishing, romance scams, and "pig butchering" cryptocurrency fraud. Seoul's National Police Agency sought warrants for 58 of the 64, with one already arrested and five released. The suspects had been operating from scam compounds in Cambodia where workers are lured through fake job offers and trafficked into conducting fraud against victims worldwide. The South Korean government estimated approximately 1,000 of its citizens were working in Cambodian scam centres at the time. By January 2026, a total of 107 suspects had been repatriated from Cambodia, along with more than 20 additional suspects from Laos, Vietnam, Thailand, and the Philippines. The 73 suspects repatriated in January 2026 allegedly scammed fellow Koreans out of KRW 48.6 billion (USD 33 million). This repatriation effort coincided with US and UK sanctions against the Cambodia-based Prince Group for running scam centres across the region.

- **Additional repatriations:** 20+ suspects from Laos, Vietnam, Thailand, Philippines

- **Estimated Koreans in Cambodian scam centres:** ~1,000
- **Crime types:** Voice phishing, romance scams, pig butchering (cryptocurrency investment fraud), online fraud
- **International context:** Concurrent US/UK sanctions against Prince Group (Cambodia-based crime network)
- **Countries involved:** South Korea, Cambodia, Laos, Vietnam, Thailand, Philippines

## Relevance to Voice Phishing / Phone Scams

This operation directly targeted voice phishing operations run from Southeast Asian scam compounds. The case illustrates the evolution of voice phishing from China-based call centres to Southeast Asian trafficking-fuelled scam compounds. Korean nationals were both perpetrators (operating scam calls) and victims (trafficked under false job promises). The scale of the problem -- an estimated 1,000 Korean citizens working in Cambodian scam centres alone -- demonstrates the magnitude of transnational voice phishing infrastructure targeting Korean victims.
