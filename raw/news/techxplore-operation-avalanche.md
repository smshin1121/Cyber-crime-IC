### Key Findings

The source is incorrectly linked to the Avalanche operation page and should be reassigned to a reWired/BEC operation page or de-linked
- The actual article: AFP wire story about Operation reWired, a coordinated global crackdown against business email compromise (BEC) fraud that resulted in 281 total arrests — 167 in Nigeria, 74 in the United States, plus arrests in Turkey (18), Ghana (15), France, Italy, Japan, Kenya, Malaysia and Britain
- Financial seizures: Nearly US$3.7 million in funds seized globally; Nigerian authorities recovered $169,850 and 92 million naira (approximately $250,000 at 2019 exchange rates) directly from arrested suspects
- Operation reWired was jointly launched by the FBI and Nigeria's Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC) in May 2019, making this coverage a ~4-month operational wrap-up announcement
- EFCC Director quoted characterizing the results as 'tremendous successes' against 'the infamous Yahoo Yahoo boys' — the colloquial Nigerian term for online scammers/BEC operators. This framing is significant for analyzing Nigerian political/law enforcement attitudes toward cybercrime
