CyberScoop reported on the March 31, 2021 guilty plea of Tal Prihar, an Israeli national arrested by French authorities at Charles de Gaulle Airport in Paris in May 2019. Prihar admitted to conspiracy to commit money laundering for operating DeepDotWeb, a clearnet portal that referred users to darknet markets selling drugs, firearms, malware, and hacking tools. The article confirms Prihar received roughly 8,155 bitcoins (~$8.4 million) in referral kickbacks and concealed the proceeds via shell-company bank accounts. Prosecutors described the case as "the single most significant law enforcement disruption of the Darknet to date."
