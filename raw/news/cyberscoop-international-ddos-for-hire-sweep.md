### Key Findings
- Between 5-9 December 2016, FBI, Europol and law enforcement from 12 European nations plus Australia conducted a coordinated sweep targeting paying users of DDoS-for-hire (stresser/booter) services, resulting in 34 arrests and ~101 additional suspects interviewed or cautioned
- CyberScoop reports the majority of arrestees were male teenagers, characterized as 'script kiddies' — a demographic note that directly shaped the parallel awareness/prevention campaign launched alongside the sweep
- The operation was a follow-on to earlier Europol enforcement actions begun in January 2016, demonstrating sustained multi-year targeting of stresser/booter user populations rather than a one-off action
- Law enforcement publicly emphasized that while many juvenile participants 'do not realise the consequences', penalties are severe; the campaign framed youth offenders as having skills suited for legitimate cybersecurity careers — an early example of diversion-oriented messaging
- Europol statement quoted: 'The individuals arrested are suspected of paying for stressers and booters services to maliciously deploy software to launch DDoS attacks' — confirming the operation targeted users rather than operators
- CyberScoop's coverage does not assign an operation name (unlike Operation Power OFF later in the lineage), consistent with the FBI/Europol framing of this as an 'international cyber sweep' rather than a named operation
