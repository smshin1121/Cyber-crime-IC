SecurityWeek reports Iranian national Sina Gholinejad, 37, pleaded guilty in the Eastern District of North Carolina to one count of computer fraud and abuse and one count of conspiracy to commit wire fraud for his role in the Robbinhood ransomware scheme that hit Baltimore in 2019, causing more than $19 million in damages. Co-conspirators compromised victim networks of U.S. cities, corporations, and healthcare organizations. Gholinejad faces up to 30 years in prison and is scheduled to be sentenced in August 2025. The FBI Charlotte Field Office led the investigation with substantial assistance from FBI Baltimore.
