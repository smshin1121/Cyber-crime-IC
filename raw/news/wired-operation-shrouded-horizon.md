### Key Findings
- Wired reported on 15 July 2015 on **Operation Shrouded Horizon**, an 18-month international FBI-led investigation that culminated in the July 2015 seizure of the Darkode cybercrime forum and the arrest or search of suspects across 20 countries
- Darkode was an invite-only password-protected cybercrime forum with ~250-300 vetted members who traded malware, exploits, stolen data, and criminal services; administrators heavily vetted prospective members to resist LE infiltration
- Law enforcement infiltrated Darkode via undisclosed means (likely an undercover informant or compromised member account) and collected evidence over the 18-month investigation period prior to the takedown
- Operational results: searches, equipment seizures, or arrests of ~70 individuals globally, and indictments against 12 US defendants for computer fraud, wire fraud, money laundering, malicious code distribution, spamming, identity theft, racketeering, bank fraud, extortion, and access device fraud
- Lead agency: US FBI, with Europol coordination; participating countries included the US, UK, Australia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Brazil, Canada, Colombia, Costa Rica, Croatia, Cyprus, Denmark, Finland, Germany, Israel, Latvia, Macedonia, Nigeria, Romania, Serbia, and Sweden
- The FBI called Operation Shrouded Horizon 'the largest-ever coordinated law enforcement effort directed at an online cyber criminal forum' — a claim that held true until subsequent 2018 Infraud and later operations
