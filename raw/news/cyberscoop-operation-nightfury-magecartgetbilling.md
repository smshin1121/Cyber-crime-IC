### Key Findings
- 3 Indonesian men arrested on 20 December 2019 in Jakarta and Yogyakarta for Magecart-style web skimming attacks, announced by INTERPOL on 27 January 2020 as 'Operation NightFury'
- Suspects faced up to 10 years in prison under Indonesian law if convicted
- Security firm Sanguine Labs reported the group — tracked by Group-IB as 'GetBilling' — had infected 571 online marketplaces globally; INTERPOL's public statement referred to 'hundreds of websites' affected
- Group-IB provided forensic threat-intelligence data that enabled the identification of the Indonesia-based operators — an example of direct private-sector–to–INTERPOL cooperation channeled through ASEAN partner states
- INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation (IGCI) in Singapore coordinated the multi-agency operation, connecting Indonesian and Singaporean law enforcement
- Notable human-element detail: one arrested suspect reportedly told Indonesian media he had earned barely enough from the skimming operation to buy a jacket — suggesting modest financial proceeds despite wide technical reach, and consistent with the low-skill-affiliate layer of the Magecart ecosystem
