### Key Findings
- INTERPOL's Operation Delilah resulted in the March 2022 arrest of a 37-year-old Nigerian man identified as a senior figure in the SilverTerrier BEC fraud network, detained upon attempting to re-enter Nigeria after fleeing arrest in 2021
- Researchers (Palo Alto Networks Unit 42, Group-IB, Trend Micro) traced more than 240 domains registered to the suspect's aliases, with 50+ used for malware command-and-control; the group is assessed to have targeted ~50,000 victims since 2014
- Operation Delilah followed an earlier 2021 SilverTerrier takedown that arrested more than a dozen additional suspects, confirming a phased pattern of BEC enforcement rather than a single-day sweep
- Cooperation flowed through INTERPOL with Nigerian Police Force execution, supported by private-sector threat-intelligence partners — a template commonly used for West African BEC investigations where MLAT channels are slow
- BEC losses reported to the FBI IC3 reached nearly US$2.4 billion in 2021, the largest category of cybercrime loss; Operation Delilah is part of INTERPOL's long-running effort against Nigerian BEC actor groups
- CyberScoop's reporter Tonya Riley provides context connecting Delilah to Operation Falcon (2020) and Operation Falcon II (2021) — the broader SilverTerrier suppression campaign
