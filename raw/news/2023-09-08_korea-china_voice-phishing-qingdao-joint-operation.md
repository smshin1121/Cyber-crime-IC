## Summary

The Seoul Metropolitan Police Agency (SMPA), in cooperation with the Chinese police, arrested 16 suspects -- 3 Chinese nationals and 13 Koreans -- for allegedly extorting KRW 2.7 billion (USD 2 million) from 68 victims through voice phishing scams operated from December 2022 through August 2023. The SMPA launched an investigation in April 2023 after receiving a victim report and detained three Korean suspects domestically. The Chinese police detained 12 additional members, along with the 38-year-old Chinese ringleader, at the group's office in Qingdao on August 24, 2023, following a request for cooperation from Korean police. The ringleader had established the Qingdao office in December 2022 and recruited members for the scam operation. This was reported as the first significant roundup of voice phishing scammers in China following a bilateral agreement between Seoul and Beijing law enforcement authorities to jointly combat voice phishing.

- **Countries involved:** South Korea, China

## Relevance to Voice Phishing / Phone Scams

This is a direct Korea-China bilateral voice phishing enforcement action and represents a landmark case as the first significant joint operation under the Seoul-Beijing bilateral cooperation framework for combating voice phishing. The operation targeted a China-based call centre that was running voice phishing scams against Korean victims, the classic Korea-China voice phishing pattern. The successful cooperation between SMPA and Qingdao police established a precedent for the larger-scale joint operations that followed, including the major voice phishing syndicate dismantling under HAECHI V in 2024.
