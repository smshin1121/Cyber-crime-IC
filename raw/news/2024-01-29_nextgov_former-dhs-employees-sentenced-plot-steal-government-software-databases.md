Three former DHS employees were convicted of conspiring to steal government software and law enforcement databases between 2014 and 2017. Charles K. Edwards, the former acting inspector general, received 18 months in prison for theft of Microsoft product activation keys valued at $350,000. The trio intended to create a commercial case management system using stolen data containing sensitive information on over 200,000 federal employees from DHS and USPS oversight offices.
