### Key Findings
- BSI describes Avalanche as one of the world's largest botnet infrastructures and states that the Verden public prosecutor's office and the Lueneburg Central Criminal Inspectorate took it down on 30 November 2016 with international partners.
- BSI says sinkhole servers were used to identify infected systems and that victim-notification and remediation work continued after the takedown.
- The page enumerates malware families seen in the Avalanche infrastructure, including Andromeda/Gamarue, banking trojans, ransomware and downloader families.

### Source Page Summary

BSI's Avalanche background page supplies the German technical and victim-remediation perspective on the takedown. It confirms the 30 November 2016 action, explains the supporting role played by BSI, and documents that the disruption did not itself clean infected machines, requiring prolonged follow-up notification and remediation. This source is a primary German-government reference for how public authorities handled victim notification, sinkholing and post-takedown cleanup. It also helps connect operation-avalanche and andromeda-botnet-takedown because BSI explicitly lists Andromeda/Gamarue among the malware families hosted on the infrastructure.
