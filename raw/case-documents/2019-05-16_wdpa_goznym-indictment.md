## United States v. Konovolov et al. — GozNym Network Indictment

On 16 May 2019, the US Attorney's Office for the Western District of Pennsylvania unsealed an indictment charging 10 members of the GozNym criminal network with conspiracy to commit computer fraud, conspiracy to commit wire fraud and bank fraud, and conspiracy to commit money laundering.

The GozNym network exemplified "cybercrime as a service," assembling specialists recruited from underground criminal forums. The network used GozNym malware to infect over 41,000 victim computers and steal online banking credentials, attempting to steal approximately USD 100 million from victims primarily in the United States.

### Key Defendants

- **Alexander Konovolov** (aka "NoNe"), 35, of Tbilisi, Georgia — primary organizer and leader
- **Marat Kazandjian**, of Kazakhstan/Georgia — technical assistant
- **Viktor Eremenko**, of Ukraine — "casher" converting stolen funds
- **Farkhad Rauf Ogly Manokhin**, of Russia — previously arrested by Norwegian authorities in 2017
- **Krasimir Nikolov** (aka "pablopicasso"), of Varna, Bulgaria — "account takeover specialist," arrested and extradited to US

### International Cooperation Elements

This case was described as an "unprecedented" international law enforcement effort:
- **Six countries** participated: US, Bulgaria, Germany, Georgia, Moldova, Ukraine
- **Europol** coordinated the multinational investigation
- **Eurojust** facilitated judicial cooperation
- **Parallel prosecutions**: Bulgaria, Georgia, Moldova, and Ukraine conducted parallel domestic prosecutions against their own nationals
- **Extradition**: Nikolov was extradited from Bulgaria to the US
- **Manokhin** was arrested in Sri Lanka based on information from Norwegian authorities
