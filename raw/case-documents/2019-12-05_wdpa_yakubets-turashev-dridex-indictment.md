## United States v. Yakubets and Turashev — Dridex/Evil Corp Indictment

On 5 December 2019, the United States and United Kingdom jointly announced criminal charges against Maksim V. Yakubets (aka "aqua"), 32, of Moscow, Russia, related to two separate international computer hacking and bank fraud schemes spanning from May 2009 to the present.

### Charges

A federal grand jury in Pittsburgh returned a 10-count indictment charging Yakubets and Igor Turashev with conspiracy, computer hacking, wire fraud, and bank fraud in connection with the distribution of "Bugat" (later known as "Cridex" and "Dridex"), a multifunction malware package designed to automate the theft of banking credentials.

### Key Facts

- Yakubets was the leader of the Evil Corp cybercriminal organization operating from Moscow
- Dridex malware infected computers in 40+ countries and caused over USD 100 million in theft
- Estimated USD 10 million in direct US losses, GBP 20 million in UK losses
- According to the US Treasury, Yakubets also provided direct assistance to the Russian government's malicious cyber efforts

### Sanctions and Reward

- **OFAC sanctions**: 17 individuals and 7 entities associated with Evil Corp designated under IEEPA
- **USD 5 million reward** offered under the Transnational Organized Crime Rewards Program — largest cyber reward at the time

### Related: Ghinkul Arrest and Extradition

Andrey Ghinkul, a Dridex botnet administrator, was arrested in Cyprus in August 2015, extradited to the US in February 2016, and sentenced to time served in December 2018.

### International Cooperation Elements

- **FBI-NCA joint investigation**: FBI (Pittsburgh) and UK National Crime Agency conducted a parallel investigation
- **Europol EC3 and J-CAT**: Coordination and intelligence sharing
- **Germany BKA**: Investigation support
- **Botnet sinkholing**: Conducted in October 2015 by FBI, NCA, Dell SecureWorks, and Shadowserver Foundation
- **Extradition from Cyprus**: Ghinkul extradited via US-Cyprus arrangement
- **MLAT**: US-UK MLAT and US-Moldova MLAT used for evidence sharing
