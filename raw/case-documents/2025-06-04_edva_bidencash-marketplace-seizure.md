## In re Seizure of BidenCash Marketplace Domains and Cryptocurrency

On 4 June 2025, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of Virginia announced the seizure of approximately 145 darknet and traditional domains and cryptocurrency funds associated with the BidenCash marketplace. DOJ stated that the court had authorized the cryptocurrency seizure and that the domains were redirected to a U.S. law-enforcement-controlled server.

According to DOJ, BidenCash had supported more than 117,000 customers, trafficked over 15 million payment card numbers and associated personally identifiable information, and generated more than USD 17 million in revenue. DOJ further stated that between October 2022 and February 2023 the marketplace publicly dumped 3.3 million stolen payment cards as a promotional tactic.

The seizure matter is highly relevant for international-cooperation analysis because DOJ thanked the Dutch National High Tech Crime Unit, Searchlight Cyber, and The Shadowserver Foundation for investigative assistance. The case therefore appears to combine U.S. seizure warrants with cross-border infrastructure action and private-sector technical support rather than criminal charges against identified operators.
