## United States v. John Telusma

On 27 May 2022, the Department of Justice announced that John Telusma of Brooklyn, New York, had been sentenced to four years in prison for his role in the Infraud Organization, a transnational cybercrime enterprise responsible for more than USD 568 million in losses. The sentencing followed Telusma's guilty plea to one count of racketeering conspiracy on 13 October 2021 in the District of Nevada.

According to court documents summarized by DOJ, Telusma joined Infraud in August 2011 and remained active for approximately five and a half years. DOJ described him as one of the more prolific Infraud members, purchasing and fraudulently using compromised credit card numbers for personal gain within the forum's criminal marketplace.

The release also noted that the Justice Department's Office of International Affairs provided significant assistance in the investigation and prosecution. That detail strongly suggests that cross-border evidence collection or defendant-location issues remained material even in this later follow-on sentencing phase of the broader Infraud case.
