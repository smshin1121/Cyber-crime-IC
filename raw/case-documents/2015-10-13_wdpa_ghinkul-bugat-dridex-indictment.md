## United States v. Ghinkul — Bugat/Dridex Botnet Administrator

On 13 October 2015, the DOJ announced a nine-count indictment against Andrey Ghinkul (aka "Smilex"), a Moldovan national, for criminal conspiracy, unauthorized computer access with intent to defraud, damaging a computer, wire fraud, and bank fraud in connection with the Bugat/Dridex botnet.

### Key Facts

- Ghinkul was arrested in Cyprus in August 2015
- Extradited to the United States in February 2016
- Pleaded guilty
- Sentenced to time served in December 2018
- Ghinkul was a botnet administrator who helped distribute the Dridex malware

### Simultaneous Technical Disruption

On the same day the indictment was announced, the FBI, UK NCA, Dell SecureWorks, and other partners executed a technical disruption (sinkholing) of the Dridex botnet's peer-to-peer network.

### International Cooperation Elements

- **Arrest in Cyprus**: Ghinkul was arrested by Cypriot authorities at US request
- **Extradition**: US-Cyprus extradition arrangement
- **UK NCA**: Conducted parallel investigation and led the technical sinkholing
- **Dell SecureWorks**: Private-sector partner providing technical disruption capability
