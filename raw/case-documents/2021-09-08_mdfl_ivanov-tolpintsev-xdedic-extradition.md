## United States v. Glib Ivanov-Tolpintsev

On 8 September 2021, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Middle District of Florida announced the extradition of Glib Oleksandr Ivanov-Tolpintsev from Poland. DOJ said he was charged with conspiracy, trafficking in unauthorized access devices, and trafficking in computer passwords in a case tied to the xDedic Marketplace.

According to DOJ, Ivanov-Tolpintsev used automated tools to decrypt the credentials of thousands of compromised computers around the world and sold those credentials on a dark-web marketplace. The later sentencing announcement stated that he received four years in prison and forfeited USD 82,648.

The case is useful because it shows that xDedic enforcement extended to credential suppliers who monetized access at scale and were extradited from third countries.
