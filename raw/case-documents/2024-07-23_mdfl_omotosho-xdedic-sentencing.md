### Source Page Summary

On 23 July 2024, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Middle District of Florida announced the sentencing of Bamidele Omotosho to 12 years and 7 months in prison for conspiracy to commit wire fraud, conspiracy to commit money laundering, and computer intrusion. DOJ stated that Omotosho and his coconspirators purchased stolen access credentials and PII on xDedic and then used that data to run multiple fraud schemes. According to DOJ, the Omotosho-led group committed at least six different fraudulent schemes totaling more than USD 7.5 million in intended losses and more than USD 2 million in actual losses. The court ordered restitution of USD 2,051,108.70. The case is one of the strongest downstream-harm examples in the xDedic ecosystem because it ties marketplace purchases to a broad bundle of real-world frauds and a substantial prison term.
