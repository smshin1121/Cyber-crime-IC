## Emotet Botnet Disruption — Operation LadyBird

On 27 January 2021, the Department of Justice announced the disruption of the Emotet botnet, described by Europol as "the world's most dangerous malware," in an internationally coordinated operation known as Operation LadyBird.

### Legal Action (US Component)

The FBI obtained a court-authorized operation in the Middle District of North Carolina. Foreign law enforcement, working in collaboration with the FBI, replaced Emotet malware on servers in their jurisdictions with a law enforcement-created file designed to sever communication between infected computers and the botnet's operators. The remediation ran from approximately 25 January through 25 April 2021.

### Scale of the Botnet

- Emotet infected over 1.6 million computers worldwide between April 2020 and January 2021
- Approximately 45,000 infected computers were located in the United States
- One school district in M.D.N.C. suffered USD 1.4 million in losses from an Emotet infection

### International Cooperation (8 Countries)

The operation involved law enforcement from the Netherlands, Germany, United States, United Kingdom, France, Lithuania, Canada, and Ukraine.

- **Netherlands (NHTCU)**: Took control of Emotet infrastructure and deployed the update/uninstall module
- **Germany (BKA)**: Seized 17 C2 servers
- **Ukraine (Cyberpolice)**: Raided premises in Kharkiv, arrested 2 suspects
- **Europol EC3**: Operational coordination
- **Eurojust**: Judicial coordination via Joint Investigation Team

### Arrests

Two Ukrainian citizens were arrested in Kharkiv by Ukrainian Cyberpolice. They were identified as individuals who "ensured the proper functioning of the infrastructure for the spread of the virus." Ukrainian officials stated the suspects faced up to 12 years in prison under Ukrainian law.

> [!note] No US indictments
> Unlike the Silk Road or Dridex cases, no individual defendants were charged by name in US federal court. The US component was a disruption order, not a criminal prosecution of named individuals. The Ukrainian arrests were conducted under Ukrainian domestic law.
