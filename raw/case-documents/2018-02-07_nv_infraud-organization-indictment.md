## United States v. Bondarenko et al. — Infraud Organization Indictment

On 7 February 2018, a nine-count superseding indictment was unsealed by a Las Vegas, Nevada, grand jury charging 36 defendants for their alleged roles in the Infraud Organization, a transnational cybercrime enterprise that caused more than USD 530 million in actual losses and intended losses exceeding USD 2.2 billion.

The Infraud Organization was created in October 2010 by Svyatoslav Bondarenko (aka "Obnon," "Rector," "Helkern"), 34, of Ukraine, as the premier destination for "carding" — purchasing retail items with counterfeit or stolen credit card information. Operating under the slogan "In Fraud We Trust," the organization had 10,901 registered members at its peak.

Key defendants included: Sergey Medvedev (aka "Stells," "segmed"), co-founder and administrator, of Russia; and 34 additional defendants from multiple countries. Charges included racketeering conspiracy (RICO), identity theft, wire fraud, and computer fraud.

### Arrests and International Cooperation

Thirteen defendants were arrested across seven countries: United States, Australia, United Kingdom, France, Italy, Kosovo, and Serbia. Medvedev was arrested in Thailand.

### Subsequent Sentencings

- Sergey Medvedev: pleaded guilty to racketeering conspiracy, sentenced to 10 years (March 2021)
- Marko Leopard (North Macedonia): sentenced to 5 years (2019)
- John Telusma (Brooklyn, NY): sentenced to 4 years (2022)
- Multiple other defendants pleaded guilty and received sentences ranging from probation to 10 years
