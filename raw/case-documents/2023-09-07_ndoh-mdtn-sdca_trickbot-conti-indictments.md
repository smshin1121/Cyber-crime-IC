## United States v. Galochkin et al. — TrickBot/Conti Indictments

On 7 September 2023, the Department of Justice unsealed three indictments in three federal districts charging nine Russian nationals with conspiracy related to the TrickBot malware and Conti ransomware operations.

### Northern District of Ohio — TrickBot Indictment

All nine defendants were charged with conspiring to use TrickBot malware to steal money and personal information beginning in November 2015. Maximum penalty: 62 years each.

**Defendants**: Maksim Galochkin (aka "Bentley," "Volhvb"), Maksim Rudenskiy (aka "Buza"), Mikhail Tsarev (aka "Mango"), Andrey Zhuykov (aka "Defender," "Dif"), Dmitry Putilin (aka "Grad," "Staff"), Sergey Loguntsov, Max Mikhaylov (aka "Baget"), Valentin Karyagin (aka "Globus"), Maksim Khaliullin (aka "Kagas").

### Middle District of Tennessee — Conti Indictment

Galochkin, Rudenskiy, Tsarev, and Zhuykov were charged with conspiring to use Conti ransomware to attack 900+ victims worldwide in 47 US states and 31 foreign countries, beginning 2020 through June 2022. Maximum penalty: 25 years each.

### Southern District of California — Additional Charges

Galochkin was charged under a separate indictment (23CR1166-LAB).

### International Cooperation Elements

- **Arrest of Vladimir Dunaev** in South Korea (September 2021), extradited to US; sentenced to 64 months (January 2024)
- **Arrest of Alla Witte** in Miami (February 2021); sentenced to 32 months (June 2023)
- TrickBot infected an estimated 1-3 million devices worldwide
- Conti ransomware caused hundreds of millions of dollars in damages

### Prior Related Actions

The TrickBot botnet was disrupted in October 2020 through a combined effort of US Cyber Command and Microsoft's civil action, which disabled 120 of 128 C2 servers.
