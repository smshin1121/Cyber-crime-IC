### Source Page Summary

On 15 September 2021, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Middle District of Florida announced the unsealing of two indictments charging Olufemi Odedeyi, Ibrahim Davies, Adetunji Adejumo, and Ibrahim Jinadu with conspiracy to commit wire fraud, theft of public money or property, and aggravated identity theft. DOJ's later xDedic roundup linked this tax-fraud conspiracy to access and PII obtained from xDedic-compromised servers. DOJ said Odedeyi and Davies were apprehended in London by the United Kingdom's National Extradition Unit and detained pending extradition to the United States. The scheme allegedly involved unauthorized access to U.S. business servers, theft of U.S. residents' identifying information, and false tax filings seeking millions of dollars in refunds.
