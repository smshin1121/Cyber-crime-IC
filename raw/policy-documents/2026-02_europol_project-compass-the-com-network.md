Project Compass unites law enforcement agencies to tackle The Com. Due to the complexity and decentralised nature of this network, this project assists law enforcement in effectively preventing, detecting, and investigating the network's criminal activities.

## Background

In recent years, there has been an increase in extremist groups using digital platforms to commit and promote acts of extortion, manipulation, and extreme violence. These groups organise themselves into various subgroups, sometimes forming large transnational virtual networks (TVNs). Their goals include personal gain, the advancement of their ideologies, or ultimately the destabilisation of society.

These networks specifically target vulnerable members of society to carry out and participate in acts of violence, including self-harm, harm to others, harm to animals, and the production of child sexual exploitation material (CSAM). A prominent example of these kinds of networks is the nihilistic extremist network "The Com" (short for Community), which targets, victimises, and recruits minors and vulnerable young people.

The Com operates openly on various online platforms, including social media, communication and messaging apps, music platforms, and online games. It is structured into a series of subgroups, each with different objectives:

- **Cyber Com**: Targets online commerce or infrastructure by orchestrating network intrusions and deployment of ransomware.
- **Offline Com**: Encourages damage to property, harm to others or acts of terrorism to promote a violent nihilistic worldview.
- **(S)extortion Com**: Extorts minors to commit both online and offline sex crimes, as well as the promotion of self-harm and suicide, crimes against people, animals and property.

Within The Com, group **764** is notorious for its recruitment and grooming tactics, as well as its levels of violence. Since 2021, this decentralised and highly dangerous online group has continued to target young people, encouraging them to participate in violent acts and create explicit content. This material is then used to blackmail victims or shared among group members. Over time, the group's activities have become intertwined with violent extremist groups and other harmful networks, such as those promoting satanic or accelerationist ideologies, thereby escalating the potential for harm and violence.

## Objectives

Project Compass is an ongoing effort aimed at strengthening law enforcement cooperation and coordination in the fight against the phenomenon known as The Com. Its main objectives include:

- Establishing a reliable network of Member States and Third Countries that assists with law enforcement investigations. Encouraging and enabling practical cooperation among partners.
- Promoting the exchange of information, expertise, and effective strategies with collaborators.
- Assisting with current investigations conducted by various units, including counter-terrorism (CT), CSAM, and organised crime units.
- Planning and executing data sprints, where all of the invited countries present their ongoing cases.
- Advising on preventative measures that need to be taken to tackle this phenomenon.
