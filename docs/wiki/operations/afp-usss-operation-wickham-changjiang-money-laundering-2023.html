개요

Operation Wickham(위컴 작전)은 AFP(호주 연방경찰)과 USSS(미국 비밀경호국) 간의 합동수사이다. AFP는 Wickham이 2022년 8월 22일에 개시되었다고 기술하고 있는데, 이는 USSS가 AFP 주도의 합동 사이버범죄 조정센터(JPC3)에 미국에서의 투자사기 수법으로 발생한 자금이 호주 사업자 은행 계좌로 이체되고 있다는 사실을 통보한 직후의 일이다. AFP는 호주 측 수사를 Operation Wickham이라는 이름으로 진행하였다.

2023년 10월 26일자 AFP 보도자료는 Operation Wickham 하에 기소된 일곱 번째 피의자—멜버른 거주 37세 중국 국적자—를 발표하였는데, 동 피의자는 Changjiang Currency Exchange(장강 환전소) 관련 계좌 및 광역 Long River 자금세탁 조직이 통제하는 호주 내 가공 사업체 네트워크를 통하여 1억 호주달러(AUD) 이상을 세탁한 혐의를 받고 있다. AFP는 이와 관련된 투자사기 수법으로 전 세계적으로 1억 6,000만 호주달러 이상이 편취된 것으로 추산하며, 사기 수익으로 추정되는 자금 2,100만 호주달러 이상이 호주 내 은행 계좌에서 동결 조치되었다고 보고하였다.

본 수사는 진행 중인 것으로 기술되어 있으며, 본 페이지는 Wickham을 Long River / Changjiang 네트워크 와해 위에 자금세탁 혐의가 지속적으로 추가되고 있는 여전히 진행 중인 합동수사로 다룬다.

⚠️ Source-side asymmetry 본 위키의 공개 보도 내용은 AFP의 진술에 기초하고 있다. 미국 측 투자사기 피해와 관련한 USSS 및 미국 국토안보수사국(HSI)의 진술, 그리고 광역 Long River / Changjiang 테이크다운에 관한 2023년 10월 25일자 AFP 병행 보도자료는 아직 본 위키에 수록되지 않은 상태이다. 수치 주장(전 세계 편취액 1억 6,000만 호주달러; 일곱 번째 피기소인이 단독으로 세탁한 1억 호주달러; 동결 자금 2,100만 호주달러)은 모두 AFP의 진술에 따른 것으로, 본 페이지에서 독립적으로 검증된 것은 아니다.

배경

광역 Long River 자금세탁 조직과 그 운영 부문으로 알려진 Changjiang Currency Exchange(장강 환전소)는 본 Operation Wickham 보도자료 하루 전인 2023년 10월 25일자로 별도 발표된, 보다 광범위한 AFP 자금세탁 테이크다운—"Seven syndicate members charged for allegedly laundering almost AUD 229 million"(약 2억 2,900만 호주달러 자금세탁 혐의로 7명 기소)이라는 표제 하의 발표—의 대상이다. 동 병행 테이크다운은 Changjiang / Long River 인프라를 체포, 자산동결명령 및 병행 기소로 둘러쌌다. Operation Wickham은 동일 네트워크에 대한 AFP-USSS의 사이버범죄 합동수사 관점으로서, 구체적으로는 사기로 발생한 수익—특히 조작된 MetaTrader 및 암호화폐 거래 플랫폼을 통한 미국 피해자 대상 투자사기 수익—이 동 호주 송금 회랑을 통해 흘러간 부분의 자금세탁에 초점을 맞춘다.

호주 측은 국내 자금세탁 및 미등록 송금업 범죄를 표적으로 하며, 미국 측은 미국 내 투자사기 피해를 다룬다. 두 측면을 합쳐 보면, 호주-미국 양자 사이버범죄 협력이 호주 측 정보 푸시가 아닌 미국 측에서 유출되는 사기 수익에 대한 선제적 통보로 시작될 수 있음을 보여 준다.

법적 근거

AFP 보도자료는 본 합동수사의 구체적인 형사사법공조조약(MLAT) 또는 조약 근거를 명시하고 있지 않다. 협력은 USSS의 AFP JPC3에 대한 의뢰 후 AFP-USSS 합동수사라는 형태로 기술되어 있으며, 기소 및 자산동결명령 절차는 호주 국내법에 따라 집행된다. 일곱 번째 피기소인에게 적용된 두 가지 혐의는 다음과 같다.

  1. 일반범죄 수익(1,000만 호주달러 초과 가치) 거래에 결과적으로 이르는 행위에 무모하게 가담—최대 15년의 자유형.
  2. 미등록 송금 서비스 제공—최대 2년의 자유형.

Wickham을 지원하는 호주-미국 간 공식 형사사법공조 트래픽(예: AFP의 미국 피해자 측 증거 수령, AFP 금융기록 자료의 USSS / DOJ 측 환류 등)은 본 공개 보도자료에서 다루어지지 않았다.

개요Summary

Operation Wickham is a joint investigation between the Australian Federal Police (AFP) and the United States Secret Service (USSS). AFP describes Wickham as having opened on 22 August 2022, after USSS notified the AFP-led Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3) that funds generated through investment-fraud schemes in the United States were being transferred into an Australian business bank account. AFP took the Australian-side investigation forward as Operation Wickham.

The 26 October 2023 AFP media release announces the seventh alleged offender charged under Operation Wickham — a 37-year-old Chinese national, resident in Melbourne — who is alleged to have laundered more than AUD 100 million through accounts associated with the Changjiang Currency Exchange and a network of fake Australian businesses controlled by the wider Long River money-laundering organisation. AFP estimates more than AUD 160 million was stolen worldwide through the underlying investment scams, and reports more than AUD 21 million has been restrained in Australian bank accounts believed to be scam proceeds.

The investigation is described as ongoing; the page treats Wickham as a still-open joint investigation with continuing money-laundering charges layered on top of the Long River / Changjiang network disruption.

배경Background

The wider Long River money-laundering organisation and its alleged operating arm, the Changjiang Currency Exchange, are the subject of a parallel and larger AFP money-laundering takedown announced separately on 25 October 2023 — the day before this Operation Wickham release — under the headline "Seven syndicate members charged for allegedly laundering almost AUD 229 million." That parallel takedown surrounded the Changjiang / Long River infrastructure with arrests, restraining orders and parallel charges. Operation Wickham is the AFP-USSS cybercrime joint-investigation lens onto the same network: it focuses specifically on the laundering of scam-derived proceeds — particularly from US-victim investment fraud routed through manipulated MetaTrader and cryptocurrency trading platforms — that flowed through that Australian remittance corridor.

The Australian leg targets domestic money-laundering and unregistered-remittance offences, while the US side concerns the underlying investment-fraud victimisation in the United States. Together, the two legs illustrate how AU–US bilateral cybercrime cooperation can begin with a US-side proactive notification on outbound scam proceeds rather than an Australian intelligence push.

법적 근거Legal Framework

The AFP release does not name a specific MLAT or treaty basis for the joint investigation. Cooperation is described in terms of a USSS-to-AFP JPC3 referral followed by a joint AFP–USSS investigation; charges and restraining-order proceedings are executed under domestic Australian law. The two charges laid against the seventh-charged accused are:

  1. Recklessly engaging in conduct that resulted in dealing with proceeds of general crime (value exceeding AUD 10 million) — maximum 15 years' imprisonment.
  2. Providing unregistered remittance services — maximum 2 years' imprisonment.

Any formal mutual legal assistance traffic between Australia and the United States that supports Wickham (e.g., for receipt of US victim-side evidence by AFP, or for AFP financial-records material flowing back to USSS / DOJ) is not addressed in the public release.

작전 타임라인Operational Timeline

  • 22 August 2022 — operation opened: USSS advises AFP JPC3 that millions of dollars in US-investment-fraud proceeds are being transferred into an Australian business bank account; AFP opens Operation Wickham as the Australian-side joint investigation.
  • 22 August 2022 – 25 October 2023 — investigation: AFP works the Australian-side money-laundering layer; six earlier defendants are charged across this period (not separately indexed in this wiki at time of ingest).
  • 25 October 2023 — search warrants: AFP executes search warrants at residences in Sydney and Melbourne; the seventh defendant is taken into custody locally (no extradition).
  • 26 October 2023 — public release: AFP announces charges against the seventh alleged offender — a 37-year-old Chinese national resident in Melbourne — for laundering AUD 100 million+ through the Changjiang Currency Exchange and Australian shell-company accounts.
  • Post-26 October 2023 — ongoing: Operation Wickham continues; further charges and restraining-order proceedings are not detailed in the release.

결과 및 영향Results and Impact

  • Charges to date: 7 alleged offenders charged under Operation Wickham as of 26 October 2023.
  • Restrained funds: AUD 21 million+ in Australian bank accounts believed to be scam proceeds.
  • Allegedly laundered (seventh-charged accused alone): AUD 100 million+ via the Changjiang Currency Exchange and Australian shell-company accounts.
  • Worldwide scam losses (estimated): AUD 160 million+, predominantly with US victims.
  • Search activity: Warrants executed at Sydney and Melbourne residences on 25 October 2023.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

  • Police-to-police joint investigation: AFP–USSS, channelled via the AFP-led JPC3 cybercrime liaison structure. Categorised here as informal-cooperation in the absence of a cited treaty basis in the public release.
  • Domestic-charging vehicle: Australian proceeds-of-crime and unregistered-remittance offences as the disruption tool for the Australian-side laundering layer.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Treaty-citation opacity: The public release does not reveal whether US–Australia MLAT mechanisms (or the budapest-convention's evidence-cooperation provisions) supported any evidentiary transfer between USSS and AFP. Wickham, in its public form, illustrates how high-trust bilateral cybercrime cooperation can run on liaison channels and joint-investigation framing without a public MLAT citation, leaving the formal-mechanism layer invisible to outside observers — a recurring cross-border-investigation transparency gap.
  • Embedded-laundering corridor: Wickham targets a laundering layer hosted inside a nominally legitimate AU currency-exchange / remittance corridor (Changjiang Currency Exchange / Long River), requiring AML and unregistered-remittance charges rather than treaty-based extradition or production orders to make disruption stick.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • USSS-to-AFP proactive notification on outbound scam-proceeds flows is, on the Wickham record, sufficient on its own to open a high-value, multi-defendant Australian-side money-laundering investigation — strengthening the case for institutionalising standing trust channels (here JPC3) between cybercrime liaison units rather than relying solely on case-by-case MLAT requests for follow-the-money work across the AU–US corridor.
  • Major scam-money laundering layers can sit in nominally legitimate currency-exchange and remittance corridors inside the requested-state economy. Wickham illustrates that domestic AML and unregistered-remittance offences may be the practical disruption tool for those layers, even where the underlying fraud victimisation is mainly extra-territorial.
