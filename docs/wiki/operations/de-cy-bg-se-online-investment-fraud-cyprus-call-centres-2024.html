개요Summary

On 22 January 2024, Eurojust coordinated a synchronised action day across Germany, Cyprus, Bulgaria, and Sweden that dismantled a criminal network operating fraudulent online investment trading platforms from call centres in Cyprus. Four suspects were arrested. The Eurojust press release identifies cooperating prosecutorial and law-enforcement authorities in all four jurisdictions by name.

배경Background

According to the Eurojust release, the criminal network ran fake online trading platforms from physical call centres in Cyprus. Operators impersonated investment advisors and persuaded victims to deposit funds in the belief they were buying into legitimate, profitable trading. Two of the main platforms were associated with German-victim losses of approximately EUR 3.3 million and EUR 164,000 respectively, with total European damages believed to be substantially higher.

결과 및 영향Results and Impact

  • Arrests: four suspects.
  • Targets: fraudulent online trading platforms operated from call centres in Cyprus.
  • Reported damages: approximately EUR 3.3 million and EUR 164,000 attributed to two platforms targeting German victims; total European damages believed to be substantially higher.
  • Cooperation breadth: four EU jurisdictions, with prosecutorial leads in Germany and Bulgaria and policing leads in Cyprus and Sweden.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

  • Eurojust coordination meeting: judicial coordination across four jurisdictions to plan and synchronise the action day.
  • European Investigation Order: standard mechanism for cross-border evidence gathering and searches in EU cybercrime fraud cases of this configuration.
  • JIT / coordinated investigation: the configuration of a German prosecutorial home, a Cypriot operational base, a Bulgarian prosecutorial node, and a Swedish policing partner is consistent with the JIT model used by Eurojust in cyber-enabled investment-fraud cases.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Cross-border call-centre fraud: fraud-call-centre infrastructure in Cyprus targeting victims primarily in Germany requires both physical-location enforcement (Cyprus) and prosecutorial action where complaints originate (Germany), placing high demands on judicial cooperation.
  • Multilingual operational playbook: investment-fraud call centres typically organise operators by language, so victim outreach and witness work span multiple jurisdictions even when the call centre itself is in one country.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • Eurojust coordination enabled a single-day synchronised arrest and search action across four EU jurisdictions where the harm pattern, the call-centre infrastructure, the fraud proceeds chain and the prosecutorial home of the criminal complaint were each located in a different country.
  • The case is a representative example of how Eurojust-coordinated investment-fraud crackdowns since 2020 have followed a recurring template: prosecutorial home in one EU member state where victims complained; physical call-centre takedown in the host state; ancillary searches in further EU jurisdictions where infrastructure or facilitators are located.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

No Korean involvement reported in the Eurojust release. The fraud network targeted EU-resident victims via call centres in Cyprus; there is no public indication that Korean victims, Korean infrastructure, or Korean law-enforcement counterparts were involved.
