개요Summary

On 2025-11-12, the Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Berlin (Berlin Public Prosecutor's Office, Abteilung 285) and the Landeskriminalamt Berlin (LKA Berlin, Dezernat 253) issued a joint press release (No. 273) announcing the operational results of a seven-week cross-border action campaign against the "Enkeltrick" (grandparent / shock-call) phone-fraud phenomenon, running from 2025-09-15 to 2025-10-31.

The campaign was conducted under the EU-funded "ISF-LUMEN" project (Internal Security Fund), coordinated by the LKA Baden-Württemberg. It brought together police authorities from all 16 German federal states, the German Federal Criminal Police Office (BKA), Austria, Poland, Switzerland, Slovakia, Czech Republic, and Europol, under the authority of the competent specialised divisions of the responsible state prosecutors' offices.

Operational results were 16 arrests (one 26-year-old female caller; three male callers aged 24, 25 and 49; twelve money couriers aged 22-63), the dismantling of three call centres (Frankfurt am Main, Austria, Warsaw), the prevention of 44 attempted Enkeltrick cases, and aggregate prevented damages of EUR 2,007,330.

배경Background

수법 (모더스 오페란디)Modus operandi

The "Enkeltrick" (literally "grandchild trick") and the "Schockanruf" (shock call) are well-established Polish-organised phone-fraud patterns targeting elderly residents primarily in Germany. Callers — often Polish nationals — exploit telephone-directory entries to pick targets with traditional first names suggesting older birth-year cohorts. The script opens with an impersonated relative crying about a fatal car accident or other family emergency, then transitions to an impersonated police officer, prosecutor, or judicial official demanding immediate cash or jewellery "bail" payment to release the relative from supposed criminal custody. The victim is kept on the line continuously while a "money courier" (Geldabholer) is dispatched to the victim's door to collect the cash/jewellery in person. Calls are routed via VoIP through international gateways to disguise origin numbers (often spoofed as local German emergency or court numbers), and operate out of dedicated call-centre rooms ("Keiler-Zellen" / "Schockanruf-Räume") staffed by multiple callers working shifts under a cell leader.

피해자 프로파일 및 영향Victim profile and impact

Victims are predominantly elderly residents in Germany (and to a lesser degree Austria, Switzerland), selected by traditional-first-name screening of telephone directories. Per the Bundeskriminalamt's broader 2022-2026 Schockanruf reporting cited by the Berlin Generalstaatsanwaltschaft, the German victim demographic is concentrated in the 70+ age bracket, frequently living alone, and the "shock-call" pretext exploits the victim's emotional response to a fabricated relative-in-crisis scenario to bypass normal financial caution. The 2025 LUMEN action campaign prevented 44 attempted Enkeltrick cases and EUR 2,007,330 in aggregate damages — i.e., the prevented-loss figure represents the value that would have been transferred from elderly victims had the calls succeeded. Across the wider Bundeskriminalamt counter-Schockanruf effort (2022-2026), cumulative damages reached EUR 53 million; the LUMEN-2025 campaign contributed to a ~50% recent reduction in reported cases and damages.

자금 흐름Financial flow

Per the Berlin Generalstaatsanwaltschaft release and parallel BKA reporting, the Schockanruf cash-flow is a courier-based "hand-to-hand" model rather than a remote-transfer model: (i) the victim hands cash and/or jewellery to the Geldabholer (money courier) at the victim's door, ostensibly as "bail" for the supposedly arrested relative; (ii) the courier transports the cash to a regional Logistiker (logistics coordinator) — typically a separate individual to insulate the callers from physical handling of the proceeds; (iii) the Logistiker arranges aggregation and onward transfer of cash and jewellery, often by physical cross-border transport to Poland or Austria (the parallel Austria-Slovakia-Czechia Operation Casus case identified a 44-year-old Czech Logistiker from Ostrava convicted in Vienna for >EUR 500,000 in attributed damage). The cash is typically converted on the back-end via jewellery resale and structured deposits; the 12 money couriers arrested in the 2025 LUMEN action were aged 22-63, suggesting a recruited-mule pool rather than a tight family-based cell. Cryptocurrency or e-money was not the principal monetisation channel in the Schockanruf typology — physical cash and jewellery dominate.

범죄 조직 구조Criminal organization structure

The Schockanruf/Enkeltrick OCG model is a multi-tier Polish-organised structure with functional specialisation: (i) organisers/financiers based in Poland (typically not arrested in action sweeps), (ii) call-centre cell leaders managing 3-10 shift callers per cell, (iii) callers (Keiler) operating from physical call-centres in Poland, Germany, Austria, or Czech Republic, (iv) money couriers (Geldabholer) — recruited locally in the destination country, often Polish, German, or other EU nationals on temporary contract, (v) logistics coordinators (Logistiker) handling cash aggregation and cross-border transport. The 2025 LUMEN action dismantled three call-centre cells (Frankfurt am Main, Austria, Warsaw) and arrested 16 individuals — four callers (one female caller aged 26; three male callers aged 24, 25, 49) plus twelve money couriers. The cell-leader and organiser tiers, typically Poland-resident, were not enumerated in the 2025-11-12 release. The pattern is high-frequency and low-individual-loss in absolute terms but high-aggregate-damage. It does not lend itself to single-day synchronised takedown because the call-centres and the money couriers are organisationally distinct and geographically dispersed across at least Germany, Poland and Austria.

법적 근거Legal Framework

The action was conducted under domestic German criminal procedure (Strafprozessordnung) on the instructions of each German federal state's responsible specialised public prosecutor's office, with cross-border investigative steps in Austria, Poland, Switzerland, Slovakia, and the Czech Republic conducted under standard EU police-cooperation channels including informal direct contacts, Europol intelligence exchange, and — where required — mutual legal assistance.

The umbrella legal-policy framework is the EU Internal Security Fund (ISF), specifically the ISF-LUMEN project coordinated by the LKA Baden-Württemberg.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • The "Enkeltrick" / shock-call phenomenon has a long-standing Polish-organised structure that has previously resisted single-day takedowns (cf. Operation Pandora 2024 which targeted a parallel but distinct ring operating from Albania, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Kosovo and Lebanon). The 2025 LUMEN campaign opted for the seven-week sustained-tempo format precisely because of the high frequency and dispersed operational footprint of this pattern.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • Sustained-tempo "action week" format over seven weeks across all 16 German federal states plus five foreign jurisdictions enabled real-time coordination on a high-frequency phone-fraud pattern that does not lend itself to single-day synchronised takedown.
  • EU ISF project funding (ISF-LUMEN, coordinated by LKA Baden-Württemberg) provided the cross-border operational umbrella under which the prosecutorial home of each criminal complaint (16 German federal states) could be matched in real time to the location of the call-centre and the money courier (often in Poland or Austria).
