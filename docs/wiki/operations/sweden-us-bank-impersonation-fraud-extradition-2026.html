개요Summary

On 2026-04-11, Eurojust (the European Union Agency for Criminal Justice Cooperation) publicly announced that the alleged main perpetrator of a large-scale Swedish bank-employee-impersonation fraud scheme had been extradited from the United States to Sweden on 2026-04-10. The transfer was executed from U.S. Marshals Service custody in the Central District of California to Swedish law enforcement, after a US extradition proceeding handled by the U.S. Department of Justice's Office of International Affairs (office-of-international-affairs) and the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Central District of California. The extradition followed a coordinated November 2024 action day in which the main perpetrator was arrested in the United States and two accomplices were arrested in Sweden. Throughout the entire arc — from pre-action-day investigation to extradition handover — Eurojust provided the judicial-coordination spine.

The underlying alleged conduct is a telephone-based bank-employee-impersonation fraud scheme operating since approximately 2019, in which fraudsters called victims claiming to be bank employees and induced them to execute money transfers to criminal-group-controlled accounts. At least 25 victims were defrauded with estimated damages totalling approximately EUR 6 million; some victims lost their entire life savings.

배경Background

The alleged criminal group operated a vishing (voice phishing) fraud pattern that is a recurring template in EU consumer fraud: the caller poses as a bank-fraud-prevention employee, instructs the victim to "secure" their account by transferring funds to a "safe" account, and uses real-time scripted social engineering to walk the victim through the transfer. The Eurojust release does not name the bank(s) impersonated, the recruitment/operations geography of the criminal group, or the technical infrastructure used (call-routing, money-mule structure). The scheme is described as operating since "approximately 2019," giving a roughly five-to-six-year operational window before the November 2024 action day.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

The Eurojust release does not enumerate any frictions or challenges. The ~17-month interval between the November 2024 action day and the April 2026 extradition is consistent with normal US extradition timelines (which typically include initial complaint, extradition hearing, certification of extraditability, Secretary of State surrender review, and physical handover), and is characterised in the release as a "long process" but not as troubled.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • Eurojust as durable coordination spine across action-day → extradition phases. A single coordinating-body relationship (Eurojust ↔ Swedish prosecutors and police; Eurojust ↔ US DOJ OIA / USAO) carries continuous information-sharing across more than 17 months and across two distinct enforcement phases (arrest action day and extradition handover). This is a reusable cooperation template for future EU member-state ↔ US transnational fraud cases.
  • Bank-employee-impersonation vishing as a Sweden–US cooperation pattern. The fact that the main perpetrator was located in the US while victims were in Sweden — a classic third-country-principal pattern in EU consumer fraud — confirms that the US is increasingly a destination state for Swedish-victim vishing principals, requiring active Sweden–US extradition pathways.
