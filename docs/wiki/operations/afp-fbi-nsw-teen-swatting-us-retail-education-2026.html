AFP-FBI Joint Investigation — NSW Teen Charged with 12 Hoax 'Swatting' Calls Against US Retail and Education Institutions (Jan 2026)

개요Summary

On 13 January 2026 the Australian Federal Police (AFP) and the United States Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) issued a joint media release announcing the AFP's arrest and charging of a juvenile from regional New South Wales (NSW) alleged to have made 12 hoax "swatting" calls between October 2025 and January 2026 to US emergency services, each falsely reporting an active mass-shooting at major US retailers or schools. The investigation began in November 2025 after the FBI provided originating intelligence to the AFP indicating an Australia-based offender was responsible. The AFP arrested the juvenile on 9 January 2026 with NSW Police assistance, seized one prohibited firearm with ammunition, and the juvenile was remanded in custody after a NSW Children's Court appearance on 10 January 2026.

배경Background

Swatting — placing false reports of imminent violent emergencies to provoke an armed police response against an innocent victim — has been a persistent transnational cybercrime since the mid-2010s. The AFP press release situates this case within Taskforce Pompilid, the AFP's specialist taskforce established in October 2025 to disrupt the "decentralised online crime network" responsible for swatting, sadistic exploitation of youth, cyberattacks on telecommunication companies, and violent extremist material. The taskforce explicitly works with Five Eyes Law Enforcement Group partners.

참여 기관Participating Parties

  • Australian Federal Police — lead investigative agency in Australia, charged the juvenile, executed the search warrant.
  • United States Federal Bureau of Investigation — originated the lead by providing information to the AFP in November 2025 that an offender located in Australia was responsible for the US hoax calls; FBI Assistant Director Jason A. Kaplan (Criminal Investigative Division) is quoted on the joint release.
  • NSW Police — provided domestic Australian assistance during the search-and-arrest action.

법적 근거Legal Framework

  • Australian charging statutes: section 474.14(1) Commonwealth Criminal Code Act 1995 (carriage service to cause harm or menace, max 5 years per count) and section 7(1) Firearms Act 1996 (NSW) for the prohibited firearm.
  • Five Eyes Law Enforcement Group informal cooperation served as the operating mechanism for the FBI-to-AFP tip, with no formal Mutual Legal Assistance Treaty (MLAT) request necessary given the offender was within Australian jurisdiction.

작전 타임라인Operational Timeline

  • October 2025 – January 2026: 12 hoax mass-shooting calls allegedly placed against major US retail and education institutions.
  • October 2025: AFP launches Taskforce Pompilid against the decentralised online crime network.
  • November 2025: FBI provides information to AFP indicating an offender based in Australia was responsible.
  • 9 January 2026: AFP, with NSW Police assistance, executes search warrant at the juvenile's regional NSW property, arrests the juvenile, seizes one prohibited firearm with ammunition, multiple electronic devices, and digital communications.
  • 10 January 2026: First Children's Court appearance; juvenile remanded in custody.
  • 13 January 2026: Joint AFP-FBI media release announces the charges.

결과 및 영향Results and Impact

  • 1 juvenile arrested and charged with 12 counts under section 474.14(1) Cth Criminal Code and 1 prohibited firearm count under section 7(1) Firearms Act 1996 (NSW).
  • 1 prohibited firearm and ammunition seized.
  • Multiple electronic devices and digital communications seized for forensic analysis.
  • Disruption of an active, ongoing swatting campaign against US institutions; AFP characterises the case as part of broader Five Eyes disruption of the decentralised online crime network.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

  • Informal police cooperation — FBI-to-AFP tip through Five Eyes Law Enforcement Group channels without formal MLAT.
  • Joint AFP-FBI media release format — coordinated public messaging at the announcement.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Juvenile defendant requires NSW Children's Court process rather than adult federal criminal court.
  • US victim institutions and states are not publicly named in the joint release, which is consistent with FBI and AFP standard practice for swatting victims but limits public attribution.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • The tip-and-charge model (FBI intelligence → AFP domestic prosecution) is faster than extradition when the offender is identified within an allied Five Eyes jurisdiction.
  • Naming both lead-agency commanders (AFP Cybercrime Commander Marshall, FBI Assistant Director Kaplan) in the same release reinforces that the operation is a joint AFP-FBI effort, not a unilateral Australian action.

후속 조치Follow-Up Actions

  • Australian prosecution to proceed in NSW Children's Court; no extradition expected.
  • Taskforce Pompilid investigation continues against other members of the decentralised online crime network; AFP indicates further action against affiliates is anticipated.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

No Korean involvement is mentioned in the source material. The AFP-FBI joint release does not list ROK as a Five Eyes partner (ROK is not a Five Eyes member) and no Korean victims, jurisdictions, or LE assistance are referenced.

모순점 및 미해결 문제Contradictions & Open Questions

  • The joint release does not disclose which US states, schools, or retailers were targeted, which limits cross-validation against any US-side prosecutorial filings.
  • Whether any US criminal charges have been or will be filed in parallel is not stated; the AFP press release indicates the Australian prosecution is the announced disposition.
