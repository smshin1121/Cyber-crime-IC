개요Summary

On 23 April 2026, the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) issued 29 sanctions designations targeting Cambodian Senator Kok An and 28 individuals/entities in his scam-compound network under E.O. 13694 as amended. Same-day, the Scam Center Strike Force (USAO-DC + DOJ Criminal Division + FBI + USSS) announced parallel actions: charges against 2 individuals running a Burma scam compound and attempting to start one in Cambodia, the seizure of a social media messaging app used to recruit human-trafficking victims, and the seizure of 503 fraudulent web domains used for cryptocurrency investment fraud. The action is undertaken in furtherance of E.O. 14390 of 6 March 2026 ('Combating Cybercrime, Fraud, and Predatory Schemes Against American Citizens') — a new presidential authority specifically directed at foreign-backed cybercrime / cyber-enabled fraud / extortion targeting Americans.

📝 Scam Center Strike Force The release introduces (in the wiki's corpus) the Scam Center Strike Force as a formal US multi-agency cooperation framework: USAO-DC + DOJ Criminal Division + FBI + USSS, focused on Burma, Cambodia, and Laos. Establishes a named US-side institution complementing OFAC sanctions with parallel criminal prosecutions.

배경Background

Underlying scope: Americans lost USD 10 billion+ in 2024 to Southeast Asia-based scam operations (66% YoY increase). Scam pattern: digital asset investment fraud + romance/friendship lures + human-trafficking forced labor (passport confiscation + debt bondage + physical violence to coerce scam labor). The Cambodian network operates out of casinos and office parks retrofitted for fraudulent activity in Poipet, Sihanoukville, Bavet, and Phnom Penh.

This action chains to prior OFAC actions: - 12 Nov 2025: Burmese armed group + 7 others (Tai Chang scam compound, Karen State, Burma) - 14 Oct 2025: Prince Group TCO + 146 associates (US-UK joint) - 8 Sep 2025: 12 companies + 7 individuals (Cambodia/Burma) - 12 Sep 2024: Ly Yong Phat, L.Y.P. Group + 4 hotels

Together, these constitute a sustained ~18-month sanctions campaign against SE-Asia scam-compound infrastructure.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Cambodian political-protection layer: Kok An is a sitting senator; the release explicitly notes the network operates 'under the protection of Kok An and his political connections'. This likely limits Cambodia-side criminal prosecution and makes US-side OFAC designations + criminal-seizures the primary enforcement tool.
  • Burma + Laos focus area: Strike Force names Burma + Cambodia + Laos as focus areas, but the cited release does not enumerate Laos-side actions today.
  • Casino-as-laundering-vehicle pattern institutionalises laundering at the property level — the release explicitly cites that 'nearly all major scam compounds in Cambodia are connected to casinos, which serve to launder the proceeds of scams'.

교훈Lessons Learned

  1. Scam Center Strike Force is the formal US multi-agency cooperation framework (USAO-DC + DOJ-Crim + FBI + USSS) for SE-Asia scam-compound enforcement. Adds a named US-side institution to the wiki's coverage of SE-Asia scam-compound enforcement.
  2. E.O. 14390 (6 March 2026) is the new presidential authority specifically directed at foreign-backed cybercrime / cyber-enabled fraud / extortion targeting Americans — overarching policy basis for the Scam Center Strike Force.
  3. Sustained ~18-month OFAC SE-Asia sanctions campaign chain: April 2026 (this action) + Nov 2025 + Oct 2025 + Sep 2025 + Sep 2024.
  4. Casino-as-laundering-vehicle pattern: Cambodia's casino-license framework provides a structural laundering vehicle for scam-compound proceeds — relevant to comparative AML analysis on scam-compound networks.
  5. Parallel-track sanctions + criminal seizure model: 29 OFAC designations same day as 2 Strike Force charges + 1 messaging app seizure + 503 web domain seizures + USD 14M State rewards demonstrates the administrative-and-criminal-and-rewards-and-publicity full-spectrum enforcement model deployed against the highest-priority targets.
  6. Cambodian political-protection layer is an explicit structural enforcement obstacle — Kok An is a sitting senator. US enforcement is unlikely to translate to Cambodia-side criminal prosecution without significant Cambodian political change.

후속 조치Follow-Up Actions

The release notes that Treasury 'continues to take these actions in close coordination with our international partners to address the shared threat posed by scam centers'. Specific follow-on extraditions, charging documents, and asset-recovery proceedings are unknown from the cited release. The Daren Li reward suggests an active fugitive-apprehension track.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

South Korea has no documented involvement in this action. Korean victims among the SE-Asia scam-operation victim pool are unknown from the cited release — the release frames the victim base as American. Korean LE cooperation channels (e.g., KNPA Cyber Bureau) are not enumerated.
