개요

2025년 11월 18일, Fiscalía(콜롬비아 검찰) General de la Nación de Colombia는 Boletín 57752를 통해, 2021년부터 2025년 10월까지 사기성 암호화폐·증권 투자 플랫폼을 운영하여 콜롬비아, 칠레, 페루의 피해자로부터 총 COP 71.000 millones (~USD 1,700만) 상당을 편취한 조직범죄 네트워크를 해체했다고 발표했다.

수사는 Fiscalía의 Dirección Especializada contra los Delitos Informáticos (DECDI)가 주도하였다. 14명의 혐의 구성원이 검거되어 juez de control de garantías(통제판사)에게 인치되었다. 3명은 centro carcelario(교정시설)에 수감 명령을 받았고, 나머지 11명은 가택구금 명령을 받았다. 14명 중 누구도 혐의를 인정하지 않았으며, 혐의는 estafa por medios informáticos(컴퓨터 매개 사기), hurtos por medios informáticos(컴퓨터 매개 절도), violación de datos personales(개인정보 침해 — 3건 모두 modalidad delito masa[집단범죄 모드]), concierto para delinquir(범죄공모), lavado de activos(자금세탁), enriquecimiento ilícito(불법 축재)를 포함한다.

보도자료에서 명명된 혐의 주모자는 멕시코 국적자 Alejandro Maximiliano Ronquillo Treviño(별명 Daniel)이다.

⚠️ Primary-source scope (L19-strict) Fiscalía 보도자료는 피해자들의 국적국(콜롬비아, 칠레, 페루)과 한 명 피의자의 외국 국적(멕시코)을 명명하나, 외국 법집행기관, 형사사법공조조약(MLAT) 요청, AMERIPOL/INTERPOL(국제형사경찰기구) 채널, 또는 범죄인 인도 메커니즘은 명명하지 않는다. 2차 언론 보도(El Tiempo, Infobae, El Colombiano, La Veintitrés)는 본 작전이 "Operación Dominó"로 명명되었으며, 추가로 이스라엘 국적자 Boaz Iaakok Derra(별명 Jim)가 메데인 El Poblado의 호화 사무실에서 운영을 주도한 주설계자였다고 보고한다. 이러한 주장은 Fiscalía 1차 출처에 포함되어 있지 않으며, 별도의 Tier-1 검증을 거치기 전까지 어떠한 위키 주장도 그 위에 구축되지 않도록 missing_fields에 기재되어 있다.

배경

Fiscalía 보도자료에 따르면:

  • 범죄 조직은 매력적인 소셜미디어 광고를 통해 피해자의 주의를 끌었다.
  • 표적은 그 후 전화로 연락을 받아 단기 수익을 약속하는 스킴에 등록하기 위해 USD 200–250을 송금하도록 설득당했다.
  • 입금된 자금은 결코 투자되지 않았다. 스킴의 aparentes asesores("외양상의 자문가들")는 피해자들과의 소통을 유지하며, 허위 배당 그래프를 제시하고, 출연 금액을 늘리도록 요구하며, 인출이 불가능하다고 경고했다.
  • 피해자들은 자신의 투자 진행 상황을 확인할 수 있다는 명목으로 'Anydesk' 또는 'Imperios'라 불리는 원격 접속 도구를 장치에 설치하도록 지시받았다. 실제로 이 도구들은 범죄 네트워크가 피해자의 휴대전화와 컴퓨터에 원격 제어로 접근하고, 개인 자격증명을 탈취하며, 은행 상품을 장악할 수 있도록 해주었다.
  • 2021년부터 2025년 10월까지의 총 수익은 콜롬비아·칠레·페루 피해자로부터 COP 71.000 millones(2025년 말 환율 기준 약 USD 1,700만)로 추정된다.

활용된 협력 메커니즘

Fiscalía 1차 출처는 어떠한 정식 국제협력 메커니즘(MLAT, AMERIPOL, INTERPOL(국제형사경찰기구) I-24/7, 범죄인 인도, JIT, EIO 등가물)도 명명하지 않는다. 공개된 국제협력 흔적은 다음에 국한된다:

  • 외국 국적 피의자가 콜롬비아에서 구금됨: 멕시코 시민(Ronquillo Treviño).

Fiscalía가 칠레 또는 페루 검찰 당국과 피해자 식별이나 증거 교환에 관해 조정하였는지, 또는 멕시코 당국과 피의자의 기록에 관해 조정하였는지 여부는 본 1차 출처에 공개되어 있지 않다.

개요Summary

On 18 November 2025, the Fiscalía General de la Nación de Colombia announced (Boletín 57752) the dismantling of an organised criminal network that had operated fraudulent cryptocurrency- and securities-investment platforms from 2021 through October 2025, defrauding victims in Colombia, Chile, and Peru of an aggregate COP 71.000 millones (~USD 17 million).

The investigation was led by the Dirección Especializada contra los Delitos Informáticos (DECDI) of the Fiscalía. Fourteen alleged members were captured and presented to a juez de control de garantías. Three were ordered to detention in a centro carcelario; the remaining eleven were ordered to house arrest. None of the fourteen accepted the charges, which include estafa por medios informáticos, hurtos por medios informáticos, violación de datos personales (all three in modalidad delito masa), concierto para delinquir, lavado de activos, and enriquecimiento ilícito.

The alleged ringleader named in the bulletin is a Mexican national, Alejandro Maximiliano Ronquillo Treviño (alias Daniel).

⚠️ Primary-source scope (L19-strict) The Fiscalía bulletin names the countries of victims (Colombia, Chile, Peru) and the foreign nationality of one suspect (Mexico), but it does not name any foreign law-enforcement agency, MLAT request, AMERIPOL/INTERPOL channel, or extradition mechanism. Secondary press accounts (El Tiempo, Infobae, El Colombiano, La Veintitrés) report that the operation was named "Operación Dominó" and that an additional Israeli national, Boaz Iaakok Derra (alias Jim), was the principal architect operating from luxury offices in El Poblado (Medellín). These claims are NOT in the Fiscalía primary source and are listed in missing_fields for separate Tier-1 verification before any wiki claim is built on them.

배경Background

According to the Fiscalía bulletin:

  • The criminal organisation captured the attention of victims through attractive social-media advertising.
  • Targets were then contacted by telephone and convinced to transfer USD 200–250 to enroll in the scheme and obtain supposed short-term gains.
  • The deposited funds were never invested. The aparentes asesores ("apparent advisors") of the scheme maintained communication with victims, showed fictitious dividend graphs, told them to increase their contributions, and warned them that they could not withdraw.
  • Victims were instructed to install on their devices remote-access tools known as 'Anydesk' or 'Imperios', ostensibly so they could verify the progress of their investments. In reality, these tools allowed the criminal network to access and take remote control of victims' phones and computers, seize personal credentials, and take over banking products.
  • The aggregate proceeds, between 2021 and October 2025, are estimated at COP 71.000 millones (≈ USD 17 million at end-2025 rates) drawn from victims in Colombia, Chile, and Peru.

The delito masa modality is the Colombian procedural marker for offences committed against a multitude of victims as part of a unitary criminal plan, allowing charges to be aggregated across the victim population.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Asymmetric disclosure: the prosecuting state names the foreign-victim countries and the foreign-national suspect but does not name any foreign agency cooperation. Open question whether (a) such cooperation occurred and was simply not disclosed, or (b) the case proceeded primarily on Colombia-side evidence.
  • Recovery posture: the bulletin does not disclose any asset-recovery measure (forfeiture, seizure, restitution mechanism) for the COP 71.000 millones in proceeds.
  • Defendant posture: all 14 captured suspects rejected the charges, indicating a contested trial track rather than a plea-resolution track.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • For comparable cross-border investment-fraud takedowns in Latin America, the prosecuting-state press release alone is often the only Tier-1 record at the takedown moment. Multi-jurisdiction Tier-1 corroboration (here: Chilean Fiscalía, Peruvian Fiscalía, Mexican FGR) is the natural follow-on for IC-scope verification.
  • The Anydesk / 'Imperios' remote-access vector + USD 200–250 entry deposit + fictitious dividend graphs + withdrawal-blocked typology is documented verbatim by a Tier-1 prosecutorial source and is consistent with the broader "investment-fraud-with-remote-access-overlay" pattern observed in EU and Asia-Pacific cases (cf. Latvia phone-fraud takedown 2026 for the AnyDesk-impersonation-of-bank parallel).
  • Modalidad delito masa is a Colombian procedural construct that aggregates offences against a multitude of victims; useful comparative reference point for jurisdictions building mass-victim cybercrime indictments.

후속 조치Follow-Up Actions

  • The judicial process is ongoing as of 2025-11-18. The 14 defendants did not accept the charges; trial proceedings are anticipated.
  • Asset-recovery, victim-restitution, and any cross-border MLAT or AMERIPOL/INTERPOL action are not described in the Fiscalía bulletin and remain open.

모순점 및 미해결 문제Contradictions & Open Questions

  • Operation codename: secondary press (El Tiempo, Infobae, La Veintitrés) names this takedown "Operación Dominó", but the Fiscalía Boletín 57752 does not use the codename. The wiki record withholds the codename pending a Tier-1 source that names it.
  • Israeli alleged ringleader: secondary press (El Tiempo, Infobae) names Boaz Iaakok Derra (alias Jim) as the principal architect operating from luxury offices in El Poblado. The Fiscalía bulletin does not name this person and identifies the Mexican national Ronquillo Treviño as the posible cabecilla. Open question whether the Israeli national is a separately wanted person not yet captured (and therefore not in the bulletin's named-suspect list) or whether the secondary-press claim derives from prosecutorial commentary not reduced to the official boletín.
  • Geographic specificity: secondary press places the call-centre in El Poblado, Medellín, with operational raids in Sabaneta, Bello, La Ceja, and Rionegro. The Fiscalía bulletin does not state the specific location of the call-centre or the raid sites.
  • Aggregate financial scale: secondary press reports the network's monthly revenue at ~USD 5 million between 2021 and October 2025, implying a much higher aggregate than the COP 71.000 millones figure. The bulletin's stated aggregate is COP 71.000 millones of victim losses; secondary press's higher figure may include ungrossed gross inflows. Wiki record uses the bulletin figure pending reconciliation.
  • AMERIPOL involvement: Colombia hosts the Centro Especializado Contra el Cibercrimen de AMERIPOL (AC3), but the Fiscalía bulletin does not name AMERIPOL. Whether AC3 contributed to the investigation is an open question.
