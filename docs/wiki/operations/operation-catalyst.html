Operation Catalyst

개요Summary

Operation Catalyst was a 2025 INTERPOL-AFRIPOL operation targeting terrorism-linked financial flows and the illegal activity supporting them. It ran from July to September 2025 across Angola, Cameroon, Kenya, Namibia, Nigeria, and South Sudan.

This page includes the operation because the official source explicitly identifies the participation of financial-crime, cybercrime, and counter-terrorism units, and because the public results include cyber-enabled scams, illicit virtual-asset use, cryptocurrency tracing, and a cryptocurrency-based Ponzi scheme affecting at least 17 countries. It should not be used as a general counter-terrorism record outside those cyber-enabled financial-crime components.

Cooperation Model

  • INTERPOL-AFRIPOL coordination: INTERPOL and AFRIPOL jointly coordinated intelligence sharing among the six participating African countries.
  • Converged unit model: INTERPOL described the operation as the first time financial crime, cybercrime, and counter-terrorism units from multiple African countries joined forces in this context.
  • Private-sector intelligence: The operation used strategic cyber intelligence and data from Binance, Moody's, and Uppsala Security.
  • National enforcement: Participating countries screened persons of interest and entities, froze accounts, made arrests, seized funds, and pursued follow-on asset tracing.

Participating Countries

INTERPOL names Angola, Cameroon, Kenya, Namibia, Nigeria, and South Sudan as participating countries.

Country-level public examples include Angolan informal value-transfer investigations, Kenyan virtual-asset money-laundering and online recruitment matters, Nigerian terrorism-linked arrests, and a transnational cryptocurrency-based Ponzi scheme affecting at least 17 countries.

Boundary and Non-Duplication Notes

Operation Catalyst is distinct from operation-sentinel-africa, operation-red-card-2, and operation-serengeti-2. Those records target cyber-enabled fraud and organized cybercrime more directly. Catalyst is narrower for this corpus: it is retained only for its cyber-enabled financial crime, virtual asset, and money-laundering cooperation elements within a broader terrorism-financing operation.

Follow-on national prosecutions or asset-recovery actions should be linked separately only if public sources identify them as outputs of Operation Catalyst.

Evidence Notes

INTERPOL is the anchor source for the operation name, dates, coordinating bodies, country list, and metrics. HSToday and DefenceWeb provide independent confirmation of the same operation, figures, cyber-enabled scam component, virtual-asset component, and INTERPOL-AFRIPOL coordination.
