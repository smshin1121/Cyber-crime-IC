개요Summary

On 2026-04-28 Swiss authorities, led by the Public Prosecutor's Office II of the Canton of Zurich (Staatsanwaltschaft II Zürich) together with the Zurich Cantonal Police (Kantonspolizei Zürich) and the Zurich City Police (Stadtpolizei Zürich), conducted a coordinated action day across the Canton of Zurich and five other Swiss cantons, supported by police forces from those other cantons. The operation produced 10 arrests, most of those arrested of Nigerian origin, and included the Black Axe "Regional Head" for the Southern European region. The investigation was conducted in collaboration with German law enforcement (Bayerisches Landeskriminalamt — Bavarian State Criminal Police Office). Europol supported the action through information analysis and a data sprint held in Madrid, and the @ON Network (Project ISF4@ON, EU-funded) led by the Italian Antimafia Investigation Directorate (DIA) provided additional analytical support.

배경Background

The cited Europol press release describes Black Axe as a Nigerian-origin transnational criminal organisation formally linked to the Neo-Black Movement of Africa, with approximately 60 zones in Nigeria and 35 abroad and roughly 30,000 registered members worldwide (per the Europol release). The group's predicate offence portfolio in this case is romance scams and other cyber-fraud offences causing millions of Swiss francs in damages, plus money laundering. Wider activity attributed in the release includes drug trafficking, human trafficking, kidnapping, armed robbery, and fraudulent spiritual practices, with annual proceeds estimated by Europol "in the billions of euros".

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The cited release describes the operation as conducted within the framework of cross-border cooperation between Swiss and German law enforcement, with Europol providing information analysis and a data sprint held in Madrid. The release places this in continuity with the prior 34-arrest Spain action against Black Axe as evidence of an ongoing Europol-supported European cross-border enforcement strategy against the network. The @ON Network (Project ISF4@ON), EU-funded and led by the Italian Antimafia Investigation Directorate (DIA), is named as an additional analytical-support mechanism — its first recorded appearance in the wiki corpus.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

South Korea is not named in the cited Europol release. Korea is recorded elsewhere in the wiki as having Black Axe / Nigerian-OCG cyber-fraud exposure structurally similar to the Swiss pattern (romance scams, BEC), but the cited 2026-04-28 action day involves only Switzerland and Germany among named participating countries.

모순점 및 미해결 문제Contradictions & Open Questions

  • The exact number of victims and the precise damages total in Swiss francs are not enumerated in the cited release.
  • The five "other Swiss cantons" beyond Zurich are not enumerated.
  • The relationship between this action day and the prior 34-arrest Spain action (referenced as "this recent operation in Spain") is described as continuity rather than a single operation — possibly a separate but parallel operational track.
