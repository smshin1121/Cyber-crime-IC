개요Summary

On 2026-02-19, the Korean National Police Agency published a tier-1 Korean-language press release reporting a sustained joint Korea–Cambodia bilateral enforcement campaign against Korean-targeted scam organisations operating from Cambodian compounds, conducted through the embedded Korea-dedicated Squad (코리아 전담반) — a joint operational unit of Korean police personnel and the Cambodian National Police (캄보디아 경찰청), opened in November 2025 inside Cambodia. The release announces:

  • 6 INTERPOL Red Notice fugitives arrested between 2025-12-22 and 2026-02-10 (manager-class scam-organisation cadre, including 2 organisation 총책 ("masterminds") and 1 head of Korean-victim money laundering at the Taizi 태자 단지 compound).
  • Three named February 2026 takedowns:
  • 2026-02-04 — scam-organisation manager arrested after a ~500-metre foot chase on the street.
  • 2026-02-06 — scam-organisation cadre tied to ~₩8.4 billion (~USD 6M) in victim losses arrested at a hotel hideout in joint Korea-dedicated Squad / Cambodian National Police operation.
  • 2026-02-10 — investment-fraud organisation principal tied to ~₩10.6 billion (~USD 7.6M) in victim losses arrested after multi-day CCTV tracking.
  • Cumulative since Korea-dedicated Squad opening (2025-11): 12 operations; 4 Korean nationals rescued (구출); 140 organised-crime suspects arrested.

The release also reports the average in-country concealment duration for the 6 Red Notice subjects as ~1 year 10 months — a primary-source datapoint on transnational fugitive concealment.

배경Background

Cambodia is one of the major host jurisdictions for scam compounds targeting Korean-language victims, alongside Myanmar, Laos, and the Philippines. Korean LE has historically struggled with the gap between Korean criminal jurisdiction (over Korean-national perpetrators and Korean-national victims) and Cambodian territorial sovereignty over the compound infrastructure. Korean cooperation with Cambodia has therefore evolved from request-based MLA toward embedded operational liaison.

The Korea-dedicated Squad (코리아 전담반) was opened in November 2025 inside Cambodia as a joint operational unit composed of Korean police personnel (per prior reporting, ~7 Korean officers) embedded with Cambodian National Police (per prior reporting, ~12 Cambodian officers). The Squad operates as the field arm of the broader Korean whole-of-government 「초국가범죄 특별대응 전담반(TF)」 (Transnational Crime Special Response Task Force, anchored at 대통령실 — the Presidential Office), bringing together the KNPA, NIS / 국정원, MOFA (specifically the Korean Embassy in Cambodia and its embedded police attaché 경찰주재관), and the Seoul Metropolitan Police INTERPOL team (서울청 인터폴팀).

The 2026-02-19 release sits within a sequence of related Korean cooperation events in 2025-11 to 2026-04 against Cambodia-hosted scam crime; see korea-cambodia-scam-repatriation (earlier 2025 cohort), knpa-breaking-chains-2nd-international-operation-meeting-2026 (2026-02-05 multilateral meeting and Phase-1 Cambodia 15-arrest tranche), and korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026 (2026-04-27 73-person mass extradition). The current page captures the discrete bilateral arrest sequence reported in the 2026-02-19 release.

법적 근거Legal Framework

The cooperation in this release is not treaty-based extradition or formal MLA. The legal foundations exposed are:

  • Operational bilateral cooperation between KNPA and Cambodian National Police, with no MLAT cited as the controlling instrument.
  • korea-cambodia-joint-task-force-mou-2025 — the wiki's referenced bilateral instrument framing the Korea-dedicated Squad's operating remit.
  • INTERPOL Red Notice channel — used to identify and prioritise the 6 fugitives, leveraged through the Seoul Metropolitan Police INTERPOL team and the broader INTERPOL secretariat.
  • Korean domestic criminal process for downstream prosecution; the 2026-02-19 release does not detail the post-arrest extradition pipeline (compare with korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026 for the April 2026 mass-extradition cohort).
📝 Translation note 코리아 전담반 ("Korea-dedicated Squad") is the literal translation of the operational unit name. The Korean term 전담반 (jeondam-ban) implies a single-purpose dedicated team. The unit is sometimes also rendered "Korea Task Force" in English-language Korean media, but the KNPA Korean source consistently uses 전담반.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The 2026-02-19 release does not name a specific MLAT instrument. Cooperation mix:

  • Embedded bilateral operational liaison — Korea-dedicated Squad as joint KNPA / Cambodian National Police unit operating inside Cambodia.
  • Real-time bilateral information sharing — quoted: "코리아 전담반을 포함한 양국 경찰의 실시간 정보 공유" — between Squad and broader Cambodian National Police on takedown days.
  • Embassy police attaché as intelligence node — 주캄보디아 한국대사관 경찰주재관 as the named intelligence source for the 2026-02-04 and 2026-02-10 takedowns.
  • INTERPOL Red Notice / Seoul Metropolitan Police INTERPOL team for fugitive identification and the 2026-02-06 hotel-hideout localisation.
  • Korean inter-ministry information integration — KNPA + NIS (국정원) + MOFA Embassy on the Korean side, with the 대통령실-anchored TF as the policy convergence point.
  • Field-level joint operations — verbatim "한·캄 경찰 간 합동작전" — building-perimeter blocking, foot-chase apprehension, CCTV-based multi-day tracking conducted jointly.

This is informal-cooperation-anchored (informal-cooperation) field cooperation; the I-24/7 secure-messaging layer and ASEAN Desk are the institutional substrates of the INTERPOL leg.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Balloon-effect (풍선효과) — KNPA International Police Cooperation Bureau Director-General Lee Jae-young (이재영) explicitly names this risk in the release: organisations may relocate their bases or change their operating model in response to enforcement pressure in one corridor. This aligns with the same framing in knpa-breaking-chains-2nd-international-operation-meeting-2026's agenda.
  • Long in-country concealment — average ~1y10mo concealment duration for the 6 Red Notice subjects indicates mature local embedding (housing, finance, identity infrastructure) inside Cambodia, beyond short-stay tourist patterns. This raises the resourcing bar for transnational fugitive recovery.
  • Compound-by-compound enforcement pattern — Taizi (태자) compound named as a specific operational target. The release explicitly describes a pattern of "지역별 거점 스캠 단지 단속" (region-specific compound enforcement) rather than a single-shot takedown of all Cambodia compounds. This is a sustained-campaign architecture rather than a single-operation architecture.
  • Asymmetric naming — the release names many Korean-side bodies (KNPA, NIS, Embassy attaché, Seoul Metropolitan Police INTERPOL team, Presidential Office TF) but only one Cambodian-side body (Cambodian National Police as a national-level entity). Specific Cambodian provincial police, prosecutorial, or judicial counterparts are not enumerated; this asymmetry is a primary-source feature, not a wiki gap.

교훈Lessons Learned

  1. Embedded liaison units compress cooperation latency. The Korea-dedicated Squad delivered 12 operations / 140 arrests / 4 rescues in ~3 months (2025-11 → 2026-02), with named takedowns occurring within hours/days of intelligence receipt — materially faster than typical treaty-based MLA pipelines for similar fact patterns.
  2. INTERPOL Red Notices remain operationally relevant for non-treaty-extradition jurisdictions. 6 Red Notice subjects arrested in a 7-week window inside Cambodia, leveraged via embedded Korean liaison rather than via MLA — the channel remains a working operational primitive for transnational fugitive identification even where treaty-based extradition pathways are weak.
  3. Whole-of-Korean-government bilateral architecture works in parallel with multilateral architecture. This page's bilateral pattern (Korea ↔ Cambodian National Police, deep-embedded) operates simultaneously with the multilateral pattern in knpa-breaking-chains-2nd-international-operation-meeting-2026 (Korea ↔ 22 countries + 5 IOs, broad-shallow). The same Presidential Office TF anchors both.
  4. Primary-source recognition of the balloon-effect — naming the displacement risk as a formal concern of the KNPA International Police Cooperation Bureau Director-General is a rare datapoint for the wiki's challenges layer.
  5. Average ~1y10mo in-country concealment is the first wiki-recorded quantitative datapoint on transnational fugitive concealment duration inside a scam-compound host jurisdiction — useful for resource-planning analysis on transnational fugitive recovery.

This operation is Korea-led and Korea-bilateral. The Korean side architecture is:

  • KNPA — lead agency. Quote: "캄보디아 내 조직이 거점을 이동하거나 운영 방식을 변화시키는 이른바 「풍선효과」 가능성까지 주시하며 국제공조를 강화하고 있다."

  • Seoul Metropolitan Police INTERPOL team (서울청 인터폴팀) — named as the body that identified the 2026-02-06 hotel hideout location.
  • MOFA — 주캄보디아 한국대사관 (Korean Embassy in Cambodia) and the embedded 경찰주재관 (police attaché) — named as the intelligence node for the 2026-02-04 location data and the 2026-02-10 investment-fraud-principal intelligence.
  • NIS / 국정원 — named as a participating intelligence body.
  • 대통령실 「초국가범죄 특별대응 전담반(TF)」 (Presidential Office Transnational Crime Special Response TF) — Korean policy structure under which the Squad operates, identical TF naming as in korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026 and knpa-breaking-chains-2nd-international-operation-meeting-2026.
