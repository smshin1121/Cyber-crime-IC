개요Summary

Operation Firewall was a US Secret Service-led international undercover investigation that disrupted Shadowcrew, CarderPlanet, and Darkprofits, online forums used to trade stolen payment-card data, identity documents, and fraud services. The operation began in July 2003 as an access-device fraud investigation and was announced publicly on 28 October 2004.

The operation qualifies as international cooperation because official Secret Service reporting identifies 28 arrests across eight U.S. states and six foreign countries and credits foreign law-enforcement partners in the United Kingdom, Canada, Bulgaria, Belarus, Poland, Sweden, the Netherlands, and Ukraine, as well as Europol.

Operational Scope

Shadowcrew was described by DOJ as a major online marketplace for identity theft and carding. DOJ's indictment announcement states that Shadowcrew had approximately 4,000 members and that the indictment charged administrators, moderators, vendors, and others involved in selling stolen credit-card numbers and identity documents.

Public reporting places the investigation among the earliest large-scale international cyber-financial crime operations. Secret Service reporting states that investigators collected approximately two terabytes of internal communications and transaction data during the operation.

Boundary Notes

This page is the canonical operation record for the 2003-2004 Operation Firewall investigation. It should remain separate from later carding and identity-theft operations such as Operation Trident Breach, Operation Phish Phry, and Infraud Organization Takedown, which involved different forums, periods, and prosecutorial records.
