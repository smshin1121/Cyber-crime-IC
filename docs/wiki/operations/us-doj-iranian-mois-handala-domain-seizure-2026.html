대상: Iranian Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS)-controlled cyber-enabled psychological operations infrastructure operating under the 'Handala Hack' faketivist persona, comprising 4 seized domains: Justicehomeland.org, Handala-Hack.to, Karmabelow80.org, and Handala-Redwanted.to. Operations include destructive cyber-attacks (e.g., March 2026 attack on US-based multinational medical technologies firm), hack-and-leak doxing of Israeli/US/Iranian-dissident targets (including ~190 IDF/Israeli government PII; 851 GB Sanzer Hasidic community data), and death-threat emails offering USD 250,000 bounties via the Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG) network in America and Canada. Earlier 2022 activity used Justicehomeland.org to claim MOIS thefts from Albanian government in retaliation for Albanian support of Mujahedeen e-Khalq (MEK) Iranian dissident group.

개요Summary

On 19 March 2026, the U.S. Department of Justice announced the court-authorized seizure of 4 domains controlled by Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS) and used to operate the Handala Hack "faketivist" persona for cyber-enabled psychological operations and transnational repression against U.S. persons, Israeli persons, and Iranian dissidents.

Seized domains: - Justicehomeland.org - Handala-Hack.to - Karmabelow80.org - Handala-Redwanted.to

Documented Handala Hack activity (per the cited DOJ-OPA Press Release 26-251 and underlying affidavit): - March 11 2026: Destructive malware attack against a US-based multinational medical technologies firm; claimed retaliation for "ongoing cyber assaults against the infrastructure of the Axis of Resistance." - March 9 2026: Posted PII of ~190 IDF and Israeli-government personnel. - March 6 2026: Claimed theft of 851 GB confidential data from Sanzer Hasidic Jewish community. - March 1 2026: Death-threat emails via Handala_Team@outlook.com offering USD 250,000 bounty for the beheading of two Iranian-dissident/journalist targets, invoking Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG) "partners" in America and Canada.

📝 Cyber-physical-violence convergence The MOIS-controlled Handala Hack persona paired hack-and-leak operations with CJNG cartel-as-proxy death threats and USD 250K bounties, demonstrating how state-sponsored cyber operations can be operationalised into kinetic threats via criminal organisations. This is the first wiki record of this cyber-criminal-cartel proxy attack model.

배경Background

Domain attribution evidence: - Shared leak sites linking Justicehomeland.org and Handala-Hack.to - Iranian IP ranges in operational infrastructure - Common operational "playbook": destructive cyber-attacks + "faketivist" psychological operations using data stolen via hacking

Earlier MOIS use of these domains (per court documents): - July 15, 2022 and September 9, 2022: Justicehomeland.org used to claim MOIS responsibility for stealing sensitive documents from Albanian government organizations, motivated by Albanian government support for Mujahedeen e-Khalq (MEK), an Iranian dissident group.

The escalation to 2026 destructive operations followed the start of the U.S.-Iran conflict on 28 February 2026.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • State-sponsored attribution and prosecution: MOIS personnel are extremely unlikely to be apprehended given Iran's lack of US extradition relationship. Domain seizure is the most effective disruption tool available.
  • Faketivist persona pattern: 'Handala Hack' provides plausible deniability between the MOIS and observable hack-and-leak activity. The court-authorized domain seizure depends on the affidavit's attribution proof (shared leak sites + Iranian IP ranges + common playbook).
  • Cyber-criminal-cartel proxy convergence: invoking CJNG for kinetic violence against doxing targets escalates the threat posture from cyber-only to cyber-physical, requiring inter-divisional FBI coordination (Cyber Division + Counterterrorism Division + criminal divisions).
  • No multi-country LE cooperation cited: this is a US-domestic disruption action with international target/victim profile but no enumerated foreign LE partner agency.

교훈Lessons Learned

  1. State-sponsored cyber-enabled transnational repression is a discrete IC-relevant pattern: Iranian MOIS + faketivist persona + hack-and-leak + cartel-proxy violence threats.

  3. Court-authorized domain seizure is the recurring DOJ disruption tool against state-sponsored cyber operations in absence of extradition opportunity.
  4. Faketivist persona pattern (Handala Hack) provides plausible deniability between adversary state and observable cyber activity; attribution depends on shared infrastructure analysis.
  5. Albania-2022 → Israel/US-2026 trajectory documented from MOIS faketivist domain reuse, showing the same domain infrastructure was repurposed across different geopolitical contexts.
  6. DOJ Rewards for Justice USD 10M outstanding signals active fugitive-attribution track beyond domain seizure; pairs with similar Rewards offers in other 2026 actions (USD 4M Daren Li, USD 10M Tai Chang from the Kok An action).
