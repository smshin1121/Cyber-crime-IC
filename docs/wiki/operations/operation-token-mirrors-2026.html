개요Summary

On 2026-03-30 the US Attorney's Office for the Northern District of California, the FBI, and IRS Criminal Investigation (IRS-CI) announced the simultaneous initial appearance in federal court at Oakland of three defendants — Gleb Gora (Vortex CEO), Manu Singh (Contrarian CEO), and Vasu Sharma (Contrarian BDA) — who had been arrested in Singapore on 2025-10-02 at the request of the United States and extradited to the US that day. The three appearances consolidated three separate federal grand-jury indictments, brought across 2025 against ten foreign-national executives and employees of four cryptocurrency "market maker" firms (Gotbit, Vortex, Antier, Contrarian), into a unified public-announcement event. The investigation is a joint FBI + IRS-CI undercover operation known publicly as Operation Token Mirrors, in which the FBI itself created several cryptocurrency tokens to bait wash-trading market manipulation by the suspect firms. More than USD 1 million in cryptocurrency has been seized to date.

배경Background

Wash trading occurs when a single trader, or a number of traders working in coordination, act as both the buyer and the seller in the same transaction or a series of transactions, creating the appearance that a cryptocurrency token has more active, organic trading than really exists, thereby inducing investors to purchase the token at artificially inflated prices. The four firms named in the indictments (Gotbit, Vortex, Antier, Contrarian) are cryptocurrency-industry "market makers" — service providers that ostensibly provide liquidity for token issuers. The indictments allege that these firms in fact orchestrated pump-and-dump schemes by simultaneously inflating volume and price and then liquidating their own holdings into the inflated market.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The operation deploys two distinct IC mechanism classes recorded for the first time in this corpus:

  1. Singapore-US extradition cooperation for cryptocurrency-fraud cases. The cited release attributes the arrests and extraditions of Gora, Singh, and Sharma to: (a) FBI Law Enforcement Attaché's Office in Singapore; (b) DOJ Office of International Affairs (OIA); (c) Singapore Police Force; (d) Singapore Attorney General's Chambers. This is the first wiki record of Singapore acting as an active extradition partner in a US-led cryptocurrency-fraud prosecution.

  2. FBI undercover-token creation methodology. As part of the undercover operation, the FBI itself created several cryptocurrency tokens which were then offered to the suspect firms; the firms were observed and recorded conducting wash trading on the FBI-created tokens. This is a discrete IC mechanism class beyond the traditional sting / wiretap / informant toolkit, anchored in cryptocurrency-specific infrastructure.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

South Korea is not named in the cited IRS-CI release among the participating jurisdictions or the corpus of suspect firms / defendants. The case is recorded in the wiki for the Singapore-US extradition cooperation pattern in cryptocurrency-fraud cases (potentially relevant to future Korean-exposed cases involving Korean-domiciled exchanges or victim cohorts) and the FBI undercover-token methodology.
