Operation Nova (Safe-Inet VPN Takedown)

operation 작전 최종 수정: 2026-04-08 출처: 3건
📝 This operation is documented from a Tier 3 (cybersecurity media) source. Additional verification from official sources (Tier 1-2) would strengthen data reliability.

Summary

Operation Nova was an international law enforcement action that took down Safe-Inet, a bulletproof VPN service that had operated for over a decade. The operation was led by German BKA and coordinated by Europol, with participation from the FBI and law enforcement agencies in the Netherlands, Switzerland, and France. The service had been used by ransomware operators, Magecart web skimmers, and other cybercriminals to conceal their identities and locations.

The takedown of Safe-Inet represented an infrastructure-focused approach to cybercrime disruption, targeting the enabling services rather than individual criminal groups.

Background

Safe-Inet was a VPN service that catered specifically to cybercriminals, providing anonymity and protection from law enforcement investigation. Unlike legitimate VPN services, Safe-Inet functioned as "bulletproof" infrastructure, deliberately ignoring abuse complaints and law enforcement requests. The service supported multiple layers of VPN connections and was marketed in underground forums as a tool for concealing criminal activity.
