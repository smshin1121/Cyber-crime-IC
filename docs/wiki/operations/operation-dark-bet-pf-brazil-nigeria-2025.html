개요Summary

On 2025-12-16 the Polícia Federal (Brazil) launched Operação Dark Bet to dismantle a Brazilian criminal organization that lured Brazilian nationals via false employment offers in two domestic online sports-betting (BET) platforms, trafficked them to Nigeria, and forced them under armed surveillance to commit cybercrimes (online scams) abroad. The investigation was triggered by the arrest of 109 persons in Nigeria — including 5 Brazilian nationals — on cybercrime charges. PF executed 11 search-and-seizure warrants across five Brazilian states (Ceará, Maranhão, Paraná, Santa Catarina, São Paulo), 4 temporary arrests, and Justiça Federal-ordered asset blocks/sequestros exceeding R$ 446 million. Two BET platforms were taken offline by judicial order.

배경Background

The PF release frames the case as a hybrid trafficking-in-persons + cybercrime investigation. Recruitment was conducted through social media and digital platforms with promises of high salaries and online-gaming-sector employment. Brazilian victims were then trafficked to Nigeria, where they faced jornadas exaustivas (exhausting work hours), retenção de documentos (document confiscation), restrição de liberdade (restriction of liberty), vigilância armada (armed surveillance), and imposição de dívidas (debt imposition). The compelled labour consisted of online scam operations — i.e., the trafficked workforce was made to perpetrate cybercrime against third-party victims.

The case operationalises in Brazil-Nigeria flows the same scam-compound model previously documented in Southeast Asia (Cambodia, Myanmar, Laos), with brazil as origin and nigeria as destination. The MJSP mirror release (2025-12-17) characterises the BET-sector trafficking front as "a concrete and extremely serious reality" (Marina Bernardes, MJSP coordinator for human trafficking enforcement).

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The PF release describes the international dimension in general terms without enumerating a specific channel:

  • The 109-arrest event in Nigeria is described as the trigger of the Brazil-side investigation, implying information transfer from Nigerian authorities (or a third party such as Interpol) to PF, but the modality is not enumerated.
  • PF closes the release by emphasising the importance of cooperação internacional (international cooperation), without naming a Nigerian counterpart agency, MLAT instrument, or Interpol mechanism.
  • This page therefore records only informal-cooperation as the documented mechanism class. Any upgrade to a specific Interpol or MLAT mechanism requires a confirmatory secondary source (EFCC side, Interpol side, or a Brazilian judicial order page that names the channel).

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Cybercrime / trafficking convergence: The 5 Brazilian nationals are simultaneously potential victims (coerced) and potential suspects (charged with cybercrime in Nigeria). The Brazilian judicial framing leans victim-side; the Nigerian arrest event leans suspect-side. Reconciliation across jurisdictions will require future case-page records.
  • Cooperation channel opacity: Without an explicit Interpol/MLAT/EFCC-liaison citation, the case sits in the informal-cooperation class — a recurring friction point in West-African scam-compound enforcement compared with the better-established Southeast-Asia ASEANAPOL/Interpol-ASEAN pipeline.
  • Asset recovery and victim protection: The R$ 446 M+ asset block is significant by Brazilian standards but the release does not specify whether recovered assets will fund repatriation/restitution for the 5 Brazilian victims or remain in Brazilian custody pending criminal forfeiture.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • This is the first Brazil-Nigeria cybercrime operation entered in this wiki. The case extends the wiki's scam-compound geography from Cambodia / Myanmar / Laos / Philippines / UAE into West Africa, with Brazil supplying the trafficked workforce.
  • Trigger-direction inversion: Whereas spf-fpb-operation-cryptoscam-2025 illustrates destination-jurisdiction-led discovery (a Singapore complaint led to a Brazil action), Dark Bet illustrates foreign-arrest-led origin discovery (a Nigeria arrest event led to a Brazil-domestic trafficking case). Both patterns belong in the wiki's IC-pattern catalogue.
  • Documentation gap on Nigerian side: The PF release names no Nigerian agency. Future enrichment should fetch any EFCC, Nigeria Police Force, or Interpol-NCB Abuja side-source that identifies the Nigerian counterpart and the channel used.

후속 조치Follow-Up Actions

  • Watch for: (a) Nigeria-side EFCC/NPF press release confirming the Brazil-cooperation track of the December 2024–2025 Lagos cybercrime crackdown subset; (b) Brazilian judicial petition or sentencing documents enumerating Penal Code articles and defendant names; (c) any Interpol / Interpol Red Notice references in subsequent PF phases; (d) repatriation reports for the 5 Brazilian nationals.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

South Korea is not named in the cited PF release. The case is recorded in the wiki for the Brazil-Nigeria scam-compound trafficking pattern. Comparative note: the operational model — recruitment via false online-gaming jobs, debt bondage abroad, forced cybercrime production — closely matches the Cambodia / Philippines compound pattern that has been a major focus of Korean National Police Agency (KNPA) cooperation activity in 2024-2026 (see e.g. operation-haechi-vi and the Korea-Cambodia / Korea-Philippines MOU corpus). Brazil-Nigeria adds a new geographic axis to the same modality, suggesting that Korean victim-side cases originating from Latin America-to-Africa flows could require novel cooperation pathways outside ASEAN-centred structures.

모순점 및 미해결 문제Contradictions & Open Questions

  • The PF release does not name a specific Nigerian counterpart agency. The natural assumption is EFCC (which led the December 2024 Lagos crackdown), but this requires confirmatory cross-jurisdictional sourcing before being asserted.
  • The release does not enumerate the cross-border cooperation channel (Interpol Red Notice / bilateral / EFCC liaison). The wiki therefore conservatively records only informal-cooperation.
  • The release does not specify the names of the two BET-sector platforms that were taken offline.
  • The release does not enumerate Brazilian Penal Code articles. Redução à condição análoga à de escravo in standard practice corresponds to Art. 149, but this is not asserted in the release.
  • The release does not name the four temporary-arrest defendants nor the targeted corporate entities.
  • The release describes the 5 Brazilian nationals among the 109 Nigeria arrests as both coerced labour and accused cybercrime perpetrators. The Brazilian framing emphasises victim-status; the Nigerian arrest event emphasises suspect-status. The reconciliation across jurisdictions is unresolved in this primary source.
  • Whether any of the R$ 446 M+ in blocked assets will fund repatriation/restitution for the 5 Brazilian victims is not addressed.
