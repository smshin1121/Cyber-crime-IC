개요Summary

Operation HAECHI-II, led by INTERPOL with participation from 20+ countries and Hong Kong/Macau, was a four-month operation targeting online fraud, illegal online gambling, and money laundering. The operation resulted in over 1,000 arrests and the interception of more than $27 million in illicit funds. Over 2,350 bank accounts linked to criminal proceeds were frozen.

Evidence and Attribution Notes

  • URL-TOPIC MISMATCH: The stored URL links The Hacker News's article on Operation HAECHI-III (November 2022) to the wiki page for Operation HAECHI-II (2021).
  • These are distinct INTERPOL operations and the source should be reassigned to a dedicated HAECHI-III page
  • Actual operation: Operation HAECHI-III ran from 28 June to 23 November 2022, resulting in the arrest of ~975 individuals and the closure of more than 1,600 cases, with ~US$130 million in virtual assets seized — distinct from HAECHI-II (2021, 1,003 arrests, $27M intercepted)
  • Crime types targeted: Voice phishing, romance scams, sextortion, investment fraud, and money laundering linked to illegal online gambling — with a notable case involving an India-based call center scam group impersonating Interpol and Europol officers to defraud Austrian victims
  • Coordination: INTERPOL's Financial Crime and Anti-Corruption Centre (IFCACC) supported 30 participating countries — a significant evolution from HAECHI-II's 20+ country scope
  • Technical innovation: Operation HAECHI-III marked the launch of INTERPOL's Anti-Money Laundering Rapid Response Protocol (ARRP), a new global stop-payment mechanism enabling countries to submit and handle requests to restrain criminal proceeds in near-real-time
  • The Indian call center case is particularly notable: criminals operated from New Delhi and Noida, calling Austrian victims with claims that 'identities were stolen and crime pertaining to narcotics drugs were committed in their names', forcing money transfers — an example of transnational impersonation fraud
  • BleepingComputer summarized HAECHI-II as a 1,003-arrest operation across 20 countries plus Hong Kong and Macao.
