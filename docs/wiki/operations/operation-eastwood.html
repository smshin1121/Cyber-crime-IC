Operation Eastwood

개요Summary

Operation Eastwood was a July 2025 international enforcement action against the pro-Russian NoName057(16) DDoS network. The operation targeted the group's botnet, attack infrastructure, and organizer/supporter layer used to run DDoS attacks against public institutions, transport, energy, banking, and other targets across Europe and allied states.

Eurojust described the action as a twelve-country operation with Eurojust and Europol support. The operational record also shows wider support and follow-on U.S. activity: DOJ later described the NoName action as coordinated with the FBI's Operation Red Circus and said law enforcement in 19 countries disrupted more than 100 servers in July 2025.

Cooperation Model

  • Judicial coordination: Eurojust coordinated findings, action-day planning, European Investigation Orders, Mutual Legal Assistance processes, and last-minute judicial requests.
  • Police coordination: Europol supported information exchange, operational coordination, analytical support, crypto tracing, forensic support, and an action-day command post.
  • National execution: Searches and evidence-gathering took place in Germany, Latvia, Spain, Italy, Czechia, Poland, and France; the wider coalition included authorities from Czechia, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Latvia, Lithuania, the Netherlands, Poland, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, and the United States.
  • Deterrence component: Authorities notified more than 1,100 supporters and 17 administrators or linked operators about the measures taken and potential criminal liability.
