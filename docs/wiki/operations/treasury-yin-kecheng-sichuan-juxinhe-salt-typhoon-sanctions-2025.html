Treasury OFAC Sanctions on Yin Kecheng and Sichuan Juxinhe Network Technology (Salt Typhoon) (January 2025)

개요Summary

On 17 January 2025, the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) designated Yin Kecheng (尹可承) — a Shanghai-based People's Republic of China cyber actor described by Treasury as affiliated with the PRC Ministry of State Security (MSS) and associated with the December 2024 compromise of Treasury's own Departmental Offices network — and Sichuan Juxinhe Network Technology Co., LTD. (四川聚信和网络科技有限公司) — a Sichuan-based cybersecurity company assessed to have direct involvement in the Salt Typhoon cyber group's exploitation of multiple major U.S. telecommunication and internet service provider companies (Treasury press release JY-2792; reliability: high; credibility: confirmed). Both designations were issued under Executive Order 13694, as further amended by the new Executive Order on Strengthening and Promoting Innovation in the Nation's Cybersecurity (signed January 2025).

📝 IC scope flag The Treasury release names no foreign partner authority as co-designating, co-coordinating, or otherwise participating. Unlike the 2024 Evil Corp tri-lateral (US-UK-AU) or the February 2025 ZServers (US-UK-AU) actions, this is a U.S.-unilateral OFAC action against PRC state-affiliated cyber actors. The companion State Department Rewards for Justice offer (up to USD 10 million) is the only additional U.S. instrument named. Five Eyes parallel attribution may exist but is not asserted on the record by the primary source per L19.

The operation is almost certainly the most consequential 2025 OFAC designation tied to PRC state-sponsored telecom-sector intrusions (high confidence, given Treasury's on-the-record attribution to Salt Typhoon and the unusual specificity of identifying both an MSS-affiliated individual and a contractor company in a single action). It sits squarely in the hacking-ic and cybercrime-infrastructure-ic cooperation categories: the underlying cyber activity targeted U.S. critical infrastructure carriers, and its IC implications run downstream through those carriers' lawful-intercept obligations under MLAT and 24/7 Network channels.

배경Background

Salt Typhoon is a Chinese state-sponsored advanced persistent threat group active since at least 2019 and tracked by U.S. intelligence and private-sector threat researchers as responsible for numerous compromises of U.S. companies in the communications sector. In late 2024, Salt Typhoon was publicly attributed to a campaign that compromised the network infrastructure of multiple major U.S. telecommunications and internet service providers — described by Treasury as "a dramatic escalation in the Chinese cyber operations against U.S. critical infrastructure targets."

The Treasury network compromise referenced in the press release (associated with Yin Kecheng) is a separate but contemporaneous incident: in late December 2024, Treasury publicly disclosed that a "China state-sponsored Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) actor" had gained access to certain Treasury Departmental Offices workstations through a third-party software-services provider compromise, with documents accessed but no continuing access asserted at disclosure. The 17 January 2025 OFAC action attributes the Treasury intrusion specifically to Yin Kecheng.

This designation is the fourth in a documented PRC-cyber sanctions sequence by Treasury between March 2024 and January 2025:

  1. 25 March 2024: Wuhan Xiaoruizhi Science and Technology Company, Ltd. and two employees designated as APT31 malicious cyber actors.
  2. 10 December 2024: Sichuan Silence Information Technology Company, Ltd. and one employee designated for "dangerous firewall compromises."
  3. 3 January 2025: Integrity Technology Group, Inc. designated for its role in Flax Typhoon malicious cyber activity (the same actor whose Raptor Train botnet was disrupted by FBI court-authorized takeover in September 2024).
  4. 17 January 2025 (this operation): Yin Kecheng (Shanghai, MSS-affiliated) and Sichuan Juxinhe Network Technology Co., LTD. (Sichuan) designated for the Treasury compromise and Salt Typhoon telecom infrastructure exploitation.

The four-action sequence is likely the clearest 2024–2025 illustration of OFAC's deliberate maintenance-of-pressure cadence against MSS-tied cyber-exploitation contractors (medium-high confidence).

법적 근거Legal Framework

  • Executive Order 13694 (Apr. 1, 2015) — original "Blocking the Property of Certain Persons Engaging in Significant Malicious Cyber-Enabled Activities" authority.
  • Executive Order on Strengthening and Promoting Innovation in the Nation's Cybersecurity (signed January 2025) — most-recent amendment to E.O. 13694, expressly invoked in this action as the operative amended authority.
  • U.S. domestic civil and criminal penalties for sanctions violations — civil penalties enforceable on a strict-liability basis per OFAC Economic Sanctions Enforcement Guidelines.
  • No treaty-based legal basis invoked (no Budapest Convention mechanism, no MLAT, no extradition treaty cited; PRC is not a Budapest Convention party).

결과 및 영향Results and Impact

  • 1 individual designated: Yin Kecheng — all property and interests in property within U.S. jurisdiction blocked; U.S. persons prohibited from dealings; 50%-or-more-owned subsidiaries automatically blocked.
  • 1 entity designated: Sichuan Juxinhe Network Technology Co., LTD. — same blocking effects.
  • No arrests, indictments, or seizures reported in this action (sanctions designations only; PRC does not extradite its own nationals, and no DOJ indictment was unsealed concurrently per this source).
  • Reputational and access effects: SDN listing has well-documented chilling effects on the designated entity's ability to access international banking, cloud-computing, hosting, software, and supply-chain services (high confidence based on broader OFAC enforcement literature).
  • Salt Typhoon attribution as on-the-record U.S. government determination: prior to this action, "Salt Typhoon" was a private-sector threat-research label. Treasury's release elevates it into the formal U.S.-government attribution record by tying a designated MSS-linked contractor (Sichuan Juxinhe) to the activity set.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

  • U.S. interagency: Treasury / OFAC + State Department Rewards for Justice referenced. No FBI, CISA, NSA, or DOJ co-attribution appears in this primary source (other agencies have published parallel advisories on Salt Typhoon, but this Treasury release does not credit them).
  • No formal IC mechanism invoked: no MLAT, no 24/7 Network, no Joint Investigation Team, no Budapest Convention pathway cited.
  • No bilateral or multilateral co-designation: contrast with the 2024 Evil Corp (US-UK-AU) and 2025 ZServers / Media Land (US-UK-AU) tri-lateral OFAC actions.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Host-state cooperation absent: PRC is not a Budapest Convention party, does not extradite its own nationals to the United States, and has historically rejected attribution of state-sponsored cyber activity. No expectation of host-state arrest or asset recovery.
  • Sanctions-evasion risk: as with prior PRC-cyber designations (e.g., Wuhan Xiaoruizhi, Sichuan Silence, Integrity Technology Group), Sichuan Juxinhe can in principle continue operations under PRC jurisdiction without U.S.-jurisdiction asset exposure; the designation primarily limits its ability to access Western financial, hosting, and supply-chain services.
  • CALEA-system compromise downstream risk: U.S. carrier lawful-intercept systems implicated in the Salt Typhoon campaign are the same systems used to fulfill foreign LE wiretap requests under MLAT and 24/7 Network channels. Treasury's release does not address this downstream IC implication directly; LE-track responses by FBI/CISA/NSA do.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • PRC-cyber sanctions sequencing: OFAC has adopted a deliberate 2024–2025 maintenance-of-pressure cadence against MSS-tied cyber-exploitation contractors, broadly paralleling its Russian-BPH cadence in the same period, but consistently U.S.-unilateral when the target is PRC.
  • Salt Typhoon's IC compound-risk profile: even where a sanctions action contains no LE cooperation component itself, a campaign that compromises U.S. carrier lawful-intercept infrastructure threatens the integrity of cross-border MLAT and 24/7-Network-mediated wiretap fulfillment that other countries rely on (medium-high confidence).
  • Five Eyes attribution asymmetry: the systematic absence of named Five Eyes co-designating partners on PRC-cyber actions (contrast with US-UK-AU on Russian-cybercrime targets) is likely a diplomatic-political pattern rather than an evidentiary one (medium confidence).

후속 조치Follow-Up Actions

  • The Treasury release frames the designation as part of an ongoing campaign rather than a terminal action; subsequent 2025 PRC-cyber designations would be the appropriate follow-on indicators.
  • Monitoring required: any subsequent U.S. or partner-jurisdiction action that explicitly invokes MLAT/Budapest pathways with respect to Salt Typhoon victim notification or cross-border carrier remediation would be a relevant IC-track follow-on signal.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

None named in the primary source. Korea is not identified as a Salt Typhoon victim jurisdiction or as a participating authority in this action. Korean carriers and lawful-intercept systems may face indirect downstream exposure to the same class of PRC-state-sponsored telecom-infrastructure intrusion tradecraft (low confidence; not asserted by this source).

모순점 및 미해결 문제Contradictions & Open Questions

  • Specific Salt Typhoon victim carriers: this Treasury release describes them only as "multiple major U.S. telecommunication and internet service provider companies." Major-outlet reporting has named Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile, and Lumen (among others), but per L19 the assertion is not retained in this operation page's frontmatter without a tier-1 primary source explicitly naming each carrier.
  • Yin Kecheng's specific role and rank within MSS: the release states only that he is "affiliated with" MSS and has been a cyber actor "for over a decade"; no rank, unit, or specific operational role is asserted.
  • Whether a parallel DOJ indictment exists or is forthcoming: the release contains no reference to a DOJ criminal action against Yin Kecheng (contrast with the Matveev tri-track in 2023, where indictments were unsealed concurrently with sanctions). The absence is itself notable but should not be over-interpreted.
  • Whether Five Eyes partners issued parallel advisories: not addressed by this source. Joint advisories from CISA / FBI / NSA / partner FVEY cyber agencies on Salt Typhoon have been publicly reported but are not credited in this Treasury release.
