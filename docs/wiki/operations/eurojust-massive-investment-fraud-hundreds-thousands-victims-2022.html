개요Summary

On 8-9 November 2022, Eurojust and Europol supported a coordinated 10-country action against a massive cryptocurrency-investment-fraud organised crime group that defrauded hundreds of thousands of victims worldwide at an estimated rate of EUR 50 million per quarter, dating back to at least 2016. The action originated from Spanish, German, and Finnish investigative requests and was operationalised through a Sweden-led Joint Investigation Team also involving Albania, Finland, Georgia, Germany, Latvia, Spain, and Ukraine (8 JIT countries). Action days produced 5 arrests (4 in Albania, 1 in Georgia), 15 call-centre searches (6 AL, 5 GE, 3 UA, 1 MK), 27 other location searches, 5 vehicle searches, ~50 suspect/witness hearings, and seizure of 500+ electronic devices, EUR 340,000+ in cash, multiple bank accounts and cryptocurrency wallets, plus properties, ID documents, bank cards, and hundreds of other documents.

배경Background

The OCG operated dozens of call centres in several countries and hundreds of online platforms. Suspects presented themselves as brokers offering large investment returns from small initial investments, deceiving victims through professionally set-up call centres and online social engineering. Victims invested via OCG-controlled web platforms and lost large sums. The cryptocurrency-investment-fraud methodology is structurally identical to that recorded for the 2025 €100M crypto-investment-fraud takedown (Spain-Lithuania JIT) and €600M crypto money-laundering takedown (France-Belgium JIT extended).

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The cited release describes a comprehensive multi-mechanism cooperation architecture:

  1. Sweden-led 8-country Joint Investigation Team (JIT) — Sweden, Albania, Finland, Georgia, Germany, Latvia, Spain, Ukraine. Foundational instrument.
  2. Eurojust 13 coordination meetings + Eurojust coordination centre for the action days.
  3. Europol 7 operational meetings + Europol analytical support throughout.
  4. International Arrest Warrants (presumably INTERPOL Red Notices for non-EU JIT/action partners).
  5. European Investigation Orders (EIOs) — judicial-cooperation instrument for EU JIT countries.
  6. Letters of Request to third countries — for non-EU JIT/action partners.
  7. JIT-state officials deployed to action-state territories — ~33 Spanish officials in Albania; ~60 German officials in Georgia; etc. A discrete IC mechanism class for cross-territorial JIT participation.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

South Korea is not named in the cited Eurojust release among the 10 participating jurisdictions or the underlying victim cohort. The case is recorded in the wiki for the 8-country EU JIT-anchored cryptocurrency-investment-fraud takedown architecture and the JIT-state-officials-deployed-to-action-state-territory mechanism class. Korean exposure to similar cryptocurrency-investment-fraud schemes is documented elsewhere in the wiki under broader voice-phishing / online-fraud corpus.
