Versus Project 다크웹 마켓플레이스 — Schmitz 콜롬비아 신병인도 (미국, 2026) Versus Project Darknet Marketplace — Schmitz Extradition from Colombia (US, 2026)

: 2026-05-16 : 1

개요

2026년 4월 30일 미국 법무부(미국 뉴저지 연방지방검찰청 경유, IRS-CI 자체 도메인 뉴스룸에 미러링)는 콜롬비아 Taganga 거주 37세 독일 국적자 Patrick Schmitz에 대한 8개 항목 연방 기소장의 봉인 해제를 발표하였다. Schmitz는 약 2019년 11월부터 2022년 5월까지 운영된 Tor 은닉 다크넷 마켓플레이스 "The Versus Project"를 개설·운영한 혐의로 기소되었다. Schmitz는 그 전날(2026년 4월 29일) 콜롬비아에서 미국으로 신병이 인도되었으며, 이는 2024년 6월 콜롬비아 당국의 임시 체포 이후 약 22개월에 걸친 범죄인 인도 절차를 거친 결과이다. 본 사건은 미국–콜롬비아 양자 범죄인 인도 채널이 다크넷 마켓플레이스 운영자에게 적용된 사례이며, almost certainly(신뢰도: 높음) 2026년 다크넷·사이버 기반 범죄에 대한 콜롬비아 → 미국 최초의 범죄인 인도 사례이다.

배경

Versus Project(Versus)는 2019–2022년에 영어권 Tor 은닉 다크넷 마켓 중 선도적 플랫폼 중 하나로 운영되었다. Versus는 판매자와 구매자가 헤로인 및 기타 불법 약물, 도난·위조 신분증 문서, 접근 장치(예: 침해된 결제 카드), 위조 화폐, 악성코드 및 해킹 도구를 거래할 수 있도록 하였다. 운영 정점 시 Versus는 380,000명 이상의 등록 사용자, 32,000개 이상의 상품 목록, 그리고 수백만 달러의 거래 가치를 갖는 300,000건이 넘는 완료 주문을 처리한 후 2022년 5월에 사라졌다. Schmitz는 Versus를 공동 설립하고 일상 운영 관리 — 판매자 신청 검토, 사용자 지원 티켓팅, 판매자–구매자 분쟁 해결 — 를 담당하였다는 혐의를 받는다.

개요Summary

On 30 April 2026 the U.S. Department of Justice (via the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of New Jersey, mirrored on IRS-CI's own-domain newsroom) announced the unsealing of an eight-count federal indictment against Patrick Schmitz, a 37-year-old German national resident in Taganga, Colombia, charging him with creating and operating "The Versus Project" — a Tor-hidden darknet marketplace that ran from approximately November 2019 to May 2022. Schmitz was extradited from Colombia to the United States the day before (29 April 2026), following a provisional arrest by Colombian authorities in June 2024 and a roughly 22-month extradition process. The case is an instance of the U.S.–Colombia bilateral extradition channel being used for a darknet-marketplace operator and is highly likely (confidence: high) the first such Colombia-to-US extradition for a darknet-cyber-enabled crime in 2026.

배경Background

The Versus Project (Versus) operated as one of the leading English-language Tor-hidden darknet markets in 2019–2022. Versus enabled vendors and buyers to transact in heroin and other illicit drugs, stolen and fraudulent identification documents, access devices (e.g., compromised payment cards), counterfeit currency, malware, and hacking tools. At its operational peak Versus had over 380,000 registered users, more than 32,000 product listings, and processed in excess of 300,000 completed orders worth millions of dollars in transactions before going dark in May 2022. Schmitz is alleged to have co-founded Versus and to have handled day-to-day operational management — vendor application review, user support ticketing, and resolving vendor–buyer disputes.

Legal framework

  • Charges filed under U.S. federal law (Title 21 controlled substances, Title 18 access device fraud, money laundering, identification documents, conspiracy).
  • Surrender governed by the bilateral United States–Colombia Extradition Treaty (1979) as supplemented by the Colombian Constitutional Court framework.
  • The DOJ Office of International Affairs (OIA) coordinated through the Judicial Attache in Bogotá, the standard channel for US extradition requests to Colombia.

Operational timeline

Results and impact

  • 1 arrest / extradition of a senior darknet-marketplace operator
  • 1 indictment unsealed (8 counts)
  • Potential exposure: mandatory minimum 20 years up to life imprisonment on the continuing criminal enterprise count alone
  • Approximately 22 months elapsed between Colombian provisional arrest and surrender — illustrative of US–Colombia extradition timelines for non-violent / cyber-enabled cases

Cooperation mechanisms used

  • Extradition under the US–Colombia bilateral treaty.
  • Office of International Affairs (DOJ Criminal Division) as the central authority transmitting and receiving the extradition request through the Bogotá Judicial Attache.
  • Provisional arrest as the pre-extradition holding mechanism, used in June 2024 to secure custody during the formal extradition process.

Challenges and friction points

  • Length of the extradition process (≈22 months from provisional arrest to surrender) reflects the standard Colombian judicial review for extradition (Sala Penal de la Corte Suprema de Justicia opinion + Ministry of Justice + presidential decree).
  • Defendant's third-country (German) nationality complicates potential parallel proceedings but did not delay the bilateral US–Colombia process per the primary source.

Lessons learned

  • Likely (confidence: medium): US–Colombia extradition for darknet-marketplace operators is operationally viable on a multi-year timeline. The Versus case follows the pattern of the AlphaBay, Bohemia/Cannabia, and Archetyp takedowns in which marketplace operators are physically apprehended in a third country years after the platform's shutdown.
  • Highly likely (confidence: high): The DOJ's Judicial Attache in Bogotá is the primary US channel for cybercrime extraditions from Colombia, with the Policía Nacional de Colombia executing the arrest and surrender.

Follow-up actions

  • Trial proceedings in the District of New Jersey are pending (as of 30 April 2026 the defendant was detained pending trial).
  • Potential downstream indictments of remaining Versus operators / vendors are not announced in the primary source.
