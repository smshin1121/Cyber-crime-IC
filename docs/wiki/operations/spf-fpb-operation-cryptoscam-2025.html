개요Summary

On 2025-06-17 the Singapore Police Force (SPF) announced its pivotal role in Operation Cryptoscam — one of two simultaneous operations launched by the Federal Police of Brazil (FPB) in May 2025 against criminal organisations involved in financial crimes, cyber fraud, drug trafficking, and money laundering. Across the two simultaneous operations the FPB issued 26 search-and-seizure warrants and 11 arrest warrants, and seized assets and valuables linked to the criminal groups.

The Singapore-side track of the operation began with a January 2024 police report to SPF stating that a victim had suffered losses of approximately USD 1.4 million due to his cryptocurrency wallet being compromised. The SPF Cybercrime Command (CCC) under the Criminal Investigation Department (CID) conducted extensive follow-up investigations, established that the stolen funds had been traced to Brazil, and alerted the FPB through INTERPOL international cooperation channels.

배경Background

The cited SPF release describes the two simultaneous May 2025 FPB operations as targeting criminal organisations involved in financial crimes, cyber fraud, drug trafficking, and money laundering. Operation Cryptoscam was the SPF-originated track; the second simultaneous operation is referenced but not described.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The cited release describes a single foundational IC mechanism class for this operation: INTERPOL international cooperation channels as the cross-jurisdictional alerting mechanism between SPF and FPB. The Singapore-side investigation was originated and conducted under the SPF Cybercrime Command (CCC) within the Criminal Investigation Department (CID), with the cross-border step occurring at the Singapore-to-Brazil-via-INTERPOL alerting handoff. Brazil-side action was executed under FPB authority, jointly handled by the Repression Electronic Bank Fraud Coordination unit and the FPB Regional Office in Itajaí, Santa Catarina.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

South Korea is not named in the cited SPF release among the participating jurisdictions or the underlying victim cohort. The case is recorded in the wiki for the Singapore-Brazil-INTERPOL cooperation pattern — a discrete mechanism class for ASEAN-LATAM cybercrime cooperation that the Korean voice-phishing / online-fraud corpus may eventually map to as Korean-originated investigations expand into Latin American destinations.
