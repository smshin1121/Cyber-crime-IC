개요Summary

Operation Casus is the Austrian Federal Criminal Police (Bundeskriminalamt) / Landeskriminalamt Niederösterreich (LKA NÖ) federally-responsible policing unit established on 2024-12-21 to combat Schockanruf-Betrug (shock-call fraud, "bail-trick" voice phishing). On 2026-02-16, Interior Minister Gerhard Karner and Bundeskriminalamt Director Andreas Holzer presented investigation results at a press conference: 23 suspects from Poland, Czech Republic, Germany, Slovakia, Serbia and Spain identified since the beginning of 2025, 17 of whom were arrested. Total attributed fraud damage to (predominantly elderly) Austrian victims: EUR 4.8 million.

Two cross-border caller cells ("Keiler-Zellen") were targeted: one in Vienna (dismantled) and one in Brno (Brünn, Czech Republic) led by a 27-year-old Czech citizen who was responsible for telephone fraud against victims in Austria, Poland and Slovakia. The Brno cell leader was arrested in Slovakia via a European Arrest Warrant and was later sentenced to 6 years in Poland. A 20-year-old Czech woman was arrested in her home country via a WKStA-issued EAW and extradited to Austria. A 25-year-old Slovak citizen was apprehended in Vienna-Donaustadt on 2025-10-07 with information from Slovak investigative authorities.

Across the broader 2022–2026 Austrian counter-Schockanruf effort, Bundeskriminalamt Director Holzer reported 189 cumulative arrests, EUR 53 million cumulative damage, and a ~50% recent reduction in reported cases and damages — indicating that the dedicated LKA NÖ unit model is producing measurable suppression of the Schockanruf phenomenon in Austria.

배경Background

Schockanrufe (shock calls), also termed Enkeltrick or Polizistentrick in German-speaking literature, are a voice-phishing typology in which callers impersonate police officers, court officials, judges, doctors or bank employees and induce (predominantly elderly) victims to surrender cash, valuables, or banking access under a fabricated emergency pretext — typically the supposed arrest of a relative requiring immediate "bail" payment. The callers operate from VoIP-routed caller cells (Keiler-Zellen) in foreign jurisdictions, with money-mule "pickup" logistics coordinated separately.

Austrian Bundeskriminalamt reporting cited in the 2026-02-16 release shows: total damages from Schockanrufe and Enkeltrick in Austria declined from approximately EUR 20 million in 2023 to approximately EUR 10 million in 2024, with continued ~50% reductions reported into 2025-2026 — attributed by Bundeskriminalamt Director Holzer to the consolidated LKA NÖ unit model and sustained international cooperation.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The BMI Austria release names three explicit cooperation arcs:

  1. Austria ↔ Slovakia — bilateral information exchange between LKA NÖ / Bundeskriminalamt and Slovak investigative authorities leading to the 2025-10-07 Vienna-Donaustadt arrest of a 25-year-old Slovak citizen; subsequent execution of Austrian-issued European Arrest Warrant in Slovakia against the Brno cell leader.
  2. Austria ↔ Czech Republic — operational raid on the Brno (Brünn) "Keiler-Zelle"; execution of WKStA-issued European Arrest Warrant in Czech Republic against a 20-year-old Czech-citizen suspect, with subsequent extradition to Austria.
  3. Austria ↔ Poland (indirect) — Brno cell leader, after EAW arrest in Slovakia, ultimately sentenced in Poland — indicating Polish prosecutorial uptake of cross-border Schockanruf-Betrug charging concurrent with Austrian investigation.

Extradition (specifically via European Arrest Warrant) is the dominant cooperation mechanism recorded.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Multi-jurisdictional caller-cell logistics: caller cells in Vienna and Brno, with operators recruited from across at least six countries (Poland, Czech Republic, Germany, Slovakia, Serbia, Spain). Investigation required sustained cross-border information-sharing rather than a single coordinated action day.
  • Choice of convicting forum: the Brno cell leader was sentenced in Poland rather than in Austria (where victim losses were located) or Czech Republic (where the cell was physically based). The BMI release does not explain the basis for Polish prosecutorial uptake.
  • Absence of named Polish LE cooperation in primary release: although 6 of 23 identified suspects originate from Poland and the Brno cell leader was ultimately Polish-sentenced, the BMI 2026-02-16 release does not explicitly name a Polish counterpart law-enforcement or prosecutorial agency as cooperating in Operation Casus. This may reflect parallel investigations rather than formal joint investigation.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • Consolidated single-LE-unit model: a federally-responsible policing unit (LKA NÖ for Operation Casus) consolidating Schockanruf-Betrug investigation responsibility produced measurable suppression of the phenomenon within 14 months of establishment.
  • European Arrest Warrant chaining: a single Austrian-led investigation can chain multiple EAWs across Slovakia and Czech Republic, with convicting forums in third states (Poland) — demonstrating EAW's role as a primary IC instrument for cross-border Schockanruf cases.
  • Convict-in-third-state pattern: this is the second 2025-2026 wiki-recorded case (cf. latvia-phone-fraud-money-mule-network-takedown-2026) where the convicting forum is neither the country of victim losses nor the country of criminal cell physical location. The pattern reflects EU-internal flexibility in choosing convicting jurisdiction based on evidence center of gravity.

후속 조치Follow-Up Actions

  • The criminal investigation is ongoing as of 2026-02-16.
  • Six of the 23 identified suspects (origins: Poland, Czech Republic, Germany, Slovakia, Serbia, Spain) remain at the suspect stage without arrest.
  • Wider counter-Schockanruf effort continues at Bundeskriminalamt under the Operation Casus framework.

Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

South Korea is not named in the BMI Austria 2026-02-16 release as a victim, suspect-origin, or cooperation jurisdiction for Operation Casus. The Schockanruf modus operandi (VoIP-based caller cells, impersonation of police/judiciary/bank, "bail trick" pretext, primarily elderly-victim targeting) parallels Korean voice-phishing typologies recorded under knpa-cyber-bureau and seoul-metropolitan-police cases, but Operation Casus has no direct Korean nexus.
