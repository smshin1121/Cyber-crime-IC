개요Summary

This page represents the January 2024 DOJ xDedic prosecution roundup. It is kept as a follow-on operation because older generated links point to this title, but the substantive umbrella record is xdedic-marketplace-takedown. The roundup should be read as a prosecution status report for the 2019 xDedic infrastructure seizure, not as a separate marketplace takedown.

DOJ reported that xDedic offered more than 700,000 compromised servers for sale, including at least 150,000 in the United States and 8,000 in Florida. Buyers used the marketplace to obtain server credentials and personally identifiable information, which then supported tax fraud, identity-theft fraud, ransomware-enabling access, and other server-abuse schemes. The same source connects the 2019 seizure to a later prosecution pipeline covering administrators, sellers, buyers, and downstream fraud actors.

Cooperation and Legal Context

The roundup identifies the 2019 infrastructure action as an international operation involving U.S. authorities, Belgium and Ukraine, Europol, the Dutch National High Tech Crime Unit, and the German Bundeskriminalamt. It also states that later prosecutions benefited from assistance by law enforcement in Belgium, Georgia, Germany, Poland, Spain, the United Kingdom, Romania, Switzerland, Estonia, Latvia, Bulgaria, Ukraine, Lithuania, and Moldova. DOJ Office of International Affairs support is significant because the record describes extraditions from third countries and pending U.K. extradition matters.
