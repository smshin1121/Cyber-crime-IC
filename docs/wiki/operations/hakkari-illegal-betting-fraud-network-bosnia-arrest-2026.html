개요Summary

On 31 March 2026 the Republic of Türkiye's Ministry of Interior, via the official state news agency Anadolu Ajansı, announced the conclusion of a four-month investigation centred in Hakkari province against a transnational organised criminal group conducting illegal sports betting and qualified fraud (nitelikli dolandırıcılık) through information-technology systems. The sweep produced 52 detentions in Türkiye across 18 provinces and 1 Red-Notice apprehension in Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina — the latter described as the technical-infrastructure architect of the network's back-office. The Ministry estimates approximately 20 billion Turkish lira in cumulative money traffic associated with the network and describes this as the highest transaction volume identified to date in any Turkish cyber-crime / fraud investigation.

The international structure of the organisation was publicly disclosed by the Ministry as extending to server and management layers located in Bosnia and Herzegovina, making BiH not only the apprehension venue but also the hosting jurisdiction of the criminal infrastructure.

배경Background

The investigation was driven from a provincial axis rather than from a centralised national-level prosecutorial structure. The lead authorities were:

  • Yüksekova Cumhuriyet Başsavcılığı (Yüksekova Chief Public Prosecutor's Office, in Hakkari province) — coordinating prosecutor.
  • Hakkari Emniyet Müdürlüğü (Hakkari Provincial Police Directorate) — lead investigative police unit, including its Cyber Crimes Combat Branch.

Approximately four months of physical and technical surveillance preceded the action day. Cyber-patrol and technical-analysis work identified a central back-office that managed high-volume financial flows over illegal betting platforms, used a 24/7 panel system, and converted revenues obtained through third-party accounts into crypto-assets — a layering pattern that the Ministry's statement attributes specifically to this organisation.

The 20 billion TL figure refers to money traffic / illegal financial ecosystem layering, not to net victim loss. The Ministry's statement does not enumerate distinct victims or aggregate victim losses.

참여 기관Participating Parties

Türkiye

  • T.C. İçişleri Bakanlığı (Ministry of Interior of the Republic of Türkiye) — issuing authority of the public statement.
  • Hakkari Emniyet Müdürlüğü — provincial police directorate; lead investigative unit.
  • Yüksekova Cumhuriyet Başsavcılığı (Hakkari) — coordinating prosecutor; investigation continues under this office.
  • Interpol Daire Başkanlığı (Interpol Department, Turkish General Directorate of Security) — channelled the Red Notice that produced the Sarajevo apprehension.

Bosnia and Herzegovina

  • The Ministry's statement does not name the BiH counterpart agency that executed the Sarajevo arrest. Federalna policija (FUP), MUP Republika Srpska, SIPA, and Sarajevo cantonal police are all institutionally plausible executing authorities, but the AA wire credits the apprehension only to the Red Notice and to a "simultaneous operation."

International coordinating body

  • interpol — Red Notice issued through the Turkish national bureau (Interpol Daire Başkanlığı). Interpol channels the notice to all member states, including the BiH National Central Bureau, but the statement does not specify which BiH NCB action triggered the arrest.

법적 근거Legal Framework

The press release does not enumerate statutes by article, but the substantive offences identified in the Ministry's statement and the structural elements of the case map to:

  • Türkiye Ceza Kanunu (TCK) Article 158 — qualified fraud (nitelikli dolandırıcılık), including the subsection covering fraud committed via information-technology systems.
  • TCK Article 220 — establishing or being a member of an organisation for the purpose of committing crime (örgüt kurma / örgüte üye olma).
  • TCK Article 282 — laundering of assets derived from crime (suçtan kaynaklanan malvarlığı değerlerini aklama).
  • Law No. 7258 on the Regulation of Wagers and Games of Chance in Football and Other Sports Competitions — Article 5 illegal-betting offences.
  • Interpol Red Notice framework — issued through the Turkish Interpol Daire Başkanlığı and acted upon in Bosnia and Herzegovina.

The mutual-legal-assistance treaty channel underlying the BiH arrest is not specified in the public statement. Türkiye and Bosnia and Herzegovina are both parties to the Council of Europe extradition framework and to the Budapest Convention on Cybercrime, but neither instrument is invoked by name in the AA wire.

작전 타임라인Operational Timeline

  • December 2025 (approx., inferred from "approximately four months of physical and technical surveillance" reported on 2026-03-31) — Investigation begins under the Hakkari Emniyet Müdürlüğü's Cyber Crimes Combat Branch, coordinated with the Yüksekova Cumhuriyet Başsavcılığı.
  • 2026-03-31 — Action day: simultaneous raids across 18 Turkish provinces; 52 suspects detained domestically; in a synchronised foreign operation, 1 Interpol Red-Notice fugitive — described as the technical-infrastructure architect of the network — is apprehended in Sarajevo, Bosnia and Herzegovina.
  • 2026-03-31, 20:45 (Türkiye time) — Anadolu Ajansı publishes the Ministry of Interior's statement on the action, citing the NSosyal-account release.
  • 2026-05 (open period) — Subsequent reporting (Hakkari local press, 6–7 May 2026) reports that the Sarajevo-arrested suspect (identified by initials only, "M.O.B.") was extradited to Türkiye, interrogated by the Hakkari Cyber Crimes unit, and remanded in pre-trial detention by court order. The AA primary source itself does not contain this development; it is corroborating but downstream.

결과 및 영향Results and Impact

  • 53 total persons in custody as a direct result of the action day: 52 in Türkiye + 1 in Bosnia and Herzegovina.
  • 18 provinces of Türkiye involved in the simultaneous sweep.
  • ~20 billion Turkish lira in cumulative money traffic associated with the network's illegal-betting and qualified-fraud activity — recorded by the Ministry as the highest transaction volume ever identified in a Turkish cyber-crime / fraud investigation.
  • Server and management infrastructure of the organisation publicly attributed to Bosnia and Herzegovina — establishing BiH as both the apprehension venue and a hosting jurisdiction of the criminal infrastructure.
  • Large quantity of high-capacity digital materials and devices seized; forensic-IT examination is continuing under the Yüksekova Cumhuriyet Başsavcılığı.
  • Defendant-level indictment counts, victim counts, and victim-loss totals are not in the source.

활용된 공조 메커니즘Cooperation Mechanisms Used

  • Interpol Red Notice — issued through the Türkiye Interpol Daire Başkanlığı and acted upon in Sarajevo. The Red Notice is the explicitly named cooperation instrument in the Ministry's statement.
  • Synchronised action-day model — domestic 18-province raids and the foreign apprehension in Sarajevo described as occurring "simultaneously" (eş zamanlı), consistent with the action-day pattern used in EU-coordinated operations but executed here through Interpol channels rather than through Eurojust / Europol.
  • Cross-border evidence implications — because the network's servers and management layers are in BiH, the Turkish forensic-IT examination of seized devices in Türkiye will likely require complementary preservation / production from BiH; no such request is named in the Ministry's statement.

과제 및 마찰 요인Challenges and Friction Points

  • Counterpart-agency opacity on the BiH side. The press release does not name the BiH executing authority for the Sarajevo arrest. This is consistent with Turkish practice of crediting Interpol channels rather than foreign agencies in public statements, but it limits the evidentiary value of the wire for mapping BiH cyber-cooperation actor networks.
  • MLAT-instrument opacity. No specific extradition or mutual-assistance treaty is invoked by name in the public statement; the cooperation is presented as Red-Notice-driven.
  • Server-jurisdiction friction. Public attribution of the criminal infrastructure to BiH-hosted servers creates an evidence-preservation pressure point that the wire does not resolve.
  • Single-source dependency. As of 2026-05-09 the operation page rests on one tier-1 primary source (the AA wire reproducing the Ministry of Interior statement). Defendant-level court documents and any BiH-side primary source are not yet in the wiki.

교훈Lessons Learned

  • A provincial Turkish prosecutor's office can anchor an investigation that reaches a third-country apprehension without escalation to a national-level prosecutorial structure — the international layer was carried by Interpol channels, not by a centralised judicial authority.
  • An Interpol Red Notice can be the operative cooperation instrument even between two states (Türkiye, BiH) that share other treaty channels — an important data point on Red-Notice substitution behaviour.
  • A single press release from a sending-state ministry can publicly attribute a criminal organisation's server / management infrastructure to a specific foreign jurisdiction — operationally useful as a corroborating reference for cross-border data-preservation conversations, even when no formal MLA channel is named.
  • The case provides a concrete benchmark — ~20bn TL — for the scale of layering and crypto-conversion typologies in Turkish online-fraud / illegal-betting investigations.
