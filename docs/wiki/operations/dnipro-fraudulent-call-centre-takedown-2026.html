Dnipro Fraudulent Call Centre Takedown (2026)

개요Summary

Latvian, Lithuanian, and Ukrainian authorities, coordinated by Eurojust, dismantled a fraudulent call centre operating across three offices in Dnipro. The group used a fake cryptocurrency investment scheme, remote-access software, and bank and cryptocurrency transfer channels to take money from victims.

This operation qualifies for the public IC corpus because the action was a source-backed three-country JIT matter, coordinated through Eurojust, and the enforcement result is distinct from the later 2025 December Ukraine fraudulent call-centres action.

결과 및 영향Results and Impact

  • 11 members of the criminal group were arrested on 2026-02-17.
  • Authorities searched 32 locations in Ukraine.
  • Ten suspects were ordered into pre-trial detention and one was placed under house arrest.
  • Authorities seized electronic equipment, documents, computers, SIM cards, EUR 400,000 in cash, two cryptocurrency wallets, and eight luxury cars.
  • Initial investigations identified at least nine victims in Latvia and Lithuania with losses over EUR 160,000, while the call centre was set up to defraud citizens across Europe.

Fraud Pattern

The call centre promoted a fake cryptocurrency investment scheme promising large profits. Victims were also asked to pay for legal support to recover supposed lost assets. Operators used remote-access software to gain access to victims' funds and transfer money into bank accounts and cryptocurrency wallets controlled by the group.
