Operation SOGA X

개요Summary

Operation SOGA X was an INTERPOL-led global enforcement action targeting illegal online football gambling and related organized-crime activity during the UEFA 2024 European Football Championship. It ran from June to July 2024 across 28 countries and territories.

The operation is included in this corpus because the official release identifies illegal online gambling websites, cross-border servers, e-wallets, fake and mule accounts, VPN use, seized devices, cyber scams, and financial-flow tracing as part of a coordinated international enforcement model.

Cooperation Model

  • INTERPOL coordination: INTERPOL led the operation, facilitated information sharing among participating countries, and provided case mentoring and training.
  • Specialist support: The Asia-Pacific Expert Group on Organized Crime, the INTERPOL Match Fixing Task Force, and UEFA supported the operation.
  • Digital evidence: INTERPOL's Operational Support Team assisted Philippine authorities with extraction and analysis of evidence from seized devices, financial-flow tracing, and identification of victims and suspects in other jurisdictions.
  • Financial-crime focus: The operation targeted illegal betting proceeds, money-laundering syndicates, e-wallet flows, bank-account networks, and fake or mule betting accounts.
  • Platform disruption: Authorities shut down tens of thousands of illegal websites linked to betting activity.

Participating Countries and Jurisdictions

INTERPOL named 28 participating countries and territories: Australia, Belgium, Brunei, Czech Republic, Greece, Guinea Bissau, Hong Kong (China), Hungary, India, Italy, Lebanon, Macao (China), Maldives, Malta, Namibia, Philippines, Portugal, Republic of Korea, San Marino, Singapore, South Africa, Spain, Sri Lanka, Switzerland, Thailand, Trinidad and Tobago, Türkiye, and Vietnam.

Boundary and Non-Duplication Notes

Operation SOGA X is distinct from the HAECHI series. HAECHI V and HAECHI VI include illegal online gambling as one category within broader cyber-enabled financial-crime sweeps, while SOGA X was a dedicated illegal football-gambling operation tied to a major sports event.

The page should not be used as a general sports-corruption or human-trafficking operation record outside the source-backed online gambling, scam-centre, digital-evidence, money-laundering, and cross-border enforcement components.
