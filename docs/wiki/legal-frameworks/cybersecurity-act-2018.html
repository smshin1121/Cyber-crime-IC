개요Summary

The Cybersecurity Act 2018 is Singapore's principal national cybersecurity law, enacted in February 2018 and coming into force in August 2018. The Act established the role of the Commissioner of Cybersecurity (held by the Chief Executive of the Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA)) and created the legal framework for designating and regulating Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) [1].

The Act is primarily a regulatory and incident-response instrument rather than a criminal statute — cybercrime offences are defined separately in the Computer Misuse Act.

역사적 배경Historical Context

Prior to the Cybersecurity Act 2018, Singapore's cybersecurity framework relied on administrative coordination by the CSA (established 2015) without dedicated statutory authority. The 2018 Act codified CSA's role and provided statutory backing for incident response.

국제공조 관련 주요 조항Key Provisions for IC

The Act is principally domestic in focus but supports international cooperation through: - Incident information sharing with foreign CERTs and cybersecurity agencies under Commissioner's discretion - Mutual assistance via MLAT or Interpol for cross-border cyber incidents involving Singapore CII

당사국 및 참여Parties and Participation

This is a Singapore national law, not a multilateral instrument. It binds only Singapore and operates within Singapore's jurisdiction.

실무 적용Implementation in Practice

The Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA) enforces the Act and has designated CII sectors including banking, energy, water, government services, healthcare, transport, media, infocomm, and security/emergency services [2].

2024 Amendments

The Cybersecurity (Amendment) Act 2024 expanded CSA's regulatory scope to cover foundational digital infrastructure and entities of special cybersecurity interest.

모순점 및 미해결 문제Contradictions & Open Questions

  • Interplay between the Cybersecurity Act (regulatory) and the Computer Misuse Act (criminal) in cases with both regulatory and law-enforcement dimensions
  • Scope of Commissioner's information-sharing authority with foreign agencies not fully documented in public materials
