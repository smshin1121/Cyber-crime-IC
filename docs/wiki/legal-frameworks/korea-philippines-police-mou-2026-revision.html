개요Summary

The Korea–Philippines police cooperation MOU is a bilateral memorandum of understanding between the Korean National Police Agency (KNPA) and the Philippine National Police (PNP), originally signed in 2007 and revised twice since (the second revision being signed in Manila on 2026-03-03 by Acting KNPA Commissioner Yoo Jae-sung 유재성 and PNP Chief Jose Melencio Nartatez Jr.). The 2026 revision strengthens bilateral cooperation against "drugs, online scams, and sophisticated transnational crimes" (마약·온라인 스캠 등 지능화된 초국가범죄), expands the existing in-country Korean Desk at the Philippine National Capital Region Police Office, and operationalises the bilateral fugitive-extradition pathway between the two states.

국제공조 관련 주요 조항Key Provisions for IC

The 2026 revision is described in the cited Korean tier-1 source as expanding bilateral cooperation along four axes:

  1. Joint response to drugs, online scams, and sophisticated transnational crimes — the substantive scope expansion beyond traditional investigative cooperation.
  2. Investigative information sharing — expanded bilateral information sharing on transnational crime cases involving Korean nationals or Korean victims.
  3. Rapid apprehension and repatriation of overseas Korean fugitives — directly operationalises the public-record extradition pathway from the Philippines into Korea, demonstrated at scale by the 2026-04-27 73-person extradition operation (31 of 73 Korean nationals extradited from the Philippines, 28 placed in custody on remand).
  4. Korean Desk expansion — adds one additional KNPA liaison officer at the Philippine NCR Police Office during H1 2026.

The revision is signed in the context of bilateral discussions with Philippine Immigration Commissioner Joel Antonio Viado on extradition procedures and with Philippine organised-crime response committee chair Benjamin Acorda Jr.

실무 적용Implementation in Practice

The MOU operationalises bilateral cooperation through the Korean Desk model — a Korea-side liaison structure embedded in the Philippine NCR Police Office. The 2026 revision adds one additional KNPA officer to the existing 2012-established Korean Desk, expanding the desk's capacity to handle cybercrime / online-scam / drug cases involving Korean victims or Korean fugitives in the Philippines.

The bilateral Korean-fugitive extradition pathway operationalised by this MOU is documented at scale in the 2026-04-27 KNPA announcement of the 73-person extradition cycle, in which 31 Korean nationals were extradited from the Philippines (alongside 42 from Cambodia under a separate Korean field unit named 「코리아전담반」).

다른 프레임워크와의 관계Relationship to Other Frameworks

The 2026 announcement explicitly names three related multilateral structures in which the bilateral Korea-Philippines cooperation participates:

  • IICA (International Cooperation Consultative Body) — KNPA-led with Philippine participation.
  • ANCRA (Asia Narcotic Crime Response Cooperation) — Asia-region drug-cooperation framework.
  • ICON (International Drug Investigation Conference) — KNPA-hosted; scheduled for Seoul in 2026.

These multilateral structures are outside this wiki's current scope but are flagged here as context for the bilateral MOU's operating environment.

Korean Perspective (한국 관점)

The 2026 MOU revision is, on its face, a primarily-bilateral arrangement that expands a structure (Korean Desk) Korea has already operated in the Philippines for over a decade. The substantive expansion — including online-scam (사이버범죄 / 보이스피싱 / 로맨스스캠) cooperation in the scope language — connects the bilateral framework directly to the wiki's broader Korean voice-phishing / online-fraud IC corpus and to the contemporaneous 73-person extradition cycle, which shows how the MOU is operationalised at scale.
