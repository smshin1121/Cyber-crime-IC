주요 발견사항

  • On 2025-02-19 the Ontario RCMP Cybercrime Investigative Team (CIT) Toronto executed search warrants in the Greater Toronto Area and arrested two Toronto residents — Chakib Mansouri (29) and Majdouline Alouah (31) — alleged to be among the top 50 most active worldwide subscribers of the iSpoof.cc caller-ID spoofing service, having defrauded at least 570 Canadian victims of millions of dollars through spoofing, phishing and smishing schemes that impersonated banks, government and police.
  • The RCMP press release explicitly thanks the London Metropolitan Police, the Dutch National Police, EUROPOL and EUROJUST as international partners, alongside Toronto Police, Peel Regional Police, FINTRAC, the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre (CAFC) and Canada's National Cybercrime Coordination Centre (NC3). This is the Canadian-side downstream prosecution of users of the UK-led iSpoof.cc takedown coordinated through Europol/Eurojust in November 2022.
  • Charges laid include Fraud (Criminal Code s.380(1)), Unauthorized use of Computer (s.342.1), Laundering Proceeds of Crime (s.462.31), Unauthorized Possession of Credit Card Data (s.430(1.1)) and Possessing the Proceeds of Crime (s.354). The accompanying statement by Inspector Lina Dabit (Officer in Charge, CIT Toronto, Central Region RCMP) frames the case as an exemplar of cross-border cybercrime collaboration.

Source Summary

Official Royal Canadian Mounted Police press release issued by RCMP Central Region (Ontario) on 2025-02-20 announcing that the Ontario RCMP Cybercrime Investigative Team (CIT) Toronto arrested two Toronto residents — Chakib Mansouri (29) and Majdouline Alouah (31) — for operating one of Canada's most prolific spoofing-as-a-service downstream fraud schemes. The pair are alleged to have been among the top 50 worldwide subscribers of the iSpoof.cc caller-ID spoofing platform, using it (with companion phishing and smishing methods) to impersonate banks, government and police, defrauding at least 570 Canadian victims of millions of dollars.

The release explicitly enumerates an international cooperation chain — RCMP CIT Toronto cooperating with the London Metropolitan Police, the Dutch National Police, EUROPOL and EUROJUST — alongside Canadian municipal, provincial and federal partners (Toronto Police, Peel Regional Police, FINTRAC, the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre, and the National Cybercrime Coordination Centre). This places the Canadian arrest in the broader iSpoof.cc takedown investigation lineage that began with the UK Metropolitan Police's Operation Elaborate in November 2022, in which Europol and Eurojust coordinated multi-jurisdictional action against thousands of iSpoof users worldwide.
