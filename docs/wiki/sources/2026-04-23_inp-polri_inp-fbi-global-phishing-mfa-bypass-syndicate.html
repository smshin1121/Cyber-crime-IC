주요 발견사항

  • Indonesian National Police (INP) and the U.S. FBI jointly uncovered a cross-border phishing-as-a-service syndicate operated by an Indonesian couple from Jakarta, with arrest executed by INP Bareskrim on 22 April 2026.
  • Suspect GWL (self-taught developer, active since 2018) wrote and sold malicious scripts that successfully bypassed Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA), enabling Business Email Compromise schemes; suspect FYT laundered proceeds through cryptocurrency wallets.
  • Operation compromised 17,000 accounts and victimised 34,000 individuals globally, including unspecified United States victims and nine Indonesian corporate entities, with estimated losses of USD 20 million.
  • Charges filed under Indonesia's Electronic Information and Transactions (ITE) Law, carrying a maximum sentence of 15 years' imprisonment; senior INP voice is Inspector General Nunung Syaifuddin, Deputy Chief of the Criminal Investigation Agency (Bareskrim).
  • Tier-1 primary source: published on the official Indonesian National Police English newsroom (inp.polri.go.id), with publication metadata (pubdate, createdate, publishdate) all set to 2026-04-23 02:30:00.
  • Cooperation channel described as a direct INP–FBI joint investigation; the release does not identify any treaty instrument (no MLAT, no Budapest Convention reference — Indonesia is not a party to Budapest Convention).
