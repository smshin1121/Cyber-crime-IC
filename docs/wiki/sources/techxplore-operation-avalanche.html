개요Summary

The actual TechXplore article (AFP wire copy) reports on Operation reWired, not Avalanche:

  1. 281 global arrests: 167 in Nigeria, 74 in the US, 18 in Turkey, 15 in Ghana, plus arrests in France, Italy, Japan, Kenya, Malaysia, UK
  2. Financial seizures: ~US$3.7 million globally; $169,850 + 92M naira recovered in Nigeria alone
  3. Operation launch: May 2019 joint FBI-EFCC initiative
  4. Target: BEC fraud (business email compromise), characterised by Nigerian officials as 'Yahoo Yahoo boys'
  5. Operational success narrative: EFCC director quoted describing 'tremendous successes'

Why this source matters

Despite the URL-topic mismatch, the article has independent analytical value:

  • Rare AFP-syndicated FBI/EFCC cooperation documentation — useful for Nigeria country profile updates on international cooperation posture
  • Specific Nigeria-recovered cash figures ($169,850 + 92M naira) — a rare public data point on in-country asset recovery from BEC arrests
  • 'Yahoo Yahoo boys' rhetoric — documents how Nigerian law enforcement publicly frames BEC cooperation, which is relevant to nigeria cooperation assessment
  • Multi-country BEC arrest distribution — shows the geographic distribution pattern (Nigeria-heavy + US-heavy + scattered Europe/Asia) that is characteristic of BEC takedowns
