개요Summary

CBS News reports that the US Department of Justice charged two Russian nationals, Roman Berezhnoy (33) and Egor Nikolaevich Glebov (39), with operating the Phobos ransomware-as-a-service. Key facts as reported by CBS News:

  1. The Phobos criminal operation allegedly generated over USD 16 million in ransom payments
  2. Targets included hundreds of US entities, with children's hospitals, healthcare providers, and educational institutions highlighted
  3. Both defendants face multiple charges carrying 5–20 year maximum sentences
  4. Neither was in US custody at the time of the unsealing — extradition or voluntary surrender would be required for prosecution
  5. The charges follow a sustained international effort against Phobos-affiliated groups including the 8Base ransomware crew, four of whom were arrested in Thailand in February 2025
