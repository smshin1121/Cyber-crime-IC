주요 발견사항

  • 2nd tier-1 RCMP own-domain (rcmp.ca/en/gazette) source for the Ontario RCMP iSpoof.cc Toronto subscriber arrest operation, published 2025-10-30 — 8 months after the 2025-02-20 arrest press release. Authored by Paul Monteith (RCMP Federal Policing Central Region) for the RCMP Gazette in-house publication. Confirms the same 9-partner cooperation chain (London Metropolitan Police, Dutch National Police, EUROPOL, EUROJUST, Toronto Police, Peel Regional Police, FINTRAC, Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre, National Cybercrime Coordination Centre) verbatim as in the original arrest release.
  • Adds the post-arrest procedural-history information missing from the original press release: in August 2025 the couple plead guilty to all 5 charges (fraud, unauthorized use of a computer, laundering proceeds of crime, unauthorized possession of credit card data, possessing the proceeds of crime); Mansouri sentenced to 4 years 9 months, Alouah sentenced to 2 years 9 months. Resolves the 'missing_fields' item 'Outcome of subsequent court proceedings (release covers only initial charges, not any later plea or sentence)' from the operation page.
  • Adds the investigative-tradecraft detail from inside the RCMP unit: (a) UK Metropolitan Police 'late 2022' tip → RCMP about an iSpoof.cc Canada-defrauding account = the initial intelligence-sharing event that founded the Canadian investigation; (b) early breakthrough — call logs showed account users used a 'spy function / live call monitoring function' that displayed actual rather than spoofed numbers, enabling RCMP to identify the phone-number owners by open-source research; (c) Toronto Police Service responded to a stolen-car report from the suspects and inadvertently obtained body-worn-camera voice recordings that voice-matched the bank-scam recordings; (d) RCMP undercover operative gained suspects' trust and obtained on-tape demonstration of the scam methodology and cryptocurrency/online-casino laundering channels; (e) 40 mobile phones seized at search, with one phone alone yielding hundreds of victims; final estimate revised from ≈600 to 'literally thousands'.
  • Quotes 3 named RCMP officers (Cpl Erin Power, S/Sgt Wayne Wong — team lead, and now-retired Inspector Lina Dabit who led the Cybercrime Unit at the time of arrest) plus the article author Paul Monteith. The 2nd-source naming of 3 officers + 1 author from the same Federal Policing Central Region cybercrime unit substantially raises the source_tier and credibility_index of the operation page.
  • Adds the legal-precedent dimension: the Justice of the Peace's decision to remand Alouah into pre-trial custody (Mansouri did not seek bail) on 'primary, secondary, and tertiary grounds' is cited by Power as a decision 'now [able to be] cited by prosecutors in future cybercrime cases, providing legal support for keeping suspects in these trials in jail while awaiting trial' — a Canadian-domestic procedural-law contribution to the bail-conditions-for-cyber-scammers jurisprudence that flows from this operation.

Source Summary

This is an RCMP-Gazette long-form article (rcmp.ca own-domain) authored by Paul Monteith of RCMP Federal Policing Central Region, published 2025-10-30, providing the second tier-1 own-domain RCMP record of the Ontario RCMP Cybercrime Unit's investigation and prosecution of two Toronto residents — Chakib Mansouri and Majdouline Alouah — for their alleged top-50-worldwide-subscriber position on the iSpoof.cc caller-ID spoofing-as-a-service platform. It corroborates the original 2025-02-20 RCMP Central Region press release 2025-02-20_rcmp-grc-gc-ca_ontario-rcmp-shuts-prolific-cyber-fraudsters from a different RCMP publishing channel (Gazette in-house magazine rather than the operational press-release wire), 8 months after the arrest.

Beyond corroboration, this 2nd source supplies three categories of material absent from the 1st source: (a) procedural-history closure — both defendants plead guilty in August 2025 to all 5 charges; Mansouri sentenced to 4 years 9 months, Alouah sentenced to 2 years 9 months; (b) investigative tradecraft — the UK Met initial tip in "late 2022", the call-logs / spy-function breakthrough, the stolen-car-report voice-match coincidence, the RCMP undercover operative, and the 40-phones / "literally thousands of victims" final scope; (c) legal-precedent dimension — Alouah's pre-trial detention decision is presented as a citable precedent for future Canadian cybercrime bail hearings.
