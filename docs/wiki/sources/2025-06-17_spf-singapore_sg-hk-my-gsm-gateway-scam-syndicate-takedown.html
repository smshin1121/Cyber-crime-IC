주요 발견사항

  • Singapore Police Force (SPF), Hong Kong Police Force (HKPF), and Royal Malaysia Police (RMP) ran a two-month joint operation (April–June 2025) targeting a VoIP GSM gateway scam-call syndicate.
  • 11 persons aged 24–48 arrested across the three jurisdictions; 226 GSM gateway devices seized (Singapore 75, Hong Kong 141, Malaysia 10).
  • A 29-year-old suspect arrested in Malaysia on 3 June 2025 was handed over to Singapore and charged on 5 June 2025 under Penal Code s.420 r/w s.120B (criminal conspiracy to cheat).
  • Operation was coordinated via FRONTIER+, the 10-jurisdiction Cross-border Anti-Scam Collaboration Platform led by HKPF, but is a distinct case from the FRONTIER+ first joint operation (April-May 2025).
  • Syndicate used a cloud-based remote operating system to route fraudulent calls through local mobile networks, masking foreign origin from Singapore victims.
  • SPF units involved: Commercial Affairs Department (CAD), Criminal Investigation Department (CID), Police Intelligence Department (PID); David Chew, Director CAD, provided the official quote.

Source Summary

Official Singapore Police Force press release dated 17 June 2025 announcing the conclusion of a tri-lateral joint enforcement operation conducted between SPF, the Hong Kong Police Force, and the Royal Malaysia Police from April to June 2025. The operation dismantled a criminal syndicate operating Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) GSM gateway devices that routed fraudulent scam calls through local mobile phone networks in all three jurisdictions, leading victims in Singapore to believe the calls were originating domestically.

Verified Facts

  • Operation period: April 2025 – June 2025 (approximately two months of sustained intelligence gathering and investigations).
  • Arrests: 11 persons total, aged between 24 and 48, distributed across the three jurisdictions.
  • Seizures: 226 GSM gateway devices total — 75 in Singapore, 141 in Hong Kong, 10 in Malaysia.
  • Modus operandi: Cloud-based remote operating system linking to GSM mobile networks across the three jurisdictions, enabling remote routing of fraudulent calls through local cellular infrastructure to disguise call origin.
  • Specific charge in Singapore: A 29-year-old suspect arrested in Malaysia on 3 June 2025, was handed over to Singapore and charged in court on 5 June 2025 with Being a Party to a Criminal Conspiracy to commit Cheating offences under Section 420 read with Section 120B of the Penal Code 1871. Maximum penalty: 10 years' imprisonment plus fine.
  • Coordination platform: FRONTIER+, a Hong Kong–led Cross-border Anti-Scam Collaboration Platform comprising 10 jurisdictions (including SG, HK, MY).
  • Senior officer quote: David Chew, Director of the SPF Commercial Affairs Department.

Reliability Assessment

  • reliability: high — official Singapore Police Force communiqué, dual-attributed (SPF Public Affairs Department) with named senior officer.
  • credibility: confirmed — operational facts (arrests, seizures, hand-over and charge) corroborated by independent secondary reporting (SCMP, RTHK, Hong Kong Police media, Malaysia press).
  • sensitivity: public — full text published on police.gov.sg media hub.

Notes

  • This operation is distinct from the FRONTIER+ first joint cross-border anti-scam operation (28 April – 28 May 2025), which involved seven jurisdictions and produced 1,858 arrests across multi-fraud typologies. The June 17 release covers a tri-lateral, infrastructure-focused VoIP/GSM gateway takedown that ran in parallel under the same FRONTIER+ umbrella.
  • Article-level legal anchor: Singapore Penal Code 1871, sections 420 (cheating) and 120B (criminal conspiracy).
