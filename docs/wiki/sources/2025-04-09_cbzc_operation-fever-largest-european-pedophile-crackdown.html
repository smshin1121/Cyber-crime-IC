Operation FEVER — CBZC Press Release (Polish, 2025-04-09)

Source Overview

Official press release published on 9 April 2025 by Centralne Biuro Zwalczania Cyberprzestępczości (CBZC), the Polish Police's Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime. Polish-language original. Tier-1 primary source: a national cybercrime law-enforcement agency directly reporting on its own coordinated international action.

The release announces and describes Operation FEVER, a CBZC-led umbrella operation against producers, distributors, and possessors of child sexual abuse material (CSAM), conducted while Poland held the Presidency of the Council of the European Union (1 January – 30 June 2025). CBZC frames this as the largest operation it has ever organised together with the Komenda Główna Policji (KGP — National Police Headquarters).

Key findings

  • 166 suspects detained internationally; 98 in Poland (ages 22–78); 48 placed in pre-trial detention by Polish courts; 111 arrested for sharing/distributing CSAM (per the release).
  • Polish operational footprint: 159 searches, nearly 600 officers, more than 1,700 devices/data carriers seized, more than 520,000 CSAM video and image files seized; narcotics and 3 unauthorised firearms also seized.
  • Twelve named participating countries: Poland (lead) + Denmark, Estonia, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Latvia, Romania, Spain, Sweden, Bulgaria. Additionally UK (NCA) and US (FBI, HSI, NCMEC) participated at international level.
  • Coordination mechanisms: Europol and J-CAT (Joint Cybercrime Action Taskforce) are explicitly named.
  • Polish prosecutorial actor: Departament do Spraw Cyberprzestępczości i Informatyzacji Prokuratury Krajowej (cybercrime department of the National Public Prosecutor's Office).
  • Embedded sub-operations: the release specifies that "Kidflix" (with German police) was a component of FEVER, with 12 Polish persons of interest reached and the platform's server seized in Germany/Netherlands on 11 March 2025; and a separate CBZC–FBI dark web forum track.
  • Historical framing: five earlier domestic CBZC operations (ANASTAZJA, BARBOSSA, CARLOS, DAKOTA, ENOLA GAY, 2022–2024) charged 280+ people and secured 2M+ CSAM files; FEVER is presented as the international scale-up.
