개요Summary

The Group-IB press release announces Operation Lyrebird, a joint investigation between Group-IB and INTERPOL that resulted in the May 2021 arrest by Moroccan police of a suspect tracked by Group-IB as "Dr HeX".

Key attribution and operational facts:

  1. 2-year investigation — Group-IB's Threat Intelligence team began tracking Dr HeX in approximately 2019
  2. Attribution method: Group-IB analysts extracted a phishing kit, found an email address embedded in the kit, pivoted to the suspect's YouTube channel (registered under "Dr HeX"), and cross-referenced OSINT (domain registrations, forum activity) to build a full identity profile
  3. Scope of activity: Dr HeX was linked to attacks on 134 websites from 2009-2018, phishing kit development, carding kits, malware development, and fraud
  4. Victim base: French telecommunications companies, major French banks, and multinational corporations — the suspect's French-language skills enabled targeting of Francophone victims
  5. Cooperation pathway: Group-IB (private intel) → INTERPOL (coordination) → Moroccan national police (arrest) — a three-layer public-private model
  6. INTERPOL African Joint Operation against Cybercrime (AFJOC) framework — the operation was part of INTERPOL's broader capacity-building effort in Africa
