주요 발견사항

  • Hellenic Police press release dated 2025-11-13 announces Greece's participation in the November 2025 phase of the international Operation Endgame and the 2025-11-03 arrest in Attica of a 38-year-old foreign national assessed to be the principal developer/seller of the VenomRAT remote-access trojan since 2020.
  • Foreign law-enforcement cooperation explicitly named in the Hellenic Police release: Europol (operational coordination), Eurojust (judicial coordination), U.S. FBI (on-site participation at the search of the Attica residence), and French judicial authorities (issuing authority of the European Arrest Warrant under which the suspect was detained). L24 requirement of ≥2 distinct cooperating LE/prosecutorial agencies satisfied (Hellenic Police + FBI + Europol + Eurojust + French judiciary).
  • On-site seizures from the Attica residence: 7 hard drives, 3 USB devices, 11 debit cards, mobile phones, malware-development equipment, and a cryptocurrency wallet valued at approximately US$140,424. VenomRAT was sold as a subscription service (€150/month to €1,550/year) providing keystroke logging, camera/microphone access, text data exfiltration, and cryptocurrency-wallet theft.
  • Wider November 2025 Endgame phase outcomes coordinated by Europol/Eurojust (across all 11 participating jurisdictions): 1,025 servers dismantled, 20 domains seized, 11 physical search locations executed (Germany 1, Greece 1, Netherlands 9). Participating jurisdictions: Australia, Belgium, Canada, Denmark, France, Germany, Greece, Lithuania, the Netherlands, the United Kingdom, and the United States.
  • Greek arrest is filed as a companion sub-operation page under the umbrella operation-endgame-phase3 (the broader Nov 2025 Rhadamanthys/VenomRAT/Elysium phase), mirroring the pattern set by the existing rcmp-cit-v-canada-endgame-surrey-malware-loader-arrest-2025 page for the Canadian RCMP-CIT-V limb.
