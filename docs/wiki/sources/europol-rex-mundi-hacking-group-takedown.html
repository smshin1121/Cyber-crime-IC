  • On 18 May 2018, Royal Thai Police arrested a 25-year-old French national in Thailand on a French international arrest warrant — the eighth and final arrest in a year-long investigation into the Rex Mundi hacking/extortion group, coordinated by Europol's Joint Cybercrime Action Taskforce (J-CAT)

개요Summary

Europol's press release describes the final arrest in the Rex Mundi takedown — a 25-year-old French coder apprehended in Thailand on 18 May 2018, completing the dismantling of a year-long cyber-extortion ring. Key points:

  1. Investigation timeline: One year from initial attack (May 2017) to final arrest (May 2018)
  2. Victim: An unnamed UK company whose customer data was exfiltrated in May 2017
  3. Ransom structure: €580,000 (non-disclosure only) OR €825,000 (with breach methodology), with €210,000/day penalty — an unusually detailed ransom structure for 2017
  4. Arrests sequence: 5 in France (June 2017) + 2 in France (October 2017) + 1 in Thailand (May 2018) = 8 total
  5. Dark-web hacker-for-hire element: The French ringleader admitted hiring hackers through the dark web rather than personally conducting the intrusion — an important legal distinction for prosecution
  6. J-CAT role: The operation was supported by Europol's Joint Cybercrime Action Taskforce, which provided 24/7 operational coordination through the Thailand arrest phase

Why this source matters

For the IC analysis on rex-mundi-takedown this Europol release is the primary (Tier 2) source and is particularly valuable because:

  • It documents a rare EU-Thailand cybercrime cooperation success — Thailand is not always responsive to European cyber cooperation requests
  • It shows the 'hacker-for-hire' criminal structure — the main organiser and the technical attacker were different individuals, raising interesting attribution/charging questions
  • It demonstrates Europol 24/7 Operational Centre usage for a named case with concrete outcomes
  • It provides a rare named ransom schedule (€580K / €825K / €210K/day) useful for comparative extortion-demand analysis
