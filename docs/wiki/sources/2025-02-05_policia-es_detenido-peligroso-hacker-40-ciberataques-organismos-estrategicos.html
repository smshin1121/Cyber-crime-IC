주요 발견사항

  • On the Tuesday before 5 February 2025 (i.e., 4 February 2025) the Spanish National Police (Policía Nacional) and the Guardia Civil arrested in Calpe (Alicante province, Spain) an individual suspected of more than 40 cyberattacks against Spanish and international public and private entities, on charges of unauthorised disclosure of secrets, illicit access to computer systems, computer damage, and money laundering.
  • Victims explicitly named in the release include the Spanish Guardia Civil, the Spanish Ministry of Defence, the Fábrica Nacional de Moneda y Timbre (Royal Mint), the Servicio Público de Empleo Estatal, the Ministry of Education and Vocational Training and Sports, the Dirección General de Tráfico, the Generalitat Valenciana, multiple Spanish universities, NATO databases, U.S. Army databases, the United Nations, and the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO).
  • Seizures include more than 50 cryptocurrency accounts with various cryptoassets and diverse IT equipment seized at the suspect's residence during the search.
  • Multi-jurisdiction cooperation explicitly named in the primary source: Spain (Policía Nacional + Guardia Civil + Centro Criptológico Nacional / Centro Nacional de Inteligencia) and the United States (Homeland Security Investigations of the Department of Homeland Security), with EUROPOL acting as the intergovernmental coordinating partner. This is the language that anchors the operation page's L24-compliant participating_countries list (Spain + United States).
  • Trigger for the operation: the Unidad Central Operativa (UCO) of the Guardia Civil developed the investigation after the suspect's late-December 2024 attack on two Guardia Civil and Ministry of Defence databases; both Spanish forces then executed the operational exploitation jointly.
  • Tier-1 primary source: official press release on policia.es (Spanish National Police institutional domain).

Source Summary

Official press release published on 5 February 2025 by the Spanish National Police (Policía Nacional) on its institutional press-release portal (policia.es Sala de prensa, comunicado ID 16448), authored by the Dirección General de la Policía - Gabinete de Prensa. Announces the arrest in Calpe (Alicante) of a hacker described as responsible for more than 40 cyberattacks against strategic Spanish and international public and private entities, charged with "descubrimiento y revelación de secretos", "acceso ilícito a sistemas informáticos", "daños informáticos" and "blanqueo de capitales".

The suspect is not named in the public source. The release reports more than 50 cryptocurrency accounts held by the suspect and the seizure of diverse IT equipment under judicial search.

The release is published in Spanish on the Policía Nacional's own institutional domain and constitutes a tier-1 primary source under LESSONS L24.

Relevance to IC

This source matters for the cyber-IC wiki in three ways:

  1. Explicit Spain–United States cyber-LE cooperation — the release names "Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) de los EE.UU." as an international collaborator alongside EUROPOL. This is a tier-1 acknowledgment of bilateral cyber-LE cooperation for a hacking / unauthorised access case, not via FBI but via HSI (DHS), which is a distinct U.S. cyber-LE channel relative to most prior wiki Spain–U.S. operations.
  2. Multiple Spanish national-security actors — the operation visibly combines judicial police (Policía Nacional + Guardia Civil) with national cryptologic / signals-intelligence services (CCN / CNI), illustrating how high-profile cyber-attack cases on Spanish strategic targets are handled on the Spanish side. The investigation trigger came from the Guardia Civil UCO after the suspect's late-2024 attack on Guardia Civil and Ministry of Defence databases.
  3. Documented attack surface — the release lists named institutional victims including NATO, U.S. Army, UN, and ICAO databases. This is one of the few publicly available primary sources from a Spanish LE agency that names specific intergovernmental victims of a single hacker, which makes it useful for downstream analysis of cyber-attack patterns against strategic targets.
