주요 발견사항

  • On 4 March 2026 a joint operation by the French Gendarmerie's GIGN antenna in Guadeloupe, the Section de recherches de Saint-Martin / Saint-Barthélemy, and the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation arrested a 24-year-old U.S. national in Saint-Martin (French Caribbean) suspected of having stolen approximately USD 46 million in cryptocurrency before fleeing U.S. territory.
  • The cooperation channel was an international arrest warrant (mandat d'arrêt international) executed by the Gendarmerie following an FBI request transmitted via the Commandement de la gendarmerie de Saint-Barthélemy et Saint-Martin (COMGEND); the case is supervised by the parquet général de Guadeloupe.
  • Seizures included high-end IT equipment, cryptocurrency-wallet credentials, multiple phones, approximately EUR 250,000 in cash, a loaded Glock pistol, and recovery of the USD 46 million in stolen cryptocurrency.
  • The press release explicitly names cooperation partners: U.S. authorities (FBI), and local Caribbean partners 'les Britanniques et les Hollandais' (the British and the Dutch). Three FBI agents were physically present during the action day.
  • Pre-trial detention is in effect pending extradition (procédure d'extradition) to the United States; the suspect is described in French legal terms as in 'retenue judiciaire' then 'détention provisoire'.
  • Tier-1 primary source: official Gendarmerie nationale press release on its institutional domain (gendarmerie.interieur.gouv.fr).

Source Summary

Official French Gendarmerie nationale press release dated 6 March 2026, authored by commandant Céline Morin. Reports the arrest on 4 March 2026 in Saint-Martin (Caribbean overseas collectivity of France) of a 24-year-old U.S. national identified by U.S. authorities as a "target principale" — a hacker suspected of having stolen the equivalent of USD 46 million in cryptocurrency before leaving the United States.

The arrest was executed by the Antenne GIGN de Guadeloupe (about ten GIGN operators) together with three investigators from the Section de recherches de Saint-Martin, in the presence of three FBI agents, on the basis of an international arrest warrant. The suspect is in pre-trial detention pending extradition to the United States.

Relevance to IC

This source is operationally significant for cyber-IC mapping in three ways:

  1. Direct US–France cyber cooperation through the FBI legal-attaché channel and a French overseas-collectivity gendarmerie unit, executed via a specialised intervention unit (GIGN) rather than ordinary judicial police — a pattern not previously documented in this wiki.
  2. Multi-jurisdiction Caribbean coordination: the press release explicitly names the British and the Dutch as local partners, reflecting the tri-national geography of Saint-Martin / Sint Maarten and the British Overseas Territories nearby.
  3. Cryptocurrency theft → recovery: the USD 46M is reported as recovered in the same operation as the arrest, and includes seizure of wallet credentials and IT material — a tangible cyber-asset-recovery data point on the cooperation track.
