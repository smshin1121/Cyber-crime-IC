주요 발견사항

  • 2025-10-30: Brazilian Federal Police (Polícia Federal, PF) launched Phase 2 of Operação Magna Fraus, targeting an organised criminal group specialised in digital bank fraud against Brazilian banks and payment institutions managing PIX (Brazilian instant-payment) transfers.
  • Total losses: more than R$ 813 million (≈ USD 145 million at Oct 2025 rates) drained from bank/payment-institution accounts used to manage customer PIX transfers.
  • Phase 2 warrants: 42 search-and-seizure + 26 arrest warrants (19 preventive + 7 temporary) in 11 Brazilian cities across 7 states (GO, DF, SC, SP, MG, PB, BA).
  • Asset-blocking measures: up to R$ 640 million in goods and values.
  • International cooperation explicitly named: INTERPOL Command and Coordination Centre (CCC), INTERPOL National Central Bureaux in Brazil, Spain, Argentina and Portugal, and Spain's Brigada Central de Fraudes Informáticos (Polícia Nacional / Spanish National Police).
  • Joint Brazilian authorities: PF (federal) + Cyber GAECO (Grupo de Atuação Especial de Combate ao Crime Organizado) of the São Paulo State Public Prosecution Service (Ministério Público do Estado de São Paulo).
  • Charged offences (Brazilian penal code): organização criminosa (criminal organisation), invasão de dispositivo informático (computer-device intrusion), furto mediante fraude eletrônica (theft by electronic fraud), lavagem de dinheiro (money laundering).
  • Partial balance (as of 2025-10-31): 21 arrests total — 13 in Brazil + 8 abroad (6 in Spain + 2 in Argentina); 15 high-end vehicles, 25 real-estate properties, R$ 1M+ in crypto, luxury goods seized; multiple bank-account blocks (totals not yet tabulated).

Source Summary

Brazilian Federal Police (PF) press release dated 2025-10-30 (last updated 2025-10-31) announcing Phase 2 of Operação Magna Fraus, a multi-jurisdiction takedown of a criminal organisation that drained more than R$ 813 million from accounts used by Brazilian banks and payment institutions to manage customer PIX (Brazil's instant-payment system) transfers via computer-device intrusion (invasão de dispositivo informático). The action combined federal police execution in 11 Brazilian cities across 7 states with international arrests in Spain (6) and Argentina (2), coordinated through INTERPOL channels and the Spanish National Police's computer-fraud brigade.

IC Pattern

This release documents a Brazil-led, INTERPOL-mediated, Iberian-Latin American multi-NCB action-day pattern. Notable features:

  • PF as lead agency / requesting state, with Cyber GAECO of the São Paulo Public Prosecution Service (Ministério Público do Estado de São Paulo, 상파울루주 검찰) co-operating on the prosecutorial side.
  • INTERPOL Command and Coordination Centre (CCC) as the cross-jurisdictional coordination node for simultaneous arrests.
  • Four-NCB axis: INTERPOL National Central Bureaux in Brazil, Spain, Argentina, and Portugal — with Portugal listed as an NCB-cooperation partner although no Portuguese arrests were reported in the partial balance (suggesting Portugal-side investigative or asset-tracing role rather than action-day arrests; flag for verification).
  • Spain's Brigada Central de Fraudes Informáticos (Polícia Nacional) named explicitly as the operational counterpart on the Spanish side — i.e. dedicated cyber-fraud unit, not a general policing body.
  • Crime typology — bank-fraud-as-cybercrime: PIX-rail account drain via institutional-account compromise, treated under Brazil's penal code as organised crime + computer-device intrusion + electronic-fraud theft + money laundering, illustrating Brazilian prosecution's hybrid cybercrime / financial-crime framing.

Wiki Cross-Corpus Relevance

This case fills three identifiable wiki gaps:

  1. First Brazil-Spain-Argentina cybercrime IC action-day documented in the wiki. Existing Brazil-side coverage in the wiki (e.g. Operation Cryptoscam (SPF-FPB, 2025), various US-Brazil extraditions) treats Brazil as a partner state in Singapore- or US-led cases; Magna Fraus is the inverse — Brazil-led, with Iberian-LatAm arrests on the Spain/Argentina side.
  2. PIX-payment-system attack vector is a new technique-class in the wiki, complementary to existing bank-trojan and BEC pages.
  3. Cyber GAECO (MP-SP) appears for the first time as a Brazilian prosecutorial partner alongside PF; this is a state-level (not federal) public-ministry cyber unit, structurally distinct from the federal-prosecutorial bodies usually seen in cross-border cases.

Open Questions for Future Sources

  • Phase 1 of Operação Magna Fraus: this release is "Phase 2" — Phase 1 details (date, scope, prior arrests) are referenced but not described and were not located in this release.
  • Portugal's specific role: NCB Portugal listed as a cooperation partner but no Portugal-side arrests reported in the partial balance. Verify whether Portugal contributed asset-tracing, MLA, or a domestic-only investigative track.
  • Final balance: the 2025-10-31 partial balance lists 21 arrests vs. 26 issued arrest warrants; 5 outstanding warrants suggest fugitive subjects whose status would be resolved in subsequent PF updates.
  • Final loss totalisation: R$ 813M is the cumulative scheme-level loss; the share recovered via the R$ 640M asset-blocking order vs. the share permanently lost is not yet quantified.
