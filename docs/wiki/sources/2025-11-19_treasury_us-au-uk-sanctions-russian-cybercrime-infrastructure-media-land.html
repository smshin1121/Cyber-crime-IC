주요 발견사항

  • On 19 Nov 2025, OFAC, Australia's DFAT, and the UK's FCDO announced coordinated trilateral sanctions against Media Land LLC, a St. Petersburg-based bulletproof hosting (BPH) provider that supported Lockbit, BlackSuit, and Play ransomware operators and DDoS attacks against U.S. critical infrastructure.
  • OFAC, in coordination with the FBI, designated Media Land plus three sister companies (ML Cloud, Media Land Technology, Data Center Kirishi) and three leadership/affiliate individuals (Aleksandr Volosovik a.k.a. 'Yalishanda', Kirill Zatolokin, Yulia Pankova).
  • OFAC and the UK additionally designated Hypercore Ltd., a UK-registered front company used by previously-sanctioned Aeza Group (designated 1 Jul 2025) to move IP infrastructure and evade sanctions, plus two further Aeza individuals (Maksim Makarov, Ilya Zakirov) and two enabler companies in Serbia (Smart Digital Ideas DOO) and Uzbekistan (Datavice MCHJ).
  • All designations were made pursuant to E.O. 13694, as amended by E.O. 13757, E.O. 14144, and E.O. 14306 ('E.O. 13694, as further amended'). CISA released parallel BPH-mitigation guidance with international partners.

주요 발견사항Key Findings

  1. Trilateral coordination architecture (high confidence). The action is described on the record as a coordinated, simultaneous designation by OFAC, Australia's Department of Foreign Affairs and Trade, and the United Kingdom's Foreign, Commonwealth and Development Office, with FBI playing the lead U.S. law-enforcement coordinating role.
  2. Bulletproof hosting as ransomware enabler (high confidence). Treasury characterizes Media Land as a "key launching pad for ransomware," explicitly tying its infrastructure to Lockbit, BlackSuit, and Play ransomware operators and to DDoS attacks against U.S. companies and critical infrastructure.
  3. Sanctions-evasion follow-on (high confidence). The Hypercore Ltd. designation is framed as a direct response to a rebranding strategy initiated by Aeza Group's leadership after Aeza's 1 July 2025 OFAC designation; the action is presented as a maintenance-of-pressure operation against the same target ecosystem.
  4. Cross-jurisdictional shell layering (high confidence). The press release identifies Aeza front/enabler entities incorporated in three jurisdictions outside Russia: the United Kingdom (Hypercore Ltd.), Serbia (Smart Digital Ideas DOO), and Uzbekistan (Datavice MCHJ).
  5. Statutory basis (confirmed). All designations cite E.O. 13694, as amended by E.O. 13757, E.O. 14144, and E.O. 14306 — the U.S. cyber-sanctions authority chain. No criminal indictments are announced in this release.
