주요 발견사항

  • Cybercrime-Zentrum (CCZ) at Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Karlsruhe, in joint investigation with Landeskriminalamt Baden-Württemberg (LKA-BW), identified a 61-year-old Swedish national in Helsingborg, Sweden as an alleged enabler of an internationally organized online-trading fraud network defrauding German victims.
  • European Arrest Warrant (EAW) issued by CCZ Karlsruhe; executed by Swedish authorities; defendant extradited from Sweden to Germany in March 2026 and held in pretrial detention (Untersuchungshaft) on arrival.
  • Investigative findings: organized criminal group operating since at least 2018 ran professionally designed online platforms offering fake investment products in financial derivatives. Defendant allegedly set up account/company structures used to receive and forward victim deposits.
  • Quantitative summary: 24 German victims across multiple federal states (Bundesländer); investor deposits totaling more than EUR 1.17 million; defendant personally received approximately EUR 280,000 over several years for her enabling role.
  • Tier-1 IC mechanism: bilateral judicial cooperation via EAW (Framework Decision 2002/584/JHA). ≥2-country LE/judicial cooperation explicitly satisfied: Germany (LKA-BW + CCZ Karlsruhe) ↔ Sweden (executing judicial authority).
  • Investigation continues to identify additional co-perpetrators (Ermittlungen dauern an).

Summary

Official LKA Baden-Württemberg / CCZ Karlsruhe press release announcing that a 61-year-old Swedish national was extradited from Sweden to Germany in March 2026 on the basis of a European Arrest Warrant. She is alleged to have aided and abetted (Beihilfe) an organized online-trading fraud network — operating since at least 2018 — that targeted German retail investors with fraudulent derivatives "trading" platforms and converted victim deposits via corporate accounts she helped establish.

Quantitative scope reported in the primary source: 24 victims, EUR 1.17M+ in investor deposits, EUR 280K+ in personal compensation paid to the Swedish defendant over several years.

IC mechanism characterization

Bilateral judicial cooperation via the European Arrest Warrant (EAW) under Council Framework Decision 2002/584/JHA. CCZ Karlsruhe (German issuing judicial authority) → Swedish executing judicial authority → surrender. EAW is a tier-1 formal IC mechanism explicitly invoked in the primary source.

The release does not name specific Swedish judicial authority (court or prosecutor) — generic "schwedische Behörden" / "Sweden" language used. Identifiable city of arrest: Helsingborg.
