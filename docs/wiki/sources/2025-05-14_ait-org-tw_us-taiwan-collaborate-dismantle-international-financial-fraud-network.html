주요 발견사항

  • First in-country MLAA proffer interview: under the 2002 U.S.-Taiwan Mutual Legal Assistance Agreement (MLAA), Taiwanese prosecutors (TTDPO) and Criminal Investigation Bureau (CIB) investigators traveled to the United States in April 2025 to interview a Taiwanese defendant in U.S. custody — the first such on-site witness questioning by a Taiwanese prosecutor since the MLAA was signed in March 2002.
  • Scope: Taiwanese-led international criminal organisation defrauding 'hundreds of elderly victims across the United States' with 'over US$100 million' laundered through shell companies and international accounts; investigation opened May 2023.
  • Subset indictment by TTDPO names Liao (廖, age 36, alleged ring leader, arrested at Taoyuan International Airport on 2025-01-24, 10-year sentence recommended), Huang (黃, age 39, alleged collections handler, 5-year sentence recommended), with U.S.-based accomplices Hsiao (肖) and Sun (孫); three U.S. victims of US$5.32 million in this subset.
  • Cooperating agencies: Taiwan NPA Criminal Investigation Bureau (CIB), Taiwan MOJ, Taichung District Prosecutors Office (TTDPO), Administrative Enforcement Agency, Taichung City Police Department; U.S. Homeland Security Investigations Taipei, Los Angeles, and Nashville offices; AIT as facilitating channel.
  • Cooperation modality: bilateral MLAA (formal request) supplemented by direct prosecutor-to-prosecutor and police-to-HSI working contacts; AIT functions as the U.S. mission counterpart given the absence of formal diplomatic relations between Washington and Taipei.
  • Charges under Taiwan law: aggravated fraud and money laundering under the Organized Crime Prevention Act, Money Laundering Control Act, Passport Act, and Criminal Code; modus operandi includes use of fake passports to open U.S. bank accounts, romance-scam victim grooming, fraudulent investment solicitation, and laundering through family member accounts and luxury asset purchases (Swiss watch, diamond ring, Bitcoin, Tether, Lexus).

개요Summary

Official press release PR-2510 issued by the American Institute in Taiwan (AIT) on 2025-05-14, announcing the joint U.S.-Taiwan dismantling of a Taiwanese-led financial-fraud network that targeted elderly victims across the United States and laundered over US$100 million. The release accompanies a joint press conference held at the Taiwan Taichung District Prosecutors Office (TTDPO) on the same date.

This is a tier-1 primary source: AIT is the U.S. government's unofficial-relations agency for Taiwan and operates under the auspices of the U.S. Department of State, making AIT press releases authoritative for U.S.-side characterisations of Taiwan-U.S. law-enforcement cooperation. The release names participating units (Taiwan NPA CIB, MOJ, TTDPO, HSI Taipei/LA/Nashville) and identifies the legal channel (the bilateral MLAA signed in March 2002).

Reliability and Credibility

  • Reliability: high. AIT press releases are issued through the agency's official website (ait.org.tw) and serve as the U.S. State Department's primary communication channel concerning Taiwan; AIT is the institutional U.S. counterpart for U.S.-Taiwan relations.
  • Credibility: confirmed. The cooperation event (joint press conference 2025-05-14 at TTDPO) and the underlying indictment are independently corroborated by Taiwan's Focus Taiwan (CNA English service, 2025-05-14) and the Taipei Times (2025-05-15), both of which cite the TTDPO directly.
  • Sensitivity: public. The press release is the public announcement; sealed pre-arrest investigative content is not disclosed.

Notes on the Source Outlet

AIT (American Institute in Taiwan) is the de-facto U.S. embassy in Taiwan; established 1979 under the Taiwan Relations Act, AIT is administered by the U.S. Department of State and functions as the formal U.S. communication and protocol channel with Taiwan in the absence of formal diplomatic recognition. AIT press releases concerning Taiwan-U.S. law enforcement cooperation are therefore the U.S. tier-1 primary record for such cooperation, alongside Taiwan-side documents from the MOJ and TTDPO.
