주요 발견사항

  • 2024-08-08 USAO-MDTN press release announcing the indictment and arrest of Matthew Isaac Knoot (38, Nashville TN) for facilitating a DPRK remote IT-worker laptop-farm scheme operating from approximately July 2022 to August 2023.
  • Verified financial figures: DPRK IT workers paid over USD 250,000 (Knoot cell only); victim-company remediation costs more than USD 500,000.
  • Verified geography: DPRK IT workers operated from China; DPRK IT-worker scheme broadly described as operating from China and Russia. Victims included American AND British companies (UK companies as victims, not as cooperating LE).
  • Verified US prosecuting components: USAO-MDTN, NSD Cyber Section, FBI Memphis Field Office (Nashville Resident Agency), FBI Cyber Division, FBI Counterintelligence Division (the latter via 'DPRK RevGen: Domestic Enabler Initiative' launched March 2024).
  • Verified bilateral cooperation reference: October 2023 joint US-Republic of Korea (South Korea) updated guidance on DPRK IT worker threats — explicitly named in the press release.
  • Verified tri-seal advisory: May 2022 advisory by FBI + Department of the Treasury + Department of State on the DPRK IT-worker threat — designated entities include the North Korean Ministry of Defense and others tied to UN-prohibited WMD programs.
  • Verified updated guidance: May 2024 FBI guidance on indicators of DPRK IT-worker fraud and US-based laptop farms.
  • Verified Knoot charges: conspiracy to cause damage to protected computers, conspiracy to launder monetary instruments, conspiracy to commit wire fraud, intentional damage to protected computers, aggravated identity theft, conspiracy to cause the unlawful employment of aliens. Maximum penalty 20 years; mandatory minimum 2 years on aggravated identity theft.
  • Verified foreign-based facilitator alias: 'Yang Di' (paid Knoot a monthly fee). Stolen US-citizen identity used: 'Andrew M.' Falsely reported to IRS and Social Security Administration.
  • Reference to prior actions ('October 2023 and May 2024') — these prior announcements include the Christina Chapman (Arizona) and Oleksandr Didenko (Ukrainian national; arrested in Poland May 2024) cases. They are referenced but NOT republished or detailed in this 2024-08-08 release.

개요Summary

Authoritative tier-1 primary source: 2024-08-08 USAO-MDTN press release announcing the indictment and arrest of Matthew Isaac Knoot (Nashville, Tennessee) for hosting a "laptop farm" enabling DPRK remote IT workers (operating from China) to fraudulently obtain employment with U.S. and U.K. companies under a stolen U.S. identity ("Andrew M.").

The press release establishes a public-record legal anchor for the DOJ-wide DPRK RevGen: Domestic Enabler Initiative (launched March 2024 by NSD + FBI Cyber + FBI Counterintelligence), and references the October 2023 joint U.S.-Republic of Korea guidance and the May 2022 tri-seal advisory (FBI + Treasury + State) as the multi-agency cooperation backbone against DPRK IT-worker fraud.
