주요 발견사항

  • 4 defendants charged in District of Oregon: Karen Serobovich Vardanyan (Armenia, 33); Levon Georgiyovych Avetisyan (Armenia, 45); Oleg Nikolayevich Lyulyava (Ukraine, 53); Andrii Leonydovich Prykhodchenko (Ukraine, 53).
  • Vardanyan extradited from Ukraine to US on 2025-06-18; first US court appearance 2025-06-20; trial scheduled 2025-08-26 (seven-day jury trial).
  • Avetisyan: subject of US extradition request in France (status: France-side proceedings pending as of 2025-07-16).
  • Lyulyava and Prykhodchenko not in custody.
  • Charges: conspiracy + fraud in connection with computers + extortion in connection with computers; max 5 years federal prison + 3 years supervised release + USD 250,000 fine per count.
  • Activity period: March 2019 – September 2020; Ryuk ransomware deployed on hundreds of victim servers and workstations; targets included an Oregon technology company plus broad US-corporate, government, school district, critical infrastructure, hospital, healthcare cohort.
  • Ransom payments: ~1,610 bitcoins received, valued USD 15M+ at time of payment.
  • Lead: FBI; prosecutor: AUSA Katherine A. Rykken (D.Or.); IC channel: DOJ Office of International Affairs (OIA).
  • Cooperating jurisdictions explicitly named: United States (lead), Ukraine (extradited Vardanyan), France (custody of Avetisyan, extradition request pending). Armenia is the defendant nationality but not enumerated as a cooperation participant.

Source Summary

USAO-OR press release dated 2025-07-16 announcing the 2025-06-18 extradition of Karen Serobovich Vardanyan from Ukraine to the US for his role in Ryuk ransomware attacks (March 2019 – September 2020), with co-defendants in France (Avetisyan, extradition pending) and at large (Lyulyava and Prykhodchenko, both Ukrainian nationals). The Ryuk gang received ~1,610 bitcoins valued at USD 15M+ in ransom payments.

IC Pattern

This release establishes a US-Ukraine-France three-country cooperation pattern for the Ryuk ransomware prosecution thread:

  • United States: lead jurisdiction (FBI investigation; USAO-Oregon prosecution; DOJ OIA international-cooperation channel).
  • Ukraine: extradition source for Vardanyan (June 2025); explicit credit to Ukrainian authorities in the press release.
  • France: custody jurisdiction for Avetisyan; pending US extradition request.
  • Armenia: defendant nationality (Vardanyan, Avetisyan) but not a cooperation participant.
