주요 발견사항

  • DOJ unsealed an indictment returned 7 January 2025 in the Northern District of Georgia charging three Russian nationals — Roman Vitalyevich Ostapenko (55), Alexander Evgenievich Oleynik (44), and Anton Vyachlavovich Tarasov (32) — for operating Blender.io and Sinbad.io cryptocurrency mixers.
  • Charges are conspiracy to commit money laundering (18 U.S.C. § 1956(h)) and operating an unlicensed money transmitting business (18 U.S.C. § 1960).
  • Ostapenko and Oleynik were arrested on 1 December 2024 by Netherlands FIOD and Finnish NBI in a coordinated action with the FBI; Tarasov remains at large — establishing a tri-national arrest pattern documented elsewhere in the wiki.
  • DOJ release names Lazarus Group / DPRK heist proceeds (Axie Infinity Ronin Bridge USD ~620M, March 2022) and ransomware proceeds (TrickBot, Conti, Sodinokibi/REvil, GandCrab) among the laundered funds, linking the case directly to North Korea state-sponsored cybercrime monetization.
  • The indictment confirms the operational succession from Blender.io (2018–April 2022) to Sinbad.io (relaunched October 2022) under common operators, supporting blockchain-analytics findings (Elliptic, February 2023) that Sinbad was a Blender successor.

Source Summary

On 10 January 2025 the U.S. Department of Justice Office of Public Affairs announced the unsealing of a federal indictment, returned by a grand jury in the Northern District of Georgia on 7 January 2025, charging three Russian nationals with operating the cryptocurrency mixers Blender.io (2018–April 2022) and its alleged successor Sinbad.io (October 2022 onward). The release announces the prior arrest of two of the three defendants — Roman Vitalyevich Ostapenko and Alexander Evgenievich Oleynik — on 1 December 2024 in a coordinated international operation involving the Netherlands Fiscal Information and Investigative Service (FIOD), the Finnish National Bureau of Investigation (NBI), and the FBI. The third defendant, Anton Vyachlavovich Tarasov, remains at large.

Relevance to IC

The release is highly relevant to international cooperation on cybercrime in three respects: (1) it documents a discrete tri-national arrest pattern (Netherlands + Finland + United States) effecting the apprehension of two Russian-national defendants on the territory of EU member states, demonstrating that intra-EU + transatlantic informal police cooperation continues to function for cyber-monetization targets even where the home state (russia) refuses extradition cooperation; (2) it formally links Lazarus Group / DPRK state-sponsored cryptocurrency-theft monetization to U.S. federal criminal liability for service operators, complementing the prior OFAC sanctions track (Treasury Sinbad designation, 2023); and (3) it confirms a follow-on prosecution of operators of an OFAC-sanctioned mixer, exemplifying the U.S. interagency model in which sanctions, indictment, and arrest are sequenced across Treasury, FBI, foreign police partners, and the DOJ Office of International Affairs.
