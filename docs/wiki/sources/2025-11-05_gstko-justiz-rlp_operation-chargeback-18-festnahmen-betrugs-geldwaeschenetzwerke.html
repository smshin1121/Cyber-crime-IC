주요 발견사항

  • Action day 4 November 2025 — German Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Koblenz (LZC) and Bundeskriminalamt (BKA) led a 10-jurisdiction coordinated takedown of three transnational credit-card-fraud and money-laundering networks dubbed 'Operation Chargeback'.
  • Verbatim cooperating-jurisdiction roster: 'die enge Zusammenarbeit mit den Behörden in Großbritannien, Italien, Kanada, Luxemburg, den Niederlanden, Singapur, Spanien, den Vereinigten Staaten und Zypern' — UK, Italy, Canada, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Singapore, Spain, US and Cyprus. Eurojust and Europol provided coordination support.
  • 18 arrest warrants executed (5 in Germany); 44 total suspects identified; >60 search locations worldwide (29 in Germany across 8 federal states); approximately EUR 35 million seized in Germany alone.
  • Modus operandi: malicious software implanted in German payment-service-provider systems, fraudulent abuse of credit-card data from approximately 4.3 million cardholders across 193 countries (2016–2021), more than 19 million fake subscriptions on professionally-operated fake streaming/dating/entertainment websites, estimated total damage > EUR 300 million.
  • Charges in Germany: gewerbs- und bandenmäßiger Computerbetrug (gang and commercial computer fraud), money laundering, misuse of regulated payment service providers.

Source Summary — Operation Chargeback (Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Koblenz, 5 Nov 2025)

Publisher and provenance

The Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Koblenz is the regional state-prosecutor authority for the German state of Rhineland-Palatinate. Its Landeszentralstelle Cybercrime (LZC) is the prosecutorial centre of competence for transnational cybercrime cases originating in or affecting Rhineland-Palatinate (and supports the BKA on federal cases). The Koblenz press release at gstko.justiz.rlp.de/presse-aktuelles/... is the own-domain prosecutor release that names the suspects' procedural posture, the German action-day footprint and the cooperating foreign jurisdictions verbatim.
