주요 발견사항

  • Joint action day 2025-06-12: 44 locations searched across Romania and the Republic of Moldova in a coordinated operation against the laundering of phishing fraud proceeds.
  • Joint Investigation Team (JIT) set up with Eurojust assistance in March 2025; Eurojust financed the JIT operations.
  • Lead authorities: Romania — Directorate for Investigating Organised Crime and Terrorism (DIICOT), Galati Regional Structure; Moldova — Prosecutor's Office for Combatting Organised Crime and Special Cases (PCCOCS).
  • Suspect nationalities (4): Romanian, Moldovan, Ukrainian, Italian — operating from professionally organised call centres in Moldova.
  • Victim base: 30 victims identified across the European Union; aggregate financial loss EUR 20 million.
  • Modus operandi: Phishing harvested personal data; victim banking details extracted; payments redirected into network-controlled accounts; false Romanian identity documents used to open laundering bank accounts; funds moved through myriad accounts and platforms; converted into cryptocurrency and traded between wallets.
  • Activity timeline: Criminal network active since at least 2013; detailed Moldovan investigation initiated November 2022; JIT formed March 2025; action day 12 June 2025.
  • Cooperation channels: JIT (Eurojust-funded) + European Investigation Orders (EIOs) executed by both authorities; Moldova participates as a Eurojust Liaison Prosecutor country (one of twelve).

Source Summary

Eurojust press release dated 2025-06-12 reporting the same-day Romania-Moldova joint action day against an organised crime group laundering the proceeds of phishing fraud. The operation produced 44 simultaneous searches in both countries and was executed within a Joint Investigation Team structure that Eurojust set up and financed in March 2025. The criminal network — comprised of Romanian, Moldovan, Ukrainian, and Italian suspects — had operated from professionally organised call centres in Moldova since at least 2013, harvesting EU-wide victim data through phishing and laundering at least EUR 20 million across 30 identified victims.

IC Pattern

This release establishes a Romania-Moldova phishing-fraud cooperation pattern previously absent from the wiki. Notable structural features:

  • Eurojust-coordinated JIT with a non-EU third country (Moldova): Moldova is one of twelve countries holding a Liaison Prosecutor seat at Eurojust. The JIT vehicle here is the formal cooperation channel between an EU Member State (Romania) and a non-EU Liaison Prosecutor country (Moldova).
  • European Investigation Orders (EIOs) extended to Moldova as a non-EU JIT participant — a procedural pattern of EIOs paired with a JIT in which Moldovan authorities act as both requesting and requested authorities.
  • Multi-nationality OCG operating from Moldova (Romanian, Moldovan, Ukrainian, Italian) — call-centre staffing pattern that mirrors the Bangkok and Manila scam-centre cases recorded elsewhere in the wiki, but in a European phishing-and-laundering rather than investment-scam context.
  • Cryptocurrency-as-laundering-layer + false-ID Romanian bank accounts — a hybrid laundering chain rather than a single-channel pattern.

This case complements: - AFP-RTP Bangkok scam-centre takedown (2025-06-16) — multi-nationality OCG operating from a third country, AU-Anglosphere comparator. - India CBI Noida tech-support scam takedown (2025-07-07) — call-centre pattern operated against foreign victims. - Existing GoZNyM, Carbanak, and xDedic Moldova-anchored cases recorded in the wiki — establishes that Moldova-anchored cybercrime is a recurring rather than a one-off node, with Eurojust-coordinated JITs increasingly the formal cooperation channel.
