Source Summary

USAO-EDNY regional press release (dated 2025-12-19, the day after the parallel DOJ OPA national release dated 2025-12-18) announcing the guilty plea of Ukrainian national Artem Aleksandrovych Stryzhak, 35, of Barcelona, Spain, to conspiracy to commit fraud and related computer activity in connection with his role as a Nefilim ransomware affiliate. The regional EDNY release is delivered jointly by three named US officials — Joseph Nocella, Jr. (United States Attorney for the Eastern District of New York), Matthew R. Galeotti (Acting Assistant Attorney General, DOJ Criminal Division), and Christopher J.S. Johnson (Special Agent in Charge, FBI Springfield Illinois Field Office) — and explicitly thanks the Justice Department's Office of International Affairs and Computer Crime and Intellectual Property Section, as well as Spanish law enforcement authorities, for their assistance in the capture of Stryzhak, providing the L24-required verbatim acknowledgement of the foreign LE cooperation jurisdiction (Spain). Stryzhak was arrested in Spain in June 2024 and extradited to the United States on 2025-04-30, then pleaded guilty in the Brooklyn federal court on 2025-12-19 with sentencing scheduled for 2026-05-06 and a maximum statutory penalty of 10 years. The release also names the at-large administrator co-conspirator Volodymyr Tymoshchuk and the State Department's $11 million Rewards For Justice TOC bounty offered for his arrest/conviction. EDNY docket number 23-CR-324 (PKC) is stated verbatim.
