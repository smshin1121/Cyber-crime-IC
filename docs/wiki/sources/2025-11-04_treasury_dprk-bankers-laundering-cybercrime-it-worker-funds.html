주요 발견사항

  • OFAC designated 8 individuals and 2 entities for laundering DPRK cybercrime and IT worker proceeds (total 10 designations).
  • Designations issued under E.O. 13694 (as amended by E.O. 13757, 14144, 14306), E.O. 13810, E.O. 13551, and E.O. 13722.
  • Two North Korean bankers (Jang Kuk Chol and Ho Jong Son) managed $5.3 million in cryptocurrency on behalf of First Credit Bank, with funds linked to a DPRK ransomware actor that targeted U.S. victims.
  • Korea Mangyongdae Computer Technology Company (KMCTC) and its president U Yong Su designated for operating IT worker delegations in Shenyang and Dandong, China.
  • Ryujong Credit Bank designated for facilitating sanctions avoidance and money laundering between China and North Korea.
  • Five China- or Russia-based DPRK financial representatives designated: Ho Yong Chol (Korea Daesong Bank, $2.5M USD/CNY plus $85M for another DPRK group), Han Hong Gil (KCB/Ryugyong, $630K), Jong Sung Hyok (FTB Vladivostok), Choe Chun Pom (DPRK Central Bank, $200K), Ri Jin Hyok (FTB front company, $350K).
  • OFAC updated the SDN List entry for First Credit Bank to highlight cryptocurrency addresses.
  • Cumulative metric: North Korea-affiliated cybercriminals stole over $3 billion (primarily cryptocurrency) over the past three years, per Treasury.
  • DPRK IT workers earn hundreds of millions of dollars per year via false-identity employment on freelance platforms; some recruit non-DPRK freelancers as profit-splitting partners.
  • Sanctions network described as comprised of banking representatives, financial institutions, and shell companies in North Korea, China, and Russia.
  • Action grounded in the October 22 (2025) Multilateral Sanctions Monitoring Team (MSMT) report on DPRK cyber/IT worker UN sanctions evasion.
  • All blocked persons' property and interests in property in U.S. jurisdiction are frozen; 50% rule applies to derivative entities.

Source Summary

This OFAC press release (SB-0302) announces the designation of eight individuals and two entities tied to laundering proceeds from DPRK cybercrime (including a ransomware actor targeting U.S. victims) and DPRK IT worker fraud schemes. The release is highly likely the most significant Treasury action against the DPRK financial-enabler network since the 2025 MSMT report, given its explicit linkage of cryptocurrency-laundering pathways through First Credit Bank, Ryujong Credit Bank, and front-company representatives operating from China and Russia.

The action targets two distinct but interconnected revenue streams. First, ransomware and cryptocurrency-heist proceeds — Jang Kuk Chol and Ho Jong Son are designated as bankers managing $5.3 million in cryptocurrency for First Credit Bank, with at least some funds traced to a DPRK ransomware actor that previously targeted U.S. victims. Second, DPRK IT worker revenue — KMCTC (operating delegations from Shenyang and Dandong, China) and its president U Yong Su are designated for orchestrating IT worker dispatch and using Chinese-national banking proxies. The sanctions evasion side targets Ryujong Credit Bank plus five China- or Russia-based representatives of already-designated DPRK financial institutions (Korea Daesong Bank, KCB, Ryugyong Commercial Bank, FTB, DPRK Central Bank), with documented transaction volumes ranging from $200,000 to $85 million per individual.

The legal authorities cited are confirmed: cyber-enabled E.O. 13694 (as amended by 13757, 14144, 14306), DPRK-specific E.O. 13810, E.O. 13551 (DPRK successor authority), and E.O. 13722. Treasury frames the action as enforcement support for UN Security Council resolutions and the Multilateral Sanctions Monitoring Team (MSMT) — likely indicating ongoing multilateral information-sharing among MSMT participating states even though the unilateral designations themselves are U.S. action.

Per Treasury's own cumulative metric, North Korea-affiliated cybercriminals have stolen over $3 billion (primarily cryptocurrency) in the past three years; DPRK IT workers earn hundreds of millions of dollars per year. The release does not enumerate specific cooperating partner agencies for the designation itself but explicitly references MSMT collective resilience.
