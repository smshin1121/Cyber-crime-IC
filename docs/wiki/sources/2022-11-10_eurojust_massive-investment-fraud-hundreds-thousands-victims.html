주요 발견사항

  • Action days 8-9 November 2022 across Albania, Bulgaria, Georgia, North Macedonia, and Ukraine.
  • 10 participating countries: Bulgaria, Finland, Germany, Latvia, Spain, Sweden, Albania, Georgia, North Macedonia, Ukraine.
  • JIT set up by Sweden, also involving Albania, Finland, Georgia, Germany, Latvia, Spain, Ukraine (8 JIT countries).
  • JIT operational since (likely) 2018 — investigations into the cyber scam began in 2018; 7 countries opened judicial cases against the OCG by November 2022.
  • 13 Eurojust coordination meetings; 7 Europol operational meetings; Eurojust coordination centre established for the action days.
  • 15 call centres searched (6 in Albania, 5 in Georgia, 3 in Ukraine, 1 in North Macedonia); 27 other locations + 5 vehicles searched; 5 suspects arrested (4 in Albania, 1 in Georgia).
  • ~50 hearings of suspects and witnesses conducted.
  • 500+ electronic devices seized; EUR 340,000+ in cash; mobile phones, bank accounts, cryptocurrency wallets, properties, ID documents, bank cards, and hundreds of other documents.
  • Underlying fraud: hundreds of thousands of victims worldwide; cryptocurrency-investment fraud since at least 2016; estimated EUR 50 million per quarter in damages; dozens of call centres in several countries; hundreds of online platforms used.
  • International Arrest Warrants, European Investigation Orders, and Letters of Request to third countries facilitated by Eurojust.

Source Summary

Eurojust press release dated 2022-11-10 documenting an unprecedentedly large 10-country EU-coordinated cryptocurrency-investment-fraud takedown executed over 8-9 November 2022, originating from Spanish, German, and Finnish investigative requests and operationalised through a Sweden-led JIT involving Albania, Finland, Georgia, Germany, Latvia, Spain, and Ukraine. The release establishes a multi-year (since 2018) cross-jurisdiction investigative architecture against an OCG operating dozens of call centres in several countries and hundreds of online platforms.

IC Pattern

This release establishes the largest single-action EU JIT-anchored cryptocurrency-investment-fraud takedown in the wiki's pre-2025 corpus, with notable structural features:

  • Sweden-led JIT (a structurally similar model to the 2025-09 €100M Spain-Lithuania JIT crypto-fraud takedown and the 2025-11 €600M France-Belgium JIT extended to non-JIT jurisdictions).
  • 8-country JIT scope is the wiki's largest enumerated JIT membership for a single cryptocurrency-investment-fraud OCG.
  • Action-day-and-investigative cooperation across 10 countries, including non-EU partners (Georgia, North Macedonia, Ukraine, Albania).
  • Bavarian Central Office for the Prosecution of Cybercrime (ZCB Bamberg) is named for the first time in the wiki — a structural complement to the Frankfurt am Main ZIT documented in iteration 32.
  • EUR 50 million/quarter damage scale is the largest damages-per-quarter rate enumerated in the wiki's investment-fraud corpus.

Wiki Cross-Corpus Relevance

This case complements: - Eurojust €100M crypto investment fraud takedown (Spain-Lithuania JIT, 2025-09) - Eurojust €600M crypto money-laundering takedown (FR-BE-CY-DE-ES, 2025-11) - Albania-Austria call-centre investment fraud (2026-04)

Together these form a 2022-2026 EU JIT-anchored crypto-investment-fraud enforcement model with Albania appearing repeatedly as either a JIT-state or action-day-state for call-centre infrastructure dismantle.
