Source Summary

Europol newsroom announcement (2026-04-13) describing the latest action week under the multi-year Operation PowerOFF umbrella against DDoS-for-hire (booter) infrastructure. The release names 21 participating jurisdictions: Australia, Austria, Belgium, Brazil, Bulgaria, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, Germany, Japan, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Sweden, Thailand, the United Kingdom, and the United States. Tier-1 / tier-2 corroborating reporting on the same announcement records 53 domains taken down, 4 arrests, 25 search warrants executed, 75 000+ warning emails sent to identified DDoS-for-hire platform users, and 3 million+ criminal user accounts in seized databases. The release describes operational sprints leading up to the action week and a methodology emphasising disruption of booter infrastructure plus large-scale user-warning communications.

Relevance to IC

This is a tier-1 source for the 2026-04 phase of Operation PowerOFF, the long-running Europol-coordinated DDoS-for-hire umbrella since 2018. It is the first tier-1 source to record an action under the umbrella for 2026, materially expanding the SNA edge data for the participating-countries roster (21 jurisdictions) and reinforcing the umbrella's user-warning + booter-infrastructure-takedown methodology. Korean involvement is not named.
