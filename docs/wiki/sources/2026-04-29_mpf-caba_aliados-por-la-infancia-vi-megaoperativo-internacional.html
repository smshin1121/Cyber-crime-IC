주요 발견사항

  • MPF CABA's Cuerpo de Investigaciones Judiciales (CIJ) coordinated 'Aliados por la Infancia VI', a sixth-iteration multi-jurisdiction CSAM operation that ran 270 raids — 68 in Argentina across 18 provincial jurisdictions and 202 abroad — and produced 84 detentions.
  • Verified raid distribution covers 13 jurisdictions: Argentina (68), Brazil (159), Panama (7), Dominican Republic (7), Guatemala (5), Uruguay (5), Paraguay (5), Costa Rica (4), Spain (4), Peru (3), Puerto Rico (1), Honduras (1), France (1).
  • Confirmed arrests breakdown: 26 in Argentina, 44 in Brazil, 7 in Panama, 3 in Uruguay, 3 in Peru, 1 in Puerto Rico — totalling 84.
  • IC channel is documented as a tripartite arrangement among the U.S. Embassy in Argentina, U.S. Department of Homeland Security, and MPF CABA / CIJ; no specific MLAT instrument is cited.
  • Detection relied on the ICACCOPS (Internet Crimes Against Children Child Online Protection System) platform via an Argentine agreement with the National Criminal Justice Training Center (Fox Valley Technical College, Wisconsin), and on CPS (Child Protection System) developed by Child Rescue Coalition; NCMEC CyberTipline reports were the inbound tip stream.
  • Argentine line-of-effort agencies named for CABA execution: División Investigaciones de Delitos contra la Niñez y Adolescencia de la Policía de la Ciudad; División Delitos Cibernéticos contra la Niñez y Adolescencia de la Policía Federal Argentina; Escuadrón de Investigaciones de Delitos Complejos de Gendarmería Nacional; División Investigaciones de Ciberdelitos de Prefectura Naval Argentina.
  • Headline narrative cites '15 países' beyond Argentina; the raid-distribution breakdown enumerates 12 other jurisdictions — a flagged headline-vs-detail discrepancy.

Source Profile

This is the first-party institutional press release issued by the Ministerio Público Fiscal de la Ciudad Autónoma de Buenos Aires (MPF CABA) through its Cuerpo de Investigaciones Judiciales (CIJ) announcing the results of the sixth iteration of Operación Aliados por la Infancia, a multi-jurisdiction child-protection operation coordinated from Buenos Aires. The release was issued at the close of an in-person results conference attended by the MPF CABA Fiscal General, the U.S. and French ambassadors to Argentina, NCMEC (John Shehan), ICMEC (Laura Suárez Alonso), and senior Argentine federal and city law-enforcement officials.

This is the wiki's first ingest from the mpfciudad.gob.ar publisher. MPF CABA is the cybercrime-prosecution authority of the City of Buenos Aires and, per its own characterisation, the institutional coordinator of the Aliados por la Infancia operation series (now in iteration VI).
