주요 발견사항

  • Europol announced on 25 June 2015 the results of a Joint Investigation Team (JIT) action against a Zeus/SpyEye banking-Trojan network, with the coordinated action executed 18-19 June 2015 in Ukraine
  • {'"On the action days': '5 suspects arrested in Ukraine, 8 house searches conducted across 4 Ukrainian cities, and computer equipment and devices seized for forensic analysis"'}
  • {'"The JIT comprised 6 core member states': 'Austria, Belgium, Finland, Netherlands, Norway, and United Kingdom — an unusually small and tight configuration for a cybercrime JIT — with Europol and Eurojust coordination support"'}
  • Additional cooperation (non-JIT) came from Estonia, Latvia, Germany, Moldova, Poland, Ukraine, and the United States; Ukraine was the primary operational venue despite not being a JIT party
  • {'"Estimated damages': 'at least EUR 2 million from the investigated cluster; the group engaged in malware development, infection, credential harvesting, and money-mule laundering across the full cybercrime value chain"'}
  • 60 total arrests across the broader operation including 34 from a linked money-mule subsidiary network — connecting banking-Trojan developers to the downstream cash-out layer
  • This operation is cited as one of Europol's early (pre-EC3-maturity) successful uses of the Joint Investigation Team mechanism for cybercrime, establishing procedural precedent for later JITs

개요Summary

This Europol press release announces a Joint Investigation Team (JIT) takedown of a Zeus/SpyEye banking-Trojan crew on 18-19 June 2015 in Ukraine. The JIT included 6 EU/EEA members (Austria, Belgium, Finland, Netherlands, Norway, UK) with Eurojust judicial support; additional cooperation from 7 more countries including the US.

Action-day results: - 5 arrests in Ukraine - 8 house searches across 4 Ukrainian cities - Forensic seizures of computing equipment

Broader operation: 60 total arrests including 34 money mules from a linked subsidiary network; ~EUR 2 million in documented damages.

Why this source matters

The Zeus/SpyEye JIT is procedurally significant because it is an early, well-documented example of JIT as a functional cybercrime cooperation instrument. For zeus-spyeye-jit-takedown and the JIT mechanism page this release provides:

  • Named JIT member states (6 countries) — rare to find this precisely in press releases
  • Ukraine as operational venue without JIT membership — demonstrates the JIT-plus-bilateral-cooperation hybrid model
  • Money-mule layer integration — the 34 mule arrests from the subsidiary operation document how JITs extend beyond technical actors to cash-out networks
  • Eurojust role — judicial coordination evidence

This source should be cited whenever the Zeus, SpyEye, or JIT mechanism pages make claims about European cybercrime cooperation history.
