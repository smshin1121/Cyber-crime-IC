주요 발견사항

  • Korean National Police Agency (경찰청 국가수사본부) announced on 2025-12-29 the extradition to Korea of a 29-year-old Lithuanian national identified only as 'A', accused of distributing a memory-hacking clipper malware bundled inside cracked Microsoft Windows activation tooling 'KMSAuto' between 2020-04-08 and 2023-01-06.
  • Three-state cooperation chain: Lithuania (host of suspect, joint search 2024-12), Georgia (arrest April 2025 on INTERPOL Red Notice on entry from Lithuania), and South Korea (requesting state, extradition completed late 2025).
  • Mechanism stack named in the release: INTERPOL Red Notice (적색 수배), criminal MLA via Korean Ministry of Justice and Supreme Prosecutors' Office (형사사법공조), joint search-and-seizure with Lithuanian authorities (압수수색영장 집행 — 22 items including phone and laptop), and a formal extradition request from Korea to Georgia.
  • Scale: 2.8 million KMSAuto distributions worldwide; victims in 234 countries (ISO basis); 3,100 distinct cryptocurrency addresses drained over 8,400 transactions for ~₩1.7 billion (~USD 1.18M) in 14 different crypto assets; 8 confirmed Korean victims totalling ~₩16M.
  • Investigation began 2020-08 from a single bitcoin-misdirection victim report; total elapsed time from case opening to extradition was 5 years 4 months.
  • Korean spokesperson: 박우현 경찰청 사이버수사심의관 (Park Woo-hyun, KNPA Cyber Investigation Senior Officer); operating unit: 사이버테러대응과 (Cyber Terror Response Division).
  • KNPA explicitly frames the operation as a model for transnational cooperation in cases where a foreign national overseas victimises Koreans through cybercrime ('초국가적 협업으로 끝까지 추적').
