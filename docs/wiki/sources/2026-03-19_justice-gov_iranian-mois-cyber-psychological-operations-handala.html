주요 발견사항

  • Operations directed by Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS); attributed via shared leak sites, Iranian IP ranges, and common 'playbook' (destructive cyber-attacks + 'faketivist' psychological operations using data stolen via hacking).
  • Triggering context: U.S.-Iran conflict began on 28 February 2026; Handala Hack activity escalated immediately after.
  • Documented Handala Hack activity (per court documents): March 11 2026 destructive malware attack against US-based multinational medical technologies firm; March 9 2026 leak of PII of ~190 IDF/Israeli government personnel; March 6 2026 leak of 851 GB stolen data from Sanzer Hasidic Jewish community; March 1 2026 death threats via Handala_Team@outlook.com offering USD 250,000 bounty for beheading two Iranian-dissident/journalist targets, with explicit invocation of Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG) 'partners' in America and Canada.
  • Justicehomeland.org / Karmabelow80.org: shell hacktivist entity used by MOIS for July/September 2022 claims of responsibility for stealing sensitive documents from Albanian government organizations (motivated by Albanian support for Mujahedeen e-Khalq).
  • FBI Baltimore Field Office investigating in coordination with FBI Cyber Division; USAO-D Maryland + DOJ National Security Division's National Security Cyber Section prosecuting.
  • State Department Rewards for Justice program offering up to USD 10M reward for information on persons acting at foreign government direction conducting malicious cyber activities against US critical infrastructure (CFAA).

Source Summary

DOJ-OPA Press Release 26-251 dated 19 March 2026 announcing court-authorized seizure of 4 MOIS-affiliated domains (Justicehomeland.org, Handala-Hack.to, Karmabelow80.org, Handala-Redwanted.to) used by Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS) for cyber-enabled psychological operations. The Handala Hack persona was used to claim credit for a March 2026 destructive malware attack against a US-based multinational medical technologies firm, leak PII of ~190 IDF/Israeli government personnel, leak 851 GB of data from the Sanzer Hasidic Jewish community, and send death threats with USD 250,000 bounties invoking Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG) "partners" in America and Canada.

The release establishes:

  1. State-sponsored cyber-enabled transnational repression as a discrete IC-relevant pattern: Iranian MOIS used domain infrastructure for psychological operations against US persons, Israeli persons, and Iranian dissidents.
  2. Cyber-physical-violence convergence: hack-and-leak operations were paired with CJNG cartel-as-proxy death threats, demonstrating how state-sponsored cyber operations can be operationalised into kinetic-violence threats via criminal organisations.
  3. DOJ National Security Cyber Section + FBI Baltimore Field Office cooperation track for state-sponsored cyber operations targeting US critical infrastructure (medical technologies firm = critical infrastructure under DHS framework).
  4. Albania-Iran nexus documented from the 2022 Justicehomeland.org claims (MOIS-attributed thefts from Albanian government in retaliation for Albanian support of MEK Iranian dissident group).

The cited release is heavy on US national-security framing rather than multi-country LE cooperation; the foreign cooperation surface is implicit (Albanian government as 2022 victim; Israeli persons as 2026 victims).

Reliability and Credibility

  • Reliability: high — Tier-1 DOJ press release with named AG, FBI Director, AAG-NSD, US Attorney, and FBI SAC; affidavit publicly available.
  • Credibility: confirmed — Domain attribution + cyber attack details + threat-email content come directly from court documents underlying the seizure warrant.
  • Sensitivity: public — Press release publicly distributed via DOJ-OPA. Affidavit publicly linked.
