개요Summary

Tier-1 primary press release from Spain's Guardia Civil (Portal Web de la Guardia Civil), dated 2025-11-06, announcing the arrest of Spanish national A.R., publicly known as "CryptoSpain" on social media, as the alleged leader and ultimate person responsible for an international criminal organisation operating the Madeira Invest Club (MIC), charged with large-scale fraud (estafa) and money laundering. The Guardia Civil identifies the action as "Operación PONEI."

According to the release, MIC began operating in early 2023, presenting itself as a "private investment club" offering supposed opportunities in real estate, luxury vehicles, yachts, whisky, gold, and cryptocurrencies, with fixed-yield returns and guaranteed buy-back. Investments were formalised as digital-art purchase contracts that the club promised to repurchase within a fixed term and at preset profit. Guardia Civil investigators determined that no real economic activity backed the scheme — it was a classical pyramid/ponzi structure paying earlier investors with later investors' money.

The primary release puts the global scale at more than 3,000 affected investors and more than EUR 260 million defrauded.

L24 evidence — explicit foreign-LE counterpart naming

The Guardia Civil release explicitly identifies foreign LE partners that participated in the investigation, satisfying the L24 strict requirement for ≥2-country IC cooperation:

"A lo largo de la investigación se ha contado con la colaboración de EUROPOL y de cuerpos policiales de otros países, como el Homeland Security SI de EEUU la Singapore Police Force de Singapur, la Royal Malaysia Police de Malasia y la Royal Thai Police de Tailandia."

Foreign-LE counterpart jurisdictions explicitly named: - United States — Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) - Singapore — Singapore Police Force (SPF) - Malaysia — Royal Malaysia Police (RMP) - Thailand — Royal Thai Police (RTP) - Plus EUROPOL coordination (intergovernmental)

Note: The corporate-and-banking layer of the criminal organisation is distributed across additional jurisdictions (Portugal, UK, Albania, Dominican Republic, Belgium, Hong Kong); these are mentioned in the press release as money-flow / origin-destination jurisdictions, not as cooperating LE counterpart agencies, and per L24 are recorded only in body prose, not as participating_countries.
