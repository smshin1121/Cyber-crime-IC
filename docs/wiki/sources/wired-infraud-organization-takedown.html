주요 발견사항

  • DOJ unsealed an indictment on 7 February 2018 in the District of Nevada charging 36 defendants tied to the Infraud Organization, a Dark Web carding/identity-theft forum founded in October 2010 by Ukrainian Svyatoslav Bondarenko under the slogan 'In Fraud We Trust'
  • 13 of the 36 indicted were arrested in coordinated operations across at least 7 countries on 7-8 February 2018, including Thailand (Sergey Medvedev, the alleged co-founder, arrested in Bangkok), Australia, France, Italy, Kosovo, Serbia, the UK and the US
  • Estimated US$530 million in actual losses; intended losses approached US$2.2 billion. Approximately 4.3 million credit/debit cards and bank accounts targeted across 50 countries; ~10,901 active forum members at peak
  • International cooperation involved law enforcement agencies from 16 countries; the takedown banner credited 'United States, European, Australian and Asian law enforcement agencies'. Wired's article emphasized that the operation took years of multinational coordination and undercover infiltration of the forum
  • Wired notes Infraud was 'one of the longest-running and most prolific carding forums on the dark web', operating openly for nearly 8 years before being dismantled — illustrating the sustained-investigation requirement for cybercrime forum takedowns
  • {'"Subsequent prosecutions': 'Sergey Medvedev pleaded guilty in 2020 and received a 10-year sentence; Valerian Chiochiu (Moldovan, FastPOS malware author) sentenced to 10 years; Marko Leopard (Macedonia) sentenced. Andrey Sergeevich Novak (Russian) was arrested by Russia\'s FSB in January 2022 — a rare case of Russian cooperation in a US-led cybercrime case"'}

개요Summary

Wired's coverage of the Infraud Organization takedown describes the DOJ's 7 February 2018 indictment of 36 defendants tied to one of the dark web's largest carding forums. Wired emphasizes:

  1. Forum scale and longevity — Infraud had operated for ~8 years (founded October 2010), grown to ~10,900 active members, and was almost certainly the largest English-language carding forum at the time of takedown
  2. The "In Fraud We Trust" branding and its origin with Ukrainian founder Svyatoslav Bondarenko
  3. Multi-country arrests — 13 of the 36 indicted were arrested across 7+ countries (Thailand, Australia, UK, France, Italy, Kosovo, Serbia) within a 24-hour window coordinated by US authorities
  4. Sergey Medvedev as alleged co-founder, arrested in Bangkok with substantial cryptocurrency holdings
  5. Cooperative scale — 16 country law enforcement agencies participated in the takedown coordination
  6. Loss estimates — actual losses ~$530M, intended losses ~$2.2B, ~4.3M card numbers compromised
