주요 발견사항

  • New Zealand Police's Financial Crime Group arrested a Wellington-based individual in Auckland on or before 16 May 2025, as part of a U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) joint investigation into a multi-victim cryptocurrency-theft and money-laundering RICO conspiracy.
  • The underlying conspiracy stole approximately US$265 million (NZD$450 million) in cryptocurrency from seven victims between March and August 2024, with proceeds laundered across multiple cryptocurrency platforms.
  • Coordinated search warrants were executed in Auckland, Wellington and California over a three-day period; 13 individuals were arrested in total across the New Zealand and U.S. legs.
  • The Wellington-based suspect was indicted by the U.S. Department of Justice under federal law on RICO conspiracy, conspiracy to commit wire fraud, and conspiracy to commit money laundering. He received interim name suppression at Auckland District Court and was scheduled to reappear on 3 July 2025.
  • The press release names only two IC partner jurisdictions explicitly — New Zealand and the United States — and three agencies: NZ Police Financial Crime Group, the FBI, and the U.S. Department of Justice.

Source Summary

On 16 May 2025, New Zealand Police's Financial Crime Group announced the arrest in Auckland of a Wellington-based individual as part of an FBI-led investigation into a transnational cryptocurrency-theft and money-laundering ring. The release frames the case as a joint NZ Police / FBI investigation supporting a United States Department of Justice indictment under RICO, wire-fraud conspiracy, and money-laundering conspiracy statutes.

The cited theft cohort numbered seven victims and the headline aggregate theft was US$265 million (NZD$450 million) in cryptocurrency, allegedly stolen between March and August 2024 and laundered across multiple platforms. Coordinated search-warrant execution spanned Auckland, Wellington and California over a three-day period and yielded 13 arrests in total across the two-country operation.

The suspect was granted interim name suppression at Auckland District Court and was scheduled to reappear on 3 July 2025. NZ Police framed the case publicly as a worked example of why cross-border criminal activity demands "strong international law-enforcement partnerships" with the FBI.
