주요 발견사항

  • First joint operation of the Cross-border Anti-Scam Collaboration Platform 'FRONTIER+' ran 2025-04-28 to 2025-05-28 across seven jurisdictions (Hong Kong SAR, Macao SAR, Malaysia, the Maldives, Singapore, South Korea, Thailand).
  • 1,858 arrests (ages 14–81); 9,268 scam cases addressed; aggregated loss US$225M; 32,607 bank accounts frozen; ~US$20M fraudulent funds intercepted.
  • Per-jurisdiction arrest breakdown (Annex): HKSAR 337, Macao SAR 9, Malaysia 708, Maldives 0, Singapore 106, South Korea 116, Thailand 582.
  • FRONTIER+ platform was jointly established in October 2024; HK release lists 10 member jurisdictions (Australia, Canada, HKSAR, Indonesia, Macao SAR, Malaysia, Maldives, Singapore, South Korea, Thailand). Singapore CAD 2024 report lists 11 (adds Brunei).
  • Predominant scam typologies: government-official impersonation, impersonating customer service, investment, rental, online romance, friend impersonation, employment, online shopping.
  • Cooperation modality: real-time intelligence exchange via FRONTIER+ platform plus simultaneous coordinated enforcement, not formal MLAT.

개요Summary

Official Hong Kong SAR Government Information Services press release (P2025060300475) of 2025-06-03 reporting the results of the first joint operation conducted under the Cross-border Anti-Scam Collaboration Platform "FRONTIER+", executed 2025-04-28 to 2025-05-28. The release names six partner jurisdictions (Macao SAR, Malaysia, the Maldives, Singapore, South Korea, Thailand) plus Hong Kong, and provides aggregate enforcement totals along with a per-jurisdiction Annex.

This is a tier-1 primary source: official Hong Kong government press dissemination service issuing on behalf of the Hong Kong Police Force. The accompanying PDF Annex (also on info.gov.hk infrastructure) provides per-jurisdiction breakdowns of subjects interviewed, persons arrested, scam cases detected, financial loss, accounts frozen, and amounts recovered.

Reliability and Credibility

  • Reliability: high. Issued through HKSAR's official government information service (info.gov.hk) and identifies the issuing body (Hong Kong Police Force, Commercial Crime Bureau).
  • Credibility: confirmed. Per-jurisdiction Annex figures cross-checked against the Singapore Police Force's CAD Annual Report 2024 (already in raw corpus, file 2025-12-01_police-gov-sg_cad-report-2024-operation-first-light.md), which independently confirms Singapore's figure of 106 arrests and S$7.7M (≈US$5.98M) seized in this operation, and also describes FRONTIER+ formalisation on 22 October 2024.
  • Sensitivity: public.
