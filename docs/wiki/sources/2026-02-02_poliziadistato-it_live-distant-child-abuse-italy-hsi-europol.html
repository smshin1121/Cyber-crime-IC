주요 발견사항

  • Italian Polizia Postale's Centro Nazionale per il Contrasto alla Pedopornografia Online (C.N.C.P.O.), coordinated by the Procura di Milano, dismantled a transnational 'live distant child abuse' network: 2 arrests (provinces of Trento and Reggio Calabria) and 4 persons reported at liberty (denunciate, ages 47–57, provinces of Roma, Latina, Brescia and Milano). Announced 2026-02-02 (page modified 2026-02-03).
  • International cooperation explicitly named in the primary source: U.S. Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) and Europol — described as 'determinante' (decisive). No specific MLAT or treaty instrument is cited in the press release.
  • Modus operandi: 'buyers' commissioned real-time sexual abuse of minors from 'traffickers/vendors' physically present with the victims; payments labelled 'tips' were routed through 'i principali money business services' to evade financial controls.
  • Investigative method: undercover online infiltration by C.N.C.P.O. and the Centro operativo per la sicurezza cibernetica di Milano, supported by Roma, Trento and Reggio Calabria units; advanced de-anonymisation techniques used to penetrate closed abuser communities.
  • Initial contact occurred on 'piattaforme asiatiche dedicate agli adulti' (Asian adult-content platforms); private rooms were then used to broker access to children. Investigators are examining whether suspects also travelled to Asian countries to commit physical abuse in person.
  • Italian original-language legal terms preserved: 'pedopornografia online' (online CSAM), 'denuncia in stato di libertà' (charge while suspect remains at liberty), 'perquisizione' (search), 'flussi finanziari' (financial flows), 'live distant child abuse' (English-language industry term used in original Italian text).

Source Profile

Official press release issued by the Italian State Police (Polizia di Stato) Press Office on poliziadistato.it, the central institutional domain. The release announces the conclusion of an undercover transnational investigation into "live distant child abuse" run by the Centro Nazionale per il Contrasto alla Pedopornografia Online (C.N.C.P.O.) of Polizia Postale e per la sicurezza cibernetica, coordinated by the Procura di Milano (Public Prosecutor's Office, Milan), and conducted in international cooperation with the U.S. Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) and with Europol.

The release is a tier-1 primary source: it is published on the official .poliziadistato.it Italian Polizia di Stato domain, signed institutionally by the Press Office, and provides operational figures (arrests, denunciations, geographic distribution) directly from the lead investigative agency. The Italian-language text (preserved verbatim in the corresponding raw file) is authoritative; the working English summary in this entry is for indexing only.

The investigation has been described in Italian secondary press as "Operazione Light Bridge" but that operation name does not appear in the official Polizia di Stato press release; it should therefore be treated as an unverified secondary-press alias.

📝 Operation-name caveat "Operazione Light Bridge" is widely cited in Italian secondary press (LSD Magazine, La Milano, Sky TG24, Matrice Digitale) for this same investigation. The official Polizia di Stato press release does not use that name; this wiki therefore treats "Light Bridge" as an unverified alias and uses descriptive titling pending confirmation from a Polizia di Stato or Procura di Milano source that explicitly uses the operation name.
