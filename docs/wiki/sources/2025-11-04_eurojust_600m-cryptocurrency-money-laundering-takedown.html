Source Summary

Eurojust newsroom press release (dated 2025-11-04) describing a synchronised 5-country operation on 27 and 29 October 2025 that arrested 9 suspects in Cyprus, Spain, and Germany for their involvement in a EUR 600 million cryptocurrency money-laundering network. The network operated dozens of fake cryptocurrency investment platforms recruiting victims through social media advertising, cold calling, fake news articles, and fake celebrity testimonials, then laundered the proceeds using blockchain technology. Eurojust set up a joint investigation team (JIT) between French and Belgian authorities and brought prosecutors and investigative judges from France, Belgium, Cyprus, Spain, and Germany together at Eurojust's premises in The Hague to plan the takedown. Action-day seizures: EUR 800,000 in bank accounts, EUR 415,000 in cryptocurrencies, EUR 300,000 in cash. The release names participating authorities for each of the 5 countries verbatim, including the Spanish Mossos d'Esquadra and Policia Nacional cybercrime units, the Cypriot MOKAS, and the German Cologne Criminal Police.
