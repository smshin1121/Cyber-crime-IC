주요 발견사항

  • Tier-1 own-domain (rcmp.ca, Canadian national LE) confirms an arrest in the Lower Mainland of British Columbia in March 2025 (publicly announced 2025-04-09 as release no. 2025-53) of a Surrey resident who allegedly operated a network of thousands of infected computers serving as a malware-loader botnet — explicitly framed by the RCMP as a national disposition under the umbrella of Operation Endgame, not a stand-alone Canadian case.
  • The release explicitly names six foreign LE partner jurisdictions whose law-enforcement agencies coordinated with the RCMP on this disposition: Denmark, France, Germany, the Netherlands, the Czech Republic, and the USA (verbatim quote attributed to Superintendent Adam MacIntosh, Officer in Charge of the Cyber and Financial Investigation Teams in the Pacific Region) — satisfying the strict ≥2-country IC cooperation requirement at the tier-1 primary-source level.
  • Canadian operational lead is RCMP Federal Policing — Pacific Region's Cybercrime Investigative Team — Vancouver (CIT-V), one of five national-level RCMP cybercrime teams (CIT-Edmonton, CIT-Ottawa, CIT-Montreal, CIT-Toronto, CIT-Vancouver); CIT-V is supported nationally by the RCMP National Cybercrime Coordination Centre (NC3) and internationally by Europol.
  • The release self-links to a same-day Europol newsroom note 'Operation Endgame follow-up leads to five detentions and interrogations as well as server takedowns' — the RCMP arrest is the Canadian limb of that broader 2025-04 Operation Endgame follow-up tranche, distinct from the May 2025 Phase 2 ransomware-kill-chain disruption tranche.
  • No suspect name, charges-laid status, victim count, malware-loader family name, or server/asset-seizure figures are disclosed in the release ('investigation is ongoing'); the release reports the arrest itself, not a sentencing or indictment milestone.
