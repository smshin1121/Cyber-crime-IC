개요Summary

Europol announces the conclusion of an eleven-country action week (16-20 October 2023) against the Ragnar Locker ransomware group, coordinated by Europol's European Cybercrime Centre (EC3) and Eurojust. The investigation was led by the French National Gendarmerie's Cybercrime Centre (C3N), supported by law enforcement and judicial authorities from Czechia, Germany, Italy, Japan, Latvia, the Netherlands, Spain, Sweden, Ukraine, and the United States.

The "key target," suspected of being a developer of the Ragnar group, was arrested in Paris on 16 October 2023 and brought before examining magistrates of the Paris Judicial Court; his home in Czechia was searched. Five additional suspects were interviewed in Spain and Latvia. Ransomware infrastructure was seized in the Netherlands, Germany, and Sweden, and the group's Tor-hosted "Wall of Shame" data leak website was taken down in Sweden.

This action follows a first-wave October 2021 arrest of two prominent Ragnar Locker operators in Ukraine, conducted by the French Gendarmerie, the US FBI, Europol, INTERPOL, and the Ukrainian National Police.
