주요 발견사항

  • Met Police seized 61,000 Bitcoin (valued at over £5.5 billion by Sept 2025) from Chinese national Zhimin Qian (a.k.a. Yadi Zhang) — believed to be the single largest cryptocurrency seizure in the world.
  • Qian and Seng Hok Ling pleaded guilty at Southwark Crown Court on 29-30 September 2025 to Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 offences (acquisition/possession/transfer of criminal property in cryptocurrency).

Source Summary: Two people convicted following world's largest crypto seizure (Met Police, 2025-09-29)

Overview

This is the official Metropolitan Police Service (UK) press release announcing the conviction of Zhimin Qian (47, Chinese national, also known as Yadi Zhang) and Seng Hok Ling (46, of Matlock, Derbyshire) at Southwark Crown Court on 29-30 September 2025 for cryptocurrency money laundering offences under the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002.

The case arises from a 7-year Met Economic Crime team investigation that began in 2018 on intelligence about the transfer of criminal assets. It centres on 61,000 Bitcoin seized from Qian — the single largest cryptocurrency seizure ever made by any police force globally, with an estimated value of more than £5.5 billion as of the date of publication. The underlying predicate offence is a 2014-2017 large-scale investment fraud that Qian orchestrated in China, defrauding over 128,000 victims; she converted the proceeds to Bitcoin, fled China using false documents, and attempted to launder the proceeds in the UK through property purchases beginning September 2018.

Why this matters for the IC wiki

This is a tier-1 primary source documenting explicit multi-country LE/prosecutor cooperation between the United Kingdom and the People's Republic of China — a rare and operationally significant pairing. The release quotes both Will Lyne (Head of Economic and Cybercrime Command, Met Police) and Detective Sergeant Isabella Grotto explicitly naming "Chinese law enforcement teams in Tianjin and Beijing with whom we have collaborated throughout." This satisfies the L24 strict-IC rule (≥2 distinct cooperating LE/prosecutor agencies named verbatim in the tier-1 primary source).

The case is also a major international precedent for cryptocurrency confiscation through a dual track of criminal forfeiture + civil recovery under the Proceeds of Crime Act 2002, and for evidence-sharing between UK and Chinese authorities on a transnational predicate fraud followed by laundering in a Western jurisdiction.

Cooperating agencies named in the source

United Kingdom - Metropolitan Police Service — Economic Crime team / Economic and Cybercrime Command (lead investigator) - National Crime Agency (named explicitly as a supporting partner) - Crown Prosecution Service (prosecution lead, quoted via Robin Weyell, Deputy Chief Crown Prosecutor)

People's Republic of China - Chinese law enforcement teams in Tianjin and Beijing (named as collaborating throughout the investigation)
