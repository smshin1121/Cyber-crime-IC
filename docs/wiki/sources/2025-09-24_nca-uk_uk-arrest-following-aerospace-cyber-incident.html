주요 발견사항

  • UK NCA arrested a man in his 40s in West Sussex on 23 September 2025 (evening) on suspicion of Computer Misuse Act offences linked to the 19 September 2025 cyber incident at Collins Aerospace, which disrupted Heathrow and other European airports.
  • Suspect was released on conditional bail; the NCA's National Cyber Crime Unit (NCCU), led by Deputy Director Paul Foster, leads the investigation, supported by the South East Regional Organised Crime Unit (ROCU).
  • NCA frames the case as part of a broader 'persistent global threat' and references partners 'here and overseas'; specific foreign agencies are not named in the release.

Key Facts Reported

  1. A man in his forties was arrested in West Sussex (UK) on the evening of 23 September 2025 by the National Crime Agency, supported by the South East ROCU.
  2. Arrest was made on suspicion of Computer Misuse Act offences (specific section not stated).
  3. The suspect was released on conditional bail.
  4. The arrest is connected to a cyber incident at Collins Aerospace reported on 19 September 2025, which disrupted flights at Heathrow and other European airports over the following weekend.
  5. The investigation is led by the NCA's National Cyber Crime Unit (NCCU), headed by Deputy Director Paul Foster.
  6. Foster characterises the investigation as "in its early stages" and notes work "alongside our partners here and overseas."
