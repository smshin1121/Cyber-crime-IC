Source Summary

Tier-1 U.S. Department of Justice press release dated 18 September 2025, jointly publicising the unsealing of a federal complaint in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey against Thalha Jubair, a 19-year-old United Kingdom national of London. Jubair is charged with seven counts — computer fraud conspiracy, two substantive counts of computer fraud, wire fraud conspiracy, two substantive counts of wire fraud, and money laundering conspiracy — for his alleged role in approximately 120 Scattered Spider network intrusions worldwide between May 2022 and September 2025, including at least 47 U.S.-based victim entities that paid more than USD 115 million in ransom. The release singles out two named federal-victim intrusions: a U.S.-based critical infrastructure company and the U.S. Courts (the federal judiciary), both occurring in October 2024 and January 2025. Jubair was arrested by U.K. authorities on 16 September 2025 in connection with a parallel, separate U.K. investigation into an attack on U.K. critical infrastructure (TfL).

Relevance to IC

Highly likely (80–95%) that the dual-track represents an explicit prosecutorial division of labor: UK takes the TfL conduct under the Computer Misuse Act 1990 with no US extradition request, while the US runs a parallel D.N.J. complaint covering the 47 U.S.-victim ransom-extortion conduct (USD 115M+) and the federal-judiciary intrusion. This is a useful comparative model alongside the us-v-kai-west-intelbroker-2025 track (US-as-venue + France-as-arrest-venue with extradition).

Treating the federal court system as a critical infrastructure–adjacent target reframes Scattered Spider's threat profile and intensifies international cooperation pressure.
  • Cooperation footprint of named foreign agencies: The release credits "significant assistance" from the UK NCA, City of London Police, West Midlands Police; Netherlands national police + Dutch Prosecutor's Office; Romanian DIICOT and Romanian National Police; Royal Canadian Mounted Police; and Australian Federal Police. This eight-agency / six-country cooperation footprint is consistent with a Scattered Spider transnational task-force model and is broader than the cooperation publicly identified in the parallel UK NCA release on the same date.
  • OIA + CCIPS coordination role: Office of International Affairs is named as having "provided significant assistance" — consistent with OIA's standing role coordinating multi-jurisdictional MLA traffic and extradition packages on cybercrime indictments.
