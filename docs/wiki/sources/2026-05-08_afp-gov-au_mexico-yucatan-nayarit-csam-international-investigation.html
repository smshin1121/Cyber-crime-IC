주요 발견사항

  • AFP, Queensland Police Service Task Force Argos, Mexico FGR-FEMDO, and Mexico SSPC issued a joint announcement on 8 May 2026 of a Mexico-Australia international child-protection investigation.
  • Australian intelligence (QPS Task Force Argos, Brisbane) was passed to AFP Liaison Officers in Mexico City and onward to Mexican authorities, who obtained domestic judicial warrants for two separate suspects.
  • Yucatán case (7 April 2026): 38-year-old man arrested by SSPC, FGR-FEMDO and Secretaría de Marina; ordered to stand trial for aggravated human trafficking; alleged grooming via football training over approximately a decade; electronic devices seized for victim identification.
  • Nayarit case (March 2026): 31-year-old man arrested by SSPC and FGR-FEMDO; alleged distribution of child sexual abuse material via instant messaging platforms and the dark web; one child brought to safety; mobile phones, computers, hard drives, USB devices, clothing and bedding seized.
  • Spanish-language Mexican press identifies the named suspects as Josué Dorantes Escamilla (38, Kanasín, Yucatán) and Carlos Fernando Aguilar González (31, Tuxpan, Nayarit); the AFP release withholds names.
  • Cooperation channel is described as a long-standing AFP-FGR/SSPC relationship; no specific MLAT instrument is cited in the joint release.
  • Tier-1 primary source: published on the official Australian Federal Police domain (afp.gov.au) as an institutionally signed joint media release with the Mexican prosecution and federal security ministries.

Source Profile

This is a joint media release issued on 8 May 2026 by the Australian Federal Police (afp.gov.au) together with the Queensland Police Service (QPS), the Mexican Fiscalía General de la República through the Fiscalía Especializada en Materia de Delincuencia Organizada (FEMDO), and the Secretaría de Seguridad y Protección Ciudadana (SSPC). The Mexican Secretaría de Marina is named as an operational participant in the Yucatán enforcement action.

The release announces two arrests — one in Nayarit (March 2026) and one in Yucatán (7 April 2026) — and describes the AFP–FGR/SSPC channel through which Australian intelligence developed by QPS Task Force Argos in Brisbane was relayed to Mexican prosecutors.

Reliability and Credibility

  • Reliability: high — official institutional release on the Australian Federal Police domain, with named partner agencies on the Mexican side.
  • Credibility: confirmed — first-party announcement by the agencies that conducted the investigation; corroborated by Mexican press conference coverage in the same news cycle.
  • Sensitivity: public — distributed for general media use; AFP withholds suspect names in line with Australian pre-trial reporting practice while Mexican outlets identify them following the FGR/SSPC press conference.
