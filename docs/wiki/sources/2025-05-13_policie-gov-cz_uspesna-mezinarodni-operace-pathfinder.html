주요 발견사항

  • Czech NCTEKK + Ukrainian SBU joint investigation team (JIT) supported by Eurojust dismantled a Zakarpattia-based fake-investment-platform call-centre OCG over 6 months of cooperation, with action days 2025-04-29 to 2025-05-02 directly involving a 4-officer NCTEKK on-site team.
  • 70 individuals detained including 4 main organizers; 4 organizers charged under Ukrainian Criminal Code § 190(5) (fraud on a particularly large scale), facing up to 12 years' imprisonment and asset forfeiture; remanded in custody.
  • Czech damage: at least 9 million CZK across at least 37 Czech victims; total EU-wide damage estimated in millions of EUR pending forensic analysis of seized data carriers.
  • Modus operandi: fake investment platforms with high graphical sophistication; victims deceived into installing remote-access applications; funds laundered through bank-account chains to cryptocurrency; in-house IT specialists at the call-centre simultaneously produced fake investment ads.
  • Call-centre infrastructure: 2-floor administrative building in Zakarpattia Ukraine, dedicated server room, physical security guards, electronic alarm system, dozens of CCTV cameras — illustrates the institutionalized scale of fake-investment-platform OCGs operating from Ukraine against EU victims.
  • Czech procedural lead: NCTEKK Cybercrime Department under supervision of the District State Prosecutor's Office in Hradec Králové (Okresní státní zastupitelství v Hradci Králové).
  • Ukrainian procedural lead: SBU under regional prosecutor's office (krajské prokuratury) in Zakarpattia.
  • Eurojust JIT framework formally established for the cooperation; Europol involvement not explicitly named in this Czech-language release (single named JIT-coordinating body: Eurojust).
  • Searches yielded hundreds of pieces of computing equipment, computers, data carriers, and telephones.

Source Summary

Czech NCTEKK (Národní centrála proti terorismu, extremismu a kybernetické kriminalitě) press release dated 2025-05-13, signed by NCTEKK director plk. JUDr. Břetislav Brejcha, announcing the successful conclusion of action days (2025-04-29 to 2025-05-02) of international operation "PATHFINDER" — a 6-month Eurojust JIT-framework cooperation between the Czech NCTEKK Cybercrime Department and the Security Service of Ukraine (SBU), under procedural supervision of the District State Prosecutor's Office in Hradec Králové (Czech side) and the regional prosecutor's office in Zakarpattia (Ukrainian side). The operation dismantled a Zakarpattia-based fake-investment-platform call-centre OCG with 70 detained, including 4 organizers charged under Ukrainian Criminal Code § 190(5).

IC Pattern

This release documents a bilateral CZ–UA Eurojust JIT with on-site Czech police participation in Ukrainian action days — a configuration that distinguishes itself from the broader multi-jurisdiction Eurojust JITs (e.g., the Latvia-led <a href="../../wiki/operations/operation-octopus-investment-fraud-czechia-ukraine-2025.html" class="wikilink">operation-octopus-investment-fraud-czechia-ukraine-2025</a>-style four-jurisdiction arrangements) by its two-state, single-victim-jurisdiction structure (Czech Republic as primary victim jurisdiction; Ukraine as host of the criminal infrastructure). Notable IC features:

  • NCTEKK as the Czech cybercrime authority — first wiki record of an NCTEKK-led action where Czech officers directly accompanied Ukrainian search-and-seizure operations in Zakarpattia.
  • SBU as Ukrainian counterpart — Ukraine's intelligence service (rather than the Cyber Police of Ukraine) led the Ukrainian side of the JIT, reflecting the cybercrime/national-security overlap framework increasingly used by Ukraine for transnational fraud OCGs operating from its territory.
  • Eurojust JIT as the sole named coordinating body — Europol/EC3 involvement is NOT named in this Czech release, distinguishing PATHFINDER from typical Europol-Eurojust dual-coordination patterns.
  • Zakarpattia as call-centre geography — adds a distinct geographic data point alongside Dnipro / Ivano-Frankivsk / Kyiv (recorded in <a href="../../wiki/operations/ukraine-fraudulent-call-centres-takedown-2025.html" class="wikilink">ukraine-fraudulent-call-centres-takedown-2025</a> and <a href="../../wiki/operations/dnipro-fraudulent-call-centre-takedown-2026.html" class="wikilink">dnipro-fraudulent-call-centre-takedown-2026</a>) for Ukraine-hosted EU-victim-targeting fake-investment OCGs.
