Source Summary

On 20 July 2017, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of California announced the shutdown of AlphaBay and disclosed that Alexandre Cazes, the alleged creator and administrator of the marketplace, had already been charged by indictment in Fresno federal court on 1 June 2017. DOJ stated that the indictment charged racketeering conspiracy, narcotics distribution conspiracy and substantive distribution counts, identity-theft conspiracy, access-device-fraud conspiracy, device-making-equipment trafficking, and money-laundering conspiracy. DOJ also stated that Thai authorities arrested Cazes on 5 July 2017 on behalf of the United States, and that a separate civil forfeiture complaint was filed on 19 July 2017 against assets tied to Cazes and his wife in Thailand, Cyprus, Liechtenstein, and Antigua and Barbuda. Millions of dollars in cryptocurrency and luxury real estate were targeted as forfeitable AlphaBay proceeds. The prosecution did not proceed to adjudication because Cazes died in Thai custody on 12 July 2017 before extradition. Even so, the case remains central to international-cooperation analysis because it tied together a U.S. indictment, a Thailand arrest, cross-border asset preservation, and operational support from European and Canadian partners.
