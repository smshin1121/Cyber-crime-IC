주요 발견사항

  • Politie (Netherlands) and FBI (United States) jointly took the VerifTools fake-ID marketplace offline on 27 August 2025 and seized its servers; the cooperation continued through follow-on user-side enforcement.
  • On 7-8 April 2026 Dutch police executed a nationwide first wave of enforcement against identified VerifTools users: 8 arrests of men aged 20-34 for identity fraud, forgery and cybercrime offences, plus 9 summonses (including 2 minors) and seizures of phones, laptops, weapons, cash and cryptocurrency.
  • Server data revealed 636,847 user accounts (Feb 2021-Aug 2025) and 915,655 generated fake-ID images (May 2023-Aug 2025), including 5,169 fake Dutch documents (passports, driving licences, residence permits) and 236,002 US documents purchased for USD 1,468,836.98 between Jul 2024 and Aug 2025.
  • VerifTools' last-year revenue is estimated at over EUR 3 million; criminals used the generated images to bypass Know-Your-Customer (KYC) verification at banks and online services, enabling phishing, bank-helpdesk fraud and other downstream offences.
  • The Dutch investigation is led by Team Cybercrime Rotterdam together with the AVIM (Vreemdelingenpolitie, Identificatie en Mensenhandel) unit, in coordination with the Openbaar Ministerie; further arrest waves are explicitly announced.

개요Summary

Official Dutch National Police (Politie) press release dated 9 April 2026 (last modified 22 April 2026) announcing the first wave of arrests in the user-side phase of the joint Netherlands-US VerifTools fake-ID marketplace investigation. The release confirms that the August 2025 takedown of VerifTools was conducted in cooperation with the US FBI and that the post-seizure data exploitation has produced concrete enforcement action against platform users on Dutch territory.

Tier-1 Primary Source Justification

  • Official Dutch national police communication on its institutional politie.nl newsroom domain.
  • Names the cooperating foreign authority (FBI) and the Dutch units involved (Team Cybercrime Rotterdam, AVIM, Openbaar Ministerie).
  • Provides quantitative results (arrests, account counts, document counts, monetary figures) from the police's own data analysis of the seized servers.
  • Both the original seizure (27 Aug 2025) and the user-side arrests (7-8 Apr 2026) are described as one continuous investigation, satisfying the international-cooperation scope (Netherlands + United States, ≥ 2 countries).
