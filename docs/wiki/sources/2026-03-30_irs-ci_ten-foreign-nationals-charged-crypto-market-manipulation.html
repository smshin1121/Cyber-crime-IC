주요 발견사항

  • 10 foreign-national executives and employees of four cryptocurrency 'market maker' firms (Gotbit, Vortex, Antier, Contrarian) indicted across three federal grand-jury indictments (Gotbit indictment 2025-03-25; Vortex 2025-08-28; Contrarian/Antier 2025-09-04).
  • Investigation: 'Operation Token Mirrors' — joint FBI + IRS-CI undercover operation in which the FBI created several cryptocurrency tokens to bait wash-trading market manipulation.
  • 3 defendants (Gleb Gora — Vortex CEO; Manu Singh — Contrarian CEO; Vasu Sharma — Contrarian BDA) arrested in Singapore on 2025-10-02 at the request of the United States and extradited to the US for initial appearance in Oakland on 2026-03-30.
  • 2 defendants pled guilty before the 2026-03-30 announcement: Antoine Tsao (Gotbit BDM, Taiwan) — pled guilty 2025-06-02; Nemanja Popov (Gotbit Account Manager, Serbia) — pled guilty and sentenced 2026-02-10.
  • More than USD 1 million in cryptocurrency seized to date.
  • International cooperation: FBI Law Enforcement Attaché Singapore + DOJ Office of International Affairs + Singapore Police Force + Singapore Attorney General's Chambers — for arrests and extraditions of Gora, Singh, and Sharma.
  • Statutes charged: 18 U.S.C. §§ 1343 (wire fraud) and 1349 (wire fraud conspiracy); max 20 years per count plus USD 250,000 fine.

Source Summary

IRS-CI press release dated 2026-03-30 announcing the simultaneous initial appearance in federal court at Oakland of three defendants extradited from Singapore — Gleb Gora (Vortex CEO), Manu Singh (Contrarian CEO), and Vasu Sharma (Contrarian BDA) — and consolidating prior indictments and pleas across three indictments (Gotbit, Vortex, Contrarian/Antier) into a unified "Operation Token Mirrors" public-announcement event. The operation is a joint FBI + IRS-CI undercover investigation in which the FBI itself created cryptocurrency tokens to bait wash-trading market manipulation by illicit market makers operating across Taiwan, Russia, Serbia, India, and other jurisdictions, with extradition cooperation by Singapore.

IC Pattern

This release establishes a previously-undocumented Singapore-US extradition cooperation pattern in the wiki for cryptocurrency-fraud cases — Singapore is recorded for the first time as an extradition source in a US-led cryptocurrency-fraud prosecution. The FBI Law Enforcement Attaché Singapore + DOJ OIA + Singapore Police Force + Singapore Attorney General's Chambers cooperation chain is the operational mechanism. The FBI undercover-token creation methodology (FBI agents creating cryptocurrency tokens to bait wash trading) is also a novel mechanism class for the corpus.
