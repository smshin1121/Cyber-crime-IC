주요 발견사항

  • Operation date: announced 30 January 2026 (Friday). DOJ press release number 26-82, Office of Public Affairs, Topic: Cybercrime.
  • U.S. government executed seizure warrants against three U.S.-registered internet domains allegedly engaged in illegal distribution of copyrighted works: zamunda.net, arenabg.com, and zelka.org.
  • All three sites were allegedly operated from Bulgaria; one of the domains is often ranked among the top 10 most visited domains in Bulgaria.
  • Bulgarian partners named: National Investigative Service (NIS); Ministry of the Interior's General Directorate Combating Organized Crime (GDCOC/GDBOP); State Agency for National Security; Prosecutor's Office.
  • U.S. domestic partners: U.S. Attorney's Office for the Southern District of Mississippi; HSI New Orleans Field Office; National Intellectual Property Rights Coordination Center (IPR Center).
  • Other coordination/technical partners: Europol; HSI Athens office (Greece); Customs and Border Protection (CBP) Sofia office (Bulgaria).
  • Cooperation channel: Justice Department's International Computer Hacking and Intellectual Property (ICHIP) program — jointly administered by OPDAT and CCIPS, with the ICHIP based in Bucharest, Romania providing significant assistance.
  • Charging announcement made by Assistant Attorney General A. Tysen Duva (Criminal Division), U.S. Attorney Baxter Kruger (S.D. Miss.), and Acting SAC Matt Wright (HSI New Orleans). Case handled by Senior Counsel Matthew A. Lamberti (CCIPS) and AUSA Max Meyers (S.D. Miss.). HSI is investigating the matter.

  • Independently corroborated by Bulgarian News Agency (BTA) 30 January 2026 16:14: four individuals detained; 30 addresses searched across Bulgaria; supervised by Sofia City Prosecutor's Office and Sofia District Prosecutor's Office; coordinated by Acting Prosecutor General Borislav Sarafov.

Source Summary

US Department of Justice Office of Public Affairs press release 26-82, dated Friday, 30 January 2026, announcing that the U.S. government executed seizure warrants against three U.S.-registered internet domains — zamunda.net, arenabg.com, and zelka.org — used by commercial websites allegedly engaged in the illegal distribution of copyrighted works belonging to U.S. companies and individuals (movies, television shows, video games, software, e-books, and other content). The three websites were allegedly operated from Bulgaria; per the affidavits, the three domains receive "tens of millions of visits a year" and one is "often ranked as one of the top 10 most visited domains in Bulgaria."

The release establishes:

  1. U.S. – Bulgaria coordinated takedown structure: simultaneous U.S. seizure of the .net/.com/.org domains plus Bulgarian search-and-seizure operations targeting the underlying infrastructure and operators.
  2. ICHIP Bucharest as the cooperation hub: the DOJ's International Computer Hacking and Intellectual Property advisor based in Bucharest, Romania provided significant assistance — cross-border IP/cybercrime capacity-building turning into a tier-1 enforcement assist.
  3. HSI Athens + CBP Sofia + Europol triangulated technical assistance: the U.S. Department of Homeland Security's HSI Athens office (Greece) and CBP Sofia attaché are explicitly named alongside Europol as having "critical roles" in coordinating efforts and providing technical assistance.
  4. First-of-its-kind Bulgarian piracy-platform-operator statute: the related DOJ ICHIP April 2026 summary records that the Bulgarian arrests and prosecutions proceeded under a "new criminal statute that criminalizes operating piracy platforms, the first law of its kind in Europe, drafted with OPDAT and CCIPS assistance" — i.e., the Bulgarian statute was itself a product of U.S.–Bulgaria legislative-drafting cooperation.
