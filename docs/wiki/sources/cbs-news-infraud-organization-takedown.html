주요 발견사항

  • DOJ indicted 36 defendants tied to the Infraud Organization carding/identity-theft forum in a case unsealed on 7 February 2018; the forum had operated since 2010 under the slogan 'In Fraud We Trust' and had nearly 11,000 members
  • 13 arrests were made on a single day across California, New York and Alabama (within the US); additional suspects were arrested in France, United Kingdom, Italy, Australia, Kosovo, and Serbia awaiting extradition
  • Sergey Medvedev, a 31-year-old Russian national, was identified as co-founder (alongside Ukrainian Svyatoslav Bondarenko) and operator of the payment system; he assumed ownership/administrative control when Bondarenko disappeared in 2015
  • Thai authorities arrested Medvedev at his Bangkok apartment on 2 February 2018 at the request of the United States — an example of informal police cooperation preceding formal extradition
  • Investigation began in 2014 when a US Homeland Security (HSI) officer deployed an undercover agent to infiltrate the forum — 4-year undercover operation before arrests
  • 4.3 million credit cards, debit cards, and bank accounts were traded on the forum; total losses exceeded USD 530 million

개요Summary

CBS News' coverage of the Infraud Organization takedown focuses especially on the cross-border arrest of Sergey Medvedev (age 31, Russian national) at his Bangkok apartment by Thai police on 2 February 2018 at US request, five days before the DOJ publicly unsealed the indictment on 7 February 2018.

CBS emphasizes:

  1. Medvedev's role — co-founder of Infraud (alongside Ukrainian Svyatoslav Bondarenko), payment-system operator, and de-facto owner/administrator after Bondarenko's disappearance in 2015
  2. Domestic-vs-international arrest split — 13 arrests inside the US (California, New York, Alabama) on the day of the DOJ announcement; additional suspects apprehended in France, UK, Italy, Australia, Kosovo, and Serbia, all awaiting extradition
  3. 4-year undercover operation — US Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) deployed an undercover agent inside the forum starting in 2014, illustrating the long timeline required for cybercrime forum dismantlement
  4. Scale of the forum — 4.3 million card/account records traded, USD 530 million in losses, ~11,000 forum members

Why this source matters

For the infraud-organization-takedown operation this CBS/AP report provides:

  • Confirmation that US-Thailand cooperation was informal police-to-police before any formal extradition proceeding (US "request" to Thai authorities, rather than MLAT)
  • HSI attribution — HSI, not FBI alone, initiated the undercover operation, which is relevant for the SNA edge data on the operation page
