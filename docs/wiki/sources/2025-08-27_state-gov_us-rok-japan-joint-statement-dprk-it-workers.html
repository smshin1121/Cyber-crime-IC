U.S.-ROK-Japan Joint Statement on DPRK Information Technology Workers

개요Summary

On August 27, 2025, the U.S. Department of State, Office of the Spokesperson, jointly with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Korea, issued a trilateral joint statement reaffirming coordinated US-Japan-ROK action against DPRK information technology workers who generate revenue for the regime's UN-sanctioned WMD and ballistic-missile programs.

The statement formalizes ongoing trilateral coordination on: 1. Identifying and disrupting DPRK IT-worker dispatch networks worldwide (North America, Europe, East Asia); 2. Updating joint public-private threat advisories; 3. Coordinating designations against foreign facilitators (Russia, Laos, China facilitators concurrently sanctioned by U.S. Treasury OFAC same week); 4. Strengthening cooperation with the private sector — exemplified by the August 26, 2025 Tokyo public–private event co-hosted with Mandiant (Google Cloud) to brief industry on tradecraft updates.

Named cooperating government bodies

Per the joint statement and companion MOFA Japan / METI Japan releases on August 27, 2025:

Country Lead foreign affairs body Other named participating agencies
United States U.S. Department of State U.S. Department of the Treasury (OFAC)
Japan Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) National Police Agency (NPA / 警察庁); Ministry of Finance; Ministry of Economy, Trade and Industry (METI)
Republic of Korea Ministry of Foreign Affairs (외교부) (Republic of Korea National Police Agency knpa is cooperating LE counterpart but not separately enumerated in the State Department release; ROK Foreign Ministry leads the IT-worker advisory effort)

L24 strict assessment

This release satisfies the strict L24 ≥2-country IC requirement with explicit naming of cooperating government agencies of three sovereign states. Among the named agencies, the Japanese National Police Agency (NPA) is unambiguously a law-enforcement entity, and the U.S. Department of the Treasury's OFAC is a federal sanctions-enforcement entity. The cooperation is between at least three distinct national-government agencies of three different countries acknowledged in the tier-1 primary source.

The publisher (U.S. Department of State) is a national-government own-domain (state.gov), satisfying L25 tier-1 publisher requirements (not a private wire, not Village Media affiliate, not ANI/AP/Reuters; primary published by the issuing sovereign government).

Crime types implicated

  • money-laundering-ic (sanctions evasion via fraudulent foreign IT employment proceeds)
  • online-fraud-ic (identity fraud / impersonation by DPRK IT workers using stolen or AI-generated identities)
  • hacking-ic (DPRK IT workers also engage in malicious cyber activity, particularly cryptocurrency theft in blockchain industry)

Korean perspective (한국 관점)

The Republic of Korea's MOFA leads the ROK domestic advisory effort on DPRK IT-worker risks to private companies; this trilateral statement is one of the operational outputs of the Republic of Korea — Japan — United States diplomatic working group on DPRK cyber activities (fourth meeting held August 2025). The Korean National Police Agency (knpa) coordinates downstream LE follow-through where DPRK IT workers are identified inside or transiting ROK jurisdiction.
