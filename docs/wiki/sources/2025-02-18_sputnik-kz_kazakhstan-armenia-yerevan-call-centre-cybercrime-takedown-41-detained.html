주요 발견사항

  • On 2025-02-18 the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Kazakhstan announced a joint Kazakh-Armenian operation that closed two call centres in Yerevan and detained 41 members of a transnational criminal group.
  • Lead Kazakh participant: Department for Combating Cybercrime of the MIA of Kazakhstan, headed by Zhandos Suiynbai (Жандос Сүйінбай). Lead Armenian participant: Main Directorate for the Investigation of Especially Important Cases of the Investigative Committee of the Republic of Armenia.
  • The 41 detainees were citizens of Armenia, Georgia, Iran, Syria, and Lebanon. Targeted victim populations: citizens of Kazakhstan and European countries. Modus operandi: investment-fraud call centres pitching fake 'large investment companies'.
  • Seized assets: more than 70 units of computer equipment, approximately 1 kg of gold, 5 vehicles, USD 30,000, Russian rubles, Georgian lari, and a cold cryptocurrency wallet.
  • As of the press date the Armenian side was continuing investigative and operational-search measures, supported by Kazakhstan's MIA Cybercrime Counteraction Department, to establish additional episodes, accomplices, and the total damage to Kazakh citizens.

Source Summary

Sputnik Kazakhstan release dated 2025-02-18, datelined Astana, attributed in-byline to the press service of the Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Republic of Kazakhstan. The release contains a named, on-record quotation by Zhandos Suiynbai, head of the MIA's Department for Combating Cybercrime, describing a joint Kazakh-Armenian special operation that closed two investment-fraud call centres in Yerevan, detained 41 members of a transnational criminal group, and seized cash, gold, vehicles, computers, and a cold cryptocurrency wallet.
