주요 발견사항

  • Eurojust coordinated the extradition of the alleged main perpetrator of a Swedish bank-impersonation fraud scheme from the United States to Sweden, executed on 2026-04-10.
  • The cooperation explicitly named tier-1 LE/prosecutorial agencies on both sides: Sweden (Swedish Prosecution Authority, National Unit Against Organised Crime; Swedish Police) and United States (DOJ Office of International Affairs; US Attorney's Office for the Central District of California; IRS-Criminal Investigation; Homeland Security Investigations; US Marshals Service).
  • Scheme: telephone-based bank-employee impersonation since approximately 2019; at least 25 victims defrauded; total damages approximately EUR 6 million.
  • Action day in November 2024 led to arrests of the main perpetrator and two accomplices (one in the US, two in Sweden); the main suspect's extradition followed roughly 17 months later.

Source Summary

Eurojust press release dated 2026-04-11 announcing the extradition (executed 2026-04-10) of the alleged main perpetrator of a large-scale bank-employee-impersonation fraud scheme from the United States to Sweden. The suspect had been detained in the US since late 2024 following coordinated arrests on a November 2024 action day. The scheme is attributed to telephone calls in which fraudsters posed as bank employees and instructed at least 25 victims to transfer money to accounts controlled by the criminal group, causing approximately EUR 6 million in damages.
