개요Summary

The Europol press release announces the 26 January 2023 international operation that dismantled the Hive ransomware group's infrastructure. The operation, led by the FBI with operational support from the US Secret Service, German BKA, Dutch National Police, and Europol EC3, involved 13 countries total.

The most notable operational detail is that the FBI had covertly infiltrated Hive's network since July 2022 — roughly 7 months before the public takedown — and used its covert access to secretly obtain decryption keys and share them with Hive victims in real time, without alerting the criminals. This covert phase prevented an estimated USD 130 million / EUR 120 million in ransom payments during the 7-month infiltration window.

Hive operated as a ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) affiliate model with a standard 80/20 revenue split (affiliates/developers) and had targeted 1,500+ victims in 80+ countries since June 2021, including hospitals, school districts, financial firms, and critical infrastructure.

Operational significance for cybercrime IC

The Hive takedown is a reference case for:

  1. Covert-infiltration model — rather than seizing infrastructure immediately after access, law enforcement maintained covert presence for 7 months to maximize victim protection. This model has been discussed as a template for future RaaS disruptions.
  2. Victim-centric success metric — the operation is measured not primarily by arrests but by ransom prevented (EUR 120M) and victims notified/decrypted (~300 victims during the covert phase per DOJ).
  3. Sustained multi-country coordination — 13 countries participating at the infrastructure-disruption level is among the largest single-operation country counts for ransomware takedowns.
