Source Summary

Austrian Federal Ministry of the Interior (Bundesministerium für Inneres, BMI) tier-1 own-domain press release dated 2026-02-16 announcing the results of Operation Casus, the federally-responsible Austrian police unit against Schockanruf-Betrug (shock-call fraud), established at the Landeskriminalamt Niederösterreich (LKA NÖ, Lower Austria State Criminal Office) on 2024-12-21. The release announces 23 identified suspects originating from Poland, Czech Republic, Germany, Slovakia, Serbia, and Spain — of whom 17 were arrested — with total fraud damage of EUR 4.8 million to (predominantly elderly) Austrian victims. The investigation was conducted by LKA NÖ in cooperation with the Zentrale Staatsanwaltschaft zur Verfolgung von Wirtschaftsstrafsachen und Korruption (WKStA, Central Public Prosecutor's Office for the Prosecution of Economic Crimes and Corruption) and "ausländische Polizeibehörden" (foreign police authorities), with specific named cooperation from Slovak investigative authorities (information leading to 2025-10-07 Vienna-Donaustadt arrest of 25-year-old Slovak citizen) and Czech authorities (Brno [Brünn] caller-cell raid; WKStA-issued European Arrest Warrant executed against a 20-year-old Czech woman extradited to Austria). The 27-year-old Czech cell leader operating from Brno (charged with at least four completed offences causing EUR 483,000 damage in Austria) was arrested in Slovakia via European Arrest Warrant and was sentenced to six years in Poland. Interior Minister Gerhard Karner and Bundeskriminalamt Director Andreas Holzer presented the investigation success at a 2026-02-16 press conference; Holzer reported 189 cumulative arrests with EUR 53 million in total damage across the wider 2022-2026 counter-Schockanruf effort and a recent ~50% reduction in reported cases and damages.

Relevance to IC

Tier-1 primary source from the Austrian Ministry of Interior documenting a multi-jurisdictional cooperation pattern (Austria as lead + Slovakia + Czechia explicitly named, with Polish, German, Serbian, Spanish suspects extradited via European Arrest Warrant or arrested abroad). Operation Casus is a discrete federally-responsible policing unit (LKA NÖ) coordinating cross-border counter-fraud investigation, with the BMI release explicitly attributing intelligence sharing to Slovak counterpart agencies and identifying a Brno (Czech Republic) caller cell as a focal target. The IC pathway is European Arrest Warrant-driven, with WKStA issuing EAWs and Slovak/Czech authorities executing arrests on Austrian-issued warrants. Crime-type categorisation is voice-phishing / Schockanrufe (shock-call) with VoIP-based foreign caller-cell infrastructure. South Korea is not named in the release; the Schockanruf modus operandi (impersonation of police/judiciary + bail-trick pretext) parallels Korean voice-phishing typologies but the case has no direct Korean nexus.
