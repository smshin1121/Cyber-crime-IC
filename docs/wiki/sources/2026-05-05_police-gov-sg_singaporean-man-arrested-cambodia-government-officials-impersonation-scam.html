주요 발견사항

  • SPF announced the 4 May 2026 repatriation-on-arrival arrest of Singaporean Ngiam Siow Jui (30) for facilitating offences linked to a Phnom Penh-based organised criminal group conducting Government Officials Impersonation Scams targeting Singapore residents.
  • The release explicitly attributes the foreign arrest to the Cambodian National Police: 'The Cambodian National Police (CNP) arrested Ngiam in Phnom Penh, Cambodia,' and characterises the action as following 'close collaboration and intelligence sharing between the SPF and the CNP.'
  • The OCG's Phnom Penh scam compound was disrupted during a joint SPF-CNP operation on 9 September 2025; the OCG is linked to at least 535 reported scam cases and approximately S$53 million in losses.
  • Two further Singaporean suspects (Jonathan Boneta, 27; Lee Ding Hao, 33) remain at large under INTERPOL Red Notices issued on 25 March 2026.

Source Summary

The Singapore Police Force's Public Affairs Department published this press release on 5 May 2026 announcing the repatriation-on-arrival arrest of a Singaporean national, Ngiam Siow Jui, in connection with a Phnom Penh-based organised criminal group conducting Government Officials Impersonation Scams against Singapore residents. The release names the Cambodian National Police as the foreign arresting agency, situates the action in a continuing SPF-CNP cooperation envelope (with a prior joint compound-disruption on 9 September 2025), and confirms two outstanding INTERPOL Red Notices issued by the SPF on 25 March 2026 against Jonathan Boneta and Lee Ding Hao.
