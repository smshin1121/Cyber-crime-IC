주요 발견사항

  • Action days 14-17 April 2024: 70 addresses searched globally; 37 suspects arrested.
  • 4 UK arrests linked to the running of the site, including LabHost's original developer.
  • LabHost = phishing-as-a-service (PhaaS) platform, $249/month subscription, 170+ fake-website templates, integrated LabRat campaign-management tool capturing 2FA codes.
  • Scale: ≥40,000 LabHost-linked phishing domains; ~10,000 users worldwide.
  • Investigation led by UK London Metropolitan Police; supported by Europol EC3 + J-CAT (hosted at Europol HQ).
  • Europol involvement since September 2023; operational sprint at Europol HQ with all participating countries.
  • Action-phase Europol specialist deployed to Dutch National Police for enforcement support.
  • 19 participating countries: Australia (AFP-led JPC3), Austria (BKA), Belgium (Federal Judicial Police Brussels), Canada (RCMP), Czechia (Criminal Police Bureau), Estonia (Police and Border Guard Board), Finland (Poliisi), Ireland (An Garda Síochána), Lithuania (Lithuania Police), Malta (Malta Police Force), Netherlands (Central Netherlands Police), New Zealand (NZ Police), Poland (Central Office for Combating Cybercrime), Portugal (Judicial Police), Romania (Romanian Police), Spain (Policía Nacional), Sweden (Swedish Police Authority), United Kingdom (Met Police), United States (USSS + FBI).

Source Summary

Europol newsroom press release dated 2024-04-18 announcing the disruption of the LabHost phishing-as-a-service platform during 14-17 April 2024 international action days. The release establishes a 19-country joint cooperation pattern led by the UK Met Police and supported by Europol EC3 + J-CAT, with action-phase Europol specialist deployment to the Dutch National Police. Names every participating jurisdiction and its lead authority.

IC Pattern

This release establishes the most fully-articulated 19-country action-day enumeration in the wiki for a phishing-as-a-service takedown — covering EU member states, EFTA partners, Anglosphere Five Eyes, and the Pacific. Notable features:

  • UK-led + Europol EC3 + J-CAT-hosted investigation with action-phase specialist deployment to the Netherlands.
  • Five Eyes participation outside the EU (US, UK, Canada, Australia, NZ) co-acting with EU member states under Europol J-CAT umbrella.
  • PhaaS commoditisation of phishing — $249/month subscription, 170+ fake-website templates, LabRat 2FA-capture campaign-management tool.
  • September 2023 → April 2024 investigation timeline (~8 months).
