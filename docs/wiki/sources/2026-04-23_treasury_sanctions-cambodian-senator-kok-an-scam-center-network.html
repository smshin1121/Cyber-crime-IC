주요 발견사항

  • OFAC press release SB-0469 dated 23 April 2026 designating Cambodian Senator Kok An plus 28 individuals/entities (29 total) under E.O. 13694, as amended by E.O. 13757, E.O. 14144, and E.O. 14306.
  • Action is in furtherance of President Trump's E.O. 14390 of 6 March 2026 'Combating Cybercrime, Fraud, and Predatory Schemes Against American Citizens'.
  • Coordinated with the Scam Center Strike Force — joint initiative of USAO-DC, DOJ Criminal Division, FBI, and USSS — focused on Burma, Cambodia, and Laos.
  • Same-day Scam Center Strike Force actions: charges against 2 individuals running a scam compound in Burma + attempting to start one in Cambodia; seizure of social media messaging app used to recruit human trafficking victims; seizure of 503 fraudulent web domains used for cryptocurrency investment fraud.
  • Designated principals: Kok An (Cambodian senator, Crown Resorts founder, Anco Brothers chairman); Rithy Raksmei (K99 Group head); Aik Paung and Sai Aung Linn (Burmese-born Cambodian-citizens; Nan Hai/Xihu/Heng Feng owners); Luo Hong (Bolai/Huan Hai/SSDD founder).
  • Designated entities: Crown Resorts, Anco Brothers, K99 Group, Xing Tian Di, Nan Hai (Nan Tian International Hotel), Xihu, Heng Feng Group, Heng Feng Cambodia Bank plc, Bolai (Brilliancy Sihanoukville), plus 13 other Kok An / Rithy / Aik Paung / Sai Aung Linn-owned entities (855 Investment Corp, A Wee Trading, Anco Specialized Bank, Anco Water Supply, KEP Hydropower, Khmer Electrical Power, Mekong Potable Water, Aurum Co Ltd, Kaissa Travel, Khemreah Co Ltd, Xiong Wei Investment, Xiong Wei Real Estate, Fung Yuet Resort Hotel) plus Huan Hai and SSDD.
  • Properties identified as scam compounds: locations in Poipet, Sihanoukville, Bavet, and Phnom Penh in Cambodia.
  • Scam pattern: digital asset investment fraud + romance/friendship lure + human trafficking with passport confiscation + debt bondage + physical violence to coerce scam labor.
  • Underlying scope: Americans lost at least USD 10 billion in 2024 to Southeast Asia-based scam operations (66% YoY increase). USSS-investigated US-based group laundered USD 73 million+ in victim funds; 8 US-based co-conspirators pleaded guilty; Daren Li convicted/sentenced in Central District of California, fled US after removing ankle monitor.
  • Same-day State Department rewards: USD 10M for info on Tai Chang scam compound proceeds (Burma's Karen State); USD 4M for arrest of fugitive Daren Li.
  • Prior chained OFAC actions: 12 Nov 2025 (Burmese armed group + 7 others, Tai Chang); 14 Oct 2025 (Prince Group TCO + 146 associates, US-UK joint); 8 Sep 2025 (12 companies + 7 individuals Cambodia/Burma); 12 Sep 2024 (Ly Yong Phat, L.Y.P. Group + 4 hotels).
  • Tier-1 primary source: U.S. Treasury Department press release SB-0469 (https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sb0469).

Source Summary

OFAC press release SB-0469 dated 23 April 2026 designating Cambodian Senator Kok An plus 28 individuals/entities (29 total) under E.O. 13694 as amended. The action targets a Cambodia-based, casino-and-scam-compound network that uses digital asset investment fraud + romance-friendship lures + human-trafficking forced labor to defraud Americans, with a documented underlying loss of USD 10 billion in 2024 to Southeast Asia-based scam operations (66% YoY increase).

The action is coordinated with the newly-named Scam Center Strike Force (joint initiative of USAO-DC, DOJ Criminal Division, FBI, USSS) focused on Burma, Cambodia, and Laos, and is undertaken in furtherance of E.O. 14390 of 6 March 2026 ('Combating Cybercrime, Fraud, and Predatory Schemes Against American Citizens'). Same-day Strike Force actions include charges against 2 Burma-Cambodia scam-compound operators, seizure of a social media messaging app used to recruit trafficking victims, and seizure of 503 fraudulent web domains used for cryptocurrency investment fraud.

The release also chains to prior OFAC actions: - 12 Nov 2025: Burmese armed group + 7 others (Tai Chang scam compound, Karen State, Burma) - 14 Oct 2025: Prince Group TCO + 146 associates (US-UK joint) - 8 Sep 2025: 12 companies + 7 individuals (Cambodia/Burma) - 12 Sep 2024: Ly Yong Phat, L.Y.P. Group + 4 hotels

Same-day State Department rewards: USD 10M for info on Tai Chang scam-compound proceeds; USD 4M for arrest of fugitive Daren Li (convicted in C.D. Cal., removed ankle monitor, fled US).

The release establishes:

  1. The Scam Center Strike Force as the formal US multi-agency cooperation framework for SE-Asia scam-compound enforcement (USAO-DC + DOJ-Crim + FBI + USSS), focused on Burma + Cambodia + Laos.
  2. E.O. 14390 (6 March 2026) as the new presidential authority specifically directed at foreign-backed cybercrime / cyber-enabled fraud / extortion targeting Americans.
  3. Cambodian-political-protection layer as a structural feature of SE-Asia scam-compound enforcement — Kok An is a sitting senator, not just a businessman.
  4. 503 fraudulent crypto-investment web domains seized as a discrete operational metric.
  5. Casino-as-laundering-vehicle pattern explicitly cited: "Nearly all major scam compounds in Cambodia are connected to casinos, which serve to launder the proceeds of scams."
