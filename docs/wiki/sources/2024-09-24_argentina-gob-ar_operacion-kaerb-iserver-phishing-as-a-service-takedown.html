주요 발견사항

  • Operación KAERB (announced 24 September 2024) dismantled the iServer phishing-as-a-service platform operated since at least 2019, with the administrator residing in Santa Fe Province, Argentina.
  • Six-country coordinated enforcement (Spain, Argentina, Colombia, Chile, Ecuador, Peru) produced 17 arrests, 28 home searches starting 10 September 2024, and seizure of 921 electronic devices.
  • Estimated 483,000 victims globally, of which Chile 77,000, Colombia 70,000, Ecuador 42,000, Peru 41,500, Spain 30,000, Argentina 29,000, plus ~193,500 in other jurisdictions; iServer had 2,000+ registered subscriber-cybercriminals (desbloqueadores) using it for unlocking stolen high-end phones via credential phishing.
  • Coordination architecture is explicitly named in the release: EUROPOL (Agencia de la Unión Europea para la Cooperación Policial), AMERIPOL (Comunidad de Policías de América), PAcCTO 2.0 (Programa de Asistencia Contra el Crimen Transnacional Organizado, EU-funded), with private-sector support from Group-IB.
  • Argentine enforcement footprint: 5 arrests, 12 raids — Autonomous City of Buenos Aires (5), Santa Fe (3), Córdoba (3), Jujuy (1); operational leads cited are Secretary of Security Alejandra Monteoliva, División Ciberpatrullaje of the Policía Federal Argentina (Comisario Gustavo Cabada), and the Gendarmería Nacional Argentina (Comandante General Claudio Miguel Brilloni).
  • Tier-1 primary source: institutional press release on the official Government of Argentina domain (argentina.gob.ar) under the Ministerio de Seguridad Nacional / Policía Federal Argentina path; corroborated by Group-IB's PRNewswire announcement and by Eurojust/Europol coverage in the same news cycle.
  • Spanish-language primary source — adds the LATAM-side specifics (PAcCTO 2.0 invocation, regional victim distribution, Argentine-judicial geographic distribution, Argentine spokesperson roster) that the Europol English-language announcement does not enumerate.

Source Profile

This is an institutional press release dated 24 September 2024, published on the Government of Argentina's official portal (argentina.gob.ar) under the Ministerio de Seguridad Nacional / Policía Federal Argentina path. It announces the dismantlement of the iServer phishing-as-a-service platform through the multinational law-enforcement action Operación KAERB. The Spanish-language release names the participating cooperation architecture (EUROPOL, AMERIPOL, PAcCTO 2.0, Group-IB), enumerates the six participating countries, breaks down the global victim count by country, and details the Argentine-side enforcement footprint (provincial distribution of warrants, named operational leads).

Reliability and Credibility

  • Reliability: high — official Government of Argentina domain (argentina.gob.ar), institutionally signed press release under the Ministerio de Seguridad Nacional / Policía Federal Argentina path.
  • Credibility: confirmed — first-party announcement by one of the lead enforcing jurisdictions; corroborated by Group-IB's PRNewswire release of the same week and by overlapping Europol / Eurojust newsroom coverage that confirms the headline figures (17 arrests, six countries, 483,000 victims, 921 devices).
  • Sensitivity: public — distributed for general media use; identifies the Argentine administrator only by residency (Santa Fe Province), not by name; secondary press identifies him as Iván David Cudde — that secondary identification is not asserted from this primary record.
