주요 발견사항

  • VOA reported on 9 February 2018 on the arrest of Sergey Medvedev, the alleged co-founder of the Infraud Organization, at his Bangkok apartment on 2 February 2018 at the request of US authorities
  • Medvedev (age 31 at time of arrest) was indicted by the US DOJ on 7 February 2018 in the District of Nevada along with 35 co-defendants; the indictment named Ukrainian Svyatoslav Bondarenko as the original founder and Medvedev as the 'owner and administrator' of the forum after Bondarenko went missing in 2015
  • Infraud was founded in October 2010 under the slogan 'In Fraud We Trust'; at peak it had ~10,900 active members who traded 4.3+ million stolen credit cards, debit cards, and bank accounts, causing $530 million+ in losses to legitimate users and businesses
  • Thai authorities coordinated with the US to execute the arrest at Medvedev's apartment; Medvedev was subsequently extradited to the US to face prosecution — an example of Thai-US cybercrime cooperation outside formal treaty channels
  • VOA frames the case as 'part of a global dark-web crackdown' — placing it within the broader 2018 pattern of US-led multilateral cybercrime forum enforcement actions
  • Medvedev's eventual 2020 guilty plea and 10-year sentence, plus Valerian Chiochiu's 10-year sentence for FastPOS malware authoring, validate VOA's 2018 reporting as probably-true going forward

개요Summary

VOA's 9 February 2018 article documents the Thai arrest of Sergey Medvedev, alleged Infraud Organization co-founder, executed at his Bangkok apartment on 2 February 2018 at US request. The article summarizes:

  • The 7 February 2018 DOJ unsealed indictment against 36 defendants in the District of Nevada
  • Infraud's "In Fraud We Trust" branding and October 2010 founding by Ukrainian Svyatoslav Bondarenko
  • Medvedev's operational role as payment-system operator and — after Bondarenko's 2015 disappearance — forum owner and administrator
  • Scale: ~10,900 active members, 4.3M+ stolen cards/accounts, ~US$530M in losses
  • Medvedev's extradition to the US to face prosecution
