주요 발견사항

  • DOJ unsealed an indictment in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Texas on October 1, 2024 charging Russian national Aleksandr Viktorovich Ryzhenkov with multiple Computer Fraud and Abuse Act violations and conspiracy to commit money laundering for BitPaymer ransomware attacks against U.S. victims dating from June 2017.
  • DOJ release expressly notes the unsealing was coordinated with OFAC's same-day addition of Ryzhenkov and six other Evil Corp related persons to the Specially Designated Nationals list, and acknowledges UK National Crime Agency reporting that Ryzhenkov is also a suspected LockBit affiliate — establishing tier-1 own-domain US-UK LE cooperation acknowledgement.
  • The DOJ release identifies Ryzhenkov as second-in-command of Evil Corp, Russia-based cybercriminal organisation also believed responsible for the Dridex malware, and confirms he is believed to remain in Russia (fugitive).
  • Investigating offices named in the release: FBI Dallas Field Office (investigation), U.S. Attorney's Office for the Northern District of Texas, and CCIPS of the Criminal Division (prosecution).
  • Confirms the venue as U.S. District Court for the Northern District of Texas — corrects the operation-page frontmatter which previously stated 'DOJ-EDTX' (Eastern District). District should be Northern District of Texas per this tier-1 own-domain release.

Source Summary

Tier-1 U.S. Department of Justice Office of Public Affairs press release dated October 1, 2024 announcing the unsealing of a Northern District of Texas indictment against Aleksandr Viktorovich Ryzhenkov for BitPaymer ransomware attacks. The release expressly coordinates with the same-day OFAC tri-lateral US-UK-AU sanctions action (Treasury JY-2623) and acknowledges UK National Crime Agency intelligence on Ryzhenkov's separate LockBit affiliate role. The pairing is the canonical "designate + indict" pattern for Russia-based ransomware actors who are physically out of U.S. reach.

Relevance to IC

  • DOJ-OFAC own-domain pairing. Provides the second tier-1 own-domain source for treasury-evil-corp-tri-lateral-us-uk-au-sanctions-2024 — Treasury was previously the lone source. With OPA added the operation now has both the financial-sanctions side (Treasury JY-2623) and the criminal-prosecution side (DOJ OPA) of the October 1, 2024 Evil Corp action recorded from primary tier-1 own-domain releases.
  • US-UK LE-cooperation acknowledgement. The DOJ release directly names the UK National Crime Agency as a reporting source for Ryzhenkov's LockBit-affiliate status, satisfying L24's ≥2-country tier-1-acknowledged LE-cooperation requirement on the DOJ side independently of the Treasury release.
  • Venue correction. The operation page frontmatter previously asserted "DOJ-EDTX". The DOJ OPA release identifies the venue as Northern District of Texas (USAO-NDTX), not Eastern District. The operation page body and frontmatter should be reconciled to NDTX.
