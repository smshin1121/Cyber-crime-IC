주요 발견사항

  • OFAC re-designated Garantex Europe OU under E.O. 13694 (cyber authority), in addition to its prior April 2022 designation under E.O. 14024 (Russia financial-services sector).
  • OFAC concurrently designated Grinex (Garantex successor exchange), three Garantex executives (Mendeleev, Mira Serda, Karavatsky), and six associated companies in Russia and the Kyrgyz Republic (A7, A71, A7 Agent, Old Vector, InDeFi Bank, Exved).
  • Action explicitly attributed to U.S. Secret Service Cyber Investigative Section with FBI assistance; builds on the March 6, 2025 USSS-led infrastructure seizure (with German and Finnish law enforcement) and March 7, 2025 DOJ indictments.
  • Garantex received millions in cryptocurrency directly from Conti, Black Basta, LockBit, NetWalker, and Phoenix Cryptolocker ransomware proceeds; also serviced Ryuk-associated actors.
  • A7A5 ruble-backed token (issued by Kyrgyzstani Old Vector for Russian A7 LLC) was used to compensate Garantex customers for funds frozen during the March 2025 enforcement, demonstrating active sanctions-evasion infrastructure.
  • State Department offered up to USD 5 million reward for Mira Serda and up to USD 1 million for other Garantex leaders under Transnational Organized Crime Rewards Program.

개요Summary

Official OFAC press release (SB-0225) announcing a coordinated sanctions package targeting the Garantex cryptocurrency exchange ecosystem and its post-disruption successor Grinex. The release is the financial-sanctions complement to the March 6-7, 2025 U.S. Secret Service-led law enforcement disruption of Garantex (domain seizure, USD 26 million crypto freeze, DOJ indictments of Mira Serda and Besciokov). Highly reliable as primary government source.

Key Entities

  • Garantex Europe OU — re-designated cryptocurrency exchange (Russia-operated, Estonia-registered, license revoked Feb 2022)
  • Grinex — newly designated successor exchange
  • Aleksandr Mira Serda, Sergey Mendeleev, Pavel Karavatsky — Garantex executives newly designated
  • A7 LLC, A71 LLC, A7 Agent LLC — Russian cross-border settlement firms (Shor / PSB-controlled)
  • Old Vector — Kyrgyzstani A7A5 token issuer
  • InDeFi Bank, Exved — Mendeleev-controlled financial entities

Legal Authority

  • E.O. 13694 (as amended by E.O. 14144 and E.O. 14306) — malicious cyber-enabled activities
  • E.O. 14024 — Russia financial services sector (basis of April 2022 designation, retained)

Cooperation Footprint

  • USSS Cyber Investigative Section (lead)
  • FBI (assistance)
  • Department of State (rewards program)
  • Department of Justice (parallel indictments)
  • Historical (March 2025): German and Finnish law enforcement, Indian arrest of Besciokov, prior Estonian FIU enforcement (2022)

Reliability Assessment

  • Reliability: high (primary government press release, official designation document)
  • Credibility: confirmed for the fact of designation; analytical claims about ransomware nexus and transaction volumes are highly likely accurate given OFAC's evidentiary thresholds, but quantitative figures (USD 100 million+ in illicit transactions, USD 26 million frozen) are not independently verifiable from this source alone.
