주요 발견사항

  • Hellenic Police press release dated 2026-03-06 announces the 2026-03-05 dismantling of a transnational multi-member criminal organisation operating cryptocurrency-investment fraud since at least 2019; 12 arrests across Crete, Attica, and Igoumenitsa.
  • Total illicit gain exceeded €14.5 million; 73 victims identified at preliminary inquiry with at least €760,900 in confirmed losses; on-site forensic examination of digital wallets and devices identified circulation of digital currencies above USD 2,000,000 at current value.
  • International cooperation explicitly named: European Investigation Orders issued to France, Germany and Malta; Mutual Legal Assistance requests issued to the United Kingdom, Ireland and Denmark; two members linked to four offshore companies in Bulgaria — establishing IC scope across at least six foreign jurisdictions plus Greece.
  • Lead unit: Department for the Confrontation of Organised Crime of Crete (Τ.Α.Ο.Ε.Κ. Κρήτης) within the Hellenic Police DAOE; supporting Greek services include the Forensic Sciences Division Sub-Directorate for Digital Forensic Investigation and Analysis, the Cyber Crime Directorate, and the Hellenic Capital Market Commission as crypto-asset supervisor.
  • Charges: criminal organisation, aggravated fraud, money laundering, provision of crypto-asset services without certification, concealment of assets to obtain bankruptcy, and weapons-law offences. Modus operandi: Ponzi structure with 21 internal ranks (e.g., ambassador, leader, founder's council), 90-day in-house 'academy' training, rebranding when platforms collapsed.

The release explicitly names the foreign cooperation channels and counterpart jurisdictions, satisfying the IC scope requirement (≥2 named foreign countries) with seven jurisdictions in total: Greece (lead) plus France, Germany, Malta (EIO recipients); United Kingdom, Ireland, Denmark (MLA recipients); and Bulgaria (locus of four linked offshore companies discovered in cooperation with the Greek anti-money-laundering authority).
