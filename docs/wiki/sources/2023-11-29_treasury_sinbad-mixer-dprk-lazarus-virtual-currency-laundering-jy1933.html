주요 발견사항

  • OFAC designated Sinbad.io, a Bitcoin-based virtual currency mixer, as a key money-laundering tool of the OFAC-designated Lazarus Group (DPRK state-sponsored cyber actor).
  • Sinbad was used to launder a significant portion of the $100M Atomic Wallet heist (June 3, 2023), the $620M Axie Infinity heist (March 2022), and the $100M Horizon Bridge heist (June 2022) — totaling ~$820M in DPRK-linked crypto theft routed through this single mixer.
  • Designation issued pursuant to E.O. 13694 (as amended by E.O. 13757) for cyber-enabled malicious activity, and pursuant to E.O. 13722 for materially supporting the DPRK government.
  • Industry experts believe Sinbad is highly likely a successor to Blender.io, OFAC's first-ever virtual-currency-mixer designation (May 6, 2022).
  • Action positioned within a sequence of OFAC mixer/exchange enforcement: Blender.io (2022-05-06), Tornado Cash redesignation (2022-11-08), OTC trader designations (2023-04-24), Sinbad (2023-11-29).
  • Lazarus Group cumulative theft cited at over $2 billion in digital assets across multiple thefts in 10+ years of operation.
  • Sinbad also used by non-state cybercriminals for sanctions evasion, drug trafficking, purchase of child sexual abuse material (CSAM), and darknet marketplace sales — enforcement targets a multi-purpose laundering vehicle, not only state-sponsored theft.
  • Sanctions implications: all U.S. property/interests of Sinbad blocked; secondary sanctions exposure for non-U.S. persons transacting with Sinbad.
  • Lazarus Group itself was designated 2019-09-13 under E.O. 13722 as an agency/instrumentality/controlled entity of the DPRK government.
  • Designation explicitly framed as continuing pressure on DPRK revenue streams that fund WMD and ballistic missile programs.

Source Summary

This OFAC press release (JY-1933, 2023-11-29) announces the designation of Sinbad.io, a Bitcoin-based virtual-currency mixer, as a "key money-laundering tool" of the previously-designated DPRK Lazarus Group. The action is highly likely the most operationally significant 2023 mixer designation by OFAC because it directly ties a single laundering vehicle to three specific DPRK cryptocurrency heists with a combined ~$820 million in stolen funds: the $620M Axie Infinity heist (March 2022), the $100M Horizon Bridge heist (June 2022), and the $100M Atomic Wallet heist (June 3, 2023).

The release establishes a clear OFAC enforcement pattern against virtual-currency mixers used by Lazarus Group: Blender.io (2022-05-06, first-ever mixer designation), Tornado Cash (2022-11-08 redesignation), two OTC traders (2023-04-24), and now Sinbad.io (2023-11-29). Industry experts believe Sinbad is highly likely a successor to Blender.io, suggesting infrastructure resilience among DPRK laundering networks despite repeated designations.

The legal authorities cited are confirmed: E.O. 13694 as amended by E.O. 13757 (cyber-enabled activity authority) and E.O. 13722 (DPRK government / Workers' Party authority — used because the Lazarus Group itself is a designated DPRK government instrumentality). The release does not invoke E.O. 13687 or the DPRK-specific E.O. 13810/13551 used in later 2025 DPRK financial-network designations, indicating that the cyber-enabled and DPRK-government authorities were the prosecutor's chosen frame for a mixer infrastructure target.

The release also documents a secondary use case: Sinbad is described as serving non-state cybercriminals for "sanctions evasion, drug trafficking, the purchase of child sexual abuse materials, and additional illicit sales on darknet marketplaces." This is consistent with the multi-purpose laundering profile common to Bitcoin mixers and supports reading Sinbad as both a state-sponsored-theft laundering pathway and a general criminal-utility mixer.
