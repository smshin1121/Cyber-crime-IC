주요 발견사항

  • Italian Polizia di Stato (Servizio Polizia Postale e per la Sicurezza Cibernetica) officially announces operation 'Cagliostro' — the seizure of over USD 7.5 million in cryptocurrency, described as the largest crypto seizure ever performed in Italy, in the underlying 'Voltaiko' transnational photovoltaic-investment Ponzi scheme case.
  • Italian agency-chain explicitly named: (1) Servizio polizia postale e per la Sicurezza cibernetica, (2) Centro operativo per la Sicurezza cibernetica Emilia-Romagna, (3) Guardia di finanza — Comando provinciale di Bologna, (4) Procura della Repubblica di Bologna (lead prosecutor coordinating the joint investigation).
  • International cooperation explicitly acknowledged: 'L'ufficio italiano ad Eurojust si e' occupato del coordinamento sovranazionale nell'esecuzione degli ordini di congelamento delle cryptovalute, e ha tenuto i contatti con le autorita' francesi competenti, consentendo di arrivare al blocco dei portafogli digitali' (the Italian Eurojust desk handled supranational coordination for executing crypto-freezing orders and maintained contacts with the competent French authorities, enabling the blocking of digital wallets).
  • Investigation chronology: investigation opened October 2025; pre-existing seizures (95 bank accounts frozen, voltaiko.com website taken offline, two Rimini-province villas, electronic devices, luxury goods, gold bullion — total approx. EUR 2 million); latest action 2026-04-29 with USD 7.5M+ crypto seizure.
  • Victim/scale figures: approximately 6,000 Italian investor-victims; investment fraud framed as a Ponzi scheme promising returns from purported 'green' photovoltaic investments abroad which were non-existent.
  • L24 IC requirement satisfied: ≥2 distinct countries' law-enforcement / judicial authorities — Italy (Polizia di Stato + Guardia di Finanza + Procura Bologna) and France (competent French judicial authorities) — explicitly cooperated through Eurojust on judicial cryptocurrency-asset-freezing orders, with the cooperation explicitly acknowledged in the tier-1 primary source itself (this Polizia di Stato release).

개요Summary

This is the Italian primary-source press release announcing the latest operational phase of operation "Cagliostro", the Polizia Postale + Guardia di Finanza investigation into the "Voltaiko" transnational photovoltaic-investment Ponzi scheme. The release marks (a) what the Italian Postal Police describe as the largest cryptocurrency seizure ever performed in Italy (USD 7.5+ million), and (b) the operational record of an Italy ↔ France judicial-cooperation chain executed via Eurojust to freeze cryptocurrency wallets located in French jurisdiction.

The release is the canonical tier-1 primary source for this operation; no foreign-state primary source mirrors it (the French side is not separately named beyond "competent French authorities", consistent with French judicial-secrecy practice on ongoing cases).

Reliability assessment

  • reliability: high — Polizia di Stato official press release on poliziadistato.it (the Italian National Police's main public communications channel). Tier-1 primary source from the Italian executive branch.
  • credibility: probably-true — operational details (agency chain, EUR/USD figures, prosecutor's office, Eurojust coordination, French side acknowledgement) are consistent with the format of other recent Polizia di Stato press releases on transnational crypto cases. No defendant individual names are published, in line with Italian presumption-of-innocence reporting conventions; the EUR / USD figures are rounded but specific enough to be auditable.
  • sensitivity: public — published on the public-facing poliziadistato.it newsroom.
