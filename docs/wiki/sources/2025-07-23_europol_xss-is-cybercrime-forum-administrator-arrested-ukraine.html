주요 발견사항

  • Action day 22 July 2025 in Kyiv, Ukraine: suspected administrator of xss.is (formerly DaMaGeLaB) cybercrime forum arrested.
  • Forum scale: 50,000+ registered users; active 2013-2025 (12+ years); marketplace for stolen data, hacking tools, malware, compromised system access, ransomware-related services.
  • Suspect background: nearly 20 years of cybercrime activity; pseudonym 'toha' (per third-party reporting; not in primary Europol release).
  • Suspect revenue: EUR 7,000,000+ in advertising and facilitation fees from xss.is + thesecure.biz private cybercriminal messaging platform.
  • Suspect role: trusted-third-party arbiter for criminal disputes; transaction-security guarantor.
  • Investigation timeline: French Police initiated July 2021; September 2024 operational phase moved to Ukraine; French police investigators deployed on the ground; 2025-07-22 action day.
  • Europol contributions: virtual command post (2024-09); operational + analytical support throughout; Europol mobile office deployed to Kyiv during the action week for on-site coordination + evidence collection; mapped cybercriminal infrastructure + linked suspect to other major threat actors.
  • France lead authorities: Paris Prosecutor (Parquet de Paris — JUNALCO); French Police — Paris Police Prefecture (Préfecture de Police de Paris — Brigade de lutte contre la cybercriminalité / BL2C).
  • Ukraine partner authorities: General Prosecutor's Office of Ukraine; Security Service of Ukraine (SBU) — Cybercrime Department.
  • Domain seizure: clearnet domain of xss.is taken over by 'la Brigade de Lutte Contre la Cybercriminalité with assistance of the SBU Cyber Department' (per third-party reporting of the seizure banner).

Source Summary

Europol newsroom press release dated 2025-07-23 announcing the 2025-07-22 arrest in Kyiv, Ukraine, of the suspected administrator of xss.is — a 12-year-running Russian-speaking cybercrime forum (50,000+ registered users) and one of the central marketplaces for stolen data, hacking tools, and ransomware-related services in the global cybercrime ecosystem. Lead investigation by French Police (Brigade de lutte contre la cybercriminalité / BL2C) + Paris Prosecutor (Parquet de Paris JUNALCO), with Ukrainian SBU Cybercrime Department + General Prosecutor's Office of Ukraine partnership and Europol EC3 coordination including a virtual command post (Sept 2024) and a Kyiv mobile office during the action week.

IC Pattern

This release establishes a France-Ukraine bilateral cybercrime cooperation pattern with several distinct IC mechanism classes:

  • French Police BL2C (Brigade de lutte contre la cybercriminalité, Préfecture de Police de Paris) + Paris Prosecutor (Parquet de Paris — JUNALCO) as France-side cybercrime authorities — first wiki record of these specific French federal-cybercrime authorities as lead actors in a transnational cybercrime forum takedown.
  • Europol virtual command post (September 2024) — first wiki record of this discrete IC support mechanism class.
  • Europol mobile office on-site deployment to Kyiv for on-site coordination and evidence collection — structurally similar to the Europol cryptocurrency-expert deployment to Portugal in the September 2025 €100M Eurojust crypto-fraud takedown and the Europol specialist deployment to the Dutch National Police in LabHost takedown.
  • Trusted-third-party arbiter role of the forum administrator — establishes a discrete cybercrime-forum-business-model class in primary IC documentation: the admin not only ran the forum but also arbitrated criminal disputes and guaranteed transaction security for forum users.
  • Multi-platform takedown: xss.is forum + thesecure.biz private messaging service for cybercriminals — both attributed to the same administrator.
