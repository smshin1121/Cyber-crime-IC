주요 발견사항

  • Europol announced Operation Pleiades on 12 January 2016, describing a coordinated 15-16 December 2015 action against the DD4BC (DDoS for Bitcoin) cybercriminal group responsible for Bitcoin extortion campaigns since mid-2014
  • Lead agencies were the Austrian Cybercrime Competence Center (C4) and Bosnia and Herzegovina's Federal Police, supported by the German Bundeskriminalamt and the UK Metropolitan Police, with additional support from Australia, France, Japan, Romania, Switzerland, and the USA (FBI, US Secret Service)
  • {'"Operational outputs': 'one main target arrested and one additional suspect detained, with extensive property searches and substantial evidence seizures during the December 2015 action day"'}
  • DD4BC's modus operandi targeted online gambling operators initially, then expanded to financial services and the entertainment industry; victims received DDoS threats accompanied by Bitcoin ransom demands, with follow-on reputational threats for non-compliance
  • Europol noted that companies paying ransom 'risk appearing vulnerable and being targeted again for a higher amount' — institutional guidance against ransom payment
  • Operation Pleiades is an early (2015-2016) case study of INTERPOL-Europol joint coordination on crypto-extortion and is often cited as precedent for later DDoS-for-ransom enforcement actions

개요Summary

Europol's 12 January 2016 release describes Operation Pleiades, the coordinated 15-16 December 2015 action against the DD4BC ("DDoS for Bitcoin") crypto-extortion group. Lead agencies: Austrian C4 and Bosnia and Herzegovina Federal Police, with German BKA, UK Metropolitan Police, and 6 additional supporting countries plus INTERPOL coordination.

Results: 1 main target arrested, 1 additional suspect detained, property searches, evidence seizures.

DD4BC scheme: Bitcoin-denominated DDoS extortion campaigns starting mid-2014, beginning with online gambling targets then expanding to financial services and entertainment. The group threatened reputational damage in addition to DDoS outages.

Why this source matters

Operation Pleiades is significant as a pre-ransomware-era crypto-extortion case (2015-2016), predating the explosion of encrypt-and-ransom ransomware attacks. It illustrates that cybercrime IC capability to address crypto-ransom predation existed well before ransomware became a top-priority crime type. For operation-pleiades this release provides:

  • Lead-agency identification (Austrian C4, Bosnian Federal Police) — non-standard lead-agency configuration worth noting for SNA analysis
  • Coordinated Europol-INTERPOL workflow — early example of dual-body coordination
  • Official ransom-payment policy language usable on ransomware and extortion-ic challenge pages
