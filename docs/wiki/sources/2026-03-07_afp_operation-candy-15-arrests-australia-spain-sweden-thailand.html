주요 발견사항

  • Operation Candy was led by Swedish authorities (Swedish Police Authority); investigation began November 2023 after Swedish LE seized 2 mobile phones from an alleged drug trafficker.
  • Action day: 4 March 2026, ~20 simultaneous house searches in Spain, Sweden, and Thailand from command posts at several European locations.
  • Total arrests: 15 = 13 (overseas, action day 4 March 2026) + 2 (in Victoria, Australia, prior 2025 phase from German Customs referral).
  • Criminal asset seizures (4 March 2026 phase): cash, watches, jewellery, vehicles and yachts worth EUR 4 million with further tracing pending.
  • Cross-jurisdictional crime architecture exposed via encrypted-message forensic analysis of 2 mobile phones: synthetic drugs networks, large-scale money laundering, online drug distribution to Nordic region (Thailand-side operations), parallel money laundering structures, and Spanish high-value target facilitating large-scale narcotics trafficking.
  • Australia-side track: German Customs detected and seized 1.2 tonnes of illicit drugs in February 2025 destined for Melbourne; Australian law enforcement substituted inert material; surveillance-controlled delivery led to arrests of two men (aged 32 and 52) in Sunshine (Melbourne suburb).
  • Cooperation framework: Sweden + Spain + Thailand + Australia + Germany + Europol + Eurojust, with Eurojust judicial coordination from 5 countries explicitly cited (joint actions, common prosecutorial strategy).
  • Digital evidence chain: 'encrypted communications and online marketplaces' explicitly cited by Europol's Andy Kraag (Head of European Serious and Organised Crime Centre) as the technical attack surface; mobile-phone forensics uplift was the foundational evidence.
  • Tier-1 primary source: AFP media release (https://www.afp.gov.au/news-centre/media-release/international-operation-targets-global-organised-crime-networks-15).

Source Summary

AFP media release dated 7 March 2026 describing Operation Candy — a Swedish-led, ~2.5-year transnational organised crime takedown that began November 2023 with the seizure of 2 mobile phones from an alleged drug trafficker in Sweden, expanded via Europol-supported forensic analysis to expose synthetic-drug-distribution + money-laundering networks across Sweden, Thailand, Spain, Germany, and Australia, and culminated in a 4 March 2026 coordinated action day with ~20 simultaneous house searches in Spain/Sweden/Thailand. Total arrests: 15 (13 overseas action-day + 2 prior Australia-side from a 2025 German Customs referral that intercepted 1.2 tonnes of illicit drugs destined for Melbourne). Criminal asset seizures: EUR 4 million in cash, watches, jewellery, vehicles, and yachts.

The release establishes:

  1. Phone-forensics-first investigative model — small-scale device seizure → Europol-supported forensic analysis → multi-country expansion. Demonstrates the value of even two seized phones when paired with sustained Europol analytical support.
  2. Sweden + Australia + Thailand cooperation track — relatively rare in the wiki's existing corpus (vs. typical EU + US + UK formations).
  3. Eurojust 5-country judicial coordination explicitly cited as essential to any future prosecutions and trials, not just to action-day arrests.
  4. Digital-physical convergence pattern — encrypted communications + online marketplaces enabled the criminal coordination, while physical drug shipments (1.2 tonnes via Germany→Australia) carried the actual harm.

Reliability and Credibility

  • Reliability: high — Tier-1 AFP media release with named officials from Swedish Police Authority, AFP, Victoria Police, ABF, Europol, and Eurojust.
  • Credibility: probably-true — Operational figures (15 arrests, EUR 4M seizures, 1.2t drug interception) corroborated across the named partner agencies; no contradicting source identified.
  • Sensitivity: public — Press release is publicly distributed via AFP Newsroom.
