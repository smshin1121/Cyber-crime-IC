주요 발견사항

  • OFAC designated six individuals (Nguyen Quang Viet, Do Phi Khanh, Hoang Van Nguyen, Yun Song Guk, Hoang Minh Quang, York Louis Celestino Herrera) and two entities (Amnokgang Technology Development Company — DPRK; Quangvietdnbg International Services Company Limited — Vietnam) for facilitating DPRK IT worker fraud schemes targeting U.S. businesses, pursuant to E.O. 13810 (six designations) and E.O. 13382 (two designations: Do and Hoang).
  • Press release attributes nearly USD 800 million in DPRK IT worker revenue in 2024 to the broader scheme — the highest single-year scale figure published by OFAC in this enforcement chain to date and a substantial upward revision from earlier 'hundreds of millions' framings.
  • Documents the Amnokgang (DPRK IT delegation manager) → Nguyen Quang Viet / Quangvietdnbg (Vietnam-based front company providing currency conversion) facilitation pattern: between mid-2023 and mid-2025, Nguyen converted approximately USD 2.5 million into cryptocurrency for North Koreans, including illicit earnings from Amnokgang-associated IT workers. This is the first OFAC designation in the public record naming a Vietnamese national + Vietnam-based front company in the DPRK IT worker monetization chain.
  • Documents a parallel Laos-based facilitation cell: DPRK national Yun Song Guk leading freelance IT workers operating from Boten, Laos, with Hoang Minh Quang coordinating dozens of transactions totaling over USD 70,000 with Yun, and York Louis Celestino Herrera developing freelance IT service contracts for Yun.
  • Confirms Spain as a fourth jurisdictional nexus for DPRK IT worker enforcement (in addition to DPRK, Vietnam, Laos), expanding the geographic footprint named in the U.S. enforcement chain that previously emphasized Russia, China, and Laos.

ℹ️ Source Summary Tier-1 official US Treasury press release (SB-0416) announcing OFAC sanctions on six individuals and two entities across DPRK, Vietnam, Laos, and Spain in the DPRK IT worker fraud enforcement chain. Key Quotes

  • "Today, the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanctioned six individuals and two entities for their roles in Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) government-orchestrated information technology (IT) worker schemes that systematically defraud U.S. businesses and generate revenue to fund the DPRK's weapons of mass destruction (WMD) programs, including nearly $800 million in 2024."
  • "The North Korean regime targets American companies through deceptive schemes carried out by its overseas IT operatives, who weaponize sensitive data and extort businesses for substantial payments." — Secretary of the Treasury Scott Bessent.
  • "OFAC's action today targets several DPRK IT worker networks by designating facilitators based in the DPRK, Vietnam, Laos, and Spain."
  • "Between mid-2023 and mid-2025, Nguyen converted approximately $2.5 million into cryptocurrency for North Koreans, which included converting illicit earnings from IT workers associated with Amnokgang."
