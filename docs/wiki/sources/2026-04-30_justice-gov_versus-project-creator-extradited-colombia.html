주요 발견사항

  • DOJ Press Release 26-428 dated 30 April 2026 announcing the extradition from Colombia to the United States on 29 April 2026 of Patrick Schmitz (37, German national, residing in Taganga, Colombia), a cofounder and day-to-day operator of 'The Versus Project' dark web marketplace.
  • Versus operated about November 2019 through about May 2022 — over 380,000 registered users, 32,000+ product listings, 300,000+ completed orders, millions of dollars in transactions.
  • Product categories: 'drugs,' 'fraud,' 'digital items,' 'services,' and 'software & malware.' Specific items included heroin and other illicit drugs, stolen and fraudulent identification documents and access devices, counterfeit currency, malware, and hacking tools.
  • Payment-rail design: Versus required Bitcoin and Monero only — no fiat — explicitly designed to bypass traditional financial systems' KYC/AML programs.
  • Schmitz's role: cofounder; day-to-day management (user tickets, vendor application review, dispute resolution); recruited and supervised reporting staff; promoted Versus on the dark web; recruited vendors; developed monetization strategies; received profit share; cryptocurrency wallets transacted millions of dollars.
  • Pre-extradition timeline: arrested in Colombia in June 2024 pursuant to a US provisional arrest request; extradited 29 April 2026 (~22-month gap from arrest to extradition).
  • Charges (8 counts): continuing criminal enterprise (20-yr mandatory minimum, max life); narcotics conspiracy (10-yr mandatory minimum, max life); conspiracy to import controlled substances (max 20 yrs); distribution of controlled substances by means of the internet (max 20 yrs); use of a communications facility (max 4 yrs); conspiracy to commit access device fraud (max 10 yrs); conspiracy to unlawfully transfer an identification document (max 15 yrs); money laundering conspiracy (max 20 yrs).
  • Investigative agencies: HSI Newark (lead), FBI Kansas City Field Office, IRS-CI Newark Field Office; arrest/extradition assistance from DOJ Office of the Judicial Attaché in Bogotá, Policía Nacional de Colombia, DOJ Office of International Affairs, and US Marshals Service.
  • Prosecution: Trial Attorneys Jorge Gonzalez and Stefanie Schwartz of CCIPS; AUSA Andrew Kogan of the Cybercrime Unit of USAO-NJ.
  • Initiative attribution: Operation Take Back America (OCDETF + PSN umbrella) and JCODE (Joint Criminal Opioid Darknet Enforcement) — the FBI-led darknet-opioid task force.
  • Tier-1 primary source: DOJ-OPA press release (https://www.justice.gov/opa/pr/leading-dark-web-marketplace-creator-and-operator-extradited-colombia-united-states).

Source Summary

DOJ-OPA Press Release 26-428 dated 30 April 2026 announcing the extradition of Patrick Schmitz (37, German national, residing in Taganga, Colombia) from Colombia to the United States on 29 April 2026 on an 8-count indictment in the District of New Jersey for cofounding and operating 'The Versus Project' dark web marketplace. Versus operated November 2019 through May 2022 with 380,000+ registered users, 32,000+ product listings, and 300,000+ completed orders generating millions of dollars in cryptocurrency transactions across product categories spanning drugs (heroin), fraud, digital items, services, and "software & malware" (including hacking tools).

The release establishes: 1. A 22-month US-Colombia extradition timeline (arrest June 2024 in Colombia → extradition 29 April 2026), illustrating the practical pace of US-Colombia cybercrime extradition cooperation. 2. The Bitcoin + Monero exclusive payment rail as a deliberate KYC/AML bypass — a recurring design pattern in dark web marketplaces (Archetyp used a similar approach). 3. The HSI Newark + FBI Kansas City + IRS-CI Newark + DOJ-OIA + US Marshals + Policía Nacional de Colombia stack as the operational template for US-Colombia darknet-marketplace cybercrime extraditions. 4. Continuing criminal enterprise (CCE) charging — a felony tier reserved for senior dark web marketplace principals — applied alongside narcotics, fraud, and money-laundering conspiracy counts.
