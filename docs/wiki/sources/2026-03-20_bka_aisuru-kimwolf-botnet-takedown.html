주요 발견사항

  • ZAC NRW (Zentral- und Ansprechstelle Cybercrime Nordrhein-Westfalen) and BKA carried out an internationally coordinated action on 19 March 2026 'gemeinsam mit Strafverfolgungsbehoerden aus Kanada und den USA' (together with law enforcement authorities from Canada and the United States) against two of the world's currently largest IoT botnets.
  • Botnets disrupted: Aisuru (multi-million IoT devices: routers, webcams) and Kimwolf (multi-million Android TV boxes; also rented as residential-proxy network).
  • Two suspected administrators identified; searches conducted at residences in Germany and Canada; numerous data carriers and cryptocurrency in five-digit EUR range seized.
  • Federal Office for Information Security (BSI) provided sinkholing of the perpetrator infrastructure.
  • US-side corroboration (DOJ/FBI, 19-20 March 2026) names a broader set — Aisuru, Kimwolf, JackSkid, and Mossad — with DCIS leading the US investigation supported by FBI Anchorage; US release attributes >300,000 DDoS attack commands across the four botnets, with Aisuru alone issuing >200,000.

개요Summary

Press release jointly issued by the German Federal Criminal Police Office (Bundeskriminalamt, BKA) and the Central and Contact Point Cybercrime North Rhine-Westphalia (Zentral- und Ansprechstelle Cybercrime Nordrhein-Westfalen, ZAC NRW), dated 20 March 2026, announcing a Germany-Canada-United States internationally coordinated 19 March 2026 action that disrupted the technical infrastructure of the Aisuru and Kimwolf IoT botnets and identified two administrators with searches in Germany and Canada. This is the canonical tier-1 primary source for the German leg of the operation; the corroborating US Department of Justice / FBI announcement covers the same operational date and adds the JackSkid and Mossad botnets to the disrupted set.
