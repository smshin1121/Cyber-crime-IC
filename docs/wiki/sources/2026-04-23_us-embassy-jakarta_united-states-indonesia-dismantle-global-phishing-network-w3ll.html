주요 발견사항

  • U.S. Embassy Jakarta press release (2026-04-23) confirms the W3LL phishing kit / W3LLSTORE takedown as a multi-year joint investigation between the FBI Atlanta Field Office and the Indonesian National Police (INP), characterised as a 'first-of-its-kind joint cyber investigation between the United States and Indonesia' targeting a phishing kit developer.
  • Joint announcement made at a press conference at INP Criminal Investigation Division (Bareskrim Polri) headquarters in Jakarta on Wednesday, 2026-04-22 — one day before the U.S. Embassy press release publication.
  • Named US officials on the record: FBI Atlanta Special Agent in Charge Marlo Graham (statement issued); FBI Representative Robert Lafferty (present at the Jakarta press conference, on-record quote about cooperation choreography).
  • Cooperation choreography (verbatim from FBI Representative Lafferty): 'The success of the takedown relied on the coordinated execution between cyber investigators from FBI Atlanta, INP Bareskrim, and East Nusa Tanggera (Kupang Polri),' with FBI monitoring 'digital footprints and traced financial flows within the United States' and INP conducting 'critical on-the-ground operation to locate the operation's mastermind and collect critical digital evidence.'
  • Sub-national Indonesian unit identified for the first time in tier-1 reporting: Kupang Polri (East Nusa Tenggara provincial police) — adds a sub-national operational dimension to the previously identified Bareskrim-HQ-level Indonesian role.
  • Suspect G.L. — apprehended by the INP — and search warrants executed by the INP resulted in the seizure of assets and hardware containing evidence of thousands of global targets. Scale figures align with the FBI Atlanta 2026-04-10 release: W3LLSTORE 2019-2023 facilitated sale of 25,000+ compromised accounts and unauthorised system accesses; 2023-2024 the phishing kit targeted 17,000+ victims across nearly every continent; total attempted fraud > USD 20 million (~ Rp 350 billion stated in the release as the Indonesian-Rupiah equivalent).
  • Publisher (id.usembassy.gov) is a U.S. Department of State official .gov subdomain — tier-1 own-domain US federal government publisher, distinct from fbi.gov, providing a second independent tier-1 own-domain attestation of the W3LL takedown cooperation (FBI Atlanta release was the first).

Source Summary

Tier-1 English-language press release issued by the U.S. Embassy Jakarta (Embassy of the United States in Indonesia, U.S. Department of State) on 2026-04-23, reporting the joint dismantlement of the W3LL phishing kit / W3LLSTORE marketplace by the FBI Atlanta Field Office and the Indonesian National Police (INP). The release describes a 2026-04-22 press conference at Bareskrim Polri headquarters in Jakarta where the takedown was announced jointly by the two LE bodies, and provides on-record statements from FBI Atlanta SAC Marlo Graham and FBI Representative Robert Lafferty that explicitly characterise the cooperation as "first-of-its-kind" and detail the division of operational labour (US: digital footprint monitoring and financial-flow tracing; Indonesia: on-the-ground mastermind apprehension and digital evidence collection). The Indonesian sub-national unit Kupang Polri (East Nusa Tenggara provincial police) is named for the first time as a participating agency alongside Bareskrim HQ.

Relevance to IC

This is the second independent tier-1 own-domain attestation of the W3LL phishing-kit takedown, complementing the FBI Atlanta Field Office press release of 2026-04-10 already in the wiki (<a href="../../wiki/sources/2026-04-10_fbi-atlanta_global-phishing-network-takedown-w3ll.html" class="wikilink">2026-04-10_fbi-atlanta_global-phishing-network-takedown-w3ll</a>). It enriches the wiki's reading of the w3ll-phishing-kit-takedown-2026 operation in five concrete ways:

  1. Independent US own-domain confirmation — id.usembassy.gov is operated by the U.S. Department of State, not by the FBI; the two US-side sources are therefore distinct own-domain attestations (DOJ/FBI-side investigative narrative vs. State Department diplomatic-relationship narrative) of the same takedown. This satisfies the wiki's preference for tier-1 own-domain corroboration without resorting to private aggregators or media wires.

  2. First-of-its-kind framing. The release characterises the W3LL takedown as a 'first-of-its-kind joint cyber investigation between the United States and Indonesia' targeting a phishing kit developer — corroborating the lessons-learned note already on the operation page that this was the first US-Indonesia coordinated action against a phishing kit developer.

  3. Sub-national Indonesian unit named. The release adds Kupang Polri (East Nusa Tenggara provincial police) to the cooperation roster. This is significant because the prior FBI Atlanta release named only "Indonesian National Police" / Polri at the national level; the U.S. Embassy release attributes the on-the-ground mastermind apprehension to a specific sub-national unit — a level of organisational specificity that allows the wiki to record the Indonesian provincial dimension of cross-border phishing-kit-developer apprehension.

  4. Named US officials on the record. FBI Atlanta SAC Marlo Graham (issuer of the 'full-service cybercrime platform' characterisation) and FBI Representative Robert Lafferty (on-record at the Jakarta press conference describing the division of operational labour) are both named, allowing personal attribution of the cooperation framing that was previously attributable only to "FBI Atlanta" institutionally.

  5. Diplomatic-channel framing. The release is published by the State Department arm rather than the LE arm of the U.S. side, indicating that the US W3LL takedown was elevated to a US-Indonesia bilateral-diplomatic talking point — relevant for the wiki's US country profile and Indonesia country profile bilateral-cooperation track records.

L24 verbatim quote (from FBI Representative Lafferty, U.S. Embassy Jakarta release 2026-04-23):

While FBI monitored digital footprints and traced financial flows within the United States, the INP conducted critical on-the-ground operation to locate the operation's mastermind and collect critical digital evidence."
