Source Summary

KNPA press release (정책브리핑 newsId 156740857, dated 2026-04-27) announcing the extradition to Korea of 73 Korean-national online-scam suspects from Cambodia (42) and the Philippines (31) over a roughly two-month operation period beginning 2026-02-26. The release breaks down the cohort by crime type — voice phishing (24, 368 victims, ~₩51.7B), romance scam (14, 53 victims, ~₩2.3B), illegal gambling-site operation (3, ~₩5.9T operating scale), 2011 capital-firm data breach (1, ~1.75 million records), and 2022–2024 auto-loan fraud (3, ~₩12B) — and identifies the cooperating partners as 경찰청, 외교부, 법무부, 국가정보원, the Cambodian National Police, and the Philippine National Police, working through the whole-government 「초국가범죄 특별대응TF」 task force and the Korea-only field unit known as 「코리아전담반」 in Cambodia. 박준성 경찰청 국제치안협력국장(직무대리) is named as a spokesperson; 69 of the 73 suspects are in custody on remand orders. The release explicitly characterises the operation as the largest single-country cybercrime extradition into Korea on record.

Relevance to IC

This is the principal tier-1 Korean-language source for the 2026 Korea–Cambodia–Philippines whole-government extradition operation. It supplies (a) Korea-as-requesting-state extradition flows from two named jurisdictions in the same announcement, (b) the rare multi-agency Korean cooperation pattern (KNPA + 외교부 + 법무부 + NIS) coordinated through a named whole-government task force, (c) explicit reference to a Korea-side operational construct (코리아전담반) as a field-deployed Korean police unit operating inside Cambodia, (d) a multi-crime-type cohort in a single extradition cycle (voice phishing, romance scam, gambling, data breach, auto-loan fraud, sex exploitation), and (e) the largest-on-record framing for the public Korean IC corpus.
