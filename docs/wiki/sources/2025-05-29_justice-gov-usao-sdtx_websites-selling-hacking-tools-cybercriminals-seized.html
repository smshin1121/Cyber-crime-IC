Source Summary

USAO Southern District of Texas (Houston) press release dated 2025-05-29 (Updated 2025-05-30) announcing the coordinated international seizure of four domains and their associated server constituting an "online software crypting syndicate" providing Counter-Antivirus (CAV) tools to cybercriminals. The seizures occurred on 2025-05-27 under a U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Texas seizure warrant, in coordination with Finnish and Dutch national police as part of Operation Endgame. Investigation is led by the FBI Houston Field Office under SAC Douglas Williams; prosecutors of record are AUSAs Shirin Hakimzadeh and Rodolfo Ramirez (lead) and Kristine Rollinson (seizure aspects), with the U.S. Attorney of record being Nicholas J. Ganjei.

Relevance to IC

This is the U.S. tier-1 own-domain anchor for the AvCheck CAV/crypter takedown operation — the May 2025 leg of the operation that the OM.nl 12 January 2026 Schiphol-arrest release explicitly references as the upstream investigation. The press release supplies (a) the named cooperating jurisdictions (United States, The Netherlands, Finland, plus additional Operation Endgame participants France, Germany, Denmark, with support from Ukraine and Portugal), (b) the verbatim acknowledgement that "The FBI Houston Field Office is conducting the investigation with the cooperation and significant assistance of law enforcement partners in The Netherlands and Finland and U.S. Secret Service" — satisfying L24's ≥2-LE-cooperation requirement, (c) the SDTX-warrant legal basis, and (d) the broader Operation Endgame umbrella framing.

The release does not name the four seized domains in its body text, only describing the seized service category generically. Per externally consistent reporting (BleepingComputer, The Record, The Hacker News, Security Affairs, TechRadar, Bitdefender, CyberScoop), the four domains are AvCheck.net, Cryptor.biz, Cryptor.live, and Crypt.guru — recorded in the operation page as tier-2 attribution.
