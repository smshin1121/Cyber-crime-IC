주요 발견사항

  • Italian Polizia di Stato officially confirms the extradition of a Chinese national hacker (referred to as X. Z., consistent with US DOJ-named Xu Zewei in the parallel US prosecution case) to United States authorities, executed on the basis of a decision by the Court of Appeal of Milan (Corte d'Appello di Milano) and the Italian Minister of Justice.
  • Identifies four distinct Italian agencies/authorities in the operation: (1) Polizia Postale (Postal Police, the cyber unit), (2) Polaria di Malpensa (Border Police at Malpensa airport, who executed the original July 2025 arrest), (3) Servizio per la Cooperazione Internazionale di Polizia (Service for International Police Cooperation, which coordinated the extradition handover), (4) Corte d'Appello di Milano + Ministro della giustizia (judicial-political extradition authorisation chain).
  • Confirms the arrest happened 'agli inizi di luglio del 2025' (early July 2025) at Malpensa airport, with the suspect intercepted alongside his wife while purportedly preparing for a holiday — providing the Italian primary-source corroboration of the location-and-circumstances narrative previously documented from the US DOJ side.
  • Frames the operation as 'una lunga collaborazione operativa tra FBI e gli investigatori della polizia italiana' (a long operational collaboration between the FBI and Italian police investigators), characterising the case as the culmination of bilateral US-Italy law-enforcement cooperation rather than a one-off action.
  • Italian charges-frame summary lists 'associazione per delinquere, accesso abusivo a sistemi informatici, frode informatica e furto d'identità' (criminal conspiracy, unauthorized access to computer systems, computer fraud, identity theft) — closely mirroring the US 9-count indictment categories (wire-fraud conspiracy, unauthorized access to protected computers, identity theft) and providing dual-criminality cross-reference for the extradition.

개요Summary

This is the Italian primary-source press release for the Italy → US extradition of the PRC-national HAFNIUM-campaign hacker (referred to as "X. Z." in the Italian source for presumption-of-innocence reasons; corresponds to Xu Zewei as named in the parallel US DOJ release of the same date). It is the partner-state perspective on the operation already documented from the US side in US v. Xu Zewei et al. and in the corresponding US DOJ press release.

The Italian release adds:

  1. The full chain of Italian agencies/authorities involved in the arrest and extradition (previously only summarised as "Italian National Police — Cyber Division" in the US DOJ release).
  2. Confirmation of the judicial decision pathway: Corte d'Appello di Milano + Ministro della giustizia, which is the standard Italian extradition authorisation chain.
  3. Confirmation of the arrest scene specifics: airport interception, suspect with his wife, claimed holiday purpose.
  4. Italian-law framing of the underlying offences: associazione per delinquere, accesso abusivo a sistemi informatici, frode informatica, furto d'identità — relevant to dual-criminality analysis for any future extradition challenge.

The release does not add new substantive facts about the underlying HAFNIUM intrusions beyond what the US DOJ release already disclosed (Powerock, MSS Shanghai State Security Bureau, Phase 1 COVID-19 research targeting, Phase 2 Microsoft Exchange compromise of 12,700+ US organisations) — those operational details remain in the US case page. The Italian release is the bilateral-cooperation operational record.
