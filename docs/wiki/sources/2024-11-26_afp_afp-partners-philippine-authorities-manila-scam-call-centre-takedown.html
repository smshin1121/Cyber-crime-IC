AFP partners with Philippine authorities in combating scam call centres (Manila, 26 November 2024)

개요Summary

Tier-1 primary source: Australian Federal Police official news-centre press release dated 26 November 2024, announcing the joint takedown of a Manila-based scam call centre by the Philippine Presidential Anti-Organized Crime Commission (PAOCC) and the Philippine National Bureau of Investigation (NBI), with intelligence, victim-identification, technical and cryptocurrency-tracing support provided by the AFP through its Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3) under Operation Firestorm.

The release explicitly describes this as "the first significant joint takedown of a Philippine scam compound under Operation Firestorm" — the operation was launched by the AFP-led JPC3 in August 2024 to disrupt offshore Southeast-Asian scam centres targeting Australians. A subsequent AFP press release of 1 April 2025 confirms that this Manila takedown was the first of three Manila scam-centre disruptions under Operation Firestorm in the period August 2024 – April 2025.

L24 cooperating-LE quote

"We commend the PAOCC and NBI for their work in identifying this boiler room and taking swift action to shut it down. I thank them for the trust they have shown in the AFP by allowing us to work with them." — AFP Assistant Commissioner David McLean (Cybercrime Operations), 26 November 2024

This quote names two foreign-LE counterpart agencies — the Philippine Presidential Anti-Organized Crime Commission (PAOCC) and the Philippine National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) — both as cooperating bodies, distinct from the AFP. Per L24, this is a two-jurisdiction IC operation with explicit foreign-LE acknowledgement in the tier-1 primary source.

Cooperation structure

Role Agency Jurisdiction
On-site lead (raid execution, suspect arrests, prosecution) PAOCC Philippines
On-site investigative lead (cybercrime evidence, computer forensics) NBI Cyber Crime Division Philippines
Intelligence, victim-identification, cryptocurrency-tracing, JPC3 framework Australian Federal Police (AFP) Australia

PAOCC and NBI Cyber Crime Division are acknowledged in the AFP statement but do not yet have wiki entity pages. Per L23, they are referenced in body prose only and not used in lead_agency / coordinating_body frontmatter on the operation page.

Why this matters

  • Establishes the structural template for the AFP–Philippines cooperation channel, which has subsequently produced two further Manila scam-centre takedowns and one Bangkok scam-centre takedown (afp-rtp-bangkok-scam-centre-operation-firestorm-2025) under the same Operation Firestorm umbrella.
  • Documents the AFP cryptocurrency-tracing capability deployed to identify Australian victim flows to non-Philippine jurisdictions — a recurring IC pattern in Southeast-Asian scam-compound enforcement.
  • Provides the first wiki record of PAOCC and NBI Cyber Crime Division as named Philippine partner agencies for AFP cybercrime cooperation; future ingest cycles can promote these to dedicated organization pages once additional sources accumulate.
