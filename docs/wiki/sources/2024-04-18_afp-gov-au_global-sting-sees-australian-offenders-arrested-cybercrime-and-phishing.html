주요 발견사항

  • Australian arm of the international LabHost takedown codenamed Operation Nebulae, led by the AFP Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3).
  • 5 Australian arrests on 2024-04-17: 1 Melbourne man + 1 Adelaide man charged with cybercrime offences as alleged LabHost users; 3 additional Melbourne men charged with drug-related offences during the warrants.
  • 22 Australian search warrants executed across five states: 14 Victoria, 2 Queensland, 3 NSW, 1 South Australia, 2 Western Australia. >200 AFP/state/territory officers involved.
  • 207 criminal servers taken down by JPC3 in the Australian operation.
  • More than 100 LabHost-using suspects identified in Australia targeting Australian victims.
  • More than 94,000 Australians targeted via LabHost phishing campaigns; Australian harm estimated at AUD 28 million by AFP Acting Assistant Commissioner Chris Goldsmid.
  • Corroborates Europol global figures: 70 simultaneous search warrants worldwide, 37 arrests globally including 4 UK-based individuals running the site (one being the LabHost original developer).
  • Confirms LabHost subscription as 'as little as $270 per month' with phishing kits + hosting + email/text content + campaign overview services; >40,000 phishing domains; >10,000 global users; 170+ fraudulent website templates.
  • Geographic origin attribution: 'LabHost originated in Canada in 2021, targeting North America, and expanded to the United Kingdom (UK) and Ireland, before going global. Australian criminals are believed to be among its top three user countries.'
  • Joint release: AFP + Victoria Police + Queensland Police Service + NSW Police Force + WA Police Force — explicit Five Eyes / Australian-federal-state IC pattern at action-phase enforcement level.

Source Summary

AFP (afp.gov.au) media release dated 2024-04-18, jointly issued with four Australian state police forces, announcing the Australian component of the international LabHost phishing-as-a-service takedown (action days 2024-04-14 to 2024-04-17). The Australian operation, codenamed Operation Nebulae, was led by the AFP's Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3) and resulted in 5 arrests, 22 search warrants across five states, the takedown of 207 criminal servers, and identification of 100+ Australian LabHost users targeting more than 94,000 Australian victims.
