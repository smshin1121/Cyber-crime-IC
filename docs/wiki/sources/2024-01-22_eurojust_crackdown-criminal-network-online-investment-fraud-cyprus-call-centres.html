주요 발견사항

  • Eurojust-supported coordinated action on 22 January 2024 dismantled a criminal network operating fraudulent online investment trading platforms from call centres in Cyprus, with four suspects arrested across Germany, Cyprus, Bulgaria, and Sweden.
  • Authorities verbatim: Germany (Public Prosecutor's Office of Göttingen; Centre for Cybercrime, Police Göttingen); Bulgaria (Sofia City Public Prosecutor's Office; General Directorate Bulgarian National Police); Cyprus (International Police Cooperation Sub-Directorate - Police Cooperation Bureau Cyprus Police); Sweden (Swedish Police Authority).
  • Reported damages of EUR 3.3 million and EUR 164,000 from two main platforms targeting German victims; total European damages believed to be substantially higher.

Source Summary

This Eurojust press release of 22 January 2024 announces a coordinated action day, supported by Eurojust, against a criminal network that operated fraudulent online investment platforms from call centres in Cyprus. Acting on the same day, authorities in Germany, Cyprus, Bulgaria, and Sweden arrested four suspects and conducted searches.

The release explicitly enumerates the cooperating authorities by name, providing strong tier-1 evidence of multilateral law-enforcement and prosecutorial cooperation across four EU jurisdictions.
