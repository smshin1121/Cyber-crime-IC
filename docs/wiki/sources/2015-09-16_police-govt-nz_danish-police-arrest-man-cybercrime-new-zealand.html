주요 발견사항

  • Operation Cavern is a joint cybercrime investigation between the New Zealand Police, the Danish National Cyber Crime Centre (NC3, organisationally within Denmark NC3 / National Special Crime Unit), and South-East Jutland Police, that culminated in the September 2015 arrest in Vejle, Denmark of a 24-year-old Danish citizen for online posting of private photos and hacking of private computers under Danish law, with an Auckland school girl as the named victim.
  • The cited New Zealand Police press release contains explicit verbatim attribution of multi-country IC cooperation: Danish prosecutor Niels Tipsmark of South-East Jutland Police characterises the case as a 'close cooperation between South-East Jutland Police, NC3, and the New Zealand Police' that 'has been smooth, mutual and fruitful' with 'a country on the other side of the world.' NZ Detective Senior Sergeant Cliff Clark of the NZ Police Cybercrime Unit confirms the case 'demonstrated the value of effective co-operation with overseas law enforcement partners.'
  • Operation Cavern is one of the early Asia-Pacific / Northern-European bilateral cybercrime IC datapoints in the wiki corpus — a 2015-vintage joint investigation where the suspect, victim, and offending jurisdiction are deliberately separated across hemispheres (suspect+offending+arrest jurisdiction = Denmark; victim+investigation-initiating jurisdiction = New Zealand), demonstrating the cooperation pattern used when domestic cybercrime offending is conducted from a foreign safe-haven jurisdiction with no fugitive-extradition dimension.

Source Summary

New Zealand Police news release dated Wednesday 16 September 2015 (5:12 pm) announcing the arrest in Vejle, Denmark of a 24-year-old Danish citizen as part of Operation Cavern — a joint investigation between the New Zealand Police, the Danish National Cyber Crime Centre (NC3), and South-East Jutland Police. The suspect was charged under Danish law with cybercrimes relating to the online posting of private photos and the hacking of private computers. The named victim is an Auckland (NZ) school girl. Further case details were withheld at the time of the release due to closed-doors judicial hearings in Denmark.

Relevance to IC

This is the principal tier-1 own-domain source for the 2015-09 Operation Cavern joint investigation. It supplies (a) the explicit named cooperation between NZ Police and Danish authorities (NC3 and South-East Jutland Police), (b) the verbatim prosecutor-on-the-record attribution of "close, smooth, mutual, fruitful" multi-country cybercrime IC, (c) a clean 2-jurisdiction, 3-LE-agency template for the domestic-victim, foreign-perpetrator cooperation pattern in the absence of extradition (the suspect is a Danish citizen prosecuted in Denmark under Danish law, with NZ supplying the investigative lead and evidence), and (d) one of the earliest tier-1 NZ Police own-domain cybercrime cooperation releases in the wiki corpus.

Cooperation pattern note

Operation Cavern is structurally distinct from later Europol-coordinated takedowns: it is a 2-country bilateral investigation with no intergovernmental coordinating body named (no Europol, no Eurojust, no INTERPOL credit in the release), and with no extradition step (the Danish citizen suspect is prosecuted in Denmark). This is the "remote-victim domestic-prosecution" pattern for cybercrime, distinguishable from the more common "foreign-suspect extradition-to-victim-jurisdiction" pattern.

Note on text fidelity

Full body verbatim captured via WebFetch from police.govt.nz. The release deliberately withholds the suspect's name, the victim's name and exact age, the manner of hacking, and the nature of the photos posted, citing closed-doors judicial proceedings in Denmark at the time of publication.
