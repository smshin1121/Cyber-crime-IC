개요Summary

Academic paper by Naeem AllahRakha (Tashkent State University of Law) examining how international legal frameworks — particularly the Budapest Convention on Cybercrime — address cross-border investigations. Published February 4, 2025 in ADLIYA: Jurnal Hukum dan Kemanusiaan.

The paper applies doctrinal research methodology to analyze cybercrime regulations and identifies four major obstacles to effective international cooperation:

  1. Fragmented legal systems — current frameworks for collecting and sharing digital evidence across jurisdictions remain fragmented, limiting practical cooperation
  2. Procedural delays — MLA requests in cybercrime cases experience slowdowns due to coordination issues and procedural bottlenecks
  3. Technical and linguistic barriers — language differences, conflicting legal systems, and inadequate technical capacity hamper investigations
  4. Cloud evidence gaps — significant obstacles exist for lawful access to electronic evidence, particularly with cloud-based platforms

The author concludes there is an "urgent need for a coordinated and standardised global strategy" beyond the Budapest Convention.
