주요 발견사항

  • On 9 March 2026 the Polícia Federal in São Paulo arrested a Belgian citizen residing in Brazil on a Brazilian Supreme Federal Court (STF) arrest warrant issued for extradition purposes (mandado de prisão para extradição), in response to a Belgian extradition request.
  • The Belgian charges underlying the extradition request, as enumerated by PF, include falsificação de documentos (document forgery), ameaça (criminal threat), fraude (fraud), falsificação de tecnologia da informação (information-technology forgery — computer-related forgery), abuso de confiança (breach of trust), and unspecified additional offences. The IT-forgery charge places the case squarely within Budapest Convention Art. 7 territory.
  • The defendant is identified only as a cidadão belga residente no Brasil with prior criminal incidents in Brazil; no name is disclosed in the public release.
  • Procedurally, the case is a passive extradition (extradição passiva) under Brazilian law, with STF jurisdiction (Brazilian Constitution Art. 102, I, 'g'). The defendant is in Brazilian custody pending the STF extradition proceeding.
  • The PF release does NOT name (a) the Belgian counterpart authority, (b) the bilateral or multilateral treaty basis (Brazil-Belgium 1953 extradition treaty; Brazilian Lei de Migração 13.445/2017 Title VIII; Budapest Convention Chapter III), (c) the Belgian Code pénal articles charged, or (d) whether an Interpol Red Notice (Difusão Vermelha) preceded the arrest. These are item-nonresponse fields.

Source Summary

Official press release issued on 2026-03-09 by the Comunicação Social da Polícia Federal em São Paulo (SCS/SR/SP) announcing the arrest of a Belgian national in São Paulo on a Brazilian Supreme Federal Court (STF) arrest warrant for extradition purposes, in response to a Belgian extradition request. The Belgian underlying charges enumerated by PF include document forgery, criminal threat, fraud, information-technology forgery (falsificação de tecnologia da informação), breach of trust, and unspecified additional offences.

This places the case in the cybercrime international cooperation domain because the IT-forgery charge corresponds to what the Council of Europe Budapest Convention classifies as computer-related forgery (Article 7) — a Convention to which both Brazil (acceded 2023) and Belgium (Party since 2012) are bound.

📝 Translation note Falsificação de tecnologia da informação is not a Brazilian Penal Code charge category — it is the PF's translation of the Belgian charge, which under Belgian law most likely corresponds to falsification informatique / informaticavalsheid (Art. 210bis Code pénal / Strafwetboek). This in turn maps to computer-related forgery (Art. 7) in the Budapest Convention. The PF wording is functionally equivalent but is not a Brazilian statutory term, so the Brazilian counterpart offence in the dual-criminality assessment (see dual-criminality) would be analysed under Lei 12.737/2012 (Lei Carolina Dieckmann) and Penal Code Arts. 154-A/154-B (computer-data invasion) plus Art. 298 (document forgery).
