주요 발견사항

  • KNPA hosts the 2nd International Cooperation Operations Meeting (작전명 Breaking Chains / 브레이킹 체인스) in Seoul on 2026-02-05–06, attended by 22 country law-enforcement agencies plus 5 international organisations (INTERPOL, ASEANAPOL, AFRIPOL, IOM, UNODC). KNPA frames this as a Korea-led multilateral platform against transnational scam crime.
  • The press release reports cumulative outcomes from the 1st meeting cycle (held 2025-11): 26 cases and 75 tracking leads exchanged; 5 joint enforcement actions concluded against organisations based in Vietnam/China/etc., yielding 31 suspect arrests and 15 repatriations to Korea; plus a Cambodia-based 'female-matching' fraud organisation arrest of 15 (Phase-1 Breaking Chains operation, 2025-12-04, ~₩2.589B losses, 27 victims) and a separate Cambodia-Thailand human-trafficking ring leader arrest tied to a Korean-national victim murder case in Cambodia.
  • Phase-1 Breaking Chains operational outcome (2025-12-04, edaily 2025-12-05): 28 total arrests across two organisations — Cambodia 15 (Poipet, Thai border, female-matching fraud, ~₩2.589B / 27 victims; intelligence from 충남경찰청 형사기동대) and Thailand 13 (Bangkok office raid, voice-phishing impersonating prosecutors/FSS, ~₩100M, with sextortion-style image demands; intelligence from 서울경찰청 금융범죄수사대).
  • 2nd meeting agenda includes 13 countries running bilateral/multilateral coordination conferences sharing 45 cases and 80 priority tracking leads, plus a 'balloon-effect' (풍선효과) discussion track on organisations relocating to evade enforcement.
  • Named senior officials: 박성주 경찰청 국가수사본부장 (KNPA National Investigation Headquarters Commissioner) for the 2nd meeting; 이재영 경찰청 국제협력관 (KNPA International Cooperation Officer) on Phase-1 results.
  • Operational link to the wider Korean whole-of-government scam-compound architecture: 대통령실 '초국가범죄 특별대응 전담팀(TF)' (Presidential Office Transnational Crime Special Response TF) is named in the body as the policy structure that combined with the 1st-meeting intelligence-sharing to produce real arrests; 코리아전담반 (Korea-dedicated Squad in Cambodia) is the operational unit named for the 15-arrest Cambodia tranche (also referenced in korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026).

Source Summary

KNPA press release (정책브리핑 newsId 156743085, dated 2026-02-05) announcing the 2nd Korea-hosted International Cooperation Operations Meeting under the operation codename Breaking Chains (브레이킹 체인스), convened in Seoul over 2026-02-05–06 with 22 country-level law-enforcement agencies and 5 international organisations — INTERPOL, ASEANAPOL, AFRIPOL, the International Organization for Migration (IOM), and the UN Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC). The release describes Breaking Chains as a Korea-led ("韓 주도") multilateral operational platform against transnational scam crime — including scam compounds (스캠 센터), human trafficking (인신매매), and online fraud (온라인 사기) — and reports cumulative results from the 1st meeting cycle: 26 cases / 75 tracking leads exchanged; 31 arrests + 15 Korea-bound repatriations across Vietnam-China-based scam operations; a Cambodia-based female-matching fraud takedown (15 arrests; 27 victims; ~₩2.589 B losses); and a Cambodia–Thailand human-trafficking ring leader arrest tied to a Korean-national victim murder case in Cambodia. The 2nd meeting agenda includes 13 countries running bilateral/multilateral conferences with 45 cases and 80 priority tracking leads, plus an explicit 'balloon-effect' (풍선효과) discussion track. 박성주 (NIH Commissioner) speaks for the 2nd meeting; 이재영 (International Cooperation Officer) speaks for the Phase-1 takedown announcement.

Relevance to IC

This is a tier-1 Korean-language primary source for a Korea-as-coordinator multilateral cybercrime cooperation pattern that is materially distinct from the Korea-as-requesting-state extradition pattern already documented in the wiki (korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026, korea-cambodia-scam-repatriation). Specifically the source establishes:

  1. A Korean-hosted standing multilateral cooperation forum ("국제공조 작전회의") with a named operational codename ("Breaking Chains" / 브레이킹 체인스), structured as a meeting series (1st: 2025-11; 2nd: 2026-02-05) plus interleaved field operations (Phase-1: 2025-12-04 Cambodia + Thailand).
  2. Five-international-organisation participation in a Korean-hosted forum (INTERPOL, ASEANAPOL, AFRIPOL, IOM, UNODC), notably extending Korean-coordinated cooperation into the African (AFRIPOL) and migration-focused (IOM) institutional spaces beyond the Asia-Pacific frame more typical of prior Korean cybercrime IC.
  3. Three cooperation-by-mechanism types in a single source: (i) information sharing and tracking-lead exchange (75 → 80 leads), (ii) joint enforcement / coordinated raid (Cambodia 15 + Thailand 13 + 31 prior arrests = 59 cumulative), (iii) repatriation/extradition into Korea (15 returned in Phase-1 prior arrests; 1 trafficker total head arrested for Korean-national victim case).
  4. An explicit balloon-effect (풍선효과) policy track — i.e., recognition that strengthened cooperation in one corridor relocates organisations to weaker-enforcement jurisdictions — which is rare in primary press releases and useful for the wiki's challenges frame.
  5. Linkage to the wider Korean whole-of-government scam-compound architecture — the 대통령실 '초국가범죄 특별대응 TF' is named as the coordinating Korean policy structure, and the 코리아전담반 (Korea-dedicated Squad) is named as the field operational unit, providing a rare cross-source bridge to korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026.
