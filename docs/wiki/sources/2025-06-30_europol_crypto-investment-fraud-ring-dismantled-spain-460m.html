Source Summary

Europol newsroom press release dated 2025-06-30 reporting the 25 June 2025 action day in Spain (Canary Islands and Madrid) that dismantled a cryptocurrency investment fraud network. The release names a four-jurisdiction cooperation chain — Spain, Estonia, France (New Caledonia gendarmerie), and the United States — with Europol coordination since 2023 and on-day deployment of a Europol crypto specialist. Headline financial figure: EUR 460 million laundered, 5,000+ victims worldwide. Action-day outcomes: 5 arrests and 5 searches (3 Canary Islands, 2 Madrid). The release also identifies a Hong Kong-based corporate-and-banking layer used by the network for fund laundering through payment gateways and third-party exchange accounts.

Relevance to IC

This is the principal tier-1 primary source for a 2025 four-jurisdiction crypto investment fraud takedown that combines (a) EU member-state cooperation (Spain + Estonia) with (b) French overseas-territory gendarmerie participation (New Caledonia) and (c) US federal involvement via DHS Homeland Security Investigations, all coordinated by Europol. The HSI participation is a notable instance of US involvement in an EU-led crypto fraud action that is not framed as an extradition or sanctions case — it documents an EU-coordinated investigative-cooperation pathway involving HSI's financial-crime mandate. The Hong Kong corporate-and-banking layer is the cross-jurisdictional money-laundering pattern of interest, although Hong Kong is not named as a participating jurisdiction in the action day.
