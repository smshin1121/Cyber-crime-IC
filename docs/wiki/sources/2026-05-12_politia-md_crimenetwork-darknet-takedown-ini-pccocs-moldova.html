주요 발견사항

  • Moldovan national police (politia.md / IGP–INI Cybercrime Centre) and PCCOCS prosecutors publicly confirm joint participation with Germany, Spanish Police, and EUROJUST in dismantling the relaunched 'Crimenetwork' darknet platform, published 12 May 2026.
  • Release explicitly states INI officers were part of a Joint Investigation Group (grupul comun de investigație), confirming Moldova's role as an active LE/judicial cooperation partner rather than merely a hosting jurisdiction.
  • Confirms platform technical infrastructure was hosted on servers of a company in the Republic of Moldova; 22,000+ users, 100+ active vendors, €3.6M+ turnover, ~€194,000 in goods/materials seized — figures aligned with the BKA/ZIT 8 May 2026 release.
  • Confirms arrest of 35-year-old German national in Mallorca by a special unit of the Spanish National Police on a European Arrest Warrant.
  • Activities listed: stolen-data trade (bank cards, credentials, personal documents), distribution of malware/ransomware/DDoS-as-a-service, anonymous hosting and money-laundering facilitation.

개요Summary

Press release of Poliția Republicii Moldova (the General Police Inspectorate of the Republic of Moldova), dated 12 May 2026, jointly authored by its Centrul pentru combaterea crimelor cibernetice (Centre for Combating Cybercrime, within the INI — National Investigation Inspectorate) and the PCCOCS (the Prosecutor's Office for Combating Organized Crime and Special Cases). It is the Moldovan tier-1 own-domain counterpart to the BKA/ZIT release of 8 May 2026 and explicitly documents Moldovan participation in the international Crimenetwork takedown.

  • Reliability: high — direct first-party publication of the Moldovan national police on its own institutional domain (politia.md), co-attributed to the lead investigating cyber unit (INI Centrul pentru combaterea crimelor cibernetice) and the lead prosecutor (PCCOCS).
  • Credibility: confirmed — the publishing authority is the lead Moldovan investigative agency for this operation.
  • Sensitivity: public — published on the Moldovan police's public "Poliția în acțiune" press page; no operational restrictions noted.
