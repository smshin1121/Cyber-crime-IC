주요 발견사항

  • Fiscalía General de la Nación (Colombia) Boletín 57752 dated 18 November 2025 announcing the dismantling of an organised criminal network operating fraudulent cryptocurrency-investment platforms targeting victims in Colombia, Chile, and Peru.
  • Period of criminal activity: 2021 through October 2025. Total proceeds estimated at 71.000 millones de pesos colombianos (approximately COP 71 billion / ~USD 17M at end-2025 rates).
  • Modus operandi: social-media advertising → telephonic outreach → induce victims to deposit USD 200–250 → fictitious 'dividend' charts shown → demand for additional deposits → withdrawal blocked. In parallel, victims were coerced into installing remote-access tools 'Anydesk' or 'Imperios', enabling the group to seize banking credentials and take over devices.
  • 14 alleged members captured and presented to a juez de control de garantías. Charges (not accepted): estafa por medios informáticos, hurtos por medios informáticos, violación de datos personales (all in modalidad delito masa); concierto para delinquir; lavado de activos; enriquecimiento ilícito.
  • Lead investigative authority: Fiscalía General de la Nación, specifically the Dirección Especializada contra los Delitos Informáticos (DECDI). Bulletin signed LKSC/JARG/LMMA/DECDI.
  • Custody outcome: three suspects ordered to detention in a carceral facility (centro carcelario); remaining 11 ordered to house arrest.
  • Suspect of foreign nationality named in source: Mexican citizen Alejandro Maximiliano Ronquillo Treviño (alias Daniel) — described as posible cabecilla (alleged ringleader).
  • Other named suspects (Colombian unless noted): José Mauricio Murcia Martínez (alias Murcia, money mover); Daniel López Uribe (lawyer, shell-company creation); call-centre managers and advisors Jefferson Duván Sánchez Lopera, Valentina Galvis Quintero, Robert Cepeda Orozco, Juan Carlos González Alian; and María Paula Gonzáles Rojas, Rodolfo Andrés Cepeda Orozco, Andrés Felipe Villada Trujillo, Yudy Viviana Tabares Gonzáles, Paula Andrea Londoño Isaza, José Simón Mosquera Muñoz, Juliana Tabares León.
  • International cooperation surface in this primary source: countries-of-victims explicitly named (Colombia + Chile + Peru) — meeting ≥2 IC scope. The bulletin does NOT name any foreign agency, MLAT, AMERIPOL, INTERPOL, or other formal IC mechanism. Foreign-leader claims (Israeli citizen Boaz Iaakok Derra, alias Jim) circulating in secondary press are NOT in this primary source.
  • Tier-1 primary source: Fiscalía Colombia bulletin 57752 (https://www.fiscalia.gov.co/colombia/noticias/desarticulada-red-senala-de-disenar-un-modelo-de-estafa-a-traves-de-falsas-plataformas-de-inversion-de-criptoactivos/).

개요Summary

Tier-1 primary press release from Colombia's Fiscalía General de la Nación reporting the dismantling of an organised criminal network that ran fraudulent cryptocurrency- and securities-investment platforms from 2021 through October 2025, defrauding victims in Colombia, Chile, and Peru of an aggregate ~COP 71 billion. Fourteen alleged members were captured and charged in Colombia; three were ordered to pretrial detention in a carceral facility, and the remaining eleven to house arrest. The alleged ringleader named in the bulletin is a Mexican national, Alejandro Maximiliano Ronquillo Treviño (alias Daniel). The Dirección Especializada contra los Delitos Informáticos (DECDI) of the Fiscalía led the investigation.

International cooperation footprint (per this primary source)

  • Cross-border victimisation: Colombia, Chile, Peru.
  • Suspect of foreign nationality: Mexico (one suspect, alleged ringleader).
  • No foreign agency, MLAT request, AMERIPOL/INTERPOL channel, or extradition action is named in the bulletin.
📝 Primary-source scope Per L19-strict, this source page only asserts what the Fiscalía bulletin explicitly states. Secondary press (El Tiempo, Infobae, El Colombiano) report that the operation was named "Operación Dominó" and that an additional Israeli national (Boaz Iaakok Derra, alias Jim) is allegedly the principal architect. These claims are NOT in the Fiscalía bulletin and are flagged for separate verification before any wiki claim is built on them.
