주요 발견사항

  • On 2026-02-17 the Polish Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime (CBZC) announced the arrest of a 47-year-old man in Małopolskie Voivodeship suspected of producing, acquiring and distributing computer programs used to unlawfully obtain information stored in IT systems (Polish Criminal Code, Article 269b § 1; max 5 years imprisonment).
  • The operation was a joint action of CBZC officers from the Katowice and Kielce divisions; police seized a computer and mobile phones containing logins, passwords, credit card numbers and server IP addresses, with the investigation supervised by the District Prosecutor's Office in Gliwice (Prokuratura Okręgowa w Gliwicach).
  • The Polish CBZC press release attributes the arrest to participation in Operation Aether coordinated by Europol — a multi-jurisdictional law enforcement campaign against persons linked to the Phobos Ransomware-as-a-Service ecosystem.
  • The release explicitly names cooperation with the United States: it cites the prior extradition of the alleged Phobos administrator to the USA and references US Department of Justice documents giving total Phobos ransom proceeds exceeding USD 16 million; reported victims include US public schools (California, Connecticut), Maryland healthcare providers and a US Department of Defense contractor.
  • Phobos is described as a Ransomware-as-a-Service organized cybercriminal group with more than 1,000 victims worldwide; the suspect allegedly contacted the Phobos group via encrypted messengers but is charged for tooling/preparatory offences (Art. 269b § 1) rather than for direct extortion.

개요Summary

Polish-language tier-1 press release from Poland's Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime (CBZC, Centralne Biuro Zwalczania Cyberprzestępczości) announcing the 2026-02-17 arrest in Małopolskie Voivodeship of a 47-year-old Polish national suspected of producing, acquiring and distributing hacking tools (Polish Criminal Code Art. 269b § 1) and of contacting the Phobos ransomware group via encrypted messengers. The arrest is publicly tied to CBZC's participation in Operation Aether, a Europol-coordinated multi-jurisdictional campaign against the Phobos RaaS ecosystem.
