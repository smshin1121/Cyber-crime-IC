주요 발견사항

  • Italian Polizia di Stato concluded undercover online operation 'Mad Hatter' against transnational CSAM distribution networks; investigation started in 2024 and was announced on 2026-04-28.
  • C.N.C.P.O. (Centro Nazionale per il Contrasto alla Pedopornografia Online) led the undercover infiltration; coordinated by Procura della Repubblica di Roma.
  • Results: 3 in-flagrante arrests (men aged 70/63/28 in provinces of Belluno, Mantova, Como), 6 reported at liberty (provinces of Bari, Oristano, Massa, Firenze, Lecco, Frosinone), 9 simultaneous search warrants executed in 9 Italian cities, thousands of CSAM files seized.
  • Foreign cooperation partner explicitly named: U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI), which provided information enabling the identification of subjects of interest.
  • C.O.S.C. (Centri Operativi per la Sicurezza Cibernetica) of Bari, Cagliari, Firenze, Milano, Roma, Torino, Venezia plus S.O.S.C. units deployed approximately 50 specialised operators nationwide.
  • Material seized included CSAM generated using artificial intelligence, referencing victims of very young age — explicit acknowledgement of AI-generated CSAM in an Italian primary source.
  • Italian original-language legal terms: 'pedopornografia online' (online child sexual abuse material), 'denuncia in stato di libertà' (report/charge while at liberty, i.e., not detained), 'arresto in flagranza' (in-flagrante arrest), 'perquisizione domiciliare/personale/informatica' (domiciliary/personal/computer search).

Source Profile

Official press release issued by the Italian State Police (Polizia di Stato) via the Questura di Milano press portal, announcing the conclusion of "Operazione Mad Hatter" — a transnational undercover investigation against online CSAM distribution conducted by the C.N.C.P.O. (Centro Nazionale per il Contrasto alla Pedopornografia Online), the national CSAM-investigation centre of Polizia Postale e per la sicurezza cibernetica. The investigation was coordinated by the Procura della Repubblica di Roma and conducted in cooperation with the U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation.

The release is a tier-1 primary source: it is published on the official questure.poliziadistato.it Polizia di Stato domain, signed institutionally, and provides operational figures (arrests, search warrants, deployed personnel, regional COSC participation) directly from the responsible Italian agency.

Reliability and Credibility

  • Reliability: high — official agency publication on a .poliziadistato.it Polizia di Stato sub-domain.
  • Credibility: confirmed — first-party announcement by the lead investigative agency, with the foreign cooperation partner (FBI) explicitly named.
  • Sensitivity: public — the press release is intended for general distribution; defendants' identities are protected (only ages and provinces are stated, consistent with the Italian presumption-of-innocence formula at the close of the text).
