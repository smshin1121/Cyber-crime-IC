주요 발견사항

  • USAO Central District of California unsealed (1) a federal grand jury indictment against four U.S.-resident defendants — Elbadawy (TX), Urban (FL), Osiebo (TX), Evans (NC) — and (2) a federal criminal complaint against UK national Tyler Robert Buchanan, on 2024-11-20, for an SMS-phishing-to-credential-theft-to-cryptocurrency-drain scheme operating ~September 2021 to April 2023.
  • Charges per indictment: conspiracy to commit wire fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1349), conspiracy (18 U.S.C. § 371), and aggravated identity theft (18 U.S.C. § 1028A). Buchanan's complaint adds substantive wire fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1343).
  • Cooperating LE explicitly named in the press release: FBI Los Angeles Field Office (lead, AD Akil Davis); FBI field offices Charlotte / Denver / Houston / Portland; USAO Eastern District of North Carolina; Police Scotland. Spanish authorities, NCA, Eurojust, and Europol are NOT named in this release.
  • Group attribution: the DOJ release does not use the name 'Scattered Spider' / Octo Tempest / UNC3944 / 0ktapus. Secondary reporting (Krebs, Bloomberg, The Record, Hacker News, Hogan Lovells, Axios, BankInfoSec, CyberScoop, etc.) identifies these defendants as Scattered Spider members and notes Buchanan's earlier arrest in Spain in June 2024 by Spanish national police while attempting to board a flight to Italy — facts NOT in the DOJ release itself.
  • Statutory maximum exposure per defendant: 27 years (20 wire-fraud-conspiracy + 5 conspiracy + 2 mandatory-consecutive aggravated-identity-theft). Buchanan: up to 47 years (additional 20 for substantive wire fraud).

Source Summary

This is the U.S. Department of Justice — USAO Central District of California — press release (No. 24-289), dated Wednesday, November 20, 2024, announcing the unsealing of a federal grand jury indictment in the U.S. District Court for the Central District of California charging four U.S.-resident defendants (Ahmed Hossam Eldin Elbadawy of College Station TX; Noah Michael Urban of Palm Coast FL; Evans Onyeaka Osiebo of Dallas TX; Joel Martin Evans of Jacksonville NC) and a parallel federal criminal complaint charging Tyler Robert Buchanan, a UK national, with conspiracy to commit wire fraud, conspiracy, aggravated identity theft, and (Buchanan only) substantive wire fraud, arising out of a smishing-to-credential-theft-to-cryptocurrency-drain scheme operating from at least September 2021 to April 2023.

The release names the FBI Los Angeles Field Office as the lead investigative agency (Assistant Director in Charge Akil Davis) and credits assistance from the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of North Carolina, Police Scotland, and the FBI field offices in Charlotte, Denver, Houston, and Portland. AUSAs Lauren Restrepo (CCIPS / Cyber and IP Crimes Section) and Sue J. Bai (Terrorism and Export Crimes Section) are the line prosecutors.

Relevance to IC

The source supports us-v-five-scattered-spider-members-2024 and is a tier-1 primary source for:

  1. Cross-border defendant chain (US ↔ UK) — Buchanan, a UK national, is charged by U.S. complaint on the same day the U.S. indictment is unsealed against the four U.S.-resident co-defendants. The release does not announce extradition or arrest of Buchanan, but the parallel U.S.-domestic / UK-foreign-national filing structure is a direct artifact of the U.S. extraterritorial reach over hacking-ic and wire-fraud-via-SMS schemes touching U.S. wires.
  2. Police-to-police cross-border cooperation (US ↔ Scotland / United Kingdom) — Police Scotland is named as a contributing agency. This is one of the few wiki-documented cases naming Police Scotland (rather than the UK NCA, City of London Police, or West Midlands Police) as the UK-side cooperator on a U.S. cybercrime indictment, likely (55–80%) because Buchanan's residence ties to Dundee, Scotland (per secondary reporting).
  3. Companion to the 2025 Jubair complaint — sets up the Scattered Spider prosecution chain as a multi-year, multi-defendant U.S. effort. The November 2024 case (this page) covers conduct September 2021 to April 2023; the September 2025 D.N.J. complaint against Thalha Jubair (us-v-jubair-scattered-spider-2025) covers conduct May 2022 to September 2025. The two cases together document the most extensive U.S.-led Scattered Spider prosecutorial response to date.
📝 Group-attribution caveat The DOJ press release does not use the name "Scattered Spider," "Octo Tempest," "UNC3944," or "0ktapus." The Scattered Spider attribution to these five defendants is sourced from secondary tier-2/3 reporting (KrebsOnSecurity, Bloomberg, The Record, Hacker News, Hogan Lovells, Axios, BankInfoSec, IT Pro, TechTarget, CyberScoop, CyberSecurity Dive). The case page should treat the group attribution as highly likely (80–95%) but explicitly secondary-sourced.

[!note] Spain-arrest fact not in source Tyler Buchanan's well-reported June 2024 arrest in Spain by Spanish authorities (while attempting to board a flight to Italy) is not mentioned in this DOJ press release. It is documented in secondary reporting and parallel Spanish-side announcements. The case page records it with an explicit secondary-source citation pending ingestion of a tier-1 Spanish or U.S. extradition filing.
