주요 발견사항

  • Thomasz Szabo (Romanian, 27), aka Plank/Jonah/Cypher, sentenced 2026-04-29 to 48 months in prison + 3 years supervised release in U.S. District Court (DC) for leading a U.S.-targeting online swatting ring; pleaded guilty 2025-06-02 to conspiracy and threats involving explosives.
  • Group's swatting/bomb-threat spree (Dec 2023 – early Jan 2024) targeted 25+ Members of Congress (and family), 6+ senior U.S. Executive Branch officials, 13+ senior federal law-enforcement officials, multiple federal judges, 27+ state officials, 4 religious institutions, multiple journalists.
  • Earlier Szabo personal threats included a Dec 2020 threat of a mass shooting at New York City synagogues and a Jan 2021 threat to detonate explosives at the U.S. Capitol and kill the President-elect.
  • Extradited from Romania to the United States in November 2024 — Romania's authorities described as 'critical' to the case; Office of International Affairs (DOJ) used mutual legal assistance requests to secure evidence from abroad.
  • U.S. side: Secret Service Washington Field Office + Criminal Investigative Division (lead investigator), Secret Service Bucharest Resident Office (in-country liaison), FBI Washington Field Office (Counterterrorism Division) + Minneapolis Field Office, FBI Legat Bucharest, U.S. Capitol Police, DOJ Office of International Affairs, DOJ National Security Division Counterterrorism Section, multiple cooperating USAOs (WD WA, D SD, MD FL, SD FL, SD IL, ND NY).
  • U.S. Attorney Jeanine Ferris Pirro announced sentencing; presiding judge Amy Berman Jackson; federal prosecutors had requested 57 months.
  • One of Szabo's subordinates internally bragged: 'I did 25+ swattings today,' '$500,000+ in taxpayers wasted in just two days.'

개요Summary

U.S. Secret Service press release (republishing U.S. Attorney for D.C. announcement) on the 2026-04-29 sentencing of Romanian national Thomasz Szabo to 48 months in federal prison for leading a transnational online swatting ring that, between Dec 2023 and early Jan 2024, directed bomb threats and false police-emergency calls at over 75 senior U.S. public officials, four religious institutions, and multiple journalists. The release is also the most explicit available primary-source description of the U.S.–Romania international cooperation that produced Szabo's November 2024 extradition: it credits the DOJ Office of International Affairs, mutual legal assistance requests for evidence abroad, the FBI Bucharest Legat office, the Secret Service Bucharest Resident Office, and Romanian authorities (whose assistance is described as "critical").

Why This Source Matters

  • Tier-1 primary-source confirmation of an extradition-driven prosecution against a non-U.S. ringleader for cyber-enabled threats to U.S. critical political infrastructure (Congress, federal judiciary, federal LE leadership).
  • Documents an unusually broad U.S. inter-agency coalition (Secret Service + FBI + Capitol Police + multiple USAOs + NSD + OIA) jointly relying on Romanian cooperation as the operational hinge.
  • Useful for cross-referencing how <a href="../../wiki/mechanisms/mlat-process.html" class="wikilink">mlat-process</a> / <a href="../../wiki/mechanisms/mutual-legal-assistance.html" class="wikilink">mutual-legal-assistance</a> / <a href="../../wiki/mechanisms/extradition.html" class="wikilink">extradition</a> / <a href="../../wiki/organizations/office-of-international-affairs.html" class="wikilink">office-of-international-affairs</a> are operationalised in practice for non-financial cybercrime cases.

Notes

  • The release does not state the indictment number or the underlying U.S. Code statutes (only "conspiracy" + "threats involving explosives"); cross-reference with the earlier 2024 indictment / 2025-06-02 guilty plea press release will be needed for full charge-by-charge precision.
  • The release does not name Szabo's named co-conspirators or the group's online platform; "subordinate members" are described only generically.
