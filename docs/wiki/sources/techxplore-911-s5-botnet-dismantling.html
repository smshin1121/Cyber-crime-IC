주요 발견사항

  • Microsoft, US DOJ, Europol, and Japan's Cybercrime Control Center dismantled the Lumma Stealer infostealer malware operation in a coordinated action announced on 22 May 2025 — NOT the 911 S5 botnet
  • Lumma Stealer had infected 1.8 million devices globally during 2024; between March and May 2025 alone, 394,000+ Windows computers were compromised
  • Microsoft filed a federal civil lawsuit in Atlanta federal court (Northern District of Georgia) on 13 May 2025, obtaining a temporary restraining order against 10 John-Doe defendants including the primary developer using the alias 'Shamel' (reportedly based in Russia)
  • ~2,300 domains comprising Lumma's command-and-control infrastructure were seized as part of the operation
  • {'"Business model': 'Lumma operated as a subscription-based malware-as-a-service (MaaS) sold on dark-web forums, targeting financial credentials, banking data, and cryptocurrency wallets across transportation, finance, healthcare, and education sectors — active since 2022"'}
  • {'"Cooperation model': 'Microsoft civil-litigation lead (novel approach), US DOJ criminal-asset seizure, Europol coordination, Japan Cybercrime Control Center for Asian infrastructure — a hybrid civil+criminal international cooperation template"'}

개요Summary

The TechXplore article (originally from The Conversation) describes the 22 May 2025 announcement of the Lumma Stealer infostealer takedown, jointly executed by:

  • Microsoft (civil litigation lead) — filed a federal civil lawsuit in the US District Court for the Northern District of Georgia (Atlanta) on 13 May 2025
  • US Department of Justice (criminal asset seizure)
  • Europol (European coordination)
  • Japan's Cybercrime Control Center (Asian infrastructure)

Key operational facts:

  1. Lumma Stealer: subscription-based malware-as-a-service (MaaS) active since 2022, sold on dark-web forums for stealing financial credentials, banking data, and cryptocurrency wallets
  2. 1.8 million infected devices globally during 2024
  3. 394,000+ Windows computers compromised between March-May 2025 alone
  4. ~2,300 C&C domains seized
  5. Primary developer alias: "Shamel" — reportedly based in Russia (not arrested)
  6. 10 John-Doe defendants in Microsoft's civil suit
  7. Target sectors: transportation, finance, healthcare, education

Why this source matters

The Lumma Stealer takedown is a reference case for:

  1. Microsoft-led civil litigation model — Microsoft's Digital Crimes Unit has increasingly used civil-litigation tools (John-Doe suits, temporary restraining orders, domain seizure via civil process) as a force multiplier for criminal prosecution. Lumma is a 2025-era example.
  2. Multi-jurisdictional civil+criminal hybrid — civil litigation in Atlanta combined with DOJ criminal seizure and Europol/Japan coordination represents a novel cooperation template distinct from pure criminal prosecution
  3. MaaS ecosystem disruption — Lumma's subscription-based model made it a "critical infrastructure" target within the broader infostealer criminal ecosystem
  4. Russian non-extradition pattern — the primary developer ("Shamel") reportedly remains in Russia, continuing the post-2022 pattern of Russia-based cybercriminal impunity
