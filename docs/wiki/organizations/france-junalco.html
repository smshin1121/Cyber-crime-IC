개요Summary

JUNALCO (Juridiction Nationale de Lutte contre la Criminalité Organisée) is a specialized French national jurisdiction created in 2019 and installed at the Paris Judicial Tribunal in January 2020. It has concurrent nationwide competence over organized crime cases of very high complexity, including complex cybercrime matters handled by its dedicated J3 cybercrime section [1][2].

JUNALCO is highly likely the most operationally significant French prosecutorial body for international cybercrime cases, working in partnership with Gendarmerie C3N and OFAC.

임무와 권한Mandate and Authority

JUNALCO was formalized by circular of 17 December 2019 on the concurrent national competence of the Paris tribunal for the fight against organized crime of very great complexity [2]. It handles serious cybercrime alongside other high-complexity organized crime categories (drug trafficking, financial crime, money laundering).

The J3 section is the cybercrime prevention and prosecution unit within JUNALCO, historically led by Vice-Prosecutor Johanna Brousse [1].

⚠️ Legal status check needed According to reporting, JUNALCO's caseload is expected to transfer to the new PNACO (Parquet National Anti-Criminalité Organisée) upon its creation on 5 January 2026 [1]. This page should be reviewed after that date.

사이버범죄 국제공조 관련 구조Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

  • Part of the Paris Parquet (Public Prosecutor's Office)
  • J3 section = dedicated cybercrime prosecutors
  • Works with investigating judges assigned to cybercrime matters
  • Coordinates with investigative services (C3N, OFAC)

국제공조 역량IC Capabilities

  • Judicial authority to issue European Investigation Orders (EIO) and MLAT requests
  • Routine coordination with Eurojust for cross-border cases
  • Europol/EC3 partnership on ransomware and dark web operations
  • French representation in major multinational cybercrime takedowns (e.g. Operation Endgame)

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

France is a party to the Budapest Convention and a founding Europol member. JUNALCO almost certainly uses both Budapest Convention MLA channels and EU instruments (EIO, JITs via Eurojust) for its cross-border cybercrime work.
