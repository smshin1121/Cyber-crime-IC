Ministry of Science and ICT (Korea)은(는) 국가기관으로 2017-07-26년 설립되었으며 Sejong, South Korea에 본부를 두고 있다.

임무: South Korean government ministry responsible for science, ICT, broadcasting,

소속국: South Korea

⚠️ Status — Renamed and restructured The current 과학기술정보통신부 (Ministry of Science and ICT, MSIT) was created in its present form on 2017-07-26 under the Moon Jae-in administration, inheriting the functions of the prior 미래창조과학부 (Ministry of Science, ICT and Future Planning, MSIP, 2013–2017), which itself was created by merging parts of the earlier 정보통신부 (Ministry of Information and Communication) and other bodies. The English short form has occasionally been confused with "Ministry of Science, ICT and Future Planning" or miswritten as "과학수사정보통신부" (an incorrect form conflating 과학수사 and 과기정통부). The correct current name is 과학기술정보통신부 / Ministry of Science and ICT [1][2].

As of early 2026 MSIT is undergoing an internal restructuring centered on information security: the 네트워크정책실 has been renamed 정보보호네트워크정책실, a 5-person 사이버침해조사팀 has been newly created to handle cyber-incident investigation, and Minister 배경훈 has publicly signaled that a broader government-level reorganization of the national information-protection apparatus is under active discussion [3][4]. These changes are a response to the 2025 large-scale data breaches (KT, SKT, Coupang, telecom providers) and do not involve any change to MSIT's legal identity.

개요Summary

The Ministry of Science and ICT (과학기술정보통신부, MSIT) is South Korea's central government department responsible for science, ICT, broadcasting, information security policy, and AI policy. In the international cooperation on cybercrime ecosystem, MSIT's role is policy-making and oversight, not investigation or prosecution. Its primary operational arm is KISA (한국인터넷진흥원), which operates KrCERT/CC as Korea's national CSIRT and handles technical incident coordination with foreign counterparts.

MSIT is not a law enforcement agency. Criminal cybercrime cases are handled by the Korea National Police Agency and, after October 2026, the 공소청·중수청 successor entities depending on final organizational structure.

Current Status (as of 2026-04-10)

Present-day name: 과학기술정보통신부 (Ministry of Science and ICT, MSIT) — unchanged since 2017-07-26 Minister (2026): 배경훈 (부총리 겸 과학기술정보통신부 장관) [3] Headquarters: Government Complex Sejong Status: Active, internally restructured January 2026

Post-January 2026 internal changes [3][4]: - 네트워크정책실 → 정보보호네트워크정책실: name change reflecting information-protection priority - 사이버침해조사팀 (Cyber Breach Investigation Team) — newly created: 5 staff (up from 2 prior investigators), responsible for ministry-level cyber incident investigation and coordination with KISA/KrCERT/CC - AI 기반 침해 대응 자동화 체계 (AI-based breach-response automation): announced 2026 work plan, to be developed with KISA - ISMS-P 인증제도 개편: revised information security certification scheme, co-developed with 개인정보보호위원회 [4]

Minister 배경훈 has publicly stated that a 근본적 정보보호 조직 개편 (fundamental restructuring of the national information-protection apparatus) is under inter-ministerial discussion with a timeline for public announcement; details not yet released [3].

임무와 권한Mandate and Authority

  • Policy authority: Information and Communications Network Act (정보통신망법), Information Protection Industry Promotion Act, and related statutes
  • Supervisory authority over KISA: KISA is a public corporation under MSIT; its budget, strategic direction, and leadership are subject to MSIT oversight
  • National cybersecurity policy coordination: works with 국가정보원 (NIS) on public-sector and critical-infrastructure security policy; with KISA/KrCERT/CC on private-sector incident response
  • International engagement: ITU, APEC TEL, ASEAN-ROK cooperation on ICT/cybersecurity capacity building
  • Not a law-enforcement agency: no arrest or investigation power; criminal cybercrime cases are handled by police and, as applicable, prosecution/수사청 entities

Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC (post-2026-01)

  • 정보보호네트워크정책실 (Information Protection and Network Policy Office): renamed and expanded from 네트워크정책실
  • 사이버침해조사팀 (Cyber Breach Investigation Team): 5-person ministry-level team created 2026 for cyber-incident investigation and coordination
  • 국제협력관 / 디지털국제협력관: international ICT cooperation; participates in ITU, APEC TEL, ASEAN-ROK cooperation
  • KISA (subordinate agency): operational arm for cybersecurity, CSIRT, and technical international cooperation

Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

MSIT is the policy-level counterpart for international ICT and cybersecurity cooperation. For operational cybercrime cooperation, foreign counterparts typically engage either: - KISA/KrCERT/CC for technical/incident response - KNPA cyber enforcement line for criminal investigation - 대검찰청 (→ 공소청/중수청 from 2026-10) for prosecutorial/MLAT cooperation

모순점 및 미해결 문제Contradictions & Open Questions

  • Whether the "fundamental information-protection organizational reorganization" signaled by Minister 배경훈 in January 2026 will result in a new standalone cybersecurity agency, an upgrade of KISA, or redistribution of functions across MSIT, NIS, and other bodies — not yet publicly decided [3]
  • Whether MSIT's 사이버침해조사팀 has any operational overlap with KNPA cybercrime investigation or KISA/KrCERT/CC incident response — not yet clearly delineated in public sources
  • Exact budget and staffing levels for the 2026 restructuring are not publicly disclosed
