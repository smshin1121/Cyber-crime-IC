개요Summary

ZIT Frankfurt is the cybercrime-focused prosecutorial unit associated with the General Public Prosecutor's Office Frankfurt am Main in Hessen. The official Hessen source identifies ZIT as a specialized central office for internet crime, established on 1 January 2010 and later relocated to Frankfurt am Main in July 2019.

Cybercrime Cooperation Role

ZIT is one of the core prosecutorial partners behind Germany's major cybercrime takedowns. In the IC corpus, its most visible role is as the legal and judicial counterpart to Germany's BKA in cases involving darknet markets, malware infrastructure, ransomware ecosystems, and cross-border server seizures.

The Nemesis Market takedown is the clearest source-backed example. The BKA/ZIT release states that German and Lithuanian server infrastructure was seized in a coordinated action on 20 March 2024, with German, U.S., and Lithuanian authorities cooperating since October 2022. That release is important because it ties ZIT directly to operational judicial action rather than only to an institutional mandate.

IC Relevance

When German cybercrime operations involve search warrants, seizure orders, forfeiture, suspect charging, or mutual legal assistance, ZIT is often the prosecutorial node that explains how BKA technical-investigative work becomes executable legal action.
