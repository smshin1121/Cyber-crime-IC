개요Summary

Indonesia Immigration is relevant to the corpus because the Bali villa raid was handled through immigration enforcement rather than a completed Indonesian cybercrime prosecution. Public reporting describes the detention of 103 foreign nationals in Bali suspected of cybercrime activity, followed by planned deportation because the alleged victims were outside Indonesia.

Cybercrime Cooperation Role

In bali-villa-cybercrime-raid-2024, Indonesian immigration officials raided a villa in Tabanan regency, Bali, detained 91 men and 12 women, and seized hundreds of phones and other electronic devices. The reporting describes suspected targeting of victims abroad, including Malaysia, and identifies immigration violations as the immediate enforcement route.

This is important for IC analysis because it shows a common scam-compound problem: the host jurisdiction can disrupt an operation through immigration and deportation even when direct cybercrime prosecution is difficult because victims, evidence, and harms sit outside the territorial forum.

IC Relevance
