к°ңмҡ”Summary

The Nationellt IT-brottscentrum вҖ” National IT Crime Centre, also branded SC3 (Swedish Cybercrime Centre) and more recently NC3 вҖ” began operations on 1 October 2015 as the Swedish Police Authority's expert function for investigating complex cybercrimes [1][2]. It is part of the Nationella operativa avdelningen (NOA) вҖ” the National Operations Department of Polismyndigheten, based in Stockholm [3].

Since its establishment, the Centre has highly likely significantly increased Sweden's capacity to handle cybercrime, and seven regional IT crime centres have been established across the Swedish police regions to complement the national centre [2].

рҹ“қ Naming Swedish sources use multiple labels: "Nationellt IT-brottscentrum," "SC3" (Swedish Cybercrime Centre), and "NC3." These refer to the same operational unit.

мһ„л¬ҙмҷҖ к¶Ңн•ңMandate and Authority

The Centre is Sweden's national-level police expert function for investigating [1][2]:

  • Complex cybercrimes (ransomware, DDoS, data breach)
  • Internet-related crimes against children
  • Criminal infrastructure used for other crimes
  • Crimes where the internet is the central element

It is part of the Swedish Police Authority's National Operations Department (NOA), placing it alongside Sweden's other national-priority crime functions.

мӮ¬мқҙлІ„лІ”мЈ„ көӯм ңкіөмЎ° кҙҖл Ё кө¬мЎ°Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

  • National centre (Stockholm) вҖ” specialist investigators, digital forensics, coordination
  • 7 regional IT-crime centres вҖ” distributed across Swedish police regions for local cyber investigation [2]
  • NOA reporting chain вҖ” ensures national prioritization and international liaison

көӯм ңкіөмЎ° м—ӯлҹүIC Capabilities

  • Europol National Unit channel for cybercrime (Sweden is a Europol member)
  • Interpol NCB Stockholm channel
  • Budapest Convention cooperation (Sweden is a party)
  • Nordic law enforcement cooperation вҖ” standard regional channels
  • Ransomware and DDoS investigation focus [3]
