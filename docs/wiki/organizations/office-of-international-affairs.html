개요Summary

The Office of International Affairs (OIA) is the DOJ Criminal Division component that carries much of the United States' formal cross-border legal process in cybercrime matters. In this corpus, OIA is not usually the technical investigator; it is the legal-cooperation mechanism that makes foreign evidence collection, search requests, arrests, extraditions, and evidence sharing usable in US prosecutions.

Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The DOJ CCIPS source frames OIA as part of the US international cybercrime cooperation architecture, with OIA handling central-authority and MLAT functions while CCIPS provides cybercrime prosecutorial expertise. Operation records then show how that role appears in practice.

In qqaazz-money-laundering-takedown, DOJ described OIA coordination for searches, arrests, extraditions, and evidence sharing across a transnational laundering case tied to high-level cybercriminal groups. In proxy-service-takedown-2026-03, the SocksEscort disruption source lists DOJ OIA among the US support components for a coordinated action with Eurojust, Europol, DCIS, FBI, IRS-CI, and European partners.

IC Relevance

Use OIA as the institutional node for formal US legal cooperation, especially when an operation involves extradition, foreign search-and-seizure work, MLAT evidence channels, or multiple foreign prosecutions. Keep investigative attribution on the lead agency pages such as fbi-cyber-division, us-dcis, or us-secret-service.

Source Coverage

Coverage is good for role and case examples. Direct DOJ OIA-specific public pages would improve exact mandate wording and current internal organization, but operation records already establish the practical cooperation function.
