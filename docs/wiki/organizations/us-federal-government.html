개요Summary

US Federal Government is an umbrella page for cases where source material attributes an action to the US government broadly or where multiple federal agencies share the cybercrime role. It should not replace named agency pages; it is a routing page for whole-of-government actions and multi-agency federal architecture.

Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The US cybercrime cooperation model is distributed. us-doj and office-of-international-affairs handle prosecution, MLAT, extradition, and central-authority functions. fbi-cyber-division and IC3 provide cyber investigation and national internet-crime reporting. us-secret-service handles cyber-enabled financial crime through Cyber Fraud Task Forces. dea and us-postal-inspection are central where darknet narcotics or mailed contraband are involved.

Operation records show the whole-of-government pattern. bidencash-takedown is explicitly framed as a US government seizure involving Secret Service, FBI, DOJ, Dutch police, and private-sector support. proxy-service-takedown-2026-03 combines DOJ, FBI, DCIS, IRS-CI, FinCEN, Eurojust, Europol, and European partners against the SocksEscort proxy service.

IC Relevance

Use this page when a source says "US government" or when a chart needs a single umbrella node for multi-agency US federal participation. For attribution, prefer the named agency page whenever the source identifies DOJ, FBI, Secret Service, DEA, DCIS, USPIS, or another component.
