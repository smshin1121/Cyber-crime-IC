개요Summary

The Fiscale Inlichtingen- en Opsporingsdienst (FIOD) is the investigation service of the Dutch Ministry of Finance, responsible for combating financial fraud, money laundering, cybercrime and corruption [1]. Unlike the Dutch National Police cyber units, FIOD operates with financial-crime jurisdiction and specializes in the monetary and cryptocurrency dimensions of cybercrime.

FIOD's Financial Advanced Cyber Team (FACT) is almost certainly one of Europe's most active cryptocurrency criminal investigation units, with substantial expertise in crypto tracing, exchange takedowns and crypto-enabled money laundering [2][3]. FACT works closely with the Dutch National Police's High Tech Crime Unit (NHTCU), US Secret Service and Europol on major operations.

임무와 권한Mandate and Authority

FIOD traces offenders of financial cybercrime, targeting crimes committed with digital devices including hacking, online fraud and money laundering with digital currency [1]. Its statutory base is the financial-crime mandate of the Dutch Ministry of Finance, making it distinct from the Politie's police cybercrime capacity.

사이버범죄 국제공조 관련 구조Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

FACT — Financial Advanced Cyber Team

FIOD hosts specialized teams under the FACT brand with substantive expertise in cryptocurrencies, conducting investigations that primarily relate to cybercrime, drug trafficking, fraud and money laundering offences [1]. FACT is highly likely one of the most technically capable European crypto-forensics units.

국제공조 역량IC Capabilities

  • Cryptocurrency tracing: Deep expertise in on-chain analytics, mixer/tumbler investigation, and exchange disruption
  • Europol integration: Routine cooperation via Europol EC3 and European Financial and Economic Crime Centre (EFECC)
  • US partnership: Documented joint operations with US Secret Service [2]
  • Dutch domestic integration: Works alongside Dutch National Police Team High Tech Crime (THTC / NHTCU)
  • Asset recovery: Substantial seized-crypto track record (EUR 41M in 2024 alone) [3]
