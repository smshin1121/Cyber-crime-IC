개요Summary

The Office anti-cybercriminalité (OFAC) is the French Police Nationale's national cybercrime office, created by decree on 23 November 2023 and attached to the Direction Nationale de la Police Judiciaire [1][2]. OFAC replaced the former Sous-Direction de Lutte Contre la Cybercriminalité (SDLC) and the Office Central de Lutte Contre la Criminalité liée aux Technologies de l'Information et de la Communication (OCLCTIC) [2][3].

Based in Nanterre, OFAC had approximately 180 personnel at its launch (police officers, gendarmes, engineers, technicians, administrative staff and contractors) and is scheduled to roughly double in size by 2027 [2][3]. It is highly likely the primary Police Nationale point of contact for international cybercrime cooperation, running in parallel with the Gendarmerie C3N.

임무와 권한Mandate and Authority

Established by Décret n° 2023-1083 du 23 novembre 2023 [1], OFAC holds national competence to:

  • Combat all forms of delinquency emerging with information and communication technologies [2]
  • Coordinate complex investigations into cyberattacks, darknet crime and online fraud [2]
  • Centralize cybercrime police expertise within the Police Nationale
  • Operate a territorial network of branches and detachments [2]

사이버범죄 국제공조 관련 구조Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

OFAC is organized into four operational "poles" structured around its four missions, plus a territorial network [2]. Leadership at launch: - Head: Nicolas Guidoux, Contrôleur Général des services actifs de la police nationale [2] - Deputy: Cécile Augeraud, Commissaire Divisionnaire [2]

국제공조 역량IC Capabilities

  • National Police Nationale coordination point for cybercrime
  • Europol EC3 cooperation (France is a founding member)
  • Interpol National Central Bureau channels
  • Darknet, ransomware and online fraud investigative expertise
  • Dedicated Paris 2024 Olympics cyber threat focus at launch [3]
