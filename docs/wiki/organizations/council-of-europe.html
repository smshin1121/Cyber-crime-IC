Council of Europe

: 2026-04-29 : 3

개요

Council of Europe은(는) international-organization으로 Strasbourg, France에 본부를 두고 있다.

임무: European intergovernmental organization that administers cybercrime treaty cooperation and global capacity-building programmes.

국제공조 역할

  • Administers the Budapest Convention ecosystem for cybercrime law harmonization and international cooperation.
  • Maintains treaty status and party-observer information for Convention 185.
  • Runs C-PROC capacity-building programmes from Bucharest.

협력 기관

C Proc, Interpol, Unodc

개요Summary

The Council of Europe is the institutional home of the Budapest Convention on Cybercrime and a central non-police actor in cybercrime international cooperation. Its relevance to this corpus is treaty architecture: harmonized criminal offences, investigative powers, expedited preservation, production and search powers, and cross-border cooperation channels that make cybercrime investigations legally interoperable.

The corpus has three local anchors for this role: the Council of Europe's overview of the Budapest Convention, the treaty-signature/status chart for Convention 185, and the Council of Europe Cybercrime Programme Office (C-PROC) page. Together they support the Council of Europe as a treaty administrator, status-record source, and capacity-building coordinator.

Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The Council of Europe does not operate as an arresting agency. It supplies the legal and cooperation framework used by states, prosecutors, and police units when cybercrime cases cross borders. The Budapest Convention is particularly important because many non-European states participate in the convention system, making it a global cooperation baseline rather than a purely regional European instrument.

C-PROC extends that framework through practical capacity building. It supports countries implementing cybercrime legislation, electronic-evidence procedures, and institutional cooperation. In operational analysis, this means the Council of Europe often explains why two jurisdictions can cooperate even when the visible enforcement action is carried out by national police, prosecutors, europol-ec3, interpol, or eurojust.

IC Relevance

Use this organization page when a case depends on treaty status, 24/7 contact-point cooperation, capacity building, or comparisons between the Budapest Convention and other cybercrime-law frameworks. Do not attribute arrests, infrastructure seizures, or direct operational command to the Council of Europe unless a specific source says so.

Source Coverage

The current source set is strong for treaty role and programme-office context, but less complete for committee governance, protocol development, and historical convention drafting. Future enrichment should add direct pages for the Cybercrime Convention Committee and additional protocol material where available in the local corpus.
