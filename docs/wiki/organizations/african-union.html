개요

African Union은(는) regional-organization으로 Addis Ababa, Ethiopia에 본부를 두고 있다.

임무: Continental policy coordination for African Union member states, including cyber security and data protection norms.

국제공조 역할

  • Sets continental cyber-security, cybercrime, electronic-commerce, and personal-data-protection policy baselines through AU instruments.
  • Provides the regional treaty context used by African states and partners when harmonizing cybercrime laws and digital-evidence powers.

협력 기관

Afripol, Interpol, Unodc

개요Summary

The African Union (AU) is the continental policy organization for African member states. In the cybercrime corpus, its main relevance is not day-to-day police command; it is the regional legal and policy frame that shapes how African states approach cyber security, data protection, electronic transactions, and criminalization of ICT-enabled offences.

The key AU instrument tracked here is the African Union Convention on Cyber Security and Personal Data Protection, often discussed as the Malabo Convention. The local source coverage emphasizes how national cybercrime laws can be mapped against AU and Budapest Convention concepts, including illegal access, illegal interception, data and system interference, misuse of devices, child sexual abuse material offences, and procedural rules for digital evidence.

Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The AU functions as a regional harmonization layer. Its convention and policy work help create common expectations for national legislation, which matters when cross-border investigations require compatible offences, preservation powers, disclosure rules, and mutual legal assistance procedures. That role is especially important in African-facing operations where law-enforcement partners may include national police, afripol, interpol, and UN capacity-building actors rather than an AU operational unit.

IC Relevance

For IC analysis, the AU should be treated as a norm-setting and capacity-building context rather than as a takedown agency. References to African regional cooperation, cybercrime law reform, or data-protection harmonization can be anchored here. Operational pages should link to this page only when they discuss regional policy context or African institutional coordination; direct investigative participation should instead be attributed to the specific national agency or international organization named by the source.

Source Coverage

Current coverage is based on a legal-policy source summarizing AU cyber-security and personal-data-protection convention themes. More official AU source harvesting would further improve treaty status, institutional ownership, and implementation tracking.
