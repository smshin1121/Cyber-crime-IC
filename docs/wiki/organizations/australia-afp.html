개요Summary

The Australian Federal Police (AFP) is the primary federal law enforcement agency of Australia and operates the country's lead cybercrime investigation capability. The AFP established a dedicated Cyber Command in January 2022, funded with AUD 89.9 million through Australia's Cyber Security Strategy 2020. Cyber Command leads the AFP's response to cybercrime and operates the Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3), a purpose-built facility in Sydney that brings together all Australian state and territory police jurisdictions.

As a Five Eyes partner, the AFP is almost certainly among the most operationally integrated national cybercrime units with US, UK, Canadian, and New Zealand counterparts. The AFP's predecessor cybercrime unit, the Australian High Tech Crime Centre (AHTCC), was established in July 2003, and in 2008 the AFP launched the High Tech Crimes Operations (HTCO) branch.

Current Status (as of 2026-04-10)

Present-day name: Australian Federal Police — Cyber Command (under the Crime portfolio) Status: Active — Cyber Command and JPC3 continue to operate under the same names [5][6] Current Cyber Command lead: Assistant Commissioner Scott Lee (appointed under the 2025 restructure) [6] Reports to: Deputy Commissioner, Crime (responsible for AFP crime and cyber strategy)

As of April 2026 the AFP Cyber Command and the Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3) remain the lead Australian federal entities for cybercrime. A material restructure at the senior-executive level took place in 2025:

  • 2025 AFP senior-executive restructure: The AFP adopted a new four–deputy-commissioner structure in 2025 under Commissioner Reece Kershaw, explicitly "to better respond to complex cybercrime and other tech-enabled criminality" [5][6]. The four deputy commissioners are: Ian McCartney (National Security), Grant Nicholls (Crime — responsible for cyber strategy), Neil Gaughan (ACT Policing), and Lesa Gale (International & Specialist Capabilities).
  • Cyber Command leadership: Assistant Commissioner Scott Lee, formerly Assistant Commissioner for Counter Terrorism and Special Investigations Command, now leads Cyber Command [6].
  • JPC3 continuing operations: JPC3 in Sydney continues to bring together all Australian state and territory police jurisdictions for high-harm, high-volume cybercrime enforcement [5].
  • Indo-Pacific role: The AFP almost certainly retains its leading regional cybercrime partner role through 2025–2026, with continued cooperation on scam compound enforcement with Thai CCIB, the Philippines, and Indonesia.

The 2025 restructure did not rename or dissolve Cyber Command or JPC3; it elevated the cyber portfolio within the AFP senior-executive structure.

임무와 권한Mandate and Authority

The AFP has statutory authority to investigate federal offences including cybercrime under the Crimes Act 1914 (Cth), the Criminal Code Act 1995 (Cth), and the Cybercrime Act 2001 (Cth). Cyber Command focuses on:

  • Identifying risks to Australian networks and citizens
  • Prosecuting cybercriminal perpetrators
  • Domestic and international law enforcement cooperation
  • Cybercrime prevention and capacity building

  • JPC3 (Sydney): Multi-jurisdictional operations centre bringing together all Australian policing agencies
  • International Network: AFP International Network of liaison officers in strategic embassies worldwide
  • AHTCC (historical): Pre-2008 cybercrime centre, folded into HTCO then reorganised

국제공조 역량IC Capabilities

  • Five Eyes cooperation: Deep bilateral integration with FBI, UK NCA, RCMP, and New Zealand Police
  • INTERPOL and Europol liaison: AFP officers coordinate with interpol-igci and europol-ec3
  • Indo-Pacific focus: Capacity-building and operational cooperation with Southeast Asian partners (Philippines, Indonesia, Thailand, Malaysia, Vietnam)
  • JPC3 intelligence fusion: Pools intelligence across all Australian policing jurisdictions

주요 작전 및 사건Key Operations and Cases

The AFP has participated in Europol/FBI-coordinated operations against ransomware and botnet infrastructure and regularly conducts joint operations with Southeast Asian partners on online scams and child exploitation.

The AFP's cooperation capacity is highly likely one of the strongest in the Asia-Pacific, driven by Five Eyes integration, Indo-Pacific regional leadership, and the JPC3 domestic coordination model.
