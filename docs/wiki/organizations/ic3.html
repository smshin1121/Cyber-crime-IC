개요Summary

The Internet Crime Complaint Center (IC3) is the FBI Cyber Division's central intake and analysis unit for public reports of internet-facilitated crime. Established in May 2000 originally as the Internet Fraud Complaint Center, IC3 has become the single most comprehensive US national dataset on cyber-enabled fraud and cybercrime victimization.

IC3's 2024 Annual Internet Crime Report recorded 859,532 complaints with reported losses exceeding USD 16.6 billion — a 33% increase in losses over 2023. Over the 2020–2024 period, IC3 received 4.2 million complaints with a total reported loss of USD 50.5 billion (approximately 836,000 complaints per year on average).

임무와 권한Mandate and Authority

IC3's mandate is not investigative; rather, it is intake, analysis, and referral:

  • Public reporting portal (ic3.gov): Accepts complaints from victims of internet-facilitated crime, including business email compromise (BEC), romance scams, investment fraud, tech support fraud, ransomware, and cryptocurrency-enabled fraud.
  • Data analysis: Aggregates complaints to identify trends, campaigns, and criminal networks.
  • Referral: Routes actionable complaints to FBI field offices, other federal agencies (US Secret Service, DOJ, FTC, Treasury), and international partners.
  • Recovery Asset Team (RAT): Works with victim banks and foreign financial institutions to freeze fraudulently transferred funds, particularly in BEC cases where wire transfers can sometimes be reversed within the "golden hour."

사이버범죄 국제공조 관련 구조Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

  • Intake and Triage: Complaint processing and initial victimology analysis.
  • Analysis Unit: Trend analysis feeding FBI investigations and public reporting.
  • Recovery Asset Team (RAT): Freeze/recover stolen funds; works through FBI Legat offices with foreign banks.
  • Publication: Annual Internet Crime Report and periodic alerts/advisories.

국제공조 역량IC Capabilities

  • International liaison via FBI Legat network: IC3 data shared with foreign partners through the FBI Cyber Division's 63+ Legal Attache offices.
  • Cross-border fund recovery: RAT coordinates with foreign banks (particularly in Hong Kong, UAE, UK, China, and other common BEC transit jurisdictions) to freeze fraudulent transfers.
  • Intelligence products: Trend data informs international operations against BEC, romance scam, and investment fraud networks.

주요 작전 및 사건Key Operations and Cases

IC3 data routinely supports specific FBI and multinational operations, including:

  • BEC takedowns (e.g., Operation Rewired, Operation WireWire)
  • Romance scam arrest sweeps
  • Tech support fraud prosecutions
ℹ️ Specific operations citing IC3 data should be added as sources are ingested.

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

IC3 is almost certainly the most-cited US source for cybercrime victimization statistics globally and provides a unique intelligence advantage to the FBI's international cybercrime cooperation. Foreign law enforcement agencies highly likely rely on IC3 data when investigating US-origin or US-targeting cybercrime.

모순점 및 미해결 문제Contradictions & Open Questions

  • IC3's recovery rate via the Recovery Asset Team for fraudulent wire transfers is not fully disclosed. Public reporting suggests high "freeze" success rates but lower actual recovery rates.
  • How much IC3 data is shared with foreign law enforcement, and under what legal basis (MLAT, Budapest Convention, informal)?
  • Does IC3 accept complaints from non-US residents, and if so, how are those routed?
