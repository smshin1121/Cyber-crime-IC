개요Summary

The French High-Tech Crime Unit entry represents the French National Police cybercrime unit named by Europol as OCLCTIC-DCPJ in the Rex Mundi hacking/extortion investigation. The page should be used when a source names the unit directly, while broader police-institution context belongs on france-national-police.

Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The strongest repository anchor is rex-mundi-takedown. Europol's source describes a year-long investigation into a French cyber-extortion group that breached company databases and demanded bitcoin payments. The unit participated alongside the London Metropolitan Police, Royal Thai Police, Europol's 24/7 Operational Centre, and J-CAT, with the final arrest occurring in Thailand on a French international arrest warrant.

That case makes the unit relevant for three cooperation patterns: French domestic suspect identification, European operational coordination through Europol, and third-country arrest support when a French subject is located outside Europe.

IC Relevance

Use this page for French specialist police participation in cyber-extortion, hacker-for-hire, and breach-based ransom cases. Do not use it as a generic synonym for all French cyber enforcement; C3N/Gendarmerie and judicial-prosecution pages have separate roles in the corpus.

Source Coverage

Coverage is currently operation-specific. Additional French government sources would improve institutional history and current organizational naming, so older OCLCTIC-DCPJ references should be treated as source-bound rather than assumed current structure.
