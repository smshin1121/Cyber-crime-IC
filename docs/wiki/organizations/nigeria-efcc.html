개요Summary

The Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC) is Nigeria's principal financial-crime enforcement agency in this corpus. Its cybercrime relevance is strongest in business email compromise (BEC), online scamming, and fraud cases where Nigerian-based actors are identified through foreign victim reporting, malware telemetry, or INTERPOL coordination.

Cybercrime Cooperation Role

In bec-nigeria-2016, INTERPOL described an EFCC-linked arrest in Port Harcourt tied to a global online-fraud network estimated at USD 60 million in losses. That case combined INTERPOL coordination, EFCC domestic enforcement, and private intelligence from Trend Micro and Fortinet FortiGuard.

In operation-killer-bee, INTERPOL reported simultaneous Nigerian raids in Lagos and Benin City against suspects using Agent Tesla malware to support BEC and credential theft. The case again shows EFCC-relevant Nigerian enforcement as part of a wider international pipeline: private telemetry, INTERPOL's ASEAN Cybercrime Operations Desk, and foreign victim geographies across Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and North Africa.

IC Relevance

EFCC is a key institutional node for Nigeria-origin online-fraud enforcement. Use this page when the question is domestic Nigerian enforcement capacity or Nigerian execution of foreign-developed leads. Use operation pages for victim geography, malware details, and prosecution outcomes.

Source Coverage

The source set is solid for two named BEC/fraud operations but still light for EFCC's internal cyber organization. A direct EFCC institutional source would strengthen mandate and unit-level detail.
