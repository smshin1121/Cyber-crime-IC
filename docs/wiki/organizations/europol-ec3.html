개요Summary

The European Cybercrime Centre (EC3) is Europol's dedicated unit for combating cybercrime across the EU. Established in January 2013, it serves as the central coordination hub for major EU cybercrime operations, providing analytical, forensic, and operational support to member states' investigations.

EC3 is almost certainly the most operationally active international coordination body for cybercrime takedowns, having supported or coordinated landmark operations including: - Operation Cronos Phase 1 (Feb 2024): Disrupted LockBit ransomware group; 34 servers seized, 2 arrests, decryption keys released - Operation Endgame Phase 1 (May 2024): Largest-ever botnet takedown (IcedID, Smokeloader, Pikabot, Bumblebee, Trickbot); 4 arrests, 100+ servers taken down, 2,000+ domains seized - Operation Cronos Phase 3 (Oct 2024): 4 new LockBit arrests, financial sanctions against Evil Corp affiliates - Phobos/8Base Crackdown (Feb 2025): 4 arrests of 8Base leadership, 27 servers taken down; includes South Korea arrest and extradition - Operation Endgame Phase 2 (May 2025): 300 servers taken down, 650 domains neutralized, EUR 3.5M seized, 20 arrest warrants issued - Emotet takedown (Jan 2021): Disrupted Emotet botnet infrastructure across multiple countries [^emotet]

[^emotet]: > [!note] This information is based on general knowledge and has not been verified against a collected source.

임무와 권한Mandate and Authority

EC3 operates under Europol's mandate (EU Regulation 2016/794). It does not have independent investigative powers but supports and coordinates EU member state operations. Key functions:

  • Operational coordination: Hosting operational meetings, coordination centers during action days
  • Joint Investigation Teams: Providing logistical, analytical, and financial support for JITs
  • Analysis: Maintaining databases, cross-matching intelligence across investigations
  • Forensics: EC3's digital forensics lab supports member states with technical analysis
  • IOCTA: Annual Internet Organised Crime Threat Assessment

국제공조 역량IC Capabilities

  • Operational analysis and cross-matching of data from multiple investigations
  • Support for Joint Investigation Teams
  • Hosting of operational coordination centers for multi-country action days
  • Connection to 40+ non-EU partner countries through cooperation agreements
  • J-CAT (Joint Cybercrime Action Taskforce): Permanent team of liaison officers from member states and partners co-located at EC3

These operations demonstrate EC3's role as the almost certainly indispensable coordination hub for major EU cybercrime operations, particularly against ransomware infrastructure.

Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

Korea has a Strategic Agreement with Europol enabling cooperation. Korean liaison officers and data sharing arrangements facilitate cooperation on cases affecting both Korean and European victims.

The Phobos/8Base crackdown (2025) is the most directly relevant Europol-coordinated operation for Korea: a Phobos administrator was arrested in South Korea in June 2024 and extradited to the US in November 2024 as part of this Europol-coordinated investigation.
