개요Summary

The United Kingdom is one of the most active countries in international cybercrime cooperation. The National Crime Agency (NCA), through its National Cyber Crime Unit (NCCU), led Operation Cronos against the LockBit ransomware group — almost certainly the most high-profile ransomware takedown to date. The UK is a Five Eyes member, a party to the budapest-convention, and holds the world's first CLOUD Act executive agreement with the United States (2019).

Post-Brexit, the UK has had to navigate new cooperation arrangements with the EU, but operational cooperation on cybercrime has likely remained strong through the UK-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement and bilateral channels.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

Computer Misuse Act 1990 (CMA): - Unauthorized access to computer material (§ 1) - Unauthorized access with intent to commit further offences (§ 2) - Unauthorized acts with intent to impair operation of computer (§ 3) - Making, supplying or obtaining articles for use in offences (§ 3A, added 2006) - Unauthorized acts causing serious damage (§ 3ZA, added 2015)

Fraud Act 2006: - Broadly covers cyber-enabled fraud (phishing, BEC, online scams)

Serious Crime Act 2015: - Participation in organized crime groups, including cybercrime syndicates

Investigatory Powers Act 2016 (IPA): - Comprehensive surveillance framework; interception warrants, data retention notices, equipment interference - International cooperation provisions for responding to foreign requests

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

  • Search and seizure: Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 (PACE); warrants from magistrates
  • Production orders: PACE Schedule 1; Terrorism Act 2000
  • Data retention: IPA 2016; retention notices to providers for up to 12 months
  • Interception: IPA 2016; requires warrant from Secretary of State
  • Equipment interference: IPA 2016; remote access to devices (hacking) for law enforcement

Mutual Legal Assistance

  • Central authority: UK Home Office (incoming requests); Attorney General's Office (outgoing requests)
  • Crime (International Co-operation) Act 2003: Domestic legislation implementing MLA obligations
  • CLOUD Act Agreement with US (2019): First bilateral executive agreement; allows direct data requests to US providers
  • Post-Brexit EU cooperation: UK-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement (2021) includes MLA provisions; no longer party to European Arrest Warrant (EAW), uses surrender arrangements instead
⚠️ Legal status check needed Verify the current status of UK-EU operational cooperation agreements post-Brexit, including Europol working arrangement details.

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

As Requesting State

The UK actively requests MLA, particularly from the US (major tech companies) and EU member states. Post-Brexit, the UK has sought to maintain speed and efficiency of cooperation through: - CLOUD Act agreement with US - Bilateral arrangements with EU member states - Continued operational cooperation with Europol under TCA

As Requested State

The UK is likely a responsive cooperation partner with well-established processes. The NCA serves as the primary coordination point for incoming international cybercrime cooperation requests.

Notable Partnerships

  • Five Eyes: Core intelligence-sharing partner (US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand)
  • Europol: Close operational partner; NCA officers participate in JITs; operation-cronos-phase1, operation-endgame-phase1, phobos-8base-crackdown
  • US-UK: Deepest bilateral cyber cooperation relationship, including CLOUD Act agreement
  • operation-destabilise: NCA-led operation targeting Russian money laundering networks facilitating cybercrime

Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

Korea-UK cybercrime cooperation is likely conducted primarily through multilateral channels:

  • Operation Cronos: UK-led LockBit takedown; while South Korea was not a named participant, LockBit victims included Korean entities
  • operation-haechi-v: UK participated in Korea-led INTERPOL HAECHI V operation
  • Five Eyes+ engagement: Korea participates in expanded Five Eyes frameworks for cyber threat information sharing
  • Budapest Convention: Both parties since 2011 (UK) and 2024 (Korea), providing mutual MLA basis under the Convention
