개요Summary

The Netherlands is almost certainly the most operationally active European country for cybercrime takedowns relative to its size. The Dutch National Police (netherlands-politie), through its National High Tech Crime Unit (NHTCU), has been instrumental in major international operations including Operation Endgame, Operation Cronos, Hive Ransomware Takedown, and Operation Talent.

The Netherlands hosts both Europol and eurojust in The Hague, providing unique institutional proximity that highly likely contributes to the country's outsized role in cybercrime cooperation. The 2019 Wet computercriminaliteit III gave Dutch law enforcement pioneering powers for remote computer access (hackbevoegdheid), making the Netherlands a leader in innovative investigation techniques.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

Wetboek van Strafrecht (Criminal Code): - Art. 138ab: Unauthorized access to computer systems (computervredebreuk) - Art. 138b: Unauthorized access to automated work via public telecommunications network - Art. 138c: Interception of data - Art. 350a: Data manipulation (wijzigen gegevens) - Art. 350b: Computer sabotage - Art. 350c-d: Additional computer crime provisions

Wet computercriminaliteit III (Computer Crime Act III, 2019): - Significantly expanded law enforcement powers for cybercrime investigation - Introduced "hackbevoegdheid" — legal authority for police to remotely access computer systems - Controversial but likely the most advanced police hacking authority in Europe

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

  • Search and seizure: Wetboek van Strafvordering (Code of Criminal Procedure); includes digital evidence
  • Remote access (hackbevoegdheid): Wet computercriminaliteit III (2019); allows police to remotely access suspects' devices, install monitoring software, and copy data — requires judicial authorization
  • Interception: Requires judicial authorization; applied to telecommunications and internet communications
  • Preservation orders: Available under domestic and international cooperation frameworks
  • Production orders: Can compel providers to produce subscriber and traffic data

Mutual Legal Assistance

  • Central authority: Ministry of Justice and Security
  • EU instruments: European Investigation Order (EIO) for intra-EU requests; European Arrest Warrant (EAW)
  • Proximity advantage: Physical co-location with Europol and Eurojust in The Hague facilitates rapid informal coordination before formal MLA processes
  • FIOD (netherlands-fiod): Fiscal Intelligence and Investigation Service handles financial cybercrime MLA

주요 기관Key Agencies

Dutch National Police (Politie) — National High Tech Crime Unit (NHTCU)

FIOD (Fiscale Inlichtingen- en Opsporingsdienst)

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

As Requesting State

The Netherlands actively initiates international cooperation, particularly: - Investigations into dark web marketplaces hosted abroad - Ransomware investigations requiring US evidence - Financial crime investigations with cross-border elements

As Requested State

The Netherlands is highly likely one of the most responsive and proactive cooperation partners in Europe: - High English proficiency eliminates language barriers - Physical proximity to Europol/Eurojust enables rapid coordination - Advanced technical capabilities facilitate complex digital evidence requests

Infrastructure Host for Takedowns

The Netherlands has hosted critical infrastructure for major operations: - operation-endgame-phase1 / operation-endgame-phase2: Dutch police played central role in botnet takedown infrastructure - operation-cronos-phase1: Key participation in LockBit infrastructure seizure - hive-ransomware-takedown: Dutch police contributed to Hive infrastructure takeover - operation-talent: Participation in Cracked/Nulled forum takedown
