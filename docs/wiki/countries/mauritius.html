개요Summary

Mauritius holds a unique position in international cybercrime cooperation: it was the first African country to accede to the Budapest Convention (2013) and subsequently also ratified the African Union's Malabo Convention (2018), placing it in the small group of states party to both frameworks. Mauritius is frequently used by the Council of Europe and GLACY+ programs as a regional capacity-building hub.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

Cybersecurity and Cybercrime Act 2021 (replacing the Computer Misuse and Cybercrime Act 2003): - Illegal access - Illegal interception - Data and system interference - Computer-related forgery and fraud - Content-related offences

Data Protection Act 2017 provides a data protection framework largely aligned with the EU GDPR.

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

The 2021 Act provides preservation, production, and search-and-seizure powers aligned with Budapest Convention Articles 16-21.

Mutual Legal Assistance

  • Central authority: Attorney General's Office
  • Governing law: Mutual Assistance in Criminal and Related Matters Act
