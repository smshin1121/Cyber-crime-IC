요약

중국은 국제 사이버 범죄 협력 환경에서 복잡하고 중대한 행위자입니다. 첨단 기술 역량을 갖춘 세계 2위의 경제대국인 중국은 높은 디지털 포렌식 역량을 보유하고 있지만 국제 협력에 있어서 부다페스트 협약 프레임워크와 상당히 다른 주권 중심의 접근 방식을 취하고 있습니다. 중국은 budapest-convention의 당사국이 아니며 임시 위원회 프로세스에 따라 별도의 UN 사이버 범죄 협약을 적극적으로 옹호해 왔습니다.

중국은 양자 채널을 통해 일반적인 사이버 범죄, 특히 중국과 해외 피해자에게 영향을 미치는 국경 간 사기 및 보이스 피싱에 대해 선택적으로 협력합니다. Korea-China Qingdao voice phishing operation(2023)은 상호 이익이 되는 범죄 유형에 대해 실질적인 협력이 가능함을 보여줍니다. 그러나 i-Soon/APT27 사례에서 알 수 있듯이 국가가 후원하는 사이버 작전에 대한 협력은 거의 확실히 불가능합니다.

개요Summary

China is a complex and consequential actor in the international cybercrime cooperation landscape. As the world's second-largest economy with advanced technical capabilities, China possesses high digital forensics capacity but takes a sovereignty-focused approach to international cooperation that diverges significantly from the Budapest Convention framework. China is not a party to the budapest-convention and has actively advocated for a separate UN Cybercrime Convention under the Ad Hoc Committee process.

China cooperates selectively on ordinary cybercrime — particularly cross-border fraud and voice phishing affecting Chinese and foreign victims — through bilateral channels. The Korea-China Qingdao voice phishing operation (2023) demonstrates that practical cooperation is achievable for mutual-interest crime types. However, cooperation on state-sponsored cyber operations is almost certainly not available, as demonstrated by the i-Soon/APT27 case.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

刑法 (Criminal Law, 1997, amended 2009, 2015): - Art. 285: Illegal intrusion into computer information systems (非法侵入计算机信息系统罪) - Art. 285(2): Illegal acquisition of computer data (非法获取计算机信息系统数据罪) - Art. 285(3): Providing tools for illegal intrusion (提供侵入计算机信息系统程序、工具罪) - Art. 286: Destruction of computer information systems (破坏计算机信息系统罪) - Art. 287: Using computers to commit other crimes - Art. 287-bis: Illegal use of information networks (非法利用信息网络罪) — added 2015 - Art. 287-ter: Aiding information network criminal activities (帮助信息网络犯罪活动罪) — added 2015

网络安全法 (Cybersecurity Law, 2017): - Comprehensive cybersecurity framework - Data localization requirements (Art. 37) - Critical information infrastructure protection - Network operator obligations

数据安全法 (Data Security Law, 2021): - Data classification and protection framework - Restrictions on cross-border data transfer - National security review for data exports

个人信息保护法 (Personal Information Protection Law, 2021): - Restrictions on cross-border personal information transfer - Government access provisions

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

  • Search and seizure: 刑事诉讼法 (Criminal Procedure Law); includes electronic evidence
  • Interception: Broad surveillance powers under national security and criminal procedure laws
  • Data access: Law enforcement has extensive powers to compel data from domestic providers
  • Real-time collection: Technical monitoring capabilities are almost certainly advanced
  • Cross-border data restrictions: Data localization requirements under Cybersecurity Law and Data Security Law significantly restrict outbound data flows

Mutual Legal Assistance

  • Central authority: Ministry of Justice (司法部)
  • 国际刑事司法协助法 (International Criminal Judicial Assistance Law, 2018): Domestic MLA framework
  • Art. 4 sovereignty clause: "International criminal judicial assistance shall not impair the sovereignty, security and public interest" — broad refusal grounds
  • Bilateral MLATs: China has MLATs with 50+ countries, including Korea, US (limited), and others
  • Political sensitivity: MLA responses are likely influenced by political and diplomatic considerations
  • INTERPOL channel: China participates in INTERPOL operations selectively

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

Bilateral Cooperation (Case-Specific)

China cooperates on cybercrime when it aligns with mutual interests: - korea-china-voice-phishing-qingdao (2023): First major joint Korea-China voice phishing operation. Seoul Metropolitan Police and Qingdao Public Security Bureau arrested 16 suspects for voice phishing targeting Korean victims. Demonstrated that cooperation is achievable for mutually recognized crime types. - operation-haechi-v (2024): Chinese authorities cooperated with Korean police to dismantle a voice phishing syndicate responsible for USD 1.1 billion in losses. - operation-first-light-2024: China participated in INTERPOL anti-fraud operation.

Areas of Non-Cooperation

  • State-sponsored cyber operations: The i-Soon/APT27 case (2025) revealed extensive Chinese government-contracted hacking operations targeting, among others, South Korea's foreign ministry. Cooperation on such cases is almost certainly not available.
  • Politically sensitive cases: Requests involving Uyghur activists, Hong Kong democracy movement, Taiwan-related matters, or other politically sensitive topics are almost certainly refused.

Sovereignty-Focused Approach

China has consistently advocated for: - A new UN Cybercrime Convention (rather than expanding Budapest Convention) - Data sovereignty and data localization - State control over cross-border data flows - Non-interference in internal affairs as a cooperation principle

Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

Korea-China cybercrime cooperation is significant and complex, driven by:

Voice Phishing Cooperation

  • korea-china-voice-phishing-qingdao (2023): Landmark joint operation; 16 arrests in Qingdao for voice phishing targeting Korean victims (KRW 2.7 billion / USD 2 million in losses)
  • operation-haechi-v (2024): Korean and Chinese authorities jointly dismantled a voice phishing syndicate (USD 1.1 billion in losses, 1,900+ victims)
  • Voice phishing (보이스피싱) networks targeting Korean victims have historically operated from China-based call centers

Geographic Shift

The evolution from China-based call centers (Qingdao, 2023) to Southeast Asian scam compounds (Cambodia, Laos, 2025) reflects a geographic shift in criminal infrastructure. This may reduce the relative importance of China-specific cooperation while increasing the need for cooperation with ASEAN countries.

Korea-China MLAT

  • In force since 2000
  • Primary formal channel for MLA requests between the two countries
  • Effectiveness is roughly even chance — depends on case type and political climate

State-Sponsored Threat

  • isoon-apt27-indictment (2025): South Korea's Ministry of Foreign Affairs was among the targets of Chinese government-contracted hackers
  • This creates tension between cooperation on ordinary cybercrime and the state-sponsored threat dimension
