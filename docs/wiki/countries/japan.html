요약

일본은 능력이 뛰어난 국제 협력 파트너이자 budapest-convention(2012) 당사국이 된 아시아 최초의 국가입니다. 경찰청(NPA)은 국가 차원에서 사이버 범죄 수사를 조정하고, 일본 사이버 범죄 통제 센터(JC3)는 사이버 범죄 정보 공유를 위한 민관 파트너십 프레임워크를 제공합니다.

일본은 인터폴 작전에 적극적으로 참여해 왔으며 특히 미국 및 한국과 강력한 양자 협력 관계를 발전시켜 왔습니다. 일본이 한국과 함께 Operation Cyber Guardian에 참여함으로써 CSAM에 대한 지역적 협력이 입증되었습니다. 일본은 Five Eyes+ 파트너입니다(Five Eyes의 정식 회원은 아니지만 확장된 협력 프레임워크에 참여하고 있음).

個人情報保護법(개인정보보호법): - 데이터 침해 통지 의무 - 국경 간 데이터 전송 제한

절차적 권한

  • 원격 검색: Art. 99(2), 218(2); 검색된 위치에서 접속 가능한 컴퓨터 검색 가능
  • 보존: 부다페스트 협약 체계 및 양자 협력 하에 이용 가능

  • 데이터 보존: 필수 보존 체제가 없습니다. 자발적인 공급자 협력에 의존

개요Summary

Japan is a highly capable international cooperation partner and the first Asian country to become a party to the budapest-convention (2012). The National Police Agency (NPA) coordinates cybercrime investigations at the national level, while the Japan Cybercrime Control Center (JC3) provides a public-private partnership framework for cybercrime intelligence sharing.

Japan has been an active participant in INTERPOL operations and has developed strong bilateral cooperation relationships, particularly with the United States and South Korea. Japan's participation in Operation Cyber Guardian alongside Korea demonstrated regional cooperation on CSAM. Japan is a Five Eyes+ partner (not a full Five Eyes member, but engaged in expanded cooperation frameworks).

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

不正アクセス禁止法 (Unauthorized Computer Access Act, Act No. 128 of 1999, amended 2012): - Art. 3: Prohibition of unauthorized access (不正アクセス行為の禁止) - Art. 4: Prohibition of acts facilitating unauthorized access - Art. 5: Prohibition of unauthorized acquisition of identification codes - Art. 6: Prohibition of unauthorized storage of identification codes

刑法 (Penal Code): - Art. 161-2: Unauthorized creation of electromagnetic records (電磁的記録不正作出) - Art. 168-2: Unauthorized creation/provision of programs (不正指令電磁的記録に関する罪) — added 2011, criminalizes malware creation - Art. 234-2: Obstruction of business by damaging a computer (電子計算機損壊等業務妨害) - Art. 246-2: Computer fraud (電子計算機使用詐欺)

不正競争防止法 (Unfair Competition Prevention Act): - Trade secret protection; relevant to economic cyber espionage

個人情報保護法 (Personal Information Protection Act): - Data breach notification obligations - Restrictions on cross-border data transfer

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

  • Search and seizure: 刑事訴訟法 (Code of Criminal Procedure) Art. 99, 218; includes electronic evidence; requires warrant
  • Remote search: Art. 99(2), 218(2); allows search of computers accessible from the searched location
  • Preservation: Available under Budapest Convention framework and bilateral cooperation
  • Interception: 通信傍受法 (Act on Wiretapping for Criminal Investigation, 1999); limited to specific serious offences; requires court order
  • Data retention: No mandatory retention regime; relies on voluntary provider cooperation

Mutual Legal Assistance

  • Central authority: Ministry of Justice, International Affairs Division
  • 国際捜査共助等に関する法律 (Act on International Assistance in Investigation and Other Related Matters): Domestic MLA implementation
  • Cautious approach: Japan's MLA process is likely thorough but relatively slow due to procedural rigor and internal review processes
  • Dual criminality: Generally required for MLA; applied broadly for cybercrime offences

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

As Requesting State

Japan sends MLA requests primarily to: - US: Evidence from technology companies (most Japanese internet users rely on US platforms) - Asian neighbors: Regional cybercrime investigations - EU member states: For European-connected investigations

As Requested State

Japan is likely a reliable but methodical cooperation partner. The procedural rigor of the Japanese justice system means requests are handled carefully, which may result in longer processing times compared to some Western counterparts.

Notable Operations

  • operation-cyber-guardian (2025): Multi-country CSAM operation; Japan participated alongside Korea, Australia, and others — 544 arrests across 6 countries
  • operation-haechi-v / operation-haechi-vi: Japanese participation in INTERPOL operations against cyber-enabled financial fraud
  • operation-cronos-phase1: Japanese police identified LockBit victims and contributed intelligence
  • operation-synergia-ii: Japanese participation in INTERPOL operation against phishing/ransomware infrastructure
  • Emotet takedown (2021): Japanese police participated in the international Emotet botnet takedown [^emotet]

Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

  • INTERPOL operations: Both countries participate in operation-haechi-v (2024), operation-haechi-vi (2025), and operation-first-light-2024

  • Geographic proximity: Facilitates regional cooperation on cyber-enabled fraud and CSAM
  • Five Eyes+ engagement: Both Korea and Japan participate in expanded Five Eyes forums for cybersecurity
  • Diplomatic channels: Korea-Japan judicial cooperation proceeds through diplomatic channels; political tensions may occasionally affect cooperation pace
