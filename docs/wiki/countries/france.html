개요Summary

France is a foundational partner in European cybercrime cooperation. It was one of the early ratifying parties to the Budapest Convention on Cybercrime (ratified 10 January 2006; entry into force 1 May 2006) and operates a mature 24/7 Point of Contact at the OCLCTIC since 2000.

The 2023 LOPMI law (Loi d'orientation et de programmation du ministère de l'Intérieur) significantly modernised France's cybercrime procedural arsenal and has already been used in high-profile cases such as the 2024 investigation of Telegram CEO Pavel Durov.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

  • Godfrain Law (1988): Loi n° 88-19 du 5 janvier 1988 on computer fraud — pioneering legislation, codified at Articles 323-1 to 323-7 of the French Penal Code. Covers unauthorised access, hindering system operation, and data alteration.
  • LOPMI (2023): Loi n° 2023-22 du 24 janvier 2023 — Orientation and Programming Law of the Ministry of the Interior. Expanded procedural powers, introduced obligations for cyberattack victims to file complaints as a condition for insurance coverage.
  • Budapest Convention offences implemented via the Godfrain framework and subsequent amendments.

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

All procedural powers under Articles 14-21 of the Budapest Convention are implemented through the Code de procédure pénale (CPP).

주요 기관Key Agencies

OCLCTIC — Office central de lutte contre la criminalité liée aux TIC

  • Created in 2000 within the Direction Centrale de la Police Judiciaire (DCPJ)
  • Now part of the Sous-direction de lutte contre la cybercriminalité (SDLC)
  • Operates France's 24/7 Point of Contact for Budapest Convention, G7, Europol, and INTERPOL networks
  • See france-national-police

JUNALCO — Juridiction nationale de lutte contre la criminalité organisée

  • National Jurisdiction for Combating Organised Crime, Paris Public Prosecutor's Office
  • Specialised cybercrime prosecution section
  • See france-junalco

Gendarmerie Nationale — C3N

  • Centre de lutte contre les criminalités numériques (C3N)
  • See france-gendarmerie

ANSSI — Agence nationale de la sécurité des systèmes d'information

  • Created 2009; national cybersecurity authority (not law enforcement)
  • Technical support to investigations

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

France is deeply embedded in EU cooperation via European Investigation Order (EIO), Europol EC3, and Eurojust. As a Budapest Convention party and G7 24/7 Network participant, France maintains continuous cybercrime cooperation channels.

역량 평가Capacity Assessment

Almost certainly high digital forensics capacity and mature 24/7 availability. English proficiency in formal MLA correspondence is likely medium — French remains the primary working language for formal requests.
