요약

호주는 매우 유능한 국제 협력 파트너이자 Five Eyes 정보 동맹과 아시아 태평양 지역 간의 핵심 가교입니다. 호주 연방 경찰(AFP)은 세계 최대의 국제 연락관 네트워크 중 하나를 유지하고 있으며 특히 Operation Ironside/AN0M을 통해 초국가적 사이버 범죄에 대한 혁신적인 접근 방식을 시연했습니다. 이는 전 세계적으로 조직 범죄에 대한 증거를 수집하기 위해 은밀하게 운영된 FBI 관리 암호화 통신 플랫폼의 배포입니다.

호주는 아시아 태평양 지역의 사이버 혁신과 역량 구축을 위한 핵심 허브인 인터폴 글로벌 혁신 단지(IGCI)를 (싱가포르와 협력하여) 싱가포르에 개최합니다. 호주는 budapest-convention의 당사국이며 광범위한 양자 MLAT 네트워크를 유지하고 있습니다.

2021년 감시법 개정(식별 및 방해)법: - 데이터 중단 보증: AFP/ACIC는 장치의 데이터를 중단시킬 수 있습니다. - 네트워크 활동 영장: 범죄 네트워크에 대한 정보 수집 - 계정 탈취 영장: 온라인 계정에 대한 통제권을 행사합니다.

개요Summary

Australia is a highly capable international cooperation partner and a key bridge between the Five Eyes intelligence alliance and the Asia-Pacific region. The Australian Federal Police (AFP) maintains one of the world's largest international liaison officer networks and has demonstrated innovative approaches to transnational cybercrime, most notably through Operation Ironside/AN0M — the deployment of an FBI-managed encrypted communications platform that was covertly operated to collect evidence on organized crime globally.

Australia hosts (in partnership with Singapore) the INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation (IGCI) in Singapore, a key hub for cyber innovation and capacity building in the Asia-Pacific region. Australia is a party to the budapest-convention and maintains an extensive network of bilateral MLATs.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

Criminal Code Act 1995 (Cth), Part 10.7 — Computer Offences: - § 477.1: Unauthorized access to, or modification of, restricted data - § 477.2: Unauthorized impairment of electronic communication - § 477.3: Unauthorized impairment of data held in a computer disk/device - § 478.1: Unauthorized access to, or modification of, restricted data (lesser offences) - § 478.3: Possession or control of data with intent to commit a computer offence - § 478.4: Producing, supplying or obtaining data with intent to commit a computer offence

Cybercrime Act 2001: - Amended the Criminal Code to introduce Part 10.7 computer offences - Implemented Budapest Convention obligations domestically

Surveillance Legislation Amendment (Identify and Disrupt) Act 2021: - Data disruption warrants: AFP/ACIC can disrupt data on devices - Network activity warrants: Collect intelligence on criminal networks - Account takeover warrants: Take control of online accounts

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

  • Search and seizure: Crimes Act 1914; includes electronic evidence provisions
  • Interception: Telecommunications (Interception and Access) Act 1979 (TIA Act); requires warrant
  • Metadata retention: Mandatory 2-year retention by telecommunications providers (2015 amendments to TIA Act)
  • Stored communications access: TIA Act; requires warrant for content
  • Data disruption and network activity warrants: 2021 Act; innovative powers for proactive disruption

Mutual Legal Assistance

  • Central authority: Attorney-General's Department, International Crime Cooperation Central Authority
  • Mutual Assistance in Criminal Matters Act 1987: Domestic legislation implementing MLA obligations
  • Extradition Act 1988: Governs extradition to and from Australia
  • Australia has bilateral MLATs with 30+ countries
