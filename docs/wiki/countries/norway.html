요약

노르웨이는 비EU 국가이지만 깊이 통합된 사이버 범죄 협력에 관한 유럽 파트너입니다. Kripos(Den nasjonale enhet for bekjempelse av Organisert og annen alvorlig kriminalitet)는 국가범죄수사국(National Criminal Investigation Service)이며 국가사이버범죄센터(NC3)를 운영하고 있습니다. 노르웨이는 2006년 부다페스트 협약을 비준했으며 유럽경제지역(EEA) 협정 및 솅겐 참여를 통해 EU 메커니즘과 긴밀히 협력하고 있습니다.

📝 정확한 비준 날짜(2006-06-30)는 CETS 185에 대한 유럽 평의회 조약 사무국 서명 차트와 비교하여 확인되어야 합니다.

개요Summary

Norway is a non-EU but deeply integrated European partner on cybercrime cooperation. Kripos (Den nasjonale enhet for bekjempelse av organisert og annen alvorlig kriminalitet) is the National Criminal Investigation Service and hosts the National Cyber Crime Centre (NC3). Norway ratified the Budapest Convention in 2006 and cooperates closely with EU mechanisms via the European Economic Area (EEA) agreement and Schengen participation.

📝 Exact ratification date (2006-06-30) should be verified against the Council of Europe Treaty Office chart of signatures for CETS 185.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

Straffeloven (Penal Code) — 2005 general penal code, in force 2015: - Section 201: Preparation to commit data espionage - Section 204: Unauthorized access to computer systems - Section 205: Data interference / sabotage - Section 206: System interference - Section 208: Misuse of devices

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

Norwegian Criminal Procedure Act provides: - Search and seizure of electronic evidence - Production orders - Preservation orders - Telecommunications interception (court-ordered) - Real-time data collection

Mutual Legal Assistance

  • Central authority: Ministry of Justice and Public Security
  • Operational: Kripos serves as contact point for MLA and extradition, including in the absence of treaty
  • Schengen cooperation: Norway participates in Schengen Information System (SIS II)
