개요Summary

The Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK; commonly North Korea) is a non-cooperative jurisdiction in the public IC corpus on cybercrime. The state is the named threat-source for multiple US-jurisdictional public-record prosecutions, including DPRK-led ransomware activity attributed to entities such as the Reconnaissance General Bureau and Lazarus Group, and the fraudulent remote IT worker revenue-generation schemes documented in the Knoot/Prince case (May 2026) and the broader DPRK RevGen: Domestic Enabler Initiative.

IC Posture (Public-Record Summary)

Public US enforcement against DPRK cyber and IT-worker revenue-generation operations proceeds through US-jurisdictional charges against US-based enablers (US-based "laptop farmers" hosting victim-company-issued laptops in their residences while DPRK IT workers operate the laptops remotely from China and elsewhere) and through OFAC sanctions actions. The cited 2026-05-07 DOJ release attributes DPRK IT worker collective revenue at "hundreds of millions of dollars annually," with individual workers earning up to USD 300,000 per year, on behalf of DPRK Ministry of Defense and other entities directly involved in the DPRK's weapons programs.

There is no public-record DPRK-side cooperation in the cybercrime IC corpus. Korean (Republic of Korea / South Korea) cooperation against DPRK threats is documented separately at the bilateral US-ROK level, including the October 2023 joint US-ROK guidance on DPRK IT worker threats referenced in the cited 2026-05-07 DOJ release.
