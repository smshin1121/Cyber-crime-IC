개요Summary

Kenya enacted its first comprehensive cybercrime legislation, the Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act (Act No. 5 of 2018), assented on 16 May 2018 and commenced on 30 May 2018. The Act was drafted drawing on both the Budapest Convention and the African Union Convention on Cyber Security and Personal Data Protection (Malabo Convention).

The Act established the National Computer and Cybercrimes Coordination Committee (NC4) as the multi-agency entity responsible for coordinating Kenya's cybersecurity and cybercrime response, including facilitating international cooperation.

Kenya is not a party to the Budapest Convention. According to the Council of Europe Octopus country profile, the Kenyan Cabinet has approved accession, though formal ratification/accession is likely still pending.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act, 2018 (CMCA)

  • Act No. 5 of 2018
  • Assented 16 May 2018; commenced 30 May 2018
  • Key substantive offences:
  • Unauthorised access, interception, interference
  • Computer forgery and fraud
  • Cyber harassment, cybersquatting
  • Child sexual abuse material
  • Identity theft
  • Establishes procedural powers for electronic evidence
  • Establishes NC4 (Part II)
  • Contains provisions on international cooperation (Part VI)

Judicial Review

  • Several provisions of the 2018 Act were challenged in constitutional petitions on freedom-of-expression grounds
  • Provisions on "false information" and certain content-related offences have been subject to judicial scrutiny

주요 기관Key Agencies

NC4 — National Computer and Cybercrimes Coordination Committee

  • Multi-agency body established by the 2018 Act
  • Coordinates national cybersecurity; detection, prevention, investigation, and prosecution
  • Facilitates international cooperation for cybercrime matters

DCI Cybercrime Unit

  • Directorate of Criminal Investigations, Cybercrime Unit
  • Investigates cyber-related offences; established post-2020

KE-CIRT/CC — National Kenya Computer Incident Response Team / Coordination Centre

  • Mandated by the Kenya Information and Communications Act 1998
  • Hosted by the Communications Authority of Kenya
  • Officially launched 2014
  • National CERT; 24/7 operations

Office of the Director of Public Prosecutions (ODPP)

  • Prosecutes cybercrime offences
