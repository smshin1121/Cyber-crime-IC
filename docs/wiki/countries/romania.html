개요Summary

Romania is a major European cybercrime cooperation partner. It is a Budapest Convention party since 2004 (one of the earliest) and hosts the Council of Europe Cybercrime Programme Office (C-PROC) in Bucharest, which runs global capacity-building programmes.

Romania has a dedicated specialized prosecutor service, DIICOT (Direcția de Investigare a Infracțiunilor de Criminalitate Organizată și Terorism, romania-diicot), and a police cybercrime service within the Romanian Police's Directorate for Combating Organized Crime (romania-police).

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

실체법Substantive Law

Law 161/2003 (Legea 161/2003), Title III — on Preventing and Combating Cybercrime — was enacted to align Romanian law with the Budapest Convention prior to accession. The Criminal Code Arts. 360-366 later consolidated cybercrime offences [1][2].

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

Romanian criminal procedure provides for preservation orders, production orders, search and seizure of computer data, and interception. DIICOT has specialized investigative tools for organized cybercrime.

Mutual Legal Assistance

Central authority is the Ministry of Justice (Ministerul Justiției). Romania operates as both sending and receiving state in substantial MLAT volume.

조약 가입Treaty Memberships

  • Budapest Convention — party (ratified 2004-09-12) [3]
  • Second Additional Protocol — signatory (2022-05-12) [3]

주요 기관Key Agencies

  • DIICOT — Directorate for Investigating Organized Crime and Terrorism (specialized cybercrime prosecutors)
  • Poliția Română DCCO — Directorate for Combating Organized Crime, cybercrime service
  • C-PROC — Council of Europe Cybercrime Programme Office (international org, hosted in Bucharest)

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

Romania is almost certainly a high-volume cybercrime IC partner. Prior to 2010s it was a significant source country for financial cybercrime targeting Western Europe and North America; Romanian authorities have cooperated extensively with US and EU counterparts in takedowns.

역량 평가Capacity Assessment

High institutional capacity (DIICOT, specialized cybercrime judges), EU integration, C-PROC hosting, and experienced prosecutorial practice place Romania in the "high capacity" tier. English proficiency in prosecutorial ranks reported as medium.
