개요Summary

Hong Kong is a Special Administrative Region of the People's Republic of China with a distinct common-law legal system under the "One Country, Two Systems" framework. Unlike mainland China, Hong Kong maintains its own MLA regime, operates its own cybercrime enforcement (via the Hong Kong Police Force's Cyber Security and Technology Crime Bureau), and handles its own MLAT requests through the Department of Justice.

📝 Hong Kong is not a party to the Budapest Convention. The People's Republic of China has declined to join the Convention, and as the sovereign party, China is the only actor that could accede on Hong Kong's behalf. Hong Kong is therefore almost certainly not a Budapest Convention party and does not operate a Budapest 24/7 Point of Contact.

Hong Kong does not have a unified cybercrime ordinance. Cybercrime offences are scattered across the Crimes Ordinance (Cap. 200), the Telecommunications Ordinance (Cap. 106), and other statutes. In 2022 the Law Reform Commission Sub-committee on Cybercrime issued a consultation paper on cyber-dependent crimes and later published a report proposing reform, drawing on both the Budapest Convention and the UN Cybercrime Convention.

사이버범죄 법적 체계Legal Framework for Cybercrime

Substantive Criminal Law

  • Crimes Ordinance (Cap. 200)
  • Section 161: Access to computer with criminal or dishonest intent
  • Section 60: Destroying or damaging property (applied to data)
  • Section 59: Definition including "misuse of a computer"
  • Telecommunications Ordinance (Cap. 106)
  • Section 27A: Unauthorised access to computer by telecommunications
  • Theft Ordinance (Cap. 210): Offences applied to computer-related deception
  • Provisions likely outdated relative to modern cybercrime typologies

절차적 권한Procedural Powers

  • Search warrants under the Criminal Procedure Ordinance
  • Production orders available under statute
  • Specialised e-evidence powers likely limited compared to Budapest Convention parties

Law Reform Commission Work (2022)

  • Consultation Paper on Cyber-Dependent Crimes and Jurisdictional Issues (July 2022)
  • Report on Cyber-Dependent Crimes (subsequent)
  • Drew on Budapest Convention and UN Cybercrime Convention frameworks
  • Status of implementing legislation unknown

주요 기관Key Agencies

Hong Kong Police Force — Cyber Security and Technology Crime Bureau (CSTCB)

  • Primary operational cybercrime agency
  • Division within the Commercial Crime Bureau until CSTCB was established as a standalone bureau
  • Participates in INTERPOL and Asia-Pacific regional networks

Department of Justice — International Law Division

  • Central authority for Mutual Legal Assistance in Criminal Matters Ordinance (Cap. 525)
  • Handles incoming and outgoing MLA requests

공조 실적Cooperation Track Record

  • Bilateral MLA agreements under Hong Kong's own Mutual Legal Assistance in Criminal Matters Ordinance
  • INTERPOL NCB Hong Kong
  • Participation in APAC regional cybercrime forums
