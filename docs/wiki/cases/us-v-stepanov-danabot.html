United States v. Stepanov et al. (DanaBot — Operation Endgame)

: 2026-04-12 : 1

개요Summary

United States v. Stepanov et al. is the 16-count indictment of 16 Russian nationals for operating the DanaBot malware network, announced on 23 May 2025 as part of Operation Endgame Phase 2. DanaBot infected 300,000+ computers worldwide and caused an estimated USD 50 million+ in financial damages. The malware served dual purposes: cybercrime (credential theft, fraud) and espionage (targeting government and military entities).

⚠️ Source verification needed Key details (16 defendants, 300K infections, USD 50M damages) are primarily from Tier 3 news sources. Official DOJ indictment text has not been independently verified due to justice.gov access restrictions.

Facts

DanaBot was a banking trojan that evolved into a multi-purpose botnet platform. Key named defendants include Aleksandr Stepanov (aka "JimmBee"), 39, and Artem Kalinkin (aka "Onix"), 34, both of Novosibirsk, Russia. The malware's dual-use nature (cybercrime and espionage) distinguishes it from other botnet prosecutions.

International Cooperation Elements

Multinational Operation

The DanaBot indictment was part of Operation Endgame Phase 2, involving seven countries (Canada, Denmark, France, Germany, Netherlands, United Kingdom, United States) coordinated by Europol and Eurojust.

Infrastructure Takedown

Concurrent with the indictment, the broader operation resulted in 300 servers taken down, 650 domains neutralized, EUR 3.5 million in cryptocurrency seized, 20 international arrest warrants, and 18 EU Most Wanted list additions.

Legal Analysis

Jurisdiction

Public reporting suggests US jurisdiction was based on DanaBot's impact on US victims and networks, but the precise district-level theory remains under-documented because the underlying court record is not yet well surfaced in official sources.

Key Legal Issues

  • Source-verification gap: The present public narrative relies heavily on secondary reporting, creating a documentation problem that is unusual for a supposedly major US indictment.
  • Hybrid malware use-case: DanaBot's alleged use for both financially motivated crime and espionage complicates classification, victim analysis, and attribution narratives.

Precedent Value

Medium. If the official charging record is confirmed, the case will matter as part of a very large coordinated botnet action, but its documentary value remains limited until primary-source court materials are secured.
