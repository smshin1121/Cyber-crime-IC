United States v. Bondarenko et al. (Infraud Organization)

: 2026-04-12 : 1

개요Summary

United States v. Bondarenko et al. is the prosecution of 36 members of the Infraud Organization, a transnational cybercrime forum that operated from October 2010 to February 2018 under the slogan "In Fraud We Trust." The nine-count superseding indictment, unsealed on 7 February 2018, charged the defendants with racketeering conspiracy (RICO), identity theft, wire fraud, and computer fraud. Actual losses exceeded USD 568 million, with intended losses exceeding USD 2.2 billion.

The case is almost certainly one of the largest cybercrime forum prosecutions in history by defendant count.

Facts

The Infraud Organization had 10,901 registered members at its peak and operated as a "one-stop shop" for cybercriminals to buy and sell stolen financial data, malware, counterfeit documents, and other cybercrime services. The organization facilitated the sale or purchase of over 4 million compromised credit and debit card numbers.

International Cooperation Elements

Evidence Gathering

The multi-year investigation involved evidence gathering across at least seven countries. Public DOJ summaries do not identify the precise instruments used, but the arrest geography and synchronized charging posture strongly suggest a mix of formal requests and police-to-police coordination.

Arrest and Extradition

Thirteen defendants were arrested across seven countries: United States, Australia, United Kingdom, France, Italy, Kosovo, and Serbia. Sergey Medvedev, the co-founder, was arrested in Thailand and subsequently extradited to the United States.

Legal Analysis

Jurisdiction

The District of Nevada asserted jurisdiction based on the forum's operations affecting US victims and financial institutions.

Key Legal Issues

  • RICO for cybercrime forums: The indictment treated an online criminal marketplace as an enterprise rather than as a loose collection of unrelated offenders.
  • Distributed evidence and custody gaps: The case highlights how multinational evidence collection can succeed operationally even when several principal actors remain outside US custody.

Precedent Value

High. The application of RICO (racketeering) charges to an online cybercrime forum established that such forums could be prosecuted as organized criminal enterprises, not merely as collections of individual offenders.

Cooperation Effectiveness

The global arrest sweep (13 arrests across 7 countries on a single day) demonstrates effective multinational coordination, though the cooperation mechanisms used have not been fully documented in public sources. Cooperation was clearly operationally effective, but final judicial outcomes remain uneven because several core defendants were never placed before the US court.
