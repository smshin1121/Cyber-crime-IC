United States v. Chen Zhi (Prince Group / EDNY indictment + USD 15 billion BTC civil forfeiture)

: 2026-05-09 : 1

개요Summary

On 2025-10-14, the US Department of Justice announced the unsealing of an EDNY (Brooklyn) federal grand jury indictment charging Cambodian national Chen Zhi (a.k.a. Vincent, 37) — founder and chairman of Prince Holding Group (Prince Group), a Cambodian multinational business conglomerate with dozens of business entities in 30+ countries — with wire fraud conspiracy and money laundering conspiracy. The defendant remains at large; if convicted, he faces a maximum penalty of 40 years in prison.

Concurrently, the U.S. Attorney's Office EDNY and the DOJ National Security Division filed a civil forfeiture complaint against approximately 127,271 Bitcoin — currently worth approximately USD 15 billion — proceeds and instrumentalities of the defendant's fraud and money laundering schemes. The Bitcoin was previously stored in unhosted cryptocurrency wallets whose private keys the defendant personally held. The funds are presently in the custody of the U.S. government. The complaint is the largest forfeiture action in the history of the Department of Justice.

Facts

Since approximately 2015, Chen Zhi has been the founder and chairman of Prince Group, ostensibly focused on real estate development, financial services, and consumer services. The indictment alleges that, in secret, the defendant and his top executives grew Prince Group into one of Asia's largest transnational criminal organizations.

Forced-labor scam compounds

Under the defendant's direction, Prince Group operated forced-labor scam compounds across Cambodia perpetrating fraudulent cryptocurrency investment ("pig butchering") schemes. Compounds housed vast dormitories surrounded by high walls and barbed wire and functioned as violent forced labor camps. The defendant maintained:

  • Ledgers tracking profits and which fraudulent schemes were run out of which rooms.
  • Documents describing and depicting "phone farms" — automated call centers using thousands of phones and millions of mobile telephone numbers.
  • Photographs depicting beatings and other methods of torture of trafficked workers; the defendant communicated directly about beating individuals who "caused trouble" while specifying that the victims should not be "beaten to death".

US-targeted scam infrastructure

A Brooklyn-based Prince Group local network facilitated the fraudulent transfer and laundering of millions of dollars on behalf of Prince Group from 250+ victims in New York and across the United States.

Laundering methodology

The defendant and his co-conspirators laundered scheme proceeds through:

  • Professional money laundering operations.
  • Prince Group's online gambling and cryptocurrency mining operations — the defendant boasted that the mining business operating capital came from stolen victim funds, so "the profit is considerable because there is no cost."
  • 'Spraying' and 'funneling' cryptocurrency laundering techniques — disaggregation across many virtual currency addresses, then re-consolidation into fewer addresses to obscure the source of funds.
  • Bribery of foreign public officials in multiple countries to protect the criminal enterprise.

Asset use

Some criminal proceeds were used for luxury travel, entertainment, and extravagant purchases including watches, yachts, private jets, vacation homes, high-end collectables, and rare artwork — including a Picasso painting purchased through a New York City auction house.

Legal Analysis

Jurisdiction

US federal jurisdiction in EDNY (Brooklyn) attached through the Brooklyn-based Prince Group local laundering network and 250+ NY victims. The civil forfeiture complaint relies on the US government's prior custody of approximately 127,271 BTC tied to the defendant's wallets. The defendant himself is at large in Cambodia.

Charges

  • Wire fraud conspiracy (18 U.S.C. § 1349) — max 20 years.
  • Money laundering conspiracy (18 U.S.C. § 1956(h)) — max 20 years.
  • Total max: 40 years.

Precedent Value

This is the wiki's first record of the largest forfeiture action in DOJ history (127,271 BTC ≈ USD 15 billion). The case adds Chen Zhi + Prince Group to the wiki's pig-butchering / forced-labor scam-compound prosecution corpus, documenting:

  • 'Spraying' and 'funneling' cryptocurrency laundering techniques as a discrete laundering-methodology class.
  • Phone-farm criminal-infrastructure pattern (thousands of phones + millions of mobile numbers) as a discrete scam-compound operational class.
  • Trafficked-worker forced-labor scam compounds documented in primary IC documentation.
  • Concurrent administrative-and-criminal action architecture with the OFAC + FinCEN + UK FCDO/OFSI sanctions track on the same date.

Cooperation Effectiveness

The case demonstrates a fully-articulated US-UK criminal-cooperation pattern parallel to the OFAC-OFSI administrative sanctions cooperation under the January 2025 OFAC-OFSI MOU (described in the sanctions operation page). The DOJ OIA + UK NCA + Isle of Man Constabulary's Proactive International Money-Laundering Investigations Team + UK FCDO chain enabled the historic-scale forfeiture. The defendant's at-large status in Cambodia is the principal limitation on the criminal-track effectiveness; the asset-forfeiture track succeeded in capturing approximately 127,271 BTC.

Korean Relevance (한국 관련성)

South Korea is not directly named in the cited DOJ release. The case is structurally relevant to Korea given:

  1. Cambodian scam-compound exposure to Korean victims: Prince Group's pig-butchering scam compounds operate in Cambodia and target victims worldwide; Korean nationals are a known victim cohort for Cambodia-based scam operations as documented in Korea-Cambodia-Philippines repatriation pattern and broader Korea-Cambodia repatriation track.
  2. Trafficked-worker forced-labor pattern: scam compounds traffic workers from multiple Asian countries; Korean and Korean-diaspora workers have been documented as both victims (recruited under false job pretexts) and perpetrators (Cambodia-Philippines-trafficked Korean defendants in Korean repatriations).

Direct Korean LE involvement in this 2025-10-14 EDNY indictment is not enumerated.
