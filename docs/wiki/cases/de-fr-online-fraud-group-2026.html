개요Summary

In March 2026, Eurojust announced that a criminal group running an online fraud scheme in Germany had been dismantled following bilateral judicial cooperation with France, coordinated by Eurojust. The alleged leader of the group was based in France, making cross-border judicial cooperation essential to halt the group's activities. Victim losses are estimated at approximately EUR 1 million.

The case is almost certainly a routine example of Eurojust's everyday cybercrime casework — bilateral judicial coordination on a mid-scale online fraud prosecution — rather than a high-profile takedown. Its inclusion in this wiki documents the day-to-day operational rhythm of EU judicial cooperation on cyber-enabled fraud, even where individual cases attract limited public detail.

Facts

According to the Eurojust press release [1]:

  • A criminal group ran an online fraud scheme operating in Germany
  • The scheme defrauded victims of approximately EUR 1 million
  • The alleged leader of the group was based in France
  • The group was dismantled following Eurojust-coordinated judicial cooperation
  • "Leaders" of the group were arrested (specific count not stated)

Evidence collection likely involved coordination between German investigators (where the fraud scheme operated) and French authorities (where the alleged leader resided).
