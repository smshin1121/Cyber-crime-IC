## Summary

Asset Recovery is a cooperation or enforcement mechanism node used for operations where public reporting identifies recovered criminal proceeds, returned victim funds, or coordinated recovery of assets linked to cross-border cybercrime.

Current corpus coverage links this node to [[operation-serengeti-2|Operation Serengeti 2.0]], where INTERPOL reported USD 97.4 million recovered during a multinational African cybercrime and fraud operation.

## International-Cooperation Relevance

Asset recovery matters because cybercrime operations often separate victims, infrastructure, suspects, mule accounts, and proceeds across different jurisdictions. Recovery requires evidence sharing, payment-trail analysis, national seizure or recovery procedures, and coordination with financial or virtual-asset intermediaries.
