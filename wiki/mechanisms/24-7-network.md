## Summary

The 24/7 Network is a network of national contact points established under Art. 35 of the [[budapest-convention]]. Each participating state designates a point of contact available around the clock to provide **immediate assistance** for cybercrime investigations — particularly urgent data preservation requests to prevent evidence deletion while formal [[mlat-process|MLAT]] procedures are initiated.

Approximately **70+ countries** participate, including all Budapest Convention parties and some non-parties through the G7 24/7 Network (which predates and feeds into the Budapest network).

## Legal Basis

**Budapest Convention Art. 35:**
> "Each Party shall designate a point of contact available on a twenty-four hour, seven-day-a-week basis, in order to ensure the provision of immediate assistance for the purpose of investigations or proceedings concerning criminal offences related to computer systems and data."

The contact point shall be capable of:
- Facilitating or directly carrying out **technical advice**
- **Preservation of data** pursuant to Art. 29
- **Collection of evidence**, provision of legal information, and locating suspects

## How It Works

1. **Urgent need arises** — an investigator discovers evidence on a foreign server that may be deleted
2. **Contacts national 24/7 point** — through their own country's designated contact
3. **National point contacts foreign counterpart** — through the 24/7 Network
4. **Foreign contact initiates preservation** — expedited preservation under Art. 29 (60-day hold)
5. **Formal MLAT follows** — to compel actual production of the preserved data

The network operates on **police-to-police** or **prosecutor-to-prosecutor** channels, bypassing the slower diplomatic/central authority route for the initial urgent action.

## Scope and Capabilities

**Can do:**
- Urgent data preservation requests (Art. 29)
- Technical advice and guidance
- Facilitate rapid communication between investigators
- Provide legal information about domestic requirements
- Locate suspects

**Cannot do:**
- Compel production of evidence (requires formal MLA)
- Obtain content data
- Execute searches or seizures
- Substitute for formal MLAT in any binding way

## Practical Usage

The 24/7 Network is most valuable as a **rapid first response** that prevents evidence loss while formal processes catch up. The typical pattern:

```
Hour 0:    Evidence at risk of deletion identified
Hour 1-4:  24/7 Network contact → preservation request sent
Hour 4-24: Foreign 24/7 contact initiates domestic preservation
Day 1-7:   Preservation confirmed (60-day hold begins)
Week 2+:   Formal MLAT request prepared and transmitted
Month 6+:  MLAT executed, evidence produced
```

Without the 24/7 Network, the evidence would likely be gone by the time the MLAT request reaches the foreign central authority.

## Advantages

- **Speed:** Hours instead of months for initial action
- **24/7 availability:** Cybercrime doesn't respect business hours
- **Direct channel:** Bypasses diplomatic routes for urgent matters
- **Low barrier:** Informal request, no formal legal drafting required
- **Wide coverage:** 70+ countries

## Limitations

- **Preservation only:** Cannot compel production — a formal MLAT must follow
- **Uneven capacity:** Some designated contacts are not genuinely available 24/7
- **No enforcement:** If the foreign contact is unresponsive, there is no enforcement mechanism
- **Personnel dependent:** Effectiveness depends on the individual designated contacts and their relationships

## Korean Usage (한국의 활용)

With Korea's 2024 accession to the Budapest Convention, Korea has designated a 24/7 contact point. This enables Korean investigators to request urgent data preservation from 70+ countries through the network.
