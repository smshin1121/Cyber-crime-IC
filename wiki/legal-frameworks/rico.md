## International-Cooperation Relevance

RICO is relevant when a cybercrime group is treated as a durable enterprise rather than as a single incident. That framing can support broader conspiracy, forfeiture, and extradition narratives in cases involving distributed administrators, vendors, money mules, or infrastructure operators.

The practical question for this wiki is not merely whether the framework exists, but how it affects cross-border evidence, extradition or surrender, provider disclosure, asset restraint, seizure, forfeiture, or dual-criminality analysis.
