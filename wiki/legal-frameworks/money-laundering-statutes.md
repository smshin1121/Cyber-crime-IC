## International-Cooperation Relevance

Money-laundering charges are central to international cybercrime cooperation because proceeds often move through exchanges, banks, shell accounts, mule networks, and cross-border cryptocurrency services. These statutes support asset tracing, seizure, forfeiture, and mutual assistance requests tied to financial evidence.
