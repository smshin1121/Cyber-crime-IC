## International-Cooperation Relevance

For Korean cybercrime cooperation, the treaty is the formal route for admissible criminal evidence and assistance when direct provider requests, police-to-police coordination, or informal intelligence exchange are not enough. It is especially relevant to U.S.-hosted platforms and service providers.

The practical question for this wiki is not merely whether the framework exists, but how it affects cross-border evidence, extradition or surrender, provider disclosure, asset restraint, seizure, forfeiture, or dual-criminality analysis.
