## Summary

The **Cybersecurity Act 2018** is Singapore's principal national cybersecurity law, enacted in February 2018 and coming into force in August 2018. The Act established the role of the **Commissioner of Cybersecurity** (held by the Chief Executive of the [[singapore-csa|Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA)]]) and created the legal framework for designating and regulating **Critical Information Infrastructure (CII)** [1].

The Act is primarily a regulatory and incident-response instrument rather than a criminal statute — cybercrime offences are defined separately in the **Computer Misuse Act**.

## Historical Context

Prior to the Cybersecurity Act 2018, Singapore's cybersecurity framework relied on administrative coordination by the [[singapore-csa|CSA]] (established 2015) without dedicated statutory authority. The 2018 Act codified CSA's role and provided statutory backing for incident response.

## Key Provisions for IC

The Act is principally domestic in focus but supports international cooperation through:
- **Incident information sharing** with foreign CERTs and cybersecurity agencies under Commissioner's discretion
- **Mutual assistance** via MLAT or Interpol for cross-border cyber incidents involving Singapore CII

## Parties and Participation

This is a Singapore national law, not a multilateral instrument. It binds only Singapore and operates within Singapore's jurisdiction.

## Implementation in Practice

The **[[singapore-csa|Cyber Security Agency of Singapore (CSA)]]** enforces the Act and has designated CII sectors including banking, energy, water, government services, healthcare, transport, media, infocomm, and security/emergency services [2].

## 2024 Amendments

The Cybersecurity (Amendment) Act 2024 expanded CSA's regulatory scope to cover foundational digital infrastructure and entities of special cybersecurity interest.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- Interplay between the Cybersecurity Act (regulatory) and the Computer Misuse Act (criminal) in cases with both regulatory and law-enforcement dimensions
- Scope of Commissioner's information-sharing authority with foreign agencies not fully documented in public materials
