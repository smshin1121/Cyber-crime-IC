## International-Cooperation Relevance

For international cooperation, the CFAA matters because many cross-border investigations require a U.S. charging anchor for unauthorized access, botnet activity, credential theft, malware deployment, or computer damage. The statute helps frame dual-criminality discussions when suspects, victims, infrastructure, or evidence are outside the United States.

The practical question for this wiki is not merely whether the framework exists, but how it affects cross-border evidence, extradition or surrender, provider disclosure, asset restraint, seizure, forfeiture, or dual-criminality analysis.
