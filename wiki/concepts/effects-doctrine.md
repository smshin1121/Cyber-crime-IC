## Summary

The effects doctrine is a public-international-law and criminal-jurisdiction concept used to justify legal action against conduct that occurs abroad but causes significant domestic effects.

## Relevance to IC

In cybercrime matters, the doctrine is often discussed alongside [[territoriality-principle]], [[extraterritorial-jurisdiction]], and [[jurisdictional-conflicts]] because online conduct frequently crosses borders while still producing concrete domestic harm.
