## Summary

Extraterritorial jurisdiction is the legal basis for applying domestic criminal or regulatory law to conduct beyond national borders.

## Relevance to IC

In cybercrime matters, extraterritorial jurisdiction is frequently analyzed together with [[territoriality-principle]], [[effects-doctrine]], and [[jurisdictional-conflicts]] because networked conduct, victims, infrastructure, and suspects often span multiple states.
