## Source Profile

Institutional press release from the **Fiscalia General del Estado de Ecuador** (FGE), Ecuador's national prosecutor's office, dated **19 September 2024**, announcing the Ecuadorian leg of **Operacion KAERB** — the multinational takedown of the **iServer** phishing-as-a-service platform that targeted victims of mobile-phone theft across Latin America and Spain. The release frames the operation as a six-country prosecutor-and-police collaboration (Spain, Argentina, Colombia, Chile, Ecuador, Peru) supported by EUROPOL, AMERIPOL, PAcCTO 2.0, and the private-sector firm Group-IB.

This source enriches the existing Argentine Ministry of Security press release (2024-09-24, [[2024-09-24_argentina-gob-ar_operacion-kaerb-iserver-phishing-as-a-service-takedown]]) — they describe the same operation but emphasize different aspects:

- The Argentine source emphasizes the police-cooperation architecture, the Argentine-administrator apex, and the EUROPOL/AMERIPOL/PAcCTO 2.0 coordination scaffolding.
- The Ecuadorian source emphasizes the **prosecutorial-coordination dimension** (fiscalias of all six countries are named alongside their police counterparts), the Ecuador-side enforcement specifics (Santo Domingo de los Tsachilas, 2 arrests), and the per-country detainee distribution (which extends to detention locations beyond the six participating countries, including Bulgaria, Venezuela, and Uruguay).

## Reliability and Credibility

- **Reliability: high** — official Government of Ecuador domain (`fiscalia.gob.ec`), institutional press release under the Ecuadorian national prosecutor's office; tier-1 own-domain status per L24 / L25 strict.
- **Credibility: confirmed** — first-party announcement by one of the six enforcing prosecutor's offices; corroborates and supplements the Argentine source (Ministerio de Seguridad / Policia Federal Argentina, 2024-09-24); aggregate figures (17 arrests, six countries, 483,000 victims) match across the two sources.
- **Sensitivity: public** — distributed for general media use; identifies arrest counts and locations but not names of detainees.

> [!note] Translation note
> The Spanish term "fiscalia" denotes the public-prosecution authority in each civil-law jurisdiction; in Ecuador the corresponding institution is the "Fiscalia General del Estado" (FGE). The press release explicitly distinguishes "fiscalias" (prosecution authorities) from "cuerpos policiales" (police forces) when naming the cooperating institutions of each of the six participating countries — making this a useful cross-jurisdictional prosecutor-side artifact that supplements the Argentine-side police-emphasized release.
