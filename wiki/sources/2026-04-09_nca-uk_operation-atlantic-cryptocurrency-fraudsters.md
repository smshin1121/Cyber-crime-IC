## Source Summary

UK National Crime Agency Newsroom press release dated **9 April 2026** announcing **Operation Atlantic** — an NCA-led, weeklong, NCA-hosted international operation against cryptocurrency 'approval phishing' fraud held the prior month (approximately early-to-mid March 2026). Co-hosted by **NCA + US Secret Service + Ontario Provincial Police + Ontario Securities Commission**, with additional UK participation from **City of London Police + Financial Conduct Authority** and unnamed other international law enforcement bodies.

**Outcomes** (per NCA): **20,000+ victims identified** across UK, Canada and the United States; **$12 million+ frozen** in suspected criminal proceeds; **$45 million+** in cryptocurrency fraud identified globally; one UK victim with documented loss exceeding £52,000. A corresponding US Secret Service release adds that **120+ web domains** used by scammers were identified and disrupted.

The release establishes:

1. **NCA-led, US-Canada-UK tri-jurisdiction cooperation pattern** — a structurally distinct format from previous UK-led joint actions (which have typically paired UK with one other jurisdiction or with EU partners).
2. **'Approval phishing' as a named cryptocurrency-fraud sub-category** — a scam technique in which victims are tricked into *granting wallet access* to criminals (rather than handing over keys directly). Distinct from credential-phishing and investment-scam-impersonation patterns previously documented.
3. **NCA HQ as a coordination hub** — physical co-location at London NCA HQ as a deliberate operational format complementing remote intelligence-sharing.
4. **Private-sector real-time tracing as core mechanism** — explicitly cited as 'critical' to real-time illicit-transaction tracing and victim identification, *meaning their money could be secured before it was moved*.

## Reliability and Credibility

- **Reliability: high** — Tier-1 NCA press release with named NCA Deputy Director of Investigations and named co-host agencies.
- **Credibility: probably-true** — Operational figures (20,000 victims, $12M frozen, $45M total fraud identified) come directly from NCA. Same-day US Secret Service release independently corroborates the operation and adds the 120+ domain figure.
