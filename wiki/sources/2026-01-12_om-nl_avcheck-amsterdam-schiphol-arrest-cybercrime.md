# Source Summary — OM.nl 12 January 2026 Schiphol Arrest (AvCheck follow-up)

## What this source is

Press release ("Nieuwsbericht") issued by the Dutch Public Prosecution Service (Openbaar Ministerie, OM) — specifically the Landelijk Parket (LP, "national prosecutor's office") — on 12 January 2026, announcing the Sunday-evening (11 January) arrest at Amsterdam Schiphol Airport of a 33-year-old Dutch national suspected of operating a Counter-Antivirus (CAV) testing service used by malware developers.

The release links the arrest explicitly to a coordinated international operation of May 2025 in which Dutch THTC, together with the United States and Finland, dismantled a "key service for malware developers" (in externe verslaggeving geïdentificeerd als AvCheck.net en gerelateerde crypter-domeinen — onderdeel van [[operation-endgame]]).

## Why it matters for IC

- Tier-1 primary source from a Dutch prosecutor (OM.nl) — under-sourced publisher in the wiki.
- Documents a follow-up arrest in the Netherlands of a Dutch suspect who had relocated to the UAE; international flag (signalering) was a precondition for the Schiphol stop.
- Confirms named tri-lateral cooperation: Netherlands–United States–Finland.
- Demonstrates the typical post-takedown investigative cycle: infrastructure seizure → intelligence extraction → identification of operators → international flagging → arrest at border.

## Cross-checks

- Independent reporting (The Register 2026-01-13, Infosecurity Magazine, SC Media) corroborates that the Schiphol-arrested 33-year-old is the alleged AvCheck operator and that the underlying May 2025 takedown was the AvCheck.net / Cryptor.biz / Cryptor.live / Crypt.guru seizure announced by the U.S. DOJ on 27 May 2025 (Operation Endgame).
- The OM.nl release itself does not name "AvCheck" — that linkage is asserted in tier-2 reporting. Treat the AvCheck identification as **highly likely** (consistent timeline, agencies named, suspect profile) but not directly confirmed by tier-1 source. Flag in the operation page.
