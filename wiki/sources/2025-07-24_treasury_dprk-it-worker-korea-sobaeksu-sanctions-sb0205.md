## Reliability Assessment

Tier-1 official U.S. Treasury press release announcing OFAC designations. Reliability **high**, credibility **confirmed**, sensitivity **public** — this is a public sanctions notice tied to SDN List entries that are independently verifiable on the OFAC recent-actions page.

## Key Findings

1. OFAC designated **Korea Sobaeksu Trading Company** (also known as Sobaeksu United Corporation) and three DPRK individuals — **Kim Se Un**, **Jo Kyong Hun**, and **Myong Chol Min** — on 24 July 2025 for evading U.S. and UN sanctions through fraudulent IT worker schemes and front-company activity supporting the DPRK's nuclear and ballistic missile programs.
2. **Sobaeksu** is identified as a front company for the U.S.-designated **Munitions Industry Department** (the DPRK body overseeing the nuclear program and ballistic missile development). Sobaeksu has dispatched IT worker teams overseas, including to Vietnam, to generate revenue, and has been involved in nuclear procurement activities.
3. **Legal basis** for the designations is highly likely the most authoritative element of the release: Sobaeksu, Kim Se Un, and Jo Kyong Hun are designated pursuant to **E.O. 13382** (proliferation finance); Myong Chol Min is designated pursuant to **E.O. 13810** (North Korea-related). The parallel DOJ unsealed indictments of seven DPRK nationals invoke the **International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA)** for counterfeit-cigarette trafficking.
4. The release describes an explicit interagency posture: OFAC designations are coordinated with DOJ unsealings, State Department TOC Rewards offers (up to $15 million total, including up to $3 million each for Kim Se Un and Myong Chol Min), and operational support from FBI and HSI. It cross-references the FBI's 23 January 2025 Public Service Announcement on DPRK IT workers and the 16 May 2022 tri-agency IT Worker Advisory.
5. The release notes that DPRK IT workers in some cases "introduced malware into company networks to exfiltrate proprietary and sensitive data," characterizing the scheme as combining sanctions evasion, identity fraud, and potential cyber intrusion.
