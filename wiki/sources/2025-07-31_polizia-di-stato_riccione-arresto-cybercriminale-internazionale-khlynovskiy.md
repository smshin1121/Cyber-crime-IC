## Source Summary

Polizia di Stato (Italian State Police) press release dated 2025-07-31 announcing the arrest in Riccione (Rimini province, Emilia-Romagna) of an internationally wanted foreign national accused of having actively participated in serious cyber-extortion offences committed between 2023 and the first months of 2025 against U.S.-based organisations ("strutture statunitensi"). Ransom demands attributed to the cyber-attacks ran into millions of dollars. The investigation was conducted by the Polizia Postale within an "international cooperation device" between Italian police and the U.S. FBI; the federal arrest provision was executed by officers of the **Centro nazionale anticrimine informatico per la protezione delle infrastrutture critiche (Cnaipic)** together with agents of the **Centro operativo per la sicurezza cibernetica Emilia-Romagna** and the **Sezione operativa per la Sicurezza cibernetica di Rimini**. The suspect was located at a hotel in Riccione where he was on holiday with his family. Numerous IT devices and several thousand euros in cash were seized. The release closes by characterising the operation as the result of consolidated cooperation between Italian police and the U.S. law-enforcement community, strengthened by the deployment of liaison experts at the respective embassies.

The official Polizia di Stato communiqué does NOT name the suspect, his nationality, his age, the specific U.S. victim organisations, the specific ransom-demand totals (beyond "milioni di dollari"), the prison of remand, or the formal extradition instrument. Italian regional media on the same day reported the suspect as 42-year-old Kazakh national **Roman Khlynovskiy**, the FBI-attributed illicit profit total as approximately USD 31 million, and the place of remand as Busto Arsizio prison; these media-derived particulars are recorded as contextual notes in the raw file but are not asserted in tier-1 wiki fields.
