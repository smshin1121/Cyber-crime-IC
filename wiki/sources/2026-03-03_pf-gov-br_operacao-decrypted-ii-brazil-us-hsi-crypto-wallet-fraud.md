## Source Summary

Brazilian Federal Police (Polícia Federal, PF) press release dated **2026-03-03** announcing **Operação Decrypted II**, a Brazil-US bilateral cooperation action against a Brazil-based criminal association responsible for electronic fraud against cryptocurrency wallets held by a US-headquartered exchange and for **transnational money laundering**. The operation is presented as the explicit product of PF cooperation with **Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) — El Dorado Task Force, New York**, following information shared by the US agency. The Brazilian action-day component executed in **Imperatriz/MA (Maranhão state)** consisted of 1 preventive arrest warrant + 1 search-and-seizure warrant + asset-sequestration measures, predicated on continuity-of-crime evidence (dissimulated high-value crypto transfers after first-phase searches).

The release confirms an approximate underlying victim loss of **US$ 2.6 million** in stolen cryptocurrency, identified after ~1 year of PF investigations following the HSI information referral.

## IC Pattern

This release documents a **bilateral Brazil-US action-day** pattern in transnational cryptocurrency-fraud investigation, with the US side (**HSI El Dorado Task Force New York**) acting as the originating tipper and the Brazilian side (**PF**) executing the in-country arrest, search, and asset-sequestration on Brazilian soil. The operation is a **second-phase** continuation, indicating the first phase produced search warrants that the principal investigated party continued to evade — leading to escalation to preventive arrest. Notable features:

- **Bilateral cooperation explicitly named in subtitle and body** — PF + HSI El Dorado Task Force, New York.
- **Asymmetric direction**: US-side LE referred the original information; Brazil-side LE executed action on Brazilian territory targeting Brazil-resident participants who funded crypto-wallet theft from a US-based exchange's customers.
- **Continuity-of-crime as preventive-arrest predicate**: the Brazilian legal mechanism (prisão preventiva) was applied because one of the investigated parties continued the offence after first-phase search warrants.
- **No foreign extradition or arrest abroad** is reported — this is a Brazil-domestic action driven by US information referral.
