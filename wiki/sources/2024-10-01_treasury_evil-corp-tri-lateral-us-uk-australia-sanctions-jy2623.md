## Source Summary

Tier-1 U.S. Treasury (OFAC) press release JY-2623 documenting the October 1, 2024 tri-lateral US-UK-AU cyber-sanctions action against the Russia-based Evil Corp ransomware/Dridex group, plus a concurrent DOJ-EDTX indictment of Aleksandr Ryzhenkov for BitPaymer ransomware. The release expressly references the December 5, 2019 OFAC Evil Corp designations as the predicate, and is timed with day 2 of the U.S.-hosted Counter Ransomware Initiative summit.

## Relevance to IC

- **Tri-lateral sanctions cooperation pattern.** First wiki-recorded US-UK-AU 3-country financial-sanctions coordination on cyber. The same template (US Treasury + UK FCDO + Australia DFAT, E.O. 13694 / 14024 basis) recurs in the 2025 BPH chain (Zservers Feb 2025; Aeza Group July 2025; Media Land November 2025) — that 9-month BPH chain is the operational sequel to the 2024 Evil Corp action recorded here.
- **Sanctions-plus-prosecution combo.** OFAC designation paired with concurrent DOJ-EDTX indictment unsealing of one of the designees (Aleksandr Ryzhenkov, BitPaymer) is the canonical "designate + charge" pattern.
- **State-nexus designation.** The dual-authority designation of Eduard Benderskiy (E.O. 13694 cyber + E.O. 14024 Russian-state) ties Evil Corp formally to the Russian state via FSB / Russian Duma adjacency.
- **5-year sanctions chain.** Documents the 2019 → 2024 evolution of OFAC pressure on Evil Corp (original Dec 5 2019 designation of Maksim Yakubets and core members → Oct 2022 designations of NIK / Chayka / Troshin under E.O. 14024 → Oct 1 2024 expansion to 7 more individuals and 2 entities).
