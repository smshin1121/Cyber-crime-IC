## Summary

Europol newsroom announcement marking the one-year anniversary of **Operational Taskforce GRIMM (OTF GRIMM)**, the Sweden-led, Europol-coordinated international cooperation against **violence-as-a-service (VaaS)** — the model in which serious organised-crime principals outsource physical violence by recruiting young, often inexperienced perpetrators through online platforms. The release reports **280 arrests** across **11 participating countries** in the first 12 months (April 2025 – April 2026), with **more than 1,400 individuals** identified as linked to VaaS networks and **approximately 14,000 digital accounts** associated with online recruitment and coordination mapped.

OTF GRIMM is a cyber-enabled-violence node in the Europol cooperation portfolio: while the ultimate harm is physical, the taskforce's investigative subject is the **online ecosystem** through which violent contracts are advertised, priced, and assigned — encrypted messaging apps, gaming platforms, and mainstream social media — together with the **provider-cooperation channel** Europol has built with Google, Meta, TikTok, and Snapchat. The release does not designate OTF GRIMM as a Joint Investigation Team (JIT) and does not name Eurojust; cross-border enforcement proceeds via domestic powers, European Arrest Warrants where applicable, and Europol-channelled police-to-police cooperation across the 11 jurisdictions.

## Key Findings (extended)

1. **Verbatim 11-country roster**: Belgium, Denmark, Finland, France, Germany, Iceland, the Netherlands, Norway, Spain, Sweden, and the United Kingdom. The taskforce was originally launched in April 2025 with eight founding countries; the 11-country roster is the current Europol-named participating set as of the one-year anniversary.

2. **Coordination architecture**: Coordinating body — Europol's European Serious and Organised Crime Centre (ESOCC). Lead country — Sweden, with the Swedish Police Authority publicly named via OTF GRIMM author Asa Granstrom in Europol-aligned analytic outputs (NOTIONES, 2025-11-12).

3. **Sweden-specific footprint**: Approximately 120-130 of the 280 detentions are connected to Swedish criminal environments (Foxtrot network and adjacent groups). Swedish police superintendent Theodor Smedius is publicly quoted on the recruitment-of-children dimension.

4. **EU Most Wanted layer**: The anniversary action published three OTF GRIMM-linked high-value fugitives (two Swedish, one German) on the EU Most Wanted website. This converts a Europol-internal taskforce output into public-facing pressure on specific named principals — a procedural escalation step.

5. **Notable cases attributed to OTF GRIMM**:
   - **2025-03-28** — Three suspects (Sweden / Germany) arrested over the killing of three people in Oosterhout, Netherlands.
   - **2025-05-12** — Murder attempt in Tamm, Germany.
   - **2025-06** — Seven minors / young adults arrested in Denmark for encrypted-app recruitment.
   - **2025-07-01** — Six arrested in Spain over a planned murder; firearms seized.
   - **2025-07 to 08** — Dutch national arrested as alleged getaway driver for two minors involved in explosions in Germany.
   - **2026-01** — Minor arrested in Sweden for alleged involvement in a shooting outside the Alphen aan den Rijn prison facility (Netherlands); Europol's OTF GRIMM was central in establishing the Sweden-Netherlands case link.
   - **(undated 2025/26)** — Swedish organiser of serious violence linked to the Foxtrot network arrested in Iraq; extradition proceedings underway.
