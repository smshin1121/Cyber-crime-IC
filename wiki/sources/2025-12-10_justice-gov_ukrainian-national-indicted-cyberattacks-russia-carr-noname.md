## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release (Number 25-1158, dated 2025-12-10) announcing two Central District of California indictments unsealed against Ukrainian national Victoria Eduardovna Dubranova (33; aliases Vika, Tory, SovaSonya) for her role supporting two Russian state-tied hacking groups: **CyberArmyofRussia_Reborn (CARR / Z-Pentest)**, founded, funded, and directed by the Russian GRU; and **NoName057(16)**, a covert project staffed in part by employees of the Russian Presidential-decree-established CISM (Center for the Study and Network Monitoring of the Youth Environment, October 2018). Dubranova was extradited to the United States earlier in 2025 on the CARR indictment. She pleaded not guilty in both cases and is scheduled to begin trial in the NoName matter on 2026-02-03 and in the CARR matter on 2026-04-07. Statutory maximum penalties: 27 years (CARR) and 5 years (NoName). Concurrent with the release, the US Department of State offered rewards of up to USD 2 million (CARR) and up to USD 10 million (NoName), and a multi-agency Joint Cybersecurity Advisory was published by the FBI, CISA, NSA, DOE, EPA, and DC3.
