## Source Summary

Slovenska policija (Slovenian Police, Ministry of the Interior of the Republic of Slovenia) press release dated 2024-05-03, English-edition news archive, reporting on Slovenian participation in Europol-supported **Operation PANDORA**. The release confirms (a) that Slovenia was a victim country in the operation; (b) that the Slovenian police "took an active part in the operation Pandora as well, as there were also victims from the Slovenian territory"; (c) that the case turned on **months of intensive cooperation, in particular with the German law enforcement authorities**; (d) that since July 2023, Slovenian police had detected more than 40 cases of telephone-fraud calls under the guise of bank technical assistance, mainly targeting **legal persons (companies)**, with total damage of around **EUR 3.8 million** and **stolen funds flowing into bank accounts in Germany**; and (e) that International cooperation and prompt reaction by law enforcement working with banks were crucial to preventing transfers to **money-mule accounts abroad**. The release links explicitly to the Europol primary release of 2 May 2024 announcing that Operation PANDORA shut down 12 phone-fraud call centres on the **18 April 2024 action day**, with **21 persons taken into custody** and **39 suspects** identified, in raids by **German, Albanian, Bosnian-Herzegovinian, Kosovar* and Lebanese police forces** (Kosovo asterisk per UNSCR 1244/1999). The Europol record names the lead investigating units as the **Baden-Württemberg State Criminal Police Office (Landeskriminalamt Baden-Württemberg)**, supported by the **Federal Criminal Police Office (Bundeskriminalamt)**, with prosecutorial supervision by the **Freiburg Public Prosecutor's Office (Staatsanwaltschaft Freiburg)** and later the **Karlsruhe Public Prosecutor's Office (Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Karlsruhe)**, and notes specialisation by country (Bosnia-Herzegovina – debt-collection fraud; Kosovo – online banking fraud; Albania – investment fraud; Lebanon – prepaid-card fraud).

## Relevance to IC

Tier-1 primary source from a national police authority documenting the **victim-state perspective** of an Europol-supported, German-led multi-country call-centre takedown. Adds three IC-specific facts not visible from the Europol release alone: (1) Slovenia as an additional **victim jurisdiction** beyond the five Europol "participating countries"; (2) a **bilateral Slovenia–Germany police-to-police cooperation arc** that ran for "months" before the action day, with Slovenian police preventing "hundreds of thousands of euros" in losses to Slovenian citizens; (3) the **money-laundering vector**: stolen funds from Slovenian victims flowed into bank accounts in Germany, structurally connecting the call-centre takedown to a parallel money-mule layer. The release also independently corroborates the Europol-reported call-centre typology (technical-assistance impersonation of bank employees) from the Slovenian-victim angle. South Korea is not named in this release; no Korean nexus reported.
