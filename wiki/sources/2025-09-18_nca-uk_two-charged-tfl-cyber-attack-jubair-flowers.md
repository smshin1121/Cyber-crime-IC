## Source Summary

Tier-1 UK National Crime Agency press release dated 18 September 2025, jointly publicising the City of London Police arrest and Crown Prosecution Service charging of two UK-based defendants — Thalha Jubair (19, East London) and Owen Flowers (18, Walsall) — in connection with the 31 August 2024 cyber attack on Transport for London (TfL). NCA attributes the TfL intrusion to the online criminal collective Scattered Spider. Owen Flowers, separately, is charged with conspiring to infiltrate and damage the networks of US healthcare providers SSM Health Care Corporation and Sutter Health. Jubair faces an additional RIPA charge for refusing to disclose passwords for seized devices.

## Relevance to IC

- **UK-as-prosecution-venue for cross-border cyber attacks on US victims**: Flowers' SSM Health / Sutter Health charges demonstrate that the UK can stand up domestic cybercrime prosecutions for attacks on overseas victim organisations, reducing reliance on extradition where the defendant is a UK national resident on UK soil. Highly likely (80–95%) to require US-side evidentiary cooperation (victim incident data, server logs) which is consistent with the NCA's reference to FBI partnership.
- **Domestic-LE-as-cybercrime-channel**: The arrest and charging are structured around a UK chain (NCA + City of London Police + CPS + West Midlands ROCU + British Transport Police), with the FBI named only as an "international partner" supporting the broader Scattered Spider effort. Useful as a contrast case to extradition-track prosecutions ([[us-v-kai-west-intelbroker-2025]]).
