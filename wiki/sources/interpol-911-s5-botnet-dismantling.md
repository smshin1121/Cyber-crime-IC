## Summary

The INTERPOL press release announces the **9 April 2015** coordinated takedown of the **Simda botnet**, led by **INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation (IGCI)** in Singapore — IGCI's first major operational success after its 2014 establishment.

Key technical and cooperation facts:

1. **770,000+ infected computers** across **190+ countries**
2. **90,000+ new infections in the US** during January-February 2015 alone
3. **Top affected countries**: US, UK, Turkey, Canada, Russia
4. **10 servers seized in the Netherlands**; additional servers taken in the US, Russia, Luxembourg, and Poland
5. **Cooperation partners**:
   - INTERPOL IGCI (lead coordination)
   - Microsoft Digital Crimes Unit (MSRC-DCU) — forensic intelligence and sinkhole support
   - Kaspersky Lab — technical malware analysis
   - Trend Micro — threat intel
   - Japan Cyber Defense Institute — regional support
   - Dutch NHTCU — physical seizure execution
   - FBI — US-side cooperation
   - Luxembourg Grand-Ducal Police
   - **Russian Ministry of Interior Department "K"** — one of the few instances of direct Russian cybercrime-unit cooperation in a multinational operation
6. **Business model**: Simda was monetized as a pay-per-install (PPI) platform — Simda operators charged criminal customers per successful malware installation, making it a malware distribution service rather than an end-user attack tool

## Why this source matters

For [[simda-botnet-takedown]] this INTERPOL press release is the **primary Tier-2 source** and provides:

- **Authoritative confirmation** of the 770K infections / 190+ countries / 10 servers in Netherlands figures
- **Named Russian cooperation** — the participation of Russia's Department K is an important data point for the *historical* analysis of US/EU-Russia cybercrime cooperation, which has largely collapsed since 2022
- **IGCI operational debut** — this is IGCI Singapore's first major takedown and establishes the model for later Asia-Pacific-coordinated actions
- **Pay-per-install business model** detail — important for economic analysis of botnet monetization
- **Physical server seizure locations** — enables mapping of where Simda infrastructure was hosted

## Operational significance for cybercrime IC

The Simda takedown is a reference case for:

1. **Russia-inclusive cooperation model** — Department K's participation is *highly uncommon* and contrasts sharply with the post-2022 environment where Russian cooperation on cybercrime IC is effectively frozen
2. **INTERPOL IGCI as an Asia-Pacific coordination hub** — Singapore-based coordination for a Europe-heavy takedown demonstrates the *global* role IGCI was intended to play
3. **Public-private partnership at scale** — Microsoft, Kaspersky, Trend Micro, and Japan Cyber Defense Institute all participating reflects the 2015-era PPP model
