## Summary

Tier-1 press release issued by the Federal Criminal Police Office of Germany (BKA, bka.de) announcing the 2024-10-31 coordinated takedown of two cybercrime platforms — **Flight RCS** (clearnet drug marketplace) and **Dstat.CC** (DDoS-as-a-Service stresser directory) — operated by two German nationals (19 and 28) from Hesse and Rhineland-Palatinate. The action was led by the **Zentralstelle zur Bekaempfung der Internetkriminalitaet (ZIT)** of the Frankfurt am Main Generalstaatsanwaltschaft, jointly with **Hessisches Landeskriminalamt (HLKA)**, the **Bundeskriminalamt (BKA)**, and **Polizei Nordrhein-Westfalen**. Seven premises were searched across Frankfurt, Darmstadt, Rhein-Lahn-Kreis, and Rheinisch-Bergischer-Kreis.

The press release explicitly frames the action as part of the international **Operation PowerOff** umbrella, and explicitly acknowledges foreign-LE cooperation: "Im Wege der internationalen Kooperation erfolgten Sicherstellungen und Unterstuetzungen in Frankreich, Griechenland, Island und den Vereinigten Staaten von Amerika." (Through international cooperation, seizures and assistance occurred in France, Greece, Iceland, and the United States of America.)

## Operation Context

Operation PowerOff is the multi-year international DDoS-as-a-Service umbrella ([[operation-power-off]]) led primarily by US FBI + Europol with 15+ supporting countries. The October 2024 Hesse / Rheinland-Pfalz / NRW action constitutes a distinct German-led phase ([[de-hesse-rlp-flight-rcs-dstat-cc-poweroff-takedown-2024]]) within the umbrella, with the dstat.cc seizure being the platform-disruption component and Flight RCS being a co-incident drug-marketplace prosecution against the same dual-administrator defendants. The phase is distinct from the May 2025 Polish-led DDoS-for-hire phase ([[operation-power-off-2025-05]]) and the April 2026 PowerOff action week ([[operation-power-off-2026-04]]).
