## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release (Number 26-205, dated 2026-03-04) announcing the seizure of LeakBase — described by DOJ as one of the world's largest online forums for cybercriminals to buy and sell stolen data and cybercrime tools, with over 142,000 members and over 215,000 messages between members. On 2026-03-03 and 2026-03-04 law enforcement in 14 countries took synchronized action coordinated by Europol in The Hague: the United States and other countries shut down LeakBase, seized data and two domains, posted seizure banners, sent prevention messages to members, and collected additional evidence. Search warrants, arrests, and interviews were executed in the United States, Australia, Belgium, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Spain, and the United Kingdom; Canada, Germany, Greece, Kosovo, Malaysia, and the Netherlands provided significant assistance.

## Relevance to IC

This is the principal tier-1 source for the LeakBase 14-country takedown. It supplies (a) the cooperation roster split between active-enforcement countries (8) and assistance countries (6); (b) the coordinating venue (Europol in The Hague); (c) the US lead investigative office (FBI Salt Lake City Field Office); (d) the prosecutorial line (USAO–Utah, CCIPS Senior Counsel); (e) the named domestic US task-force participants (FBI San Diego Field Office, Utah Department of Public Safety, Provo Police Department); and (f) explicit lineage with prior DOJ-led marketplace takedowns RaidForums (2022) and BreachForums (2023, with founder convicted in 2025). The release does not enumerate the LeakBase domain names beyond "two of the domains used by the forum" or name a specific operating entity.
