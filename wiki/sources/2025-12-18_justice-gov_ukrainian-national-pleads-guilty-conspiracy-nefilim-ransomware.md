## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release (Number 25-1224, dated 2025-12-18) announcing the plea of conspiracy to commit computer fraud by Ukrainian national Artem Aleksandrovych Stryzhak, 35, of Barcelona, Spain, for his role as a Nefilim ransomware affiliate. Stryzhak was arrested in Spain in June 2024 and extradited to the United States on 2025-04-30. He is scheduled for sentencing on 2026-05-06 with a maximum penalty of 10 years. According to the release, Stryzhak gained access to the Nefilim ransomware code via the operator-known online "panel" platform in June 2021 in exchange for 20 percent of his ransom proceeds, and was directed by Nefilim administrators to target companies in the United States, Canada, or Australia with annual revenues exceeding USD 100 million (later USD 200 million). The Nefilim group's victim-leak site is named verbatim in the release as "Corporate Leaks."

## Relevance to IC

This is the principal tier-1 source for the Stryzhak Nefilim affiliate plea — the closing-out wave of a US-Spain bilateral extradition pathway opened in June 2024 (Spain arrest) and completed on 2025-04-30 (US custody transfer). It supplies (a) the Nefilim affiliate-tier compensation structure (20% revenue split), (b) the explicit Nefilim victim-targeting profile (US/Canada/Australia, USD 100M-200M revenue companies), (c) the "Corporate Leaks" leak-site name, (d) the named co-conspirator Volodymyr Tymoshchuk (administrator-tier; State Department TOC reward up to USD 11 million), and (e) the EDNY prosecutorial line with FBI Springfield Field Office (Illinois) as the investigating field office. This case parallels the [[us-v-volkov-yanluowang-sentencing|Volkov US-Italy extradition pathway]] and the [[us-v-deniss-zolotarjovs|Zolotarjovs US-Georgia extradition pathway]] in providing a third 2024-2025 European-arrest US-extradition record for ransomware-affiliate prosecution.
