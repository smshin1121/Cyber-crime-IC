## Source Summary

This is the canonical DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release for the January 23, 2025 indictment of Jin Sung-Il, Pak Jin-Song, Pedro Ernesto Alonso De Los Reyes, Erick Ntekereze Prince, and Emanuel Ashtor in the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Florida, a multi-defendant prosecution arising out of the DPRK RevGen: Domestic Enabler Initiative. The release is tier-1 (issuing prosecutorial body) and contains the indictment-date facts that anchor the case page [[us-v-jin-pak-alonso-prince-ashtor-dprk-it-workers-2025]].

## Relevance to IC

The release documents two distinct cross-border IC datapoints: (1) cooperation with Dutch authorities resulting in the January 10, 2025 arrest of Pedro Alonso in the Netherlands on a U.S. arrest warrant, and (2) acknowledgment of substantial assistance from the DOJ Office of International Affairs (the U.S. central authority for MLAT and extradition matters). It also names the IEEPA conspiracy theory for sanctioned-state actors as a charging tool against DPRK nationals working through U.S. entities — a recurring legal architecture in DPRK revenue-generation prosecutions.
