## Summary

This LKA NRW Pressestelle press release dated 18 September 2023, 13:36 announces the **second operational phase** of the Ermittlungskommission "Parker" (EK Parker) investigation against the DoppelSpider / DoppelPaymer ransomware group. The first phase had been announced on 6 March 2023 with simultaneous searches in Germany and Ukraine and the public Europol Most-Wanted listing of Igor Olegovich Turashev and Igor Garshin. The second phase ("erneuter Schlag" / renewed strike) executed search warrants in the week before 18 September 2023 against a different pair of suspects — a 44-year-old Ukrainian alleged key network figure (searched in Ukraine) and a 45-year-old man in southern Germany alleged to have received ransom proceeds (searched in Germany).

The release is significant for IC documentation because it explicitly names the cooperation structure: LKA NRW and ZAC NRW lead investigation in Germany; **U.S. Secret Service partnered on blockchain investigations** that identified the two new suspects; **Europol coordinates the investigation globally** and hosts the Most-Wanted publications; and **Ukrainian police executed the Ukraine-side search warrants** ("Polizeien anderer Länder ... in Deutschland und der Ukraine"). The release describes seizures of electronic devices, cash, and other assets at the prosecutor's request, parallel to the searches.

DoppelPaymer / DoppelSpider is one of the most consequential ransomware-extortion brands of the late-2010s and early-2020s, with over 600 victims worldwide, including the catastrophic 2020 Universitätsklinik Düsseldorf hospital attack (linked to one indirect fatality), the 2020 Funke Mediengruppe newspaper-publisher attack, and (per the LKA NRW account) an earliest-known attack against UK NHS healthcare in May 2017. Turashev's name also appears in the U.S. Dridex / Evil Corp investigations (see [[us-v-igor-turashev]] for the U.S. case), reflecting overlap between the DoppelPaymer crew and the Indrik Spider / Evil Corp ecosystem.

## Translation notes

- "Ermittlungskommission" (EK) = "investigation commission" — a German police task-force structure for a specific investigation, NOT a separate organizational unit
- "Zentral- und Ansprechstelle Cybercrime" (ZAC NRW) = "Central Office and Contact Point for Cybercrime" — the NRW prosecutor-side cybercrime liaison body, attached to the Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Köln
- "Schlüsselfunktion" = "key function" — used in the release to characterize the 44-year-old Ukrainian's alleged role
- "gewerbsmäßige, digitale Erpressung" = "commercial digital extortion" — German criminal law category, §§ 253, 263a StGB
