## Summary

CyberScoop covers the **Operation Falcon** announcement — a year-long investigation led by Interpol, with intelligence support from Group-IB and field action by the Nigeria Police Force, targeting the Nigerian BEC group known as **TMT**. Key facts reported:

1. **Three suspects** arrested in Lagos (identities withheld, released only as initials O.C., I.O., O.I.)
2. The three suspects were allegedly tied to **approximately 50,000 direct victims**
3. Group-IB's wider intelligence assessment suggested TMT compromised **500,000+** victims overall since 2017, across **more than 150 countries**
4. **TMT methodology**: mass phishing campaigns, commodity info-stealers (AgentTesla, Loki, AzoRult) and RATs (NetWire); COVID-19 themed lures (fake aid offers)
5. **Macro context**: FBI IC3 recorded ~USD 1.7 billion in BEC losses for 2019 alone, illustrating the scale of the Nigerian BEC threat
