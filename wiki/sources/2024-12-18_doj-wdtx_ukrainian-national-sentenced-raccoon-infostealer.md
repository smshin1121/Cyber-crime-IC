## Source Summary

USAO-WDTX press release dated 2024-12-18 announcing the 60-month federal-prison sentencing of Ukrainian national Mark Sokolovsky for operating the Raccoon Infostealer malware-as-a-service. The release records the full international-cooperation chain: arrest by Dutch authorities in March 2022 concurrent with a joint FBI + Italy + Netherlands infrastructure dismantle of the Raccoon Infostealer; extradition from the Netherlands to the US in February 2024; guilty plea in October 2024; and 2024-12-18 sentencing.

## IC Pattern

The release establishes a US-Netherlands-Italy three-country cooperation pattern for malware-as-a-service infrastructure takedown plus single-defendant extradition prosecution. Specifically: simultaneous March 2022 (a) Dutch arrest of Sokolovsky and (b) FBI + Italy + Netherlands joint dismantling of Raccoon Infostealer infrastructure, followed by Netherlands → US extradition in February 2024 (24 months between arrest and extradition, partly attributable to defendant's contested-extradition appeal). This is a discrete IC-mechanism class — coordinated arrest-of-operator + concurrent infrastructure-takedown — that the wiki has documented previously for botnet operations but not specifically for the Raccoon Infostealer / MaaS class until this source.
