## Source Summary

Official press release issued on **2026-03-09** by the **Comunicação Social da Polícia Federal em São Paulo** (SCS/SR/SP) announcing the arrest of a **Belgian national** in São Paulo on a Brazilian Supreme Federal Court (STF) **arrest warrant for extradition purposes**, in response to a Belgian extradition request. The Belgian underlying charges enumerated by PF include document forgery, criminal threat, fraud, **information-technology forgery (falsificação de tecnologia da informação)**, breach of trust, and unspecified additional offences.

This places the case in the cybercrime international cooperation domain because the IT-forgery charge corresponds to what the [[budapest-convention|Council of Europe Budapest Convention]] classifies as **computer-related forgery (Article 7)** — a Convention to which both Brazil (acceded 2023) and Belgium (Party since 2012) are bound.

> [!note] Translation note
> *Falsificação de tecnologia da informação* is not a Brazilian Penal Code charge category — it is the PF's translation of the Belgian charge, which under Belgian law most likely corresponds to **falsification informatique / informaticavalsheid** (Art. 210bis Code pénal / Strafwetboek). This in turn maps to **computer-related forgery (Art. 7)** in the Budapest Convention. The PF wording is functionally equivalent but is not a Brazilian statutory term, so the Brazilian counterpart offence in the dual-criminality assessment (see [[dual-criminality]]) would be analysed under Lei 12.737/2012 (Lei Carolina Dieckmann) and Penal Code Arts. 154-A/154-B (computer-data invasion) plus Art. 298 (document forgery).
