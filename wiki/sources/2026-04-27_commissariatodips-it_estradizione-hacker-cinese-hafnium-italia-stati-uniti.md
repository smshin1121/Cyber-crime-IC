## Summary

This is the **Italian primary-source press release** for the Italy → US extradition of the PRC-national HAFNIUM-campaign hacker (referred to as "X. Z." in the Italian source for presumption-of-innocence reasons; corresponds to **Xu Zewei** as named in the parallel US DOJ release of the same date). It is the partner-state perspective on the operation already documented from the US side in [[us-v-xu-zewei-hafnium-extradited-italy|US v. Xu Zewei et al.]] and in [[2026-04-27_justice-gov_prolific-chinese-state-sponsored-contract-hacker-extradited-italy|the corresponding US DOJ press release]].

The Italian release adds:

1. The full chain of Italian agencies/authorities involved in the arrest and extradition (previously only summarised as "Italian National Police — Cyber Division" in the US DOJ release).
2. Confirmation of the **judicial decision pathway**: Corte d'Appello di Milano + Ministro della giustizia, which is the standard Italian extradition authorisation chain.
3. Confirmation of the **arrest scene specifics**: airport interception, suspect with his wife, claimed holiday purpose.
4. Italian-law framing of the underlying offences: associazione per delinquere, accesso abusivo a sistemi informatici, frode informatica, furto d'identità — relevant to dual-criminality analysis for any future extradition challenge.

The release does **not** add new substantive facts about the underlying HAFNIUM intrusions beyond what the US DOJ release already disclosed (Powerock, MSS Shanghai State Security Bureau, Phase 1 COVID-19 research targeting, Phase 2 Microsoft Exchange compromise of 12,700+ US organisations) — those operational details remain in the US case page. The Italian release is the bilateral-cooperation operational record.
