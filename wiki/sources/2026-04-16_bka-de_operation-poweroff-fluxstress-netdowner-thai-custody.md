## Summary

Joint press release from the German Federal Criminal Police Office (Bundeskriminalamt, BKA) and the Frankfurt am Main General Prosecutor's Office / Central Office for Combating Internet Crime (Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Frankfurt am Main – Zentralstelle zur Bekämpfung der Internetkriminalität, ZIT), dated 2026-04-16, reporting the latest sprint of the long-running Europol-supported Operation PowerOFF. The release explicitly identifies the **German national in Thai custody** as the ZIT-led suspect alleged to have operated **Fluxstress** and **Netdowner**, two of the world's most significant DDoS-for-hire stresser services. This is the German LE side of the Bangkok 2026-04-11 arrest reported by the Royal Thai Police / Cybercrime Investigation Bureau.

## Why this source matters

The primary source for the Bangkok arrest (Thailand CCIB, 2026-04-11) anchors the operation on the Thai LE side. This BKA + ZIT release anchors the same operational fact from the **German LE perspective**: it confirms (a) Thai custody is the suspect's current location, (b) Fluxstress + Netdowner are the platforms in question, (c) the underlying German arrest warrant is § 127 StGB-based, (d) ZIT is the German lead-prosecution authority. Adding this as the operation page's second source meets the L24 strict tier-1 own-domain requirement from a second jurisdiction (bka.de = German federal police own domain) and provides the bilateral DE-TH cooperation evidence required for IC classification.
