## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release (Number 25-929, dated 2025-09-09) announcing the unsealing in the Eastern District of New York of a superseding indictment charging Ukrainian national Volodymyr Viktorovich Tymoshchuk (aliases: deadforz, Boba, msfv, farnetwork) as an administrator of the LockerGoga, MegaCortex, and Nefilim ransomware schemes. The activity period is December 2018 to October 2021. The Tymoshchuk indictment is the administrator-tier counterpart to the affiliate-tier prosecution of Artem Stryzhak (extradited from Spain to the EDNY in April 2025; pleaded guilty 2025-12-18). The two indictments are explicitly cross-linked in the cited release: "Tymoshchuk and the other Nefilim administrators provided other Nefilim ransomware affiliates, including co-defendant Artem Stryzhak, who was extradited from Spain and faces charges in the Eastern District of New York, with access to the Nefilim ransomware in exchange for 20 percent of the ransom proceeds extorted from Nefilim victims."

The release records (a) the multi-country victim cohort: more than 250 US companies plus hundreds worldwide in countries including the US, France, Germany, the Netherlands, Norway, and Switzerland; (b) the September 2022 release of LockerGoga and MegaCortex decryption keys via the No More Ransomware Project; (c) the multilateral cooperation roster of 9 cooperating countries (France, Czech Republic, Germany, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Netherlands, Norway, Switzerland, Ukraine) plus Europol and Eurojust via ICHIP The Hague; and (d) the State Department TOC Rewards Program offering up to USD 11 million for information leading to Tymoshchuk's arrest, conviction, or location.

## Relevance to IC

This is the principal tier-1 source for the administrator-tier prosecution of the LockerGoga/MegaCortex/Nefilim ransomware cluster. It supplies (a) the explicit administrator/affiliate compensation structure (20% of ransom proceeds to administrators), (b) the multi-jurisdictional victim cohort and country roster, (c) the September 2022 decryption-key release that demonstrates DOJ's "proactive law enforcement action" approach, (d) the explicit cross-link to the [[us-v-stryzhak-nefilim|Stryzhak affiliate-tier case]], and (e) the 9-country cooperation roster plus Europol and Eurojust through the ICHIP The Hague venue.
