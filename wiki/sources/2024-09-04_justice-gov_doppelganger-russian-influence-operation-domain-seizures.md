## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release, dated 2024-09-04, announcing (a) seizure of 32 internet domains used in Russia's Doppelganger covert foreign malign influence operation, and (b) the unsealing of an SDNY indictment of two RT employees for funneling ~$9.7M to Tennessee-based Tenet Media to publish covert pro-Russian content. Treasury OFAC issued parallel designations the same day. The DOJ release names no foreign cooperation partner.

## Relevance to IC

This source documents [[us-doj-doppelganger-russian-influence-op-domain-seizures-2024]]. It is a borderline IC-scope source: the underlying conduct is transnational (Russian state-directed influence affecting U.S. and "elsewhere"), and the indicted defendants are Russian nationals beyond U.S. arrest reach, but no foreign LE counterpart is credited. The release is preserved for the wiki because it illustrates the U.S. pattern of substituting **unilateral domain seizure + sanctions** for cooperation pathways that are unavailable when the host state (Russia) is uncooperative — a useful comparison for IC effectiveness analysis.
