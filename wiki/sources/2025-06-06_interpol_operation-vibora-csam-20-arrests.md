# INTERPOL — 20 arrested in international operation targeting child sexual abuse material (Operation Vibora)

INTERPOL press release dated 6 June 2025 announcing results of Operation Vibora, a multinational child sexual abuse material (CSAM) investigation initiated and led by the Spanish National Police (Cuerpo Nacional de Policía) and coordinated with INTERPOL across the Americas and Europe.

## Summary

The Spanish National Police launched the investigation in late 2024 after online patrols identified messaging groups distributing CSAM. INTERPOL's Crimes against Children unit facilitated cross-regional cooperation, inviting Spanish investigators to attend the December 2024 Latin America Victim Identification Task Force meeting in Chile. There, Spanish authorities presented Operation Vibora to specialised officers from Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Costa Rica, El Salvador, Honduras, and Paraguay. Follow-up sessions in April 2025 aligned operational efforts ahead of the March-May 2025 arrest wave.

## Results

- 20 suspects arrested across 12 countries (March-May 2025).
- 7 arrests in Spain (including a healthcare worker and a teacher).
- 10 arrests across 7 Latin American countries (including 3 in El Salvador, 1 teacher in Panama).
- Remaining arrests across other European countries and the United States.
- 68 additional suspects identified; investigations ongoing.
- Seizures: desktop computers, laptops, mobile phones, tablets, digital storage devices.
