# Source Summary — Taiwan CIB-NPA (17 July 2024) Trilateral Mobile Trojan Fraud Dismantling

## Provenance

Official press release from the Criminal Investigation Bureau, National Police Agency of Taiwan (CIB-NPA), published in the agency's English News Releases section on 17 July 2024. The release announces the conclusion of a trilateral joint investigation with the Singapore Police Force (SPF) and the Royal Malaysian Police (CCID-RMP) against a transnational Android-mobile-trojan fraud syndicate that victimised Singaporeans in 2023–2024.

This release is the **Taiwan-side tier-1 primary statement** for the same operation that the SPF announced on 14 June 2024 and the Hong Kong Police Force separately codenames **DISTANTHILL**. The CIB-NPA release covers the Taiwan-investigation perspective (Kaohsiung City raid, Taiwan money-mule and crypto-laundering arrests, Taiwan-side asset seizures, Ciaotou prosecutorial forwarding) and is the only tier-1 own-domain source documenting the operational naming convention "first instance of trilateral police cooperation among Taiwan, Singapore, and Malaysia."

## Key Findings

- **L24-compliant cooperation architecture**: Three distinct LE/prosecutor agencies named in the tier-1 primary release —
  1. Criminal Investigation Bureau, National Police Agency (Taiwan)
  2. Criminal Investigation Department (CID) + Commercial Affairs Department (CAD), Singapore Police Force
  3. Commercial Crime Investigation Department, Royal Malaysian Police (CCID-RMP)

  Plus the Taiwan Ciaotou District Prosecutors Office (prosecutorial body) and four Taiwan local police-department criminal investigation brigades (Kaohsiung City, Pingtung County, Penghu County, Taipei City Nangang Precinct).

- **Codename consistency check**: CIB-NPA does not use "DISTANTHILL" (the HKPF Force-internal codename). CIB-NPA describes the same operation operationally as "the first instance of trilateral police cooperation among Taiwan, Singapore, and Malaysia in fighting crime." Naming triangulation: SPF = "multi-jurisdiction operation" (no codename); HKPF = "DISTANTHILL"; CIB-NPA = "first trilateral TW-SG-MY cooperation."

- **Taiwan-side operational results**:
  - **Arrests**: 4 Taiwanese nationals (Tsai 44M, Hsieh 30M, Wu 33M, Chen 22F) on 15 May 2024 in Kaohsiung City.
  - **Cryptocurrency seized (Taiwan)**: 3,794 USDT + 5,206 DOGE + 1,293 TRX = ~NTD 151,755.
  - **Other assets frozen (Taiwan)**: ~NTD 43.01 million in bank accounts, real estate and other assets (consistent with the SPF release's "approximately US$1.33 million in cryptocurrency and real estate seized" figure for the Taiwan-side action).
  - **Equipment**: Mobile phones, computer equipment, cryptocurrency wallet backup phrases recovered.

- **Malaysia-side results** (per CIB-NPA): 2 Malaysian-national principals arrested in Penang on 12 June 2024 by CCID-RMP, with SPF and CIB-NPA support, prior to handover to SPF for charging in Singapore.

- **Victim baseline (Singapore)**: 1,899 victims in 2023, NTD 784.3 million (S$34.1 million) reported losses — consistent with the SPF tier-1 release figures.

- **Prosecutorial routing**: Taiwan defendants → Taiwan Ciaotou District Prosecutors Office. Malaysian-national defendants → Singapore State Court.

## Reliability / Credibility

- **Reliability: high** — Official own-domain statement from the Taiwan national criminal-investigation bureau (CIB-NPA), with named investigating units, named defendant initials and ages, itemised asset seizures, prosecutorial forwarding identified, and cooperation framing consistent with the SPF 14 June 2024 release and HKPF DISTANTHILL announcements.
- **Credibility: confirmed** — Independent third-jurisdiction tier-1 confirmation of the same trilateral architecture, with figures cross-consistent with the SPF release (1,899 victims, S$34.1m losses, 2 Malaysia arrests, ~US$1.33m Taiwan seizure).
- **Sensitivity: public** — Officially released.

## IC Significance

This Taiwan CIB-NPA release is the third independent tier-1 stream (SPF + HKPF + CIB-NPA) documenting the trilateral Asia-Pacific cooperation pattern in [[sg-hk-my-android-malware-distanthill-takedown-2024|Operation DISTANTHILL]]. It is the only tier-1 publisher to:

1. Name the operation as "the first instance of trilateral police cooperation among Taiwan, Singapore, and Malaysia" — establishing the operation's status as a precedent-setting Asia-Pacific cooperation case study.
2. Itemise the Taiwan-side cryptocurrency seizures by token type (USDT/DOGE/TRX), providing forensic granularity not in the SPF/HKPF releases.
3. Document the Taiwanese-national defendants (Tsai/Hsieh/Wu/Chen) and the Taiwan Ciaotou District Prosecutors Office routing — establishing that the Taiwan side is not merely an "info-sharing partner" (as characterised in the SPF release) but a co-equal joint-investigation jurisdiction with its own prosecutorial chain.

The CIB-NPA framing also slightly contradicts the SPF release's description of Taiwan as receiving information from the SPF — CIB-NPA characterises the cooperation as a co-equal trilateral arrangement. This wiki page treats both framings as compatible: the operational investigation was trilateral TW-SG-MY (per CIB-NPA), while the SPF-led principal-extradition chain (Singapore → Malaysia handover) was bilateral SPF-RMP (per SPF) and the HKPF infrastructure takedown was a parallel HKPF-led action against the malware-controlling servers and money mules in Hong Kong.

> [!note] Korean relevance
> The CIB-NPA-articulated four-jurisdiction Asia-Pacific cooperation typology (Taiwan-Singapore-Malaysia-Hong Kong, against Android RAT-enabled scams with money-mule and cryptocurrency-laundering nodes) is a directly relevant reference model for KNPA cooperation with Taiwan CIB-NPA — historically constrained by Taiwan's non-INTERPOL status — through Hong Kong / Singapore / Malaysia intermediated channels. The Kaohsiung-node fraudulent-customer-service-centre typology mirrors KNPA-investigated voice-phishing call-centre patterns in Cambodia and Vietnam.
