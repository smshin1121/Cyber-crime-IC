## Source Summary

This joint press release of the Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Berlin (Berlin Public Prosecutor's Office, Press Release No. 273 of 2025-11-12) and the [[germany-lka|Landeskriminalamt Berlin]] (LKA Berlin, Dezernat 253) announces the operational results of a seven-week, six-country joint action campaign (2025-09-15 to 2025-10-31) against the **"Enkeltrick"** (grandparent / shock-call) phone-fraud phenomenon. The campaign was organised under the **EU-funded "ISF-LUMEN" project**, coordinated by the LKA Baden-Württemberg, and brought together police authorities from **all 16 German federal states**, the [[germany-bka|Federal Criminal Police Office (BKA)]], **Austria**, **Poland**, **Switzerland**, **Slovakia**, **Czech Republic**, and **[[europol-ec3|Europol]]**, under the authority of the competent specialised divisions of the responsible state prosecutors' offices.

The release reports **16 arrests** (1 female caller age 26; 3 male callers ages 24, 25, 49; 12 money couriers ages 22-63), the **dismantling of three call centres** (Frankfurt am Main, Austria, Warsaw), the **prevention of 44 attempted Enkeltrick cases**, and **aggregate prevented damages of EUR 2,007,330**. Perpetrators used emotionally charged shock-call scripts — impersonating family members in fake accidents and then impersonating police or prosecutors demanding "bail" payments — and targeted elderly victims via telephone-directory entries.
