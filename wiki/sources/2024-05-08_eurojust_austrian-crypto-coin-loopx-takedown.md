## Source Summary

Eurojust press release dated **08 May 2024** announcing a coordinated takedown of an Austrian-based cryptocurrency exit-scam scheme that had operated between December 2017 and February 2018. National authorities in **Austria, Cyprus, and the Czech Republic**, supported by **Eurojust** and **Europol**, executed **six arrests** of the six main Austrian suspects and **six location searches**, freezing or seizing **EUR 750,000 in assets**, a **property worth EUR 1.4 million**, and **two cars**. The scheme defrauded investors of approximately **EUR 6 million**.

Eurojust enumerates the participating authorities verbatim:

- Austria: Wirtschafts- und Korruptionsstaatsanwaltschaft (WKStA, Central Public Prosecutor's Office for Economic Crimes and Corruption); Bundeskriminalamt (Criminal Intelligence Service Austria) – Cybercrime Competence Center (C4).
- Cyprus: Cyprus Police (per the Europol parallel release, specifically Cyprus Police in Larnaca).
- Czech Republic: National Organised Crime Agency (per Europol, Národní centrála proti organizovanému zločinu – NCOZ, Police of the Czech Republic).

The cooperation mechanism set is explicit: **Eurojust coordination centre** for real-time judicial communication, **Europol Virtual Command Post** at Eurojust, **rapid execution of European Arrest Warrants and search warrants**, **five Europol operational meetings**, and **deployment of a Europol specialist with mobile office to Cyprus** plus **on-site Austrian police presence in Cyprus** during the action-day search and main-suspect interrogation.

## Relevance to IC

This is the principal tier-1 source for the 2024 Operation LoopX takedown — a small but useful **multi-year exit-scam prosecution case study** for cooperation between an Austrian lead agency (BK/C4 + WKStA) and two host jurisdictions (Cyprus, Czech Republic) where suspects and assets were located. The case illustrates:

1. **EAW + EIO + Eurojust coordination** as the dominant judicial cooperation toolset for intra-EU multi-jurisdictional cryptocurrency fraud prosecutions, distinct from the more recent JIT-led operations (e.g., [[latvia-sim-box-cybercrime-as-a-service-takedown-2025]] = Operation SIMCartel JIT Austria/Estonia/Latvia/Finland 2025).
2. **Cryptocurrency seizure on host jurisdiction** — Austrian officers travelled to Cyprus to advise local police on crypto-seizure procedure, a recurring IC capacity-building motif.
3. **Cold-case multi-year cybercrime prosecution** — the underlying 2017-2018 ICO exit scam was prosecuted six years later, showing that crypto-fraud international prosecutions can succeed long after the original criminal activity.
4. **Cross-jurisdiction concentration on Austrian-based scheme** — all suspects are Austrian nationals, but the investigation footprint extends to Cyprus (Europol mobile office, Austrian police presence) and the Czech Republic (NCOZ search support).
