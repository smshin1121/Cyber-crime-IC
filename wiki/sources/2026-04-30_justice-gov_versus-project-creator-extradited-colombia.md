## Source Summary

DOJ-OPA Press Release 26-428 dated **30 April 2026** announcing the extradition of **Patrick Schmitz** (37, German national, residing in Taganga, Colombia) from Colombia to the United States on **29 April 2026** on an 8-count indictment in the District of New Jersey for cofounding and operating **'The Versus Project'** dark web marketplace. Versus operated November 2019 through May 2022 with **380,000+ registered users**, **32,000+ product listings**, and **300,000+ completed orders** generating millions of dollars in cryptocurrency transactions across product categories spanning drugs (heroin), fraud, digital items, services, and "software & malware" (including hacking tools).

The release establishes:
1. A **22-month US-Colombia extradition timeline** (arrest June 2024 in Colombia → extradition 29 April 2026), illustrating the practical pace of US-Colombia cybercrime extradition cooperation.
2. The **Bitcoin + Monero exclusive payment rail** as a deliberate KYC/AML bypass — a recurring design pattern in dark web marketplaces ([[archetyp-market-takedown-operation-deep-sentinel-2025|Archetyp]] used a similar approach).
3. The **HSI Newark + FBI Kansas City + IRS-CI Newark + DOJ-OIA + US Marshals + Policía Nacional de Colombia** stack as the operational template for US-Colombia darknet-marketplace cybercrime extraditions.
4. Continuing criminal enterprise (CCE) charging — a felony tier reserved for senior dark web marketplace principals — applied alongside narcotics, fraud, and money-laundering conspiracy counts.
