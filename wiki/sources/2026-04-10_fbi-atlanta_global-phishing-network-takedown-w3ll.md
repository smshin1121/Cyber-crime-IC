## Source Summary

FBI Atlanta Field Office press release dated **10 April 2026** announcing a **first-of-its-kind US-Indonesia joint takedown** of the **W3LL phishing kit** and its supporting marketplace **W3LLSTORE**. The W3LL phishing kit (USD 500 per access) was specifically engineered to **capture session data alongside credentials, enabling MFA bypass and persistent account access** — making it a *full-service cybercrime platform* (per FBI Atlanta SAC Marlo Graham). The W3LLSTORE marketplace facilitated **25,000+ compromised account sales (2019–2023)**; the kit was used to target **17,000+ victims worldwide (2023–2024 alone)**, with documented attempted fraud exceeding **USD 20 million**.

The release establishes:

1. The **first coordinated US-Indonesia action against a phishing kit developer** — explicitly highlighted by FBI Atlanta as a precedent.
2. **MFA-bypassing session-data capture** as the technical innovation that made W3LL "a full-service cybercrime platform" rather than a generic phishing kit.
3. The **post-takedown migration pattern** — after W3LLSTORE shut down in 2023, the kit continued to be marketed via encrypted messaging platforms and was rebranded — illustrating how PhaaS (phishing-as-a-service) ecosystems persist beyond marketplace-level enforcement.
