## Summary

Polish-language tier-1 press release from Poland's Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime (CBZC, [[poland-police|Centralne Biuro Zwalczania Cyberprzestępczości]]) announcing the 2026-02-17 arrest in Małopolskie Voivodeship of a 47-year-old Polish national suspected of producing, acquiring and distributing hacking tools (Polish Criminal Code Art. 269b § 1) and of contacting the [[ransomware-ic|Phobos ransomware]] group via encrypted messengers. The arrest is publicly tied to CBZC's participation in **Operation Aether**, a [[europol-ec3|Europol]]-coordinated multi-jurisdictional campaign against the Phobos RaaS ecosystem.

## International Cooperation Elements

- **Coordinating body**: [[europol-ec3|Europol]] (CBZC explicitly described as a participant in Europol-coordinated Operation Aether).
- **Named cooperation with the United States**: prior extradition of the alleged Phobos administrator to the [[united-states|USA]]; reference to [[us-doj|US Department of Justice]] documents on total Phobos ransom proceeds (>USD 16 million); US victim categories named (schools in California and Connecticut, Maryland healthcare, US DoD contractor).
- **Domestic prosecutorial supervision**: District Prosecutor's Office in Gliwice (Prokuratura Okręgowa w Gliwicach).
- The release uses the phrase "skoordynowane zatrzymania w Europie i poza nią" (coordinated arrests in Europe and beyond) without naming additional specific jurisdictions in this text.
