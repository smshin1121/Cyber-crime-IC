## Source Profile

This is an English-language official news release on the **INTERPOL** website (`interpol.int`), the public-facing newsroom of the International Criminal Police Organization, dated **8 August 2023**. The dateline is **Singapore**, the location of the INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation (IGCI). The release announces the takedown of the "16shop" phishing-as-a-service (PaaS) platform and the arrest of three suspects — the administrator and one facilitator in Indonesia, and a second facilitator in Japan.

INTERPOL is an intergovernmental organisation comprising 196 member countries; its newsroom is treated as a tier-1 primary source under the wiki's L24 publisher rule. The release names two senior officials on the record: Bernardo Pillot, INTERPOL's Assistant Director of Cybercrime Operations, and Brigadier General Adi Vivid Agustiadi Bachtiar, Director of the Indonesian National Police's Cyber Crime Investigation.

## Key Excerpts

The release explicitly states (verbatim, on cooperation):

- "A notorious 'phishing-as-a-service' (PaaS) platform known as '16shop' has been shut down in a global investigation coordinated by INTERPOL, with Indonesian authorities arresting its operator and one of its facilitators, with another arrested in Japan."
- "The three arrests ... was made possible due to the intensive intelligence-sharing between the INTERPOL General Secretariat's cybercrime directorate, national law enforcement in Indonesia, Japan and the United States and private sector partners ..."
- "Following the successful apprehension of the administrator, further information was shared between the National Police Agency of Japan and the Indonesian National Police resulting in the identification and arrest of two facilitators."
- "Because the platform's servers were hosted by a company based in the United States, analysts liaised with the INTERPOL National Central Bureau in Washington and the Federal Bureau of Investigation to secure key information for Indonesian investigators."

These passages explicitly name **four cooperating LE entities** (INTERPOL HQ Cybercrime Directorate, Indonesian National Police's Directorate of Cyber Crimes, Japan's National Police Agency, and the FBI / INTERPOL NCB Washington), satisfying the wiki's L24 strict ≥2-LE requirement.
