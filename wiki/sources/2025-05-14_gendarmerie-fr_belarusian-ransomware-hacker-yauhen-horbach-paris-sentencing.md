## Source Summary

Tier-1 French national Gendarmerie press release (Gendinfo, 14 May 2025) reporting the Paris court sentencing of Belarusian national Yauhen Horbach for ransomware attacks involving 2,300 victims worldwide (including 65 in France) and approximately USD 750,000 in bitcoin ransom proceeds. Operating under the pseudonym "JohnSmith" and nicknamed "Daniel" by the investigation team (after Daniel Craig), Horbach was identified at the end of a four-year cyber-investigation led by the Antenne C3N of the Section de recherches (S.R.) de Rennes following an attempted breach on Coriolis Composites in Morbihan in February 2020. After INTERPOL issued a Red Notice, Horbach was arrested and detained by Georgian authorities in August 2022, then extradited to France approximately eight months later. The judicial investigation proceeded under JUNALCO authority; the tribunal judiciaire de Paris handed down a 5-year sentence (4 firm, 1 suspended) plus a EUR 100,000 fine on 28 April 2025.

## Relevance to IC

- **First-in-France-led ransomware incarceration milestone**: The release positions the case as the first French-led (the source uses the phrase "enquête 100 % française") investigation to result in incarceration of a ransomware author — a significant capacity-building marker for the Gendarmerie's cyber-investigations branch and a useful benchmark for cross-jurisdictional comparison with FBI/UK NCA/Dutch ransomware prosecutions.
- **Red Notice → foreign arrest → extradition pipeline**: The case illustrates the INTERPOL Red Notice mechanism as a force-multiplier for medium-tempo cybercrime investigations: French Gendarmerie identified the suspect through patient digital forensics, but the actual physical arrest required leveraging Georgian LE under an INTERPOL alert plus a France-Georgia extradition arrangement. The 8-month detention-to-extradition timeline is consistent with standard European extradition timelines.
- **JUNALCO as cybercrime central authority**: The release confirms that JUNALCO — France's national jurisdiction for organised crime — handled the judicial investigation phase. This reinforces JUNALCO's role as a recurring node in French international cybercrime cooperation, complementing the Gendarmerie's C3N operational arm.
- **Cooperation footprint of named foreign agencies**: Georgian LE (named obliquely as the authorities of "la Géorgie" that detained Horbach) and INTERPOL (Red Notice issuance) are the two explicitly named foreign IC counterparts. Belarus is named only as the suspect's nationality and is not credited with any cooperative role — consistent with the broader pattern of Belarusian non-cooperation in cybercrime cases.
