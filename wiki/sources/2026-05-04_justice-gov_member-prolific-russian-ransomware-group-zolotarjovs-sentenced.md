## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release (Number 26-444, dated 2026-05-04 / updated 2026-05-05) announcing the 102-month federal prison sentence in the Southern District of Ohio of Deniss Zolotarjovs (Денисс Золотарёвс), 35, a Latvian national of Moscow, Russia, for his role as a ransom-negotiator member of a Russian ransomware organization led by former Conti leaders that operated under multiple brand names — Conti, Karakurt, Royal, TommyLeaks, SchoolBoys Ransomware, and Akira. The release supplies detailed verbatim figures: 54+ companies attacked between June 2021 and August 2023, with attacks on 13 named victims producing over USD 56 million in losses (including USD 2.8 million in ransom payments), 41 additional victims paying USD 13 million in ransom, and the government estimating total period losses to be in the hundreds of millions of dollars. Notable verbatim findings: weaponisation of pediatric children's healthcare information for extortion, shutdown of a government 911 system, and organisation operations from Lakhtinskaya Street in St. Petersburg, Russia, including former Russian law-enforcement officers among the membership. Zolotarjovs was arrested in Georgia in December 2023, transferred to US custody in August 2024 after contesting extradition, and pleaded guilty in July 2025 to conspiracy to commit money laundering and wire fraud.
