## Source Summary

Official Royal Canadian Mounted Police press release issued by RCMP Central Region (Ontario) on 2025-02-20 announcing that the Ontario RCMP Cybercrime Investigative Team (CIT) Toronto arrested two Toronto residents — Chakib Mansouri (29) and Majdouline Alouah (31) — for operating one of Canada's most prolific spoofing-as-a-service downstream fraud schemes. The pair are alleged to have been among the top 50 worldwide subscribers of the iSpoof.cc caller-ID spoofing platform, using it (with companion phishing and smishing methods) to impersonate banks, government and police, defrauding at least 570 Canadian victims of millions of dollars.

The release explicitly enumerates an international cooperation chain — RCMP CIT Toronto cooperating with the London Metropolitan Police, the Dutch National Police, EUROPOL and EUROJUST — alongside Canadian municipal, provincial and federal partners (Toronto Police, Peel Regional Police, FINTRAC, the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre, and the National Cybercrime Coordination Centre). This places the Canadian arrest in the broader iSpoof.cc takedown investigation lineage that began with the UK Metropolitan Police's Operation Elaborate in November 2022, in which Europol and Eurojust coordinated multi-jurisdictional action against thousands of iSpoof users worldwide.
