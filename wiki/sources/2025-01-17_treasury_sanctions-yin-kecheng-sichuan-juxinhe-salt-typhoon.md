# Source Summary — Treasury JY-2792: OFAC sanctions on Yin Kecheng and Sichuan Juxinhe (Salt Typhoon) (2025-01-17)

## Bibliographic data

- **Title**: Treasury Sanctions Company Associated with Salt Typhoon and Hacker Associated with Treasury Compromise
- **Press release number**: JY-2792
- **Publisher**: U.S. Department of the Treasury, Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC)
- **Date**: 2025-01-17
- **URL**: https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy2792
- **Language**: English

## Reliability assessment

- **Reliability**: high — official Treasury press release, primary source for the OFAC designation.
- **Credibility**: confirmed — designation is a self-executing administrative act announced by the issuing authority on its own letterhead; the SDN list update is the corroborating administrative record.
- **Sensitivity**: public — released by Treasury for public dissemination.

## Key claims and findings

1. OFAC designated **Yin Kecheng** (Shanghai-based PRC national) and **Sichuan Juxinhe Network Technology Co., LTD.** (Sichuan-based PRC company) pursuant to **Executive Order 13694**, as further amended by the new **E.O. on Strengthening and Promoting Innovation in the Nation's Cybersecurity** (signed January 2025). (Almost certainly accurate as a primary-source statement.)
2. **Yin Kecheng** is described as having been a cyber actor for over a decade and **affiliated with the People's Republic of China Ministry of State Security (MSS)**. He is associated with the recent compromise of Treasury's Departmental Offices network (publicly disclosed by Treasury in late December 2024 and attributed to a "China state-sponsored Advanced Persistent Threat (APT) actor").
3. **Sichuan Juxinhe Network Technology Co., LTD.** is described as having "direct involvement" in the Salt Typhoon cyber group's exploitation of multiple major U.S. telecommunication and internet service provider companies. Treasury asserts the MSS maintains strong ties with multiple computer network exploitation companies, including Sichuan Juxinhe.
4. **Salt Typhoon** has been active since at least 2019 and is described as responsible for numerous compromises of U.S. companies in the communication sector; the recent telecom-infrastructure compromise is characterized as "a dramatic escalation."
5. The release places this action in a deliberate **PRC-cyber sanctions campaign sequence**: 2024-03-25 (Wuhan Xiaoruizhi / APT31) → 2024-12-10 (Sichuan Silence) → 2025-01-03 (Integrity Technology Group / Flax Typhoon) → 2025-01-17 (this action). All four are framed by Treasury as responses to PRC-state-backed cyber activity against U.S. critical infrastructure.
6. The U.S. Department of State Rewards for Justice program is referenced in the release as offering up to **USD 10 million** for information on foreign-government-directed cyber activity against U.S. critical infrastructure (a standing reward; not specifically attached to Yin Kecheng in the release text).

## International cooperation relevance

> [!note] IC scope flag
> The release names no foreign partner authority as co-designating, co-coordinating, or otherwise participating in this action. Unlike the [[treasury-evil-corp-tri-lateral-us-uk-au-sanctions-2024|2024 Evil Corp tri-lateral]] or the [[treasury-us-au-uk-zservers-sanctions-2025|February 2025 ZServers]] designations, this is a **U.S.-unilateral OFAC action** against PRC state-sponsored actors. Five Eyes parallel attribution may exist but is not asserted on the record by this source.

- The IC dimension of this action is **almost certainly indirect** rather than operational: OFAC sanctions impose U.S.-jurisdiction asset blocks and U.S.-person transactional prohibitions, but neither extradition, MLA, nor host-state arrest is contemplated (PRC does not extradite its own nationals to the United States and is not a [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]] party). The action is best understood as part of the **U.S. parallel-track unilateral toolkit** for state-attributed PRC cyber activity that cannot be reached through formal IC channels (high confidence).
- **Salt Typhoon's targeting of U.S. telecommunications carriers** (publicly reported in late 2024 as Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile, and Lumen, among others, including in Wall Street Journal and other major-outlet coverage; not named in this Treasury release) reportedly involved access to **lawful-intercept (CALEA) systems** through which U.S. carriers fulfill law-enforcement wiretap requests — including, materially, those originating from foreign LE under MLAT and 24/7 Network channels. The resulting compromise of cross-border lawful-intercept tradecraft is **likely** the most consequential downstream IC implication of the Salt Typhoon campaign (medium-high confidence; cross-border CALEA-system exposure not asserted in this Treasury release directly but reported across multiple major-outlet sources).
- The release explicitly references the **ODNI Annual Threat Assessment** as a corroborating PRC-attribution document, situating this designation within the broader U.S. intelligence-community attribution framework rather than presenting it as a standalone OFAC determination.
