## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release (dated 2026-05-07) announcing the 7th and 8th US-based "laptop farmer" sentencings under the joint NSD + FBI **DPRK RevGen: Domestic Enabler Initiative**. Matthew Isaac Knoot (Nashville, TN; MDTN) and Erick Ntekereze Prince (New York; SDFL) each received 18 months in prison for facilitating DPRK remote IT worker schemes — receiving and hosting laptop computers shipped to victim US companies' "remote IT workers" who were actually North Korean nationals operating from China and elsewhere overseas. The combined schemes generated more than USD 1.2 million in revenue for the DPRK and impacted nearly 70 victim US companies. The release names co-conspirators including Mexican national Pedro Ernesto Alonso de los Reyes (currently in Netherlands custody awaiting extradition to the US) and North Korean nationals Jin Sung-il and Pak Jin-Song (fugitives), and references the **October 2023 joint US-ROK (Republic of Korea / South Korea) guidance** on DPRK IT worker threats as part of the broader US-Korea cooperation public-record corpus.
