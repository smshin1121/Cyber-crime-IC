## Source Summary

Europol newsroom press release dated **2026-03-04** announcing the coordinated public-private disruption of **Tycoon 2FA**, one of the largest phishing-as-a-service (PhaaS) platforms worldwide. The disruption took down 330 domains forming the platform's core infrastructure (phishing pages + control panels) and was executed by law enforcement in **six countries** — Latvia, Lithuania, Portugal, Poland, Spain, and the United Kingdom — coordinated by Europol's European Cybercrime Centre (EC3). The technical takedown was led by **Microsoft** with a coalition of private partners. Investigation triggered by intelligence-sharing from **Trend Micro** through Europol's **Cyber Intelligence Extension Programme (CIEP)**, a first-of-its-kind Europol framework that hosts private-sector experts at The Hague to work alongside EC3 analysts on specific operational projects.

## IC Pattern

This release establishes several discrete IC mechanism classes worth recording:

- **Cyber Intelligence Extension Programme (CIEP)** — first Europol-described public-private operational framework that brings industry experts physically to The Hague to work alongside EC3 analysts and investigators on specific projects. Distinct from the EC3 Advisory Groups (information-sharing forums) and from one-off mobile-office deployments. The Tycoon 2FA case is described as the first publicised CIEP-driven operational outcome.
- **Microsoft-led technical takedown + LE-led seizure split** — a recurring pattern in PhaaS/malware-as-a-service disruptions where a private vendor with platform-level visibility (Microsoft, Cloudflare) executes the technical disruption (domain takedowns, control-panel seizures, abuse-channel processing) in parallel with national LE seizing physical / hosted infrastructure and pursuing operator identification. Compare with [[labhost-phishing-as-a-service-takedown-2024|LabHost (April 2024)]] (Europol J-CAT 19-country PhaaS takedown) and the [[lumma-stealer-takedown|Lumma Stealer takedown (May 2025)]] (Microsoft-led with US DOJ + Europol).
- **EC3 Advisory Groups → CIEP escalation** — the release frames Trend Micro's intelligence-sharing as flowing first through EC3 Advisory Groups (steady-state info-sharing), then escalating into CIEP (operational embedding) once the strategy required deeper public-private collaboration. This is a documented two-tier public-private mechanism class.
