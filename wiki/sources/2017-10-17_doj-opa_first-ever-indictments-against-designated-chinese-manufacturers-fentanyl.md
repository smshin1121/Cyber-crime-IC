## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs announces the first-ever U.S. indictments against designated Chinese fentanyl manufacturers. The release packages the District of Oregon indictment of Jian Zhang (for four Oregon overdoses) with parallel indictments in North Dakota and Southern Mississippi. Attorney General Sessions, DEA Acting Administrator Patterson, and OCDETF leadership announced the case as a multi-district federal initiative.

## Relevance to IC

This source provides parallel agency confirmation (DOJ headquarters) for [[operation-us-v-jian-zhang]] and situates the District of Oregon individual case within the broader China-origin fentanyl trafficking framework that ultimately involved State Department reward offers and Treasury sanctions.
