## Source Summary

NCA press release dated 2025-07-11 reporting on the 2025-07-07 raid by India's CBI of a fraud call-centre in Noida, Uttar Pradesh, India. The release establishes a four-party 18-month collaboration (CBI / NCA / FBI / Microsoft) and the underlying intelligence chain: Microsoft → NCA-USA International Liaison Office → comparison with City of London Police Action Fraud Reports → joint NCA + FBI + Microsoft suspect-identification → NCA International Liaison Officers in India briefing the CBI → CBI raid on action day.

## IC Pattern

This case is a fully-articulated public-private four-party transnational cybercrime cooperation pattern: India + UK + US (three named law-enforcement jurisdictions) plus Microsoft (private sector). Notable features:

- **CBI as action-day lead** in a transnational cooperation initiated and supported by Western LE (NCA + FBI), inverting the more common pattern where US/UK LE acts on India-resident suspects under MLAT.
- **NCA International Liaison Officer network** as the primary inter-jurisdictional intelligence-sharing channel (US LO, Delhi LO, Washington DC LO).
- **City of London Police Action Fraud Reports** as a UK domestic-victim data feed driving offshore enforcement.
- **UK-US operational arrangement on call-centre fraud (2024)** referenced as the foundational bilateral instrument enabling the cross-Atlantic side of the cooperation.
- **Microsoft Digital Crimes Unit** as the private-sector intelligence originator, demonstrating the public-private cooperation model for tech-support scam enforcement.
