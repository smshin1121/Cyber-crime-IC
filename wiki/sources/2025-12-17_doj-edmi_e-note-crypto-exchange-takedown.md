## Source Summary

US Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of Michigan announcement (2025-12-17 press conference) of the takedown of the E-Note cryptocurrency exchange and payment-processing service, alleged to have laundered USD 70 million+ in cybercrime proceeds since 2017 (with personal money-mule activity dating to 2010). A federal indictment was unsealed against Mykhalio Petrovich Chudnovets, 39, a Russian national alleged to have controlled and operated E-Note from approximately 2011 through 2025, on one count of money laundering conspiracy carrying a maximum 20-year prison term. Three domains (e-note.com, e-note.ws, jabb.mn) and associated servers and mobile applications were seized. The German Federal Criminal Police Office (Bundeskriminalamt / BKA) and the Finnish National Bureau of Investigation (Keskusrikospoliisi / KRP) are named as cooperating European authorities. US-side participants: USAO-EDMI (United States Attorney Jerome F. Gorgon, Jr.), FBI Detroit Division (SAC Jennifer Runyan), Michigan State Police, and the Michigan Cyber Command Center.
