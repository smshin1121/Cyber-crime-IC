## Source Profile

Official press release issued by Denmark's National Special Crime Unit
(National enhed for Særlig Kriminalitet — NSK / Nationalt Cyber Crime Center —
NC3), published on the Rigspolitiet domain `politi.dk`. NSK is the unit that
initiated and led the international phase of the investigation; the press
release names the operation as a Danish-led action and announces the
co-publication by Europol.

A secondary tier-1 source — the Europol newsroom press release of the same
date (2025-02-28) — is cross-cited in the raw file because it provides the
authoritative operation codename **Operation Cumberland** and the full roster
of 21 named participating agencies across 19 countries. Both releases are
preserved verbatim (within fair-use length) in the raw file so that any
subsequent fact-check can verify wording without re-fetching.

The Danish release is the *primary* tier-1 source for the Danish-side facts
(domestic conviction, the 29-year-old's biographical details, the two Danish
buyer suspects, the appeal posture). The Europol release is the *primary*
tier-1 source for the multilateral coordination role (J-CAT, EC3) and the
country/agency roster. Neither contradicts the other; the two sets of figures
match exactly.

## Reliability and Credibility

- **Reliability: high** — official publication on the Rigspolitiet domain
  `politi.dk` (Danish national police); Europol cross-citation also tier-1.
- **Credibility: confirmed** — first-party announcement by the lead
  investigative agency, with the action-day date and figures stated
  unambiguously and matched by Europol.
- **Sensitivity: public** — both releases are intended for general
  distribution. Defendants' identities are protected (only ages and
  jurisdictional police districts are stated for the two Danish customers and
  the producer).

## Why this source is durable for the wiki

1. It is the *first* publicly documented multi-country police action squarely
   addressing fully synthetic, AI-generated CSAM — a case-study reference for
   the [[csam-ic]] crime-type page and for any future entry on legal gaps in
   criminalising AI-generated material.
2. It illustrates the *J-CAT* coordination model in production: a national
   force (NSK) initiating, Europol's J-CAT providing the operational
   coordination layer, EC3 facilitating information exchange and analysis.
3. It cleanly demonstrates a financial-trail attribution chain
   (betalingssporet) used to identify buyers across 19 jurisdictions from a
   single seized platform.
