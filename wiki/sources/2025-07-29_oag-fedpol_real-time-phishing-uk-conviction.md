## Source Summary

Official press release issued on 29 July 2025 by the Office of the Attorney General of Switzerland (Bundesanwaltschaft / Ministère public de la Confédération / Ministero pubblico della Confederazione, "OAG"), in cooperation with the Federal Office of Police (fedpol), announcing that joint Swiss investigations had led to the UK conviction of a British national for developing and distributing a phishing kit used in a real-time-phishing scheme that defrauded Swiss bank customers of approximately CHF 2.4 million.

The release is mirrored across the Swiss federal news portal (`bag.admin.ch/en/newnsb/...` and `news.admin.ch/en/newnsb/...`) and was independently reported on the same day by Keystone-SDA / SWI swissinfo.ch ("English student sentenced for defrauding Swiss bank customers"). Background detail on the modus operandi and the October 2023 arrest of the suspect in England is provided in the fedpol Report 2024 entry "Phishing scam by an international network uncovered."

## Relevance to IC

This source is a tier-1 primary record of a Swiss-UK cybercrime cooperation case in which the Swiss federal prosecution authority transferred / discontinued its proceedings in favour of UK prosecution after the developer and distributor of the phishing kit was identified, located, and arrested in England. It documents:

- Bilateral coordination between the OAG and UK prosecution authorities, including transfer of jurisdiction.
- Operational support from fedpol, Europol, and Eurojust (per the fedpol annual-report account).
- Use of the "real-time phishing" technique against Swiss cantonal-bank e-banking customers, including bypass of two-factor authentication.
