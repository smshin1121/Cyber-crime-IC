## Source Summary

AFP (afp.gov.au) media release dated 2024-04-18, jointly issued with four Australian state police forces, announcing the Australian component of the international [[labhost-phishing-as-a-service-takedown-2024|LabHost phishing-as-a-service takedown]] (action days 2024-04-14 to 2024-04-17). The Australian operation, codenamed **Operation Nebulae**, was led by the AFP's Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3) and resulted in 5 arrests, 22 search warrants across five states, the takedown of 207 criminal servers, and identification of 100+ Australian LabHost users targeting more than 94,000 Australian victims.

## IC Pattern

This release provides the second tier-1 own-domain primary source for the LabHost takedown (alongside the 2024-04-18 Europol primary), confirming and extending the original primary with explicit Australian arrest, search-warrant, server-takedown, suspect-identification, victim-count, and AUD-harm figures.

- **National-LE own-domain (afp.gov.au):** AFP is a national LE agency; the release is on its own primary domain. Joint-release with four state police forces (Victoria, Queensland, NSW, WA) adds explicit sub-national LE coverage of the Australian enforcement footprint.
- **Operation codename surfaced:** Operation Nebulae (Australian arm) — a discrete operation identity that the Europol primary did not include.
- **Australian-side corroboration of Europol figures:** 37 global arrests, 70 global searches, 4 UK individuals running the site (incl. original developer), ~10,000 global users, >40,000 phishing domains, ~$270/month subscription.
- **Named officer quotes:** AFP Acting Assistant Commissioner Cyber Command Chris Goldsmid, Victoria Police Det Supt Tim McKinney, QPS Det Supt Craig McGrath, NSW Acting Det Supt Gillian Lister, WA Det Supt Peter Foley.
