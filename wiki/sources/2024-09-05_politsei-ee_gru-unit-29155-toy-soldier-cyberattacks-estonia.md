## Summary

Estonian Police and Border Guard Board (PPA) press release dated **2024-09-05** announcing the attribution and indictment phase of joint operation **Toy Soldier**, in which **14 services across 10 states** cooperated with the Estonian Internal Security Service to identify Russian GRU military unit 29155 as the actor behind cyberattacks on Estonian state authorities (2020) and on Ukraine, NATO, and EU member states from 2020 onward.

The Estonian National Criminal Police, under the direction of the Office of the Prosecutor General, brought criminal proceedings against three named GRU officers (Yuri Denisov, Nikolay Korchagin, Vitali Shevchenko); the Harju District Court issued in-absentia custody orders. Parallel US prosecution by the FBI charged Denisov and Korchagin and was paired with a $10 million State Department reward. The press release explicitly invokes hybrid-warfare doctrine.

## International-Cooperation Relevance

The press release documents (i) **multilateral attribution** under joint operation Toy Soldier (14 services / 10 states), (ii) **parallel prosecution** between Estonia and the United States, and (iii) **in-absentia indictment** of state-actor suspects believed to be sheltered in Russia. The Russia-shelter pattern, combined with the FBI reward and Estonian custody-in-absentia order, illustrates how partner states absorb the limits of MLAT against non-cooperating jurisdictions.
