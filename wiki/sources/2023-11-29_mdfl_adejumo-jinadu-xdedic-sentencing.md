## Source Summary

On 29 November 2023, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Middle District of Florida announced the sentencing of Adetunji Adejumo and Ibrahim Jinadu for conspiracy to commit wire fraud in an international tax-fraud scheme. DOJ's xDedic roundup later linked this case to access and PII obtained from xDedic-compromised servers. According to DOJ, international conspirators obtained unauthorized access to U.S. business servers, stole U.S. residents' identifying information, and used that information to file false tax forms seeking millions of dollars in refunds. Adejumo and Jinadu, who were based in the United States, handled fraud proceeds directed to prepaid debit cards and bank accounts they controlled. DOJ reported that Adejumo received 33 months and Jinadu 27 months. The case is significant because it shows how xDedic access was converted into an organized refund-fraud pipeline involving both foreign and U.S.-based actors.
