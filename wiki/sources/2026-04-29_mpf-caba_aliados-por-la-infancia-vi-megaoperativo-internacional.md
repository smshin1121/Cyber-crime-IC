## Source Profile

This is the first-party institutional press release issued by the **Ministerio Público Fiscal de la Ciudad Autónoma de Buenos Aires (MPF CABA)** through its **Cuerpo de Investigaciones Judiciales (CIJ)** announcing the results of the sixth iteration of *Operación Aliados por la Infancia*, a multi-jurisdiction child-protection operation coordinated from Buenos Aires. The release was issued at the close of an in-person results conference attended by the MPF CABA Fiscal General, the U.S. and French ambassadors to Argentina, NCMEC (John Shehan), ICMEC (Laura Suárez Alonso), and senior Argentine federal and city law-enforcement officials.

This is the wiki's first ingest from the **mpfciudad.gob.ar** publisher. MPF CABA is the cybercrime-prosecution authority of the City of Buenos Aires and, per its own characterisation, the institutional coordinator of the Aliados por la Infancia operation series (now in iteration VI).

## Reliability and Credibility

- **Reliability: high** — official institutional release on the prosecuting authority's own domain (mpfciudad.gob.ar), naming the operational lead, the U.S. cooperation partner, the named tooling agreement, the in-person speakers, and the per-jurisdiction enforcement breakdown.
- **Credibility: confirmed** — first-party announcement; aggregate figures (270 raids, 84 detentions, country roster) are corroborated by Argentine provincial fiscal offices' independent press notes (Salta, Santa Cruz, Chubut, Neuquén) and by Buenos Aires Province MPBA news 2655.
- **Sensitivity: public** — distributed for general media use.

## Limitations

- The release narrative says the operation reached "otros 15 países" beyond Argentina, while the per-country raid breakdown enumerates only 12 other jurisdictions. The 3-country gap is not reconciled in the source. The operation page records only verified jurisdictions per L19.
- Mexico, Colombia, and El Salvador are referenced in some secondary Spanish-language press summaries but are NOT enumerated in the MPF CABA primary release's raid breakdown; they are therefore excluded from the operation page's `participating_countries` and noted under Contradictions & Open Questions instead.
- Foreign-jurisdiction agency names (e.g. Brazil's PF or PCs, Panama's Procuraduría) are not enumerated in this Argentine release; foreign agency identification will require ingestion of the corresponding national prosecutor releases in a later iteration.
