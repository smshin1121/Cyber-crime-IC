## Summary

Official Dutch National Police (Politie) press release dated 9 April 2026 (last modified 22 April 2026) announcing the first wave of arrests in the user-side phase of the joint Netherlands-US VerifTools fake-ID marketplace investigation. The release confirms that the August 2025 takedown of VerifTools was conducted in cooperation with the US FBI and that the post-seizure data exploitation has produced concrete enforcement action against platform users on Dutch territory.

## Tier-1 Primary Source Justification

- Official Dutch national police communication on its institutional `politie.nl` newsroom domain.
- Names the cooperating foreign authority (FBI) and the Dutch units involved (Team Cybercrime Rotterdam, AVIM, Openbaar Ministerie).
- Provides quantitative results (arrests, account counts, document counts, monetary figures) from the police's own data analysis of the seized servers.
- Both the original seizure (27 Aug 2025) and the user-side arrests (7-8 Apr 2026) are described as one continuous investigation, satisfying the international-cooperation scope (Netherlands + United States, ≥ 2 countries).
