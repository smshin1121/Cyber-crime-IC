# Source summary — NSW teen charged for multiple hoax calls to major retail, education institutions in United States (U.S. Embassy Canberra reproduction)

## Overview

The U.S. Embassy in Canberra's 14 January 2026 release on `au.usembassy.gov` reproduces the AFP-FBI joint media release announcing the AFP's charging of a regional New South Wales juvenile with 12 counts of using a carriage service to convey false information about danger and one count of unauthorised possession of a prohibited firearm. The Embassy release adds an additional cooperation quote from Chargé d'Affaires Erika Olson and confirms the case from the United States diplomatic side.

## International cooperation elements

- Embassy release is itself a tier-1 own-domain U.S. State Department publication on `au.usembassy.gov` (.gov), the second tier-1 own-domain confirmation of the operation alongside the AFP's `afp.gov.au` release.
- Adds a third named senior official quote (Chargé d'Affaires Erika Olson, U.S. Embassy Canberra) alongside the AFP Acting Assistant Commissioner Graeme Marshall and FBI Assistant Director Jason A. Kaplan quotes already in the AFP release.
- Explicitly characterises the operation as a "strong partnership between the FBI and AFP" — direct L24 cooperation language from the U.S. side.
- Embassy release confirms Five Eyes Law Enforcement Group framing of the operation.

## Reliability assessment

- **Reliability:** high — primary source, U.S. Department of State own-domain publication (`au.usembassy.gov`).
- **Credibility:** confirmed — direct reproduction of the AFP-FBI joint release with additional Embassy attribution.
- **Sensitivity:** public.

## Cross-source divergences with the AFP own-domain release

- Search-warrant date: Embassy release states 18 December 2025; AFP own-domain states arrest on 9 January 2026 with search executed during that action. Reconcilable as separate search vs. arrest events; operation page flags this divergence.
- FBI Kaplan's division: Embassy release lists "International Operations Division"; AFP own-domain release lists "Criminal Investigative Division". Operation page records both attributions and flags for resolution.
