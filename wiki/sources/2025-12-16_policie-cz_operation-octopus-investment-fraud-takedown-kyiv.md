## Summary

Czech Police press release dated 2025-12-16 jointly announcing two Eurojust-coordinated international operations against fraudulent call centres in Ukraine. Operation OCTOPUS (concluded 2025-11-20) targeted an investment-fraud network and produced 17 arrests in Kyiv, 19 searches, approximately CZK 33 million in seizures, and identified damage of CZK 43 million across 138 victims; the press release frames the perpetrators as a single criminal group with a 31-year-old organizer from the Donetsk region. Operation CONNECT (concluded 2025-12-09) targeted a separate police/bank impersonation network running three call centres in Kyiv, Ivano-Frankivsk, and Dnipro; that operation involved a four-country (Czech Republic, Ukraine, Lithuania, Latvia) JIT.

## Reliability Notes

This is a tier-1 primary source: the official press release from the Czech national cybercrime police unit (NCTEKK) carrying a named spokesperson. The numbers for OCTOPUS (17 arrests, 19 searches, CZK 33M, CZK 43M, 138 victims) appear only in this Czech Police release among public tier-1 sources accessed during ingest. The Eurojust press release of the same date covers the CONNECT branch but does not enumerate OCTOPUS separately.
