## Source Summary

DOJ District of New Jersey press release (dated 2024-05-07) unsealing a 26-count federal indictment against Dmitry Yuryevich Khoroshev — alias LockBitSupp / LockBit / putinkrab; 31, of Voronezh, Russia — as the developer and administrator of the LockBit ransomware group from approximately September 2019 through May 2024. The release describes LockBit as having attacked more than 2,500 victims in at least 120 countries (1,800 in the United States), with at least USD 500 million in ransom proceeds and billions of dollars in broader losses, and Khoroshev personally receiving at least USD 100 million through his 20% developer-share of LockBit ransom payments. The DOJ press release frames the indictment as a follow-on to the February 2024 NCA-led disruption of LockBit and is announced concurrently with (a) a US Treasury (OFAC) sanctions designation, (b) a US Department of State reward of up to USD 10 million for information leading to Khoroshev's apprehension or conviction, and (c) parallel UK (OFSI) and Australian (DFAT, AFP) sanctions actions.
