## Summary

This Europol press release announces a **Joint Investigation Team (JIT)** takedown of a Zeus/SpyEye banking-Trojan crew on 18-19 June 2015 in Ukraine. The JIT included 6 EU/EEA members (Austria, Belgium, Finland, Netherlands, Norway, UK) with Eurojust judicial support; additional cooperation from 7 more countries including the US.

**Action-day results**:
- 5 arrests in Ukraine
- 8 house searches across 4 Ukrainian cities
- Forensic seizures of computing equipment

**Broader operation**: 60 total arrests including 34 money mules from a linked subsidiary network; ~EUR 2 million in documented damages.

## Why this source matters

The Zeus/SpyEye JIT is procedurally significant because it is an early, well-documented example of **JIT as a functional cybercrime cooperation instrument**. For [[zeus-spyeye-jit-takedown]] and the [[joint-investigation-team|JIT mechanism page]] this release provides:

- **Named JIT member states** (6 countries) — rare to find this precisely in press releases
- **Ukraine as operational venue** without JIT membership — demonstrates the JIT-plus-bilateral-cooperation hybrid model
- **Money-mule layer integration** — the 34 mule arrests from the subsidiary operation document how JITs extend beyond technical actors to cash-out networks
- **Eurojust role** — judicial coordination evidence

This source should be cited whenever the Zeus, SpyEye, or JIT mechanism pages make claims about European cybercrime cooperation history.
