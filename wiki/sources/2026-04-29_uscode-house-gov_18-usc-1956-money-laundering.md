## Source Summary

18 U.S.C. 1956 is a principal U.S. money-laundering statute for transactions involving proceeds of specified unlawful activity. It is frequently relevant to cybercrime cases involving cryptocurrency flows, mule networks, fraud proceeds, darknet markets, and asset tracing.
