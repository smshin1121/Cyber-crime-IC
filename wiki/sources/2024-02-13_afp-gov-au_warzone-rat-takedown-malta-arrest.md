## Summary

Australian Federal Police media release announcing the arrest in Malta of a 27-year-old man identified as a key promoter of the Warzone Remote Access Trojan, following intelligence provided by the AFP to international partners (Europol and FBI). The operation was coordinated with the U.S. Department of Justice's parallel domain-seizure and indictment action announced four days earlier (DOJ Press Release 24-163, 9 February 2024), which charged Daniel Meli (Malta) and Prince Onyeoziri Odinakachi (Nigeria) and listed acknowledgments to law enforcement in Australia, Canada, Croatia, Finland, Germany (Saxony LKA), Japan, Malta, Netherlands, Nigeria, and Romania.

This is the first AFP-published source page in the wiki documenting this operation; the entity is being introduced as a fresh tier-1 primary source for the Warzone RAT takedown.

## Provenance and reliability

Tier-1 primary press release issued by the Australian Federal Police via its official Media Release channel (afp.gov.au). Reliability rated **high** as a national law-enforcement statement on its own operational role. Credibility rated **probably-true** pending judicial outcome — indictments are allegations until proven; the AFP statement uses "alleged" and "will be alleged" consistently.

The AFP release is corroborated by:

- Court filings: Northern District of Georgia indictment (Meli, 12 Dec 2023, four counts); District of Massachusetts indictment (Odinakachi, 30 Jan 2024, conspiracy to commit multiple computer intrusion offenses).
