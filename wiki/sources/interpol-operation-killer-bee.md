## Summary

INTERPOL's 30 May 2022 release announces **Operation Killer Bee**, which resulted in the arrest of three Nigerian nationals in Lagos and Benin City for operating a BEC scheme using **Agent Tesla** RAT to hijack corporate wire transfers across Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and North Africa.

Key attributes:

- **11-country scope** (Nigeria + 10 affected countries: Brunei, Cambodia, Indonesia, Laos, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam)
- **Lead**: INTERPOL ASEAN Cybercrime Operations Desk
- **Funding**: Japan-ASEAN Integration Fund 2.0 + Singapore government support
- **Private-sector intelligence**: Trend Micro Agent Tesla telemetry
- **Conviction**: Hendrix Omorume sentenced to 12 months (3 counts of serious financial fraud); 2 others in trial

## Why this source matters

For [[operation-killer-bee]] this is the **primary INTERPOL source**. It provides several distinctive data points:

- **Cross-region enforcement**: Nigeria-origin actors against ASEAN victims is unusual; most Nigerian BEC enforcement has involved Western targets. This broadens the geographic footprint of the SilverTerrier actor cluster
- **Non-Western funding**: the Japan-ASEAN Integration Fund 2.0 (JAIF 2.0) as the enabling funding source is a distinctive model that should be documented on [[capacity-building-ic]] and [[japan|Japan country profile]]
- **Singapore coordination role**: reinforces Singapore's growing reputation as an ASEAN cybercrime coordination hub
- **Trend Micro private-sector integration**: adds another data point to the pattern of private-firm-sourced threat intelligence anchoring INTERPOL BEC enforcement
