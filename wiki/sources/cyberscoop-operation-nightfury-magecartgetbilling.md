## Summary

CyberScoop's coverage of **Operation NightFury** reports the **27 January 2020** announcement by INTERPOL that **3 Indonesian men** were arrested on **20 December 2019** in Jakarta and Yogyakarta on charges of operating a Magecart-style web skimming group tracked as **GetBilling**.

Key specifics:

1. **571 infected online marketplaces** reported by Sanguine Labs; INTERPOL's own statement referred more conservatively to "hundreds of websites"
2. **Group-IB threat intelligence** drove the identification of the Indonesia-based operators — an example of direct private-sector intelligence sharing with INTERPOL
3. **INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation (IGCI)** in Singapore coordinated the operation through the ASEAN Cybercrime Operations Desk (ASEAN Desk)
4. **Maximum sentence**: 10 years in Indonesian prison for each defendant if convicted
5. **Human-element detail**: CyberScoop includes the unusual vignette that one arrested suspect told Indonesian media he had earned barely enough to buy a jacket from the skimming operation — suggesting the arrested individuals were low-level affiliates rather than Magecart leadership
