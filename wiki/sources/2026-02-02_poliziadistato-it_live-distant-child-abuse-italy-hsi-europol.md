## Source Profile

Official press release issued by the Italian State Police (Polizia di Stato) Press Office on `poliziadistato.it`, the central institutional domain. The release announces the conclusion of an undercover transnational investigation into "live distant child abuse" run by the Centro Nazionale per il Contrasto alla Pedopornografia Online (C.N.C.P.O.) of [[italy-polizia-postale|Polizia Postale e per la sicurezza cibernetica]], coordinated by the Procura di Milano (Public Prosecutor's Office, Milan), and conducted in international cooperation with the U.S. Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) and with [[europol-ec3|Europol]].

The release is a tier-1 primary source: it is published on the official `.poliziadistato.it` Italian Polizia di Stato domain, signed institutionally by the Press Office, and provides operational figures (arrests, denunciations, geographic distribution) directly from the lead investigative agency. The Italian-language text (preserved verbatim in the corresponding raw file) is authoritative; the working English summary in this entry is for indexing only.

The investigation has been described in Italian secondary press as "Operazione Light Bridge" but that operation name does **not** appear in the official Polizia di Stato press release; it should therefore be treated as an unverified secondary-press alias.

> [!note] Operation-name caveat
> "Operazione Light Bridge" is widely cited in Italian secondary press (LSD Magazine, La Milano, Sky TG24, Matrice Digitale) for this same investigation. The official Polizia di Stato press release does not use that name; this wiki therefore treats "Light Bridge" as an unverified alias and uses descriptive titling pending confirmation from a Polizia di Stato or Procura di Milano source that explicitly uses the operation name.

> [!note] Translation note
> "Denuncia in stato di libertà" has no exact English equivalent and is distinct from both U.S. "indictment" and U.S. "summons" practice; it denotes that a formal criminal report has been transmitted to the prosecutor while the suspect remains at liberty. The Italian text uses the English-language industry term "live distant child abuse" verbatim, reflecting standard Europol/HSI usage in this offence stream.
