## Source Summary

Official Guardia Civil press release (Spanish national gendarmerie / cybercrime authority — tier-1 primary source) announcing the dismantling of the **GXC Team / GoogleXcoder** Crime-as-a-Service phishing-kit operation. Investigation led by the **Departamento contra el Cibercrimen de la Unidad Central Operativa (UCO)** under the **Juzgado de Instrucción número 1 de San Vicente de la Barquera (Cantabria)**, with international cooperation from the **Brazilian Federal Police (Policía Federal de Brasil)** and private-sector support from **Group-IB**.

Key elements:

- **Target:** "GoogleXcoder" — 25-year-old Brazilian national, principal developer of CaaS phishing kits cloning Spanish banks and public-administration websites; lived as a "digital nomad" across multiple Spanish provinces using fraudulent identities, prepaid SIM cards and prepaid payment cards.
- **Customers:** Spanish-speaking phishing crews, contacted via Telegram, paid hundreds of euros per day; one criminal Telegram channel was openly named "Robarle todo a las abuelas".
- **Impact:** millions of euros stolen; victims numbered in the thousands; dozens of impersonated banking and government entities; Spanish-language phishing market substantially driven by this single supplier from 2023 onward.
- **IC scope:** Brazil ↔ Spain, with Brazilian Federal Police cooperation underpinning identification of the Brazilian-national suspect; private-sector technical support by Group-IB.
- **Outcome:** main developer arrested in San Vicente de la Barquera (Cantabria); six entries and searches in Valladolid, Zaragoza, Barcelona, Palma de Mallorca, San Fernando and La Línea de la Concepción; six additional users of the CaaS service identified; Telegram channels deactivated; investigation remains open.
