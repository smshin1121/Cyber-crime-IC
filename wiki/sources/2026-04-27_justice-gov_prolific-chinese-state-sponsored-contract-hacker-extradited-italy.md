## Source Summary

DOJ-OPA Press Release 26-404 dated 2026-04-27 announcing the weekend extradition from Italy of Xu Zewei (PRC national, 34) — a contractor for Shanghai Powerock Network Co. Ltd. who acted under direction of PRC MSS Shanghai State Security Bureau (SSSB) officers — and his first US court appearance in Houston on a 9-count S.D. Texas indictment for COVID-19 research intrusions (early 2020) and HAFNIUM Microsoft Exchange Server intrusions (late 2020 — early 2021). Xu was arrested at Milan Malpensa airport in July 2025; extradition coordinated by DOJ OIA + Italian National Police Cyber Division (Polizia Postale). Co-defendant Zhang Yu remains at large.

## IC Pattern

This release establishes a US-Italy bilateral extradition pattern for Chinese state-sponsored hackers — the first wiki record of such a pattern. Notable features:

- **Italy as extradition source for a Chinese state-sponsored hacker** — characterized as "rare" in third-party reporting; first wiki record.
- **PRC MSS / SSSB direction with private-contractor architecture** (Shanghai Powerock Network Co. Ltd.): the PRC uses an extensive network of private companies and contractors to obscure state involvement in hacking, with contractors casting a wide net for vulnerable computers and selling stolen information back to the PRC government.
- **Two-phase activity attribution**: COVID-19 research intrusions (Phase 1, early 2020) and HAFNIUM Microsoft Exchange campaign (Phase 2, late 2020 – early 2021) under the same SSSB direction.
- **DOJ OIA + Italian Polizia Postale Cyber Division** as the operational extradition channel.
- **HAFNIUM scale**: 12,700+ US organizations compromised — the wiki's first record of HAFNIUM-attributed intrusion-volume figures in primary IC documentation.
