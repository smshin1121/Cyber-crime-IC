# NPA Japan + FBI + DC3 joint attribution — TraderTraitor / DMM Bitcoin $308M cryptocurrency theft (2024-12-24)

## Summary

On **2024-12-24**, the **National Police Agency of Japan (警察庁 / NPA)** — together with the **U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)** and the **U.S. Department of Defense Cyber Crime Center (DC3)** — published a joint advisory attributing the May 2024 theft of **4,502.9 BTC ≈ USD 308 million / JPY ≈ 48.2 billion** from **DMM Bitcoin**, a Japan-based cryptocurrency exchange, to the **North Korea-backed cyber attack group "TraderTraitor"** (also tracked as Jade Sleet, UNC4899, Slow Pisces; assessed sub-cluster of the Lazarus Group family).

The joint advisory was published simultaneously on NPA's own domain (`npa.go.jp/bureau/cyber/koho/caution/caution20241224.html` — Japanese narrative + Japanese-language PDF + English-language PDF on `cyber.go.jp/eng`) and on the FBI's own domain (`fbi.gov/news/press-releases/...`).

The advisory is the first formal multi-government attribution of the May 2024 DMM Bitcoin loss — at the time of attribution one of the largest single-incident cryptocurrency thefts ever publicly tied to North Korean state-affiliated cyber actors.

## Why this matters for IC

1. **Bilateral US-Japan cyber cooperation as operational not just diplomatic.** This release pairs NPA Cyber Affairs Bureau with FBI + DC3 on **operational attribution** rather than diplomacy-level coordination. The technical attack-chain narrative is jointly endorsed — a step beyond parallel statements.
2. **Public-attribution-as-IC-instrument.** The seven-month gap between the May 2024 loss and the December 2024 joint attribution illustrates that joint attribution is a deliberate IC product, not a real-time response — it requires evidence sharing under bilateral cyber MoUs, joint forensic analysis (DC3's contribution), and political concurrence to publish.
3. **DPRK crypto-theft as recurring fact pattern.** The release joins the [[treasury-sinbad-mixer-dprk-lazarus-sanctions-2023|Sinbad mixer DPRK sanctions]], the [[treasury-sanctions-dprk-it-worker-network-andreyev-chinyong-2025|Chinyong/Andreyev IT-worker sanctions]], and the [[us-doj-coordinated-nationwide-actions-dprk-it-workers-2025|DOJ 2025 nationwide IT-worker actions]] as a multi-year pattern of US-led + partner-LE attributions of DPRK crypto-theft and revenue-generation operations.
4. **Korean / Japanese cooperation interlock.** Japan's joint attribution mirrors the trilateral US-ROK-Japan cyber cooperation announced in 2025 ([[us-rok-japan-joint-statement-dprk-it-workers-2025-08]]), demonstrating a hardening US-Japan-Korea front against DPRK cyber-revenue generation.
