## Source Summary

New Zealand Police news release dated Wednesday 16 September 2015 (5:12 pm) announcing the arrest in Vejle, Denmark of a 24-year-old Danish citizen as part of **Operation Cavern** — a joint investigation between the **New Zealand Police**, the **Danish National Cyber Crime Centre (NC3)**, and **South-East Jutland Police**. The suspect was charged under Danish law with cybercrimes relating to the online posting of private photos and the hacking of private computers. The named victim is an Auckland (NZ) school girl. Further case details were withheld at the time of the release due to closed-doors judicial hearings in Denmark.

## Relevance to IC

This is the principal tier-1 own-domain source for the 2015-09 Operation Cavern joint investigation. It supplies (a) the explicit named cooperation between [[new-zealand|NZ Police]] and Danish authorities ([[denmark-police|NC3]] and South-East Jutland Police), (b) the verbatim prosecutor-on-the-record attribution of "close, smooth, mutual, fruitful" multi-country cybercrime IC, (c) a clean 2-jurisdiction, 3-LE-agency template for the **domestic-victim, foreign-perpetrator** cooperation pattern in the absence of extradition (the suspect is a Danish citizen prosecuted in Denmark under Danish law, with NZ supplying the investigative lead and evidence), and (d) one of the earliest tier-1 NZ Police own-domain cybercrime cooperation releases in the wiki corpus.

## Cooperation pattern note

Operation Cavern is structurally distinct from later Europol-coordinated takedowns: it is a **2-country bilateral** investigation with no intergovernmental coordinating body named (no Europol, no Eurojust, no INTERPOL credit in the release), and with no extradition step (the Danish citizen suspect is prosecuted in Denmark). This is the "remote-victim domestic-prosecution" pattern for cybercrime, distinguishable from the more common "foreign-suspect extradition-to-victim-jurisdiction" pattern.
