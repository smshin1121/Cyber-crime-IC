## Source Summary

USAO District of Maryland press release (dated 2026-03-04) announcing the wire fraud conspiracy guilty plea of Evgenii Ptitsyn, 43, a Russian national, for his role as a Phobos ransomware administrator. Phobos ransomware, through its affiliates, is alleged to have victimised more than 1,000 public and private entities worldwide and extorted more than USD 39 million in ransom payments. **Ptitsyn was extradited from South Korea in November 2024**, marking the first wiki-recorded instance of South Korea extraditing a Russian ransomware administrator to the United States. Sentencing is scheduled for 2026-07-15. Ptitsyn faces a maximum 20-year sentence on the wire fraud count.

The release records (a) the operating model of the Phobos ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS): administrators developed and sold access on a darknet site, affiliates hacked victims (often via stolen credentials), encrypted data, and extorted payment, with administrators receiving decryption-key fees plus a portion of ransom payments — a 2021-2024 financial trail records that decryption fees were transferred from each affiliate's unique cryptocurrency wallet to a wallet Ptitsyn controlled; (b) the explicit international cooperation roster of 10 cooperating countries (South Korea, United Kingdom, Japan, Spain, Belgium, Poland, Czech Republic, France, Romania) plus Europol and the US Department of Defense Cyber Crime Center; and (c) the named US prosecutorial team (AUSA Thomas M. Sullivan + CCIPS Senior Counsel Frank Lin).
