## Source Summary

Tier-1 English-language primary press release issued by the **Philippine National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) Cybercrime Division (NBI-CCD)** on 2025-11-28 (with internal press-statement date 2025-11-27), announcing the arrest in Parañaque City, Metro Manila, of Chinese national **Li Zhenjie** for operating a US-impersonation cryptocurrency-extortion scam targeting victims in the United States. The release details the legal pathway used (RTC Makati WSSECD granted 2025-11-20; executed 2025-11-21), the substantive Philippine charges (RA 12010 §4(b)(1) Social Engineering Schemes in relation to RA 10175), and the modus operandi (number-spoofing impersonation of New Haven PD and FBI Washington, fake nationwide-online-arrest-warrant pretext, USD 3,000–5,000 cryptocurrency demand to alias "GENERAL"), and is signed off in voice by NBI OIC Director Atty. Angelito DLP Magno.

## Relevance to IC

The release documents a **Philippine-domestic prosecution of an outbound transnational cyber-fraud cell**: a cell physically located in the Philippines, run by a foreign national (Chinese), targeting victims in a third country (United States), and impersonating US law-enforcement agencies. The international-cooperation interest of the source is therefore at three levels:

1. **Substantive-law coverage of outbound transnational scams.** The use of Republic Act No. 12010 §4(b)(1) (Social Engineering Schemes) in relation to Republic Act No. 10175 demonstrates that Philippine substantive cybercrime law is being used to prosecute conduct whose victims are located outside the Philippines, without waiting for a US extradition request or US prosecution. This is a "host-state criminalisation of outbound scam infrastructure" pattern.
2. **Domestic-judicial fast-path: WSSECD.** The Warrant to Search, Seize, and Examine Computer Data (WSSECD) granted by the RTC Makati on 2025-11-20 and executed on 2025-11-21 is a Philippine procedural tool (under the 2018 Rule on Cybercrime Warrants, A.M. No. 17-11-03-SC) that supports rapid evidence-preservation actions on Philippine soil — relevant for any future MLAT/preservation request from the United States.
3. **Cooperation-gap documentation.** The release does not name a US, Chinese, or third-country counterpart in the operational chain. This is itself analytically significant: a host-state takedown of a cell targeting another country's victims, using only domestic authority, with no public attestation of inbound US victim notification, FBI cooperation, or Chinese-side investigation handover. The wiki should record this as a single-source attestation pending follow-on US/Chinese sources.
