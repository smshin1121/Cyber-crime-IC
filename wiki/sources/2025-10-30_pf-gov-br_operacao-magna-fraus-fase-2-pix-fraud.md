## Source Summary

Brazilian Federal Police (PF) press release dated 2025-10-30 (last updated 2025-10-31) announcing Phase 2 of **Operação Magna Fraus**, a multi-jurisdiction takedown of a criminal organisation that drained more than R$ 813 million from accounts used by Brazilian banks and payment institutions to manage customer **PIX** (Brazil's instant-payment system) transfers via computer-device intrusion (invasão de dispositivo informático). The action combined federal police execution in 11 Brazilian cities across 7 states with international arrests in Spain (6) and Argentina (2), coordinated through INTERPOL channels and the Spanish National Police's computer-fraud brigade.

## IC Pattern

This release documents a **Brazil-led, INTERPOL-mediated, Iberian-Latin American multi-NCB action-day** pattern. Notable features:

- **PF as lead agency / requesting state**, with Cyber GAECO of the São Paulo Public Prosecution Service (Ministério Público do Estado de São Paulo, 상파울루주 검찰) co-operating on the prosecutorial side.
- **INTERPOL Command and Coordination Centre (CCC)** as the cross-jurisdictional coordination node for simultaneous arrests.
- **Four-NCB axis**: INTERPOL National Central Bureaux in Brazil, Spain, Argentina, and Portugal — with Portugal listed as an NCB-cooperation partner although no Portuguese arrests were reported in the partial balance (suggesting Portugal-side investigative or asset-tracing role rather than action-day arrests; flag for verification).
- **Spain's Brigada Central de Fraudes Informáticos (Polícia Nacional)** named explicitly as the operational counterpart on the Spanish side — i.e. dedicated cyber-fraud unit, not a general policing body.
- **Crime typology — bank-fraud-as-cybercrime**: PIX-rail account drain via institutional-account compromise, treated under Brazil's penal code as organised crime + computer-device intrusion + electronic-fraud theft + money laundering, illustrating Brazilian prosecution's hybrid cybercrime / financial-crime framing.

## Wiki Cross-Corpus Relevance

This case fills three identifiable wiki gaps:

1. **First Brazil-Spain-Argentina cybercrime IC action-day** documented in the wiki. Existing Brazil-side coverage in the wiki (e.g. [[spf-fpb-operation-cryptoscam-2025|Operation Cryptoscam (SPF-FPB, 2025)]], various US-Brazil extraditions) treats Brazil as a partner state in Singapore- or US-led cases; Magna Fraus is the inverse — Brazil-led, with Iberian-LatAm arrests on the Spain/Argentina side.
2. **PIX-payment-system attack vector** is a new technique-class in the wiki, complementary to existing bank-trojan and BEC pages.
3. **Cyber GAECO (MP-SP)** appears for the first time as a Brazilian prosecutorial partner alongside PF; this is a state-level (not federal) public-ministry cyber unit, structurally distinct from the federal-prosecutorial bodies usually seen in cross-border cases.
