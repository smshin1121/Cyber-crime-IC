## Source Summary

Tier-1 U.S. Department of Justice Office of Public Affairs press release dated October 1, 2024 announcing the unsealing of a Northern District of Texas indictment against Aleksandr Viktorovich Ryzhenkov for BitPaymer ransomware attacks. The release expressly coordinates with the same-day OFAC tri-lateral US-UK-AU sanctions action ([[2024-10-01_treasury_evil-corp-tri-lateral-us-uk-australia-sanctions-jy2623|Treasury JY-2623]]) and acknowledges UK National Crime Agency intelligence on Ryzhenkov's separate LockBit affiliate role. The pairing is the canonical "designate + indict" pattern for Russia-based ransomware actors who are physically out of U.S. reach.

## Relevance to IC

- **DOJ-OFAC own-domain pairing.** Provides the second tier-1 own-domain source for [[treasury-evil-corp-tri-lateral-us-uk-au-sanctions-2024]] — Treasury was previously the lone source. With OPA added the operation now has both the financial-sanctions side (Treasury JY-2623) and the criminal-prosecution side (DOJ OPA) of the October 1, 2024 Evil Corp action recorded from primary tier-1 own-domain releases.
- **US-UK LE-cooperation acknowledgement.** The DOJ release directly names the UK National Crime Agency as a reporting source for Ryzhenkov's LockBit-affiliate status, satisfying L24's ≥2-country tier-1-acknowledged LE-cooperation requirement on the DOJ side independently of the Treasury release.
- **Venue correction.** The operation page frontmatter previously asserted "DOJ-EDTX". The DOJ OPA release identifies the venue as **Northern District of Texas** (USAO-NDTX), not Eastern District. The operation page body and frontmatter should be reconciled to NDTX.
