## Source Summary

Europol Newsroom press release announcing **Operation Alice** (action days 9–19 March 2026, press release published around 21 March 2026), a German-led, Europol-coordinated, 23-country cybercrime takedown. The operation closed a nearly five-year investigation into a Chinese national operating **373,000+ dark web onion domains** that fraudulently advertised CSAM and cybercrime-as-a-service (CaaS) products without delivering them. Action results: 1 perpetrator identified (35-year-old male, PRC; international arrest warrant issued), 440 customers worldwide identified (100+ subject to ongoing investigations), 373,000+ onion domains shut down, 105 servers seized in Germany, electronic devices seized. Operator earned EUR 345,000+ from ~10,000 paying customers (Feb 2020–Jul 2025) via Bitcoin payments for nominal CSAM 'packages' priced EUR 17–215.

The release establishes:
1. The **largest documented dark-web onion-domain takedown** in the wiki corpus by domain count (373,000+ vs. tens of domains for typical takedowns).
2. The **fraud-CSAM hybrid pattern** — CSAM-advertised but undelivered platforms simultaneously hosting CaaS offerings.
3. The **customer-prosecution model** in CSAM-related fraud cases — paying customers prosecuted under their national CSAM laws regardless of non-delivery.
4. **23-country participation** as the largest CSAM-related Europol-coordinated cooperation in the wiki corpus to date.
