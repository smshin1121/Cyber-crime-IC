## Source Summary

National Prosecuting Authority of South Africa (NPA) Media Release dated **21 April 2026** announcing that the Gauteng Division of the High Court, Johannesburg granted the NPA's **Asset Forfeiture Unit (AFU)** a **provisional restraint order under section 26 of the Prevention of Organised Crime Act 121 of 1998 (POCA)** on **26 February 2026**, restraining assets valued at approximately **R43.9 million** linked to a global investment-fraud call-centre syndicate operating from Bryanston, Johannesburg. The restraint order followed the arrest of suspects on **27 January 2026** and was executed on the ground on **14 April 2026** with auctioneers taking possession of luxury and utility vehicles.

The NPA release describes the underlying conduct as a long-running international investment-fraud scheme run by an "international Israeli syndicate" using South African-based call centres to target retiree-age victims in **Australia, New Zealand, Canada, the United States of America, and various other countries**. Victims were lured through internet "click bait" advertisements, contacted by call-centre agents who used aliases and grooming techniques, and persuaded to invest in **non-existing products** under promises of high returns.

The release establishes the following tier-1 facts:

1. **NPA-led asset-forfeiture posture** — the article-of-record action is a POCA section 26 *provisional* restraint order; final confiscation depends on outcome of the underlying criminal case.
2. **Domestic inter-agency cooperation only (within this source)** — the explicit cooperation pattern named is DPCI + NPA SCCU + NPA AFU. While other reporting (news media) names the United Kingdom National Crime Agency, the United States Department of Homeland Security and Secret Service, the Australian Federal Police, INTERPOL, and private-sector partners as participants in the **27 January 2026 arrest action**, *the NPA tier-1 source itself names none of these foreign agencies.* Per [`LESSONS.md`](../../LESSONS.md) L19, this wiki only asserts as participating-agency what tier-1 primary sources explicitly name.
3. **International dimension is sourced through victim geography and syndicate nationality** — the NPA source itself names Australia, New Zealand, Canada, the United States, and Israel (as syndicate origin), giving the case clear cross-border IC scope even on the conservative reading.
4. **Court venue and statutory basis explicit** — Gauteng Division High Court Johannesburg (restraint order); Palm Ridge Magistrates' Court (criminal case); POCA s.26 (statutory basis).
5. **R43.9 million asset value** is itemised: multiple Gauteng immovable properties (some >R3M each) plus enumerated motor vehicles.
