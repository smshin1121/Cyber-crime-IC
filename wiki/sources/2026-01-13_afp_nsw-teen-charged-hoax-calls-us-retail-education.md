# Source summary — NSW teen charged for multiple hoax calls to major retail, education institutions in United States

## Overview

Joint media release issued 13 January 2026 by the Australian Federal Police (AFP) and the United States Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) announcing the AFP's charging of a juvenile from regional New South Wales with 12 counts of using a carriage service to cause harm or menace and one prohibited firearm offence. The juvenile is alleged to have made 12 false reports of imminent mass shootings against US retail and education institutions between October 2025 and January 2026, each call resulting in armed law-enforcement deployment, lockdowns, and emergency evacuations in the United States.

## International cooperation elements

- The investigation was initiated by the AFP in November 2025 after the FBI provided information indicating an Australian-based offender was responsible for the US swatting calls.
- Two distinct national LE agencies are explicitly named as cooperating: the AFP and the FBI (joint media release attribution at the top of the release).
- The investigation falls within AFP **Taskforce Pompilid**, established October 2025 to disrupt decentralised online crime networks, and is described as part of ongoing Five Eyes Law Enforcement Group cooperation.
- NSW Police provided domestic assistance to the AFP at the 9 January 2026 search-and-arrest action.
