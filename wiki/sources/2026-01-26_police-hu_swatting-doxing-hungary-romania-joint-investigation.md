## Source Summary

Hungarian National Police press release of 2026-01-26 announcing the conclusion of a six-month investigation that began in mid-July 2025 with a wave of swatting and doxing offences in Hungary. The release confirms a 2026-01-20 coordinated joint action between three Hungarian agencies (KR NNI, TEK, Nagykanizsa Police HQ) and Romanian police across four locations in Hungary (Budapest, Nyíregyháza, Kisvárda) and Romania (Bihor County). All four perpetrators — three Hungarian and one Romanian, ages 16-20 — were identified through reconnaissance work supported by the Counter Terrorism Centre (TEK). Discord was the contact-and-escalation platform; victim data harvested in Discord disputes was used to place false emergency calls.

The piece is a tier-1 primary source: it is the official announcement by the Hungarian National Bureau of Investigation (KR NNI), the operational lead, and is publicly accessible in Hungarian on police.hu (no paywall, no log-in). Reliability is high; credibility is confirmed for the procedural facts (date, agencies, locations, charges) that it asserts directly. The Romanian counterpart agency is identified only generically as "the Romanian partner authority" / "Romanian police" — a name-level gap noted in the operation page.

## IC Pattern

This release establishes a previously unrepresented cooperation pattern in the wiki:

- **Hungary-Romania bilateral cybercrime cooperation on swatting/doxing** with overlapping cross-border participation: not a parallel-investigation pattern but a shared-team pattern, with Hungarian KR NNI cyber investigators physically present at the Romanian-led Bihor County search. This is closer in structure to a joint investigation team (JIT) than to a sequential MLAT pattern, although the press release does not explicitly describe a Eurojust-supported JIT.
- **TEK (Counter Terrorism Centre) reconnaissance of swatting/doxing offences** — Hungarian classification of swatting bomb threats against schools and police units as terrorism-adjacent offences, justifying TEK involvement in the reconnaissance phase. The Romanian 17-year-old is charged with "terrorist act" (terrorcselekmény) — confirming this classification at the indictment level.
- **Discord-as-vector** is documented at the official-source level for an operational case rather than only in academic or media commentary.
- **Underage and just-of-age cohort (16-20)** suggests a youth-cybercrime pattern that is increasingly transnational at the regional (CEE) level.
