## Summary

Primary tier-1 release from CBZC (Polish Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime) reporting on Poland's participation in Europol's Victim Identification Taskforce (VIDTF) action of 13–24 April 2026, hosted at Europol headquarters in The Hague. The action tentatively identified 12 child victims of online sexual exploitation and generated 204 leads forwarded to national authorities.

## Key findings

- 34 Europol + INTERPOL victim-identification specialists worked alongside experts from 31 countries during the 13–24 April 2026 window.
- The taskforce tentatively identified 12 children in CSAM material; 204 leads were referred to national authorities for follow-up.
- 317 datasets were analysed during the two-week action.
- Cumulative VIDTF results since 2014: 330 perpetrators arrested; 1,190 victims secured; 118+ million files processed since 2016.
- CBZC describes the cooperation in Polish as "34 specjalistów Europolu i Interpolu ds. identyfikacji ofiar oraz ekspertów z 31 krajów".
