## Source Summary

DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release (Number 26-171, dated 2026-02-20) announcing the plea of one count of obtaining information from a protected computer and one count of aggravated identity theft by Romanian national Catalin Dragomir, 45, formerly of Constanta, Romania, in the District of Oregon. Dragomir is alleged to have sold access in June 2021 to a computer on the network of an Oregon state government office, plus access to the computer networks of numerous other US victims, causing losses of at least USD 250,000. The release records (a) the arrest in Romania in November 2024, (b) extradition to the United States in January 2025 (~2-month transit), (c) the plea on 2026-02-19, (d) sentencing scheduled for 2026-05-26, with a statutory maximum of 5 years (computer-information offence) plus a 2-year mandatory consecutive sentence (aggravated identity theft), and (e) the bilateral US-Romania extradition pathway with named Romanian counterparts (Romanian Ministry of Justice — Directorate for International Law and Judicial Cooperation, Romanian judiciary). Private-sector partner Darkweb IQ is named as having assisted the investigation.
