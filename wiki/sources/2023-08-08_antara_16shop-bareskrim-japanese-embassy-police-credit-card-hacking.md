## Source Profile

This is an English-language caption on **ANTARA Foto** (`antarafoto.com`), the photojournalism arm of **Perum LKBN ANTARA**, Indonesia's state news agency. The frame and caption document the **8 August 2023 press conference** held at Bareskrim Mabes Polri HQ in Jakarta at which Indonesia's Directorate of Cyber Crimes (*Direktorat Tindak Pidana Siber*, "Dirtipidsiber") publicly announced its share of the 16shop phishing-platform takedown — coordinated with the **8 August 2023 INTERPOL release from Singapore** that publicly announced the same operation at the international level.

The caption is the editorial-staff written summary by ANTARA editor **Hermanus Prihatna**; the photograph is by ANTARA photographer **Muhammad Adimaja**. The frame shows three officials on stage:

- **Brigadier General Pol. Adi Vivid Agustiadi Bachtiar** (Director, Dirtipidsiber Bareskrim Polri) — the same officer quoted on the record by INTERPOL in its 8 August 2023 release.
- **Brigadier General Pol. Ahmad Ramadhan** (Karo Penmas, Public Information Bureau Head, Humas Polri).
- **Mr. Miyagawa** (Police attaché, Japanese Embassy for Indonesia).

Under the wiki's **L24 strict** publisher rule, ANTARA qualifies as a tier-1 source (Indonesian state news wire reproducing an official LE statement at an official Polri press conference). L25 disqualifies private commercial wires; ANTARA is state-owned (Perum / Public Corporation under Presidential Regulation No. 40/2007) and is the structural analogue of the L24-enumerated state wires (BTA Bulgaria, Yonhap Korea, ANSA Italy, Tribrata News Polri).

In the case **Ditipidsiber Bareskrim Polri together with the Japanese Police party managed to secure two suspects of WNI along with proof tools including smart phones, laptops and hardisks** in the case of a credit card hack that results in a total loss of Rp1.6 billion."

## Cross-Check vs INTERPOL Release (Same Day)

The Antara Foto caption is fully consistent with the 8 August 2023 INTERPOL release. Antara documents the **Jakarta-side** announcement at Bareskrim HQ and the **in-country diplomatic-LE channel** (Japanese Embassy police attaché on stage at Bareskrim), while INTERPOL documents the **Singapore-side** intergovernmental coordination and the **NPA-INP bilateral channel**. Both describe the same three-arrest sequence (administrator + 1 facilitator in Indonesia; second facilitator in Japan) and the same lead Polri officer (Brigjen Pol Adi Vivid Agustiadi Bachtiar).

No contradictions. The Indonesian-sub-case loss figure (Rp1.6 billion) reported by Antara is a different scope from INTERPOL's global victimisation figure (>70,000 users across 43 countries) and the two are complementary rather than contradictory.
