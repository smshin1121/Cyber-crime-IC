## Source Summary

On 5 December 2022, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Middle District of Florida announced the sentencing of Allen Levinson, formerly known as Allen Ameh, to 78 months in prison for wire-fraud conspiracy tied to an international tax-fraud scheme. DOJ's later xDedic summary identified Levinson as a prolific xDedic Marketplace buyer. According to DOJ, Levinson purchased access to U.S.-based Certified Public Accounting firms through xDedic and used the information obtained from those servers to file hundreds of false tax returns. The scheme attempted to obtain more than USD 63 million in fraudulent refunds, and the IRS actually paid more than USD 5.5 million. Levinson was arrested in the United Kingdom in December 2020, extradited to the United States, and pleaded guilty in February 2022. The case is a strong example of how xDedic access sales translated into downstream tax and identity fraud prosecuted in U.S. federal court.
