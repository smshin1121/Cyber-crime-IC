## Source Summary

On 22 March 2022, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Eastern District of Texas announced that a federal grand jury had returned an indictment against Igor Dekhtyarchuk, a Russian national, for operating "Marketplace A," a cybercriminal carding shop that allegedly sold thousands of stolen login credentials, personally identifiable information, and authentication tools. The indictment was returned on 16 March 2022. According to the indictment, Marketplace A claimed to have sold access to more than 48,000 compromised email accounts and more than 39,000 compromised online accounts while averaging roughly 5,000 daily visitors. The FBI's online covert employee made 13 undercover purchases between March and July 2021 from the Eastern District of Texas, obtaining access devices for 131 victim accounts. The case is highly relevant for international-cooperation analysis because the investigation reportedly involved the FBI Legal Attaché office in Riga and the State Police of Latvia, which likely reflects a mix of formal and informal cross-border investigative support even though the public release does not specify the precise legal mechanism.
