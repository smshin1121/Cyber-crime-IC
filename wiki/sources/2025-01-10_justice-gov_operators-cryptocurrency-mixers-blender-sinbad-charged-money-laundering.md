## Source Summary

On 10 January 2025 the U.S. Department of Justice Office of Public Affairs announced the unsealing of a federal indictment, returned by a grand jury in the Northern District of Georgia on 7 January 2025, charging three Russian nationals with operating the cryptocurrency mixers Blender.io (2018–April 2022) and its alleged successor Sinbad.io (October 2022 onward). The release announces the prior arrest of two of the three defendants — Roman Vitalyevich Ostapenko and Alexander Evgenievich Oleynik — on 1 December 2024 in a coordinated international operation involving the Netherlands Fiscal Information and Investigative Service (FIOD), the Finnish National Bureau of Investigation (NBI), and the FBI. The third defendant, Anton Vyachlavovich Tarasov, remains at large.

## Relevance to IC

The release is highly relevant to international cooperation on cybercrime in three respects: (1) it documents a discrete tri-national arrest pattern ([[netherlands|Netherlands]] + [[finland|Finland]] + [[united-states|United States]]) effecting the apprehension of two Russian-national defendants on the territory of EU member states, demonstrating that intra-EU + transatlantic informal police cooperation continues to function for cyber-monetization targets even where the home state ([[russia]]) refuses extradition cooperation; (2) it formally links Lazarus Group / [[north-korea|DPRK]] state-sponsored cryptocurrency-theft monetization to U.S. federal criminal liability for service operators, complementing the prior OFAC sanctions track ([[treasury-sinbad-mixer-dprk-lazarus-sanctions-2023|Treasury Sinbad designation]], 2023); and (3) it confirms a follow-on prosecution of operators of an OFAC-sanctioned mixer, exemplifying the U.S. interagency model in which sanctions, indictment, and arrest are sequenced across [[us-treasury|Treasury]], [[fbi|FBI]], foreign police partners, and the [[office-of-international-affairs|DOJ Office of International Affairs]].
