## Source Summary

Eurojust press release dated 29 October 2024 announcing the 28 October 2024 takedown of the RedLine and META infostealers under the publicly-codenamed Operation Magnus. The release establishes Eurojust as the coordinating body and names six participating jurisdictions (Netherlands, US, Belgium, Portugal, UK, Australia) and their executing agencies. It quantifies operational results (3 servers seized in NL, 2 domains seized, 2 arrests in BE, charges unsealed in US, client database retrieved) and contextualizes scale (>1,200 servers across dozens of countries; "millions" of victims).

## IC Pattern

Eurojust-coordinated multilateral malware-as-a-service takedown with: (a) the Netherlands hosting both the infrastructure seizures (3 servers) and the lead investigative team (Limburg cybercrime unit), (b) Belgium executing the only physical arrests (2), (c) the United States providing the prosecutorial / indictment leg (Maxim Rudometov charges unsealed), (d) UK and Australia and Portugal in supporting investigative roles, and (e) a private-sector tip-off (unnamed security company) seeding the Dutch investigation. The structure mirrors the 2022 Raccoon Infostealer pattern (NL host + US prosecution) but expands to six jurisdictions and adds a Belgium-arrest leg, signalling continued maturation of the EU-coordinated MaaS-takedown template.
