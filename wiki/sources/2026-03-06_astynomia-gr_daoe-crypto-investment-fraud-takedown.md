## Why This Source Matters

This is the **controlling tier-1 primary source** for a Greek-led international cooperation operation against a Ponzi-structured cryptocurrency-investment fraud network. The release explicitly names the foreign cooperation channels and counterpart jurisdictions, satisfying the IC scope requirement (≥2 named foreign countries) with seven jurisdictions in total: Greece (lead) plus France, Germany, Malta (EIO recipients); United Kingdom, Ireland, Denmark (MLA recipients); and Bulgaria (locus of four linked offshore companies discovered in cooperation with the Greek anti-money-laundering authority).

Distinguishing features for the IC corpus:

- **Dual-track formal cooperation in one case**: simultaneous use of EU European Investigation Order (Directive 2014/41/EU) for three EU Member States and classical MLA letters of request for the UK, Ireland and Denmark — a deliberate mechanism choice driven by the post-Brexit UK position and Denmark's opt-out from the EIO Directive.
- **Crypto-financial-investigation collaboration model**: combines a national organised-crime field unit (DAOE Crete) with the Hellenic Capital Market Commission as the crypto-asset business supervisor and the Forensic Sciences Digital Forensic Sub-Directorate for on-scene wallet analysis.
- **Asset-tracing into Bulgaria via four offshore vehicles** documented in cooperation with the Greek FIU.
