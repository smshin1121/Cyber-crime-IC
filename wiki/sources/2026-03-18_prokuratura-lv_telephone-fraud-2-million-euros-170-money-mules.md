## Source Summary

Latvia Prosecutor General's Office (Latvijas Republikas prokuratūra) announcement dated 2026-03-18 reporting on a consolidated criminal investigation by the State Police (Valsts policija) Cybercrime Combating Department, 2nd Division, into a campaign of phone-fraud (vishing) against Latvian residents during 2023–2024. Fraudsters, posing as State Police officers and bank employees, induced victims to install AnyDesk remote-access software and log in to internet banking, defrauding Latvian residents of approximately EUR 2,000,000 across 35 consolidated criminal matters. The investigation identified an international organised crime group that had operated in Latvia since 2023, engaged in laundering criminally obtained funds across EU member states. The release records a multi-jurisdictional cooperation arc: the group leader was detained in Germany in 2024 in cooperation with Estonian law-enforcement and later convicted in Estonia; three group members were detained and sentenced in Latvia (3 years each); an illegal crypto-asset exchanger received more than six years; and on 2026-03-12 two further suspects who had fled to Ukraine were detained in Ivano-Frankivsk in cooperation with Ukrainian law-enforcement, Eurojust, and the Latvia Prosecutor General's Office. Latvian charges: Criminal Law Section 177 (3) [fraud, large scale, organised group], Section 195 (3) [money laundering, large scale], Section 193 (2) [illegal acquisition / use of payment instruments]. Asset arrest: EUR 829,650.

## Relevance to IC

Tier-1 primary source from the Latvian prosecution authority documenting a four-jurisdiction (Latvia, Estonia, Germany, Ukraine) cooperation pattern coordinated through Eurojust against an organised vishing-and-money-laundering group. Confirms: (a) the Estonia–Latvia–Germany 2024 detention/conviction sequence for the group leader; (b) the 2026-03-12 Ukraine arrest action (Ivano-Frankivsk) as a Eurojust-supported operation extending the case beyond the EU into a non-EU jurisdiction; (c) specific Latvian Criminal Law sections invoked. The release also independently corroborates the AnyDesk remote-access-software vector commonly seen in EU-wide vishing operations, and the use of Russian/Latvian-language-speaking call-centre operators. South Korea is not named in the release; the AnyDesk + impersonation-of-police vector is structurally similar to Korean voice-phishing typologies but the case has no direct Korean nexus.
