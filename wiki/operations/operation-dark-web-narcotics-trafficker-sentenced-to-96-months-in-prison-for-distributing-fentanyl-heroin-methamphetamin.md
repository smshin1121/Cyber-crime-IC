## Summary

This page captures the defendant-specific enforcement sequence against Joanna De Alba, also known as `RaptureReloaded`, for selling narcotics over the Wall Street Market dark-web marketplace in exchange for cryptocurrency. The public record shows a progression from indictment in January 2020 to guilty plea in April 2021 and sentencing in November 2021.

## Background

According to DOJ and DEA reporting, De Alba sold heroin, fentanyl disguised as oxycodone, methamphetamine, MDMA, cocaine, oxycodone, and methadone over Wall Street Market. The cited releases state that she accepted Bitcoin and Monero, used fake business names and shipping addresses, and relied on the identity of her deceased husband to receive parcels and fund operational logistics.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

This was a domestic U.S. prosecution, but the cited charging record has a cross-border evidence dimension. Investigators publicly linked intercepted drug parcels to the Netherlands and Canada, while the defendant was identified as being from Tijuana, Mexico. That makes the case relevant to the repository's international-cooperation lens even without a public extradition stage.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- The public releases state that De Alba pleaded guilty in April 2021, but the repository does not yet hold the underlying plea filing.
