## Summary

On 2025-08-27, the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) issued press release SB-0230 announcing sanctions on four targets — one Russian national (Vitaliy Sergeyevich Andreyev), one Russia-based DPRK consular official (Kim Ung Sun), one Chinese front company (Shenyang Geumpungri Network Technology Co., Ltd.), and one DPRK trading company (Korea Sinjin Trading Corporation) — for facilitating revenue generation for [[north-korea|the DPRK]] through the Chinyong Information Technology Cooperation Company IT worker network. All four designations were made pursuant to Executive Order 13687. The action documented approximately USD 600,000 in crypto-to-USD conversions since at least December 2024 and over USD 1 million in cumulative profits since 2021. The press release likely (>80% confidence) represents a coordinated diplomatic moment: it was issued the same day as a joint statement on DPRK IT worker threats by the U.S. Department of State and the foreign ministries of Japan and the Republic of Korea.

## Background

The DPRK government employs overseas IT worker delegations to generate hundreds of millions of dollars per year for its weapons of mass destruction and ballistic missile programs, in violation of U.S. and United Nations sanctions. Chinyong Information Technology Cooperation Company is the DPRK defense-ministry-affiliated entity that employs IT worker delegations deployed to Russia and Laos. SB-0230 explicitly expands the prior Chinyong designation, targeting additional entities in its network and "combatting its use of cryptocurrency for sanctions evasion." It is the third OFAC action in this enforcement chain in roughly two months — building on July 8 and July 24, 2025 OFAC sanctions — and is highly likely (>80% confidence) part of the same broad whole-of-government posture that produced the [[us-doj-coordinated-nationwide-actions-dprk-it-workers-2025|June 30, 2025 DOJ RevGen Initiative wave]] and was followed by the [[treasury-sanctions-dprk-bankers-laundering-cybercrime-2025|November 4, 2025 OFAC SB-0302 designations]] of DPRK bankers laundering cybercrime and IT worker proceeds.

> [!note] Participation classification
> The release lists Russia, China, and Laos as host or operating jurisdictions for designees, not as cooperating partners. They are highly unlikely (<20% confidence) to have provided any active cooperation in this designation — the action is unilateral OFAC enforcement targeting persons operating from those territories.

## Legal Framework

- **E.O. 13687 (January 2, 2015)** — Imposing Additional Sanctions With Respect To North Korea — is the sole executive order cited as authority for all four designations.
  - Andreyev: material support to Chinyong (a person already blocked under E.O. 13687)
  - Kim Ung Sun: acting for or on behalf of the Government of North Korea
  - Shenyang Geumpungri: owned/controlled by, or acting for, Chinyong
  - Korea Sinjin Trading Corporation: acting for or on behalf of the Government of North Korea

> [!note] Legal-framework deviation from related actions
> Unlike the [[treasury-sanctions-dprk-bankers-laundering-cybercrime-2025|November 4, 2025 OFAC SB-0302 action]], which cited E.O. 13694 (cyber-enabled activities), E.O. 13551, E.O. 13722, and E.O. 13810 in addition to NK-specific authorities, SB-0230 cites only E.O. 13687. This is consistent (highly likely, >80% confidence) with framing the conduct as DPRK revenue generation / sanctions evasion rather than as cyber-enabled malicious activity per se, even though the underlying IT worker delegations include data extortion and malware insertion as documented in the related FBI January 23, 2025 PSA cited in the release body.

## Results and Impact

- **4 SDN List designations** under E.O. 13687.
- **USD 600,000+** documented in crypto-to-USD conversions on the Andreyev–Kim Ung Sun channel since at least December 2024.
- **USD 1,000,000+** in cumulative profits documented through Shenyang Geumpungri's DPRK IT worker delegation since 2021.
- **Network mapping**: action publicly maps the four-actor structure feeding Chinyong (Russian-national facilitator + Russia-based DPRK consular official + Chinese front company + DPRK trading company subordinate to the DPRK Ministry of People's Armed Forces General Political Bureau) — likely (>55% confidence) the most explicit single-document mapping of this quadrilateral pattern in the 2025 enforcement record.
- **Secondary-sanctions exposure**: foreign financial institutions facilitating significant transactions on behalf of the designees risk loss of correspondent / payable-through accounts in the United States.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **[[asset-freezing]]** — primary mechanism: blocking of all property and interests in property of the four designees in the United States or in U.S. persons' possession; secondary 50%-rule blocking of any entities they own.
- **[[informal-cooperation]]** — same-day joint statement by the U.S. Department of State and the foreign ministries of Japan and the Republic of Korea on DPRK IT worker threats; characterized in the release as part of the U.S. "whole-of-government effort to counter the DPRK's wide-ranging revenue generation schemes, in close coordination with our allies and partners." This is highly likely (>80% confidence) a diplomatic-political coordination channel rather than a formal MLAT or Budapest Convention pathway.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- **Host-jurisdiction enforcement gap**: Russia, China, and Laos are named as operating jurisdictions but are highly unlikely (<20% confidence) to enforce the OFAC designations domestically. Asset freezes are confined to U.S. persons / U.S. jurisdiction.
- **Cryptocurrency cash-out resilience**: the Andreyev–Kim Ung Sun channel demonstrates that cryptocurrency-to-fiat conversion through individual non-DPRK nationals can sustain hundreds of thousands of dollars in flow before designation; secondary-sanctions deterrence is the primary downstream lever.
- **Single-EO scoping**: reliance on E.O. 13687 alone (rather than the cyber EO 13694) limits the cyber-enabled-activity framing despite documented data extortion and malware insertion in the underlying conduct.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Quadrilateral structural pattern**: a Russian national + Russia-based DPRK consular official + Chinese front company + DPRK trading company subordinate to the DPRK Ministry of People's Armed Forces General Political Bureau is a discrete, IC-relevant structural finding that is highly likely (>80% confidence) to recur in future DPRK IT worker enforcement.
2. **Crypto-to-fiat chokepoint**: the cash-out conversion service is a designable role even when performed by a single non-DPRK national, and yields a unilateral US enforcement action without requiring host-country cooperation.
3. **Coordinated diplomatic messaging**: same-day US–Japan–ROK joint statements signal a consolidating tri-national diplomatic track for DPRK IT worker enforcement, even where formal sanctions remain unilateral US designations.

## Follow-Up Actions

- The [[treasury-sanctions-dprk-bankers-laundering-cybercrime-2025|November 4, 2025 OFAC SB-0302 action]] (eight individuals + two entities; DPRK bankers and Ryujong Credit Bank / KMCTC) extended the same enforcement chain into the financial-institution layer above the IT worker monetization layer addressed by SB-0230.
- Continued whole-of-government coordination with [[us-doj-coordinated-nationwide-actions-dprk-it-workers-2025|DOJ RevGen Initiative]] enforcement waves.

## Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

- The Republic of Korea (한국) is named in the release as a co-issuer, with the United States (Department of State) and Japan, of a same-day joint statement on the threats posed by DPRK IT workers. This is highly likely (>80% confidence) the most concrete IC element on the Korean side: diplomatic/political alignment with the OFAC action via tri-national messaging rather than legal cooperation.
- ROK is not named as a host jurisdiction for any of the four designees and is not the source of any documented evidence underlying the designations.
- For the underlying IC pattern this confirms: ROK Foreign Ministry coordination on DPRK IT worker enforcement is operating on the diplomatic-political track parallel to (rather than through) the OFAC sanctions designation track. Further verification on the formal Korean cooperation page should reference the State Department / MOFA / MOFA-Japan tri-lateral statement text directly when it becomes available in primary form.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- The release does not state a specific dollar amount of cryptocurrency or fiat seized as a result of the designation; only the prior transaction volumes ($600K crypto-to-USD; $1M cumulative profits) are documented.
- Whether the December 2024 start date for Andreyev–Kim Ung Sun activity reflects the actual onset or merely the earliest documented transfer is not specified.
- Whether China, Russia, or Laos provided any cooperation (as opposed to merely hosting designees) is unspecified; based on host-state DPRK posture this is highly unlikely (<20% confidence).
- E.O. 13694 (cyber) is not invoked despite documented data extortion / malware insertion in the underlying IT worker conduct; the choice of E.O. 13687 alone may reflect prosecutorial / diplomatic framing preferences rather than evidentiary limits.
