## Summary

In June 2021, law enforcement from **9 countries** coordinated the seizure of **DoubleVPN**, a VPN service widely marketed on Russian cybercrime forums as a tool for concealing criminal activity. The operation was led by the [[netherlands-politie|Dutch National Police]] and coordinated by [[europol-ec3|Europol]] and [[eurojust|Eurojust]].

DoubleVPN offered multiple layers of VPN encryption, which made it particularly attractive to cybercriminals seeking to hide their digital footprints during ransomware attacks, phishing campaigns, and other cybercrimes.

## Background

DoubleVPN was a VPN service that provided double, triple, and quadruple VPN connections, routing traffic through multiple servers in different countries to make tracing extremely difficult. The service was heavily advertised on Russian-language and English-language cybercrime forums as a tool specifically designed for concealing criminal activity from law enforcement.
