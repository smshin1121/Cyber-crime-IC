## Summary

On 2025-11-04 [[eurojust|Eurojust]] announced a synchronised 5-country operation that **arrested 9 suspects** at their homes in **Cyprus, Spain, and Germany** on **27 and 29 October 2025** for their involvement in a cryptocurrency money-laundering network that scammed victims out of approximately **EUR 600 million**. The network operated **dozens of fake cryptocurrency investment platforms**, recruiting victims via social media advertising, cold calling, fake news articles, and fake celebrity testimonials, then laundering the proceeds through blockchain. Eurojust set up a **France-Belgium Joint Investigation Team** and brought prosecutors and investigative judges from France, Belgium, Cyprus, Spain, and Germany together at **Eurojust's premises in The Hague** to plan the takedown.

## Background

Investigations into the network started when authorities received several complaints from victims. The network's recruitment toolkit included social media advertising, cold calling, fake news articles, and fake celebrity / successful-investor testimonials. When victims would transfer cryptocurrency to the platforms, they were never able to recover their money. The crypto assets earned through the various scams were laundered using blockchain technology, with the network reportedly able to launder approximately EUR 600 million.

## Results and Impact

- **9 arrests** at homes in Cyprus, Spain, and Germany.
- **EUR 800,000** frozen in suspects' bank accounts.
- **EUR 415,000** frozen in cryptocurrency accounts.
- **EUR 300,000** in cash seized.
- Approximately **EUR 600 million** in laundered proceeds from the underlying victim cohort.
- Dozens of fake cryptocurrency investment platforms taken down (specific platform names not enumerated in cited release).

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The cited release describes a **France-Belgium Joint Investigation Team (JIT)** established at Eurojust as the foundational instrument, extended through Eurojust coordination meetings to Cyprus, Germany, and Spain for the synchronised action days. Action-day arrest, search, and asset-freezing warrants were executed under the domestic authority of the arrest jurisdictions (Cyprus, Spain, Germany). Eurojust premises in The Hague served as the coordination centre for the synchronised action days.
