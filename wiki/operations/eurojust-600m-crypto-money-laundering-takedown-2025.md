## Summary

On 2025-11-04 [[eurojust|Eurojust]] announced a synchronised 5-country operation that **arrested 9 suspects** at their homes in **Cyprus, Spain, and Germany** on **27 and 29 October 2025** for their involvement in a cryptocurrency money-laundering network that scammed victims out of approximately **EUR 600 million**. The network operated **dozens of fake cryptocurrency investment platforms**, recruiting victims via social media advertising, cold calling, fake news articles, and fake celebrity testimonials, then laundering the proceeds through blockchain. Eurojust set up a **France-Belgium Joint Investigation Team** and brought prosecutors and investigative judges from France, Belgium, Cyprus, Spain, and Germany together at **Eurojust's premises in The Hague** to plan the takedown.

## Background

Investigations into the network started when authorities received several complaints from victims. The network's recruitment toolkit included social media advertising, cold calling, fake news articles, and fake celebrity / successful-investor testimonials. When victims would transfer cryptocurrency to the platforms, they were never able to recover their money. The crypto assets earned through the various scams were laundered using blockchain technology, with the network reportedly able to launder approximately EUR 600 million.

## Results and Impact

### Phase 1 (2025-10-27)
- **9 arrests** at homes in Cyprus, Spain, and Germany.
- **EUR 800,000** seized in suspects' bank accounts.
- **EUR 415,000** seized in cryptocurrency accounts.
- **EUR 300,000** in cash seized.
- **Digital devices** and **high-value watches** seized.

### Phase 2 (2025-11-25 to 2025-11-26)
- Searches and additional operational measures across Belgium, Bulgaria, Germany, and Israel.
- Targets: companies that had previously offered affiliate marketing services for the OCG's deepfake-celebrity / media-outlet / politician impersonation ad campaigns.

### Cumulative
- **>EUR 700 million** laundered through cryptocurrency exchanges (per Europol 2025-11-26 release; Eurojust 2025-11-04 release stated approximately EUR 600 million as the Phase 1-time figure).
- **Thousands of victims** worldwide.
- **Numerous fake cryptocurrency investment platforms** disrupted (specific platform names not enumerated in either release).

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

### Phase 1
The Eurojust 2025-11-04 release describes a **France-Belgium Joint Investigation Team (JIT)** established at Eurojust as the foundational instrument, extended through Eurojust coordination meetings to Cyprus, Germany, and Spain for the synchronised action days. Action-day arrest, search, and asset-freezing warrants were executed under the domestic authority of the arrest jurisdictions (Cyprus, Spain, Germany). Eurojust premises in The Hague served as the coordination centre for the synchronised action days.

### Phase 2 (Europol-coordinated)
The Europol 2025-11-26 release describes Europol's central coordination role for both phases, with specific contributions including:
- Operational meetings involving law enforcement agencies from all participating countries to coordinate operational strategy and intelligence.
- Logistical support for the action days, including operational arrangements and resources.
- Deployment of dedicated case specialists and analysts to cross-check data, develop intelligence, and ensure key insights were shared among partners.
- Deployment of a Europol cryptocurrency specialist to support the identification and seizure of illicit cryptocurrency.
- Operational and crypto-analysis intelligence products on the movement of illicit funds and the structure of the fraud network.

The Phase 2 action targeted **affiliate marketing infrastructure** — companies behind fraudulent advertising campaigns on social media platforms using **deepfake video impersonations** of renowned media outlets, celebrities, and politicians. The data of potential investors obtained through manipulated advertising is described as crucial to the functioning of the crypto-scam industry as a whole.
