## Summary

On 2026-02-05–06, the [[knpa|Korean National Police Agency]] hosted in Seoul the **2nd International Cooperation Operations Meeting** (제2차 국제공조 작전회의), held under the operation codename **Breaking Chains (브레이킹 체인스)**. The release describes Breaking Chains as a Korea-led ("韓 주도") multilateral operational platform against transnational scam crime — including scam compounds (스캠 센터), human trafficking (인신매매, *insinmaemae*), and online fraud (온라인 사기). The 2nd meeting drew 22 country-level law-enforcement agencies plus five international organisations: [[interpol|INTERPOL]], [[aseanapol|ASEANAPOL]], [[afripol|AFRIPOL]], the International Organization for Migration (IOM), and [[unodc|UNODC]].

The release also reports cumulative outcomes from the 1st meeting cycle (held 2025-11) and the Phase-1 field operation conducted 2025-12-04, demonstrating the meeting-to-operation conversion of the platform.

## Background

The Breaking Chains forum was set up by KNPA as a Korea-hosted standing multilateral cooperation forum with a recurring meeting cadence — 1st meeting in 2025-11 (Seoul); 2nd meeting on 2026-02-05–06 (Seoul) — interleaved with named field operations (Phase-1, 2025-12-04, Cambodia + Thailand). The operation codename "Breaking Chains" is explicitly framed in the press release as the policy intent to *break* the criminal *chains* binding scam-compound, human-trafficking, and online-fraud victims.

The forum is institutionally connected to a wider Korean whole-of-government scam-compound architecture:
- **대통령실 「초국가범죄 특별대응 전담팀(TF)」** (Presidential Office Transnational Crime Special Response Task Force) — named in the body of the release as the policy structure that combined with the 1st-meeting intelligence-sharing to convert leads into arrests.
- **「코리아전담반」** (Korea-dedicated Squad) — Korean police field unit deployed inside Cambodia, named here for the Phase-1 Cambodia 15-arrest tranche, and previously named in the wiki as the field unit for the [[korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026|Korea–Cambodia–Philippines 73-person extradition]].

The Breaking Chains forum overlaps with — but is not identical to — the broader **국제공조협의체 (IICA, International Cooperation Consultative Body)** that the KNPA proposed in 2025-10 as a 46-country / 3-international-organisation initiative; subsequent Korean reporting (2026-03 헤럴드경제) frames Breaking Chains as the operational arm of IICA, but the 2026-02-05 release itself does not explicitly use the IICA acronym.

## Legal Framework

The Breaking Chains forum is **not** a treaty instrument; it is an operational cooperation construct led by a national police agency. The legal foundations exposed in the release are:

- **Korean domestic criminal process** — for the extradition-into-Korea flows in Phase-1 outcomes (15 repatriations).
- **Bilateral law-enforcement instruments** in force during the operation period:
  - [[korea-cambodia-joint-task-force-mou-2025|Korea–Cambodia Joint Task Force MOU (2025)]] — operationally consonant with the 코리아전담반 deployment.
  - [[korea-china-anti-scam-mou-2025|Korea–China Anti-Scam MOU (2025)]] — operationally consonant with the China-track joint enforcement reported in Phase-1 follow-on.
  - [[korea-philippines-police-mou-2026-revision|Korea–Philippines Police MOU 2026 revision]] — though Philippines is not separately named in the 2026-02-05 release roster, this is the wiki's referenced bilateral frame for the broader Korea–Southeast Asia scam-compound enforcement axis.
- **Informal multilateral cooperation** ([[informal-cooperation]]) for the meeting body and tracking-lead exchange.
- **Existing institutional channels** ([[interpol-i24-7]], [[interpol-asean-desk]]) for the INTERPOL/ASEANAPOL information-sharing layer.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The 2026-02-05 release does not name a specific MLAT instrument; the cooperation is described as "韓 주도" (Korea-led) multilateral operational coordination. The mechanism mix is:

- **Korea-hosted multilateral forum** ("국제공조 작전회의" / Operation Breaking Chains) — a non-treaty operational construct.
- **Bilateral law-enforcement cooperation** between [[knpa|KNPA]] and the Cambodian, Thai, Vietnamese, and Chinese national police authorities for the joint-enforcement layer.
- **International-organisation channels** — [[interpol|INTERPOL]] (incl. [[interpol-i24-7|I-24/7]] / [[interpol-asean-desk|ASEAN Desk]]), [[aseanapol|ASEANAPOL]], [[afripol|AFRIPOL]], IOM, and [[unodc|UNODC]] — for information sharing and intelligence-product exchange.
- **Korea-side operational integration** through the 대통령실 '초국가범죄 특별대응 TF' (Presidential Office TF), bridging KNPA, MOFA, MOJ, and NIS — same TF construct named in [[korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026]].
- **Field-deployed Korea liaison/operational unit** 「코리아전담반」 (Korea-dedicated Squad) embedded in Cambodian operations.
- **Extradition channels** ([[extradition]]) for the 15-person repatriation tranche from Vietnam/China-track cases.

## Challenges and Friction Points

The press release is unusually explicit on one structural challenge:

- **Balloon-effect (풍선효과, *pungseon hyogwa*)** — international cooperation strengthening in one corridor pushes scam organisations to relocate to weaker-enforcement jurisdictions or to re-shape (조직을 재편). The 2nd-meeting agenda explicitly includes a discussion track on countering this displacement effect. This is a primary-source-confirmed structural challenge for transnational scam enforcement and aligns with the wiki's broader challenges frame on cooperation gaps.

The release does not detail formal frictions (e.g., MLA delays, dual-criminality issues), consistent with its character as a Korea-led informal-cooperation forum rather than a treaty mechanism.

## Lessons Learned

1. The Breaking Chains forum is the wiki's first recorded **Korea-as-coordinating-host** standing multilateral cybercrime cooperation forum with a named operation codename and a recurring meeting cadence. Korea here is not the requesting state (contrast with [[korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026]]) but the convener of multi-jurisdiction operational coordination.
2. The 1st-meeting → Phase-1 conversion (2025-11 → 2025-12-04, ~1 month) is a measurable cooperation-throughput data point: 26 cases / 75 leads → 28 in-jurisdiction arrests across two organisations + ~31 follow-on arrests + 15 repatriations.
3. The five-international-organisation participation set (INTERPOL + ASEANAPOL + AFRIPOL + IOM + UNODC) materially expands Korea's institutional cooperation surface beyond the Asia-Pacific frame, especially via AFRIPOL (African continental policing) and IOM (migration and trafficking-victim protection).
4. The explicit balloon-effect (풍선효과) policy-track inclusion is rare in primary press releases and supplies direct evidence for the wiki's challenges layer.

## Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

This operation is Korea-led: the **convening state, the operational codename owner, and the lead agency** are all the [[knpa|Korean National Police Agency]]. The forum is hosted in Seoul; the operational platform is named in Korean ("국제공조 작전회의") with an English codename ("Breaking Chains"); and Korean-side organisational and policy structures are explicitly named:

- **박성주 경찰청 국가수사본부장** (Park Seong-ju, KNPA National Investigation Headquarters Commissioner) — public spokesperson for the 2nd meeting. Quoted: "초국가 스캠범죄는 국경을 넘나들며 연결된 범죄인 만큼 대응 역시 국경을 넘어 연결돼야 한다" ("Transnational scam crime is connected across borders, so the response must equally be connected across borders").
- **이재영 경찰청 국제협력관** (Lee Jae-young, KNPA International Cooperation Officer) — public spokesperson for the Phase-1 takedown announcement (2025-12-05 이데일리). Quoted: "글로벌 국제공조 작전을 바탕으로 ... 코리아전담반 등 경찰 파견 인력과 해외 법집행기관과의 긴밀한 협력을 통해 온라인 스캠·보이스피싱과 같은 국제 조직 범죄 척결에 앞장서겠다."
- **충남경찰청 형사기동대** (Chungnam Provincial Police Mobile Investigation Unit) — supplied core intelligence for the Cambodia 15-arrest tranche.
- **서울경찰청 금융범죄수사대** (Seoul Metropolitan Police Financial Crime Investigation Unit) — supplied tracking infrastructure for the Thailand 13-arrest tranche.
- **대통령실 「초국가범죄 특별대응 전담팀(TF)」** (Presidential Office Transnational Crime Special Response TF) — Korean policy structure named as the integrating body for cross-ministry response.
- **「코리아전담반」** (Korea-dedicated Squad) — Korean police field unit deployed inside Cambodia.

Korea's victim-side harms surface in the Phase-1 cohort (27 Cambodia-track victims, ~₩2.589 B losses; ~₩100 M Thailand-track losses) and in the Korean-national victim murder case in Cambodia underlying the Cambodia–Thailand trafficking ring leader arrest. The Korean policy framing throughout the release — including the operation codename meaning ("스캠 센터와 인신매매, 온라인 사기 등 초국가적 범죄 피해자를 범죄 사슬로부터 해방하겠다는 의지") — explicitly emphasises victim release alongside arrest.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- The 2nd-meeting roster size is given as "22 country law-enforcement agencies" but only 4 countries (US, China, Japan, Cambodia) are verbatim-named in the meeting paragraph; Thailand and Vietnam are named only in Phase-1 outcome paragraphs. The frontmatter `participating_countries` list reflects the verbatim-named intersection only (per LESSONS L17). The remaining 16 country names are not enumerated in the cited primary source.
- The "16 countries" figure given in the Phase-1 outcome reporting (이데일리 2025-12-05) and the "22 countries" figure given for the 2nd meeting (KNPA 2026-02-05) are different counts taken at different points in the operation cycle. Both are tier-1-sourced; the discrepancy is a roster expansion (16 → 22) over time, not a contradiction.
- The relationship between the **Breaking Chains** operational forum and the broader **국제공조협의체 (IICA, 46개국 + 3 IOs)** is not clarified in the 2026-02-05 release itself; the IICA naming surfaces only in 2026-03 secondary reporting (헤럴드경제). Whether IICA is the formal umbrella platform and Breaking Chains its operational arm — or whether the two are co-equal labels — is an open question for future ingest.
- Phase-1 organisation-leader identities are not named in the cited release.
- Post-2nd-meeting operational outcomes (2026-02 onward) are not within the scope of the 2026-02-05 primary source; subsequent KNPA disclosures should be ingested separately and back-linked.
