## Summary

On 2026-03-12, the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) issued press release SB-0416 announcing sanctions on six individuals and two entities for their roles in DPRK government-orchestrated IT worker schemes that systematically defraud U.S. businesses. The action targets facilitators based in [[north-korea|the DPRK]], [[vietnam|Vietnam]], [[laos|Laos]], and [[spain|Spain]]. Six designations were made pursuant to Executive Order (E.O.) 13810 and two pursuant to E.O. 13382 (Do Phi Khanh and Hoang Van Nguyen, both for support to U.S.-sanctioned DPRK nuclear procurement facilitator Kim Se Un). The release attributes nearly USD 800 million in DPRK IT worker revenue in 2024 to the broader scheme — the highest single-year scale figure in OFAC's public enforcement record on DPRK IT workers to date. The action introduces the **Amnokgang → Quangvietdnbg / Vietnam-based front-company** structure (Nguyen Quang Viet's company converted approximately USD 2.5 million into cryptocurrency for North Koreans between mid-2023 and mid-2025) as a discrete, IC-relevant facilitation pattern.

## Background

The DPRK government employs overseas IT worker delegations to generate revenue for its weapons of mass destruction (WMD) and ballistic missile programs, in violation of U.S. and United Nations sanctions. Per the SB-0416 release, "the DPRK government reportedly appropriates the majority of the wages earned by these overseas IT workers, generating hundreds of millions of dollars to support the regime's WMD and ballistic missile programs … including nearly $800 million in 2024." DPRK-affiliated workers commonly use fraudulent documentation, stolen identities, and fabricated personas to obtain employment with U.S. and allied-country businesses, and in certain instances "have also covertly introduced malware into company networks to extract proprietary and sensitive information."

SB-0416 builds on a chain of prior OFAC actions in this enforcement track, most directly:

- The August 27, 2025 OFAC SB-0230 action ([[treasury-sanctions-dprk-it-worker-network-andreyev-chinyong-2025|Andreyev / Chinyong network]]) — Russian-national facilitator + Russia-based DPRK consular official + Chinese front company + DPRK trading company.
- The November 4, 2025 OFAC SB-0302 action ([[treasury-sanctions-dprk-bankers-laundering-cybercrime-2025|DPRK bankers / Ryujong Credit Bank / KMCTC]]) — financial-institution layer above the IT worker monetization layer.
- The June 30, 2025 DOJ RevGen Initiative actions ([[us-doj-coordinated-nationwide-actions-dprk-it-workers-2025]]) — domestic U.S. criminal track on DPRK IT worker fraud.

The release is highly likely (>80% confidence) part of the same continuing whole-of-government posture, and is described in the body as part of "the United States' whole-of-government effort to counter the DPRK's wide-ranging revenue generation schemes."

## Legal Framework

Two executive orders are invoked across the eight designations:

- **E.O. 13810 (September 21, 2017)** — *Imposing Additional Sanctions With Respect To North Korea* — cited for six designees (Amnokgang; Nguyen Quang Viet; Quangvietdnbg; Yun Song Guk; Hoang Minh Quang; York Louis Celestino Herrera).
  - Amnokgang: operating in the IT industry in North Korea.
  - Nguyen Quang Viet: material support to Amnokgang.
  - Quangvietdnbg: owned/controlled by, or acting for, Nguyen.
  - Yun Song Guk: North Korean person engaged in commercial activity that generates revenue for the Government of North Korea or the Workers' Party of Korea.
  - Hoang Minh Quang and York Louis Celestino Herrera: material support to Yun.
- **E.O. 13382 (June 28, 2005)** — *Blocking Property of Weapons of Mass Destruction Proliferators and Their Supporters* — cited for two designees (Do Phi Khanh; Hoang Van Nguyen).
  - Both: providing or attempting to provide financial, material, technological or other support for, or goods or services in support of, U.S.-sanctioned DPRK nuclear procurement facilitator Kim Se Un.

> [!note] Two-EO framing
> The split EO usage signals (highly likely, >80% confidence) that OFAC differentiated facilitators by primary nexus: Amnokgang / Yun-network designees are framed as IT-industry / DPRK-revenue support (E.O. 13810), while Kim Se Un's two proxies are framed as WMD-proliferation support (E.O. 13382). This differs from the August 2025 SB-0230 action ([[treasury-sanctions-dprk-it-worker-network-andreyev-chinyong-2025]]) which used only E.O. 13687, and from the November 2025 SB-0302 action ([[treasury-sanctions-dprk-bankers-laundering-cybercrime-2025]]) which used a multi-EO basket including the cyber EO 13694.

## Results and Impact

- **8 SDN List designations** under E.O. 13810 and E.O. 13382.
- **Approximately 21 cryptocurrency addresses** across multiple blockchains reported as designated alongside the named persons (figure reported by external trackers; not enumerated in the press release body).
- **USD 2.5 million** in cryptocurrency conversions by Nguyen Quang Viet / Quangvietdnbg for North Koreans, mid-2023 to mid-2025.
- **USD 70,000+** in dozens of transactions between Yun Song Guk and Hoang Minh Quang for Laos-based IT services.
- **USD 200,000+** in the 2022 counterfeit-cigarette deal facilitated by Hoang Van Nguyen on Kim Se Un's behalf.
- **USD 800 million scale figure published**: nearly USD 800 million in DPRK IT worker revenue in 2024 attributed to the broader scheme. This is the highest single-year DPRK-IT-worker revenue figure in OFAC's public enforcement record to date and represents a substantial upward revision from prior "hundreds of millions" framings.
- **Geographic expansion of named jurisdictions**: this action introduces Vietnam and Spain as named jurisdictional nexuses in the DPRK IT worker enforcement chain, complementing the prior Russia / China / Laos triangle (DPRK, Vietnam, Laos, Spain in this action).
- **First Vietnamese-national / Vietnam-based-entity designation** in the DPRK IT worker monetization chain in OFAC's public record.
- **Secondary-sanctions exposure**: foreign financial institutions facilitating significant transactions on behalf of the designees risk loss of correspondent / payable-through accounts in the United States.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **[[asset-freezing]]** — primary mechanism: blocking of all property and interests in property of the eight designees in the United States or in U.S. persons' possession; secondary 50%-rule blocking of any entities they own.
- **[[informal-cooperation]]** — the release frames the action as part of the U.S. "whole-of-government effort to counter the DPRK's wide-ranging revenue generation schemes" and states that "in collaboration with our allies and partners, the United States will continue to counter DPRK's IT worker schemes." This is highly likely (>80% confidence) a diplomatic-political coordination posture rather than a formal MLAT or Budapest Convention pathway. Unlike the SB-0230 action, no same-day allied joint statement is named in the SB-0416 body.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- **Host-jurisdiction enforcement gap**: Vietnam, Laos, and Spain are named as operating jurisdictions but the release does not state any of them have parallel domestic enforcement underway. Vietnam in particular hosts an incorporated company (Quangvietdnbg) — domestic Vietnamese enforcement on a Vietnam-incorporated entity would be the most actionable downstream cooperation lever and is unlikely (20-45% confidence) to materialize without bilateral engagement.
- **Vietnamese-national front-company structure as a sanctions-evasion innovation**: prior named DPRK IT worker monetization structures used non-Vietnamese-national facilitators (Russian national Andreyev in SB-0230; Chinese front company Shenyang Geumpungri). The Quangvietdnbg pattern shows DPRK monetization migrating into Southeast-Asian-national-led corporate vehicles, which is highly likely (>80% confidence) harder to designate in advance and harder to deter via secondary sanctions where Vietnamese banks have limited U.S. correspondent exposure.
- **Cryptocurrency address scope ambiguity**: the press release body does not enumerate the approximately 21 cryptocurrency addresses; analytical use must rely on the SDN List entries directly.
- **Nationality ambiguity for two designees**: the release does not explicitly state the nationality of Do Phi Khanh and Hoang Minh Quang. Names follow Vietnamese naming conventions, suggesting Vietnamese nationality, but this is an inference (likely, 55-80% confidence) rather than a release-stated fact.
- **Two-EO framing**: invoking E.O. 13382 (WMD proliferation) for Do Phi Khanh and Hoang Van Nguyen alongside E.O. 13810 (DPRK) for the rest highlights that WMD-proliferation framing depends on the upstream Kim Se Un nexus rather than the IT worker fraud conduct itself, complicating a unified narrative.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Vietnam-based, Vietnamese-national-led front company is a discrete IC-relevant pattern**: Amnokgang (DPRK IT delegation manager) → Nguyen Quang Viet (Vietnamese national CEO) → Quangvietdnbg (Vietnam-incorporated currency-conversion vehicle) → North Korean recipients, with USD 2.5 million in cryptocurrency conversions across two years. This expands the public enforcement record beyond the previously documented Russia-based and China-based front-company structures and is highly likely (>80% confidence) to recur in future actions.
2. **DPRK IT worker monetization geographic footprint is broader than the Russia / China / Laos triangle**: the inclusion of Vietnam and Spain in this single action signals a diversified host-jurisdiction footprint that complicates enforcement triage and is highly likely (>80% confidence) to drive future capacity-building and bilateral engagement priorities.
3. **USD 800M / 2024 scale figure**: Treasury's authoritative scale figure for the IT worker revenue stream is now nearly USD 800 million for a single year, a substantial upward revision from prior "hundreds of millions" framings. This figure is likely (55-80% confidence) to anchor downstream policy work.
4. **Differential EO framing within a single action**: combining E.O. 13810 (DPRK / IT industry) with E.O. 13382 (WMD proliferators) in one designation set is a deliberate framing choice that ties IT worker monetization to upstream WMD procurement networks via Kim Se Un — useful for cross-network targeting but complicates clean attribution of the IT worker fraud nexus alone.

## Follow-Up Actions

- The release announces no specific follow-up; the closing language commits to continued counter-DPRK-IT-worker action "in collaboration with our allies and partners."
- The action extends the chain of [[treasury-sanctions-dprk-it-worker-network-andreyev-chinyong-2025|August 2025 SB-0230]] and [[treasury-sanctions-dprk-bankers-laundering-cybercrime-2025|November 2025 SB-0302]] enforcement, and parallels the [[us-doj-coordinated-nationwide-actions-dprk-it-workers-2025|June 2025 DOJ RevGen Initiative]] criminal track.

## Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

- The Republic of Korea (한국) is **not named** in the SB-0416 release as a co-issuer, host jurisdiction, or evidentiary source. This contrasts with the prior [[treasury-sanctions-dprk-it-worker-network-andreyev-chinyong-2025|SB-0230 action]] which was accompanied by a same-day U.S.–Japan–ROK joint statement on DPRK IT worker threats.
- The absence of named ROK involvement in SB-0416 does not, on the available source alone, imply a downgrading of the U.S.–Japan–ROK diplomatic track on DPRK IT workers. It is roughly evenly balanced (45-55% confidence) between two readings: (a) ROK was simply not part of this particular designation's coordination cycle, or (b) the U.S.–Japan–ROK trilateral track has shifted to address the financial-institution layer and the IT worker monetization layer separately.
- Korean-language media coverage (where available) and ROK Foreign Ministry / NIS statements should be cross-checked when more sources on this action are ingested.
- For the underlying IC pattern this action exposes: the Vietnamese-national-led, Vietnam-based front-company structure is highly relevant to ROK enforcement priorities — DPRK IT workers are documented to target ROK businesses as well as U.S. businesses, and the Quangvietdnbg pattern likely (55-80% confidence) extends to ROK-victim revenue chains even though only U.S. business victimization is specified in this release.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- The release does not state a specific dollar amount of cryptocurrency or fiat seized as a result of the designation; only the prior transaction volumes (USD 2.5M; USD 70K+; USD 200K+) are documented as historical conduct.
- The "approximately 21 cryptocurrency addresses across multiple blockchains" figure is reported by external trackers and is not enumerated in the press release body; the SDN List entries are the primary source for this count.
- Nationality of Do Phi Khanh, Hoang Minh Quang, and (to a lesser extent) Hoang Van Nguyen is not explicitly stated in the release; Vietnamese naming conventions suggest Vietnamese nationality but this is inferential.
- Whether Vietnam, Laos, or Spain provided any cooperation (as opposed to merely hosting designees) is unspecified; based on host-state DPRK posture and the absence of any non-U.S. agency named in the release, this is highly unlikely (<20% confidence).
- Whether E.O. 13694 (cyber-enabled activities) was considered and rejected for the IT-worker designations is unspecified; the choice of E.O. 13810 alone for IT-worker-network targets may reflect prosecutorial / diplomatic framing preferences rather than evidentiary limits, paralleling the SB-0230 framing choice.
- The release attributes "nearly $800 million in 2024" to DPRK IT worker schemes broadly but does not break this figure down by country, by Amnokgang vs. Chinyong vs. other IT worker employers, or by monetization channel.
