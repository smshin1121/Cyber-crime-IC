## Summary

On 2024-10-08, the Prague Regional Police Directorate (Krajské ředitelství policie hlavního města Prahy / KŘP hl. m. Prahy), 3rd Department, announced the arrest of an elderly Czech-American dual-national male whom the Czech Police characterised as having been, "at a certain time, approximately among the three largest distributors of male-child pornography in the world" on the Dark Net.

The arrest is notable for its international-cooperation lineage: the suspect had been under U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) interest for "nearly ten years" without prior identification, and his identity was ultimately revealed through an international identification operation **coordinated by Europol**. The Prague Police, acting on the Europol-coordinated lead, conducted more than one year of follow-up investigation, executed the arrest, and obtained forensic access to several terabytes of originally encrypted material.

The defendant was placed in pre-trial custody and charged under Czech Criminal Code §192 (production and other handling of child pornography) and §193 (exploitation of a child for production of pornography), facing up to eight years' imprisonment on conviction.

## Background

The press release describes the suspect as a Czech citizen who, in earlier life, also obtained United States citizenship, and who was already described in the 1980s by U.S. media as an "electronic genius." This biographical context establishes both (1) the rationale for sustained FBI investigative interest over approximately a decade and (2) the technical sophistication that allowed the suspect to operate undetected on the Dark Net while ranking, at peak, among the three largest worldwide distributors of male-child CSAM.

The Czech Police statement does not disclose:

- the public codename of the Europol-coordinated identification operation (if one exists);

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **Europol coordination of multinational CSAM identification operation** — operationally the central mechanism that converted the FBI's long-standing but unactioned intelligence into a Czech-actionable identification.
- **Informal police-to-police information sharing** between FBI and Europol over the ~10-year intelligence-accumulation period.
- **Czech executive action** (search, seizure, arrest) on the identified suspect by KŘP hl. m. Prahy 3rd Department.
- **Forensic / electronic-evidence handling** to defeat strong encryption on several terabytes of seized material.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- **A decade of FBI intelligence did not yield identification.** The suspect operated successfully on the Dark Net despite being on the FBI's radar for "nearly ten years," indicating that single-jurisdiction intelligence holdings on technically sophisticated Dark Net CSAM offenders are insufficient without multilateral correlation.
- **Strong encryption of seized material.** The data was originally encrypted and required deliberate operational planning during the takedown phase to preserve full forensic access — a recurring challenge in Dark Net CSAM cases involving technically capable suspects.
- **Operational opacity.** No public codename is given for the Europol-coordinated identification operation, limiting the wiki's ability to cross-reference this arrest with any sibling arrests in other jurisdictions that may have flowed from the same operation.

## Lessons Learned

- **Long-running single-country intelligence holdings on Dark Net CSAM offenders may require regional / multilateral correlation to convert into prosecutorial action.** The decade-long FBI lead on this suspect crystallised into an identification only after integration into a Europol-coordinated multinational operation.
- **Coordinated takedown planning is critical for encrypted evidence preservation** in technically sophisticated CSAM cases at the terabyte scale.
