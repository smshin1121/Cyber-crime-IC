## Summary

**Operation Cronos Phase 3** was the third phase of the international enforcement campaign against the **LockBit** ransomware group, announced on 1 October 2024. This phase resulted in 4 new arrests across France, the UK, and Spain, the seizure of 9 servers in Spain, and coordinated **financial sanctions** by Australia, the UK, and the US against a prolific LockBit affiliate linked to **Evil Corp**.

The introduction of financial sanctions alongside traditional criminal enforcement represented a *likely* evolution in international ransomware strategy, combining law enforcement tools with economic pressure instruments typically associated with national security responses.

## Background

Following the [[operation-cronos-phase1|Phase 1 takedown]] in February 2024, the Operation Cronos task force continued its investigation, resulting in additional arrests and a significant expansion of enforcement tools. Phase 3 revealed the operational link between LockBit and Evil Corp, a notorious Russian cybercrime group, through a shared affiliate who was identified and sanctioned.

Cumulative Operation Cronos results (Phase 1 + Phase 3):
- **6 total arrests** (2 in Phase 1, 4 in Phase 3)
- **43 total servers seized** (34 in Phase 1, 9 in Phase 3)

## Legal Framework

### Criminal Enforcement
- **France:** Arrest of suspected LockBit developer
- **United Kingdom:** Arrest of 2 individuals supporting affiliate activity
- **Spain:** Arrest of 1 bulletproof hosting administrator; 9 servers seized

### Financial Sanctions
- **Australia, UK, US:** Joint sanctions against a prolific LockBit affiliate linked to Evil Corp
- **UK:** Sanctioned 15 Russian citizens for Evil Corp involvement
- **US:** Sanctioned 6 citizens
- **Australia:** Sanctioned 2 citizens

The use of coordinated financial sanctions represented a *likely* first in the context of ransomware-specific enforcement actions at this scale.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **Europol/Eurojust coordination:** Continuing task force from Phase 1
- **Financial sanctions coordination:** Trilateral Australia-UK-US sanctions
- **Intelligence sharing:** Cross-referencing LockBit affiliates with Evil Corp membership

## Lessons Learned

1. **Phased operations sustain pressure:** The 8-month gap between Phase 1 and Phase 3 demonstrates that sustained investigation after initial takedowns yields additional arrests and intelligence.
2. **Sanctions as complementary tool:** Financial sanctions broaden the enforcement toolkit beyond criminal prosecution, particularly against actors in jurisdictions where extradition is not feasible.
3. **Mapping criminal networks:** Identifying the LockBit-Evil Corp nexus through a shared affiliate enabled sanctions against a wider network than the original target.

## Follow-Up Actions

The investigation is *likely* ongoing, with additional phases possible as intelligence from seized infrastructure and arrested individuals is exploited.
