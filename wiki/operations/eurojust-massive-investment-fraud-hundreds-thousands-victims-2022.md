## Summary

On **8-9 November 2022**, [[eurojust|Eurojust]] and [[europol-ec3|Europol]] supported a coordinated 10-country action against a massive cryptocurrency-investment-fraud organised crime group that defrauded **hundreds of thousands of victims worldwide** at an estimated rate of **EUR 50 million per quarter**, dating back to at least **2016**. The action originated from **Spanish, German, and Finnish** investigative requests and was operationalised through a **Sweden-led Joint Investigation Team** also involving Albania, Finland, Georgia, Germany, Latvia, Spain, and Ukraine (8 JIT countries). Action days produced **5 arrests** (4 in Albania, 1 in Georgia), **15 call-centre searches** (6 AL, 5 GE, 3 UA, 1 MK), 27 other location searches, 5 vehicle searches, ~50 suspect/witness hearings, and seizure of 500+ electronic devices, EUR 340,000+ in cash, multiple bank accounts and cryptocurrency wallets, plus properties, ID documents, bank cards, and hundreds of other documents.

## Background

The OCG operated **dozens of call centres** in several countries and **hundreds of online platforms**. Suspects presented themselves as **brokers** offering large investment returns from small initial investments, deceiving victims through professionally set-up call centres and online social engineering. Victims invested via OCG-controlled web platforms and lost large sums. The cryptocurrency-investment-fraud methodology is structurally identical to that recorded for the 2025 [[eurojust-100m-crypto-investment-fraud-takedown-2025|€100M crypto-investment-fraud takedown (Spain-Lithuania JIT)]] and [[eurojust-600m-crypto-money-laundering-takedown-2025|€600M crypto money-laundering takedown (France-Belgium JIT extended)]].

## Results and Impact

- **5 arrests**: 4 in Albania, 1 in Georgia.
- **15 call centres searched**: 6 in Albania, 5 in Georgia, 3 in Ukraine, 1 in North Macedonia.
- **27 other locations** searched + **5 vehicles**.
- **~50 hearings** of suspects and witnesses.
- **500+ electronic devices** seized (computers, laptops, USBs, hard disks).
- **EUR 340,000+ in cash** seized.
- Several mobile phones, **several bank accounts**, **cryptocurrency wallets**, **properties**, ID documents, bank cards, hundreds of other documents seized.
- **Hundreds of thousands of victims worldwide** in the underlying disrupted fraud network.
- **EUR 50 million per quarter** estimated damage rate.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The cited release describes a comprehensive multi-mechanism cooperation architecture:

1. **Sweden-led 8-country Joint Investigation Team (JIT)** — Sweden, Albania, Finland, Georgia, Germany, Latvia, Spain, Ukraine. Foundational instrument.
2. **Eurojust 13 coordination meetings** + **Eurojust coordination centre** for the action days.
3. **Europol 7 operational meetings** + **Europol analytical support** throughout.
4. **International Arrest Warrants** (presumably INTERPOL Red Notices for non-EU JIT/action partners).
5. **European Investigation Orders (EIOs)** — judicial-cooperation instrument for EU JIT countries.
6. **Letters of Request to third countries** — for non-EU JIT/action partners.
7. **JIT-state officials deployed to action-state territories** — ~33 Spanish officials in Albania; ~60 German officials in Georgia; etc. A discrete IC mechanism class for cross-territorial JIT participation.
