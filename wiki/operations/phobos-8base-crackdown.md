## Summary

**Phobos/8Base Ransomware Crackdown** was a sustained multinational investigation against the **Phobos** ransomware ecosystem and **8Base** operators. Public official material supports a six-year investigative arc, **four arrests** announced in February 2025, **27 servers taken down**, and a meaningful Asia-Europe-U.S. linkage through the **arrest in [[south-korea|South Korea]] and extradition to the [[united-states|United States]] of a Phobos administrator**.

## Background

The operation did not arise from a single action day. Europol states that it had supported the investigation since February 2019, with dozens of operational meetings and hundreds of SIENA exchanges. Prior arrests in Italy and South Korea fed intelligence into the final February 2025 action against key 8Base figures.

This is one of the cleaner examples in the corpus of a ransomware operation where cross-border coordination is visible not only in the final action day but across the full investigative lifecycle.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The sources explicitly support three mechanisms: Europol SIENA-based operational exchange, Eurojust judicial coordination, and extradition. They do not fully disclose the arrest and extradition paperwork used in every jurisdiction, so the page avoids claiming instruments not named in public reporting.

## Korean Involvement

This operation has confirmed Korean relevance. Official material states that a Phobos administrator was arrested in [[south-korea|South Korea]] in June 2024 and extradited to the [[united-states|United States]] in November 2024. That makes this a stronger international-cooperation case than many ransomware entries because the Korea-U.S. link is not inferred; it is directly stated in the official narrative.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- Public sources do not identify every agency involved in the South Korea arrest and extradition process.
- The public record supports the existence of a Korea-U.S. extradition step but not the full legal filing chain.
- Later prosecution outcomes for all four February 2025 arrestees remain thin in the currently collected source set.
