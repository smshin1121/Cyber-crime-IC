## Summary

On **28 October 2025**, a special working group of **Lai Châu Provincial Police of Việt Nam** led — in coordination with the **Representative Office of the Ministry of Public Security of Việt Nam in Cambodia** (Cơ quan đại diện Bộ Công an Việt Nam tại Campuchia), the **Criminal Police Department of the MPS** (Cục Cảnh sát Hình sự — Bộ Công an), and **Cambodian functional forces** — the takedown of a Vietnamese-staffed online-fraud cell operating from the **Bokor Hill area within the Bokor Ecological Park complex, Tuek Chhou ward, Bokor city, Kampot province, Kingdom of Cambodia**.

The operation produced **59 on-site arrests** (44 male, 15 female), all Vietnamese nationals. As of the date of the press release, **66 suspects in total** had been arrested in connection with the case. Direct command was held by **Senior Lieutenant General Nguyễn Văn Long, Deputy Minister of Public Security and Head of the Police Investigation Agency** of the MPS. The named ringleader is **Sùng Thị Mải** (alias "Vi"), born 1999, from Mon Đào hamlet, Thái Niên commune, Bảo Thắng district, Lào Cai province (now Bảo Thắng commune, Lào Cai).

The press release reports the cell's modi operandi as **impersonation of shippers (delivery drivers), Police, and Military**, plus **romance-fraud (lừa đảo tình cảm)**, with **more than 8,000 victims nationwide** in Việt Nam and an **estimated appropriated total of more than 300 billion VND** (~12 million USD at late-2025 exchange rates). After Cambodian-side triage, **Cambodian functional forces handed over all 59 suspects and seized physical evidence** to the Police Investigation Agency — Lai Châu Provincial Police of Việt Nam, for Vietnamese-domestic prosecution.

## Background

The takedown was placed by Báo Chính phủ in the immediate diplomatic context of the **Signing Ceremony of the United Nations Convention against Cybercrime (the "Hà Nội Convention")**, hosted by Việt Nam in Hà Nội on **25-26 October 2025**, which had ended just days before the operation. The press release explicitly frames the takedown as evidence of "close cooperation between Việt Nam and the Kingdom of Cambodia in general, and between the police forces of the two countries in particular," in that immediate post-signing context.

> [!note] Translation note
> The Vietnamese phrase "lực lượng chức năng của Campuchia" is rendered here as "Cambodian functional forces" — the generic Vietnamese formulation for "competent / responsible Cambodian authorities," without naming a specific Cambodian agency. The most plausible operational counterpart is the [[cambodian-national-police|Cambodian National Police]], but the source as written does not specify the Cambodian agency.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The press release names the following cooperation mechanisms, all police-to-police rather than treaty-based:

1. **Vietnamese-side institutional triad**: Lai Châu Provincial Police (operational lead) + the Representative Office of the MPS of Việt Nam in Cambodia (in-Cambodia liaison) + the Criminal Police Department of the MPS (central support).
2. **Bilateral on-the-ground coordination** with Cambodian functional forces during the takedown itself.
3. **Cambodian-host hand-over of suspects and seized items** to the Vietnamese-side investigative agency for home-state prosecution — performed without (in the source) a named extradition instrument; consistent with [[informal-cooperation|informal police-to-police cooperation]] as the operative channel.

> [!warning] Cooperation-chain attestation gap
> The Cambodian agency that effected the on-site detention and the hand-over is identified in the press release only generically as "lực lượng chức năng của Campuchia" (Cambodian functional forces). A second tier-1 source — ideally a Cambodian Ministry of Interior, Cambodian National Police, or Cambodian Anti-Cyber Crime Department release naming the unit and any formal Cambodian legal basis — is needed before this operation can be linked to a specific Cambodian agency in the wiki's bidirectional graph beyond the country-level [[cambodia|Cambodia]] participation.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- **Generic Cambodian-counterpart description.** The press release names no specific Cambodian agency; "Cambodian functional forces" is the only formulation.

This is consistent with a host-to-home, police-to-police hand-over of co-nationals, but the press release does not document the legal pathway in detail.
- **No technical-evidence detail.** No servers, domains, cryptocurrency wallets, or telecommunications infrastructure are enumerated. The "shipper / police / military impersonation + romance-fraud" modus is described in generic terms.

## Lessons Learned

> [!note] Confidence
> Likely (55-80%): the lessons below are drawn from a single tier-1 primary source. They are consistent with the broader wiki picture of Cambodian-soil scam-compound takedowns followed by host-to-home transfer of co-national suspects, but are not yet corroborated by a second independent tier-1 attestation specific to this case.

1. **Vietnamese MPS Representative Office in Cambodia is a documented liaison node for cybercrime cooperation.** This press release attests, by name, to the operational role of the Representative Office of the Ministry of Public Security of Việt Nam in Cambodia (Cơ quan đại diện Bộ Công an Việt Nam tại Campuchia) in a 2025 Việt Nam–Cambodia takedown — useful as primary evidence of an institutionalised in-Cambodia Vietnamese police liaison for criminal-investigation purposes, distinct from JIT setups.
2. **Host-to-home hand-over pattern for scam-compound co-nationals.** Cambodia-host triage followed by hand-over of all on-site Vietnamese-national suspects to the Vietnamese investigative agency parallels in form the [[korea-cambodia-scam-repatriation|Korea-Cambodia scam repatriation]] and [[korea-cambodia-philippines-73-extradition-2026|KNPA-led 73-person Korea-Cambodia-Philippines extradition (2026-04-27)]]. The legal pathway in this case is not enumerated and may differ.
3. **Diplomatic-context framing tied to the Hà Nội Convention.** The Vietnamese-government framing places the takedown immediately after the [[un-cybercrime-convention-2024|UN Convention against Cybercrime (Hà Nội Convention)]] signing in Hà Nội (25-26 October 2025). The wiki should record this framing as a Vietnamese-government narrative deliverable, not as evidence that the Convention was operationally invoked as a legal basis (which would be premature, given the Convention's signing-not-in-force status as of the operation date).
