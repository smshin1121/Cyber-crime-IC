## Summary

Operazione "Mad Hatter" (Operation Mad Hatter) is an Italian Polizia di Stato investigation against the transnational online distribution of child sexual abuse material (pedopornografia online), announced on 28 April 2026 by the Questura di Milano. The investigation was conducted by the [[italy-polizia-postale|Polizia Postale]]'s national CSAM-investigation centre, the **Centro Nazionale per il Contrasto alla Pedopornografia Online (C.N.C.P.O.)**, coordinated by the **Procura della Repubblica di Roma** (Public Prosecutor's Office of Rome), and supported by the U.S. [[fbi|Federal Bureau of Investigation]] for the identification of subjects of interest.

The operation began in 2024 as a long-duration undercover online infiltration of criminal distribution channels and concluded with a coordinated national action day yielding 3 in-flagrante arrests, 6 additional persons reported at liberty to the prosecutor, and 9 simultaneous search warrants in 9 Italian cities — with the explicit identification, in the seized material, of CSAM generated using artificial intelligence.

## Background

The investigation, opened in 2024, was conducted "infiltrandosi negli ambienti virtuali criminali" — undercover infiltration of criminal virtual environments — by C.N.C.P.O. specialists. The press release describes the targeted distribution channels as transnational (a livello transnazionale), and the seized material as including CSAM "riferibile anche a vittime in tenera età, generato anche con l'utilizzo dell'intelligenza artificiale" (referable also to victims of very young age, generated also through the use of artificial intelligence).

The collaboration with the FBI is the only foreign cooperation channel explicitly named in the source. The press release states that this collaboration "ha consentito di acquisire utili informazioni per l'individuazione dei soggetti d'interesse" — enabled the acquisition of useful information for the identification of subjects of interest — implying U.S.-side intelligence inputs feeding Italian target development.

## Legal Framework

The investigation was prosecuted under Italian criminal-law provisions on online child sexual abuse material (pedopornografia online), specifically the offences of **detenzione** (possession) and **divulgazione di materiale illecito** (dissemination of illicit material). The press release identifies these offences expressly in describing the search warrants:

> "9 decreti di perquisizione domiciliare, personale e informatica nei confronti degli indagati per detenzione e divulgazione di materiale illecito"
> ("9 decrees of domiciliary, personal and computer search against those under investigation for possession and dissemination of illicit material")

Italian criminal-procedure law authorises the long-duration undercover online infiltration conducted by the C.N.C.P.O. specialists, and the simultaneous execution of mixed (domiciliary / personal / computer) search warrants on the action day.

The FBI cooperation is described in informal terms — institutional-to-institutional intelligence sharing — and no specific Mutual Legal Assistance Treaty, Council of Europe instrument, or other formal channel is cited in the source.

## Operational Timeline

- **2024** — Investigation opened. C.N.C.P.O. specialists begin undercover infiltration of online CSAM distribution channels at the transnational level.
- **2024 – April 2026** — Continuous monitoring (esplorare e monitorare in maniera continuativa) of distribution channels; FBI provides information for identification of subjects of interest; AI-generated CSAM identified in the trafficked material.

- **2026-04-28** — Polizia di Stato (Questura di Milano) press release publicly announces the operation.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The operation rests on a single explicitly named cross-border channel: bilateral institutional cooperation between the Italian C.N.C.P.O. (within the [[italy-polizia-postale|Polizia Postale]]) and the [[fbi|U.S. FBI]]. The Italian source describes this as the FBI providing actionable intelligence for subject identification ("acquisire utili informazioni per l'individuazione dei soggetti d'interesse"). Because no MLAT, Council of Europe Budapest Convention article, or judicial-cooperation instrument is cited in the source, the cooperation is best classified — at this evidentiary stage — as [[informal-cooperation|informal law-enforcement cooperation]] rather than a formal MLA channel.

> [!warning] Legal status check needed
> The Italian press release does not cite the legal instrument (MLAT, Budapest Convention article, FBI Legal Attaché agreement, or other) under which the FBI–C.N.C.P.O. exchange occurred. Italy and the United States are both parties to the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention on Cybercrime]] and have a longstanding bilateral MLAT, so multiple plausible channels exist; the specific channel should be confirmed against U.S.-side filings or follow-up Italian reporting before being asserted.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- **AI-generated CSAM** — The Italian release expressly flags AI-generated CSAM in the seized material. This raises ongoing questions about evidentiary classification, victim identification (where no real child is depicted), and the legal categorisation of synthetic material under Italian and U.S. CSAM statutes — questions the press release does not resolve.
- **Transnational distribution but only Italian enforcement disclosed** — The investigation targeted "canali di distribuzione del materiale illecito a livello transnazionale" yet enforcement actions are reported only on Italian territory. Whether U.S. or third-country enforcement followed is not disclosed.
- **Single explicitly named foreign partner** — Although the press release describes the targeted distribution as transnational, only the FBI is named as a foreign cooperation partner. Other partners (e.g., Europol [[europol-vidtf|Victim Identification Task Force]], INTERPOL [[interpol-icse-database|ICSE database]], or other national CSAM units) are neither confirmed nor excluded by this source.

## Lessons Learned

- Long-duration undercover infiltration (2 years) by a centralised national CSAM unit, paired with a single coordinated national action day, remains the Italian C.N.C.P.O.'s preferred operational pattern for transnational CSAM cases.
- AI-generated CSAM is now treated, in mainstream Italian Polizia Postale enforcement, as part of the same evidentiary stream as conventionally produced material — not as a separate "AI" caseload.
- Foreign-partner identification inputs (here, FBI) feed Italian target development without (in the public source) requiring a publicly cited formal MLA mechanism — consistent with the broader pattern of FBI Legal Attaché informal cooperation in CSAM cases.

## Follow-Up Actions

- The 6 persons reported at liberty (denuncia in stato di libertà) remain in pre-trial procedural status; subsequent indictments and trial-court proceedings are expected.
- AI-generated CSAM evidentiary handling will require classification under the relevant Italian Criminal Code articles, which will likely surface in later judicial filings.
