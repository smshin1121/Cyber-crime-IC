## Summary

On **16 April 2026** the Hong Kong Police Force (HKPF) concluded **STEALTHNET**, the **Counter Cyber and Physical Terrorism Joint Exercise 2026** — a multilateral live drill jointly organised by HKPF Crime Wing and Operations Wing with the explicit participation of [[interpol|INTERPOL]], the **Macao Judiciary Police** (Polícia Judiciária), the [[singapore-police|Singapore Police Force]], the [[singapore-csa|Cyber Security Agency of Singapore]] and the [[knpa|Korean National Police Agency]]. More than **380 participants** from local law enforcement agencies, overseas counterparts, government departments, financial regulators and the banking sector took part. The drill simulated a terrorist-financing scenario followed by a cross-border terrorist cell conducting cyber + physical attacks against the financial, aviation and healthcare systems across Hong Kong, Macao, Singapore and Korea, including explosive devices and drones deployed at major event venues. The closing statement framed cross-border law enforcement cooperation and intelligence sharing as essential for safeguarding critical infrastructures.

> [!note]
> STEALTHNET is a **capacity-building exercise**, not an enforcement operation. No arrests, seizures, or indictments resulted. It is ingested here because it constitutes a high-tier-1, named-codename multilateral IC drill explicitly bridging East and Southeast Asia on counter-cyber-terrorism — a class of cooperation activity that is foundational but underrepresented in the operations corpus.

## Background

STEALTHNET 2026 was organised under the [[hong-kong-police|Hong Kong Police Force]]'s Crime Wing and Operations Wing as part of the HKSAR's broader counter-terrorism and cyber-security exercise programme. The exercise builds on a multi-year pattern of HKPF leading regional drills with [[interpol|INTERPOL]], [[singapore-police|Singapore]], [[knpa|Korean]] and Macao Judiciary Police partners.

The scenario was designed around a transnational threat profile: terrorist-financing flows moving through cyber-enabled channels, followed by a cross-border cell executing coordinated cyber attacks (against financial, aviation, healthcare systems) and physical attacks (explosive devices, drones at major event venues) across four jurisdictions simultaneously. The exercise tested whether the participating agencies could share intelligence and coordinate response in real time without the latency that formal MLAT instruments would impose.

## Legal Framework

STEALTHNET was conducted as a joint training exercise — no arrest warrants, coercive measures, or formal MLA instruments were invoked.

- **Hong Kong**: HKPF organised under the Police Force Ordinance (Cap. 232) and HKSAR counter-terrorism mandate.
- **INTERPOL**: Constitution Art. 2 / Art. 3 facilitative-cooperation role (lawful international police cooperation, non-political scope).
- **Macao, Singapore, Korea**: each foreign LE participant acted under its respective domestic statute; participation in the drill required no formal-cooperation instrument.
