## Summary

On **2025-07-07** India's **Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI)** raided a fraud call-centre based in **Noida, Uttar Pradesh, India**, arresting two individuals. The action followed approximately **18 months of collaboration** between the CBI, the **UK National Crime Agency (NCA)**, the **US FBI**, and **Microsoft** (Digital Crimes Unit) — who jointly identified the organised crime group, analysed data, and targeted complex IT infrastructure used by the fraudsters. **More than 100 UK victims** had been contacted by the call-centre, and UK losses are estimated at over **£390,000**; US citizens were also targeted by the same call-centre.

## Background

The investigation commenced in **early 2024** after **NCA International Liaison Officers in the USA** received intelligence from **Microsoft**, which was then cross-compared with **City of London Police Action Fraud Reports**. The fraudsters posed as **Microsoft employees**, contacting victims via **browser pop-ups** falsely claiming the victim's device was infected or had been hacked and offering paid "software solutions" for an attack that had never occurred. To disguise their identity, the fraudsters used **spoofed phone numbers** and **Voice Over Internet Protocol (VoIP)** to route calls through multiple servers in several countries. The NCA's **Liaison Office in Washington DC** brokered intelligence-sharing with the FBI under what the NCA describes as the "operational arrangement reached between the UK and the US last year" (i.e. **2024**) specifically to tackle call-centre fraud.

## Results and Impact

- **2 arrests** at the 2025-07-07 Noida action day.
- **100+ UK victims** contacted; **£390,000+** in estimated UK losses.
- **US citizens also targeted** by the same call-centre (specific US-victim totals not enumerated; FBI cited the national-level USD 1.9 billion call-centre-scam-loss total contextually).
- **Microsoft Digital Crimes Unit** identified as the private-sector intelligence originator that triggered the investigation.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The cited release describes a four-party intelligence-sharing-driven cooperation model with three core mechanism classes:

1. **NCA International Liaison Officer network** (US, Delhi, Washington DC) — the primary inter-jurisdictional intelligence-sharing channel for both UK-US and UK-India sides of the cooperation.
2. **UK-US operational arrangement on call-centre fraud (2024)** — the foundational bilateral instrument for cross-Atlantic intelligence-sharing on call-centre fraud, referenced by the NCA NECC Deputy Director.
3. **Public-private cooperation** with Microsoft Digital Crimes Unit as the originating intelligence party, plus City of London Police Action Fraud Reports as the UK domestic-victim cross-comparison data feed.
