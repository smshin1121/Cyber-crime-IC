## Summary

On **19 March 2026**, the U.S. Department of Justice announced the **court-authorized seizure of 4 domains** controlled by Iran's **Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS)** and used to operate the **Handala Hack** "faketivist" persona for cyber-enabled psychological operations and transnational repression against U.S. persons, Israeli persons, and Iranian dissidents.

Seized domains:
- **Justicehomeland.org**
- **Handala-Hack.to**
- **Karmabelow80.org**
- **Handala-Redwanted.to**

Documented Handala Hack activity (per the cited DOJ-OPA Press Release 26-251 and underlying affidavit):
- **March 11 2026**: Destructive malware attack against a US-based multinational medical technologies firm; claimed retaliation for "ongoing cyber assaults against the infrastructure of the Axis of Resistance."
- **March 9 2026**: Posted PII of ~190 IDF and Israeli-government personnel.
- **March 6 2026**: Claimed theft of 851 GB confidential data from Sanzer Hasidic Jewish community.
- **March 1 2026**: Death-threat emails via Handala_Team@outlook.com offering **USD 250,000 bounty** for the beheading of two Iranian-dissident/journalist targets, invoking **Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG)** "partners" in America and Canada.

> [!note] Cyber-physical-violence convergence
> The MOIS-controlled Handala Hack persona paired hack-and-leak operations with **CJNG cartel-as-proxy** death threats and USD 250K bounties, demonstrating how state-sponsored cyber operations can be operationalised into kinetic threats via criminal organisations. This is the first wiki record of this *cyber-criminal-cartel proxy* attack model.

## Background

Domain attribution evidence:
- **Shared leak sites** linking Justicehomeland.org and Handala-Hack.to
- **Iranian IP ranges** in operational infrastructure
- **Common operational "playbook"**: destructive cyber-attacks + "faketivist" psychological operations using data stolen via hacking

Earlier MOIS use of these domains (per court documents):
- **July 15, 2022** and **September 9, 2022**: Justicehomeland.org used to claim MOIS responsibility for stealing sensitive documents from **Albanian government organizations**, motivated by Albanian government support for **Mujahedeen e-Khalq (MEK)**, an Iranian dissident group.

The escalation to 2026 destructive operations followed the start of the **U.S.-Iran conflict on 28 February 2026**.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- **State-sponsored attribution and prosecution**: MOIS personnel are extremely unlikely to be apprehended given Iran's lack of US extradition relationship. Domain seizure is the most effective disruption tool available.
- **Faketivist persona pattern**: 'Handala Hack' provides plausible deniability between the MOIS and observable hack-and-leak activity. The court-authorized domain seizure depends on the affidavit's attribution proof (shared leak sites + Iranian IP ranges + common playbook).
- **Cyber-criminal-cartel proxy convergence**: invoking CJNG for kinetic violence against doxing targets escalates the threat posture from cyber-only to cyber-physical, requiring inter-divisional FBI coordination (Cyber Division + Counterterrorism Division + criminal divisions).
- **No multi-country LE cooperation cited**: this is a US-domestic disruption action with international target/victim profile but no enumerated foreign LE partner agency.

## Lessons Learned

1. **State-sponsored cyber-enabled transnational repression** is a discrete IC-relevant pattern: Iranian MOIS + faketivist persona + hack-and-leak + cartel-proxy violence threats.
2. **Cyber-physical-violence convergence via criminal-cartel proxy** is the first such pattern documented in the wiki: MOIS leveraged CJNG for kinetic violence threats with USD 250K bounties.
3. **Court-authorized domain seizure** is the recurring DOJ disruption tool against state-sponsored cyber operations in absence of extradition opportunity.
4. **Faketivist persona pattern** (Handala Hack) provides plausible deniability between adversary state and observable cyber activity; attribution depends on shared infrastructure analysis.
5. **Albania-2022 → Israel/US-2026 trajectory** documented from MOIS faketivist domain reuse, showing the same domain infrastructure was repurposed across different geopolitical contexts.
6. **DOJ Rewards for Justice USD 10M outstanding** signals active fugitive-attribution track beyond domain seizure; pairs with similar Rewards offers in other 2026 actions (USD 4M Daren Li, USD 10M Tai Chang from the Kok An action).
