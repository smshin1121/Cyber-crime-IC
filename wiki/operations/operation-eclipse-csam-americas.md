# Operation Eclipse (CSAM Americas)

## Summary

Operation Eclipse was an INTERPOL-coordinated operation against child sexual abuse and exploitation material across nine countries in Central America, North America, and the Caribbean. The operational window ran from February 2025 to January 2026, with public results announced on 5 March 2026.

The operation focused on identifying victims, reopening older cases, locating suspects, and synchronizing cross-border evidence. INTERPOL reported support from UNODC and the Child Rescue Coalition, with national authorities conducting arrests, searches, victim-protection measures, and extradition follow-up.

The INTERPOL release highlights several country examples: Panama resolved a long-unidentified victim case; the Dominican Republic arrested two suspects in a case involving two child victims; Costa Rica confirmed a victim from older child abuse material; and Panama-Dominican Republic coordination located two suspects wanted by Panamanian authorities in Dominican territory, prompting extradition proceedings.

## Boundary and Non-Duplication Notes

This record is the 2026 INTERPOL-coordinated CSAM operation across nine countries. It is distinct from unrelated domestic operations that may also use the name "Eclipse", including a 2024 Panama domestic operation reported by Panama's public prosecutor.

The page includes only high-level, victim-protective details. It does not reproduce identifying details, images, or granular abuse descriptions.
