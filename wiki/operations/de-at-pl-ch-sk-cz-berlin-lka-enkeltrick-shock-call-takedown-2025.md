## Summary

On **2025-11-12**, the Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Berlin (Berlin Public Prosecutor's Office, Abteilung 285) and the [[germany-lka|Landeskriminalamt Berlin]] (LKA Berlin, Dezernat 253) issued a joint press release (No. 273) announcing the operational results of a seven-week cross-border action campaign against the **"Enkeltrick"** (grandparent / shock-call) phone-fraud phenomenon, running from **2025-09-15 to 2025-10-31**.

The campaign was conducted under the **EU-funded "ISF-LUMEN" project** (Internal Security Fund), coordinated by the LKA Baden-Württemberg. It brought together police authorities from **all 16 German federal states**, the German [[germany-bka|Federal Criminal Police Office (BKA)]], **[[austria|Austria]]**, **[[poland|Poland]]**, **[[switzerland|Switzerland]]**, **[[slovakia|Slovakia]]**, **[[czechia|Czech Republic]]**, and **[[europol-ec3|Europol]]**, under the authority of the competent specialised divisions of the responsible state prosecutors' offices.

Operational results were **16 arrests** (one 26-year-old female caller; three male callers aged 24, 25 and 49; twelve money couriers aged 22-63), the **dismantling of three call centres** (Frankfurt am Main, Austria, Warsaw), the **prevention of 44 attempted Enkeltrick cases**, and **aggregate prevented damages of EUR 2,007,330**.

## Background

The "Enkeltrick" (literally "grandchild trick") and the "Schockanruf" (shock call) are well-established Polish-organised phone-fraud patterns targeting elderly residents primarily in [[germany|Germany]]. Callers — often Polish nationals — exploit telephone-directory entries to pick targets with traditional first names suggesting older birth-year cohorts. The script opens with an impersonated relative crying about a fatal car accident, then transitions to an impersonated police officer or prosecutor demanding immediate cash or jewellery "bail" payment, handed to a money courier at the victim's door.

The pattern is high-frequency and low-individual-loss in absolute terms but high-aggregate-damage. It does not lend itself to single-day synchronised takedown because the call-centres and the money couriers are organisationally distinct and geographically dispersed across at least Germany, Poland and Austria.

## Legal Framework

The action was conducted under domestic German criminal procedure (Strafprozessordnung) on the instructions of each German federal state's responsible specialised public prosecutor's office, with cross-border investigative steps in Austria, Poland, Switzerland, Slovakia, and the Czech Republic conducted under standard EU police-cooperation channels including informal direct contacts, Europol intelligence exchange, and — where required — mutual legal assistance.

The umbrella legal-policy framework is the **EU Internal Security Fund (ISF)**, specifically the **ISF-LUMEN** project coordinated by the LKA Baden-Württemberg.

## Results and Impact

- **Arrests:** 16 — one 26-year-old female caller; three male callers aged 24, 25 and 49; twelve money couriers aged 22-63
- **Call centres dismantled:** 3 — in Frankfurt am Main, in Austria, and in Warsaw
- **Attempted cases prevented:** 44 "Enkeltrick" cases
- **Aggregate damages prevented:** **EUR 2,007,330**
- **Scope of synchronisation:** all 16 German federal states plus 5 foreign jurisdictions, sustained over 7 action weeks

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **EU Internal Security Fund (ISF-LUMEN) project framework** — coordinated by LKA Baden-Württemberg
- **[[europol-ec3|Europol]] operational and analytical support**
- **Direct LE-to-LE channels** between German federal-state LKAs and foreign police authorities (Austria, Poland, Switzerland, Slovakia, Czech Republic)
- Prosecutorial coordination among the responsible specialised divisions of the German federal-state public prosecutors' offices

## Challenges and Friction Points

- The "Enkeltrick" / shock-call phenomenon has a long-standing Polish-organised structure that has previously resisted single-day takedowns (cf. [[operation-pandora-12-phone-fraud-call-centres-2024|Operation Pandora 2024]] which targeted a parallel but distinct ring operating from Albania, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Kosovo and Lebanon). The 2025 LUMEN campaign opted for the seven-week sustained-tempo format precisely because of the high frequency and dispersed operational footprint of this pattern.

## Lessons Learned

- Sustained-tempo "action week" format over seven weeks across all 16 German federal states plus five foreign jurisdictions enabled real-time coordination on a high-frequency phone-fraud pattern that does not lend itself to single-day synchronised takedown.
- EU ISF project funding (ISF-LUMEN, coordinated by LKA Baden-Württemberg) provided the cross-border operational umbrella under which the prosecutorial home of each criminal complaint (16 German federal states) could be matched in real time to the location of the call-centre and the money courier (often in Poland or Austria).
