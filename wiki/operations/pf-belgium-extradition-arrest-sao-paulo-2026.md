## Summary

On **2026-03-09** the **Polícia Federal (PF)** in **São Paulo** arrested a **Belgian national** residing in Brazil on a **Brazilian Supreme Federal Court (STF) arrest warrant for extradition purposes** (*mandado de prisão para extradição*), in response to a **Belgian extradition request**. The Belgian underlying charges, as enumerated by the PF release, include **document forgery**, **criminal threat**, **fraud**, **falsificação de tecnologia da informação** (information-technology forgery — corresponding to **computer-related forgery, Budapest Convention Art. 7**), **breach of trust**, and additional unspecified offences. The defendant is in Brazilian custody pending the STF passive-extradition proceeding.

## Background

The PF release frames the case as a routine *prisão para extradição* — the standard Brazilian device for detaining a person sought by a foreign state pending a STF decision on a passive-extradition petition. The defendant was already known to Brazilian law enforcement (the release notes *outras ocorrências criminais* — additional Brazilian criminal incidents) and had been residing in Brazil before the Belgian extradition request was formalised.

The Belgian charges include a hybrid cluster — document forgery, fraud, IT-forgery, and breach of trust — that is characteristic of computer-enabled financial-fraud cases under Belgian law. *Falsification informatique / informaticavalsheid* (Belgian Code pénal / Strafwetboek Art. 210bis) corresponds to the Budapest Convention's **Art. 7 — computer-related forgery**. *Fraude informatique / informaticabedrog* (Art. 504quater) corresponds to **Art. 8 — computer-related fraud**. The PF wording *falsificação de tecnologia da informação* is a Portuguese rendering of the IT-forgery charge that is not a Brazilian statutory term but is functionally equivalent.

> [!note] Translation note
> The release uses the phrase *falsificação de tecnologia da informação* — literally "falsification of information technology". This is the PF's translation of the Belgian charge label, not a Brazilian Penal Code offence category. In Brazilian dual-criminality analysis (see [[dual-criminality]]), the closest counterparts are likely Lei 12.737/2012 (Lei Carolina Dieckmann) Penal Code Arts. **154-A** (invasão de dispositivo informático) and **154-B**, combined with Art. **298** (falsificação de documento particular) where computer data is treated as document. The release does not assert this mapping; it is offered here as background only.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- **Cybercrime extradition under a hybrid charge cluster**: The Belgian charges combine traditional offences (document forgery, threat, breach of trust) with a cybercrime offence (IT forgery — Budapest Art. 7). Under [[dual-criminality]], STF must verify that each Belgian charge has a Brazilian counterpart that is criminal under Brazilian law at the time of the request. The IT-forgery charge will likely be analysed against Lei 12.737/2012 Arts. 154-A/154-B + Penal Code Art. 298, but this analysis is not addressed in the public release.
- **Information opacity around Belgian counterpart authority**: The release names neither a Belgian agency, a Belgian prosecutor, nor a Belgian investigating judge. This makes wiki cross-referencing on the Belgian side limited until secondary documentation appears.

## Lessons Learned

- **Belgium-Brazil cybercrime extradition axis is documented for the first time in the wiki on the Belgium-as-requesting / Brazil-as-requested side.** The complementary Belgium-as-base side has been documented in [[fgp-oost-vlaanderen-belgium-phishingpanelen-caas-arrest-2026]]. The two cases together represent the Belgium-Brazil cooperation thread in both directions.
- **PF press-release publication policy systematically withholds defendant identity and Belgian counterpart authority** in extradition-arrest releases. This is a recurring item-nonresponse field across multiple 2026 PF extradition stories (Belgian, Albanian, Lebanese, Portuguese-Belgian) and should be treated as a publication-policy gap, not a case-specific data hole.
- **Translation pattern noted**: PF renders Belgian *falsification informatique / informaticavalsheid* (Code pénal Art. 210bis — Budapest Convention Art. 7) as *falsificação de tecnologia da informação*. This is functionally — not statutorily — equivalent under Brazilian law, where the closest counterparts run through Lei 12.737/2012 (Lei Carolina Dieckmann) Arts. 154-A/154-B combined with Penal Code Art. 298.
