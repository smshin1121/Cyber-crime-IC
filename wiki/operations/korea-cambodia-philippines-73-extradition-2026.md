## Summary

On 2026-04-27 the [[knpa|Korean National Police Agency]] announced the extradition into Korea of 73 Korean-national online-scam suspects from [[cambodia|Cambodia]] (42 persons) and the [[philippines|Philippines]] (31 persons) over a roughly two-month operation period beginning 2026-02-26. The KNPA release describes the operation as the largest single-country cyber-fugitive extradition into Korea on record. 69 of the 73 suspects entered post-arrival custody on remand orders.

The action was coordinated through a Korean whole-government task force formally identified as 범정부 「초국가범죄 특별대응TF」 (Whole-Government Transnational Crime Special Response Task Force), bringing together the [[knpa|KNPA]], the [[ministry-of-foreign-affairs-korea|Ministry of Foreign Affairs]], the [[ministry-of-justice-korea|Ministry of Justice]], and the [[national-intelligence-service-korea|National Intelligence Service]] alongside cooperation with the Cambodian National Police and the Philippine National Police. In Cambodia specifically, a Korea-side operational unit known as 「코리아전담반」 (Korea-dedicated Squad) is named as the field unit that produced the 42-person Cambodian tranche.

## Background

This 2026 operation continues the broader Korea-led scam-compound enforcement frame previously documented in the wiki as [[korea-cambodia-scam-repatriation]] (the 2025 Cambodia voice-phishing repatriation line) and is announced against the larger backdrop of recurring Cambodian and Philippine scam-compound disclosures during 2025–2026, including the Cambodia kidnapping–murder incident referenced in Korean year-end reporting for 2025. The 2026 announcement materially widens scope along three axes: (a) two requested jurisdictions in the same announcement (Cambodia and Philippines, not Cambodia alone), (b) a whole-government task force frame rather than a KNPA-only frame, and (c) a multi-crime-type cohort spanning voice phishing, romance scam, illegal gambling, a 2011 data breach, auto-loan fraud, and sex exploitation.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The KNPA release does not name a specific MLAT instrument; the cooperation is described in the language of "유기적 공조" (organic cooperation) with the Cambodian and Philippine police authorities, plus the whole-government Korean task force structure. The mechanism mix is therefore:
- Bilateral law-enforcement cooperation between [[knpa|KNPA]] and the Cambodian and Philippine National Police authorities, leading to extradition into Korea.
- Korea-side operational integration through the 「초국가범죄 특별대응TF」 (KNPA, MOFA, MOJ, NIS).
- A field-deployed Korea-only liaison/operational unit, 「코리아전담반」, embedded in Cambodian operations.

## Korean Involvement (한국의 참여)

This operation is Korea-led: the requesting state is Korea, the lead agency is the KNPA, and the four cooperating Korean ministries (KNPA, MOFA, MOJ, NIS) are explicitly named as task-force members. The Korea-side voice-phishing victim count (368 persons, ~₩51.7 billion in losses) and the romance-scam victim count (53 persons, ~₩2.3 billion in losses) are direct Korea-jurisdiction harms. The KNPA frames the operation as the largest single-country cyber-fugitive extradition into Korea on record, and 박준성 경찰청 국제치안협력국장(직무대리) is named as a public spokesperson.
