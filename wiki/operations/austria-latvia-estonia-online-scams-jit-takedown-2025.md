# Austria-Latvia-Estonia Online Scams JIT Takedown (2025)

## Summary

Austrian, Latvian, and Estonian authorities, coordinated by [[eurojust|Eurojust]] through a joint investigation team and supplemented by [[finland-nbi|Finnish assistance]], dismantled a criminal network that defrauded victims in Austria and Latvia of more than EUR 3 million through investment scams, micro-loan/contract scams, romance scams, and phone-impersonation scams. The network ran SIM box farms that spoofed local numbers to make foreign calls appear domestic. The 2025-10 action day produced seven arrests, 26 searches, EUR 700,000 frozen, and seizure of 1,200 SIM boxes, 40,000 SIM cards, five servers, and four luxury cars. The official announcement was published by Eurojust on 2025-10-17.

## Background

The criminal network targeted German-speaking and Latvian-speaking victims with several scam patterns simultaneously, including fake high-yield investment platforms, fraudulent micro-loan and "contract recovery" schemes, romance scams, and phone calls impersonating bank, government, or technical-support personnel. The use of SIM box farms allowed criminal call centres to display Austrian or Latvian local caller IDs while operating outbound from third-country infrastructure, eroding the most basic call-screening defence available to private victims. The Austrian Public Prosecutor's Office in Vienna and the State Police of Latvia opened parallel investigations and brought them together at Eurojust as a JIT, with Estonia joining as a third party-state and Finland providing assistance.

## Results and Impact

- 7 arrests across Austria, Latvia, and Estonia (per-country breakdown not given in the public release).
- 26 search locations executed.
- 5 servers taken down.
- 1,200 SIM box devices seized.
- 40,000 SIM cards confiscated.
- 4 luxury cars seized.
- EUR 700,000 frozen across the participating jurisdictions.
- Victim baseline: 1,700+ in Austria, 1,500+ in Latvia, with total losses exceeding EUR 3 million.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- [[joint-investigation-team|Joint investigation team]] hosted and financed by [[eurojust|Eurojust]].
- [[eurojust-coordination-meeting|Eurojust coordination meetings]] before the action day.
- [[europol-ec3|Europol]] analytical support and on-site specialist deployment to Riga.
- Private-sector cooperation through the Shadowserver Foundation, facilitated by Europol, for data exchange supporting the analytical phase.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- The official release names the participating prosecutors' offices and police agencies but does not break down arrests, searches, or seizures per state, which limits per-jurisdiction attribution in the SNA edge data.
- The exact action-day date inside the 2025-10 window is not pinpointed in the public release; the announcement window (2025-10-13 to 2025-10-17) is recorded as a frontmatter range.
- SIM box infrastructure crosses telecommunications-licensing law and cybercrime law in different ways across the four jurisdictions; the JIT structure provides the coordination layer but the case will likely be prosecuted in parallel domestic proceedings.

## Lessons Learned

- A multi-pattern scam network (investment, micro-loan, romance, phone impersonation) is more efficient to dismantle as a single SIM-box-rooted infrastructure case than as separate scam-type cases, when the same operator runs all patterns through the same call-centre infrastructure.
- A four-state JIT (three party states plus one assisting state) is workable when the assisting state provides specialised cyber-enabled crime expertise without needing to be a full party to the JIT instrument.
- Europol on-site specialist deployment during the action day, combined with private-sector data integration via Shadowserver, scales analytical capacity for searches at scale (26 locations).

## Korean Relevance (한국 관련성)

No direct Korean involvement is reported in the source. The case is informative for [[knpa|Korean National Police]] practitioners as a reference architecture for coordinating multi-pattern fraud cases that combine investment scams, romance scams, and phone-impersonation scams (보이스피싱) under a single SIM box infrastructure, which is the pattern observed in many Korean voice-phishing cases run through Cambodia and Southeast Asia. The Eurojust JIT model has no direct Korean equivalent, but the coordinating-meeting + on-site specialist deployment + private-sector data exchange template is portable to ASEAN-Korea cooperation under the Cross-border Anti-Scam Collaboration Platform.
