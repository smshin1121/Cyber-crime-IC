# Kazakhstan-Armenia Yerevan Call-Centre Investment Fraud Takedown (2025)

> [!info] Provisional page (single tier-1 source)
> This page is published below the preferred source threshold of 5 because the controlling tier-1 source establishes a Central Asia / South Caucasus cooperation pairing not otherwise represented in the corpus. It will be promoted to a normal page when additional tier-1 confirmations (Investigative Committee of Armenia, polisia.kz primary post, charging documents) are ingested.

## Summary

On **2025-02-18** the press service of the **Ministry of Internal Affairs of the Republic of Kazakhstan** announced, through Sputnik Kazakhstan, that officers of the **Department for Combating Cybercrime of the MIA of Kazakhstan** had carried out a joint special operation in Yerevan together with the **Main Directorate for the Investigation of Especially Important Cases of the Investigative Committee of the Republic of Armenia**. The operation closed two call centres in Yerevan, detained 41 members of a transnational criminal group, and seized cash, gold, vehicles, computers, and a cold cryptocurrency wallet. The operation is described as ongoing, with the Armenian side continuing investigative actions supported by the Kazakh MIA Cybercrime Counteraction Department.

## Background

The press release describes a transnational organized criminal group operating two call centres in Yerevan that pitched fake "large investment companies" to citizens of Kazakhstan and European countries. The fraud pattern is consistent with the broader pattern of investment / "broker" call-centre operations now documented across Europe (see [[albania-austria-call-centre-investment-fraud-2026]]) but with a different operating geography (South Caucasus host jurisdiction, Central Asia primary victim pool, European secondary victim pool).

## Legal Framework

The released press text does not name a specific bilateral instrument, MLAT request, or joint investigation team agreement. The work is described as "joint operational actions" by the two named units, with the Armenian side carrying the formal procedural file. Both Kazakhstan and Armenia have separately developed cybercrime cooperation channels through Council of Europe / Octopus capacity-building, but the controlling source for this operation does not invoke a specific framework.

## Operational Timeline

- **Pre-2025-02-18:** Joint preparatory work between the Kazakh MIA Cybercrime Counteraction Department and the Armenian Investigative Committee Main Directorate for the Investigation of Especially Important Cases.
- **On or before 2025-02-18:** Action day in Yerevan; two call centres closed; 41 individuals detained; searches conducted with seizure of cash, gold, vehicles, computer equipment, and a cold cryptocurrency wallet.
- **2025-02-18 09:21 (Astana):** Kazakh MIA press service issues the public statement; relayed by Sputnik Kazakhstan.
- **From 2025-02-18 onward:** Armenian side continuing investigative actions (additional episodes, accomplices, Kazakh-victim damage quantification) with Kazakh MIA Cybercrime Counteraction Department support.

## Results and Impact

- 41 individuals detained.
- Two call centres in Yerevan closed.
- More than 70 units of computer equipment seized.
- Approximately 1 kilogram of gold seized.
- 5 vehicles seized.
- USD 30,000 in cash seized; additional Russian rubles and Georgian lari noted in the source.
- One cold cryptocurrency wallet seized; amount not disclosed.
- Detainees: citizens of Armenia, Georgia, Iran, Syria, and Lebanon.
- Total damage to Kazakh and European victims: not yet quantified by the source.

## Lessons Learned

- Central Asia / South Caucasus cybercrime cooperation can produce action-day results outside of EU JIT / Eurojust scaffolding, on the basis of bilateral coordination between a national MIA cybercrime unit and a national Investigative Committee. The corpus should expect a different documentation pattern (state news agency with a named on-record official) compared to Europol / Eurojust newsroom releases.
- For investment-fraud call centres hosted in non-EU South Caucasus jurisdictions but targeting both regional and EU victim pools, asset and evidence seizure can be substantial (cash, gold, vehicles, cryptocurrency) and warrants a [[cryptocurrency-seizure]] and asset-tracing follow-up beyond the announcing jurisdiction.
