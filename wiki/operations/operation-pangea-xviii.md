# Operation Pangea XVIII

## Summary

Operation Pangea XVIII was an INTERPOL-coordinated global enforcement operation targeting the illegal online sale and distribution of unapproved, counterfeit, substandard, and falsified medical products. The enforcement window ran from 10 to 23 March 2026, with public results announced on 7 May 2026.

INTERPOL describes the action as spanning 90 countries and territories. The structured country list above mirrors the named participating jurisdictions in the official release, normalized to existing wiki country nodes where possible; Northern Ireland is treated under the United Kingdom rather than as a separate country node.

## Cooperation Model

- **INTERPOL coordination:** INTERPOL coordinated the global action and published the operation-level results and participation list.
- **Regulator-customs-police model:** National health regulators, customs authorities, police agencies, postal inspectors, and border partners combined physical inspections with online marketplace enforcement.
- **Digital enforcement:** Authorities targeted websites, social media pages, channels, automated bots, marketplace listings, and other online routes used to market and sell illegal medicines.
- **International support network:** INTERPOL reported support from national health regulatory agencies, OLAF, Europol, the International Narcotics Control Board, the Pharmaceutical Security Institute, the Transnational Alliance to Combat Illicit Trade, UNODC, the Universal Postal Union, the World Customs Organization, and the World Health Organization.
- **National action examples:** UK MHRA, Singapore HSA, and the Danish Medicines Agency each reported country-level seizures or online takedown activity connected to Pangea XVIII.

## Boundary and Non-Duplication Notes

Operation Pangea XVIII is included because official sources frame it as an international enforcement operation against online pharmaceutical crime, with explicit digital sales infrastructure disruption and cross-border regulator, customs, law-enforcement, and private-sector cooperation.

It is distinct from [[operation-pangea-xvii|Operation Pangea XVII]], which covered the prior December 2024 to May 2025 enforcement cycle. Pangea XVIII is not a dark-web market takedown record; its value in this corpus is the recurring global model for disrupting online illicit-medicine distribution across web platforms, social media, e-commerce, courier flows, and border checks.

## Evidence Notes

INTERPOL is the anchor source for the operation name, global timeframe, 90-country scope, arrests, criminal groups, investigations, search warrants, seizure volume, online disruption count, support organizations, and named participation list. UK MHRA, Singapore HSA, and the Danish Medicines Agency provide independent national official confirmation of the same operation and add country-level digital and seizure metrics.
