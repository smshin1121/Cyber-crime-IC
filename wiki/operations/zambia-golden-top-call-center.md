## Summary

In **April 2024**, Zambian authorities raided the **Golden Top Support Services** call center in Roma, a suburb of Lusaka, resulting in **77 arrests** — including 22 Chinese nationals (who subsequently pleaded guilty in June 2024), 1 Cameroonian, and 54 Zambian youths aged 20-25 (Source: [[2024-06-05-therecord-zambia-golden-top-guilty-pleas]], [[2024-04-15-bitdefender-zambia-golden-top-arrests]]). The raid was led by Zambia's **Drug Enforcement Commission (DEC)** in coordination with Zambia Police, immigration, and anti-terrorism unit. Seizures included vehicles, two firearms, 97 desktop PCs, 13,000+ SIM cards, 11 SIM boxes, and 6 properties.

> [!warning] Date correction (2026-04-14)
> Earlier version of this page recorded the raid as "early 2025" based on a Tier-3 source. The Tier-2/3 sources verified in this enrichment pass confirm **April 2024** as the actual raid date. Frontmatter and case_id updated accordingly.

The case illustrates the growing phenomenon of transnational criminal organizations setting up fraudulent call centers in African countries, often involving workers from Asia.

## Background

Fraudulent call centers have become a major cybercrime-enabled threat, with criminal organizations establishing operations in countries with lower labor costs and potentially less stringent enforcement. Zambia, like several other African countries, has seen an increase in such operations, often involving Chinese-organized crime networks that recruit or traffic workers from China and other Asian countries.
