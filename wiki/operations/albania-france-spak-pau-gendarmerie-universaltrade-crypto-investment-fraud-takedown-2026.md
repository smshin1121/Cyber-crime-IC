# Albania-France SPAK / Pau Gendarmerie UNIVERSAL TRADE Cryptocurrency Investment Fraud Joint Operation (2026-04)

> [!info] Provisional / single-source
> This page rests on a single tier-1 primary source — the SPAK Albania official press release dated 2026-04-23. It is below the preferred 5-source threshold for a normal operation page and is filed as a provisional bilateral cyber investment-fraud cooperation record. It will be promoted when (a) a Pau Public Prosecutor's Office statement, (b) a French Gendarmerie release, or (c) a Eurojust press release corroborating the joint operation is ingested.

## Summary

On **22 April 2026**, the **Albanian Special Structure Against Corruption and Organised Crime** ("SPAK" / Prokuroria e Posaçme) and the **Pau Gendarmerie Financial and Digital Investigations Division** (France) jointly conducted searches at one call centre and four residences in Tirana, Albania, in a joint investigation into the offence of "Computer Fraud" against the cryptocurrency investment-fraud platform **UNIVERSAL TRADE** (universaltrade.io). The cooperation envelope had been initiated by **Eurojust spontaneous information exchange**, by which the Pau gendarmerie investigation section transmitted to the Albanian Special Prosecutor's Office data from a Pau Public Prosecutor's Office preliminary investigation into cryptocurrency investment fraud and aggravated money laundering.

Five Albanian nationals were identified (by initials) as part of the wallet-operations layer of the scheme: **A.B., E.Sh., B.Z., F.B.** and **G.P.** Following searches and interrogation, **G.P.** was determined to be the principal cryptocurrency-wallet operator, and a **SPAK detention order** was executed against him by investigators of the **Albanian National Bureau of Investigation** (BKH / Byroja Kombëtare e Hetimit). The operation seized 4 cold-wallet cryptocurrency wallets, computers, storage media, phones, suspected forged identity documents, a luxury vehicle, automatic money-counting equipment, business stamps, and currency in USD/CAD/EUR/GBP/ALL totalling approximately 5,000,000 ALL (≈ EUR 50,000).

Albanian charging proceedings were registered as criminal proceeding **no. 39/20255** under **Articles 28/4, 143/b, 333/a and 334** of the Albanian Criminal Code. France's parallel proceeding is the Pau Public Prosecutor's Office preliminary investigation against UNIVERSAL TRADE for cryptocurrency investment fraud and aggravated money laundering.

This case qualifies for the IC corpus under the L24 ≥2-country IC requirement: the SPAK release explicitly identifies the Pau Gendarmerie Financial and Digital Investigations Division (France) as the cooperating foreign LE agency in a joint investigation, and Eurojust as the channel that triggered the joint cooperation through spontaneous information exchange. France is here a cooperating jurisdiction (LE counterpart and victim-side prosecutor), not merely a victim/origin/destination state.

## Background

The UNIVERSAL TRADE platform (universaltrade.io) was used by agents operating with fake French identities to contact French citizens and induce them to invest in cryptocurrency through the platform. Funds deposited by French victims were transferred into cryptocurrency wallets opened and operated by Albanian residents in Tirana, who then forwarded the funds to the upstream fraud authors.

Per the SPAK release, the **Pau Public Prosecutor's Office** had been conducting a preliminary investigation into the platform for organised-group cryptocurrency investment fraud and aggravated money laundering, on the basis of complaints filed by **four French citizens**. The investigation period covered by the documented victim losses runs from **February 2023 to May 2023**, with cumulative recorded losses reaching **EUR 845,400** against the four named French complainants and **EUR 1.1 million** in aggregate against **nine additional identified French complainants**.

The Pau gendarmerie investigation section then transmitted its accumulated findings to SPAK through Eurojust's spontaneous information exchange function. SPAK responded by registering Albanian criminal proceeding no. 39/20255 and conducting the 22 April 2026 searches in Tirana with on-the-ground participation by Pau gendarmerie judicial-police officers.

## Legal Framework

**Albania (charging state):**

- **Albanian Criminal Code Article 143/b** — Computer fraud (mashtrim kompjuterik). Cited in the SPAK release as the principal substantive offence.
- **Albanian Criminal Code Article 28/4** — Commission in cooperation (a complicity / joint-commission rule).
- **Albanian Criminal Code Article 333/a** — Structured criminal group (grupi i strukturuar kriminal).
- **Albanian Criminal Code Article 334** — Commission of offences by a criminal organisation and structured criminal group.
- **Albanian criminal proceeding no. 39/20255** — registered by SPAK on the basis of the Pau gendarmerie's spontaneous-exchange transmission via Eurojust.
- **SPAK detention order** against G.P. — executed by investigators of the Albanian National Bureau of Investigation (BKH).

**France (parallel charging state):**

- Pau Public Prosecutor's Office preliminary investigation into:
  - Fraud through cryptocurrency investments, committed in the framework of an organised group.
  - Aggravated money laundering (pastrim parash në rrethana rënduese, per the SPAK paraphrase).
- Specific French Penal Code provisions are not enumerated in the SPAK release.

**Coordination instrument:**

- **Eurojust spontaneous information exchange.** Cited explicitly in the release: "Nga shkëmbimi spontan i informacionit, i dërguar nga Eurojust, seksioni i kërkimeve të xhandarmërisë në Pau i ka përcjellë Prokurorisë së Posaçme të dhënat mbi hetimin..." ("Through spontaneous information exchange transmitted via Eurojust, the Pau gendarmerie investigation section forwarded to the Special Prosecutor's Office data on the investigation..."). The release does not characterise the cooperation as a formal Joint Investigation Team (JIT); the coordination instrument of record is spontaneous information exchange under the Eurojust framework rather than a JIT agreement.

**Treaty framework (background, not invoked by name in the release):**

- Both Albania and France are parties to the [[budapest-convention|Council of Europe Convention on Cybercrime (Budapest Convention)]] and to the Council of Europe Convention on Mutual Assistance in Criminal Matters; Albania is also a Eurojust cooperation partner. The release does not invoke any of these instruments by name; the coordination of record is the Eurojust spontaneous-exchange envelope.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **Eurojust spontaneous information exchange** — the channel of record by which Pau-side findings reached SPAK and triggered the joint search-day operation. This is documented as a working channel for Albania-France cyber-fraud cooperation outside the EU JIT framework.
- **Direct LE-to-LE judicial police cooperation** — Pau Gendarmerie Financial and Digital Investigations Division officers were present in Tirana on 22 April 2026 alongside SPAK and BKH, conducting searches as part of the joint operation rather than only providing remote evidence.
- **Domestic Albanian inter-agency coordination** — Police Oversight Agency (Agjencia e Mbikëqyrjes Policore) and Albanian State Police (Policia e Shtetit) supported SPAK on the search-day execution side.

The release does not identify formal MLAT instruments, EIO-equivalents, or Budapest Convention Article 35 24/7 Network channels as the cooperation pathway. The cooperation envelope is best described as **Eurojust-initiated spontaneous information exchange operationalised into a same-day binational search-and-detention operation in Tirana**.

## Challenges and Friction Points

1. **Split charging across jurisdictions.** Albania charges call-centre operators and wallet-handlers in Tirana under domestic Albanian Criminal Code articles; France runs a parallel preliminary investigation against UNIVERSAL TRADE for fraud and money laundering through the Pau Public Prosecutor's Office. Coordination of parallel proceedings (forfeiture, witness victim compensation, restitution) is not described in the release.
2. **Cryptocurrency wallet attribution.** Tracing victim funds through cryptocurrency wallets to identify the wallet operators in Tirana is described in the release as the operation's principal investigative achievement. Cold-wallet contents have not yet been enumerated.
3. **Scope of fraud not fully bounded.** The release identifies 13 French complainants (4 named + 9 additional identified) but does not state the total number of victims or whether non-French victims exist.
4. **Suspect anonymisation.** The release identifies suspects only by initials, limiting cross-corpus identity attribution and follow-on case linkage.
5. **Universaltrade.io domain status.** The release does not state whether the universaltrade.io domain has been seized, taken offline, or referred for civil action; the platform may continue to operate against new victims.
6. **French-side public reporting absent at this stage.** The corresponding Pau Public Prosecutor's Office statement, French Gendarmerie release, and Eurojust press release have not been ingested as of 2026-05-10, leaving the wiki page reliant on a single tier-1 source.

## Lessons Learned

- **Eurojust spontaneous information exchange is operationally usable to initiate Albania-France joint cybercrime investigations even without a formal Joint Investigation Team or EU-membership framework.** Albania is not an EU member, and the action proceeded through spontaneous-exchange transmission rather than via a JIT.
- **Western-Balkan-base call-centre / Western-Europe-victim crypto-fraud pairing** is a recurring pattern in 2025–2026 documented across [[bulgaria-sofia-call-centre-investment-fraud-takedown-2025]], [[crypto-investment-fraud-spain-460m-takedown-2025]], [[ukraine-fraudulent-call-centres-takedown-2025]], [[dnipro-fraudulent-call-centre-takedown-2026]] and now this Tirana-base / France-victim case. Cooperation flows from victim-state LE to operating-state prosecutor, frequently through Eurojust.
- **Cold-wallet seizure** is a routine Tirana-base call-centre crypto-fraud outcome category. The 4-wallet seizure here parallels the cold-wallet outcomes of comparable 2025–2026 boiler-room takedowns and reinforces the wiki's pattern of cold-wallet seizure being a sustained 2026 enforcement tool against organized cryptocurrency-fraud operations.
- **Fake-victim-country-identity agent staffing** of the call centre — agents using fake French identities to contact French victims — is consistent with the language-and-identity matching staffing pattern documented in other Western-Balkan call-centre cases targeting Western European victims.
