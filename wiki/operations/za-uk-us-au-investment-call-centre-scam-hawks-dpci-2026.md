## Summary

On 2026-01-29, the **South African Government News Agency (SAnews)** reported that the **Directorate for Priority Crime Investigation ([[south-africa-hawks|DPCI / Hawks]])** had arrested **23 suspects** in Gauteng the same week for operating an alleged international investment-fraud call-centre syndicate. The National Prosecuting Authority (NPA) Gauteng, per spokesperson Phindi Mjonondwane, characterised the arrests as "the culmination of prosecutorial, internationally coordinated investigations" jointly conducted by South African and foreign law-enforcement agencies. The primary tier-1 source explicitly names three distinct LE jurisdictions cooperating: South Africa (DPCI + [[south-africa-saps|SAPS]] Crime Intelligence + Financial Intelligence Centre), the United Kingdom ([[uk-nca|National Crime Agency]]), and the United States ([[us-dhs|Department of Homeland Security]]). Likely confidence: **high** for the cooperator list and arrest count; **medium** for downstream prosecutorial outcomes (case is still pre-bail).

## Background

The investigation was opened in 2022 by the Gauteng Hawks Serious Commercial Crime Investigation team after the unit received 43 complaints from Australian victims about a South-Africa-based call-centre cluster offering bogus investment products via social-media adverts and follow-up contact on Skype, Messenger, Zoom, and WhatsApp. Victims were drawn primarily from retirement-age populations in [[united-states|US]], [[united-kingdom|UK]], [[australia|Australia]], and [[new-zealand|New Zealand]] with reported aggregate losses exceeding **R1 billion** (South African rand) across 40+ identified victims. Suspects were not registered with the South African Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) under the **Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act (FAIS Act) 37 of 2002**, which forms the statutory basis for the charges.
