## Summary

On 29-30 September 2025, the **Metropolitan Police Service** announced the conviction of **Zhimin Qian** (47, Chinese national, also known as **Yadi Zhang**) and **Seng Hok Ling** (46) at Southwark Crown Court for cryptocurrency money-laundering offences under the **Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (UK)**. The convictions follow a 7-year Met Economic Crime team investigation that began in 2018 on intelligence received about the transfer of criminal assets. The case centres on the seizure of **61,000 Bitcoin** — worth more than **£5.5 billion** by September 2025 valuations — which the Met describes as the **single largest cryptocurrency seizure made by any police force globally**.

The underlying predicate offence is a **2014-2017 large-scale investment fraud** that Qian orchestrated in China, defrauding **over 128,000 victims**. She converted the proceeds to Bitcoin, fled China using false documents, and entered the UK in 2018 to launder the proceeds via property purchases. Met surveillance of Seng Hok Ling (a Derbyshire-based associate) led to the discovery of Qian's presence in Scotland and later York; she and Ling were arrested in April 2024.

The case is operationally significant for the international-cooperation wiki because it documents **explicit Met Police–Chinese law enforcement cooperation** named verbatim in the tier-1 primary source — a relatively rare and high-value cooperation pairing for cybercrime / cyber-enabled money laundering.

## Background

Between **2014 and 2017**, Zhimin Qian orchestrated what UK authorities describe as a large-scale fraud in China, defrauding **more than 128,000 victims**. The proceeds were converted to **Bitcoin** and stored as crypto-assets. Qian then fled China using **false documents** and entered the UK. In September 2018, she attempted to launder the proceeds by **purchasing property**, with assistance from co-defendant Jian Wen.

The Met's Economic Crime team launched an inquiry in **2018** based on intelligence about the transfer of criminal assets. Met surveillance of Seng Hok Ling, a Derbyshire-based associate, led the team to Qian, who had been "evading justice for five years" (per DS Isabella Grotto's quoted statement).

## Legal Framework

The defendants pleaded guilty to offences under the UK **Proceeds of Crime Act 2002 (POCA)**:

| Defendant | Section / Offence | Plea date |
|---|---|---|
| Zhimin Qian | POCA 2002 — acquired criminal property (cryptocurrency); possessed criminal property (cryptocurrency) | 29 September 2025 |
| Seng Hok Ling | POCA 2002 — transferred criminal property (cryptocurrency) | 30 September 2025 |
| Jian Wen (earlier co-defendant) | POCA 2002 — facilitating movement of 150 BTC wallet (worth £1.7m at the time) | Sentenced 22 May 2024 (6 years 8 months) |

POCA 2002 enables the criminal confiscation of property that represents the proceeds of crime regardless of where the predicate offence occurred — provided a UK act of acquisition, possession, or transfer can be evidenced. The CPS announced that it "will now work to ensure, through criminal confiscation and civil proceedings, that the criminal assets remain beyond the fraudsters' reach."

## Results and Impact

- **61,000 Bitcoin** seized — believed to be the **single largest cryptocurrency seizure** in the world. Valued at **more than £5.5 billion** at September 2025 prices.
- **2 guilty pleas** (Qian + Ling) at Southwark Crown Court; **1 earlier conviction** (Jian Wen, 6y8m, 22 May 2024).
- Additional asset seizures (encrypted devices, cash, gold, further crypto) valued at **~£11 million** as of July 2025.
- "One of the largest money laundering cases in UK history and among the highest-value cryptocurrency cases globally" (Will Lyne).

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **Bilateral evidence sharing** between Met Police and Chinese law enforcement teams in Tianjin and Beijing — sustained over multiple years. The Met characterized this as "**unprecedented cooperation with Chinese law enforcement**."
- **Domestic inter-agency coordination** within the UK: Met Police (investigation) + NCA (specialist support) + CPS (prosecution).
- The primary source does not name the specific judicial-cooperation instrument(s) used (e.g., letters rogatory, mutual legal assistance treaty request) — these should be reconstructed from CPS/court records during enrichment.

## Challenges and Friction Points

- Qian evaded justice for five years between fleeing China (using false documents) and being located in the UK by the Met.
- The investigation required **"evidence from multiple jurisdictions and the careful review of thousands of documents"** (DS Isabella Grotto).
- UK–China LE cooperation on cybercrime / cyber-enabled fraud is historically rare; the Met's own framing of "unprecedented cooperation" reflects this.

## Lessons Learned

- Long-tail (7-year) financial investigations into laundering of **foreign predicate fraud** can yield very large cryptocurrency confiscations when the laundering-jurisdiction LE invests sustained resources and obtains evidence from the predicate jurisdiction.
- **POCA 2002 (UK)** remains effective for criminal confiscation of cryptocurrency even when the predicate offence is committed entirely abroad, provided UK acts (acquisition/possession/transfer) can be evidenced.
- The case is a notable precedent for **UK–China LE cooperation on cyber-enabled fraud**, despite the political-diplomatic friction that historically constrains such cooperation.

## Follow-Up Actions

- **Sentencing** for Qian and Ling: 10–11 November 2025 at Southwark Crown Court.
- The CPS announced it will pursue **criminal confiscation + civil recovery** proceedings to ensure the seized assets remain beyond the fraudsters' reach.
