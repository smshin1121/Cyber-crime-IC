## Summary

Operation Shrouded Horizon was the FBI-led takedown of Darkode, an invitation-only English-language cybercrime forum used to trade malware, botnet access, stolen personally identifiable information, credit-card data, hacked-server credentials, spam infrastructure and related criminal services. The public action was announced on 2015-07-15 after an 18-month FBI infiltration of the forum.

The operation was coordinated with Europol's European Cybercrime Centre and law-enforcement partners in 19 other countries. Official DOJ and FBI releases describe actions involving about 70 Darkode members and associates worldwide, including charges, arrests, searches and seizures, and U.S. charging documents against 12 defendants.

## Background

Darkode functioned as a vetted criminal marketplace rather than an open discussion board. Prospective members needed sponsorship from an existing member and were expected to demonstrate criminal capability or useful services before admission. FBI and DOJ source material describes a membership base of roughly 250 to 300 people.

The forum's value to offenders came from concentration: members could buy or trade malware, botnets, spam services, compromised credentials, stolen data and exploit capabilities in one closed venue. DOJ characterized Darkode as one of the most serious criminal hacking forums affecting data and computer integrity in the United States and abroad.

The case is analytically important because the public record identifies a covert-access method at the forum level: the FBI infiltrated Darkode's membership and used that access to collect intelligence and evidence before the coordinated action day.

## Participating Parties

**Lead and coordination**

- FBI (United States)
- U.S. Department of Justice, including the Western District of Pennsylvania and the Criminal Division's Computer Crime and Intellectual Property Section
- Europol European Cybercrime Centre (EC3)
- National Cyber-Forensics & Training Alliance (NCFTA)

**Participating countries named by DOJ**

United States, United Kingdom, Sweden, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Romania, Serbia, Latvia, Cyprus, Australia, Nigeria, Denmark, Canada, Colombia, Costa Rica, Croatia, Finland, Germany, Israel, Macedonia/North Macedonia and Brazil.

## Results and Impact

- Darkode's web domain and servers were seized, removing the forum as a live criminal marketplace.
- Law-enforcement actions in 20 countries covered about 70 Darkode members and associates. The official formulation is broader than arrests alone: it includes charges, arrests, searches and seizures.
- The United States announced charges against 12 defendants linked to the forum. The charged conduct included computer-fraud conspiracy, wire-fraud conspiracy, money-laundering conspiracy, spam, identity theft, racketeering, bank fraud, access-device fraud and malware distribution.
- DOJ named separate U.S. venues for portions of the case, including the Western District of Pennsylvania, Eastern District of Wisconsin, District of Columbia, Western District of Louisiana and Northern District of Georgia.
- The action disrupted a service ecosystem that connected malware developers, botnet operators, spammers, credential traffickers and buyers of compromised infrastructure.

## Defendant and Case Structure

The DOJ release identifies several defendant clusters rather than a single monolithic indictment:

- Western District of Pennsylvania: Johan Anders Gudmunds, Morgan C. Culbertson, Eric L. Crocker, Naveed Ahmed, Phillip R. Fleitz, Dewayne Watts and Murtaza Saifuddin were associated with charges involving computer fraud, wire fraud, money laundering, malicious code, spam and identity-theft conduct.
- Eastern District of Wisconsin: Daniel Placek was tied to the creation of the Darkode forum and alleged malware activity.
- District of Columbia: Matjaz Skorjanc, Florencio Carro Ruiz and Mentor Leniqi were linked to racketeering, wire/bank fraud, computer-fraud, access-device and extortion allegations involving malware known as ButterFly.
- Western District of Louisiana: Rory Stephen Guidry was accused of selling botnets on Darkode.
- Northern District of Georgia: the SpyEye-related Panin/Bendelladj matter was described as related because SpyEye was advertised to Darkode members.

This structure matters for the wiki model: Operation Shrouded Horizon is the operation-level takedown; defendant-specific pleas, convictions, sentencings or extraditions should be linked as follow-on case records rather than counted as new operations unless a later source describes a distinct coordinated action.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

Publicly documented mechanisms include:

- FBI undercover infiltration of Darkode's membership over approximately 18 months.
- Coordinated action by law-enforcement authorities in 20 countries.
- FBI information sharing with foreign partners so they could pursue actions against Darkode actors in their own jurisdictions.
- Europol EC3 assistance for cross-border law-enforcement coordination.
- Domestic U.S. search warrants and charging documents.
- Public-private support through the Pittsburgh-based NCFTA.

The public sources do not identify a specific treaty instrument for every foreign action. The page therefore records the cooperation model as joint investigation and information sharing, while keeping the precise cross-border legal basis as an unresolved field.

## Operational Timeline

- 2014-01 approx.: FBI infiltration and evidence collection against Darkode began, based on the 18-month period reported in source material.
- 2015-07-01: Wired reported that law-enforcement activity against Darkode was underway.
- 2015-07-15: DOJ and FBI publicly announced the takedown, U.S. charges and the 20-country action.
- 2015-07-15 onward: follow-on prosecutions proceeded through multiple U.S. districts and foreign jurisdictions.
