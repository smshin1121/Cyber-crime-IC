## Summary

On **2025-06-16**, the **Australian Federal Police (AFP)** — under its **Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3) Operation Firestorm** — and the **Royal Thai Police (RTP)** executed search warrants at a six-bedroom residential property on the outskirts of Bangkok operating as an investment scam 'boiler room'. The operation produced **13 arrests** across **four nationalities** (5 Australian, 6 UK, 1 Canadian, 1 South African), all charged under **Section 210 of the Thai Criminal Code** (conspiracy to commit an offence; max 5 years' imprisonment). This was the **first Thailand takedown** under Operation Firestorm, with three prior takedowns in Manila.

## Background

The boiler room had operated from **April 2025** selling **fake high-yield fixed-income bonds** to Australian victims. The scammers presented themselves as representatives of international financial firms, used scripted high-pressure sales tactics, presented forged documentation, and fraudulently misappropriated an **authentic financial services licence**. In an unprecedented move, the boiler room workers carried out **face-to-face online video meetings** to build victim trust. Victim funds were laundered through **cryptocurrency** before moving to other accounts.

## Results and Impact

- **13 men arrested** (5 AU, 6 UK, 1 CA, 1 ZA), all charged under Section 210 Thai Criminal Code.
- **Six-bedroom Bangkok-suburb residential property** taken offline.
- **AUD 1,900,000+** in suspected stolen funds amassed in two months.
- **Australian victim cohort** targeted via fake high-yield fixed-income bond pitches.
- **Items seized**: dozens of mobile phones and laptops, scam scripts, a digital spreadsheet with thousands of Australian target names.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

The cited release describes three discrete IC mechanism classes:

1. **AFP-led Joint Policing Cybercrime Coordination Centre (JPC3) Operation Firestorm** — long-running cybercrime cooperation framework launched August 2024 to disrupt offshore scam centres deceiving Australians. First wiki record of this framework as a discrete IC mechanism class.
2. **AFP International Network** — discrete cross-jurisdictional alerting channel that surfaced credible information from Australian sources to the RTP. Structurally complementary to the NCA International Liaison Officer network and the FBI Legal Attaché network documented elsewhere in the wiki.
3. **AFP-RTP bilateral cooperation** — direct intelligence-sharing on suspects, locations, and operational planning between AFP and RTP, executed under Thai domestic warrant authority on the action day.
