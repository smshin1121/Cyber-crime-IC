## Summary

Jack R. Dove III's March 30, 2021 guilty plea was a U.S. follow-on prosecution arising from the Welcome To Video international takedown. The collected references contain substantially more detail than the earlier auto-generated overview: they identify the defendant, the account/payment evidence, the foreign server seizure, and the multi-agency international investigation path.

This page remains a follow-on operation record rather than the canonical global Welcome To Video operation. Its IC significance is that foreign server evidence and cryptocurrency tracing from the broader takedown supported a later U.S. district prosecution.

## Key Facts

- Jack R. Dove III of Lakeland, Florida pleaded guilty on March 30, 2021 to receiving and possessing CSAM.
- Dove had been indicted on January 29, 2019 in the Middle District of Florida.
- The DOJ release states that Dove used Bitcoin to buy access or points on the Welcome To Video darknet marketplace.
- Korean National Police seized the Welcome To Video server in South Korea in March 2018; server analysis linked Dove to multiple accounts.
- ICE-HSI's broader Welcome To Video release reports 38-country lead dissemination, 337 arrests worldwide, and rescue outcomes tied to the wider operation.

## International Cooperation Path

The public sources identify a layered cooperation model. Korean National Police seized the operational server in South Korea. IRS-CI, HSI, the UK National Crime Agency, and Korean National Police led or coordinated the international investigation. Data from the seized server was shared with law enforcement partners worldwide, producing leads for domestic prosecutions, including the Florida case against Dove.

The Dove plea therefore should not be represented as a bare domestic prosecution. The court event occurred in the United States, but the evidence path and investigative context are tied to a multinational darknet-market takedown.

## Evidence and Attribution
