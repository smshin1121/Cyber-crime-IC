## Summary

On **25 June 2025**, the [[spain-guardia-civil|Spanish Guardia Civil]], with the support of [[europol-ec3|Europol]] and law enforcement partners from **Estonia, France, and the United States**, dismantled a cryptocurrency investment fraud network operating from Spain. The action day produced **5 arrests and 5 searches** (3 on the Canary Islands and 2 in Madrid). The cited Europol release attributes **EUR 460 million** in laundered illicit profits to the network and identifies more than **5,000 victims worldwide**. Europol began supporting the investigation in **2023**; the investigation is described as ongoing as of the 2025-06-30 release.

The criminal organisation allegedly used a network of associates spread around the world to raise funds through cash withdrawals, bank transfers, and crypto-transfers, while routing fund-laundering operations through a **corporate-and-banking layer based in Hong Kong** that used payment gateways and user accounts in different exchanges in the names of third parties.

## Background

The Europol release does not state when the underlying fraud activity began, what specific crypto investment fraud typology was used (pig-butchering, fake exchange, romance-baiting, or otherwise), or how the criminal network's clients were initially recruited. What is documented is the scale figure (EUR 460 million / 5,000+ victims), the laundering pivot (Hong Kong corporate-and-banking layer with third-party-named exchange accounts), and the ~2-year Europol investigative support window (2023 → 2025-06-25 action day).

## Legal Framework

The cited Europol release does **not** identify a formal **Joint Investigation Team (JIT)**, **European Investigation Order (EIO)**, or named **MLAT** instrument as the cooperation legal basis. The cooperation is described as Europol-coordinated (since 2023), with Europol providing operational coordination, strategic analysis, and on-day crypto specialist support. Cross-border investigative steps with Estonia, France (New Caledonia), and the US (HSI) are described in the release without an explicit formal-instrument label.

> [!note] Legal-basis specificity
> The absence of an explicit JIT/EIO/MLAT designation in the public release is itself an IC-relevant data point: Europol-EFECC-coordinated multi-jurisdiction crypto fraud takedowns can proceed under informal investigative cooperation plus Europol coordination services without a publicly named formal instrument. This is consistent with other recent Europol financial crime actions in the corpus.

## Results and Impact

- **5 arrests** (3 on the Canary Islands, 2 in Madrid)
- **5 searches** (3 on the Canary Islands, 2 in Madrid)
- **EUR 460 million** in laundered proceeds attributed to the network across the investigation
- **5,000+ victims worldwide** identified

The Europol release does **not** publish action-day asset-seizure figures (no euro or crypto-seizure totals are stated for the 25 June operation itself), and does **not** enumerate post-arrest indictment counts.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **Europol coordination** — EFECC-led operational coordination, strategic analysis, on-day crypto specialist deployment.
- **Bilateral/multilateral investigative cooperation** — Spain × Estonia × France (New Caledonia gendarmerie) × USA (HSI), without a publicly named formal JIT/EIO/MLAT instrument.
- **Public-private cooperation** — not enumerated in the release; no private-sector partner is named.

## Challenges and Friction Points

The Europol release does not enumerate friction points. Two structural challenges are inferable from the published facts:

1. **Hong Kong layer** — the alleged corporate-and-banking pivot in Hong Kong is not part of the action-day cooperation chain. Whether further cooperation with Hong Kong authorities is contemplated is not stated. Hong Kong is **not** named as a participating jurisdiction in the cited release; we do not assert otherwise here (per LESSONS L19).
2. **Cross-territory French participation** — the French partner is the **New Caledonia** National Gendarmerie, an overseas-territory unit. The case file does not explain why New Caledonia (rather than mainland France) was the operationally relevant French unit, but the geographic profile (Asia-Pacific time zone, US-EU bridging) suggests the New Caledonia connection may relate to victim-side or money-flow facts.

## Lessons Learned

- Four-jurisdiction crypto investment fraud cooperation can proceed under Europol-EFECC coordination without a publicly named formal JIT/MLAT, on a 2-year investigation-support timeline.
- HSI's participation as the US investigative partner — rather than FBI or DOJ — is the IC-relevant US-agency data point for this case-class. HSI's financial-crime and cross-border investigative mandate is the operational rationale.
- The Hong Kong corporate-and-banking layer is a recurrent laundering pattern in EU-coordinated crypto fraud takedowns; it appears in the public narrative without formal participation by the named jurisdiction.

## Follow-Up Actions

The Europol release states the investigation **is ongoing**. Follow-up indictments, defendant identities, and any further arrests are not publicly enumerated as of the 2025-06-30 release.
