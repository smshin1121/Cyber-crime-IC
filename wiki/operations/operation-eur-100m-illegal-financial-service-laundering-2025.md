## Summary

An international investigation supported by [[eurojust|Eurojust]] and [[europol-ec3|Europol]] dismantled a laundering service that moved approximately EUR 100 million for other criminal organisations. The operation linked [[spain]], [[cyprus]], [[germany]] and [[france]] through a JIT and culminated in arrests, searches, cash seizures and cryptocurrency freezes.

## Background

Eurojust reported that the scheme began to surface in 2023 after suspicious trips carrying large sums of cash were identified in Spain. The network used Cyprus as a key laundering node, transported cash across borders and relied on cryptocurrency to shift proceeds between criminal clients and laundering facilitators.

## Participating Parties

- Coordination: [[eurojust|Eurojust]]
- Operational support: [[europol-ec3|Europol]]
- Core participating authorities: Spain, Cyprus, Germany
- Supporting French judicial and anti-fraud authorities

## Cooperation Mechanisms

- Joint Investigation Team hosted at Eurojust

## Results and Impact

- 23 arrests
- 91 searches
- EUR 8 million in cash seized
- EUR 2 million in bank accounts seized
- EUR 27 million in cryptocurrency frozen
