## Summary

**Operation Cyber Guardian 2026** is the second iteration of the Asian regional joint enforcement campaign against online child sexual exploitation, conducted from **23 March 2026 through 17 April 2026** across **seven jurisdictions**: [[japan]], [[singapore]], [[thailand]], [[south-korea]], [[hong-kong]], [[brunei]], and [[malaysia]]. It expands on the 2025 [[operation-cyber-guardian|first-edition Cyber Guardian]] (six countries) by adding Brunei.

The [[japan-npa|Japan National Police Agency]] announced the multilateral results on 27 April 2026, reporting **445 suspects investigated** in total, **382 search-and-seizure locations** across the seven jurisdictions, and an aggregate haul of digital devices including 116 PCs, 340 mobile phones, and 140 external storage media. Japan alone accounted for **99 arrests** at 131 locations. The Malaysian Royal Police (PDRM) announced **69 arrests** in its portion of the campaign, with **204,934 CSAM files** plus **293,756 adult-pornography files** seized.

## Background

The 2025 Operation Cyber Guardian established a regional CSAM-enforcement coordination model among six Asian jurisdictions. The Japan NPA explicitly framed the 2026 campaign period as a "Period of Strengthened International Cooperation for the Eradication of Child Pornography" (児童ポルノ撲滅に関する国際協力強化期間), institutionalizing the cooperation as a standing annual exercise.

The Japan NPA published the consolidated multilateral results, but framed itself as one of seven peer participants, not a coordinator.

## Legal Framework

Each participating jurisdiction acted under its own domestic CSAM-enforcement statutes (e.g., Japan's Act on Punishment of Activities Relating to Child Prostitution and Child Pornography). The cross-border cooperation rests on **informal police-to-police cooperation** rather than treaty-based MLA, with mutual real-time information sharing among the 7 partner agencies during the enforcement window.

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **Informal police-to-police cooperation** ([[informal-cooperation]]) — the principal mechanism, with mutual information-sharing during the operation window
- Standing bilateral relationships between regional cyber-crime units (carryover from the 2025 edition's working framework)
- Each jurisdiction conducted its own arrests, searches, and prosecutions under domestic law

## Challenges and Friction Points

- The press materials do not name a single coordinating body — the operation is peer-coordinated, which can complicate after-action reporting and consistency of data points across the 7 jurisdictions
- Multilateral seizure totals from Japan NPA (382 locations, 445 suspects) and the country-by-country breakdowns publicly reported (Japan 131/99 + Malaysia ?/69) leave a gap of roughly 251 locations / 277 suspects spread across 5 other jurisdictions whose individual disclosures were not consolidated as of the ingest date

## Lessons Learned

- The Cyber Guardian framework appears to be becoming an annual standing campaign — likely highly likely (80-95%) given the 2025→2026 continuity and Japan NPA's explicit "strengthened cooperation period" framing
- Adding Brunei in 2026 demonstrates incremental expansion of the regional model

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- Country-by-country breakdown of the 445 suspects and 382 search locations remains incomplete — only Japan (99 arrests / 131 locations) and Malaysia (69 arrests) have published per-country figures.
- The relationship to the 2025 6-country edition is not explicitly described as a "second annual" iteration in the NPA PDF — but the structural continuity (same 6 countries + Brunei, same operation name, same operational logic) makes that interpretation **highly likely**.
