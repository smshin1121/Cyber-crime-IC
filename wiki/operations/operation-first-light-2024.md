## Summary

Operation First Light 2024 was an INTERPOL-coordinated global crackdown on online scams conducted from March to May 2024 across **61 countries**. The operation resulted in **3,950 arrests**, identification of **14,643 additional suspects**, freezing of **6,745 bank accounts**, and seizure of assets worth **USD 257 million**. Bolstered by AFRIPOL, ASEANAPOL, GCCPOL, and Europol.

## Background

Operation First Light is a longstanding INTERPOL operation series originally focused on phone scams and telecom fraud. The 2024 iteration expanded to cover all forms of online scams including phishing, investment fraud, fake online shopping, romance scams, and impersonation scams.

## Participating Parties

- **Coordination:** [[interpol-igci|INTERPOL IGCI]]
- **Regional partners:** [[afripol|AFRIPOL]], ASEANAPOL, GCCPOL, [[europol-ec3|Europol]]
- **61 countries** participating globally

## Results and Impact

- **3,950 arrests** across 61 countries
- **14,643 additional suspects** identified
- **USD 257 million** seized:
  - USD 135 million in fiat currency
  - USD 2 million in cryptocurrency
  - ~USD 120 million in physical assets (real estate, luxury cars, jewelry)
- **6,745 bank accounts** frozen

### Notable Cases

- **Namibia:** 88 youths rescued from forced scam operations; 163 computers and 350 mobile phones seized
- **Spain:** USD 331,000 intercepted in BEC fraud via INTERPOL's I-GRIP mechanism
- **Australia:** AUD 5.5 million recovered for impersonation scam victim

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **I-GRIP (Global Rapid Intervention of Payments):** INTERPOL's mechanism for rapid cross-border fund recovery, demonstrated in the Spain BEC interception case
- Regional policing body coordination (AFRIPOL, ASEANAPOL, GCCPOL, Europol)
