## Summary

On **2025-12-23** the Spanish National Police ([[spanish-national-police|Policía Nacional]]) announced a Spain–Argentina coordinated detention against an international CSAM-producer / CSAM-distributor cell. The Spanish leg, led by the Policía Nacional **Unidad Central de Ciberdelincuencia (UCC)** with the **Brigada Provincial de Policía Judicial de Alicante**, opened from an [[ncmec|NCMEC]]-tipped intelligence pipeline and arrested a Spanish national in Alicante province who used cloud storage services and instant-messaging applications to store and exchange large volumes of CSAM. Forensic analysis of his devices recovered "ingente cantidad de archivos de pornografía infantil," including newly-created CSAM allegedly depicting a minor to whom an Argentine counterpart claimed personal access.

Spain's UCC then activated the [[informal-cooperation|AMERIPOL hemispheric police-cooperation mechanism]] urgently via the Spanish National Police liaison officer at the AMERIPOL **Centro Especializado en Cibercrimen (AC3)**. Argentine police — specifically the **Dirección de Investigaciones Cibercrimen de la Policía de la Provincia de Buenos Aires** — identified, located, and arrested the second suspect in the town of **Claypole (Buenos Aires province)**, with a search of his residence. Two arrests resulted from this bilateral action; the investigation is reported as ongoing internationally.

## Background

The Policía Nacional UCC maintains an upstream international-cooperation channel with the [[ncmec|National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC)]], which collects CSAM-related reports from internet-content industry partners and routes leads to the LE agencies of involved countries. From NCMEC-derived information, Spanish investigators identified a user inside Spain who was storing large volumes of CSAM in remote cloud servers — a typology described by the Policía Nacional as "muy común entre pedófilos de todo el mundo" who use cloud platforms to access, share, or simply archive digital CSAM.

This domestic lead matured into the Alicante arrest, after which forensic analysis of the seized devices revealed cross-border exchange chats with a counterpart in Buenos Aires province, Argentina — including images that the UCC's intelligence team classified as **newly-created CSAM** depicting a minor to whom the Argentine counterpart claimed personal access. That assessment converted the case from a CSAM-distribution investigation into a CSAM-production investigation requiring **urgent** transnational identification — the operational trigger that activated AMERIPOL/AC3.

- **Spain — Policía Nacional ([[spanish-national-police|Spanish National Police]])** — lead investigative agency. Acting units: Unidad Central de Ciberdelincuencia (UCC); Brigada Provincial de Policía Judicial de Alicante.
- **Argentina — Dirección de Investigaciones Cibercrimen de la Policía de la Provincia de Buenos Aires** (Buenos Aires Provincial Police Cybercrime Investigation Directorate) — Argentine LE counterpart that identified, located, and arrested the Argentine subject in Claypole. - **[[ncmec|NCMEC]]** — US-based public-private partner; originating CyberTipline-style lead pipeline that surfaced the Spanish suspect to the Policía Nacional UCC.

## Legal Framework

The operation is framed in the Policía Nacional release as a **bilateral police-to-police investigation** routed through the AMERIPOL hemispheric cooperation mechanism. The release does not cite a formal Mutual Legal Assistance Treaty instrument, a Joint Investigation Team, or a specific Spanish or Argentine judicial-control identifier. The named legal-cooperation channels are:

- The AMERIPOL "mecanismos de cooperación internacional" activated **urgently** through AC3, with the Spanish National Police liaison officer at AC3 as the routing point;
- The "estrecha cooperación internacional" between **la Policía Judicial de la policía Nacional de España y la Dirección de Investigaciones Cibercrimen de la Policía de la Provincia de Buenos Aires** — described in the release as the operational frame within which the Argentine investigation, identification, location, and arrest were conducted;
- Pre-existing public-private cooperation between the Policía Nacional UCC and [[ncmec|NCMEC]] for CSAM tip-line referral.

"Centro Especializado en Cibercrimen (AC3)" is preserved in original Spanish; AC3 is the AMERIPOL specialised cybercrime centre and serves as the operational nucleus for cross-border cybercrime cooperation among AMERIPOL member police forces.

## Results and Impact

- **2 arrests:** 1 in Alicante province (Spain), 1 in Claypole / Buenos Aires province (Argentina).
- **2 domiciliary searches** (one per arrest), with seizure of computing material in the Spanish leg.
- **Newly-created CSAM** allegedly depicting a minor identified as the operational trigger for the urgent AMERIPOL escalation.
- **A central part of the criminal structure** is described as "desarticulada" (dismantled) by the two arrests, but the criminal organisation's wider European and South-American membership remains under investigation.

The primary release does not publish device counts, storage volumes, victim counts beyond the single minor referenced in the production trigger, or charging detail.

## Challenges and Friction Points

The primary release does not enumerate friction points. The case is presented as a successful application of AMERIPOL's "rapid response" cooperation protocols, in which the urgent identification request crossed Atlantic jurisdictions before evidence destruction could occur. The implicit operational tension — newly-created CSAM, a minor with claimed in-person access, and a transatlantic suspect chain — is exactly the use case AMERIPOL/AC3 was designed for, and is presented as the justification for the urgent escalation.

## Lessons Learned

- AMERIPOL/AC3 + Spanish National Police liaison officer is, per this primary release, a **functional and operationally-fast** police-to-police channel for urgent CSAM identifications between EU and Latin American jurisdictions.
- The NCMEC → UCC → AMERIPOL/AC3 → counterpart-country LE escalation chain is the canonical multi-step transatlantic pattern for CSAM cases in this corpus.
- Per L24, this operation's `participating_countries` is **strictly bilateral (Spain + Argentina)**. The Policía Nacional release attributes broader "ramificaciones operativas" across Europe and South America to the criminal organisation, but those wider jurisdictions are body-prose attribution and not cooperating LE counterparts. NCMEC is recorded as a public-private partner, not as a participating LE jurisdiction.
