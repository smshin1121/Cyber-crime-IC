## Summary

The Spanish pay-TV streaming network takedown was a June 2020 Eurojust-supported action against a criminal organisation selling illegal access to subscription television channels, streaming services, and films. The source reports that the group operated mainly from Spain and used websites and servers in the EU and third countries.

The action produced 11 arrests in Spain, Denmark, Sweden, and Germany, and took down 50 servers in France, Germany, the Netherlands, Belgium, Denmark, Sweden, the Czech Republic, Poland, and Spain. Europol provided analytical support and participated with Eurojust in operational preparation.

## Results and Impact

- 11 suspects were arrested.
- 50 servers were taken down.
- The network had an estimated 2 million subscribers worldwide.
- Spanish reporting described approximately 1,000 websites and more than 40,000 channels, series, and platform feeds.
- Authorities seized assets with a reported value of about EUR 4.8 million and froze about EUR 1.1 million.
