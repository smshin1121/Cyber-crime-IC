## Summary

**Operation PATHFINDER** is a successful bilateral [[czech-republic|Czech]]–[[ukraine|Ukrainian]] [[joint-investigation-team|joint investigation team]] cooperation announced by the Czech [[czechia-police|National Centre against Terrorism, Extremism and Cybercrime (NCTEKK)]] on **2025-05-13**. The operation, supported under the [[eurojust|Eurojust]] JIT framework, ran for approximately 6 months and culminated in action days from **2025-04-29 to 2025-05-02** on Ukrainian territory, with a 4-officer NCTEKK on-site team participating directly in Ukrainian search-and-seizure operations alongside the [[ukraine-sbu|Security Service of Ukraine (SBU)]].

The operation dismantled an organised criminal group operating from a heavily secured **2-floor call-centre in Ukraine's Zakarpattia oblast**, which ran sophisticated fake online investment platforms targeting victims in the Czech Republic and other European countries. The OCG used graphical sophistication on its fraudulent platforms to impersonate legitimate investment domains, deceived victims into installing remote-access applications, and laundered defrauded funds through chains of bank accounts that were converted to cryptocurrency.

**Action-day results: 70 individuals detained, 4 main organizers charged** under Ukrainian Criminal Code Article 190 paragraph 5 (fraud on a particularly large scale, up to 12 years' imprisonment plus asset forfeiture) and remanded in custody. Hundreds of pieces of computer and communication equipment, computers, data carriers, and telephones were seized. **Czech-side damage: at least 9 million CZK** across at least 37 identified Czech victims; **total EU damage** estimated in millions of EUR pending forensic analysis of the seized data.

## Background

Operation PATHFINDER followed approximately 6 months of intensive Czech–Ukrainian cooperation, with the formal cooperation framework established as a Eurojust-supported Joint Investigation Team. The Czech procedural lead was the District State Prosecutor's Office in Hradec Králové (*Okresní státní zastupitelství v Hradci Králové*), supervising NCTEKK's Cybercrime Department. The Ukrainian procedural lead was the Zakarpattia regional prosecutor's office (*krajské prokuratury*), supervising SBU operations.

The OCG's call-centre infrastructure illustrates the institutionalized scale of fake-investment OCGs operating from Ukrainian territory against EU victims. The call-centre occupied **two floors of an administrative building** in Zakarpattia oblast, included a **dedicated server room**, and was protected by **physical security guards, an electronic alarm system, and dozens of CCTV cameras** — a configuration consistent with industrial-scale criminal call-centre operations recorded elsewhere in the wiki's coverage of Ukraine-hosted fraud OCGs.

In-house IT specialists at the call-centre simultaneously produced **fake investment advertisements** designed to drive new victims to the OCG's fake investment platforms — a vertically integrated victim-acquisition + victim-defrauding model.

## Participating Parties

- **Lead agency (Czech side):** Czech NCTEKK — Národní centrála proti terorismu, extremismu a kybernetické kriminalitě (National Centre against Terrorism, Extremism and Cybercrime), Cybercrime Department, of the Criminal Police and Investigation Service of the [[czech-police|Police of the Czech Republic]].
- **Lead agency (Ukrainian side):** Security Service of Ukraine ([[ukraine-sbu|SBU]]), under procedural supervision of the regional prosecutor's office in Zakarpattia oblast.
- **Coordinating body:** [[eurojust|Eurojust]] — provided the JIT framework that formalized the bilateral cooperation.
- **Czech prosecutorial supervision:** District State Prosecutor's Office in Hradec Králové (*Okresní státní zastupitelství v Hradci Králové*).
- **On-site Czech team during action days:** 4-officer NCTEKK Cybercrime Department team participated directly in Ukrainian search-and-seizure operations.

> [!note] Europol involvement
> The 2025-05-13 Czech NCTEKK release names Eurojust as the sole coordinating body and does not explicitly identify [[europol-ec3|Europol]] involvement. This distinguishes PATHFINDER from typical Europol+Eurojust dual-coordination patterns recorded elsewhere in the wiki for cybercrime call-centre takedowns.

## Legal Framework

- **Ukrainian Criminal Code Article 190 paragraph 5** — fraud on a particularly large scale; the substantive offence used to charge the four main organizers; carries up to **12 years' imprisonment plus asset forfeiture**.
- **Eurojust Joint Investigation Team (JIT) framework** — the procedural mechanism that enabled real-time evidence exchange and coordinated action-day execution between Czech NCTEKK and Ukrainian SBU.
- The Czech-side proceedings operate under the supervision of the District State Prosecutor's Office in Hradec Králové; the Ukrainian-side proceedings are supervised by the Zakarpattia regional prosecutor's office.

## Operational Timeline

- **2024-11 (approximate):** Czech–Ukrainian Eurojust JIT cooperation begins (the 2025-05-13 release describes "6 months of intensive international cooperation" leading up to the action days).
- **2025-04-29 to 2025-05-02:** Action days on Ukrainian territory. House searches across multiple addresses in Zakarpattia oblast. 70 individuals detained, including 4 main organizers. Hundreds of items of computing and communication equipment seized. The 4-officer NCTEKK team participates directly on-site.
- **2025-05-13:** Czech NCTEKK publishes the press release announcing the successful conclusion of the action-day phase, with the 4 main organizers already charged under Ukrainian Criminal Code § 190(5) and remanded in custody. Further investigative actions described as "ongoing".

## Cooperation Mechanisms Used

- **Eurojust [[joint-investigation-team|Joint Investigation Team]]** as the formal procedural framework — enabled real-time evidence exchange between Czech NCTEKK and Ukrainian SBU and coordinated cross-border action-day execution.
- **On-site participation of foreign officers**: a 4-officer Czech NCTEKK Cybercrime Department team participated directly in Ukrainian search-and-seizure operations during the 2025-04-29 to 2025-05-02 action days — a notable IC feature of this operation.
- **Bilateral prosecutorial coordination** between Czech (Hradec Králové District) and Ukrainian (Zakarpattia regional) prosecutors, structured under the Eurojust JIT.

## Challenges and Friction Points

The 2025-05-13 NCTEKK release does not enumerate operational friction points. The release notes that **final EU-wide damage figures are not yet known** and "will be precisely known after analysis of a large quantity of computing and communication equipment and data carriers seized during house searches" — implying a substantial post-action-day forensic-analysis workload to be performed by NCTEKK.

The release does not identify [[europol-ec3|Europol]] involvement, which is unusual for cybercrime call-centre takedowns of this scale and may reflect the bilateral (rather than multilateral) configuration of the JIT.

## Lessons Learned

- **Bilateral two-state Eurojust JIT** with on-site participation of victim-jurisdiction officers in host-jurisdiction action days is a viable, distinct IC pattern for cybercrime call-centres physically hosted in Ukraine and targeting EU-resident victims.
- **Ukrainian Criminal Code § 190(5)** (fraud, particularly large scale) is the operative substantive offence Ukrainian prosecutors use to charge OCG organizers operating call-centres on Ukrainian territory; it provides up to 12 years' imprisonment plus asset forfeiture.
- **Zakarpattia oblast** emerges in this operation as an additional western-Ukrainian call-centre cluster alongside the previously recorded eastern (Dnipro) and central (Kyiv, Ivano-Frankivsk) clusters.

## Follow-Up Actions

- Forensic analysis of hundreds of pieces of seized computing and communication equipment and data carriers — primary post-action-day workload, expected to refine the EU-wide damage figure (currently estimated "in millions of EUR").
- Further investigative actions described as "ongoing" in the 2025-05-13 release; additional charges or detentions may follow.
