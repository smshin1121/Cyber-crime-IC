## Purpose

This audit separates genuine international-cooperation operations from low-value domestic follow-on pages that were auto-generated from single-source U.S. press releases.

## Audit Rule

An operation should usually stay in the main international-cooperation catalog only if at least one of the following is true:

1. multiple countries are operationally involved, not just mentioned as victim locations
2. a cross-border mechanism is identifiable, such as a JIT, EIO, MLA, extradition, Europol, Eurojust, or an official bilateral action day
3. the page captures a genuinely multinational disruption, prosecution, or coordinated seizure sequence

Pages should usually be downgraded or removed from the operations catalog when they are:

- domestic sentencing-only follow-on actions with no visible cross-border mechanism
- duplicate operation pages created from the same underlying case at indictment, plea, and sentencing stages
- domestic criminal cases whose only international feature is a vague geography mention with no cooperation evidence

## Notes

- A domestic prosecution can still remain as a `case` page without needing an `operation` page in the international-cooperation catalog.
- Cross-border victimization alone is weaker than documented cross-border investigative or judicial cooperation.
