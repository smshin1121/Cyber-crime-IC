## Summary

Online fraud encompasses a broad spectrum of cyber-enabled financial crimes, including voice phishing (보이스피싱), romance scams, business email compromise (BEC), investment fraud, online gambling fraud, and e-commerce fraud. From an international cooperation perspective, online fraud is *almost certainly* the highest-volume cybercrime type requiring cross-border cooperation, affecting millions of victims annually across all regions.

INTERPOL's HAECHI series of operations has been the primary international enforcement framework for online fraud, with [[operation-haechi-v|Operation HAECHI V]] (2024) setting records: **5,500+ arrests** and **USD 400 million+** seized across **40 countries**.

## Criminalization Landscape

Online fraud is broadly criminalized worldwide under various statutes:
- **Fraud/deception statutes:** Most jurisdictions have general fraud provisions covering online variants
- **Cybercrime statutes:** Many jurisdictions have specific computer fraud provisions
- **Financial crime statutes:** Money laundering and proceeds-of-crime provisions frequently apply

## Typical IC Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Large-Scale Multi-Country Enforcement Sweep
**Pattern:** INTERPOL-coordinated operations targeting multiple fraud types simultaneously across many countries.
**Example:** [[operation-haechi-v|Operation HAECHI V]] — 40 countries, 5,500+ arrests, USD 400M+ seized

### Scenario 2: Regional Enforcement Operation
**Pattern:** International-regional partnership targeting fraud networks in a specific region.
**Example:** [[operation-serengeti|Operation Serengeti]] — INTERPOL-AFRIPOL, 19 African countries, 1,006 arrests

## Korean Context (한국 상황)

Korea is *almost certainly* one of the most heavily affected countries by voice phishing (보이스피싱), which has been a major law enforcement priority for the [[knpa-cyber-bureau|KNPA Cyber Bureau]]. Korea's leadership of the HAECHI series reflects this domestic priority translated into international cooperation:

- Korea led all five HAECHI operations
- In HAECHI V, Korea and China jointly dismantled a voice phishing syndicate causing **USD 1.1 billion** in losses to **1,900+ victims**
- Voice phishing schemes targeting Korean victims frequently originate from China and Southeast Asia, making international cooperation essential
