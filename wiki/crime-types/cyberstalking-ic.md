## Summary

Cyberstalking covers repeated online or electronic communications, threats, harassment, coercion, and related misuse of digital platforms against specific victims. In the international-cooperation corpus, this type is usually domestic unless offender, victim, platform evidence, or infrastructure crosses borders.

## IC Relevance

Most individual cyberstalking prosecutions are not standalone international-cooperation operations. They become relevant to IC analysis when investigators need overseas platform data, MLAT or direct provider requests, cross-border victim protection, or coordinated action against broader coercion/extortion networks.

## Classification Note

This category exists to avoid misclassifying cyberstalking cases as generic online fraud. Where a case involves demands for money, account theft, or broader scam activity, it may also overlap with [[online-fraud-ic]] or [[extortion-ic]].
