## Summary

Voice phishing (보이스피싱, vishing) is a cyber-enabled fraud technique in which criminals use telephone calls to impersonate trusted entities — prosecutors, police officers, bank officials, or government agencies — to trick victims into transferring funds. From an international cooperation perspective, voice phishing is *almost certainly* the most significant cybercrime type for **South Korea**, which has been the primary driver of international enforcement efforts through the INTERPOL HAECHI series. The crime type has evolved from China-based call centres to Southeast Asian scam compounds, requiring increasingly complex multilateral cooperation.

## Criminalization Landscape

Voice phishing is broadly criminalized under:
- **Telecommunications fraud statutes** (in jurisdictions with specific telecom crime laws)
- **General fraud provisions** (applicable in virtually all jurisdictions)
- **Cybercrime statutes** (when VoIP or internet-based calling is used)
- **Money laundering provisions** (for proceeds laundering)
- **Organized crime statutes** (for syndicate operations)

In Korea, voice phishing is prosecuted under:
- 전기통신금융사기 피해 방지 및 피해금 환급에 관한 특별법 (Special Act on Prevention of Loss from Telecommunications-Based Financial Fraud)
- 형법 Art. 347 (Fraud, 사기)
- 전자금융거래법 (Electronic Financial Transactions Act)

## Typical IC Scenarios

### Scenario 1: China-Based Call Centres Targeting Korean Victims
**Pattern:** Criminal syndicates establish call centres in Chinese cities (Qingdao, Shenyang, Dalian) staffed by Korean and Chinese nationals who call Korean victims impersonating prosecutors or bank officials.
**Example:** [[korea-china-voice-phishing-qingdao|Korea-China Qingdao Operation]] — 16 arrests, KRW 2.7 billion in losses

### Scenario 2: Southeast Asian Scam Compounds
**Pattern:** Workers (often trafficked via fake job offers) operate voice phishing and other scam operations from compounds in Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, and the Philippines.
**Example:** [[korea-cambodia-scam-repatriation|Korea-Cambodia Repatriation]] — 107+ suspects repatriated, ~1,000 Koreans estimated in Cambodian scam centres

### Scenario 3: INTERPOL Multi-Crime Sweep Including Voice Phishing
**Pattern:** Voice phishing targeted as one crime type within large-scale INTERPOL-coordinated operations.
**Example:** [[operation-haechi-iv|HAECHI IV]] (3,500 arrests), [[operation-haechi-v|HAECHI V]] (5,500+ arrests, voice phishing syndicate dismantled)

### Scenario 4: AI-Enhanced Voice Cloning
**Pattern:** Emerging use of deepfake/AI voice cloning to impersonate specific individuals.
**Example:** HAECHI IV identified AI-generated voice cloning in impersonation scams (INTERPOL Purple Notice issued)

## Geographic Pattern: The Korea-China-SEA Triangle

Voice phishing targeting Korean victims follows a distinct geographic pattern:

### Phase 1: China-Based Operations (2010s-present)
- Call centres in Chinese cities (Qingdao, Shenyang, Dalian, etc.)
- Mixed Korean/Chinese staff
- Bilateral Korea-China cooperation required
- [[korea-china-voice-phishing-qingdao|Qingdao operation]] (2023): First major joint operation under bilateral agreement
- [[operation-haechi-v|HAECHI V]] (2024): Korea-China dismantled syndicate causing USD 1.1 billion losses

### Phase 2: Southeast Asian Scam Compounds (2020s-present)
- Compounds in Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Philippines
- Workers recruited via fake job offers (trafficking)
- [[korea-cambodia-scam-repatriation|Korea-Cambodia repatriation]] (2025): 107+ suspects, ~1,000 Koreans estimated
- Concurrent US/UK sanctions against Cambodia-based Prince Group

### Phase 3: AI-Enhanced Operations (emerging)
- Deepfake voice cloning for executive impersonation
- HAECHI IV identified AI voice cloning trend
- INTERPOL Purple Notices issued

## Evidence Types and Locations

- **Call detail records (통화내역):** Held by Korean and foreign telecom providers
- **VoIP server logs:** Often located in third countries
- **Financial transaction records:** Wire transfers, cryptocurrency
- **Mobile device data:** From seized phones at scam centres
- **IP addresses:** VoIP gateway connections

## Challenges

- **Jurisdictional complexity:** Caller in China/SEA, victim in Korea, money flowing through multiple countries
- **Speed of fund movement:** Victims pressured into immediate transfers; funds moved within hours
- **Scam compound sovereignty:** Difficulty enforcing against operations in countries with weak governance
- **Trafficking intersection:** Perpetrators may also be trafficking victims, complicating prosecution
- **Volume:** Korean police estimate 1,000+ nationals in Cambodian scam centres alone
- **Language barriers:** Korean-language operations require Korean-speaking investigators in partner countries
- **Diplomatic sensitivity:** Korea-China cooperation affected by broader bilateral relations

## Korean Context (한국 상황)

Korea is *almost certainly* the country most actively driving international enforcement against voice phishing:

1. **HAECHI series leadership:** Korea initiated and leads all six HAECHI operations, which explicitly include voice phishing as a target crime type
2. **Bilateral cooperation with China:** The [[korea-china-voice-phishing-qingdao|Qingdao operation]] established precedent for Korea-China joint enforcement
3. **Cambodia repatriation:** Korea conducted the largest repatriation of scam centre suspects, demonstrating willingness to use bilateral diplomatic channels
4. **Domestic priority:** Voice phishing is consistently one of Korea's top law enforcement priorities (보이스피싱 근절 대책)
5. **Victim scale:** Korean voice phishing victims suffer annual losses estimated in the trillions of won

Key Korean agencies involved:
- [[knpa-cyber-bureau|경찰청 사이버수사국]] (KNPA Cyber Bureau) — primary investigation authority
- [[seoul-metropolitan-police|서울경찰청]] (Seoul Metropolitan Police) — conducted Qingdao bilateral operation
- Financial Supervisory Service (금융감독원) — victim prevention and fund recovery

## Prevention and Disruption

- **I-GRIP mechanism:** INTERPOL's rapid fund interception tool, demonstrated in HAECHI operations
- **Account blocking:** HAECHI IV blocked 82,112 accounts; HAECHI VI blocked 68,000+
- **Call blocking technology:** Korean telecom providers implement spam call detection
- **Public awareness campaigns:** Korean government conducts extensive anti-voice-phishing campaigns
