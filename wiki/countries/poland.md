## Summary

Poland is a European Union member and **[[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]] party**. Its cybercrime law enforcement underwent a major institutional reform in 2022 with the creation of the **Central Bureau for Combating Cybercrime** (*Centralne Biuro Zwalczania Cyberprzestępczości*, **CBZC**), a dedicated national cybercrime investigation service within the Polish Police.

Poland participates in EU cybercrime cooperation frameworks through Europol's EC3 and Eurojust.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Law

Poland's primary cybercrime provisions are contained in the **Criminal Code** (*Kodeks karny*, 1997, amended):
- Art. 267: Unauthorized access / interception
- Art. 268-268a: Data interference
- Art. 269-269b: System interference, computer sabotage

Poland's Criminal Code implements the substantive law requirements of the Budapest Convention following accession.

### Procedural Powers

Under the Code of Criminal Procedure, Polish law enforcement has access to search and seizure, production orders, data preservation, and lawful interception. *Likely* compliant with Budapest Convention procedural requirements.

### Mutual Legal Assistance

Central authority for MLAT requests is the **Ministry of Justice** (*Ministerstwo Sprawiedliwości*).
