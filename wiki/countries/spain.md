## Summary

Spain is a **key EU cybercrime cooperation partner**, with two specialised national cyber investigation bodies operating in parallel: the **Guardia Civil's Grupo de Delitos Telemáticos (GDT)** — founded 1996 as one of Europe's earliest cybercrime units — and the **Policía Nacional's Brigada Central de Investigación Tecnológica (BIT)**. Spain ratified the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]] on **3 June 2010** and modified its Criminal Code and Criminal Procedure Act in 2015 to implement Convention requirements.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Criminal Law

**Código Penal (Criminal Code):**
- Art. 197 — unlawful access to personal data / computer systems
- Art. 197 bis — illegal access to information systems (post-2015)
- Art. 197 ter — preparatory acts
- Art. 248 — computer fraud (estafa informática)
- Art. 264 — data/system interference and damage
- Art. 264 bis — system interference
- Art. 264 ter — preparatory tools

2015 reform (LO 1/2015) brought the Criminal Code into line with the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]] and EU Directive 2013/40/EU on attacks against information systems.

### Procedural Powers

**Ley de Enjuiciamiento Criminal (LECrim)** provides:
- Search and seizure of electronic evidence
- Remote search of computer systems (registro remoto)
- Communications interception (intervención de las comunicaciones)
- Data preservation and production orders

## Key Agencies

### [[spain-guardia-civil|Guardia Civil — Grupo de Delitos Telemáticos (GDT)]]
- Founded 1996 as Grupo de Delitos Informáticos
- Renamed Departamento de Delitos de Alta Tecnología, then Grupo de Delitos Telemáticos
- Investigation, forensics, R&D, intelligence

### [[spain-national-police|Cuerpo Nacional de Policía — Unidad Central de Ciberdelincuencia (UCC / BIT)]]
- Brigada Central de Investigación Tecnológica
- Parallel national cyber investigative jurisdiction

### Central Authority
- **Subdirección General de Cooperación Jurídica Internacional**, Ministerio de Justicia — MLA central authority under Budapest arts. 24 and 27
- Spain maintains **two 24/7 contact points** — one for each national force

## Cooperation Track Record

Spain is *almost certainly* a major participant in EU-coordinated cybercrime operations via Europol EC3 and Eurojust. *Likely* frequently involved in operations against Spanish-speaking cybercrime networks operating across Latin America.
