## Summary

Norway is a **non-EU but deeply integrated** European partner on cybercrime cooperation. **Kripos** (Den nasjonale enhet for bekjempelse av organisert og annen alvorlig kriminalitet) is the National Criminal Investigation Service and hosts the **National Cyber Crime Centre (NC3)**. Norway ratified the Budapest Convention in 2006 and cooperates closely with EU mechanisms via the European Economic Area (EEA) agreement and Schengen participation.

> [!note]
> Exact ratification date (2006-06-30) should be verified against the Council of Europe Treaty Office chart of signatures for CETS 185.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Criminal Law

**Straffeloven (Penal Code)** — 2005 general penal code, in force 2015:
- Section 201: Preparation to commit data espionage
- Section 204: Unauthorized access to computer systems
- Section 205: Data interference / sabotage
- Section 206: System interference
- Section 208: Misuse of devices

### Procedural Powers

Norwegian Criminal Procedure Act provides:
- Search and seizure of electronic evidence
- Production orders
- Preservation orders
- Telecommunications interception (court-ordered)
- Real-time data collection

### Mutual Legal Assistance

- **Central authority:** Ministry of Justice and Public Security
- **Operational:** Kripos serves as contact point for MLA and extradition, including in the absence of treaty
- **Schengen cooperation:** Norway participates in Schengen Information System (SIS II)

## Key Agencies

### Kripos (National Criminal Investigation Service)
See [[norway-kripos]].
- Primary investigative body for serious and organized crime
- INTERPOL NCB Norway
- Central authority for MLA and extradition in absence of other treaties
- Houses NC3

### NC3 (National Cyber Crime Centre)
See [[norway-nc3]].
- Dedicated cybercrime centre within Kripos
- Joint investigation capacity

### NSM (Nasjonal sikkerhetsmyndighet)
See [[norway-nsm]].
- National Security Authority
- National CERT (NorCERT)
