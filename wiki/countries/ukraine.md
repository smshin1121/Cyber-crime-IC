## Summary

Ukraine is a **[[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]] party** since 2005 and has been one of the most operationally active eastern European cybercrime cooperation partners. The **Cyberpolice of Ukraine** ([[ukraine-police]]) — Department of Cyber Police within the National Police of Ukraine — leads cybercrime investigations.

Despite the ongoing Russian invasion (since February 2022), Ukraine has continued to participate in multinational cybercrime operations including those targeting ransomware infrastructure. Ukrainian law enforcement has made notable arrests of suspected ransomware affiliates.

> [!caution] Operational sensitivity
> Details of active cooperation during wartime may involve operational security considerations. Information in this profile is drawn from public sources only.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Law

Ukraine's **Criminal Code** Arts. 361-363-1 criminalize unauthorized access, data interference, and distribution of malicious software [1]. Additional provisions address computer fraud and forgery.

The **Law on Cybersecurity of Ukraine** (2017) [2] established a national cybersecurity framework including responsibilities for State Service of Special Communications and Information Protection (SSSCIP).

### Procedural Powers

Ukrainian criminal procedure provides for search and seizure of electronic evidence, data preservation, production orders, and interception.

### Mutual Legal Assistance

Central authority is the **Office of the Prosecutor General** (*Офіс Генерального прокурора*).

## Treaty Memberships

- **[[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]]** — party (ratified 2005-09-10) [3]
- **[[second-additional-protocol|Second Additional Protocol]]** — signatory (2022-05-12) [3]

## Key Agencies

- **[[ukraine-police|Cyberpolice of Ukraine]]** — Department of Cyber Police, National Police of Ukraine
- **Security Service of Ukraine (SBU)** — counter-intelligence and national security cyber matters

## Cooperation Track Record

Ukraine has participated in multiple major international cybercrime operations including actions against REvil/Sodinokibi and other ransomware groups. It is *highly likely* to have strong bilateral cooperation with US and EU partners.

## Capacity Assessment

Despite wartime conditions, Ukraine maintains substantial cybercrime investigative capacity. English proficiency in specialized cyber units is high. Digital forensics capacity is well-developed.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- Operational impact of the war on specific MLAT timelines unclear
- Extent of cooperation with Russia (formally hostile state) ceased after 2014 / 2022
- Cross-border cooperation logistics may involve alternative channels during wartime
