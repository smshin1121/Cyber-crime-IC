## Summary

The Netherlands is *almost certainly* the most operationally active European country for cybercrime takedowns relative to its size. The **Dutch National Police** ([[netherlands-politie]]), through its **National High Tech Crime Unit (NHTCU)**, has been instrumental in major international operations including [[operation-endgame-phase1|Operation Endgame]], [[operation-cronos-phase1|Operation Cronos]], [[hive-ransomware-takedown|Hive Ransomware Takedown]], and [[operation-talent|Operation Talent]].

The Netherlands hosts both **Europol** and **[[eurojust]]** in The Hague, providing unique institutional proximity that *highly likely* contributes to the country's outsized role in cybercrime cooperation. The 2019 **Wet computercriminaliteit III** gave Dutch law enforcement pioneering powers for remote computer access (hackbevoegdheid), making the Netherlands a leader in innovative investigation techniques.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Criminal Law

**Wetboek van Strafrecht (Criminal Code):**
- Art. 138ab: Unauthorized access to computer systems (computervredebreuk)
- Art. 138b: Unauthorized access to automated work via public telecommunications network
- Art. 138c: Interception of data
- Art. 350a: Data manipulation (wijzigen gegevens)
- Art. 350b: Computer sabotage
- Art. 350c-d: Additional computer crime provisions

**Wet computercriminaliteit III (Computer Crime Act III, 2019):**
- Significantly expanded law enforcement powers for cybercrime investigation
- Introduced "hackbevoegdheid" — legal authority for police to remotely access computer systems
- Controversial but *likely* the most advanced police hacking authority in Europe

### Procedural Powers

- **Search and seizure:** Wetboek van Strafvordering (Code of Criminal Procedure); includes digital evidence
- **Remote access (hackbevoegdheid):** Wet computercriminaliteit III (2019); allows police to remotely access suspects' devices, install monitoring software, and copy data — requires judicial authorization
- **Interception:** Requires judicial authorization; applied to telecommunications and internet communications
- **Preservation orders:** Available under domestic and international cooperation frameworks
- **Production orders:** Can compel providers to produce subscriber and traffic data

### Mutual Legal Assistance

- **Central authority:** Ministry of Justice and Security
- **EU instruments:** European Investigation Order (EIO) for intra-EU requests; European Arrest Warrant (EAW)
- **Proximity advantage:** Physical co-location with Europol and Eurojust in The Hague facilitates rapid informal coordination before formal MLA processes
- **FIOD** ([[netherlands-fiod]]): Fiscal Intelligence and Investigation Service handles financial cybercrime MLA

## Key Agencies

### Dutch National Police (Politie) — National High Tech Crime Unit (NHTCU)
See [[netherlands-politie]] for full profile.
- Specialized cybercrime unit within the National Police
- *Almost certainly* among the top 3 national cybercrime units in Europe
- Pioneered innovative takedown techniques (e.g., "police malware" deployed to infected systems during Emotet takedown)
- Key infrastructure seizure partner in Europol operations

### FIOD (Fiscale Inlichtingen- en Opsporingsdienst)
- Fiscal Intelligence and Investigation Service
- Handles financial cybercrime, cryptocurrency tracing, money laundering investigations
- Participated in multiple international financial cybercrime operations

### Openbaar Ministerie (Public Prosecution Service)
- Specialized cybercrime prosecutors
- Close cooperation with NHTCU on international cases
- Coordinates with [[eurojust]] for cross-border judicial cooperation

## Cooperation Track Record

### As Requesting State

The Netherlands actively initiates international cooperation, particularly:
- Investigations into dark web marketplaces hosted abroad
- Ransomware investigations requiring US evidence
- Financial crime investigations with cross-border elements

### As Requested State

The Netherlands is *highly likely* one of the most responsive and proactive cooperation partners in Europe:
- High English proficiency eliminates language barriers
- Physical proximity to Europol/Eurojust enables rapid coordination
- Advanced technical capabilities facilitate complex digital evidence requests

### Infrastructure Host for Takedowns

The Netherlands has hosted critical infrastructure for major operations:
- **[[operation-endgame-phase1]]** / **[[operation-endgame-phase2]]:** Dutch police played central role in botnet takedown infrastructure
- **[[operation-cronos-phase1]]:** Key participation in LockBit infrastructure seizure
- **[[hive-ransomware-takedown]]:** Dutch police contributed to Hive infrastructure takeover
- **[[operation-talent]]:** Participation in Cracked/Nulled forum takedown

Korea-Netherlands cybercrime cooperation is *likely* conducted primarily through multilateral channels:

- **Budapest Convention:** Both parties (Netherlands since 2006, Korea since 2024), providing mutual MLA legal basis
