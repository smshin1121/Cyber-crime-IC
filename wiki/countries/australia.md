## Summary

Australia is a **highly capable** international cooperation partner and a key bridge between the **Five Eyes** intelligence alliance and the **Asia-Pacific** region. The **Australian Federal Police (AFP)** maintains one of the world's largest international liaison officer networks and has demonstrated innovative approaches to transnational cybercrime, most notably through **Operation Ironside/AN0M** — the deployment of an FBI-managed encrypted communications platform that was covertly operated to collect evidence on organized crime globally.

Australia hosts (in partnership with Singapore) the **INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation (IGCI)** in Singapore, a key hub for cyber innovation and capacity building in the Asia-Pacific region. Australia is a party to the [[budapest-convention]] and maintains an extensive network of bilateral MLATs.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Criminal Law

**Criminal Code Act 1995 (Cth), Part 10.7 — Computer Offences:**
- § 477.1: Unauthorized access to, or modification of, restricted data
- § 477.2: Unauthorized impairment of electronic communication
- § 477.3: Unauthorized impairment of data held in a computer disk/device
- § 478.1: Unauthorized access to, or modification of, restricted data (lesser offences)
- § 478.3: Possession or control of data with intent to commit a computer offence
- § 478.4: Producing, supplying or obtaining data with intent to commit a computer offence

**Cybercrime Act 2001:**
- Amended the Criminal Code to introduce Part 10.7 computer offences
- Implemented Budapest Convention obligations domestically

**Surveillance Legislation Amendment (Identify and Disrupt) Act 2021:**
- Data disruption warrants: AFP/ACIC can disrupt data on devices
- Network activity warrants: Collect intelligence on criminal networks
- Account takeover warrants: Take control of online accounts

### Procedural Powers

- **Search and seizure:** Crimes Act 1914; includes electronic evidence provisions
- **Interception:** Telecommunications (Interception and Access) Act 1979 (TIA Act); requires warrant
- **Metadata retention:** Mandatory 2-year retention by telecommunications providers (2015 amendments to TIA Act)
- **Stored communications access:** TIA Act; requires warrant for content
- **Data disruption and network activity warrants:** 2021 Act; innovative powers for proactive disruption

### Mutual Legal Assistance

- **Central authority:** Attorney-General's Department, International Crime Cooperation Central Authority
- **Mutual Assistance in Criminal Matters Act 1987:** Domestic legislation implementing MLA obligations
- **Extradition Act 1988:** Governs extradition to and from Australia
- **Australia has bilateral MLATs with 30+ countries**

## Key Agencies

### Australian Federal Police (AFP)
- Lead federal law enforcement agency; handles transnational cybercrime
- International Network: 90+ liaison officer posts in 30+ countries
- AFP-led Operation Ironside/AN0M: Landmark encrypted communications operation
- Participates in Europol JITs, INTERPOL operations, Five Eyes cooperation
- Houses INTERPOL NCB Australia

### Australian Signals Directorate (ASD) / Australian Cyber Security Centre (ACSC)
- Signals intelligence and offensive/defensive cyber capabilities
- ACSC is the public-facing cyber security authority
- Provides technical support to AFP for cybercrime investigations

### Australian Criminal Intelligence Commission (ACIC)
- Criminal intelligence agency; partner with AFP on organized cybercrime
- Joint beneficiary of Identify and Disrupt Act powers
