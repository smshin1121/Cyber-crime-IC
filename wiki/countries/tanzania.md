## Summary

Tanzania is a **non-party** to the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]]. Its primary cybercrime legislation is the **Cybercrimes Act, 2015** [1], which has been controversial for including content-related offences that civil society organizations have characterized as restricting free expression.

Tanzania cooperates internationally through **Interpol** and **Commonwealth** channels. Capacity for cross-border cybercrime cooperation is *likely* low due to limited specialized institutional infrastructure.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Law

**Cybercrimes Act No. 14 of 2015** [1] criminalizes:
- Unauthorized access and interception
- System and data interference
- Computer fraud and forgery
- Publication of false information (speech-related offences drawing civil society concern)
- Racist and xenophobic content

### Procedural Powers

The Act grants police powers to search and seize, compel production of data, and access computer systems. Real-time interception requires judicial authorization.

### Mutual Legal Assistance

Central authority for mutual legal assistance is the **Ministry of Constitutional and Legal Affairs**. Tanzania has MLA arrangements through the Commonwealth Harare Scheme and bilateral agreements.

## Treaty Memberships

- **[[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]]** — non-party [2]
- **[[malabo-convention|African Union Malabo Convention]]** — non-party (as of last check)

## Key Agencies

- **Tanzania Police Force Cyber Crime Unit**
- **Tanzania Communications Regulatory Authority (TCRA)**

## Cooperation Track Record

Limited public record of major joint cybercrime operations. Tanzania participates in Interpol Africa initiatives including [[africa-cyber-surge-ii]] (as a participating country).

## Capacity Assessment

Low institutional cybercrime capacity. English proficiency is high (English is an official language). Limited specialized digital forensics infrastructure.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- Malabo Convention status requires verification
- Extent of participation in Interpol Africa cybercrime operations
