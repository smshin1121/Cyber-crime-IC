## Summary

Latvia is a small EU and NATO member state that ratified the Budapest Convention in 2007. Cybercrime cooperation is channeled primarily through EU mechanisms (European Investigation Order, Europol) and the Council of Europe framework. The State Police operates a dedicated cybercrime combating unit and serves as the 24/7 contact point under Article 35 of the Budapest Convention.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Criminal Law

Cybercrime offences are codified in the **Criminal Law (Krimināllikums)**, Chapter XX, covering:
- Unauthorized access to automated data processing systems
- Data interference and system interference
- Misuse of devices
- Computer fraud

### Procedural Powers

Latvia's Criminal Procedure Law provides search and seizure, production orders, preservation orders, and interception capabilities aligned with Budapest Convention Articles 16-21.

### Mutual Legal Assistance

- **Central authority:** Ministry of Justice for formal MLAT requests
- **EU instruments:** European Investigation Order (EIO) for EU member state requests
- **24/7 contact:** State Police Cybercrime Unit
