## Summary

Japan is a **highly capable** international cooperation partner and the **first Asian country** to become a party to the [[budapest-convention]] (2012). The **National Police Agency (NPA)** coordinates cybercrime investigations at the national level, while the **Japan Cybercrime Control Center (JC3)** provides a public-private partnership framework for cybercrime intelligence sharing.

Japan has been an active participant in INTERPOL operations and has developed strong bilateral cooperation relationships, particularly with the United States and South Korea. Japan's participation in [[operation-cyber-guardian|Operation Cyber Guardian]] alongside Korea demonstrated regional cooperation on CSAM. Japan is a **Five Eyes+ partner** (not a full Five Eyes member, but engaged in expanded cooperation frameworks).

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Criminal Law

**不正アクセス禁止法 (Unauthorized Computer Access Act, Act No. 128 of 1999, amended 2012):**
- Art. 3: Prohibition of unauthorized access (不正アクセス行為の禁止)
- Art. 4: Prohibition of acts facilitating unauthorized access
- Art. 5: Prohibition of unauthorized acquisition of identification codes
- Art. 6: Prohibition of unauthorized storage of identification codes

**刑法 (Penal Code):**
- Art. 161-2: Unauthorized creation of electromagnetic records (電磁的記録不正作出)
- Art. 168-2: Unauthorized creation/provision of programs (不正指令電磁的記録に関する罪) — added 2011, criminalizes malware creation
- Art. 234-2: Obstruction of business by damaging a computer (電子計算機損壊等業務妨害)
- Art. 246-2: Computer fraud (電子計算機使用詐欺)

**不正競争防止法 (Unfair Competition Prevention Act):**
- Trade secret protection; relevant to economic cyber espionage

**個人情報保護法 (Personal Information Protection Act):**
- Data breach notification obligations
- Restrictions on cross-border data transfer

### Procedural Powers

- **Search and seizure:** 刑事訴訟法 (Code of Criminal Procedure) Art. 99, 218; includes electronic evidence; requires warrant
- **Remote search:** Art. 99(2), 218(2); allows search of computers accessible from the searched location
- **Preservation:** Available under Budapest Convention framework and bilateral cooperation
- **Interception:** 通信傍受法 (Act on Wiretapping for Criminal Investigation, 1999); limited to specific serious offences; requires court order
- **Data retention:** No mandatory retention regime; relies on voluntary provider cooperation

### Mutual Legal Assistance

- **Central authority:** Ministry of Justice, International Affairs Division
- **国際捜査共助等に関する法律 (Act on International Assistance in Investigation and Other Related Matters):** Domestic MLA implementation
- **Cautious approach:** Japan's MLA process is *likely* thorough but relatively slow due to procedural rigor and internal review processes
- **Dual criminality:** Generally required for MLA; applied broadly for cybercrime offences

## Key Agencies

### National Police Agency (NPA) — Cybercrime Division
- National-level coordination of cybercrime investigations
- INTERPOL NCB Japan
- Coordinates with prefectural police (47 prefectures have cyber units)
- Budapest Convention 24/7 contact point

### Japan Cybercrime Control Center (JC3)
- Public-private partnership for cybercrime intelligence
- Information sharing platform between law enforcement and private sector
- Supports threat intelligence and early warning

### Metropolitan Police Department (MPD) — Tokyo
- Largest prefectural police force; handles major cybercrime cases in Tokyo
- Specialized cyber investigation units

### Ministry of Internal Affairs and Communications (MIC) — NICT
- National Institute of Information and Communications Technology
- Cybersecurity research and NICTER network monitoring

## Cooperation Track Record

### As Requesting State

Japan sends MLA requests primarily to:
- US: Evidence from technology companies (most Japanese internet users rely on US platforms)
- Asian neighbors: Regional cybercrime investigations
- EU member states: For European-connected investigations

### As Requested State

Japan is *likely* a reliable but methodical cooperation partner. The procedural rigor of the Japanese justice system means requests are handled carefully, which may result in longer processing times compared to some Western counterparts.

### Notable Operations

- **[[operation-cyber-guardian]]** (2025): Multi-country CSAM operation; Japan participated alongside Korea, Australia, and others — 544 arrests across 6 countries
- **[[operation-haechi-v]]** / **[[operation-haechi-vi]]:** Japanese participation in INTERPOL operations against cyber-enabled financial fraud
- **[[operation-cronos-phase1]]:** Japanese police identified LockBit victims and contributed intelligence
- **[[operation-synergia-ii]]:** Japanese participation in INTERPOL operation against phishing/ransomware infrastructure
- **[[emotet-takedown|Emotet takedown (2021)]]:** Japan was not among the eight participating countries in the official [[emotet-takedown|Operation LadyBird]] action, but Japan was heavily affected by Emotet infections. JPCERT/CC coordinated domestic victim notification following the takedown.

> [!warning] Correction
> A prior version of this page stated Japanese police "participated" in the Emotet takedown. Europol's official announcement lists 8 participating countries (Netherlands, Germany, US, UK, France, Lithuania, Canada, Ukraine); Japan is not among them. Japan's role was *likely* limited to domestic victim notification and CERT coordination.

## Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

Korea-Japan cybercrime cooperation is *likely* active through multiple channels:

- **[[operation-cyber-guardian]]:** Korea-led CSAM operation where Japan was a key participant (2025); *likely* the most significant Korea-Japan cybercrime cooperation operation
- **INTERPOL operations:** Both countries participate in [[operation-haechi-v]] (2024), [[operation-haechi-vi]] (2025), and [[operation-first-light-2024]]
- **Budapest Convention:** Both parties (Japan since 2012, Korea since 2024), providing mutual MLA basis
- **Geographic proximity:** Facilitates regional cooperation on cyber-enabled fraud and CSAM
- **Five Eyes+ engagement:** Both Korea and Japan participate in expanded Five Eyes forums for cybersecurity
- **Diplomatic channels:** Korea-Japan judicial cooperation proceeds through diplomatic channels; political tensions may occasionally affect cooperation pace
