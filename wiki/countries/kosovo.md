## Summary

Kosovo is **not a party** to the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]]. As a state not universally recognised and not a member of the Council of Europe, Kosovo cannot accede to the Convention through the standard procedure. However, Kosovo participates in Council of Europe **Octopus Project** and **GLACY+** capacity-building activities as a de facto participant under the "Kosovo*" designation.

Kosovo adopted a dedicated cybercrime statute — **Law No. 03/L-166 on Prevention and Fight of the Cybercrime** — in 2010, which the Council of Europe Octopus country profile notes is "currently being reviewed." The Law is broadly aligned with Budapest Convention substantive provisions.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Law No. 03/L-166 on Prevention and Fight of the Cybercrime (2010)

- Adopted 2010
- Covers key Budapest Convention offence categories:
  - Penal acts against confidentiality, integrity, and availability of computer systems data
  - Computer-related fraud and forgery
  - Child pornography through computer systems
- Complements the Criminal Code, which does not cover all cybercrime offences on its own
- Under review as of most recent Council of Europe Octopus assessment

### Criminal Code of the Republic of Kosovo

- Contains general criminal provisions applicable to computer-related offences
- Does not provide comprehensive cybercrime coverage on its own — Law 03/L-166 fills gaps

### Criminal Procedure Code

- Provides general framework for search, seizure, production, and preservation
- Specific e-evidence procedural provisions *unknown*
