## Summary

Sweden is a **highly capable Nordic cybercrime cooperation partner**, deeply embedded in EU and Nordic cooperation networks. Its cybercrime law is primarily found in the **Brottsbalken (Penal Code)** rather than in a standalone cybercrime statute. Sweden was one of the **original 30 signatories** of the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]] on 23 November 2001.

> [!warning] Legal status check needed
> As of publicly available Council of Europe Octopus records updated October 2023, Sweden was still a signatory pursuing domestic reforms for ratification. Verify whether Sweden has deposited its instrument of ratification since.

## Legal Framework for Cybercrime

### Substantive Criminal Law

**Brottsbalken (Penal Code)** — relevant chapters:
- Chapter 4 §§ 8-9 — breach of data secrecy, unlawful interception
- Chapter 4 § 9c — data trespass / unlawful access
- Chapter 9 § 1 — fraud (including computer fraud)
- Chapter 14 — document forgery (including electronic documents)

### Procedural Powers

**Rättegångsbalken (Code of Judicial Procedure)** provides search and seizure, production orders, and interception powers, with additional provisions in specific laws on electronic surveillance.

### Mutual Legal Assistance

- **Law on International Legal Assistance in Criminal Matters (2000:562)** — primary MLA statute
- EU instruments: European Investigation Order, Nordic cooperation under the Nordic MLA Convention
- Central authority: **Ministry of Justice**

## Key Agencies

### [[sweden-police|Swedish Police Authority — Cybercrime Unit]]
- Located within the National Operations Department (NOA, Nationella operativa avdelningen)
- Specialised cybercrime investigation and digital forensics

### CERT-SE
- National Computer Emergency Response Team
- Part of the Swedish Civil Contingencies Agency (MSB)

## Cooperation Track Record

Sweden is *almost certainly* active in Europol-coordinated cybercrime operations and Nordic joint investigations. Sweden's NATO accession (2024) has *likely* deepened intelligence and law enforcement information sharing with Western partners.
