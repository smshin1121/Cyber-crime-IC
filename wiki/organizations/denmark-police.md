## Summary

The **National Cyber Crime Center (NC3 — Nationalt Cyber Crime Center)** is Denmark's specialized cybercrime center, embedded within the **National Special Crime Unit (NSK — National enhed for Særlig Kriminalitet)**. NSK was created on **January 1, 2022** by merging several specialized Danish police units, including the previous standalone NC3 and the Nationwide Center for IT-related Economic Crime [1][2].

NC3 has the overall Danish police responsibility for supporting complex IT-related crime investigations — hacking, ransomware, DDoS attacks — and also handles a wide range of internet-enabled sexual crime cases (child sexual abuse material, grooming, sextortion). The NSK as a whole handles Denmark's most complex and serious cases of financial crime, organized crime, and cybercrime [1][2].

## Mandate and Authority

NC3 and NSK operate under the **Danish National Police (Rigspolitiet)** and the Danish Prosecution Service. Key functions [1][2]:

- Supporting all Danish police districts in complex cyber investigations
- Operating specialized investigative capacity for cyber-dependent crime
- Handling IT-related sexual crimes against children
- Coordinating international cybercrime requests

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

NSK consolidated the following units into one organization on 2022-01-01 [2]:

- Nationalt Cyber Crime Center (NC3) — now a center within NSK
- Special Investigation East and West
- Nationwide Center for IT-related Economic Crime
- National Forensic Science Center
- Parts of the Public Prosecutor for Economic and International Crime

## IC Capabilities

- Europol partnership via the Danish Europol National Unit
- INTERPOL cooperation via NCB Copenhagen
- Digital forensics laboratory capacity
