## Summary

The National Crime Agency (NCA) is the [[united-kingdom|United Kingdom's]] lead law enforcement agency for tackling serious and organised crime. Established on 7 October 2013, replacing the Serious Organised Crime Agency (SOCA), the NCA has a broad mandate covering cybercrime, drug trafficking, economic crime, human trafficking, and child exploitation. Its **National Cyber Crime Unit (NCCU)** is the dedicated cybercrime arm responsible for conducting and coordinating major cyber investigations.

The NCA is *highly likely* among the top three most capable national cybercrime agencies globally, alongside the [[fbi-cyber-division|FBI Cyber Division]] and comparable agencies. Its leadership of [[operation-cronos-phase1|Operation Cronos Phase 1]] — the landmark disruption of the LockBit ransomware group in February 2024 — demonstrated the NCA's ability to lead complex multinational operations involving 10+ countries and coordinate with [[europol-ec3|Europol EC3]] and the FBI.

The NCA participated in all 5 operations covered by currently ingested sources and **led** Operation Cronos from the UK side.

## Current Status (as of 2026-04-10)

**Present-day name:** National Crime Agency (NCA) — National Cyber Crime Unit (NCCU)
**Status:** *Active* — NCA and NCCU continue to operate under the same names as of April 2026 [6][8]
**Parent:** Home Office (Director General reports to the Home Secretary)

As of April 2026 the NCA and its NCCU remain the UK's lead agencies for serious cybercrime. No immediate restructuring has occurred, but a significant **multi-year overhaul is under way**:

- **Independent police review:** An independent review of UK police force structures is due to report in **summer 2026**. It is expected to reshape how regional and national specialist units — including cyber and fraud teams — integrate across the 43 territorial forces and the NCA. Any resulting reorganization is *likely* to be multi-year and is unlikely to take effect before 2027 [6].
- **NCCU Crypto Cell:** The NCA established a dedicated **Crypto Cell** within the NCCU with an initial team of approximately five officers, expanding the NCA's cryptocurrency tracing and recovery capability for ransomware and fraud investigations [7].
- **Post-Brexit cooperation:** The NCA continues to work with [[europol-ec3|Europol]] through the post-Brexit UK-EU TCA arrangement rather than as an EU member state agency. Operational participation in multi-agency Europol actions (Cronos, Endgame, Phobos/8Base) has continued through 2024–2025.
- **Operational continuity:** The NCCU continues to lead UK cybercrime enforcement and participated in multiple 2024–2025 multinational operations, including Operation Cronos Phase 3, Operation Endgame Phase 2, and the Phobos/8Base crackdown.

No material change to the NCCU's name, level, or mandate is *likely* to occur before the summer 2026 police review report is published.

## Mandate and Authority

The NCA was established under the Crime and Courts Act 2013, with a mandate to:

- **Lead** the UK's fight against serious and organised crime, including cybercrime.
- **Command and coordinate** across UK law enforcement (NCA officers have jurisdiction throughout England, Wales, and Northern Ireland, with powers in Scotland by agreement).
- **Work internationally** to disrupt criminal networks that affect the UK.
- **Build intelligence** on serious and organised crime threats to inform the National Strategic Assessment.

The NCA's Director General has authority to direct UK police forces on serious and organised crime matters, though this power is exercised sparingly. The NCA reports to the Home Secretary and publishes an annual National Strategic Assessment of Serious and Organised Crime.

Key legislation underpinning NCA cyber operations includes:
- Computer Misuse Act 1990 (as amended)
- Proceeds of Crime Act 2002
- Serious Crime Act 2015
- Investigatory Powers Act 2016

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

### National Cyber Crime Unit (NCCU)

The NCCU is the NCA's specialist unit for investigating the most serious instances of cybercrime. Key functions include:

- **Investigation:** Leading and supporting investigations into major cyber attacks, ransomware, and cybercriminal networks affecting the UK.
- **Technical operations:** Covert online investigations, technical surveillance, and infrastructure takedown capabilities.
- **Intelligence:** Developing strategic and tactical intelligence on cybercrime threats.
- **Coordination:** Coordinating UK law enforcement cyber operations and providing support to regional organised crime units (ROCUs) and police forces.

### International Liaison Network

The NCA maintains a network of international liaison officers posted in key locations worldwide, enabling direct law enforcement-to-law enforcement cooperation. This network, though smaller than the FBI's 63+ Legat offices, provides the NCA with direct access to partner agencies in priority countries.

### Relationship with Other UK Bodies

- **National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC):** Part of GCHQ, handles defensive cyber security. The NCA and NCSC coordinate on incidents that have both criminal and national security dimensions.
- **Regional Organised Crime Units (ROCUs):** Provide regional cyber investigation capability, with NCA providing oversight and support for the most complex cases.
- **City of London Police:** Designated UK lead force for fraud, with which the NCA coordinates on cyber-enabled fraud cases.

## IC Capabilities

The NCA's international cooperation capabilities include:

- **Operation leadership:** Demonstrated ability to lead multinational operations (e.g., Cronos Phase 1), coordinating dozens of agencies across 10+ countries.
- **Europol engagement:** Active participation in [[europol-ec3|Europol EC3]]-coordinated operations and the Joint Cybercrime Action Taskforce (J-CAT).
- **International liaison officers:** Posted in key jurisdictions for real-time bilateral cooperation.
- **Five Eyes cooperation:** Deep intelligence sharing and operational integration with [[fbi-cyber-division|FBI]], Australian Federal Police, RCMP, and New Zealand Police as part of the Five Eyes law enforcement partnership.
- **JIT participation:** Experience in Joint Investigation Teams under EU frameworks and, post-Brexit, through bilateral arrangements and the UK-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement (TCA, 2021).
- **INTERPOL engagement:** Cooperation with [[interpol-igci|INTERPOL IGCI]] on global operations.

> [!note] Post-Brexit cooperation
> Following the UK's departure from the EU (January 2020/transition end December 2020), the NCA lost direct access to certain EU cooperation tools (e.g., European Arrest Warrant, direct Europol database access). The UK-EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement (TCA) established alternative arrangements, but some operational friction is *likely* compared to the pre-Brexit era. The NCA now cooperates with Europol through a working arrangement rather than as an EU member state agency.

### Operation Cronos Phase 1 — NCA Leadership

The NCA's leadership of [[operation-cronos-phase1|Operation Cronos Phase 1]] (February 2024) is the most significant demonstration of its international cooperation capability in recent years. The NCCU led the investigation from the UK, coordinating with:
- [[europol-ec3|Europol EC3]] (coordination hub)
- [[fbi-cyber-division|FBI]] (US investigation and indictments)
- [[eurojust|Eurojust]] (judicial coordination)
- National agencies from France, Germany, the Netherlands, Australia, Canada, Japan, and others

The operation resulted in the seizure of LockBit's infrastructure, including 34 servers, and the release of decryption keys to victims. The NCA notably took control of LockBit's data leak site and used it to publish information about the group's operations — a *likely* deliberate psychological disruption tactic.

## Cooperation Track Record

The NCA's cooperation track record indicates a *highly likely* status as one of the most active international partners in cybercrime operations:

- **EU operations:** Despite Brexit, the NCA continues to participate in major Europol-coordinated operations, suggesting effective post-Brexit cooperation arrangements.
- **US partnership:** Close bilateral cooperation with the [[fbi-cyber-division|FBI Cyber Division]], with joint operations and intelligence sharing on ransomware and other threats.
- **Five Eyes:** Deep integration with Five Eyes law enforcement partners.
- **Developing partnerships:** The NCA's international liaison network and involvement in capacity building programs extend its cooperation reach beyond traditional Five Eyes partners.

## Capacity Building

The NCA engages in international capacity building through:

- **Overseas liaison officers:** Provide mentoring and support to partner country law enforcement.
- **Training programs:** Contribute to international cyber investigation training through bilateral programs and multilateral initiatives (e.g., GLACY+).
- **UK Aid-funded programs:** The NCA participates in capacity building projects funded by UK development assistance in priority regions.

## Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

Direct NCA-Korea cybercrime cooperation is *likely* less developed than FBI-Korea cooperation, given the stronger US-ROK bilateral law enforcement relationship. However, cooperation channels exist through:

- **Multilateral operations:** Both the NCA and Korean agencies (e.g., KNPA) participate in Europol- and INTERPOL-coordinated operations, creating indirect cooperation pathways. The [[phobos-8base-crackdown|Phobos/8Base operation]] involved both UK and South Korean participation.
- **INTERPOL:** Both countries are active INTERPOL members, enabling cooperation through [[interpol-igci|INTERPOL IGCI]].
- **UK-ROK bilateral relationship:** The broader UK-South Korea bilateral relationship may facilitate law enforcement cooperation, though specific cybercrime cooperation agreements require further sourcing.

> [!note] Information gap
> The existence and scope of any direct NCA-KNPA cooperation arrangements on cybercrime are not confirmed by currently ingested sources.
