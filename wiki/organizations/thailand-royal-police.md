## Summary

The Cyber Crime Investigation Bureau (CCIB) of the [[thailand-royal-police|Royal Thai Police]] was established on **9 September 2020** and began operations on **22 December 2020**. It is Thailand's first dedicated national-level cybercrime police bureau and was created in response to the surge in online fraud, scam compounds, and cryptocurrency-enabled financial crime affecting Thai citizens and the region.

CCIB reportedly receives approximately **800 victim complaints per day**, the majority involving cryptocurrency-facilitated crime or money laundering. Since CCIB's inception, blockchain intelligence has played a critical role in tracing illicit financial flows — Thailand has engaged TRM Labs and other private-sector crypto intelligence providers to support investigations.

## Current Status (as of 2026-04-10)

**Present-day name:** กองบัญชาการตำรวจสืบสวนสอบสวนอาชญากรรมทางเทคโนโลยี (Cyber Crime Investigation Bureau, CCIB)
**Status:** *Active* — CCIB continues to operate under the same name and structure [1][6]
**Commissioner (as of 2025–2026):** **Pol Lt Gen Surapol Prembutr** (Commissioner of the Cyber Crime Investigation Bureau) [6]
**Parent:** [[thailand-royal-police|Royal Thai Police]]

As of April 2026 CCIB remains Thailand's lead national cybercrime bureau. Recent developments through 2025–2026:

- **Current leadership:** Pol Lt Gen **Surapol Prembutr** serves as CCIB Commissioner as of 2025–2026 [6].
- **Growing caseload:** Between **1 January and 30 September 2025**, **279,861 cybercrime petitions** were filed through the Thai Police Online platform, with reported damages of at least **28.01 billion baht** (~USD 780M). Service scams accounted for 123,865 complaints; investment scams caused the highest financial losses at **10.229 billion baht** [6].
- **2025 CIB prioritization:** The broader Central Investigation Bureau (CIB) announced in early 2025 that cybercrime and call-centre scams would be priority areas for 2025, with enhanced study of online scammer tactics [6][7].
- **Anti-scam infrastructure:** CCIB continues to partner with AIS on a scam-call alert service and operates cyber patrol task forces; the **Cyber Police Volunteers** community network supports citizen reporting [6].
- **Ongoing scam compound enforcement:** CCIB continues active enforcement against Thai-Myanmar and Thai-Cambodia border scam compound operations. 2025–2026 operations include Pattaya raids on Chinese-national-run online scam operations [8].
- **International cooperation:** CCIB *likely* continues active cooperation with the US FBI, Australian Federal Police, Chinese MPS, and the Korean KNPA on pig-butchering / romance scam / Korean-national repatriation cases from Cambodia and Myanmar scam compounds.

No structural reorganization of CCIB has been announced as of April 2026; the 2020 founding structure remains in place with a growing caseload and expanded anti-scam infrastructure.

## Mandate and Authority

CCIB's authority derives from Thailand's:

- **Computer Crime Act B.E. 2550 (2007)**, amended B.E. 2560 (2017) — Thailand's primary cybercrime statute
- **Criminal Procedure Code**
- **Anti-Money Laundering Act B.E. 2542 (1999)**
- **Emergency Decree on Measures for Prevention and Suppression of Technology Crime B.E. 2566 (2023)** — enacted to accelerate freezing of scam-related accounts

CCIB's scope initially covered defamation, threats, fake news, online gambling, and illegal trade. In cases where local police lack the means to investigate cybercrime, CCIB can take over the investigation.

Thailand is *not* a party to the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]] as of this writing.

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

CCIB is organized into multiple investigation divisions (กองบังคับการ), including divisions dedicated to:

1. **CCID 1** — general cybercrime investigations
2. **CCID 2** — online fraud and scam compounds
3. **Additional specialized divisions** for digital forensics and international cooperation

> [!note] CCIB's internal divisional structure is organized into multiple "กองบังคับการตำรวจสืบสวนสอบสวนอาชญากรรมทางเทคโนโลยี" (CCID 1, CCID 2, etc.). Specific division assignments vary.

## IC Capabilities

- **Regional scam compound enforcement:** CCIB leads Thai operations against Southeast Asian scam compounds (Myanmar, Cambodia, Laos border regions).
- **Crypto-tracing:** Deep bench of blockchain analysis capacity, including partnership with TRM Labs.
- **INTERPOL cooperation:** Active participation in INTERPOL HAECHI and regional operations.
- **Bilateral liaison:** Active cooperation with US FBI, AFP, and Asian regional partners on transnational fraud.

## Key Operations and Cases

- **Ongoing scam compound enforcement:** CCIB has participated in raids on Thai-Myanmar and Thai-Cambodia border scam operations.
- **Crypto scam network takedowns:** TRM Labs has publicly documented CCIB-led cryptocurrency scam network disruptions.

## Cooperation Track Record

CCIB's cooperation capacity is *likely* high and growing rapidly, driven by the urgency of scam compound enforcement in the Mekong sub-region. *Highly likely* a key partner for US and Australian law enforcement on romance scam and pig-butchering (sha zhu pan) investigations targeting Western victims.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- The 15-day internal investigation target mentioned in some sources may not be realistic for complex transnational cases.
- How does CCIB coordinate with the Department of Special Investigation (DSI) of the Ministry of Justice on overlapping transnational crime cases?
- Thailand's role as both a victim and a host jurisdiction for scam compounds creates a complex policing environment; CCIB's enforcement against scam compounds operating on Thai soil vs. across borders requires clearer documentation.
- The operational impact of Thailand's 2023 Emergency Decree on account freezing by CCIB needs assessment.
