## Summary

The **Unidade Nacional de Combate ao Cibercrime e à Criminalidade Tecnológica (UNC3T)** is the specialized cybercrime unit of Portugal's **Polícia Judiciária (PJ)**. It was created by Decree-Law No. 81/2016 (28 November 2016), entering into operation in late 2016, replacing the earlier Unidade Nacional da Investigação da Criminalidade Informática (established August 2015). Its structure is *almost certainly* inspired by Europol's [[europol-ec3|EC3]] model, consolidating cybercrime-related competencies previously dispersed across multiple PJ units.

## Mandate and Authority

UNC3T is the PJ's operational unit for cybercrime prevention, investigation, and repression. It handles both cyber-dependent crimes (e.g., hacking, malware) and cyber-enabled crimes where technology is central. It is integrated into Portugal's broader law enforcement architecture and supports the Public Prosecution Service in cybercrime cases.

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

UNC3T's design mirrors EC3's three-strand approach: operations, strategic analysis, and forensic/technical support. As the PJ is Portugal's criminal police responsible for serious and organized crime, UNC3T serves as Portugal's primary interface with Europol and INTERPOL on cybercrime matters.

## IC Capabilities

- **Europol integration:** Portugal's national hub for EC3 cooperation; participates in EMPACT Cyber-Attacks and Online Child Sexual Exploitation priorities (*likely*).
- **Budapest Convention 24/7 network:** Portugal is a party to the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]]; UNC3T/PJ serves as the operational contact point.
- **Digital forensics:** Concentrated forensic capabilities for digital evidence handling.
