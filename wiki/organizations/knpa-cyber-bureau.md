> [!warning] Status — Reorganized (as of 2023-12)
> The standalone **경찰청 사이버수사국** (KNPA Cyber Investigation Bureau) was dissolved in late 2023 and its functions were absorbed into **경찰청 국가수사본부 수사국**, with a new **사이버수사심의관 (Cyber Investigation Deliberation Officer)** position created inside 수사국 at *deputy-director level* rather than bureau-director level. As of 2026-04-10 the **사이버수사국 no longer exists**; cybercrime investigation is conducted by the 사이버수사심의관 and 사이버수사과 within 수사국. A re-establishment of 사이버안전국 is under active consideration but has not yet been enacted (see [Current Status](#current-status-as-of-2026-04-10) below).
>
> This page preserves the full historical arc and current state. Operations completed before December 2023 were carried out under the 사이버수사국; operations from 2024 onward are carried out by the 수사국 사이버수사심의관 and its subordinate units.

## Summary

This page documents the **national cybercrime enforcement apparatus of the Korea National Police Agency (KNPA, 경찰청)** across three named incarnations: **사이버안전국 (Cyber Safety Bureau, ~2014–2021)**, **사이버수사국 (Cyber Investigation Bureau, 2021–2023)**, and **수사국 사이버수사심의관 (Investigation Bureau's Cyber Investigation Deliberation Officer, 2023–present)**. Throughout all three iterations the unit has served as **INTERPOL NCB Korea for cyber matters** and led Korea's participation in major multilateral cybercrime operations, most notably the **HAECHI** series [1][2].

Because the operational mandate, personnel, and operational continuity have been substantially preserved across reorganizations, this page treats them as a single evolving organization rather than three separate entities.

## Current Status (as of 2026-04-10)

**Present-day name:** 경찰청 국가수사본부 수사국 — 사이버수사심의관 및 사이버수사과
**English:** KNPA National Investigation Headquarters, Investigation Bureau — Cyber Investigation Deliberation Officer and Cyber Investigation Division
**Level:** Deliberation-officer level (심의관, 경무관급), subordinate to 수사국장 (director-general of 수사국) [3]
**Parent:** 국가수사본부 (National Investigation Headquarters) → 수사국 (Investigation Bureau)

The functions previously exercised by the standalone 사이버수사국 (bureau-director level, 치안감 또는 경무관) are now exercised at deliberation-officer level within the broader 수사국. According to April 2026 press reporting, this reorganization correlated with a decline in cybercrime clearance rates (67.5% → 54.1%) and a 34.4% increase in cybercrime incidents, prompting a proposal to **re-establish a standalone 사이버안전국** [4].

**Pending re-establishment (planned, not yet in force):** The KNPA has proposed re-creating a standalone **사이버안전국 (Cyber Safety Bureau)** separated out of 수사국. The plan is subject to 행안부·기재부 협의 and the December 2026 National Assembly budget review, with implementation expected no earlier than **March 2027** if approved [4]. Under the proposal the **사이버범죄수사과** would be renamed **사이버안전과** and approximately **20 additional staff** would be added at the bureau level.

## Mandate and Authority

Across all three incarnations the unit's mandate has covered:
- Investigation of all categories of cybercrime (hacking, ransomware, online fraud, voice phishing, CSAM)
- National coordination of metropolitan/provincial police cybercrime units (지방경찰청 사이버수사대)
- International cooperation on cybercrime through INTERPOL, Europol, and bilateral channels
- Digital forensics and technical analysis support
- Cyber threat intelligence and early warning

The 2023 demotion from bureau level to deliberation-officer level did not formally strip these mandates, but the reduced organizational elevation has been *likely* to have reduced inter-ministerial negotiating weight and staff allocation, correlating with the cited decline in clearance rates [4].

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC (current, as of 2026-04-10)

Under 수사국 > 사이버수사심의관:
- **사이버범죄수사과 (Cyber Crime Investigation Division)** — primary operational division (to be renamed 사이버안전과 if the 2027 reorganization is approved)
- **Digital forensics support** integrated with 과학수사관리관 functions (now under 형사국)
- **International liaison** via the same 사이버수사 channel that previously existed under 사이버수사국

## IC Capabilities

Capabilities have been *likely* continuous across reorganizations:
- **INTERPOL I-24/7 access:** Direct connection to INTERPOL's secure global police communications network via NCB Korea
- **Budapest Convention 24/7 contact:** Korea's designated Art. 35 contact point for urgent international requests (channel preserved through reorganizations)
- **HAECHI operational command:** Korea leads and coordinates the HAECHI series through this line of command
- **Digital forensics lab:** Advanced forensic capabilities (technical capacity preserved, though organizational parent changed)
- **Cryptocurrency tracing:** Growing capability for blockchain analysis and virtual asset tracing
- **Multi-language liaison:** Officers with English, Chinese, Japanese proficiency (varies)

## Cooperation Track Record

The Korean cybercrime enforcement line *almost certainly* ranks as one of the most operationally active Asia-Pacific cybercrime cooperation partners. Primary cooperation channels:
- **INTERPOL:** Primary multilateral channel; NCB Korea function; I-24/7 access; HAECHI lead
- **Europol:** Strategic Agreement enables cooperation; liaison for EU-connected cases
- **FBI:** Bilateral cooperation on ransomware, DPRK cyber threats, financial cybercrime
- **China MPS:** Bilateral cooperation on voice phishing through Korean-Chinese police channels
- **Japan NPA:** Regional cooperation on CSAM, fraud, and mutual-interest cases

## Capacity Building

The unit has participated in INTERPOL capacity-building programs for the Asia-Pacific region and shared expertise on:
- Voice phishing investigation techniques
- Cryptocurrency tracing methodologies
- Cyber-enabled financial crime investigation

## Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

As a Korean national agency, this unit **is** the primary Korean operational entity for cybercrime IC. Its interactions with other Korean entities:
- **법무부 국제형사과 (MOJ International Criminal Affairs Division):** Formal MLAT channel; operational requests flow from the unit through MOJ to diplomatic channels
- **[[spo-international-cooperation|대검찰청 국제협력단]]:** Prosecutorial coordination for cases with IC elements
- **지방경찰청 사이버수사대 (metropolitan/provincial cybercrime units):** Coordinated via the national cybercrime command line
- **[[kisa|KISA]]:** Technical coordination, CSIRT functions, policy

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- Exact staff headcount before and after the 2023 demotion is not publicly disclosed
- Whether the 2027 re-establishment of 사이버안전국 will receive final approval depends on December 2026 budget outcomes
- The formal bilateral and multilateral cooperation channels' legal text may reference "사이버수사국" and would technically need updating if the 2027 reorganization goes ahead — whether foreign partners treat the re-establishment as a new legal entity is an open question
- Whether the 2023 reorganization changed the Budapest Convention Art. 35 contact point designation on paper (even if the same personnel continue in the role) is not publicly confirmed
