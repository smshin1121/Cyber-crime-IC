## Summary

**Policia Judiciaria (PJ)** is Portugal's criminal investigation police. For cybercrime analysis, its most important component is [[portugal-police|UNC3T]], the national unit for cybercrime and technological criminality.

## Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The official UNC3T source describes the unit as the specialized PJ body for cybercrime and technological criminality. The Portugal government source records the 2016 reform that created the modern unit structure, replacing earlier computer-crime arrangements and consolidating cybercrime competencies inside PJ.

PJ appears in the corpus in both cyber-dependent and cyber-enabled investigations. The [[operation-jackal-iii|Operation Jackal III]] source gives a recent country-level example of PJ participation in an INTERPOL-supported anti-fraud operation, while [[franco-israeli-ceo-fraud]] shows PJ's role in asset-tracing and bank-account work tied to CEO fraud and BEC laundering.
