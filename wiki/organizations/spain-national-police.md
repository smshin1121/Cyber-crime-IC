## Summary

The **Spanish National Police** is one of Spain's two core national cybercrime actors. Its **Brigada Central de Investigacion Tecnologica (BCIT)** is the specialized unit that addresses a broad range of cyber-enabled crime, including child-exploitation offences, internet fraud, communications fraud, cyber attacks, and piracy.

## Mandate and Authority

Official Policia Nacional material describes the BCIT as the unit designed to respond to the challenges posed by new forms of crime. It sits within the national and transnational policing mission of the Policia Nacional and forms one half of Spain's dual cybercrime structure, alongside the Guardia Civil's [[spain-guardia-civil|GDT]].

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

- national cybercrime capability inside the Policia Nacional
- handles national and transnational cybercrime categories
- complements Guardia Civil in Spain's dual-track enforcement structure

## IC Capabilities

- cybercrime investigation across fraud, attacks on systems, and online exploitation categories
- role in Spain's treaty-based and 24/7 cooperation architecture
- operational value for cases that require National Police rather than Guardia Civil competence

## Division of Labor Within Spain

The clearest current source for Spain's division of labor is the Council of Europe country profile. It states that Spain maintains **two 24/7 points of contact**, one for each of the two national law-enforcement forces that fight cybercrime, and that **both police forces share competence** with case allocation handled under previously agreed rules.

That means the BCIT should not be read as Spain's only national cybercrime body. It is one of two co-equal specialized channels. The National Police side appears best suited to broad national and transnational judicial-police cyber investigations, while the Guardia Civil side maintains its own specialized cyber structure and reporting workflow.

## Contradictions & Open Questions
