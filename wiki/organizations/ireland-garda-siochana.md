## Summary

**An Garda Siochana** is Ireland's national police service. In the current cybercrime corpus, its strongest source-backed role is Ireland's domestic contribution to INTERPOL's **Operation Jackal** series, locally branded as **Operation Skein**.

## Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The Garda sources provide country-level detail that complements central [[interpol|INTERPOL]] summaries. The 2022 source records early Irish enforcement activity against Black Axe-linked fraud and money-laundering networks. The 2023 update adds searches, arrests, money-laundering charges, mule-account activity, and fund-recovery work for Irish corporate victims. The July 2024 source is the strongest Irish-side official anchor for [[operation-jackal-iii|Operation Jackal III]], adding domestic figures for arrests, charges, searches, bank-account blocking, and cryptocurrency seizures.

## IC Relevance

Garda coverage is useful when tracing how a multinational fraud operation translates into national enforcement. It provides an official non-U.S. perspective on Black Axe-linked business-email-compromise, romance-fraud, mule-account, and laundering investigations. That makes the Garda page a high-value link target for Operation Jackal, Operation Skein, and related African organized cyber-fraud narratives.

## Source Coverage

Coverage is strong for the Operation Jackal / Operation Skein lineage from 2022 through 2024. It remains narrower for Garda's permanent cybercrime structure, so future enrichment should add official pages on the Garda National Cyber Crime Bureau or related specialist units if they are harvested.
