## Summary

The **Japan Cybercrime Control Center (JC3)** is a Japanese public-private cybercrime cooperation body. In the current corpus, its clearest role is in the [[lumma-stealer-takedown|Lumma Stealer takedown]], where Microsoft and secondary reporting identify Japan-side support as part of a wider Microsoft, DOJ, Europol, and Japanese action against LummaC2 infrastructure.

## Cybercrime Cooperation Role

JC3 is relevant where the operation depends on a hybrid model: private-sector telemetry, civil or criminal domain action, law-enforcement coordination, and regional infrastructure disruption. The Lumma operation is a useful example because it involved thousands of domains and required coordination across private-sector and government actors rather than a conventional arrest-first police operation.

## IC Relevance

Use JC3 when the source names Japan Cybercrime Control Center, Japan-side infrastructure disruption, or public-private Japanese cybercrime cooperation. Use [[japan-npa]] when the source names the National Police Agency or its Cyber Affairs Bureau as the official police actor.

## Source Coverage

Coverage is currently operation-specific and focused on Lumma. A direct JC3 institutional page would strengthen mandate, membership, and standing coordination mechanisms.
