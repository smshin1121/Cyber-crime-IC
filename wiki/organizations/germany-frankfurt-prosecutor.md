## Summary

The **Zentralstelle zur Bekämpfung der Internetkriminalität (ZIT)** is a specialized central office of the **Generalstaatsanwaltschaft Frankfurt am Main** (General Public Prosecutor's Office Frankfurt, Hessen state), established on **1 January 2010** [1]. Originally headquartered in Gießen, ZIT relocated to Frankfurt am Main in July 2019 [1].

ZIT is *almost certainly* the single most important German prosecutorial body for international cybercrime cases. Together with the **[[germany-bka|Bundeskriminalamt (BKA)]]**, ZIT has led the German prosecutorial side of nearly every major German-driven international cybercrime operation of the last decade, including [[operation-avalanche|Avalanche]] (2016), [[operation-endgame-phase1|Operation Endgame]] (2024), and multiple darknet forum takedowns [2][3].

## Mandate and Authority

ZIT serves as the **first point of contact for the BKA** for internet crimes where jurisdiction within Germany is unclear or where a case involves numerous suspects nationwide (mass proceedings) [1]. This functional central-office status allows ZIT to take on cases that would otherwise get stuck in Germany's federal/state (Bund/Länder) jurisdictional split.

As an operational central office, ZIT handles particularly complex investigations in four priority areas [1]:

1. **Child pornography and online child sexual abuse**
2. **Darknet criminality** (marketplace, weapons, drugs, counterfeit trafficking)
3. **Cybercrime in the strict sense** (hacking, data theft, computer fraud, ransomware)
4. **Online hate crime** (Hasskriminalität)

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

ZIT's staffing has grown significantly since founding. As reported, it comprises a leading senior prosecutor, additional senior prosecutors, and approximately 11 public prosecutors [1]. ZIT is a founding member of the **Judicial Cybercrime Network**, a European network of judicial authorities for combating internet crime [1].

## IC Capabilities

- **BKA partnership:** Investigative-prosecutorial team for all major BKA cybercrime operations
- **European judicial network participation:** Judicial Cybercrime Network founding member
- **Eurojust coordination:** Standard partner for EU cross-border cyber prosecutions
- **MLA competent authority:** Receives and issues international judicial requests for internet crime matters

- Standard partner of Europol EC3 for German cybercrime operations
- Active Eurojust coordination
- Bilateral prosecutorial cooperation with FBI/DoJ, Dutch prosecutors, UK CPS/NCA, and others
- Has hosted multiple German ministerial visits highlighting its operational significance [2]
