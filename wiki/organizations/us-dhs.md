## Summary

The **U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS)** is a federal cabinet department established on 25 November 2002. For cybercrime international cooperation, DHS operates primarily through **Homeland Security Investigations (HSI)**, a component of ICE (Immigration and Customs Enforcement), which has significant transnational cybercrime investigation authority.

## Current Status (as of 2026-04-11)

DHS/HSI remains active in international cybercrime operations. HSI has attaché offices in over 70 countries and maintains direct operational relationships with foreign law enforcement for cybercrime, child exploitation, and financial fraud investigations.

## IC Capabilities

- **Homeland Security Investigations (HSI)**: Primary investigative arm for transnational cybercrime, with authority over cyber-enabled financial fraud, child exploitation, and identity theft
- **US-CERT / CISA**: Cyber threat intelligence sharing (separate from law enforcement)
- **HSI Cyber Crimes Center (C3)**: Specialized units for dark web, cryptocurrency tracing, and digital forensics
- **International attaché network**: 70+ countries with direct law enforcement liaison

## Key Operations and Cases

- [[911-s5-botnet-takedown]]: HSI involved in takedown of world's largest botnet (2024)
- [[operation-chakra-iii]]: HSI coordinated with CBI India on tech-support scam crackdown (2024)

## Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

HSI maintains an attaché office in Seoul and cooperates with [[knpa-cyber-bureau|KNPA]] on transnational fraud and child exploitation cases.
