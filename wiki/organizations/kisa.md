> [!info] Status — Active, restructured 2026-01
> KISA remains the same legal entity (한국인터넷진흥원, established July 2009) under the [[ministry-of-science-and-ict-korea|Ministry of Science and ICT]]. It underwent an **internal organizational restructuring in January 2026** that created three new units: **AI보안산업본부 (AI Security Industry Division), 디지털위협예방본부 (Digital Threat Prevention Division), and 랜섬웨어대응팀 (Ransomware Response Team)** [5][6]. The restructuring is *highly likely* a response to the 2025 surge in large-scale personal-data breaches (KT, SKT, Coupang) and is aligned with the Lee Jae-myung administration's "AI 기본사회" national policy agenda. KrCERT/CC, FIRST/APCERT membership, and the international-cooperation function are preserved and strengthened.

These are MSIT-side changes and do not alter KISA's corporate status, but they change KISA's supervisory context.

## Summary

The Korea Internet & Security Agency (KISA, 한국인터넷진흥원) is the sub-organization of the [[ministry-of-science-and-ict-korea|Ministry of Science and ICT]] (과학기술정보통신부) responsible for internet governance and cybersecurity of the internet in South Korea. KISA was created in **July 2009** by merging three predecessor agencies: the Korea Information Security Agency (KISA, 한국정보보호진흥원), the National Internet Development Agency (NIDA, 한국인터넷진흥원 — same Korean name, different English), and the Korean IT International Cooperation Agency (KIICA).

KISA is **not a law enforcement agency**; its role in international cooperation on cybercrime is through **incident response coordination**, **threat intelligence sharing**, and **capacity building**, rather than arrests or prosecutions. KISA operates **KrCERT/CC** (launched December 2010) as South Korea's national Computer Security Incident Response Team.

## Mandate and Authority

KISA's mandate is grounded in the Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection (정보통신망법) and related statutes. Its cybercrime-relevant functions include:

- **Incident response:** KrCERT/CC coordinates response to major cyber incidents affecting Korean networks, including phishing, ransomware, DDoS, and malware campaigns.
- **Threat intelligence:** Collects, analyzes, and shares cyber threat intelligence with domestic stakeholders and international CSIRTs.
- **Internet infrastructure:** Manages .kr ccTLD and Korean IP address space.
- **Consumer reporting (보호나라):** Operates the national public portal for reporting phishing, malware, and online fraud.

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

Post-2026-01 structure [5][6]:
- **KrCERT/CC (Korea Computer Emergency Response Team / Coordination Center):** The national CSIRT function, launched December 2010, handles real-time incident coordination and international CSIRT liaison. **Preserved** through 2026 restructuring.
- **디지털위협예방본부 (Digital Threat Prevention Division) — new (2026-01):** Central unit for preventive digital-threat mitigation; *likely* houses KrCERT/CC or coordinates closely with it.
- **랜섬웨어대응팀 (Ransomware Response Team) — new (2026-01):** Dedicated ransomware response unit, reflecting the operational priority of ransomware cases in Korean IC.
- **AI보안산업본부 (AI Security Industry Division) — new (2026-01):** AI security policy, industry cultivation, R&D, workforce development — less directly relevant to IC but shapes KISA's long-term capability.
- **International Cooperation Division:** Manages KISA's participation in FIRST, APCERT, ITU, APEC TEL, and bilateral cooperation. **Preserved** through 2026 restructuring.
- **Internet Infrastructure Division:** ccTLD and IP allocation (relevant for takedown cooperation and abuse handling). **Preserved** through 2026 restructuring.

## IC Capabilities

- **FIRST membership:** KrCERT/CC is an active FIRST member, enabling trusted incident response coordination with 500+ CSIRTs worldwide.
- **APCERT leadership:** Long-standing APCERT member; Korea has hosted APCERT events and contributed to regional drills.
- **APEC TEL:** Participates in APEC Telecommunications and Information Working Group capacity-building activities.
- **ASEAN-ROK cooperation:** Partner in ASEAN-Korea Cooperation Fund cybersecurity projects.

KISA's cooperation is *highly likely* focused on **incident-level** coordination (IoC sharing, malware samples, C2 takedown) rather than criminal investigation. Criminal cybercrime cases are handled by the [[knpa-cyber-bureau|KNPA Cyber Bureau]] or [[spo-international-cooperation|Supreme Prosecutors' Office]].

## Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

As a Korean government agency, KISA is *by definition* the domestic coordinator for national CSIRT activity. It works alongside:

- [[knpa-cyber-bureau|경찰청 사이버수사국 (KNPA Cyber Bureau)]] — criminal investigation.
- [[spo-international-cooperation|대검찰청 국제협력단 (SPO International Cooperation)]] — prosecution and MLAT.
- National Intelligence Service (국가정보원, NIS) — state-sponsored threats and national security incidents.

KISA is the appropriate first point of contact for foreign CSIRTs seeking technical cooperation on Korean-origin cyber incidents.
