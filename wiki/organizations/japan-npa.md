## Summary

The Cyber Affairs Bureau (サイバー警察局, Saibā keisatsu-kyoku) of Japan's National Police Agency (警察庁, Keisatsu-chō) was established on **1 April 2022** by restructuring the Info-Communications Bureau and integrating cyber-related divisions from several other NPA bureaus. It is now the central policing authority for cyberspace in Japan, with direct jurisdiction for the first time (historically, NPA coordinated rather than directly investigated).

Alongside the Cyber Affairs Bureau, the NPA established the **National Cyber Unit** (サイバー特別捜査隊, later restructured as サイバー特別捜査部) with roughly 200 investigators to handle serious cybercrime cases, including attacks on national and local government, critical infrastructure, and significant transnational cases.

## Mandate and Authority

The Cyber Affairs Bureau's mandate encompasses:

- **Policing in cyberspace** — the central NPA authority for cyber law enforcement
- **Combat cybercrime and cyberterrorism**
- **Central consolidation and analysis** of various types of cyber information
- **Implementation of effective countermeasures** related to cyber incidents
- **Close collaboration with overseas public security authorities**

Unlike prior NPA cyber arrangements, which relied on Prefectural Police (都道府県警察) for actual investigations, the National Cyber Unit under the Cyber Affairs Bureau has **direct national investigative jurisdiction** for serious cases.

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

- **Cyber Affairs Bureau HQ (Tokyo):** Central coordination, policy, and international liaison.
- **National Cyber Unit (サイバー特別捜査部):** ~200 investigators with national jurisdiction, launched 1 April 2022.
- **Prefectural Cyber Units:** Each Prefectural Police maintains its own cyber investigation section for local cases.
- **Cyber Force (サイバーフォース):** Technical response and analysis unit.

## IC Capabilities

- **G7 and Five Eyes engagement:** Japan is not a Five Eyes member but maintains deep bilateral cooperation with the US, UK, and other Western partners.
- **INTERPOL cooperation:** Through the NPA's INTERPOL Tokyo NCB.
- **Europol cooperation:** Japan has signed a strategic agreement with [[europol-ec3|Europol]]; the NPA is the Japanese point of contact.
- **Quad cooperation:** Japan-US-Australia-India cybersecurity cooperation has increased post-2021.
- **Budapest Convention:** Japan is a party to the [[budapest-convention|Council of Europe Convention on Cybercrime]] (ratified 2012), which provides a treaty basis for cooperation with other parties.

## Key Operations and Cases

> [!info] Specific Cyber Affairs Bureau operations should be added as sources are ingested. The bureau is relatively new (established April 2022) and its public operational record is still developing.

Historically, Japanese police have participated in international operations against ransomware (e.g., reports of Japan's participation in Europol-coordinated actions) and have investigated North Korea-linked cryptocurrency thefts.

## Cooperation Track Record

Japan's cybercrime international cooperation capacity is *likely* growing rapidly post-2022 with the new Cyber Affairs Bureau structure. Prior to 2022, the lack of direct national investigative authority was *highly likely* a friction point in international cooperation, as foreign counterparts had to coordinate with Prefectural Police via NPA intermediation.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- How operationally effective has the National Cyber Unit been since its 2022 launch? Public reporting is limited.
- How does the Cyber Affairs Bureau coordinate with Japan's National center of Incident readiness and Strategy for Cybersecurity (NISC, 内閣サイバーセキュリティセンター)?
- Does Japan's historically cautious approach to international cooperation (given constitutional and data protection constraints) limit the Cyber Affairs Bureau's ability to share real-time intelligence?
- The relationship between the Cyber Affairs Bureau and Prefectural Police cyber sections on trans-prefectural cases needs clarification.
