## Summary

The **United States Postal Inspection Service (USPIS)** is the federal law-enforcement agency that connects cyber-enabled crime to the physical mail and parcel layer. In this corpus it is especially relevant to darknet drug trafficking, counterfeit pills, fentanyl distribution, and BEC/fraud operations where money mules, mailed goods, or delivery infrastructure become investigative evidence.

## Cybercrime Cooperation Role

USPIS appears in [[operation-wirewire]] as part of a broad US federal response to business email compromise. The FBI source describes the sweep as a multi-agency international BEC action, with Postal Inspection participation reflecting the fraud and money-movement components beyond purely technical intrusion work.

The strongest USPIS-owned sources are darknet narcotics cases. USPIS press releases confirm Postal Inspector roles in the Ogando/Dawodu/Spencer fentanyl-pill case and in the [[operation-us-v-okparaeke-dark-web|FentMaster]] investigation. Those records show why USPIS matters in cybercrime IC: the marketplace and cryptocurrency components are online, but the contraband and delivery chain require postal investigative authority.

## IC Relevance

Use this page for US postal/logistics enforcement in darknet marketplace cases, counterfeit-drug distribution, and BEC/fraud task forces. For technical intrusion or botnet work, use [[fbi-cyber-division]] or the lead investigative agency page.
