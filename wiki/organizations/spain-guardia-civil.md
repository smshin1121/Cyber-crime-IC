## Summary

The **Grupo de Delitos Telemáticos (GDT)** is the specialized cybercrime unit of Spain's **Guardia Civil**, a paramilitary police force with national jurisdiction. Originally created in 1997 as the **Grupo de Delitos Informáticos (GDI)** with only four officers, it was renamed **GDT** in February 2003 as its scope expanded to cover the full range of telematic/technological crime.

GDT is *likely* the most recognizable Guardia Civil brand for cybercrime reporting in Spain, operating a well-known public portal (gdt.guardiacivil.es) for citizen reporting.

## Mandate and Authority

GDT is embedded within the **Unidad Central Operativa (UCO)** — the Guardia Civil's elite Central Operational Unit for serious and organized crime. It investigates:

- Computer-related crime (hacking, malware, denial-of-service)
- Cyber fraud and economic cybercrime
- Child sexual exploitation material online
- Technological crime against industry and critical infrastructure

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

- **Central GDT** in Madrid under the UCO
- **EDITEs** (Equipos de Investigación Tecnológica) — Technological Investigation Teams decentralized across Spanish provinces, created as a response to growing caseloads

Spain has a dual national cybercrime structure: GDT (Guardia Civil) and the Brigada Central de Investigación Tecnológica (BCIT) of the Policía Nacional, each handling cases within their territorial and functional mandates.
