## Summary

The **Grupo de Delitos Telematicos (GDT)** is the Guardia Civil's specialized cybercrime unit and one of the two main national cybercrime actors in Spain's dual law-enforcement model.

## Mandate and Authority

Official Guardia Civil material presents the GDT as its specialized unit for cybercrime and internet-related offences. The Council of Europe country profile on Spain also confirms that Spain's cybercrime structure relies on specialized units in both the **Guardia Civil** and the **Cuerpo Nacional de Policia**.

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

- specialized cybercrime capability inside the Guardia Civil
- part of Spain's dual national cybercrime model together with the National Police
- role in cyber-enabled crime investigation, prevention, and citizen-facing reporting

## IC Capabilities

- treaty-based cooperation under Spain's Budapest Convention framework
- participation in Spain's 24/7 contact-point and central-authority environment
- practical value in cross-border fraud, malware, and internet-enabled crime cases

## Division of Labor Within Spain

The public record does not present Spain's cybercrime model as a simple hierarchy with one lead force and one support force. Instead, the Council of Europe profile says that **both police forces share competence** and that incoming information can be sent through either 24/7 point of contact, with case allocation handled later under agreed internal rules.

That makes the GDT important in two ways. First, it is an operational cybercrime unit in its own right. Second, it is part of Spain's institutional redundancy for expedited cooperation, rather than just a domestic reporting channel subordinate to the National Police.

## Key Operations and Cases

Open-source institutional detail remains limited, but the GDT is a recognizable public-facing Spanish cybercrime brand and is structurally positioned to contribute to multinational Europol- and Budapest-linked cooperation activity.
