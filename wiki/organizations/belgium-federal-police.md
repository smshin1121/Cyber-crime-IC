## Summary

The **Federal Computer Crime Unit (FCCU)** is Belgium's specialized national cybercrime police unit, operating under the **Federal Judicial Police (Police Judiciaire Fédérale / Federale Gerechtelijke Politie)** within the central directorate for the fight against serious and organized crime. The FCCU is complemented by **Regional Computer Crime Units (RCCUs)** in each judicial district, creating a two-tier structure that combines national coordination with local investigative capacity [1].

The FCCU focuses on investigating attacks on critical infrastructure, organized international ICT crime, and other complex cyber threats, while supporting RCCUs in dealing with computer crime and telecommunications fraud. It is *likely* the primary Belgian point of contact for Europol EC3 and the Budapest Convention 24/7 Network, though this should be verified with the Council of Europe Octopus community page [1].

## Mandate and Authority

The FCCU operates under the Belgian Federal Police structure established by the 1998 police reform (Loi du 7 décembre 1998 organisant un service de police intégré). Its core missions include [1]:

- Fighting ICT crime and protecting citizens from criminality in the virtual society
- Supporting investigations into "traditional" crime through specialized ICT analysis
- Addressing internet-enabled child sexual exploitation, online fraud, and telecommunications fraud
- Providing technical support to RCCUs across Belgian judicial districts

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

- **FCCU (central):** National-level unit within the Federal Judicial Police central directorate
- **RCCUs (regional):** Computer crime units in each judicial district, under the judicial directors
- **Cooperation with CCB (Centre for Cybersecurity Belgium):** Non-police agency handling national cybersecurity policy and CERT functions

## IC Capabilities

- Digital forensics laboratory capacity
- Europol SIENA access through the Belgian Europol National Unit
- INTERPOL cooperation through NCB Brussels
- Multilingual capability (Dutch, French, English)
