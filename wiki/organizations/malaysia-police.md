## Summary

The Royal Malaysia Police (Polis Diraja Malaysia, **PDRM**) handles cybercrime investigations primarily through its **Commercial Crime Investigation Department (CCID)**, which was formed on **1 December 2004** and is the leading department in commercial and cybercrime investigations. CCID investigates and prosecutes white collar criminals who commit fraud, criminal breach of trust, cybercrime, forgeries, and related offences.

PDRM has participated in major transnational cybercrime operations with Five Eyes and regional partners. In **November 2023**, PDRM publicly disclosed a joint intelligence-sharing operation with the [[fbi-cyber-division|FBI]] and [[australia-afp|Australian Federal Police]] that crippled a transnational cybercrime syndicate — demonstrating operational cooperation capacity with major Western agencies.

## Mandate and Authority

PDRM derives its authority under Malaysia's federal constitution and Police Act 1967. Cybercrime-relevant statutes include:

- **Computer Crimes Act 1997** — Malaysia's primary cybercrime statute
- **Communications and Multimedia Act 1998** — telecommunications and online offences
- **Penal Code** — general criminal offences, including fraud
- **Anti-Money Laundering, Anti-Terrorism Financing and Proceeds of Unlawful Activities Act (AMLA) 2001**

Malaysia is *not* a party to the [[budapest-convention|Budapest Convention]] as of this writing, though Malaysian cybercrime law reform discussions (including at the CYDES 2025 conference) have considered potential alignment.

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC

- **Commercial Crime Investigation Department (CCID):** Federal-level investigative body for commercial and cybercrime.
- **Cybercrime Investigation Division / CyberCrime Investigation section:** CCID's dedicated cyber unit.
- **State-level CCID branches:** Replicated across Malaysian states for regional cybercrime investigation.
- **INTERPOL Kuala Lumpur:** PDRM operates the INTERPOL NCB; Kuala Lumpur also hosts the [[interpol-igci|INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation (IGCI)]], providing uniquely close proximity to INTERPOL cybercrime assets.

> [!note] The host relationship with INTERPOL IGCI (opened 2015, Singapore and later consolidated activities) gives Malaysia/PDRM notable institutional access to global INTERPOL cybercrime operations, though IGCI is in Singapore, not Malaysia. This should be verified — some sources conflate the IGCI location.

## IC Capabilities

- **FBI cooperation:** The November 2023 joint takedown with FBI and AFP demonstrates operational-level cooperation capacity.
- **INTERPOL:** Strong NCB relationship; Malaysia is an active INTERPOL member.
- **ASEANAPOL:** Malaysia is a founding member of ASEANAPOL, the Association of ASEAN Chiefs of Police, which coordinates regional cooperation.
- **ASEAN cybercrime cooperation:** Malaysia participates in ASEAN senior officials meetings on transnational crime (SOMTC).

## Key Operations and Cases

- **November 2023 joint operation (FBI + AFP + PDRM):** Transnational cybercrime syndicate disruption. Details limited in public reporting.

## Cooperation Track Record

PDRM's cooperation capacity is *likely* moderate-to-high for regional and Five Eyes-coordinated operations, particularly on scam compound and romance fraud investigations (given the region's exposure to Southeast Asian scam compound activity). *Highly likely* active in cross-border scam enforcement with Singapore, Thailand, and the Philippines.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- The exact name and internal structure of PDRM's dedicated cybercrime unit vary across sources — "CyberCrime Investigation Division" may be an informal reference rather than a formal organizational name.
- Malaysia's cybercrime law reform trajectory (toward or away from Budapest Convention alignment) remains under negotiation.
- PDRM's cooperation with neighboring Thailand and Myanmar on scam compound enforcement requires more open-source documentation.
