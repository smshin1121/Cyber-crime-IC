## Summary

The **United Nations (UN)** is the institutional frame for the UN cybercrime treaty ecosystem in this repository. Its concrete cybercrime relevance is handled through [[unga]] as the adopting political body, [[unodc]] as the crime-and-drugs secretariat actor, and [[un-cybercrime-convention-2024]] as the treaty record.

## Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The UN does not function as a police agency. Its role is to create and host multilateral processes that can produce binding instruments, technical assistance, and state cooperation mechanisms. The local source set records the United Nations Convention against Cybercrime as the first globally negotiated binding treaty on cybercrime, adopted in 2024 and opened for signature in Hanoi in 2025.
