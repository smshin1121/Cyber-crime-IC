## Summary

**RCMP Federal Policing** is the parent operational context for Canada's federal cybercrime coordination function, including the [[canada-rcmp|National Cybercrime Coordination Centre (NC3)]]. The local RCMP source set ties Canadian cybercrime cooperation to the Budapest Convention framework and describes NC3 as the federal government's cybercrime coordination hub under the RCMP.

## Cybercrime Cooperation Role

The RCMP appears in the corpus as a recurring Canadian participant in multinational cybercrime operations, including VPN and malware-infrastructure takedowns. The more specialized [[canada-rcmp|NC3]] page should be used for cybercrime coordination, public reporting, and national triage. This Federal Policing page is the broader parent entry for RCMP federal enforcement posture, international cooperation, and cases where a source names "RCMP" without identifying NC3.

## IC Relevance

Canada is a Five Eyes and Budapest Convention partner, so RCMP Federal Policing is a useful bridge between Canadian cybercrime reporting, serious organized crime investigations, and international police cooperation. Use it for parent-organization context and link the NC3 page where the issue is specifically cybercrime intake, coordination, or the national reporting mechanism.
