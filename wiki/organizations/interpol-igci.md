## Summary

The INTERPOL Global Complex for Innovation (IGCI), located in Singapore, is INTERPOL's primary hub for cybercrime operations and innovation. Opened in 2015, it provides operational support, digital forensics capabilities, and capacity building for INTERPOL's 196 member countries.

## IC Capabilities

- **Cyber Fusion Centre:** Aggregates cyber threat intelligence from law enforcement, private sector, and academia to support investigations
- **I-24/7 Network:** Secure global police communications network connecting all 196 NCBs
- **Digital forensics:** Advanced laboratory supporting member countries
- **Operational coordination:** Coordinates multi-country cyber operations (HAECHI, Synergia)
- **Capacity building:** Training programs for developing countries

## Key Operations

### HAECHI Series (Financial Cybercrime)

The HAECHI series, led by [[south-korea|Korea]], is INTERPOL's flagship financial cybercrime operation:

| Operation | Year | Countries | Arrests | Seized |
|-----------|------|-----------|---------|--------|
| HAECHI III | 2022 | 30 | 975 | ~$130M | [^haechi3]
| [[operation-haechi-iv\|HAECHI IV]] | 2023 | 34 | 3,500 | $300M |

| **[[operation-haechi-v\|HAECHI V]]** | **2024** | **40** | **5,500+** | **$400M+** |
| [[operation-haechi-vi\|HAECHI VI]] | 2025 | 40 | - | $439M |

**[[operation-haechi-v|Operation HAECHI V]]** (Jul-Nov 2024): The largest HAECHI operation to date. 40 countries, **5,500+ arrests**, **USD 400M+ seized**. Korean-Chinese joint sub-operation dismantled a voice phishing syndicate responsible for USD 1.1 billion in losses affecting 1,900+ victims. Targeted voice phishing, romance scams, online sextortion, investment fraud, illegal online gambling, BEC, and e-commerce fraud.

### Synergia Series (Cyber Infrastructure)

- **Operation Synergia** (Sep-Nov 2023): Coordinated across 60+ countries. **1,300+ suspicious servers** linked to phishing/malware/ransomware taken down. [^synergia1]

[^synergia1]: > [!warning] Source needed — Operation Synergia (2023) statistics are not from a collected source. Only Synergia II (2024) has a collected source.
- **[[operation-synergia-ii|Operation Synergia II]]** (Apr-Aug 2024): **95 countries** participated. ~30,000 suspicious IPs identified, **22,800 taken down** (76%). **59 servers seized, 43 devices seized, 41 arrests**. Private sector partners: Group-IB, Trend Micro, Kaspersky, Team Cymru.

### First Light Series (Online Scams)

- **[[operation-first-light-2024|Operation First Light 2024]]** (Mar-May 2024): **61 countries**, **3,950 arrests**, **USD 257 million seized**, **14,643 additional suspects** identified. Bolstered by AFRIPOL, ASEANAPOL, GCCPOL, and Europol. Utilized I-GRIP mechanism.

### Africa-Focused Operations

- **[[operation-serengeti|Operation Serengeti]]** (Sep-Oct 2024): Joint INTERPOL-[[afripol|AFRIPOL]] operation across **19 African countries**. **1,006 arrests**, **134,089 malicious infrastructures dismantled**, 35,000+ victims identified, USD 193M in financial losses linked. Targeted ransomware, BEC, digital extortion, online scams. Funded by UK FCDO, German Federal Foreign Office, Council of Europe.
- **[[operation-sentinel-africa|Operation Sentinel]]** (Oct-Nov 2025): **19 African countries**, **574 arrests**, **USD 3 million recovered**, **6,000+ malicious links** taken down. BEC, digital extortion, ransomware. Funded by UK FCDO and GLACY-e.

## Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

Korea is a significant INTERPOL partner, particularly through the IGCI Singapore hub:
- KNPA serves as INTERPOL's National Central Bureau (NCB) for Korea
- Korea led the **Operation HAECHI** series
- Korean officers contribute to INTERPOL working groups and secondments
