> [!warning] Status — Renamed and restructured
> The current **과학기술정보통신부 (Ministry of Science and ICT, MSIT)** was created in its present form on **2017-07-26** under the Moon Jae-in administration, inheriting the functions of the prior **미래창조과학부 (Ministry of Science, ICT and Future Planning, MSIP, 2013–2017)**, which itself was created by merging parts of the earlier **정보통신부 (Ministry of Information and Communication)** and other bodies. The English short form has occasionally been confused with "Ministry of Science, ICT and Future Planning" or miswritten as "과학수사정보통신부" (an incorrect form conflating 과학수사 and 과기정통부). The correct current name is **과학기술정보통신부 / Ministry of Science and ICT** [1][2].
>
> As of early 2026 MSIT is undergoing an **internal restructuring** centered on information security: the **네트워크정책실** has been renamed **정보보호네트워크정책실**, a **5-person 사이버침해조사팀** has been newly created to handle cyber-incident investigation, and Minister 배경훈 has publicly signaled that a broader government-level reorganization of the national information-protection apparatus is under active discussion [3][4]. These changes are a response to the 2025 large-scale data breaches (KT, SKT, Coupang, telecom providers) and do not involve any change to MSIT's legal identity.

## Summary

The **Ministry of Science and ICT (과학기술정보통신부, MSIT)** is South Korea's central government department responsible for science, ICT, broadcasting, information security policy, and AI policy. In the international cooperation on cybercrime ecosystem, MSIT's role is **policy-making and oversight**, not investigation or prosecution. Its primary operational arm is [[kisa|KISA]] (한국인터넷진흥원), which operates KrCERT/CC as Korea's national CSIRT and handles technical incident coordination with foreign counterparts.

MSIT is *not* a law enforcement agency. Criminal cybercrime cases are handled by the [[knpa-cyber-bureau|Korea National Police Agency]] and, after October 2026, the [[supreme-prosecutors-office-korea|공소청·중수청 successor entities]] depending on final organizational structure.

## Mandate and Authority

- **Policy authority:** Information and Communications Network Act (정보통신망법), Information Protection Industry Promotion Act, and related statutes
- **Supervisory authority over KISA**: KISA is a public corporation under MSIT; its budget, strategic direction, and leadership are subject to MSIT oversight
- **National cybersecurity policy coordination**: works with 국가정보원 (NIS) on public-sector and critical-infrastructure security policy; with KISA/KrCERT/CC on private-sector incident response
- **International engagement**: ITU, APEC TEL, ASEAN-ROK cooperation on ICT/cybersecurity capacity building
- **Not a law-enforcement agency**: no arrest or investigation power; criminal cybercrime cases are handled by police and, as applicable, prosecution/수사청 entities

## Structure Relevant to Cybercrime IC (post-2026-01)

- **정보보호네트워크정책실 (Information Protection and Network Policy Office)**: renamed and expanded from 네트워크정책실
- **사이버침해조사팀 (Cyber Breach Investigation Team)**: 5-person ministry-level team created 2026 for cyber-incident investigation and coordination
- **국제협력관 / 디지털국제협력관**: international ICT cooperation; participates in ITU, APEC TEL, ASEAN-ROK cooperation
- **[[kisa|KISA]] (subordinate agency)**: operational arm for cybersecurity, CSIRT, and technical international cooperation

## Korean Interactions (한국과의 협력)

MSIT is the **policy-level** counterpart for international ICT and cybersecurity cooperation. For operational cybercrime cooperation, foreign counterparts typically engage either:
- [[kisa|KISA/KrCERT/CC]] for technical/incident response
- [[knpa-cyber-bureau|KNPA cyber enforcement line]] for criminal investigation
- [[spo-international-cooperation|대검찰청 (→ 공소청/중수청 from 2026-10)]] for prosecutorial/MLAT cooperation

- Whether MSIT's 사이버침해조사팀 has any operational overlap with KNPA cybercrime investigation or KISA/KrCERT/CC incident response — not yet clearly delineated in public sources
