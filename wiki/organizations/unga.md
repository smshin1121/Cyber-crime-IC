## Summary

The **United Nations General Assembly (UNGA)** is the sponsoring and adoption body for the UN cybercrime treaty process tracked in this corpus. The source set records that UNGA Resolution 74/247 created the Ad Hoc Committee process and that the United Nations Convention against Cybercrime was adopted by the General Assembly on 2024-12-24.

## Cybercrime Cooperation Role

UNGA's role is normative, not operational. It does not conduct arrests or investigations. Its relevance is that it supplies the political mandate for global treaty negotiation and adoption, while [[unodc]] and the treaty process handle secretariat, implementation, and future conference-of-states work.

## IC Relevance

Use this page when discussing the institutional path from UNGA resolution to the [[un-cybercrime-convention-2024|UN Cybercrime Convention]]. Use [[unodc]] for implementation support and technical assistance, and [[council-of-europe]] for the Budapest Convention ecosystem.
