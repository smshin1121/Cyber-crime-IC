## Summary

On **2025-07-16** the US Attorney's Office for the District of Oregon announced the **2025-06-18 extradition from Ukraine** of **Karen Serobovich Vardanyan**, a 33-year-old Armenian national, to face federal charges for his role in **Ryuk ransomware attacks** (March 2019 – September 2020) targeting an Oregon technology company and other US victims. Vardanyan is one of four defendants charged in the District of Oregon: the others are **Levon Georgiyovych Avetisyan** (Armenia, 45 — in custody France; pending US extradition request), **Oleg Nikolayevich Lyulyava** (Ukraine, 53 — at large), and **Andrii Leonydovich Prykhodchenko** (Ukraine, 53 — at large). The defendants are alleged to have received approximately **1,610 bitcoins** (valued at over **USD 15 million** at the time of payment) in ransom payments.

## Facts

According to the cited DOJ release, between March 2019 and September 2020 Vardanyan and his co-conspirators illegally accessed computer networks of victim companies to deploy Ryuk ransomware on hundreds of compromised servers and workstations. Ryuk encrypts data and prevents access to the encrypted files until a ransom is paid. Ryuk targets recorded by DOJ across the broader campaign include: private industry, state and local municipalities, local school districts, critical infrastructure, hospitals and other healthcare services and providers. Ransom notes demanded payment in Bitcoin and provided an email address for victim communications.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

US federal jurisdiction in the District of Oregon attached through the Oregon-based victim technology company among the broader US-victim cohort. Defendant Vardanyan was extradited from Ukraine following an April 2025 arrest at the request of US authorities. Avetisyan's extradition from France is pending as of 2025-07-16.

### Charges

- **Conspiracy** (18 U.S.C. § 371)
- **Fraud in connection with computers** (18 U.S.C. § 1030)
- **Extortion in connection with computers** (18 U.S.C. § 1030 / threat-related provisions)

Maximum sentence per count: 5 years federal prison, 3 years supervised release, USD 250,000 fine.

### Precedent Value

This is the first wiki record explicitly capturing a Ryuk ransomware US prosecution thread, with **Ukraine as the extradition source** for the lead defendant. The case adds Ryuk to the wiki's Conti-family / Conti-successor ransomware prosecution corpus, alongside Nefilim ([[us-v-tymoshchuk-nefilim-megacortex-lockergoga|Tymoshchuk]], [[us-v-stryzhak-nefilim|Stryzhak]]) and Conti/Karakurt/Royal/TommyLeaks/SchoolBoys/Akira ([[us-v-deniss-zolotarjovs|Zolotarjovs]]). Two Armenian + two Ukrainian defendants demonstrates the cross-Caucasus / cross-Eastern-European recruitment pattern of Ryuk-affiliate operations.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The case demonstrates a Ukraine-as-extradition-source pattern for Ryuk ransomware prosecutions — distinct from the Schengen-region pattern recorded for [[us-v-stryzhak-nefilim|Stryzhak (Spain)]], the Council-of-Europe pattern recorded for [[us-v-deniss-zolotarjovs|Zolotarjovs (Georgia)]], and the parallel France-pending track for Avetisyan in this very case. Ukraine's cooperation is explicitly credited by the USAO-OR. The DOJ Office of International Affairs (OIA) is named as the channel of significant assistance.

## Korean Relevance (한국 관련성)

South Korea is not named in the cited DOJ release among the participating jurisdictions, the underlying victim cohort, or the defendant nationalities. The case is recorded in the wiki for the Ukraine-as-extradition-source pattern in Conti-family ransomware prosecutions and the cross-Caucasus / cross-Eastern-European Ryuk-affiliate recruitment pattern. Korean exposure to Ryuk ransomware attacks is *likely* present in the broader Ryuk victim cohort but is not enumerated in this source.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- The cited release does not enumerate the specific Oregon technology company victim by name.
- Sentencing dates and post-trial outcomes are not enumerated (the trial was scheduled for 2025-08-26 but the cited release predates the trial).
- The status of the France-pending extradition request for Avetisyan is described as "pending" and post-2025-07-16 status changes are not enumerated.
- Whether the at-large defendants Lyulyava and Prykhodchenko have associated INTERPOL Red Notices or US State Department reward offers is not enumerated.
- The relationship between this Ryuk thread and the broader Conti-family prosecution corpus ([[us-v-tymoshchuk-nefilim-megacortex-lockergoga|Tymoshchuk]], [[us-v-stryzhak-nefilim|Stryzhak]], [[us-v-deniss-zolotarjovs|Zolotarjovs]]) is structural rather than evidential — DOJ has not (in this release) attributed Ryuk to Conti-successor leadership directly.
