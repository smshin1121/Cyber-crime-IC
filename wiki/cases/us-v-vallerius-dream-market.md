## Summary

United States v. Vallerius is a significant Dream Market prosecution involving a senior administrator and vendor. It is one of the clearer DOJ cases connecting dark-web marketplace management to both narcotics trafficking and cryptocurrency laundering.

## Facts

DOJ said Vallerius first sold oxycodone and Ritalin on Dream Market under the moniker "Oxymonster" and later became an administrator and senior moderator. In that role, he supported daily transactions and laundering activity on one of the largest dark web marketplaces of its era.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Asset Recovery

The most visible financial component was the forfeiture of substantial bitcoin and bitcoin cash holdings.

### Transnational Dimension

Vallerius was a French national arrested while traveling to the United States. Even in the absence of a detailed public MLA narrative, the case clearly reflects the transnational reach of U.S. dark web enforcement against foreign marketplace administrators.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case has high value because it shows that high-level marketplace moderators and administrators can face long prison sentences and large crypto forfeitures.
