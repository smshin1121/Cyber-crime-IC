## Summary

United States v. Jones is a Silk Road vendor case involving hundreds of completed online drug sales under multiple aliases.

## Facts

DOJ said Jones operated on Silk Road under aliases including "Caligirl" and distributed oxycodone and other controlled substances through the marketplace between April and September 2013. The public announcement frames the case as part of the early wave of follow-on prosecutions that converted marketplace transaction records into named federal defendants after the original Silk Road seizure.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Evidence Context

No extradition or foreign arrest was required, but the case depended on evidence recovered from the Silk Road platform and associated investigative records developed in the broader post-takedown federal investigation.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case is useful because it shows how DOJ moved from marketplace takedown to downstream vendor sentencing, establishing that the original Silk Road operation generated a durable stream of prosecutable vendor cases rather than only a single operator prosecution.
