## Summary

On **29 April 2026**, **Patrick Schmitz** — a 37-year-old German national who had been residing in Taganga, Colombia — was extradited from Colombia to the United States and arraigned in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey on an **8-count indictment** for cofounding and operating **'The Versus Project'** dark web marketplace (Nov 2019 – May 2022; **380,000+ registered users**, **32,000+ product listings**, **300,000+ completed orders**, **millions of dollars in cryptocurrency transactions**). Schmitz had been arrested in Colombia in **June 2024** pursuant to a US provisional arrest request — a 22-month gap from arrest to extradition.

The case is publicly identified by **DOJ-OPA Press Release 26-428** dated 30 April 2026.

> [!note] Continuing criminal enterprise (CCE) framing
> The lead count is **continuing criminal enterprise** under 21 U.S.C. § 848, carrying a 20-year mandatory minimum and a maximum of life imprisonment. This is an *atypical* charging framework for a dark web *marketplace operator* (more commonly applied to large-scale narcotics trafficking principals); its use here against a marketplace cofounder is *likely* to test the bounds of CCE liability theory for senior dark web platform staff.

## Facts

The Versus Project ("Versus") was a leading dark web marketplace that enabled its over 380,000 registered users to buy and sell illegal goods. Versus operated from about **November 2019 through about May 2022** and during that period offered:

- **Drugs** (heroin and other illicit drugs)
- **Fraud** items (stolen and fraudulent identification documents and access devices)
- **Digital items** (counterfeit currency)
- **Services**
- **Software & malware** (malware and hacking tools)

Versus was modeled after an e-commerce website. Users could choose a username and password to create a free account, then search for products by keyword or scroll through listings by category. Versus required transactions in **Bitcoin and Monero only** — no fiat — explicitly designed to bypass traditional financial systems' KYC/AML programs. Versus generated revenue through (a) a percentage commission on each completed transaction, and (b) penalties charged to vendors found to violate Versus' rules.

Per the indictment, **Patrick Schmitz, 37, of Taganga, Colombia**, was a cofounder of Versus and handled the day-to-day management:

- Responding to user tickets for assistance with platform issues
- Reviewing vendor applications
- Resolving disputes between vendors and customers
- Recruiting and supervising staff who reported to him
- Promoting Versus on the dark web
- Recruiting vendors
- Developing strategies to monetize Versus

Schmitz received a portion of the profits, and his virtual currency wallets transacted in cryptocurrency worth millions of dollars.

### Evidence Gathering

- HSI Newark led the US-side investigation, with parallel support from the **FBI Kansas City Field Office** (Versus jurisdictional nexus implied) and **IRS-CI Newark Field Office** (cryptocurrency-tracing track).
- **DOJ Office of the Judicial Attaché in Bogotá** and **Policía Nacional de Colombia** provided assistance concerning Schmitz's provisional arrest and extradition.

### Arrest & Extradition

- **June 2024**: Provisional arrest in Colombia pursuant to a US provisional arrest request. The cited release does not specify the legal instrument; the operative framework is the standing US-Colombia extradition treaty.
- **29 April 2026**: Extradition completed; same-day arraignment in D.N.J.; Schmitz ordered detained pending trial.
- The **DOJ Office of International Affairs** and **US Marshals Service** provided assistance with the arrest and extradition.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

US extraterritorial jurisdiction grounds: (a) US-resident user base on Versus; (b) US-physical-impact via narcotics trafficked to US; (c) US-banking-system bypass via Bitcoin/Monero. The cited release does not specify the jurisdictional theory beyond this pattern.

### Key Legal Issues

1. **Continuing criminal enterprise (CCE)** under 21 U.S.C. § 848 — applied to a dark web marketplace cofounder rather than a traditional narcotics-trafficking principal. Element of "five or more persons" in concerted action is *likely* satisfied by the documented staff-supervision facts (Schmitz "recruited and supervised staff who worked on Versus and reported to him"). 20-year mandatory minimum.
2. **Narcotics conspiracy and importation conspiracy** — predicate to CCE; 10-year mandatory minimum on conspiracy count.
3. **Distribution of controlled substances by means of the internet** — internet-specific narcotics-trafficking statute (21 U.S.C. § 841 / 829A); max 20 years.
4. **Conspiracy to commit access device fraud** — fraud-side counts driven by the "fraud" / "stolen and fraudulent identification documents and access devices" product categories.
5. **Conspiracy to unlawfully transfer an identification document** — fraudulent-ID-trafficking framework; max 15 years.
6. **Money laundering conspiracy** — driven by the Bitcoin/Monero-only payment rail; max 20 years.

### Precedent Value

- **CCE charging against a dark web marketplace cofounder** rather than a top-of-stack drug-trafficking organization principal extends CCE liability theory into platform-operator territory.
- **22-month Colombia-US extradition timeline** for a darknet marketplace cybercrime case sets a benchmark observation; comparable to other US-Colombia cybercrime extraditions.
- **JCODE attribution** (Joint Criminal Opioid Darknet Enforcement, FBI-led) confirms the operational template by which DOJ pursues darknet marketplaces selling controlled substances.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The 22-month Colombia-US extradition gap represents a *moderate-pace* extradition for a dark web marketplace cybercrime case. The case demonstrates:

- The **HSI Newark + FBI Kansas City + IRS-CI Newark + DOJ-OIA + US Marshals + Policía Nacional de Colombia** stack as a working operational template for US-Colombia darknet marketplace extraditions.
- Continuing functionality of US-Colombia extradition cooperation under the existing bilateral treaty for cybercrime / narcotics-conspiracy / money-laundering offences.
- The **JCODE darknet-opioid framework** as a sustained DOJ initiative against darknet drug-trafficking infrastructure beyond ad-hoc indictments.
