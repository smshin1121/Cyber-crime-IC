## Summary

United States v. Arias is a guilty-plea case against one of the individuals behind the darknet fentanyl vendor identity "Drug Llama."

## Facts

DOJ said Arias admitted that he was one of the two people behind the "Drug Llama" identity, which distributed misbranded fentanyl pills nationwide through the dark web. The case is analytically useful because it disaggregates a vendor persona into identifiable real-world participants.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Evidence Context

No foreign arrest or MLA request is identified in the available source. The important cooperation feature is instead attribution: investigators were able to convert a stable marketplace identity into named defendants and a federal plea.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case has practical value because it shows how prosecutors can charge shared ownership or operation of a well-known darknet vendor brand without needing to prove a single-person enterprise.
