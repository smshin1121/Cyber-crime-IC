## Summary

United States v. Banmeet Singh is a major transnational dark web narcotics case involving sales across Silk Road, AlphaBay, Hansa, and other markets. It is one of the most financially significant dark web vendor cases in the dataset.

## Facts

DOJ said Singh sold controlled substances through vendor pages on Silk Road, AlphaBay, Hansa, and other marketplaces while arranging shipment from Europe to the United States. The plea announcement is notable for the scale of the agreed forfeiture, which far exceeds most vendor-level dark web cases in the dataset.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Transnational Distribution

The case has an obvious cross-border structure: Singh is an Indian national, the drugs were shipped from Europe, and the prosecution proceeded in the United States after extradition from the United Kingdom. Even without the full court file, the public source makes this one of the clearest multinational dark web supply-chain cases in the collection.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case is especially important because it shows that prosecutors can treat a dark web vendor enterprise as a truly international narcotics network rather than a purely domestic online storefront.
