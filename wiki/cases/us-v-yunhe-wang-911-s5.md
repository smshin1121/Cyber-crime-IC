## Summary

United States v. YunHe Wang is the central U.S. criminal case tied to the 911 S5 botnet dismantling announced in May 2024. DOJ stated that Wang operated a large residential proxy service built from millions of compromised devices and enabled a broad range of cyber-enabled offenses worldwide.

## Facts

The indictment was filed in the Southern District of Texas and announced alongside coordinated enforcement actions involving partners in Germany, Singapore, and Thailand. DOJ alleged that 911 S5 compromised more than 19 million IP addresses and generated tens of millions of dollars in criminal proceeds.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Foreign Enforcement Support

The public release attributes the disruption to multinational cooperation among U.S., German, Singaporean, and Thai authorities. That strongly indicates cross-border evidence gathering and operational support rather than a purely domestic U.S. investigation.

### Asset Measures

DOJ paired the indictment with forfeiture allegations, while Treasury announced related sanctions. This combination likely increased the disruption effect beyond the criminal charges alone.
