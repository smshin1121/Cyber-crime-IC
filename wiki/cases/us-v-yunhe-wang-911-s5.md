## Summary

United States v. YunHe Wang is the central U.S. criminal case tied to the 911 S5 botnet dismantling announced in May 2024. DOJ stated that Wang operated a large residential proxy service built from millions of compromised devices and enabled a broad range of cyber-enabled offenses worldwide.

## Facts

The indictment was filed in the Southern District of Texas and announced alongside coordinated enforcement actions involving partners in Germany, Singapore, and Thailand. DOJ alleged that 911 S5 compromised more than 19 million IP addresses and generated tens of millions of dollars in criminal proceeds.

The public announcement described 911 S5 as a residential proxy service built from compromised devices rather than a conventional botnet used only for spam or DDoS. That distinction matters because the service allegedly enabled downstream fraud, bomb threats, child exploitation offenses, and sanctions evasion by selling access to infected IP addresses.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Foreign Enforcement Support

The public release attributes the disruption to multinational cooperation among U.S., German, Singaporean, and Thai authorities. That strongly indicates cross-border evidence gathering and operational support rather than a purely domestic U.S. investigation.

### Asset Measures

DOJ paired the indictment with forfeiture allegations, while Treasury announced related sanctions. This combination likely increased the disruption effect beyond the criminal charges alone.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

U.S. jurisdiction was *likely* grounded in the service's extensive effects on U.S. victims, the use of U.S.-linked communications and financial channels, and the ability of U.S. authorities to tie criminal proceeds and infrastructure to the charged conduct.

### Key Legal Issues

- **Residential proxy as criminal infrastructure:** The case helps frame botnet-enabled proxy services as prosecutable core infrastructure rather than merely neutral routing tools.
- **Cross-border seizure and arrest coordination:** The prosecution appears to depend on synchronized action across several jurisdictions rather than unilateral U.S. arrest power.

### Precedent Value

The case has *high* practical value because it links a globally distributed proxy-botnet service to a named federal indictment and parallel sanctions, providing a concrete courtroom anchor for a major multinational disruption.
