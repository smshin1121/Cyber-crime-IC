## Summary

On 2026-02-19 Romanian national **Catalin Dragomir**, 45, formerly of Constanta, Romania, pleaded guilty in the District of Oregon to one count of obtaining information from a protected computer and one count of aggravated identity theft for his role as an **initial-access broker (IAB)**. According to court documents, in June 2021 Dragomir gained unauthorized access to a computer on the network of an Oregon state government office and then sold that access — providing the prospective buyer with samples of personal identifying information from the compromised computer — and similarly sold access to the computer networks of numerous other US victims, causing losses of at least **USD 250,000**.

This case is a US-Romania bilateral extradition record completed under DOJ Office of International Affairs coordination with the Romanian Ministry of Justice's Directorate for International Law and Judicial Cooperation and the Romanian judiciary. Dragomir was arrested in Romania in November 2024 and extradited to the United States in January 2025 (~2-month transit). He is scheduled for sentencing on **2026-05-26** with a statutory maximum penalty of 5 years (computer-information offence) followed by a mandatory consecutive 2-year sentence for aggravated identity theft.

## Facts

Per DOJ-charging language, Dragomir's IAB methodology involved (a) obtaining unauthorized access to victim computer networks (Oregon state government office named, June 2021), (b) marketing the access to prospective buyers with samples of personally identifying information taken from the compromised systems, and (c) selling the access to multiple US-victim networks for a cumulative loss of at least USD 250,000. The release does not enumerate the specific Oregon state government office, the identities of the access-buyers, or the named US-victim categories beyond the Oregon government office.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

US federal jurisdiction in the District of Oregon attached through the named Oregon state government victim. Defendant was extradited from Romania in January 2025 under DOJ OIA coordination with the Romanian Ministry of Justice's Directorate for International Law and Judicial Cooperation.

### Key Legal Issues

- **Obtaining information from a protected computer** (5-year statutory maximum).
- **Aggravated identity theft** (2-year mandatory consecutive sentence).
- **Initial-access broker (IAB) marketplace conduct** — the public-record description of the IAB sales process, including pre-sale "samples of personal identifying information," provides operational visibility into the IAB market that is structurally relevant to the broader ransomware-affiliate corpus.

### Precedent Value

This is the first wiki-recorded US-Romania bilateral extradition pathway record for an IAB prosecution. The ~2-month US-Romania extradition transit (November 2024 arrest → January 2025 US custody) is materially faster than comparable European bilateral extradition pathways:

- US-Italy (Volkov Yanluowang): ~7-month transit (January 2024 arrest → August 2024 US custody)
- US-Georgia (Zolotarjovs Conti/Karakurt): ~8-month transit (December 2023 arrest → August 2024 US custody, after contested extradition)
- US-Spain (Stryzhak Nefilim): ~10-month transit (June 2024 arrest → April 2025 US custody)
- US-Romania (Dragomir IAB): ~2-month transit (November 2024 arrest → January 2025 US custody)

This is consistent with Romania's position as an EU member state with longstanding bilateral cooperation infrastructure with the United States and an established Romanian Ministry of Justice judicial-cooperation directorate.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The case exemplifies a fast US-Romania bilateral extradition pathway, with full DOJ-OIA coordination with named Romanian counterparts (Ministry of Justice — Directorate for International Law and Judicial Cooperation, Romanian judiciary). The FBI Portland Field Office investigated the case, and Darkweb IQ is publicly thanked as a private-sector cooperation partner.
