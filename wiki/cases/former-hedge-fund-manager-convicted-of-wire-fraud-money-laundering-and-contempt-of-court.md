## Facts

The Northern District of California source identifies the defendant as James Murray, formerly of Larkspur, and describes him as the sole member and investment advisor of Market Neutral Trading, LLC. A jury convicted him after a three-week trial on 22 felony counts, including wire fraud, money laundering through criminally derived property, aggravated identity theft, and contempt of court.

The fraud theory centered on two related schemes. First, Murray allegedly used Jones, Moore & Associates, a sham auditor he controlled, to make Market Neutral Trading appear audited and profitable. Second, he allegedly ran fraudulent credit-card transactions through the sham entity's merchant account, processing more than USD 650,000 in transactions and creating more than USD 550,000 in losses to the processor.

Investor-loss allegations were also substantial. The indictment described more than USD 2.5 million in investor losses, false monthly performance numbers, a fake resume, and a July 2012 representation to a New York brokerage that Market Neutral Trading had USD 5 million in available assets for a Netflix short-sale transaction. Those facts make the page relevant to investment-fraud, identity-theft, and money-laundering classification, even though it is not primarily a cross-border cyber operation.
