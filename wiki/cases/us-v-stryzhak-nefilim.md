## Summary

On 2025-12-18 the US Justice Department announced the guilty plea in the Eastern District of New York of Ukrainian national **Artem Aleksandrovych Stryzhak**, 35, of Barcelona, Spain, to one count of conspiracy to commit computer fraud for his role as a **Nefilim ransomware affiliate**. Stryzhak was arrested in Spain in June 2024 and extradited to the United States on **2025-04-30**. He is scheduled for sentencing on **2026-05-06** with a statutory maximum penalty of 10 years.

This case is a US-Spain bilateral extradition record completed under DOJ Office of International Affairs coordination with Spanish law enforcement authorities. The plea closes out one strand of the broader DOJ enforcement against Nefilim / LockerGoga / MegaCortex ransomware operators; the named administrator-tier co-conspirator **Volodymyr Tymoshchuk** remains a fugitive with a US State Department Transnational Organized Crime (TOC) reward of up to **USD 11 million**.

## Facts

According to court documents, Stryzhak gained access to the Nefilim ransomware code in **June 2021** through the operator-known online platform ("panel") in exchange for **20 percent** of his ransom proceeds. Nefilim administrators preferred to target companies located in the **United States, Canada, or Australia** with annual revenues exceeding **USD 100 million**, and in or about July 2021 directed Stryzhak to focus on companies with more than **USD 200 million** in annual revenue.

The Nefilim group's extortion methodology, as captured in DOJ-charging language, included generating a unique ransomware executable file for each victim, a corresponding decryption key, and a customised ransom note. The group used a public victim-leak site named **"Corporate Leaks"** to publish stolen data when victims declined to pay the ransom. Stryzhak and others researched potential victims after gaining unauthorised access to their networks, including by using online databases to obtain information about the companies' net worth, size, and contact information.

In one captured operational-security exchange, Stryzhak asked a co-conspirator whether he should choose a different username than one he used in other criminal activity, in case the panel "gets hacked into by the feds."

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

US federal jurisdiction in the Eastern District of New York attached through the conspiracy and computer-fraud counts, with US-jurisdiction victims included in the named victim categories (US/Canada/Australia companies with USD 100M+ annual revenue). Defendant was extradited from Spain on 2025-04-30 under DOJ OIA coordination.

### Key Legal Issues

- **Conspiracy to commit fraud related to computers** (single count, statutory maximum 10 years).
- **Affiliate-tier compensation structure documented** — the 20% revenue split between Nefilim administrators and affiliates is captured directly in DOJ-charging language, providing a public-record reference for the ransomware-affiliate compensation model.
- **Bilateral US-Spain extradition under contested-extradition timeline** — June 2024 arrest → April 30, 2025 US custody (~10-month transit), comparable to but slightly faster than the Volkov US-Italy pathway (Jan 2024 → Aug 2024) and the Zolotarjovs US-Georgia pathway (Dec 2023 → Aug 2024).

### Precedent Value

The case adds a third 2024-2025 European-arrest US-extradition pathway record for ransomware-affiliate prosecution alongside [[us-v-volkov-yanluowang-sentencing|Volkov (Italy)]] and [[us-v-deniss-zolotarjovs|Zolotarjovs (Georgia)]]. The Nefilim affiliate-tier compensation structure (20% revenue split), victim-targeting profile (US/Canada/Australia, USD 100M-200M revenue companies), and "Corporate Leaks" leak-site naming captured in DOJ-charging language extend the wiki corpus's documentation of ransomware-organisation operating economics.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The case exemplifies a US-Spain bilateral extradition pathway with a ~10-month transit (June 2024 arrest → April 30 2025 US custody). The DOJ release credits Spanish law enforcement authorities, the Criminal Division's Office of International Affairs, and the FBI's New York Field Office with substantial assistance in the case. The FBI Springfield Field Office (Illinois) is named as the investigating field office.

## Korean Relevance (한국 관련성)

South Korea is not named in the cited DOJ release as a victim, source, or cooperation jurisdiction for this case. The case is recorded in the wiki for the public-record Nefilim affiliate-tier prosecution and the US-Spain extradition pathway. Korean exposure to Nefilim / LockerGoga / MegaCortex ransomware is not directly informed by this source, but the affiliate-tier compensation structure (20% revenue split) is a structurally relevant data point for the broader Korean ransomware IC corpus, given that Nefilim's victim-targeting profile (US/Canada/Australia, USD 100M+ revenue companies) excluded Korea from the named primary-victim list.
