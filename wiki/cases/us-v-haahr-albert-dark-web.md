## Summary

United States v. Haahr and Albert is a recent guilty-plea case tied to dark web fentanyl-pill distribution from Washington state.

## Facts

According to DOJ, Haahr operated the dark web sales side while Albert supplied or supported fentanyl-pill distribution. The plea announcement is recent and therefore useful as evidence that the dark web fentanyl-pill model remains active well after the takedown of earlier flagship markets.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Evidence Context

The public release focuses on domestic investigative steps rather than foreign assistance. The case still matters for comparative analysis because it shows the persistence of dark web distribution techniques even when no large named multinational operation is attached.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case helps document the continuing shift from classic darknet heroin marketplaces toward fentanyl-pill retailing disguised as oxycodone.
