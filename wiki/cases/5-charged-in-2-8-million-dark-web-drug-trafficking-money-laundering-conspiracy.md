## Summary

5 charged in $2.8 million Dark Web drug trafficking, money laundering conspiracy is documented in the linked source material. Available source coverage includes US DOJ USAO.

## Source Coverage

- US DOJ USAO, 2020-01-15: 5 charged in $2.8 million Dark Web drug trafficking, money laundering conspiracy.

## Facts

The Southern District of Ohio source describes a 21-count indictment against Khlari Sirotkin, Kelly Stephens, Sean Deaver, Abby Jones, and Sasha Sirotkin. Prosecutors alleged that the group used darknet marketplace accounts and encrypted messaging applications from 2013 onward to sell illegal drugs online, including fentanyl-laced counterfeit pills, heroin, methamphetamine, and cocaine shipped to the Southern District of Ohio and elsewhere in the United States.

The source-backed scale markers are significant for the case graph: the indictment alleged more than one million fentanyl-laced counterfeit pills and approximately USD 2.8 million in laundered proceeds. The case is therefore modeled as both a dark-web narcotics prosecution and a proceeds-laundering case, rather than a generic online-drug entry.
