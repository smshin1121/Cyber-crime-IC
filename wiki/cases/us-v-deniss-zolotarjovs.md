## Summary

On 2026-05-04 the US Justice Department announced the 102-month (8.5-year) federal prison sentence in the Southern District of Ohio of Deniss Zolotarjovs, a 35-year-old Latvian national of Moscow, Russia, for his role as a ransom-negotiator member of a Russian ransomware organisation led by former leaders of the Conti ransomware group. Brand names attributed to the organisation in DOJ-charging language during Zolotarjovs's active period (approximately June 2021 – August 2023) include **Conti**, **Karakurt**, **Royal**, **TommyLeaks**, **SchoolBoys Ransomware**, and **Akira**. The organisation is alleged to have stolen data from over 54 victim companies during this period.

## Facts

According to the cited DOJ release of 2026-05-04 and the prior 2024-08-21 SDOH indictment, Zolotarjovs was primarily responsible for escalating pressure on victims who initially resisted ransom demands. He analysed stolen data, researched victim companies, and exploited his access to particularly sensitive personal information. In one attack on a pediatric healthcare company, he deliberately leveraged children's healthcare patient information for extortion. Other victims included a government entity whose 911 emergency-services system was forced offline.

The DOJ release further records that the organisation operated for a time out of an office building on Lakhtinskaya Street in St. Petersburg, Russia, used a hierarchical management structure with separate teams, and registered shell companies throughout Russia, Europe, and the United States to obfuscate operations. Membership reportedly included multiple former Russian law-enforcement officers, allowing the organisation to co-opt Russian government databases and law-enforcement connections to intimidate detractors and recruit new members. The release also alleges that organisation leaders paid bribes to exempt draft-age members from compulsory Russian military service.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

US federal jurisdiction in the Southern District of Ohio attached through the wire fraud and money laundering conspiracies, with US victims among the 54+ cohort. Defendant was extradited from Georgia, exercising US extradition-treaty rights and DOJ Office of International Affairs coordination.

### Key Legal Issues

- **Conspiracy to commit money laundering** (18 U.S.C. § 1956(h)).
- **Conspiracy to commit wire fraud** (18 U.S.C. § 1349; underlying offence 18 U.S.C. § 1343).
- **Contested extradition under US-Georgia bilateral arrangements** — the DOJ release explicitly notes that Zolotarjovs contested extradition before the August 2024 transfer to US custody.

### Precedent Value

This is the first wiki-recorded US public-record sentencing to attribute the multi-brand Conti-successor organisation to a single named individual at the negotiator tier. Treating Conti / Karakurt / Royal / TommyLeaks / SchoolBoys / Akira as one organisation in DOJ-charging language is operationally significant for SNA reasoning about ransomware-organisation continuity across rebranding cycles, and complements the related sentencings of [[us-v-volkov-yanluowang-sentencing|Volkov (Yanluowang IAB)]] and the [[operation-cronos-phase2|Cronos Phase 2 sanctions and indictment of Khoroshev (LockBit)]].

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The case exemplifies a successful US-led extradition pathway from a non-Russia, non-Schengen partner state (Georgia), with an ~8-month contested-extradition phase between arrest (December 2023) and US custody (August 2024). The DOJ release credits the Government of Georgia and the DOJ Office of International Affairs jointly with securing the outcome. The pattern is comparable to the US-Italy extradition pathway in [[us-v-volkov-yanluowang-sentencing|the Volkov case]], with Georgia and Italy each providing a Schengen / Council-of-Europe-region jurisdiction in which a Russian-resident operator of a Russian-organisation crime line could be apprehended and lawfully extradited to the United States.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

- The specific Georgian arresting agency, the specific Georgian court that ruled on the contested extradition, and the underlying treaty article cited in the extradition request are not enumerated in the cited release.
- The full Conti-successor organisation membership beyond Zolotarjovs is not itemised in this release. Other named-suspect attribution waves in the wiki (e.g. [[operation-multiple-foreign-nationals-charged-in-connection-with-trickbot-and-conti|TrickBot/Conti foreign-nationals charging wave]] and [[operation-us-v-galochkin-trickbot-conti|U.S. v Galochkin]]) overlap in personnel and brand attribution.
- The release does not enumerate which specific 13 victim companies bore the USD 56 million in losses, or whether any non-US-jurisdiction victims are among the 54+ cohort.
