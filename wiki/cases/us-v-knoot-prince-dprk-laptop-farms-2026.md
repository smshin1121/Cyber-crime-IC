## Summary

On 2026-05-07 the US Justice Department announced the joint sentencings of two US-based "laptop farmers" facilitating Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK) remote IT worker revenue-generation schemes:

- **Matthew Isaac Knoot** (Nashville, Tennessee, MDTN) — sentenced 2026-05-01 to **18 months in prison** + 1 year supervised release; **USD 15,100 restitution + USD 15,100 forfeiture**.
- **Erick Ntekereze Prince** (New York, SDFL) — sentenced 2026-05-07 to **18 months in prison** + 3 years supervised release; **USD 89,000 forfeiture**.

These are the **7th and 8th** US-based laptop-farmer sentencings in the last 5 months under the joint NSD + FBI **DPRK RevGen: Domestic Enabler Initiative**. The combined schemes generated **more than USD 1.2 million in revenue for the DPRK** and impacted **nearly 70 victim US companies**.

## Facts

### Methodology

Both defendants received and hosted laptop computers shipped by victim US companies to "remote IT workers" the companies had hired and believed were located at the defendants' residences. The defendants installed unauthorized remote-desktop software on the laptops so that DPRK IT workers operating from overseas locations (notably China) could control the laptops while making it appear to the victim companies that the IT worker was remote-working from the US laptop-farm address.

### Knoot scheme (MDTN)

Knoot ran a laptop farm from his Nashville residences from approximately **July 2022 through August 2023**, with victim-company laptops shipped addressed to "Andrew M." (whose identity the conspirators had stolen). DPRK IT workers operated the laptops from China. Victim companies paid the IT workers more than **USD 250,000** during this period; most or all was falsely reported to the IRS and Social Security Administration in the stolen "Andrew M." identity. Affected company remediation costs exceeded **USD 500,000**. Knoot's role ended on **2023-08-08** when the FBI executed a court-authorized search of his Nashville home; Knoot subsequently made false statements and destroyed evidence to obstruct the investigation. Knoot was indicted on **2024-08-07** and ultimately pleaded guilty to charges resolved by sentencing on **2026-05-01**.

### Prince scheme (SDFL)

Prince enabled at least three DPRK IT workers to obtain remote employment at US companies from approximately **June 2020 through August 2024**, using his Taggcar Inc. shell company to fraudulently supply "certified" IT workers. He hosted victim-company-issued laptops at New York residences with unauthorized remote-access software installed. The Prince indictment was filed on **2025-01-21** and named US national **Emanuel Ashtor**, Mexican national **Pedro Ernesto Alonso de los Reyes**, and DPRK nationals **Jin Sung-il** and **Pak Jin-Song**. The criminal scheme obtained work for North Korean IT workers from **more than 64 US companies**; victim companies paid more than **USD 943,069** in salary payments to the DPRK IT workers, plus more than **USD 1 million** in remediation costs.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

US federal jurisdiction in the Middle District of Tennessee (Knoot) and Southern District of Florida (Prince). The two cases were prosecuted separately but announced jointly by DOJ on 2026-05-07. Co-conspirator de los Reyes is the only co-defendant currently in foreign custody (Netherlands, awaiting US extradition).

### Key Legal Issues

- **Wire fraud / computer fraud / sanctions-evasion enabler conduct** — the laptop-farm methodology constitutes a deliberate scheme by US-based enablers to facilitate DPRK revenue generation in violation of US sanctions and computer-fraud law.
- **Identity-theft as an embedded element** — the Knoot scheme relied on stolen identity ("Andrew M.") falsely reported to the IRS and Social Security Administration.
- **DPRK national co-conspirator non-cooperation** — Jin Sung-il and Pak Jin-Song are fugitives, illustrating the limits of extradition cooperation when co-conspirators reside in non-cooperating states.

### Precedent Value

- **7th and 8th US laptop-farmer sentencings** under the DPRK RevGen: Domestic Enabler Initiative (in 5 months).
- **Public-record verbatim record of laptop-farm methodology** — the most detailed wiki-recorded operational description of the US-based DPRK laptop-farm enablement model.
- **US-Korea bilateral cooperation reference** — October 2023 joint US-ROK guidance on DPRK IT worker threats anchors this case to the broader US-Korea cooperation public-record corpus.
- **US-Netherlands cooperation pathway** — de los Reyes's Netherlands-custody-awaiting-extradition status adds a US-Netherlands extradition pathway record to the wiki.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The case exemplifies multi-pronged US enforcement against DPRK revenue generation: domestic enabler prosecutions (Knoot, Prince, Ashtor), one foreign-custody-awaiting-extradition record (de los Reyes in the Netherlands), and a State Department reward offer (USD 5M) designed to attract international cooperation against DPRK illicit-financial-activity actors. The joint US-ROK October 2023 advisory provides a non-prosecutorial public-record cooperation anchor between the United States and the Republic of Korea against DPRK IT worker threats.
