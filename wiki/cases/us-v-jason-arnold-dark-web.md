## Summary

United States v. Arnold is a Dream Marketplace plea case involving vendor accounts used to ship heroin and methamphetamine nationwide.

## Facts

DOJ said Arnold and his co-defendants operated Dream Marketplace vendor accounts including "TheSickness" and "SicknessVersion2" and completed more than 3,000 transactions involving heroin and methamphetamine. The public account highlights ordinary parcel-shipping concealment methods, including use of commercial packaging to disguise narcotics shipments.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Marketplace Context

The available source does not identify a foreign procedural step, but the case is still useful for comparative purposes because it shows how a large transnational darknet market generated conventional U.S. district-court prosecutions for domestic vendors.

### Precedent Value

The case demonstrates that even without a named multinational takedown operation attached to the docket, Dream Marketplace evidence could be converted into a high-volume narcotics conspiracy plea focused on vendor conduct and fulfillment logistics.
