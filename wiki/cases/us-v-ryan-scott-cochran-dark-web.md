## Summary

United States v. Cochran is a guilty-plea case against a long-running dark web vendor who sold drugs and fraudulent documents through the mail.

## Facts

According to DOJ, Cochran operated as "namedeclined" and sold fentanyl, cocaine, methamphetamine, and fraudulent identification materials on numerous dark web marketplaces over an extended period. The plea announcement also emphasizes the financial dimension of the case, with more than USD 1.1 million in combined forfeiture commitments.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Investigative Reach

The public source identifies assistance from federal agencies in New York, California, and the Bay Area rather than foreign counterparts. Even so, the fact pattern reflects a trans-jurisdictional postal and cryptocurrency investigation typical of mature U.S. dark web cases.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case is valuable because it combines narcotics distribution, document fraud, mail-based fulfillment, and digital-asset forfeiture in one prosecution, making it a compact example of how diversified dark web vendor businesses are charged.
