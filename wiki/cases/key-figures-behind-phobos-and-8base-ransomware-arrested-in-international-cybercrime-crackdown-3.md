## Evidence and Attribution Notes

- In early February 2025, a coordinated international law enforcement action resulted in the arrest of **4 Russian nationals** who were key figures behind the **8Base ransomware** group, and the takedown of **27 servers** connected to their criminal network.
- The operation involved 14 countries and built on prior actions including the arrest of a Phobos administrator in South Korea in June 2024 (extradited to the US in November 2024) and a key Phobos affiliate arrest in Italy in 2023.
- Law enforcement warned over 400 companies worldwide of ongoing or imminent ransomware attacks.
- Europol had supported the investigation since February 2019, organizing 37 operational meetings and exchanging nearly 600 operational messages via SIENA.
- **Operation date:** Early February 2025 - **Publication date:** 11 February 2025

## Source-Backed Case Detail

Europol framed the February 2025 action as a coordinated strike against two overlapping ransomware ecosystems rather than a single domestic arrest event. The four arrested suspects were described as Russian nationals leading 8Base and suspected of deploying a Phobos variant to extort victims in Europe and beyond. The same action removed 27 servers tied to the criminal network, giving the case both a person-focused enforcement component and an infrastructure-disruption component.

The source places the case in a longer Phobos enforcement arc. It notes that a Phobos administrator had been arrested in South Korea in June 2024 and extradited to the United States in November 2024, and that a key Phobos affiliate had been arrested in Italy in 2023 on a French arrest warrant. That sequence matters for international-cooperation analysis because the February 2025 arrests were not isolated; they followed earlier arrests, extradition, and warrant activity across Asia, Europe, and the United States.

The operational record also supports a victim-protection dimension. Authorities used the investigation to warn more than 400 companies worldwide about ongoing or imminent ransomware attacks. This makes the case useful as an example of enforcement that combined arrests, server takedowns, intelligence fusion, and pre-victim notification, rather than waiting for all investigative value to be converted into prosecutions.

Europol's coordination role was unusually concrete in the source: EC3 had supported the investigation since February 2019, convened 37 operational meetings and technical sprints, provided analytical, crypto-tracing, and forensic support, facilitated J-CAT intelligence exchange, and moved nearly 600 operational messages through SIENA. Eurojust separately organized two coordination meetings to support cross-border judicial cooperation and outstanding requests from participating authorities.
