## Summary

On 18 September 2025, the U.S. Department of Justice (Office of Public Affairs), the U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of New Jersey, and the FBI Cyber Division / FBI Newark Field Office announced the unsealing of a federal criminal complaint in the U.S. District Court for the District of New Jersey charging **Thalha Jubair**, 19, of London, England — a United Kingdom national operating under the online aliases "EarthtoStar," "Brad," "Austin," and "@autistic" — with conspiracies to commit computer fraud, wire fraud, and money laundering, plus substantive computer fraud and wire fraud counts. The complaint alleges that from approximately May 2022 to September 2025, Jubair and his associates carried out approximately **120 network intrusions** worldwide, including against at least **47 U.S.-based victim entities**, who collectively paid more than **USD 115 million in ransom payments**. Two named federal-victim intrusions are highlighted: in **October 2024 and January 2025**, Jubair allegedly gained unauthorized access to the networks of a **U.S.-based critical infrastructure company** and the **U.S. Courts** (the federal judiciary). The Justice Department attributes the scheme to the cyber-criminal grouping known as **Scattered Spider** (a/k/a Octo Tempest, UNC3944, 0ktapus).

The total maximum statutory penalty across the seven charged counts is 95 years in prison. Jubair was arrested in the United Kingdom on 16 September 2025 by U.K. authorities in connection with a separate U.K. investigation into an attack on U.K. critical infrastructure (the TfL intrusion, separately prosecuted at [[uk-jubair-flowers-tfl-cyber-attack-2025]]). The DOJ release does not announce a U.S. extradition request; whether the United States ultimately seeks extradition, files a parallel indictment, or defers to the U.K. proceedings is not stated. The release credits significant assistance from the UK NCA, City of London Police, West Midlands Police, Netherlands National Police and Dutch Prosecutor's Office, Romanian DIICOT and Romanian National Police, Royal Canadian Mounted Police, Australian Federal Police, and the DOJ Office of International Affairs.

## Facts

- **Defendant**: Thalha Jubair, 19, of London, England. United Kingdom national. Aliases "EarthtoStar," "Brad," "Austin," "@autistic."
- **Conduct period**: approximately May 2022 to September 2025.
- **Method**: Conspired with others to use **social engineering** techniques to gain unauthorized access into the computer networks of U.S. companies, steal and encrypt information, and demand ransom payments in exchange for regaining control of the encrypted data and preventing dissemination of the exfiltrated data. Jubair also conspired to launder the proceeds.
- **Scale**: approximately 120 worldwide network intrusions; at least 47 U.S.-based victim entities; victims paid in aggregate **more than USD 115,000,000** in ransom.
- **Named federal-victim intrusions**: in **October 2024** and **January 2025**, Jubair allegedly participated in a scheme to gain unauthorized access to the networks of (i) a **U.S.-based critical infrastructure company** (unnamed in the release), and (ii) the **U.S. Courts** (the federal judiciary).
- **Ransom-flow facts**: portions of ransom payments from at least five victims were sent to wallets on a server controlled by Jubair. In **July 2024**, while law enforcement was seizing that server — successfully seizing cryptocurrency worth approximately **USD 36 million** at the time — Jubair transferred a portion of cryptocurrency that originated from one of the victims, worth approximately **USD 8.4 million** at the time, to another wallet.
- **Group attribution**: charges arise out of an investigation into a cyber threat grouping referred to as **Scattered Spider**, **Octo Tempest**, **UNC3944**, and/or **0ktapus**.
- **Charges**: (i) computer fraud conspiracy; (ii–iii) two counts of computer fraud; (iv) wire fraud conspiracy; (v–vi) two counts of wire fraud; (vii) money laundering conspiracy. **Maximum penalty**: 95 years in prison if convicted of all counts.
- **U.K. arrest**: on **Tuesday, 16 September 2025**, U.K. authorities arrested Jubair and a second individual (per the parallel UK NCA release: Owen Flowers) in connection with a separate U.K. investigation into a U.K. critical-infrastructure intrusion (the TfL attack).
- **Investigating office (US)**: FBI Newark Field Office.
- **Prosecuting attorneys**: CCIPS Assistant Deputy Chief Adrienne L. Rose and Trial Attorney George S. Brown; AUSA Andrew Kogan (D.N.J. Cybercrime Unit).

## International Cooperation Elements

### Evidence Gathering

The DOJ release explicitly credits "significant assistance" from a six-country / eight-agency footprint:

- **United Kingdom**: National Crime Agency (NCA), City of London Police, West Midlands Police.
- **Netherlands**: National Police of the Netherlands, Dutch Prosecutor's Office (Openbaar Ministerie).
- **Romania**: Directorate for Investigating Organized Crime and Terrorism (DIICOT), Brigade for Combating Organized Crime Targu Mures, Romanian National Police, Directorate for Combating Organized Crime.
- **Canada**: Royal Canadian Mounted Police (RCMP).
- **Australia**: Australian Federal Police (AFP).

The release also identifies the **DOJ Office of International Affairs (OIA)** as having "provided significant assistance" — consistent with OIA's standing role coordinating MLA traffic and extradition packages for cybercrime indictments.

The release does **not** specify the legal vehicle (formal MLAT request, [[budapest-convention]] preservation/production order, [[informal-cooperation|informal LE-to-LE channel]], or [[mlat-process|treaty-based mutual assistance]]) underlying each foreign agency's contribution. **Highly likely** (80–95% confidence) that the package combines (a) informal FBI-to-NCA / FBI-to-RCMP / FBI-to-AFP cooperation under longstanding Five Eyes / English-speaking liaison channels, (b) Council of Europe [[budapest-convention]]–framed preservation/production assistance for the Netherlands and Romania (both Convention parties), and (c) direct OIA-mediated MLAT traffic for any country-specific evidence requests requiring formal compulsion.

### Arrest and Extradition

- **Foreign arrest, separate UK investigation**: The arrest of Jubair on **16 September 2025** was executed by U.K. authorities pursuant to a **separate U.K. investigation** into a U.K. critical-infrastructure intrusion — i.e., the TfL prosecution at [[uk-jubair-flowers-tfl-cyber-attack-2025]] — **not** on the U.S. D.N.J. complaint.
- **No U.S. extradition track announced**: The 18 September 2025 DOJ release **does not** announce a U.S. extradition request for the D.N.J. complaint. Whether the United States will (a) seek extradition under the **U.S.–U.K. extradition treaty (2003 / in force 2007)** after any U.K. trial concludes, (b) file a parallel grand-jury indictment to formalize the case for extradition, or (c) defer to the U.K. proceedings under a *non bis in idem* / specialty arrangement, is **not stated** in the source.
- **Dual criminality** ([[dual-criminality]]) **likely engaged at a future stage**: any future U.S. extradition request would readily meet the dual-criminality test because the alleged conduct (computer fraud, ransomware extortion, money laundering) is criminal under both U.K. (Computer Misuse Act 1990, Proceeds of Crime Act 2002) and U.S. (CFAA 18 U.S.C. § 1030, wire fraud 18 U.S.C. § 1343, money laundering 18 U.S.C. § 1956) law.
- **Specialty / *non bis in idem* coordination**: open question, depends on UK CPS charge selection vs. U.S. count selection. The UK TfL prosecution covers conduct against UK victims; the U.S. complaint covers conduct against 47 U.S. victims and the U.S. Courts. **Highly likely** (80–95%) that the UK + US division of labor was deliberately structured by OIA + NCA + CPS so that the two prosecutions cover non-overlapping victim sets, eliminating most *non bis in idem* friction.

### Asset Recovery

- **July 2024 cryptocurrency server seizure**: Law enforcement seized a server controlled by Jubair holding cryptocurrency worth **approximately USD 36 million** at the time of seizure. The release does not specify the seizing jurisdiction, but the U.S. forum context plus FBI Newark investigatory lead **strongly imply** (>95%) U.S. asset-forfeiture / [[cryptocurrency-seizure]] action under 18 U.S.C. § 981 / § 982 or under a related civil-forfeiture authority.
- **USD 8.4M transfer during seizure window**: Jubair transferred ~USD 8.4M (originating from one victim) to a separate wallet during the same seizure window — a fact that is itself charged conduct (money laundering conspiracy) and indicates that not all victim funds were recovered.
- The release does not specify victim-restitution figures from the USD 36M seizure or the status of the USD 8.4M transferred wallet.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

- **U.S. extraterritorial jurisdiction over CFAA conduct**: 18 U.S.C. § 1030(e)(2)(B) defines "protected computer" to include any computer "used in or affecting interstate or foreign commerce or communication," and U.S. circuit courts have applied CFAA extraterritorially where the protected computer is located in the United States. With 47 U.S.-based victim networks and the U.S. Courts (federal judiciary) named as victims, U.S. CFAA jurisdiction over Jubair's conduct is **almost certain** (>95%) regardless of his physical location at the time of the offence.
- **Wire fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1343) extraterritoriality**: U.S. wire-fraud jurisdiction extends to schemes using U.S. wires (telephone, internet) — readily satisfied by ransom communications and exfiltration traffic touching U.S. infrastructure.
- **Money laundering (18 U.S.C. § 1956)**: extraterritorial jurisdiction available where the conduct involves U.S. financial transactions or U.S. specified-unlawful-activity proceeds — both **highly likely** (80–95%) satisfied by the cryptocurrency flows and U.S.-victim-origin proceeds.
- **Venue (D.N.J.)**: USAO Habba's quoted statement specifies that Scattered Spider "victimized at least 47 U.S.-based entities, **including in New Jersey**." D.N.J. venue is anchored on at least one New-Jersey-based victim, with the rest of the conduct triable in the same district under the standard CFAA / wire-fraud venue rules.

### Key Legal Issues

- **CFAA conspiracy + substantive offences (18 U.S.C. § 1030(b) / § 1030(a))**: charged as one conspiracy count plus two substantive counts. The two substantive counts **likely** (55–80%) correspond to the two named federal-victim intrusions in October 2024 and January 2025 (the U.S. critical-infrastructure company and the U.S. Courts), though the release does not explicitly map counts to victims.
- **Wire fraud + conspiracy (18 U.S.C. § 1343 / § 1349)**: one conspiracy count plus two substantive counts; supports the ransom-extortion scheme.
- **Money laundering conspiracy (18 U.S.C. § 1956(h))**: anchors the cryptocurrency-flow facts, including the July 2024 server-seizure-window transfer of USD 8.4M.
- **U.S. Courts as victim — federal-judiciary cyber intrusion**: the inclusion of the federal judiciary as a victim of unauthorized access in October 2024 / January 2025 is itself notable. The release does not specify the affected court system(s) or whether PACER / CM-ECF / sealed filings were exposed; **likely** (55–80%) related to the publicly reported intrusions of the U.S. Courts CM/ECF system over that period, though this case page does not assert that mapping until a tier-1 source confirms it.
- **Statutory maximum (95 years)**: across all seven counts. Realistic sentencing exposure under U.S. Sentencing Guidelines would be substantially lower; the figure is a press-release statutory ceiling rather than a sentencing forecast.

### Precedent Value

- **First documented UK + US parallel-prosecution model for Scattered Spider**: the case illustrates a deliberate prosecutorial division of labor between the U.S. (D.N.J., 47 U.S. victims, USD 115M ransom, U.S. Courts intrusion) and the U.K. (NCA / Westminster Magistrates Court, TfL intrusion). The model contrasts with the **U.S.-as-venue + foreign-as-arrest-venue + extradition-track** pattern of [[us-v-kai-west-intelbroker-2025]] (Kai West / IntelBroker arrested in France, U.S. seeking extradition to S.D.N.Y.).
- **Federal-judiciary victim**: documents the U.S. Courts as a credibly named cyber-intrusion target in a tier-1 DOJ filing, expanding the wiki's catalogue of named federal-victim entities.
- **OIA-coordinated six-country cooperation footprint** for a complaint (not yet an indictment): credible illustration of OIA + CCIPS preparing transnational evidentiary support before grand-jury indictment.
- **Cryptocurrency-seizure scale**: USD 36M seized in a single July 2024 server seizure is a meaningful data point for the [[cryptocurrency-seizure]] mechanism page.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

- **Speed**: arrest (UK, 2025-09-16) and U.S. complaint unsealing (2025-09-18) are timed within 48 hours, consistent with deliberate bilateral coordination of the announcement window.
- **Breadth**: **eight named cooperating agencies across six countries** (US + UK + NL + RO + CA + AU). This is among the broadest non-takedown cooperation footprints documented in the wiki for a single-defendant cybercrime complaint. The breadth strongly suggests Scattered Spider as a whole is the underlying investigatory target, with Jubair as the first individual charged.
- **Frictions identified in the public record**: none disclosed by the DOJ release. The fact that the U.S. did not file a formal extradition request alongside the complaint may reflect (i) prosecutorial deference to the U.K. as the venue with custody of the defendant, (ii) anticipation of a future superseding indictment, or (iii) coordination on UK-first-prosecution / US-second-prosecution sequencing under specialty doctrine.
- **OIA coordination**: explicitly named — consistent with prior OIA-coordinated multi-jurisdiction cybercrime packages (cf. [[us-v-kai-west-intelbroker-2025]]).
- **Notable inclusions and exclusions**: Romania's DIICOT + multiple Romanian sub-units appear despite Romania not being publicly named in the parallel UK NCA release on the same date — **likely** (55–80%) reflects Romania-side digital-forensics / hosting-infrastructure cooperation supporting the U.S.-victim-side investigation. The Netherlands likewise appears only on the U.S. side, again consistent with hosting-infrastructure or transit-evidence cooperation. **No mention** of FIVE EYES partner New Zealand, the European Union (Eurojust / Europol EC3), or any private-sector cooperation partners — a notable absence given Scattered Spider's reported reliance on commodity infostealer ecosystems.
