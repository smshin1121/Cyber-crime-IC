## Summary

On 2025-09-09 the U.S. Justice Department announced the unsealing of a **superseding indictment** in the Eastern District of New York charging Ukrainian national **Volodymyr Viktorovich Tymoshchuk** (aliases: **deadforz, Boba, msfv, farnetwork**) as an administrator of the **LockerGoga, MegaCortex, and Nefilim** ransomware schemes. The indictment charges 7 counts: 2 counts of conspiracy to commit fraud and related activity in connection with computers; 3 counts of intentional damage to a protected computer; 1 count of unauthorized access to a protected computer; and 1 count of transmitting a threat to disclose confidential information.

Tymoshchuk remains a fugitive. The US State Department's Transnational Organized Crime (TOC) Rewards Program is offering rewards totaling up to **USD 11 million** for information leading to his arrest, conviction, or location.

The case is the administrator-tier counterpart to the affiliate-tier [[us-v-stryzhak-nefilim|U.S. v Artem Stryzhak prosecution]], in which Stryzhak was extradited from Spain in April 2025 and pleaded guilty on 2025-12-18 to conspiracy to commit computer fraud for his role as a Nefilim ransomware affiliate operating under a 20% revenue split with administrators including Tymoshchuk.

## Facts

### LockerGoga / MegaCortex Phase (2019–2020)

Between **July 2019 and June 2020**, Tymoshchuk and his co-conspirators allegedly compromised the networks of **more than 250 victim companies in the United States** and hundreds of other companies around the world with LockerGoga and MegaCortex ransomware. Per the DOJ-charging language, "many of these extortion attempts were unsuccessful because law enforcement often notified victims that their networks had been compromised before Tymoshchuk and his co-conspirators were able to deploy the ransomware" — a public-record reference to proactive law-enforcement disruption.

### Nefilim Administrator Phase (July 2020 – October 2021)

From **July 2020 through October 2021**, Tymoshchuk allegedly served as one of the administrators of the **Nefilim ransomware** strain. Tymoshchuk and the other Nefilim administrators provided affiliates — explicitly including co-defendant **Artem Stryzhak** ([[us-v-stryzhak-nefilim|extradited from Spain April 2025; pleaded guilty December 2025]]) — with access to the Nefilim ransomware in exchange for **20 percent of the ransom proceeds** extorted from Nefilim victims.

### Methodology

Per the DOJ-charging language, the perpetrators typically customized the ransomware executable file for each victim. The customization allowed the ransomware actors to create a decryption key that could only decrypt the network of the specific victim. If a victim paid the ransom, the perpetrators would send a decryption tool enabling the victim to decrypt the locked files.

### September 2022 Decryption Release

In **September 2022**, as part of an international coordinated effort against LockerGoga and MegaCortex ransomware, **decryption keys associated with those ransomware variants were made available to the public via the "No More Ransomware Project"** initiative — a concrete public-record outcome of the underlying multilateral investigation, enabling compromised victim companies and institutions to recover encrypted data without paying ransom.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

US federal jurisdiction in the Eastern District of New York attached through the conspiracy and computer-fraud counts, with US-jurisdiction victims among the 250+ US-company cohort. Tymoshchuk remains a fugitive; the prosecution is unable to proceed to plea/trial without arrest or extradition.

### Key Legal Issues

- **Two counts of conspiracy to commit fraud and related activity in connection with computers**.
- **Three counts of intentional damage to a protected computer**.
- **One count of unauthorized access to a protected computer**.
- **One count of transmitting a threat to disclose confidential information** — captures the data-leak extortion methodology.
- **20% administrator/affiliate revenue split** — directly mirrors the Stryzhak case and cements the public-record administrator/affiliate compensation structure for Nefilim.
- **Ransomware-strain progression LockerGoga → MegaCortex → Nefilim** — first wiki-recorded US public-record administrator-tier indictment to allege a single named individual administering three distinct ransomware variants in sequence.

### Precedent Value

Administrator-tier counterpart to the [[us-v-stryzhak-nefilim|Stryzhak affiliate-tier prosecution]] for the Nefilim ecosystem. The two cases together provide the most complete public-record administrator-affiliate cross-attribution for a single ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) cluster in the wiki corpus. The September 2022 No More Ransomware Project decryption-key release demonstrates the proactive disruption strategy that complements the named-suspect indictment approach.

The 9-country cooperation roster (FR, CZ, DE, LT, LU, NL, NO, CH, UA) plus Europol and Eurojust via ICHIP The Hague is one of the wider European cooperation rosters in the wiki for a single ransomware-administrator case, comparable to but distinct from the Khoroshev (Cronos Phase 2) US-UK-Australia roster and the Ptitsyn (Phobos) 10-country including-Korea roster.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The case exemplifies a multi-year multilateral coordination effort culminating in a charged-but-fugitive named-suspect indictment plus a parallel September 2022 decryption-key release that delivered measurable victim relief. The 9-country cooperation roster includes Ukraine — Tymoshchuk's nationality state — and consistent with the Volkov, Stryzhak, Zolotarjovs, Dragomir pattern, the wiki documents that European-arrest US-extradition pathway has not yet been activated in this specific case (Tymoshchuk remains at large).

## Korean Relevance (한국 관련성)

South Korea is not named in the cited DOJ release as a victim, source, or cooperation jurisdiction for this case. The cooperation roster is European and US-centric. The case is recorded in the wiki for the administrator-tier counterpart to Stryzhak, the September 2022 decryption-key release, and the 9-country European cooperation roster. Korean exposure to LockerGoga/MegaCortex/Nefilim ransomware is not directly informed by this source.
