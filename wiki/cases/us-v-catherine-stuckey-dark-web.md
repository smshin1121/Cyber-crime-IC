## Summary

United States v. Stuckey is a guilty-plea case involving heroin, methamphetamine, and cocaine sold through multiple darknet marketplaces.

## Facts

According to DOJ, Stuckey participated in darknet sales through accounts on Dream Marketplace, Silk Road 3.0, and Nightmare Marketplace. The case is useful because it captures a multi-platform vendor pattern rather than a single-market prosecution, with the conspiracy spanning heroin, methamphetamine, and cocaine.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Multi-Market Dimension

No direct foreign legal assistance is identified in the public source. The international significance lies in the way U.S. prosecutors treated evidence from several dark web markets as part of one integrated conspiracy theory.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

This case has comparative value because it shows that prosecutors can aggregate conduct across successive or parallel darknet platforms, reducing the analytical importance of the specific market brand when the same actors and methods persist.
