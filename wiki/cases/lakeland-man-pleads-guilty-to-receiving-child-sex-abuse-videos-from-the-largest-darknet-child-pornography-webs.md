## Summary

United States v. Jack R. Dove III is a Middle District of Florida follow-on prosecution from the Welcome To Video international takedown. The prior case page was a placeholder; the collected references support a fuller record identifying the defendant, the indictment and plea dates, the Bitcoin/account evidence, and the international evidence path.

## Facts

Jack R. Dove III of Lakeland, Florida pleaded guilty on March 30, 2021 to receiving and possessing CSAM. The DOJ release states that Dove had been indicted on January 29, 2019. The defendant-specific facts connect the prosecution to Welcome To Video, a darknet marketplace funded through Bitcoin payments.

The non-graphic attribution record described in the sources includes Bitcoin payment activity, Welcome To Video account records, server analysis, a U.S. search warrant at Dove's Lakeland residence, and device forensics.

## International Cooperation Elements

This case is not merely a one-country prosecution. It is a U.S. follow-on case supported by the broader Welcome To Video international investigation:

- Korean National Police seized the Welcome To Video server in South Korea in March 2018.
- IRS-CI, Homeland Security Investigations, the UK National Crime Agency, and Korean National Police are credited in the international investigation.
- ICE-HSI reports that seized server data was shared with law enforcement partners worldwide, generating leads to 38 countries and 337 arrests.
- The Dove prosecution used server-derived evidence from that international takedown alongside domestic search and forensic evidence.

## Legal Analysis

The case illustrates how darknet CSAM prosecutions can depend on cross-border digital evidence even when the courtroom event is domestic. The relevant cooperation mechanism is not extradition of the defendant; it is the transfer and exploitation of server, account, and payment evidence from a foreign infrastructure seizure.
