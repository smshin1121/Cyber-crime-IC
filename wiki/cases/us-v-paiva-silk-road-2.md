## Summary

United States v. Paiva is a domestic follow-on prosecution arising from the Silk Road 2.0 takedown. It shows how Operation Onymous generated actionable purchaser data that could be used in local narcotics prosecutions.

## Facts

DOJ said Paiva purchased significant quantities of controlled substances from Silk Road 2.0 for redistribution in Michigan. The case therefore captures a downstream enforcement use of seized dark-web market data.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Evidence Gathering

The prosecution depended on the larger international investigation and takedown of Silk Road 2.0, which made purchaser identification possible.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case has medium value as a demonstration that market seizures can lead not only to operator cases but also to later retail-level drug-distribution prosecutions.
