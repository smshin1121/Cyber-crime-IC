## Summary

United States v. Saintillus is a dark web fentanyl distribution case involving more than 1,000 deals through the vendor identity "chlnsaint."

## Facts

DOJ said Saintillus used the dark web to complete more than 1,000 narcotics transactions and sold fentanyl, oxycodone, oxymorphone, and related substances in exchange for cryptocurrency. The case was built in part through repeated undercover purchases and ties directly to the U.S. mail distribution model that appears across many post-AlphaBay narcotics cases.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Evidence Context

No public foreign arrest or MLA step is identified in the collected source. The cross-border aspect is instead technological and financial, with marketplace infrastructure and cryptocurrency serving as the operational substrate for domestic distribution.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case is a strong example of how vendor-volume metrics, controlled buys, and crypto-linked payments are combined to support significant federal sentences in dark web fentanyl prosecutions.
