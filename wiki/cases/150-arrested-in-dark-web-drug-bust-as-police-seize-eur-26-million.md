## Summary

This page tracks the multinational arrest wave publicly announced under Operation Dark HunTOR after exploitation of the seized [[darkmarket-takedown|DarkMarket]] infrastructure. Public sources support the operation-wide arrests and seizures but do not identify a full named defendant list.

## Facts

Official sources state that authorities arrested 150 suspected dark-web traffickers across nine countries and seized approximately EUR 26.7 million, 234 kg of drugs and 45 firearms. The enforcement wave followed roughly ten months of intelligence exploitation after German authorities seized DarkMarket infrastructure in January 2021.

## International Cooperation Elements

This is a high-value cooperation case cluster because the public record clearly exposes the operational architecture even when individual dockets remain fragmented:

- [[europol-ec3|Europol EC3]] and [[eurojust]] coordinated the European side
- the U.S. side was framed through DOJ/JCODE reporting
- [[uk-nca|UK NCA]] published a country-specific breakdown for the UK arrests and seizures

That makes the page appropriate as an umbrella enforcement cluster, but not as a replacement for the eventual individual case pages.

## Legal Analysis

The core integrity constraint is granularity. The public sources are strong on collective results and weak on a complete list of defendant names, case numbers and sentences. To avoid fabricating or over-aggregating legal outcomes, the page treats the defendants as a collective unidentified target set.
