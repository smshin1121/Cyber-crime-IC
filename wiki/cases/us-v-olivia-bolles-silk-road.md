## Summary

United States v. Bolles is a Silk Road case involving a licensed doctor who sold controlled substances under the vendor alias \"MDPro.\"

## Facts

According to DOJ, Bolles operated as "MDPro" on Silk Road and mailed controlled substances to customers in the United States and abroad. The case is notable because it involved a licensed physician using a darknet marketplace as a retail distribution channel, with sales volume measured in hundreds of completed shipments.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Cross-Border Aspect

The public release indicates distribution to more than 17 countries, which gives the case a clear transnational sales dimension even though the available source does not identify a formal MLA or extradition step.

## Legal Analysis

### Precedent Value

The case shows that darknet enforcement was not limited to stereotypical anonymous criminal actors; licensed professionals were also drawn into marketplace-based drug distribution, and DOJ treated those cases as conventional federal narcotics prosecutions.
