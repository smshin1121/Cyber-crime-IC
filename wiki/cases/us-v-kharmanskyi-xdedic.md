## Summary

United States v. Kharmanskyi is an xDedic administrator case arising from the same marketplace cluster as Habasescu, but with a different custody path. Instead of overseas extradition, DOJ says Kharmanskyi was arrested at Miami International Airport while entering the United States.

## Facts

DOJ identified Kharmanskyi as an xDedic administrator who advertised for the site, paid administrators, and provided customer support to buyers. This placed him in a managerial and operational role rather than in a mere buyer or seller position.

The case is useful because it fills in the middle tier of xDedic responsibility between technical mastermind and downstream users.

## International Cooperation Elements

### Evidence Gathering

The prosecution emerged from the broader xDedic investigation involving Belgium, Ukraine, Europol, the Dutch National Police, and Germany's BKA. Even though the arrest happened in the United States, the case depended on a multinational investigation.

### Arrest and Extradition

Kharmanskyi was arrested at Miami International Airport in 2019 while attempting to enter the United States. This distinguishes the case from other xDedic prosecutions that required formal extradition from third countries.

### Asset Recovery

The case is tied to the larger xDedic seizure action from January 2019, when DOJ seized the marketplace's domain names and dismantled the infrastructure.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

The Middle District of Florida had a direct U.S. nexus because xDedic sold unauthorized access devices affecting U.S. residents and infrastructure, with thousands of compromised systems in Florida alone.

### Key Legal Issues

- **Operational-role liability:** The case shows that customer-support and payment roles inside a cybercrime marketplace can still support U.S. prosecution.
- **Opportunistic-custody strategy:** It also illustrates a recurring cybercrime tactic: waiting until a foreign suspect enters a reachable jurisdiction rather than relying only on extradition from a home state.

### Precedent Value

The case has *medium to high* value because it demonstrates a distinct path to custody in multinational cybercrime cases and rounds out the xDedic administrator picture.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The case suggests that xDedic enforcement used multiple custody pathways at once: extradition where necessary, U.S.-entry arrest where available, and continued prosecution after the original site takedown.

## Korean Relevance

No South Korean role is identified. The case is still relevant because opportunistic arrests during international travel remain one of the few realistic ways to bring some foreign cybercrime actors into court.

## Contradictions & Open Questions

1. DOJ's public materials are not fully consistent on sentence labeling versus aggregate roundup language.
2. The public record does not provide a granular description of Kharmanskyi's specific transactions or communications.
3. It remains unclear how responsibility was split among the different xDedic administrators over time.
