## Facts

The Central District of California release describes an indictment against three North Korean computer programmers alleged to be members of DPRK Reconnaissance General Bureau units known in the cybersecurity community as Lazarus Group and APT38. The alleged conspiracy combined destructive attacks, bank fraud, cryptocurrency theft, malicious cryptocurrency applications, ransomware, spear-phishing, and sanctions-evasion fundraising through the Marine Chain token project.

The BEC classification is retained because the same public release links the companion money-laundering case against Ghaleb Alaumary to ATM cash-out operations, cyber-enabled bank heists, business email compromise schemes, and other online fraud. That does not make every hacking count a BEC charge, but it is part of the source-backed laundering and proceeds-movement context for this enforcement cluster.
