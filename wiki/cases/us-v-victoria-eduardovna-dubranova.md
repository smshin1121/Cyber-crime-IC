## Summary

On 2025-12-10 the US Justice Department announced two Central District of California indictments charging Ukrainian national Victoria Eduardovna Dubranova (33; aliases Vika, Tory, SovaSonya) for her role supporting two Russian state-tied hacking groups: **CyberArmyofRussia_Reborn (CARR / Z-Pentest)**, alleged in the indictment to have been "founded, funded, and directed by the Main Directorate of the General Staff of the Armed Forces of the Russian Federation (GRU)"; and **NoName057(16)**, alleged to be a covert project staffed in part by employees of the **Center for the Study and Network Monitoring of the Youth Environment (CISM)**, an organisation established by order of the President of Russia in October 2018.

Dubranova was extradited to the United States earlier in 2025 on the CARR indictment. On 2025-12-10 she was arraigned on the second (NoName) indictment, pleaded not guilty in both cases, and is scheduled to begin trial in the NoName matter on **2026-02-03** and in the CARR matter on **2026-04-07**. Combined statutory maxima: 27 years (CARR) + 5 years (NoName).

Concurrent with the indictment announcement, the US Department of State offered rewards of **up to USD 2 million** for information on individuals associated with CARR and **up to USD 10 million** for individuals associated with NoName. The FBI, CISA, NSA, DOE, EPA, and DC3 issued a joint cybersecurity advisory on the same date assessing that pro-Russia hacktivist groups target minimally secured, internet-facing virtual network computing connections to infiltrate operational technology control devices within critical infrastructure.

## Facts

### CARR Conduct

According to the CARR indictment, the group claimed credit for hundreds of cyberattacks against victims worldwide, primarily hacking industrial control facilities and conducting DDoS attacks. CARR's named US-victim categories include:

- **Public drinking water systems across several US states**, resulting in damage to controls and the spilling of hundreds of thousands of gallons of drinking water.
- **A meat processing facility in Los Angeles in November 2024**, spoiling thousands of pounds of meat and triggering an ammonia leak.
- **U.S. election infrastructure during U.S. elections.**
- **Websites for U.S. nuclear regulatory entities**, among other sensitive targets.

CARR is alleged to have had "more than 100 members, including juveniles, and more than 75,000 followers on Telegram." A GRU-affiliated individual operating as "Cyber_1ce_Killer" allegedly instructed CARR leadership on victim selection and financed CARR's access to commercial DDoS-for-hire services.

### NoName057(16) Conduct

NoName allegedly conducted hundreds of DDoS cyberattacks against government agencies, financial institutions, and critical infrastructure (including public railways and ports) using its proprietary DDoSia DDoS tool. NoName recruited volunteers from around the world to download DDoSia and used their computers to launch DDoS attacks on victims selected by NoName leaders. NoName paid top-ranking volunteers in cryptocurrency through a daily Telegram leaderboard.

## Legal Analysis

### Jurisdiction

US federal jurisdiction in the Central District of California attached through the conspiracy and computer-fraud counts, with US-jurisdiction victims including public drinking-water systems and a meat-processing facility in LA. Defendant was extradited from an unenumerated source state in 2025 under DOJ OIA coordination.

### Key Legal Issues

- **Conspiracy to damage protected computers and tamper with public water systems** (CARR indictment).
- **Damaging protected computers** (CARR indictment).
- **Access device fraud** (CARR indictment).
- **Aggravated identity theft** (CARR indictment).
- **Conspiracy to damage protected computers** (NoName indictment).
- **State-sponsored hacktivist-group attribution** — first wiki-recorded US public-record indictment to directly allege GRU founding/funding/direction of a named Telegram hacktivist group (CARR) with juvenile membership.

### Precedent Value

This case is precedent-setting in the wiki corpus on three axes:

1. **GRU-attributed hacktivist group prosecution** — direct DOJ-charging-language attribution of CARR to GRU founding, funding, and direction.
2. **CISM-attributed hacktivist group prosecution** — first wiki-recorded US prosecution to identify the Russian Presidential-decree CISM organisation as a staffing source for a hacktivist DDoS group (NoName057(16)).
3. **US-jurisdictional extradition reach for Ukrainian operators of Russian-state-tied groups** — demonstrates that Ukrainian-national operators of Russian-tied groups can be extradited to the US for prosecution.

## Cooperation Effectiveness

The case exemplifies US extradition reach for a Ukrainian-national operator of Russian-state-tied groups. The release does not enumerate the source state from which Dubranova was extradited or the timing of the extradition; only that DOJ OIA "provided significant assistance for both investigations." The case is part of the FBI Los Angeles Field Office's broader **"Operation Red Circus"** enforcement frame against Russian state-sponsored cyberthreats.
